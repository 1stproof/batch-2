## Grader A critique

**Areas for Improvement**
1. **Fallacy:** Conflating $L^2$ convergence with everywhere pointwise convergence. A spectral gap guarantees $\|P_t f - \mu(f)\|_{L^2(\mu)} \to 0$, which inherently only implies convergence for $\mu$-almost every initial condition. Asserting it holds for *every* $x$ is false without an explicitly proven smoothing mechanism (such as the Strong Feller property).
2. **Fallacy:** Unjustified limit exchange. Exchanging $\lim_{n \to \infty}$ and $\lim_{t \to \infty}$ to conclude limiting uniqueness requires uniform pointwise convergence bounds across $n$. Because the drift $G_{1/n}$ approaches a singular Dirac delta, standard pointwise error constants will blow up.
3. **Slip:** Integrating over $C_0([0,1])$ using total variation bounds in Step 3 is glossed over. The decomposition $\int P_t^n f d\mu_n - \int P_t f d\mu = \int P_t^n f d(\mu_n-\mu) + \int (P_t^n f - P_t f)d\mu$ should be written explicitly to verify why pointwise convergence coupled with TV convergence is sufficient.
