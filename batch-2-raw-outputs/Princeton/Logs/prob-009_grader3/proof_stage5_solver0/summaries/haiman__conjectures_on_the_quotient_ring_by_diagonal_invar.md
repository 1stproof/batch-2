# Conjectures on the quotient ring by diagonal invariants (M. Haiman, 1994)

## Definitions
- **Definition.** The apolar form (to use Rota’s terminology) is the non-degenerate symmetric bilinear form h·, ·i on Q[X, Y ] defined by hf, gi = f(∂x1 , . . . , ∂xn , ∂y1 , . . . , ∂yn )g(x1 , . . . , xn , y1 , . . . , yn ) |X=Y =0 , where ∂z denotes the partial derivative operator with respect to z.
- **Definition.** The space Hn of diagonal harmonics for Sn is I ⊥ , where I is the ideal defining the ring Rn in (1.2.1).
- **Definition.** Let T be a spanning tree on the n + 1 vertices {0, 1, . . . , n}. Regarding 0 as the root of the tree, we say that {i < j} ⊆ {1, . . . , n} is an inversion in T if vertex j is an ancestor of i, i.e., j lies on the path connecting i to the root 0.
- **Definition.** A function f: {1, . . . , n} → {1, . . . , n} is a parking function if for all 1 ≤ k ≤ n, the cardinality of f −1 ({1, . . . , k}) is at least k.
- **Definition.** The weight w(f) of a parking function f is n(n + 1)/2 − \sum_i f(i).
- **Definition.** The socle of Rn (or more generally of any module over a polynomial ring) consists of those elements annihilated by all xi and yi .
- **Definition.** Let J be the ideal in Q[x1 , . . . , xn , e1 , . . . , en−1 ] consisting of all polynomials p(X, E) such that the operator p(∂x1 , . . . , ∂xn , E1, . . . , En−1) kills ∆(X). Define rings Tn = Q[x1 , . . . , xn, e1 , . . . , en−1 ]/J and Un = Q[e1 , . . . , en−1 ]/(J ∩ Q[E]).
- **Definition.** The commuting variety C is the subvariety of Mn × Mn consisting of pairs of matrices (X, Y ) such that XY = Y X.
- **Definition.** A function f(n) defined for n ∈ N is a quasi-polynomial with period h if for each i modulo h there is a polynomial pi (n) such that f(n) = pi (n) for all n ≡ i (mod h).

- **Proposition 1.3.1.** If I is a homogeneous ideal, then its orthogonal complement H = I ⊥ is a homogeneous space of polynomials, closed under the taking of arbitrary partial derivatives. We also have H = {h | f(∂X, ∂Y )h = 0 ∀f ∈ I}; in other words, regarding I as a system of polynomial partial differential equations, H is its space of solutions. If H is any homogeneous space of polynomials closed under partial derivatives, then I = H ⊥ is a homogeneous ideal with H = I ⊥ .
  *Proof:* Shows the orthogonal complement is closed under differentiation by using the adjointness of multiplication and partial derivatives.
