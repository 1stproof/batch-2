# THE LICKORISH-WALLACE THEOREM (W. B. R. Lickorish (1997). An Introduction to Knot Theory. Springer.)

## Definitions
- **Definition 0.1.** An elemental r-surgery is an operation on an n-mainfold M that is accomplished by removing from M an embedded S r × Dn−r , and replacing it with a copy of Dr+1 × S n−r−1 . The replacement is made along the standard homeomophism between the boundary of S r × Dn−r and of Dr+1 × S n−r−1 , taking advantage of the simple relationship between S k and Dk+1 . A surgery in general is then a sequence of these elemental surgeries.
- **Definition 0.3.** A function f : X → Y is a homeomorphism if f is bijective, continuous, and has a continuous inverse. Two spaces X and Y are homeomorphic if there exists a homeomorphism between them.
- **Definition 0.4.** A homotopy between two continuous functions f and g from topological space X to Y is a continuous function H : X × [0, 1] → Y such that H(x, 0) = f (x) and H(x, 1) = g(x) for all x ∈ X. Two functions are homotopic if there exists a homotopy between them.
- **Definition 0.5.** Two spaces X and Y are said to be homotopy equivalent or of the same homotopy type if there existcontinous maps in both directions between them such that the composition of these maps is homotopic to the identity map in each space.
- **Definition 0.6.** An isotopy is a homotopy H between two embeddings f, g : X → Y such that for any t ∈ [0, 1], the function H(x, t) of x is also an embedding.
- **Definition 1.1.** For an n-manifold M , the operation of attaching an r-handle is done by joining the manifold M with a copy of Dr × Dn−r along the embedding of the boundary of the “handle” into the boundary of M (or the boundary created by removing two copies of Dr from M), e : ∂Dr × Dn−r → ∂M. A handlebody is then defined to be a 3-ball with 1-handles attached. The number g of handles that are attached determines the genus of the handlebody.
- **Definition 1.2.** A Heegaard splitting of a closed connected orientable 3-manifold M is a set of two handlebodies U, V ⊂ M such that U ∪ V = M, but also U ∩ V = ∂U = ∂V.
- **Definition 2.1.** For a simple closed curve C embedded in a connected compact oriented surface F , a Dehn twist (which from this point on will be referred to simply as a twist) about C is a homeomorphism that is isotopic to the homeomorphism τ : F → F that is the identity outside of an annulus A on F , and on A, τ (eiθ , t) = (ei(θ−2πt) , t), where the coordinates are with respect to A when parameterized as S 1 × [0, 1].

## Lemmas, Theorems, Propositions, Corollaries
- **Theorem 0.2 (Lickorish-Wallace Theorem).** Any closed connected orientable 3-manifold M can be obtained from S 3 by a collection of 1-surgeries.
  *Proof:* (no proof in this paper)
- **Theorem 1.3.** Any closed connected orientable 3-manifold M has a Heegaard splitting.
  *Proof:* Constructs a Heegaard splitting by taking the neighborhood of a spanning tree in the second derived subdivision of a triangulation for one handlebody, and the closure of its complement for the other.
- **Theorem 2.2.** Given simple oriented closed curves p and q on the interior of a surface F that intersect transverely at a single point, p ∼τ q.
  *Proof:* Constructs two shifted copies of the curves and applies two successive twists to create a kink that can be eliminated via an isotopy.
- **Theorem 2.3.** Given two oriented simple closed curves p, q on the interior of the surface of F that are disjoint (rather than intersecting transversely at a single point), where neither curve separates F , then p ∼τ q.
  *Proof:* Cuts the surface along the union of the two curves and introduces an intermediary curve intersecting both transversely exactly once to apply Theorem 2.2.
- **Theorem 2.4.** Suppose that p and q are oriented simple closed curves on the interior of F that do not separate F (but now need not necessarily be disjoint). Then p ∼τ q.
  *Proof:* Inducts on the number of transverse intersections by constructing a non-separating intermediary curve that bounds the intersections to reduce to base cases.
- **Theorem 2.5.** For a set of disjoint simple closed oriented curves {p1 , p2 , . . . , pn } on the interior of F with ∪pi not seperating F , and another such set {q1 , q2 , . . . , qn }, there is some homeomorphism h generated by twists such that for each 1 ≤ i ≤ n, hpi = qi .
  *Proof:* Inducts on the number of curves by cutting the surface along the first n-1 curves and applying Theorem 2.4 to the n-th curves in the non-separated complement.
- **Theorem 3.1 (Lickorish-Wallace Theorem).** Any closed connected orientable 3-manifold M can be obtained from S 3 by a collection of 1-surgeries.
  *Proof:* Extends the boundary twist homeomorphisms into collar neighborhoods of the handlebodies, demonstrating that the resulting solid tori modifications correspond exactly to 1-surgeries.
