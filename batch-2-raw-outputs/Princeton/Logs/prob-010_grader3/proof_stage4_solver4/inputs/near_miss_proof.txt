**Part 3: The Proof**

PROOF_START
**Theorem:** Let $\Gamma$ be a finite simplicial graph with $|\VV(\Gamma)| \ge 3$, and $(M_v, \tau_v)$ be a separable tracial von Neumann algebra for each $v \in \VV(\Gamma)$. If $\Gamma$ is irreducible (in the sense that $\Gamma^c$ is connected), and each $M_v$ has a trace zero unitary, then the graph product von Neumann algebra $M$ is properly proximal.

**Proof:**
We proceed by induction on $n = |\VV(\Gamma)|$. 
*Definition:* By Boutonnet, Ioana, and Peterson (2021), a tracial von Neumann algebra $N$ is properly proximal if there does not exist an $N$-central state $\varphi$ on $\mathbb{B}(L^2N)$ extending the trace $\tau_N$ such that $\varphi(\mathcal{J}_{prop}(N)) = 0$, where $\mathcal{J}_{prop}(N)$ is the norm-closed proper proximal ideal in $\mathbb{B}(L^2N)$ generated by $\{x e_D^N y \mid x, y \in N, D \subset N \text{ amenable}\}$.

**Step 1: Base Cases ($n=3$)**
Because $\Gamma^c$ is connected and $n=3$, $\Gamma^c$ is exactly the complete graph $K_3$ or the path graph $P_3$.
1. If $\Gamma^c = K_3$, the graph $\Gamma$ has no edges, yielding the free product $M = M_1 * M_2 * M_3$. We algebraically group this as $A * B$ with $A = M_1$ and $B = M_2 * M_3$. Each $M_v$ possesses a trace zero unitary, dictating $\dim(M_v) \ge 2$. Thus, $\dim(A) \ge 2$ and $\dim(B) \ge 2 \times 2 = 4 \ge 3$.
2. If $\Gamma^c = P_3$, the edges of $\Gamma$ strictly consist of a single edge connecting the two endpoints. Thus, two factors commute and the third is free, giving $M = M_2 * (M_1 \overline{\otimes} M_3)$. Grouping $A = M_2$ and $B = M_1 \overline{\otimes} M_3$, we secure $\dim(A) \ge 2$ and $\dim(B) \ge 4 \ge 3$.

In both configurations, $M$ decomposes as a free product of two tracial von Neumann algebras with individual dimensions $\ge 2$ and $\ge 3$. By Boutonnet-Ioana-Peterson (2021) Theorem 4.1, such a free product is properly proximal.

**Step 2: Inductive Amalgamated Decomposition**
Assume the theorem holds for all irreducible graphs with $k$ vertices, where $3 \le k < n$. Let $\Gamma$ be an irreducible graph with $n \ge 4$ vertices. Since $G = \Gamma^c$ is a connected graph with at least 4 vertices, it contains a non-cut vertex $v$. The induced subgraph $\Gamma' = \Gamma \setminus \{v\}$ remains irreducible because $(\Gamma')^c = G \setminus \{v\}$ is connected. By the induction hypothesis, $A = M_{\Gamma'}$ is properly proximal.

The graph product decomposes as an amalgamated free product $M = A *_C B$, where $C = M_{lk(v)}$ and $B = C \overline{\otimes} M_v$. Because $v$ has at least one neighbor in $G$, $lk(v) \subsetneq \VV(\Gamma')$, ensuring $C \subsetneq A$. 
Assume for contradiction that $M$ is not properly proximal. Then there exists an $M$-central state $\varphi$ on $\mathbb{B}(L^2M)$ with $\varphi|_M = \tau_M$ and $\varphi(\mathcal{J}_{prop}(M)) = 0$.

**Step 3: Geometric Ping-Pong on Word Spaces**
The Hilbert space decomposes as $L^2M = L^2C \oplus \mathcal{H}_A \oplus \mathcal{H}_B$, where $\mathcal{H}_A$ (resp. $\mathcal{H}_B$) is the closed linear span of reduced alternating words strictly beginning with a letter in $A \ominus C$ (resp. $B \ominus C$). Let $e_C$, $Q_A$, and $Q_B$ be the orthogonal projections onto these spaces. We have the exact equality $1 = e_C + Q_A + Q_B$.

Let $u_v \in M_v$ be a trace-zero unitary, so $u = 1 \otimes u_v \in B \ominus C$. For any vector $\xi \in \mathcal{H}_A$, the word $u \xi$ strictly begins with $u \in B \ominus C$ (no algebraic cancellation occurs into $C$ since $\xi$ strictly begins with $A \ominus C$). This translates to the spatial containment $u \mathcal{H}_A \subset \mathcal{H}_B$, yielding the operator inequality $u Q_A u^* \le Q_B$. Applying the $M$-central state $\varphi$, we deduce $\varphi(Q_A) = \varphi(u^* u Q_A u^* u) = \varphi(u Q_A u^*) \le \varphi(Q_B)$.

We now locate two elements $w_1, w_2 \in A \ominus C$ to partition $Q_B$. Let $N_G(v)$ be the neighbors of $v$ in $G = \Gamma^c$.
- **Case A ($|N_G(v)| \ge 2$):** Select distinct $x, y \in N_G(v)$. 
  - If $x$ and $y$ are adjacent in $G$, they do not commute in $\Gamma$. Let $w_1 = u_x$ and $w_2 = u_x u_y u_x$. Because $x, y \notin lk(v)$, the conditional expectation onto $C$ evaluates to $E_C(u_x u_y u_x) = 0$, ensuring $w_2 \in A \ominus C$. We evaluate $w_1^* w_2 = u_x^* u_x u_y u_x = u_y u_x \in A \ominus C$. 
  - If $x$ and $y$ are not adjacent in $G$, they commute in $\Gamma$. Let $w_1 = u_x$ and $w_2 = u_y$. Because they commute and reside in distinct factors disjoint from $C$, $w_1^* w_2 = u_x^* u_y \in A \ominus C$.
- **Case B ($|N_G(v)| = 1$):** Let $x$ be the unique neighbor. Because $|V(G)| \ge 4$ and $G$ is connected, $x$ has another neighbor $y$ in $G$. Since $y \neq v$ and $x$ is the unique neighbor of $v$ in $G$, $y \notin N_G(v)$, meaning $y \in lk(v) = C$. Let $w_1 = u_x$ and $w_2 = u_x u_y u_x$. As $x \notin lk(v)$, $E_C(u_x u_y u_x) = 0$, yielding $w_2 \in A \ominus C$. Using the $C$-bilinearity of the conditional expectation, $E_C(w_1^* w_2) = E_C(u_y u_x) = u_y E_C(u_x) = 0$, establishing $w_1^* w_2 \in A \ominus C$.

For any $\xi, \zeta \in \mathcal{H}_B$, left multiplication prepends $w_i$, mapping them into $\mathcal{H}_A$ (thus $w_i Q_B w_i^* \le Q_A$). Their inner product is $\langle w_1 \xi, w_2 \zeta \rangle = \langle \xi, w_1^* w_2 \zeta \rangle$. Because $w_1^* w_2 \in A \ominus C$, the vector $(w_1^* w_2)\zeta$ belongs to $\mathcal{H}_A$. Since $\mathcal{H}_A \perp \mathcal{H}_B$, this inner product evaluates strictly to 0. Thus, $w_1 Q_B w_1^*$ and $w_2 Q_B w_2^*$ are mutually orthogonal subprojections of $Q_A$.

Summing them yields $w_1 Q_B w_1^* + w_2 Q_B w_2^* \le Q_A$. Applying $\varphi$ provides $2 \varphi(Q_B) \le \varphi(Q_A)$.
Coupled with $\varphi(Q_A) \le \varphi(Q_B)$, this structurally forces $\varphi(Q_A) = 0$ and $\varphi(Q_B) = 0$. Consequently, $\varphi(e_C) = \varphi(1 - Q_A - Q_B) = 1$. Because $C \subset A$, the projection geometry mandates $e_C \le e_A$, dictating $\varphi(e_A) = 1$.

**Step 4: Proper Proximal Ideal Restriction (Lemma OC-4)**
Define the functional $\psi$ on $\mathbb{B}(L^2A)$ (canonically identified with $e_A \mathbb{B}(L^2M) e_A$) by $\psi(T) = \varphi(e_A T e_A)$.
1. **State Status:** $\psi(1_{L^2A}) = \varphi(e_A) = 1$, and positivity is preserved.
2. **Centrality:** For any $a \in A$, $a$ commutes with $e_A$. Hence, $\psi(a T) = \varphi(e_A a e_A T e_A) = \varphi(a e_A T e_A) = \varphi(e_A T e_A a) = \psi(T a)$, confirming $\psi$ is $A$-central.
3. **Trace Restriction:** For $a \in A$, $\psi(a) = \varphi(a e_A)$. Utilizing the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality for states, $|\varphi(a (1-e_A))|^2 \le \varphi(a a^*) \varphi(1-e_A) = 0$. Thus, $\psi(a) = \varphi(a) = \tau_M(a) = \tau_A(a)$.
4. **Ideal Vanishing:** The ideal $\mathcal{J}_{prop}(A)$ is the norm-closed two-sided ideal generated by elements $a e_D^A b$ for $a,b \in A$ and amenable $D \subset A$. Because $e_D^A = e_A e_D^M e_A$, we structurally rewrite the generator as $a e_D^A b = a e_A e_D^M e_A b = e_A (a e_D^M b) e_A$. Since $D \subset M$ is amenable and $a,b \in M$, the internal element $a e_D^M b$ inherently belongs to $\mathcal{J}_{prop}(M)$. Because $\mathcal{J}_{prop}(M)$ is a two-sided ideal in $\mathbb{B}(L^2M)$, left and right multiplication by the bounded operator $e_A$ secures $e_A (a e_D^M b) e_A \in \mathcal{J}_{prop}(M)$. By our founding contradiction assumption, $\varphi(\mathcal{J}_{prop}(M)) = 0$, meaning $\psi(a e_D^A b) = \varphi(e_A (a e_D^M b) e_A) = 0$. By linearity and norm-continuity, $\psi(\mathcal{J}_{prop}(A)) = 0$.

The existence of the state $\psi$ on $\mathbb{B}(L^2A)$ strictly violates the induction hypothesis that $A$ is properly proximal. This mathematical contradiction proves that no such state $\varphi$ can exist on $\mathbb{B}(L^2M)$. Therefore, the graph product von Neumann algebra $M$ is properly proximal.
