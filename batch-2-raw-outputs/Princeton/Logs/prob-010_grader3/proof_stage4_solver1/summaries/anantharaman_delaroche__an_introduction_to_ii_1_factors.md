# C. Anantharaman-Delaroche, S. Popa (2017). An Introduction to II_1 factors.

## Definitions
- **Definition 1.5.** A linear functional ϕ on a von Neumann algebra M is positive if ϕ(x∗ x) ≥ 0 for every x ∈ M . Whenever ϕ(x∗ x) = 0 implies x = 0, we say that ϕ is faithful. If ϕ is positive with ϕ(1) = 1 we say that ϕ is a state. A positive linear functional such ϕ(xy) = ϕ(yx) for every x, y ∈ M is a trace. If moreover it is a state, we call it a tracial state .
- **Definition 1.6.** Given a von Neumann algebra M acting on a Hilbert space H, a vector ξ ∈ H is called cyclic for M if M ξ is dense in H. It is called separating for M if, for x ∈ M , we have xξ = 0 if and only if x = 0.
- **Definition 1.7.** A tracial von Neumann algebra (M, τ ) is a von Neumann algebra M equipped with a faithful tracial state whose restriction to the unit ball is continuous with respect to the w.o. topology
- **Definition 1.11.** A type II1 factor is an infinite dimensional tracial von Neumann algebra M whose center is reduced to the scalar operators
- **Definition 1.12.** A type I factor is a factor isomorphic to some B(H). If dim H = n, we say that M (which is isomorphic to Mn (C)) is of type In . If dim H = ∞, we say that M is of type I∞ .
- **Definition 1.13.** We say that two von Neumann algebras M1 and M2 are isomorphic, and we write M1 ≃ M2 , if there exists a bijective homomorphism (i.e. an isomorphism) α : M1 → M2 .
- **Definition 1.14.** We say that the von Neumann algebras M1 , M2 , acting on H1 , H2 respectively, are spatially isomorphic if there exists a unitary operator U : H1 → H2 such that x 7→ U xU ∗ is an isomorphism (called spatial) from M1 onto M2 .
- **Definition 1.17.** A probability measure preserving (p.m.p.) action G y (X, µ) of a countable group G on a probability measure space (X, µ) is a group homomorphism from G into Aut (X, µ). The action of g ∈ G on w ∈ X will be written gw.
- **Definition 1.18.** A p.m.p. action G y (X, µ) is (essentially) free if every g ∈ G, g 6= e, acts (essentially) freely, i.e. the set {w ∈ X : gw = w} has µ-measure 0. The action is said to be ergodic if every Borel subset E of X such that µ(gE \ E) = 0 for every g 6= e is either a null set or a conull set.
- **Definition 1.24.** A countable or discrete equivalence relation is an equivalence relation R ⊂ X × X on a standard Borel space X, which is a Borel subset of X × X and whose equivalence classes are finite or countable.
- **Definition 1.26.** Let R be a countable p.m.p. equivalence relation and let A be a Borel subset of X. We denote by [A]R = p1 (p−1 2 (A)) = p2 (p−1 1 (A)) the R-saturation of A, where p1 , p2 are the left and right projections from R onto X. We say that A is invariant (or saturated) if [A]R = A (up to null sets). The relation (R, µ) is called ergodic if any invariant Borel subset is either null or co-null.
- **Definition 1.30.** We say that two countable p.m.p. equivalence relations R1 and R2 on (X1 , µ1 ) and (X2 , µ2 ) respectively are isomorphic if there exists an isomorphism θ : (X1 , µ1 ) → (X2 , µ2 ) (of probability measure spaces, i.e. θ∗ µ1 = µ2 ) such that (θ × θ)(R1 ) = R2 , up to null sets. We say that θ induces an isomorphism from R1 onto R2 . If this holds when R1 = RG1 and R2 = RG2 we say that the actions G1 y (X1 , µ1 ) and G2 y (X2 , µ2 ) are orbit equivalent. This means that for a.e. x ∈ X1 , we have θ(G1 x) = G2 θ(x).
- **Definition 1.32.** Let M be a von Neumann algebra and B a von Neumann subalgebra. A conditional expectation from M to B is a linear map E : M → B which satisfies the following properties : (i) E(M+ ) ⊂ B+ ; (ii) E(b) = b for b ∈ B ; (iii) E(b1 xb2 ) = b1 E(x)b2 for b1 , b2 ∈ B and x ∈ M .
- **Definition 1.33.** A countable group G is called amenable if there exists a left invariant mean m on G, that is a state m on `∞ (G) such that m(s f ) = m(f ) for every s ∈ G and f ∈ `∞ (G), where (s f )(t) = f (s−1 t) for all t ∈ G.
- **Definition 2.2.** Given a von Neumann algebra M and a von Neumann subalgebra A, the normalizer of A in M is the group NM (A) of unitary operators u ∈ M such that uAu∗ = A. Note that NM (A) contains the unitary group U(A) of A as a normal subgroup.
- **Definition 3.1.** Let M be a von Neumann algebras. A M -M -bimodule is a Hilbert space H which is both a left and a right M -module, and is such that the left and right actions commute.
- **Definition 3.4.** A countable group G is said to have the Haagerup property (or property (H)) if there exists a sequence (ϕn ) of positive definite functions on G such that ϕn ∈ c0 (G) for every n and limn ϕn = 1 pointwise.
- **Definition 3.7.** Let H be a subgroup of a countable group G. We say that the pair (G, H) has the relative property (T) (or that H ⊂ G is a rigid embedding) if any sequence of positive definite functions on G that converges pointwise to the constant function 1 converges uniformly on H.
- **Definition 3.10.** We say that A is rigidly embedded in M , or that the pair (M, A) has the relative property (T) if any of the two equivalent conditions (ii) and (iii) of the previous theorem is satisfied.

## Lemmas, Theorems, Propositions, Corollaries
- **Proposition 1.1.** Let ω be a linear functional on B(H). The following conditions are equivalent : (i) there exist ξ1 , . . . , ξn , η1 , . . . ηn ∈ H such that ω(x) = ∑ni=1 ωηi ,ξi (x) for all x ∈ B(H) ; (ii) ω is w.o. continuous ; (iii) ω is s.o. continuous.
  *Proof:* Extends a linear functional on a finite direct sum of Hilbert spaces and applies the Hahn-Banach or Riesz representation theorems.
- **Corollary 1.2.** The above proposition remains true when B(H) is replaced by any von Neumann subalgebra M .
  *Proof:* (immediate from Proposition 1.1)
- **Theorem 1.3 (von Neumann’s bicommutant theorem).** Let M be a unital self-adjoint subalgebra of B(H). The following conditions are equivalent : (i) M = M 00 . (ii) M is weakly closed. (iii) M is strongly closed.
  *Proof:* Proves the non-trivial inclusion by applying orthogonal projections onto cyclic subspaces and extending to $n$-tuples via a diagonal matrix construction.
- **Proposition 1.4.** Let (X, µ) be a probability measure space. We set A = L∞ (X, µ). (i) For f ∈ L∞ (X, µ), denote by Mf the multiplication operator by f on L2 (X, µ). Then Mf is a bounded operator and kMf k = kf k∞ . (ii) If A is identified to a subalgebra of B(L2 (X, µ)), then A = A0 . In particular, A is a von Neumann algebra in L2 (X, µ) and a maximal abelian subalgebra of B(L2 (X, µ)).
  *Proof:* Deduced straightforwardly from classical bounds in measure theory and verifying that elements of the commutant act as multiplication operators.
- **Proposition 1.10.** Let G be a countable group. The following conditions are equivalent: (i) L(G) is a factor; (ii) G is an ICC (infinite conjugacy classes) group, i.e. every non trivial conjugacy class gsg −1 : g ∈ G , s 6= e, is infinite.
  *Proof:* Shows that central elements correspond to square-summable functions constant on conjugacy classes, forcing non-trivial classes to be infinite.
- **Proposition 1.15.** Let H be a subgroup of a countable group G. Then the restriction of λG to H is a multiple of the left regular representation of H
  *Proof:* Decomposes the group into right H-cosets to recognize the representation as a direct sum of regular representations on invariant subspaces.
- **Theorem 1.16.** JM J = M 0 .
  *Proof:* Establishes $JMJ \subset M'$ by algebraic verification, and uses the inner product to prove $M' \subset JMJ$ by showing that $JzJ$ belongs to the bicommutant $M'' = M$.
- **Lemma 1.19.** Let G y (X, µ) be a p.m.p. action. The following conditions are equivalent : (i) the action is ergodic ; (ii) (resp. (ii’)) the only functions f ∈ L∞ (X, µ) (resp. f ∈ L2 (X, µ)) that are fixed under the G-action (i.e. σg (f ) = f for every g ∈ G) are the constant (a.e.) functions ; (iii) the only measurable functions f : X → C that are fixed under the G-action are the constant (a.e.) functions.
  *Proof:* Proves equivalence by considering the invariant level sets of measurable functions.
- **Lemma 1.20.** Let (X, B, µ) be a probability measure space, g ∈ Aut (X, µ) and σg the corresponding automorphism of L∞ (X, µ). The following conditions are equivalent: (i) g acts freely ; (ii) for every Y ∈ B with µ(Y ) > 0, there exists a Borel subset Z of Y with µ(Z) > 0 and Z ∩ gZ = ∅ ; (iii) if a ∈ L∞ (X, µ) is such that aσg (x) = xa for every x ∈ L∞ (X, µ), then a = 0.
  *Proof:* Constructs disjoint sets using a separating family of Borel subsets, proving equivalence by contradiction on the multiplication operators.
- **Proposition 1.21.** Let G y (X, µ) be a p.m.p. action and set A = L∞ (X, µ). (i) A0 ∩ (A o G) = A if and only if the action is free. (ii) Assume that the action is free. Then A o G is a factor (and thus a type II1 factor) if and only if the action is ergodic.
  *Proof:* Characterizes relative commutants and centers by expanding elements as series of canonical unitaries and using the independence of shifts.
- **Proposition 1.22.** The Bernoulli action G y X is free and ergodic.
  *Proof:* Demonstrates freeness by choosing infinite disjoint sets of indices, and establishes the stronger mixing property via a monotone class argument on cylinder sets.
- **Lemma 1.25.** Let R be a countable equivalence relation on a probability measure space (X, µ). The two following conditions are equivalent: (i) R preserves the measure µ ; (ii) for every ϕ : A → B in [[R]], we have ϕ∗ (µ|A ) = µ|B . Whenever an action G y X is given and R = RG , these conditions are also equivalent to (iii) G y X preserves µ.
  *Proof:* Partitions the equivalence relation into countable graphs of partial isomorphisms via the Lusin-Novikov theorem.
- **Proposition 1.28.** Let R be a countable p.m.p. equivalence relation on (X, µ). (i) L∞ (X, µ)0 ∩ L(R) = L∞ (X, µ), that is, L∞ (X, µ) is a maximal abelian subalgebra of L(R). (ii) The center of L(R) is the algebra of invariant functions in L∞ (X, µ). In particular, L(R) is a factor (and thus a type II1 factor) if and only if the equivalence relation is ergodic.
  *Proof:* Uses the relative commutant of multiplication operators to force general elements in the equivalence relation's von Neumann algebra to be supported only on the diagonal.
- **Theorem 1.31.** L(S∞ ) and L(F2 ) are non-isomorphic.
  *Proof:* Concludes from Proposition 1.36 and the fact that $S_\infty$ is amenable whereas $F_2$ is not.
- **Proposition 1.35.** Let G be a countable group. The following conditions are equivalent: (i) G is amenable ; (ii) there exists a sequence (ξn ) of unit vectors in `2 (G) such that, for every g ∈ G, limn kλ(g)ξn − ξn k2 = 0; (iii) there exists a sequence of finitely supported positive definite functions on G which converges pointwise to 1 ; (iv) there exists a sequence (En ) of finite, non-empty, subsets of G such that, for every g ∈ G, limn |gEn ∆En | / |En | = 0.
  *(no proof in this paper)*
- **Proposition 1.36.** Let G be a countable group and M = L(G). Then G is amenable if and only if there exists a conditional expectation E from B(L2 (M )) onto M .
  *Proof:* Constructs a left invariant mean from the trace of the conditional expectation, and conversely builds the projection using the invariant mean to average matrix elements.
- **Theorem 2.1.** Let R1 and R2 be two countable p.m.p. equivalence relations, on X1 and X2 respectively, and let θ : (X1 , µ1 ) → (X2 , µ2 ) be an isomorphism of probability measure spaces. The two following conditions are equivalent: (i) θ induces an isomorphism from R1 onto R2 ; (ii) θ∗ : L∞ (X1 ) ≃ L∞ (X2 ) extends to an isomorphism from the von Neumann algebra L(R1 ) onto L(R2 ).
  *(no proof in this paper)*
- **Theorem 2.3.** The fundamental group of L∞ (T2 ) o F2 is {1}.
  *Proof:* Leverages Popa's rigidity result on unique Cartan subalgebras to show scaled isomorphisms of the factor force orbit equivalence isomorphisms, falling back on Gaboriau's theorem for free groups.
- **Proposition 3.2.** Let G y (X, µ) be a p.m.p. action of a countable group and set A = L∞ (X), M = L∞ (X) o G. Let ϕ : G → C be a positive definite function. There is a unique completely positive map φ : M → M , whose restriction to the unit ball of M is w.o. continuous, such that φ(aug ) = ϕ(g)aug for every a ∈ A = L∞ (X) and g ∈ G.
  *Proof:* Employs the GNS representation of the positive definite function to construct a bimodule and defines the completely positive map via bounded intertwiners.
- **Proposition 3.8.** The folllowing conditions are equivalent: (i) the pair (G, H) has the relative property (T) ; (ii) for every ε > 0, there exist a finite subset F of G and δ > 0 such that if (π, H) is a unitary representation of G and ξ ∈ H is a unit vector satisfying maxg∈F kπ(g)ξ − ξk ≤ δ, then there is a H-invariant vector η ∈ H with kξ − ηk ≤ ε.
  *Proof:* Establishes the equivalence purely algebraically by bounding the difference between the given matrix coefficient and 1 using the representation vectors.
- **Theorem 3.9.** Let H be a subgroup of a countable group G. We set M = L(G) and A = L(H). The following conditions are equivalent : (i) the pair (G, H) has the relative property (T) ; (ii) for every ε > 0, there exist a finite subset F of M and δ > 0 such that for any M -M -bimodule H and any vector ξ ∈ H satisfying ∀x ∈ M, hξ, xξi = τ (x) = hξ, ξxi, and maxx∈F kxξ − ξxk ≤ δ, there exists a vector η ∈ H such that kξ − ηk ≤ ε and aη = ηa for a ∈ A. (iii) for every ε > 0, there exist a finite subset F of M and δ > 0 such that for every completely positive map φ : M → M satisfying τ ◦ φ = τ , φ(1) = 1 and maxx∈F kφ(x) − xk2 ≤ δ, then sup{a∈A,kak≤1} kφ(a) − ak2 ≤ ε.
  *Proof:* Extends relative property (T) to nearly-invariant vectors in bimodules by using canonical unitaries, and translates these back to completely positive approximations via the GNS-bimodule construction.
- **Theorem 3.11.** In M = L(Z2 )oF2 , any two rigidly embedded Cartan subalgebras A1 , A2 are unitary conjugate, that is, there exists a unitary operator u ∈ M such that uA1 u∗ = A2 .
  *Proof:* Uses the Haagerup property to find a completely positive map approximating the identity on the algebra, and projects onto the Jones basic construction to yield a finite-dimensional right-module subbimodule linking the Cartan subalgebras.
