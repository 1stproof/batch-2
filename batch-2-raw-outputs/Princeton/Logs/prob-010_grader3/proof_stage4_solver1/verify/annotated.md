**Step 1: Free product proper proximality threshold**
*Claim:* The free product of two tracial von Neumann algebras $A * B$ is properly proximal provided $\dim A \ge 2$ and $\dim B \ge 3$.

**Step 2: Existence of non-cut vertex**

**Step 3: Amalgamated graph product decomposition**

*Citation:* APPROX M. Caspers and P. Fima, Graph products of operator algebras (arXiv preprint), Section 3, "provides the structural theorem decomposing a graph product of operator algebras into an amalgamated free product"

**Step 4: Central state characterization**
*Claim:* A von Neumann algebra is properly proximal if there is no trace-extending central state vanishing entirely on the proper proximal ideal $\mathcal{J}_{prop}(M)$.
*Citation:* CONFIDENT arxiv:1809.01881, Section 2, "canonically formulates the proper proximal ideal and the exact central state equivalence"

**Step 5: $L^2$-space orthogonal word decomposition**
