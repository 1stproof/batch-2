# Essentials of Tropical Combinatorics (Joswig, 2021)

## Definitions
- **Definition.** tropical polynomial F vanishes at p :⇔ there are at least two terms where the minimum F (p) is attained
- **Definition.** tropical hypersurface T (F ) := vanishing locus of (multi-variate) tropical polynomial F
- **Definition.** Newton polytope N (F ) = convex hull of the support supp(F )
- **Definition.** tropical (d − 1)-torus Td−1 := Rd /R1
- **Definition.** fine type of p ∈ Td−1 w.r.t. V given by TV (p) ∈ {0, 1}n×d with TV (p)ik = 1 ⇔ vik − pk ≤ vij − pj for all j ∈ [d]
- **Definition.** Polytopal subdivision of P + Q into Minkowski cells is mixed if for any two of its cells P ′ + Q′ and P ′′ + Q′′ the intersections P ′ ∩ P ′′ and Q′ ∩ Q′′ both are faces.
- **Definition.** Let A = A(V ) be an arrangement on n tropical hyperplanes in Td−1 . The coarse type ideal of A is the monomial ideal It(A) = hxt(p) : p ∈ Td−1 i ⊂ K[x1 , . . . , xd ] where xt(p) = x1t(p)1 x2t(p)2 · · · xdt(p)d .

- **Proposition.** For a tropical polynomial F : Rd → R the set P(F ) := (p, s) ∈ Rd+1 : p ∈ Rd , s ∈ R, s ≤ F (p) is an unbounded convex polyhedron of dimension d + 1.

- **Corollary.** The tropical hypersurface T (F ) coincides with the image of the codimension-2-skeleton of the polyhedron P(F ) in Rd under the orthogonal projection which omits the last coordinate.
  *Proof:* (immediate from Proposition)
- **Theorem.** The tropical hypersurface T (F ) of a tropical polynomial F is dual to the 1-coskeleton of the regular subdivision of N (F ) induced by the coefficients of F .
  *Proof:* (no proof in this paper)
- **Theorem (Einsiedler, Kapranov & Lind 2006).** For f ∈ K[x1±1 , x2±1 , . . . , xd±1 ] the tropical hypersurface T (trop(f )) ∩ Qd (over the rationals) equals the set val(V (hf i)).
  *Proof:* Demonstrates the forward inclusion by evaluating the tropicalization on a zero of the original polynomial and showing the minimum must be attained at least twice to allow cancellation.
