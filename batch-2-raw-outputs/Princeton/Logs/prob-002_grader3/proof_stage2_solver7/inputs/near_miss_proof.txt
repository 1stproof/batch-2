**Part 2: The Exact Algebraic Sequence of Affine Constraints (Closing OC-1)**
On each clean triangle $\tau$, the map $f$ is affine. Let $\tau$ have its base on $y=0$ between domain coordinates $x_1$ and $x_2$, and a top vertex at $(v, 1)$. 
The affine map $f$ has a constant Jacobian matrix $M_\tau = \begin{pmatrix} u & w \end{pmatrix}$, where $u$ is the boundary tangent vector and $w$ dictates the transverse mapping. The squeeze condition enforces two constraints:
1. The base is compressed: $\|u\| < 1$.
2. The two crossing edges are strictly extended. For a domain horizontal offset $t$ between the top vertex and a base point, the unmapped length is $\sqrt{t^2+1}$. The mapped chord in $\R^3$ is $W(t) = t u + w$.

The extension condition requires that for both boundary vertices $t \in \{v-x_1, v-x_2\}$, the mapped length strictly exceeds the unmapped length. This yields the exact discrete algebraic sequence of quadratic bounds:
$$g(t) = \|t u + w\|^2 - (t^2 + 1) > 0$$
Expanding this, we get $g(t) = t^2(\|u\|^2 - 1) + 2t(u \cdot w) + \|w\|^2 - 1 > 0$. 
Because $\|u\| < 1$, the leading coefficient is strictly negative, meaning $g(t)$ represents a downward-opening parabola. For $g(t)$ to be strictly positive at both domain vertices $x_1$ and $x_2$, the entire continuous interval $t \in [v-x_2, v-x_1]$ must lie strictly between the roots of the parabola. 
Consequently, the continuous family of spatial chords $W(t)$ swept by the affine map across the entire triangle satisfies $\|W(t)\| > \sqrt{t^2+1}$ for all $t$. This forces the resulting continuous polyhedral Moebius band to be ruled by straight line segments that globally avoid the unit Minkowski hyperboloid $R(t) = \sqrt{t^2+1}$.

**Part 3: The Global Variational Bound on the Infimum**
Tracing the sequence of crossing edges across a fundamental domain $[0, 2\beta]$ yields a continuous polygonal path $W(t)$ in $\R^3$ connecting an initial chord $W_0$ to its exact reversed orientation $-W_0$.

$$2\beta > P \ge L \ge 2\sqrt{3} \implies \beta > \sqrt{3}$$
