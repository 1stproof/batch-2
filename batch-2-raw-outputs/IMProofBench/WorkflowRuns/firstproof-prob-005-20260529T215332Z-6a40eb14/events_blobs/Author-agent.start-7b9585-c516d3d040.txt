I would not try to justify this as a one-shot “random Cameron–Martin shift”. The right adapted theorem would be path-space Girsanov/Üstünel–Zakai for adapted shifts, but it requires finite \(L^2([t-\varepsilon,t];L^2)\)-energy of the drift. For the regularisations \(G_{1/n}(u)\) this energy blows like the square of an approximate delta, so that route is not stable. A Ramer/Malliavin map theorem also looks unattractive: the derivative contains \(G'_{1/n}(u)\), and I would expect determinant/negative-moment bounds to blow up.

The late-noise idea may still be viable, but I think it has to be **parabolic multiscale/triangular**, not a single split. A concrete architecture:

1. Work first with the regularised equations and expand in Dirichlet sine blocks \(\Pi_j\), \(|k|\sim 2^j\).

2. For block \(j\), reserve only a terminal time window of parabolic length
   \[
   \tau_j\sim 2^{-2j}.
   \]
   The noise from this window contributes a Gaussian component to \(\Pi_j u_t\) with covariance comparable to the Brownian-bridge covariance on that block.

3. Condition on the past and on the other noise pieces. On this finite-dimensional block/window, use ordinary adapted Girsanov. The entropy/Hellinger cost is controlled by
   \[
   \int_{t-\tau_j}^t \|\Pi_j G_{1/n}(u_s^n)\|_{L^2}^2\,ds .
   \]

4. Prove the key summable estimate, uniformly in \(n\),
   \[
   \sum_j \mathbb E\int_{t-c2^{-2j}}^t
        \|\Pi_j G_{1/n}(u_s^n)\|_{L^2}^2\,ds <\infty,
   \]
   or a conditional/localised version. Heuristically this is exactly where the local-time structure helps: if \(\delta_0(u_s(\theta))\,d\theta\) is the spatial local-time measure \(d_\theta \ell^0_s\), then
   \[
   \|\Pi_j d\ell^0_s\|_{L^2}^2 \lesssim 2^j \|\ell^0_s\|_{\mathrm{TV}}^2,
   \]
   and multiplying by \(\tau_j\sim2^{-2j}\) gives \(O(2^{-j})\), summable.

5. Use the entropy chain rule/Kakutani product criterion to pass from blockwise conditional absolute continuity to full terminal absolute continuity relative to the Gaussian bridge law. Older drift contributions are then conditionally Cameron–Martin with summable block norm because of heat smoothing.

This is close in spirit to the “Gaussian structure” arguments of Mattingly–Romito–Su for singular Burgers: fresh parabolic-scale noise beats the nonlinear remainder block by block. The central hard lemma is the uniform dyadic local-time estimate above. If the BZ drift were effectively one spatial derivative rougher, giving \(\|\Pi_j b\|^2\sim 2^{3j}\), the scheme would fail sharply. But for the measure \(d_\theta\ell^0\) scaling, it looks plausible.

I do not know a black-box theorem giving all-starting fixed-time AC for this distributional Nemytskii drift. BZ/FOT give quasi-everywhere resolvent AC; standard SPDE strong-Feller/Malliavin theorems do not seem to apply directly. So I would present this as a concrete possible proof route, with the dyadic projection/local-time energy estimate as the make-or-break input.

The late-noise decomposition is the right qualitative picture, but as you observe, the bare adapted Cameron–Martin shift cannot suffice. Here is the architecture I would attempt, and the precise place where I expect difficulty.

**Architecture (Malliavin on regularizations + uniform passage).**

(1) For each $n$, the regularized equation has bounded smooth $H$-valued drift $G_{1/n}$. The solution $u^n_t$ is Malliavin-smooth in the noise; its Malliavin derivative satisfies the linearization
\[
\partial_t \phi = \tfrac12\Delta_D \phi + G_{1/n}'(u^n)\phi,
\]
giving the Feynman–Kac representation
\[
D_{s,y}u^n_t(x) = \mathbb{E}_{B}\!\left[S_{t-s}(x,y)\,\exp\!\Big(\!\int_s^t G_{1/n}'(u_r^n(B_r))\,dr\Big)\,\Big|\, B_s=y\right].
\]
Bouleau–Hirsch nondegeneracy of $M_t^n(x) = \int_{t-\varepsilon}^t D_s u_t^n\otimes D_s u_t^n\, ds$ gives, for each $n$, a density of $P_t^n(x,\cdot)$ against $\pi_n$. The target is a *uniform-in-$n$* density bound
\[
\sup_n \|dP_t^n(x,\cdot)/d\pi_n\|_{L^p(\pi_n)} \le C(t,x), \qquad x\in K\text{ compact}.
\]
Combined with $\pi_n\to\pi$ in TV and your (A), this gives $P_t(x,\cdot)\ll\pi$ for every $x$, via lower semicontinuity of $L^p$ density along weakly convergent measures with TV-convergent reference. This is the rigorous version of your late-noise splitting: the Malliavin matrix detects exactly the "transversality of the adapted shift to the noise" that pure Cameron–Martin language cannot capture.

(2) **Sharp obstruction.** The Feynman–Kac potential $G_{1/n}'(u^n)$ has $\|G_{1/n}'\|_\infty\sim n^2$ but is supported near $\{u^n=0\}$ of Lebesgue measure $O(1/n)$; this is a borderline renormalization problem. The gradient structure $G_{1/n}' = B_n''$ with $0\le B_n\le 1$ is suggestive — integration by parts in space against $S_{t-s}$ should convert pointwise blow-up into a bounded *local-time differential* $\partial_y \ell^0$. But space-time white noise makes the spatial quadratic variation of $u^n_t(y)$ ill-defined, blocking the obvious Itô identity for $B_n(u^n)$. I am uncertain whether the cancellation survives iteration over the time interval.

**Closest precedents.**
- **Hairer–Mattingly 2018** (strong Feller for singular SPDEs) is structurally the same problem: uniform Malliavin nondegeneracy across regularizations of a renormalized drift. Their lower bound on the Malliavin matrix via control on a "good random time interval" is the prototype to adapt.
- **Mattingly–Romito–Su** (Gaussian structure of singular Burgers) targets the same regularity-of-laws question and may suggest the conditional/skeleton apparatus.
- Bogachev–Röckner-style stationary Fokker–Planck regularity does *not* apply: $\delta_0(u)$ is not in $L^2(\pi)$ as a noise-Hilbert-valued vector field.

**Alternative: Malliavin directly on the ABLM limit.** ABLM-2024's pathwise stability (with logarithmic loss) should extend to first variations in noise directions, producing a Malliavin calculus on the limiting process without going through $n$. If you can prove nondegeneracy of the limiting Malliavin matrix at every deterministic $x\in C_0$, you bypass the uniform-in-$n$ issue. The Butkovsky–Mytnik 2024 weak-uniqueness paper develops Malliavin technology for distributional drift in finite dimensions that may transfer.

**Routes I would not pursue.** Splitting noise as $W^{\le N}+W^{>N}$ to make the drift "low-frequency": local-time-type drifts are unstable under high-frequency perturbations ($\delta_0(u^{\le N}+u^{>N})-\delta_0(u^{\le N})$ is not Cameron–Martin valued), so this decoupling does not produce a usable conditional Gaussian.

Net opinion: the late-noise splitting plus uniform-in-$n$ Malliavin nondegeneracy is the most likely-to-succeed route, with the renormalization of $G_{1/n}'$ along noise-tilted Brownian trajectories being the central hard estimate.

You have correctly identified the sharp obstruction to the fixed-time "late-noise splitting" approach.

**The Sharp Obstruction:**
To use a Cameron–Martin/Girsanov theorem for a dependent shift $u_t = Z_\varepsilon + V_\varepsilon$ (where $V_\varepsilon \in \mathcal{H}$, the Cameron–Martin space of $Z_\varepsilon$), the shift $V_\varepsilon$ must be Malliavin differentiable and its Carleman–Fredholm determinant must satisfy $\det_2(I + \nabla V_\varepsilon) \neq 0$ almost surely (see, e.g., the Mattingly–Romito–Su 2022 framework for singular SPDEs). However, $V_\varepsilon = \int_{t-\varepsilon}^t S_{t-s}\delta_0(u_s)ds$ is constructed from the local time of the solution. Local time is famously not Malliavin differentiable in the classical sense; any attempt to write down $\nabla V_\varepsilon$ yields a highly singular object that destroys the determinant condition. This algebraically blocks the triangular-map argument, meaning you cannot easily establish fixed-time absolute continuity this way. 

To bypass this without relying on an unstated infinite-dimensional Zvonkin homeomorphism, I suggest one of two concrete proof architectures targeting your Alternative Criteria (iii) or (v):

**Architecture A: Asymptotic Coupling for Eventual Equicontinuity (Criterion iii)**
Instead of pursuing absolute continuity, aim directly for eventual equicontinuity (your Lemma 5) using the Hairer–Mattingly (2011) asymptotic coupling framework.
1. Decompose the driving noise into finitely many low modes $W^{(L)}$ and infinite high modes $W^{(H)}$.
2. For two starting points $x$ and $y$, couple $u^x$ and $u^y$ so they are driven by the *exact same* high-mode noise (synchronous coupling). The strong heat dissipation in the high modes naturally contracts their difference.
3. Apply a Girsanov shift *only* to the finite-dimensional low modes $W^{(L)}$ to steer the finite-dimensional projection of $u^x$ toward $u^y$. 

Because you are shifting strictly a finite-dimensional Wiener process, the required Girsanov control has a finite $L^2(0,t)$ norm, entirely sidestepping the infinite-dimensional $\int \|\delta_0(u)\|^2 ds = \infty$ blowup. Combined with the ABLM pathwise stability estimates, this yields a Wasserstein decay bound like $\mathbb{W}_1(P_t(x,\cdot), P_t(y,\cdot)) \le C d(x,y) e^{-\lambda t}$, directly fulfilling Lemma 5 and proving full uniqueness.

**Architecture B: Uniform Capacity-Krylov via Hypercontractivity (Criterion v)**
Leverage the specific analytic fact you already derived: the regularized potentials $B_n(v)$ are uniformly bounded between $0$ and $1$.
