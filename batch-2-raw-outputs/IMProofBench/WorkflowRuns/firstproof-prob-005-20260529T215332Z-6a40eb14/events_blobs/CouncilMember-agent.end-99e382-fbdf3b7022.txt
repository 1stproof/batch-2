I think the Hilbert replacement is a good strategy. For the **regularized equations**, the three ingredients you list look essentially sufficient, with a few write-up tightenings:

- The frozen estimate should be phrased as a tower-conditioning argument: condition first on \(\mathcal F_r\) to bound the \(q\)-factor by \(C\rho_{q-r}(z)^{-1/2}\), then on \(\mathcal F_s\) to bound the \(r\)-factor. This avoids any need for a two-point Gaussian density estimate. Positivity and unit mass of \(b_n=G_{1/n}\) are crucial.
- The Hardy estimate is exactly the right Dirichlet boundary compensation:
  \[
  \rho_\tau^{-1}\lesssim \tau^{-1/2}+d^{-1},\qquad
  \|d^{-1}S_\tau f\|_2\lesssim \|\partial_x S_\tau f\|_2\lesssim \tau^{-1/2}\|f\|_2.
  \]
  In the defect estimate, put absolute values in the derivative bound.
- The sewing/absorption argument is plausible, but I would make two details explicit: reduce the semigroup sewing to ordinary stochastic sewing by fixing a terminal \(T\) and using \(\widetilde A_{s,t}=S_{T-t}A_{s,t}\), or cite a semigroup version precisely; and note that \(Y_n(h)<\infty\) for fixed \(n\) because \(b_n\) is smooth/bounded, so the bootstrap is not circular.

The main hidden gap is the sentence: “the limit follows by lower semicontinuity along the ABLM approximation.” The semigroup convergence
\[
P_t^n\Phi(x)\to P_t\Phi(x)
\]
does not by itself imply convergence of the drift additive functionals \(K^n\), nor convergence of
\[
D^n_{s,t}=K_t^n-S_{t-s}K_s^n
\]
in \(H\). Weak convergence of \(u_t^n\) alone is not enough, since \(K^n=u^n-Sx-V\) involves the joint coupling with the driving noise. So the direct limiting-control proof needs an added lemma/assumption such as:
\[
(K^n_{t_1},\dots,K^n_{t_m})\Rightarrow (K_{t_1},\dots,K_{t_m})
\quad\text{in }H^m
\]
(or joint convergence of \((u^n,V)\), from which this follows). Then Fatou/Portmanteau gives
\[
\E\|D_{s,t}\|_H^2\le \liminf_n \E\|D^n_{s,t}\|_H^2.
\]

Most economical repair, in my view: avoid passing \(K^n\to K\) entirely. For each \(n\), build the dyadic adapted control \(F^n\) from \(K^n\). The uniform Hilbert estimate gives
\[
\sup_n \E\int_0^t\|F_s^n\|_H^2\,ds<\infty.
\]
Localized Girsanov plus the entropy/data-processing bound yields
\[
H\!\left(P_t^n(x,\cdot)\,\middle|\,\nu_t^x\right)
\le \frac12 \E\int_0^t\|F_s^n\|_H^2ds \le C_t,
\]
where \(\nu_t^x=\Law(S_tx+V_t)\). Since \(P_t^n(x,\cdot)\Rightarrow P_t(x,\cdot)\) and relative entropy is lower semicontinuous on the Polish space \(E\),
\[
H(P_t(x,\cdot)\mid \nu_t^x)\le C_t,
\]
hence \(P_t(x,\cdot)\ll \nu_t^x\sim\gamma\sim\pi\). This repairs the limiting Girsanov step without needing a separate convergence theorem for \(K^n\).

If you keep the direct limiting \(F\)-construction, add the joint \(K^n\)-convergence lemma and state that the terminal telescoping identity holds in \(L^2(\Omega;H)\), hence after modification a.s.; then the argument is sufficient.
