# Round 4 response: all-starting-points AC / exceptional-set gap

## Bottom line

I did **not** find an existing theorem that can be cited as-is to close the all-starting-points gap. The current proof remains correct only up to: invariant laws absolutely continuous w.r.t. \(\pi\), or invariant laws known not to charge the BZ exceptional set.

The closest possible closing mechanism is Mattingly--Romito--Su's time-shifted Girsanov theorem. It would close the gap if one proves, for every deterministic start \(x\), that the ABLM drift can be represented by adapted spatial local-time measures \(\mu_s=d_\theta\ell^0_{s,\theta}\) satisfying
\[
  \int_0^T \|S_{T-s}\mu_s\|_{L^2(0,1)}^2\,ds<\infty
  \quad\text{a.s.}
\]
Then \(P_T(x,\cdot)\ll\gamma\sim\pi\) follows for every \(x\), and the manuscript's Lemma `resolvent` proves uniqueness. I did not find this all-starting-points local-time representation/energy estimate in ABLM, BZ, BM, or later material.

## 1. Bogachev--Roeckner / Bogachev--Krylov--Roeckner route

The 1995 Bogachev--Roeckner result treats invariant/Fokker--Planck measures for
\[
  L=\Delta_H+B\cdot\nabla
\]
on finite and infinite dimensional spaces. In the Shigekawa setting it assumes an abstract Wiener space \((E,H,\gamma)\) and drift of the form
\[
  B=-\mathrm{id}_E+v,\qquad v(x)\in H,
\]
with square-integrability hypotheses; then any probability solution of \(L^*\mu=0\) is absolutely continuous w.r.t. \(\gamma\), and \(\sqrt{d\mu/d\gamma}\in D^{2,1}\). See JFA 133 (1995), 168--223, DOI `10.1006/jfan.1995.1123`; also the survey excerpt in web search records states Theorem 4.2 with \(|v|_H\in L^2(\mu)\).

The 1996 Bogachev--Krylov--Roeckner nonconstant-diffusion paper has the same obstruction in a more elliptic form: finite-dimensional \(A\) is Lipschitz uniformly elliptic and \(B\) is \(\mu\)-square-integrable; in the infinite-dimensional case the theorem again concludes \(\mu\ll\gamma\) only under square-integrable vector drift hypotheses. See JFA 138 (1996), 223--242, DOI `10.1006/jfan.1996.0063`.

For our generator
\[
  LF(v)=\frac12\operatorname{Tr}D^2F(v)
       +\left\langle \frac12\Delta_D v+\delta_0(v),DF(v)\right\rangle,
\]
the perturbing drift must be a noise-Hilbert/vector drift, naturally \(L^2(0,1)\)-valued in the SPDE coordinates. But \(\delta_0(u(\cdot))\,d\theta\) is the spatial local-time measure \(d_\theta\ell^0_\theta\), not an \(L^2\) function. The regularizations show the blow-up exactly:
\[
  \int_0^1 G_\varepsilon(u(\theta))^2\,d\theta
  =\int_\mathbb R G_\varepsilon(a)^2\,\ell^a_1\,da
  \sim c\,\varepsilon^{-1/2}\ell^0_1 .
\]
Thus the \(L^2\)-drift/Fisher-information requirement fails. The bounded Gibbs density \(e^{2\Lambda}\) is not enough; these theorems do not handle bounded-variation potentials whose logarithmic derivative is a measure-valued Nemytskii distribution.

Verdict: **does not close the gap**. Exact failed condition: perturbing drift is not Hilbert/noise-direction valued and not square-integrable.

## 2. Mattingly--Romito--Su fixed-time absolute continuity

MRS prove fixed-time absolute continuity for singular stochastic Burgers. Their abstract tool is Theorem 5.8, the time-shifted Girsanov method. In their notation, for
\[
  Lv_t=F_t\,dt+\Xi\,dW_t,\qquad Lz_t=\Xi\,dW_t,\qquad \Xi\approx A^{\beta/2},
\]
it is enough that
\[
  \int_0^T \|A^{-\beta/2}e^{-(T-s)A}F_s\|_{L^2}^2\,ds<\infty .
\tag{MRS 5.12}
\]
Then the law of \(v_T\) is absolutely continuous with respect to the corresponding Gaussian law. Their paper's main Theorem 2.1 proves this for Burgers by a decomposition into finitely many levels when \(\alpha<1\); the number of levels increases as \(\alpha\uparrow1\), and the KPZ/Burgers endpoint \(\alpha=1\) is outside their proof.

For our additive space-time white noise, \(\beta=0\). If \(F_s=\mu_s=d_\theta\ell^0_{s,\theta}\) is a finite spatial local-time measure, then MRS condition is plausible and actually easy at the Fourier level. With Dirichlet eigenvalues \(\lambda_k\sim k^2\) and \(e_k=\sqrt2\sin(k\pi\theta)\),
\[
  \widehat{K}_{T,\varepsilon}(k)
   =\int_{T-\varepsilon}^{T}e^{-\lambda_k(T-s)}
       \langle \mu_s,e_k\rangle\,ds .
\]
If \(M_\varepsilon=\sup_{s\in[T-\varepsilon,T]}\|\mu_s\|_{\mathrm{TV}}\), then
\[
  |\widehat{K}_{T,\varepsilon}(k)|
  \le C M_\varepsilon \min(\varepsilon,\lambda_k^{-1}).
\]
Hence \(K_{T,\varepsilon}\in H^a_0\) for every \(a<3/2\), in particular it is Cameron--Martin relative to Brownian bridge. The MRS Girsanov cost is even more direct:
\[
 \int_0^T \|S_{T-s}\mu_s\|_2^2ds
 \le C\int_0^T (T-s)^{-1/2}\|\mu_s\|_{\mathrm{TV}}^2ds,
\]
finite if the local-time masses are locally bounded, or under suitable moment bounds.

This route would require **one level**, not an infinite MRS decomposition, because the drift after time-shifted heat smoothing is already \(L^2\) for finite spatial measures. The issue is not the MRS theorem; the issue is verifying its input for the actual all-starting-points ABLM process. BZ gives the local-time drift for q.e. initial data. ABLM gives regularized/mild drift terms and uniqueness for every deterministic initial condition, but I found no theorem proving that for every deterministic start the drift disintegrates as finite spatial local-time measures \(\mu_s\) satisfying (MRS 5.12).

Verdict: **promising but not a citable closure**. A possible insertable lemma, once proved, is:

> Lemma. For every \(x\in C_0\) and \(T>0\), the ABLM solution admits an adapted finite-measure drift \(F_s=d_\theta\ell^0_{s,\theta}\) such that its mild drift is \(\int_0^tS_{t-s}F_sds\) and \(\int_0^T\|S_{T-s}F_s\|_2^2ds<\infty\) a.s. Consequently \(P_T(x,\cdot)\ll\gamma\sim\pi\).

Proof sketch after the lemma: apply MRS Theorem 5.8 with \(\beta=0\), \(G=0\), no explosion. The OU law \(Q_T(x,\cdot)\) is equivalent to \(\gamma\), hence \(P_T(x,\cdot)\ll\gamma\sim\pi\). This gives all-starting-points resolvent AC and uniqueness.

## 3. BZ + ABLM route

BZ Proposition 2.5 is exactly as the current draft says. It states a measurable kernel \(\rho_\lambda(x,dy)\) with \(\rho_\lambda(x,\cdot)\ll\nu\) for all \(x\), but the representing identity
\[
  R_\lambda\varphi(x)=\int\varphi(y)\rho_\lambda(x,dy)
\]
is only \(\nu\)-a.e. in \(x\). In the proof they use FOT Theorem 7.2.1 to get a zero-capacity set \(N\), continuity on \(N^c\), prove AC on \(N^c\), and then set \(\rho_\lambda(x,dy)\equiv0\) on \(N\). Thus the all-\(x\) density is not the actual resolvent on \(N\).

BZ then constructs the Markov/local-time solution only outside an exceptional set. Their own introduction says strong Feller is unavailable/out of reach. Proposition 3.1 and the Fukushima decomposition are likewise q.e.

ABLM 2024 gives strong existence/uniqueness for \(b=\kappa\delta_0\) and convergence of smooth approximations; ABLM 2025 gives equivalence of weak and mild regularized notions; BM 2024 gives weak uniqueness in the full \(\alpha>-3/2\) range. None gives strong Feller, resolvent Feller, strict capacity, or entrance from BZ exceptional sets. BM's weak uniqueness is law uniqueness for fixed initial data, not an invariant-measure classification and not a transition density theorem.

Finite-time weak Feller is insufficient: if \(x_n\to x\) and \(P_t(x_n,\cdot)\ll\pi\), weak convergence \(P_t(x_n,\cdot)\Rightarrow P_t(x,\cdot)\) does not preserve absolute continuity. Weak limits of AC measures can be singular. To upgrade q.e. information one needs strong Feller/resolvent Feller, an entrance estimate \(P_t(x,N)=0\), or a capacity-Krylov estimate. I found none in BZ/ABLM/BM.

Verdict: **does not close the gap**.

## 4. Other fixed-time AC / semilinear SPDE literature

Classical semilinear-SPDE absolute-continuity results based on Girsanov or Malliavin calculus require either an \(H\)-valued drift with
\[
  \int_0^T\|Q^{-1/2}F(u_s)\|^2ds<\infty
\]
or smooth/monotone locally Lipschitz reaction terms. They prove path-law AC or densities of finite-dimensional evaluations, not full \(C_0\)-valued transition AC for a measure-valued Nemytskii drift. Ferrario-type path-law results and Duncan--Maslowski--Pasik-Duncan-type Hilbert-space Girsanov results fail at the same point as naive Girsanov: \(\delta_0(u)\notin L^2(0,1)\). Malliavin density papers for reaction-diffusion typically concern \(u(t,x)\) at one point, not the full spatial law.

The 2026 Hilbert-space irregular-drift paper by Anzeletti--Butkovsky--Gerencser--Shaposhnikov treats \(b:H\to H\) Hölder; it explicitly notes Nemytskii distributional drifts are covered by the separate ABLM/BM theory. It does not provide transition absolute continuity or invariant-measure uniqueness here.

## 5. Regularized semigroup uniform estimates

I found no uniform-in-\(n\) estimate of either target form:
\[
\sup_{x\in K}\Bigl\|{dP_t^n(x,\cdot)\over d\pi_n}\Bigr\|_{L^p(\pi_n)}<\infty,
\qquad
\sup_{x\in K}E_x^n\int_\varepsilon^T1_O(u_s^n)ds
\le C\,\mathrm{Cap}(O)^\alpha .
\]
The standard arguments blow up for a precise reason. For \(b_n=G_{1/n}\),
\[
  \|b_n\|_\infty\asymp n^{1/2},\qquad
  \|b_n'\|_\infty\asymp n^{3/2},
\]
and along Brownian-like spatial paths
\[
  \|b_n(u)\|_{L^2(0,1)}^2
  =\int G_{1/n}(u(\theta))^2d\theta
  \sim c\,n^{1/2}\ell^0_1 .
\]
Girsanov/Novikov constants contain exponential moments of \(\int\|b_n(u_s)\|_2^2ds\). Bismut/gradient/log-Harnack estimates for bounded or Lipschitz Hilbert-valued drifts depend on \(\|b_n\|_\infty\), \(\|b_n'\|_\infty\), or \(\|b_n\|_{L^2}\), so they also diverge. The bounded primitive \(0\le B_n\le1\) gives uniform invariant-measure comparison and spectral gap, but I found no pointwise heat-kernel/capacity estimate that uses only this bounded primitive.

## Most plausible remaining proof strategy

The MRS time-shifted Girsanov route is now the most plausible route. It targets all starting points directly and avoids BZ exceptional sets. The new work needed is to prove an all-starting-points local-time/energy lemma for the ABLM Dirichlet solution:
\[
  \mu_s=d_\theta\ell^0_{s,\theta},\qquad
  K_t=\int_0^tS_{t-s}\mu_sds,\qquad
  \int_0^T\|S_{T-s}\mu_s\|_2^2ds<\infty .
\]
If that lemma is established, the manuscript can insert the MRS Theorem 5.8 proof in a short paragraph and the uniqueness gap is closed. Without it, the safest manuscript position is still: invariant reversible \(\pi\), spectral gap, uniqueness among invariant laws \(\ll\pi\), and explicit sufficient criteria excluding BZ exceptional invariant mass.
