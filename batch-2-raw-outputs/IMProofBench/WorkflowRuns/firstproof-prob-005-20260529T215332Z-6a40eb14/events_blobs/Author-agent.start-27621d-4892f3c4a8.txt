## 1. What ABLM gives for two different initial data

Let \(u^x,u^y\) be two ABLM solutions driven by the same noise, with deterministic initial data \(x,y\), and set
\[
\psi=u^x-V,\qquad \phi=u^y-V,\qquad z=u^x-u^y=\psi-\phi .
\]
In ABLM's proof of `L:condun`, the same-initial-data assumption is used only at the final contradiction step where \(z_0=0\).  The local increment estimates themselves survive with different initial data, because
\[
\psi_t-P_{t-s}\psi_s = K^x_t-P_{t-s}K^x_s,\qquad
\phi_t-P_{t-s}\phi_s = K^y_t-P_{t-s}K^y_s;
\]
the deterministic heat-flowed initial conditions cancel separately.

Thus the definitions of \(H^\psi_{s,t},H^\phi_{s,t}\) and \(R_{s,t}\) still lead to the same local estimate: for \(h=t-s\le \ell_T\),
\[
\|z_t-P_hz_s\|_{\mathcal C L_m}
\le C_T D_{s,t} h^{1/2+\delta}
+ C_T D_{s,t}|\log D_{s,t}|\,h,
\]
where \(D_{s,t}:=\sup_{r\in[s,t]}\|z_r\|_{\mathcal C L_m}\).  This is just ABLM (CPAM.tex) Lemma `L:2` plus the reduction in Step 1 of the proof of `L:condun`.  The constant depends on \(T,\|b\|_{\mathcal B^\beta_q}\), and the \(\mathcal V(3/4)\)-seminorms of the two solutions.  For \(b=\delta_0\), the ABLM a priori estimates for measure drift make those seminorms finite with constants depending on \(T,m,\|\delta_0\|\), not on the particular deterministic initial state except through the ambient bounded-initial-condition assumption.  Hence the estimates can be made uniform over compact, indeed bounded, sets of initial conditions for each fixed finite \(T\).

The final uniqueness stopping-time argument can be rerun from a small but nonzero initial gap.  If \(D_{0,T}\) were to grow from \(\|x-y\|\approx 2^{-K}\) to a fixed \(\varepsilon\approx 2^{-k_0}\), the ABLM dyadic hitting-time argument forces
\[
T \gtrsim \sum_{k=k_0}^{K}\frac{1}{k}
\sim \log K-\log k_0 .
\]
Equivalently, for a fixed horizon \(T\), keeping \(D_{0,T}<\varepsilon\) requires roughly
\[
\|x-y\|_\infty \lesssim
\exp\{-c_\varepsilon \exp(C_T T)\},
\]
up to the hidden dependence already present in \(C_T\).  This is a very poor but positive finite-horizon modulus.

Conclusion: under the assumed Dirichlet extension, ABLM should yield finite-time weak Feller continuity \(x_n\to x \Rightarrow \mathcal L(u^{x_n}_t)\Rightarrow \mathcal L(u^x_t)\) in \(C_0([0,1])\) for each fixed \(t>0\).  For bounded Lipschitz observables this follows quantitatively from the coupled estimate plus the same tight spatial regularity used in ABLM; for bounded continuous observables it follows by the usual compactness/Skorohod/`L:condun` stability argument.

## 2. Why this does not give the e-property

The estimate above is finite-horizon only.  The modulus deteriorates with \(T\), and the constants in `L:2` and in the \(\mathcal V(3/4)\) bounds are not uniform as \(T\to\infty\).  More importantly, the logarithmic term is of Osgood type,
\[
D|\log D|\,h,
\]
which permits very slow amplification of small differences over long times.  The proof contains no dissipative term strong enough to produce a time-uniform modulus.

Therefore ABLM does **not** presently verify
\[
|P_t f(x)-P_t f(y)|\le \varepsilon
\quad \text{for } y\text{ near }x,\ t\ge t_0,
\]
even for bounded Lipschitz \(f\).  It gives \(y\)-neighbourhoods depending on a finite terminal time \(T\), not one neighbourhood working for all \(t\ge t_0\).  This is the exact obstruction to using the e-property reduction in the current `answer.tex`.

## 3. Literature checked

### Bounebache--Zambotti

BZ Proposition 2.5 states a resolvent kernel \(\rho_\lambda(x,dy)\ll \nu(dy)\), but the representing identity
\[
R_\lambda\varphi(x)=\int\varphi(y)\rho_\lambda(x,dy)
\]
is only \(\nu\)-a.e. in \(x\).  In the proof they obtain a properly exceptional set \(N\), prove continuity / absolute continuity on \(N^c\), and then set the kernel to zero on \(N\).  Thus BZ gives quasi-everywhere resolvent absolute continuity, not the all-starting-points statement needed for deterministic \(x\in N\).  Their later construction of the weak local-time solution is also outside an exceptional set.

### Komorowski--Peszat--Szarek e-property theorem

KPS, Ann. Probab. 38 (2010), Theorem 1, assumes the e-property plus a lower bound for ergodic averages.  The e-property is exactly equicontinuity of \((P_t f)_{t\ge0}\) at every point for bounded Lipschitz \(f\).  ABLM gives only finite-time continuity, so KPS cannot currently be applied.

### Hairer--Mattingly asymptotic strong Feller

Hairer--Mattingly's ASF criterion implies that distinct ergodic invariant measures have disjoint supports; with a common accessible point this gives uniqueness.  But ASF requires a sequence of times and totally separating pseudo-metrics controlling transition laws asymptotically.  The ABLM estimate controls only Lipschitz observables with a horizon-dependent modulus and does not yield a TV/pseudo-metric asymptotic smoothing estimate.  No verification of ASF is presently available.

### Strong/Lp-Feller Dirichlet-form constructions

Baur--Grothaus--Stilgenbauer construct \(L^p\)-strong Feller processes from regular strongly local Dirichlet forms under local compactness and elliptic regularity assumptions \(D(L_p)\hookrightarrow C(E_1)\).  This does not apply to \(E=C_0([0,1])\), which is infinite-dimensional and not locally compact, and there is no available elliptic regularity theorem for the distorted Gaussian generator with density \(e^{2\Lambda}\).

The Shin--Trutnau / Röckner--Shin--Trutnau strict Fukushima machinery similarly gives strong Feller or strict absolute continuity in finite-dimensional weighted elliptic settings under explicit regularity of the density/domain.  It does not imply entrance from arbitrary capacity-zero sets for a distorted OU process on Brownian-bridge path space with merely bounded measurable density.

### Da Prato--Flandoli--Priola--Röckner and Kunze

The bounded measurable drift Hilbert/Banach-space strong-Feller theory treats \(H\)- or \(E\)-valued bounded measurable drifts.  Here the limiting drift is the distributional Nemytskii object \(\delta_0(u)\), not an \(L^2\)- or \(C_0\)-valued bounded map, and the smooth approximating drifts \(G_{1/n}(u)\) have norms blowing with \(n\).  The hypotheses therefore fail at the point where one would need uniform estimates.

### Butkovsky--Mytnik weak uniqueness 2025

BM 2025 proves weak uniqueness for SHE with \(b\in C^\alpha\), \(\alpha>-3/2\), in class \(\mathcal V_{\rm SHE}(5/8)\); \(\delta_0\) is included by choosing \(\alpha\in(-3/2,-1)\).  This is useful confirmation that the law for each fixed deterministic initial condition is canonical, and it gives convergence of smooth approximations for fixed initial data.  It does not give strong Feller, ASF, e-property, entrance from BZ exceptional sets, or uniqueness of invariant probabilities.

## 4. Current obstruction

The remaining obstruction is real, not just bibliographic.  The \(L^2(\pi)\) spectral gap proves mixing from \(\pi\)-a.e. starting points in the Dirichlet-form sense.  BZ gives resolvent absolute continuity only outside a capacity-zero set.  ABLM gives a strict all-starting-points Markov family, but no estimate showing that it instantly enters \(N^c\), nor any regularity strong enough to turn \(\pi\)-a.e. information into all-starting-points information.

Since capacities for the distorted form are comparable to Brownian-bridge/OU capacities, exceptional sets may contain deterministic points; singletons are expected to be polar in this infinite-dimensional Gaussian setting.  Thus one cannot dismiss the exceptional set topologically.

## 5. Most promising proof routes

1. **Uniform capacity/density estimate for regularized semigroups.**  Prove, for \(t>0\) or \(t\ge t_0\),
\[
\sup_{n}\left\|\frac{dP_t^n(x,\cdot)}{d\pi_n}\right\|_{L^p(\pi_n)}
\le C_{t,x,p},
\]
or the weaker capacity estimate
\[
P_t^n(x,O)\le C_{t,x}\operatorname{Cap}_\gamma(O)^\alpha .
\]
Constants must depend on the bounded primitive \(0\le B_n\le1\), not on \(\|G_{1/n}\|_\infty\).  Passing \(n\to\infty\) would give all-starting-point absolute continuity or at least entrance into \(N^c\).

2. **Strengthen ABLM finite-time continuity to an e-property.**  One would need a new dissipative/Osgood lemma using the Dirichlet heat semigroup decay to offset the \(D|\log D|\) amplification uniformly over arbitrarily long times.  The present ABLM proof does not contain such a mechanism.

3. **Finite-energy invariant laws.**  Show every invariant probability \(\eta\) satisfies
\[
\left|\int \varphi\,d\eta\right|
\le C_\eta \mathcal E_1(\varphi,\varphi)^{1/2}
\]
for bounded nonnegative quasi-continuous \(\varphi\in D(\mathcal E)\).  Then \(\eta\) charges no zero-capacity set, avoids the BZ exceptional set, and the existing spectral-gap argument proves \(\eta=\pi\).  I found no existing theorem giving this from ABLM stationarity; it would require a new stationary Krylov/capacity estimate.

Bottom line: the current manuscript can safely claim finite-time weak Feller continuity as a consequence of adapting ABLM stability, but should not claim eventual equicontinuity or uniqueness of invariant measures unless one of the three new estimates above is supplied.
