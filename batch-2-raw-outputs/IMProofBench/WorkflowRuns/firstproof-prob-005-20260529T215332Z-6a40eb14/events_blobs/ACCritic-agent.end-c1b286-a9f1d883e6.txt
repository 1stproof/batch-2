## 3. Mathematical audit

### Section “Problem statement and interpretation”

The adopted interpretation is mostly faithful to the problem: the author explicitly assumes pointwise weak convergence of the regularized semigroups
\[
P_t^n\Phi(x)\to P_t\Phi(x),\qquad \Phi\in C_b(E).
\]
This is a reasonable formalization of the problem’s approximation assumption.

No fatal issue here, except that the entire proof depends on additional regularity estimates for the regularized equations that are **not part of the problem assumptions** and are not fully justified later.

### Lemma 1: Uniform regularized Hilbert estimate

This is the technical heart of the proof. The claimed estimate
\[
\|K_t^n-S_{t-s}K_s^n\|_{L^2(\Omega;H)}
\le C_T |t-s|^{3/4}
\]
is essential for the later Girsanov/entropy argument. The proof is plausible in outline, but not rigorous enough as written.

Main problems:

1. **Heat-kernel estimates (4)–(6) are asserted, not proved or cited.**  
   The bounds
   \[
   \rho_\tau(y)\asymp \sqrt{\tau}\wedge d(y),
   \]
   the weighted integral estimate (5), and especially
   \[
   \|\rho_\tau^{-1}S_\tau f\|_H\le C\tau^{-1/2}\|f\|_H
   \]
   are essential. These are not completely standard in the exact weighted form used here. A complete proof or precise reference is needed.

2. **The semigroup-valued stochastic sewing lemma is not fully established.**  
   The author invokes a “Hilbert version of stochastic sewing” with a semigroup coboundary. Lê’s theorem indeed concerns stochastic sewing in Banach spaces, but the theorem as standardly stated uses ordinary coboundaries and conditions such as \(A_{s,t}\) being \(\mathcal F_t\)-measurable and estimates on \(\delta A_{s,u,t}\) and \(\mathbb E(\delta A_{s,u,t}\mid\mathcal F_s)\). ([eprints.whiterose.ac.uk](https://eprints.whiterose.ac.uk/id/eprint/196312/1/23-EJP918%20%282%29.pdf)) The semigroup-coboundary variant used in (10), with dyadic midpoint conditions and a sewn limit already identified with \(D^n\), needs a careful proof. The short orthogonality sketch is not enough for an essential lemma.

3. **The identification of \(D^n\) as the sewn integral is underproved.**  
   The sentence
   \[
   D^n_{a,r}=O_n(r-a)\quad\text{in }H
   \]
   is not enough by itself. One needs a quantitative argument showing convergence of the frozen Riemann sums to the actual drift integral in \(L^2(H)\), including the Lipschitz constant of \(b_n\), mesh dependence, and semigroup contractions.

4. **The conditional Gaussian estimates are plausible but not written rigorously.**  
   The estimates
   \[
   \mathbb E_s b_n(Z_r(y))b_n(Z_q(z))
   \le C\rho_{r-s}(y)^{-1/2}\rho_{q-r}(z)^{-1/2}
   \]
   and
   \[
   \left|\mathbb E_u\{b_n(Z_r(y))-b_n(Z_r(y)+h(y))\}\right|
   \le C\rho_{r-u}(y)^{-1}|h(y)|
   \]
   require explicit convolution-with-Gaussian arguments uniform in \(n\). These are probably true for Gaussian mollifiers, but the proof should be supplied because uniformity in \(n\) is crucial.

5. **The bootstrap using \(Y_n(h)\) depends on the sewing lemma gap.**  
   If the stochastic sewing estimate is accepted, the bootstrap looks reasonable. But since the sewing input is not rigorously proved or precisely cited, the proof of Lemma 1 remains incomplete.

**Conclusion on Lemma 1:** this is an essential unproved technical lemma. The later absolute-continuity proof depends on it, so this alone prevents answer-readiness.

### Section “The reversible Gibbs measure”

The proposed Gibbs measure
\[
\pi(dv)=Z^{-1}e^{2\int_0^1\mathbf 1_{\{v(r)>0\}}\,dr}\,\gamma(dv)
\]
has the correct formal sign and factor: for density \(e^{2\Phi}\) relative to the Gaussian invariant measure of the linear equation, the additional drift is \(\nabla\Phi\), giving \(b_n=B_n'\) and formally \(\delta_0\) in the limit.

The total variation convergence \(\pi_n\to\pi\) is also essentially correct: \(0\le B_n\le1\), \(B_n(a)\to\mathbf 1_{\{a>0\}}\) away from \(0\), and a Brownian bridge has zero Lebesgue occupation time at a fixed level.

But there are gaps:

1. **The reversibility of \(P_t^n\) is asserted from a formal generator computation.**  
   The expression
   \[
   L_nF(v)=\frac12\operatorname{Tr}D^2F(v)
   +\left\langle \frac12\Delta_Dv+b_n(v),DF(v)\right\rangle_H
   \]
   is only formal because \(\Delta_Dv\) is not defined for \(\gamma\)-typical \(v\). A rigorous proof should either work in cylinder coordinates or cite a theorem identifying the mild SPDE semigroup with the Dirichlet form semigroup.

2. **The claim “Thus \(P_t^n\) is \(\pi_n\)-invariant and reversible” needs justification.**  
   For smooth bounded Lipschitz drifts this is standard, but it is still an essential infinite-dimensional identification. It should not be left at the level of formal integration by parts.

3. **The spectral-gap passage is mostly correct but compressed.**  
   The use of Holley–Stroock from bounded perturbation of Brownian bridge is reasonable. Passing the gap from \((P_t^n,\pi_n)\) to \((P_t,\pi)\) using pointwise convergence and total variation convergence is also plausible. Still, it relies on the previous unproved identification of \(P_t^n\) as the symmetric semigroup generated by \({\cal E}_n\).

### Section “Fixed-time absolute continuity”

This section is clever and potentially strong, but it has several serious missing details.

1. **The dyadic control algebra is plausible.**  
   The definitions
   \[
   D_k^n=K^n_{r_{k+1}}-S_{\Delta_k}K^n_{r_k},
   \qquad
   F_s^n=\sum_k \Delta_{k+1}^{-1}\mathbf 1_{(r_{k+1},r_{k+2}]}(s)
   S_{s-r_{k+1}}D_k^n
   \]
   are designed correctly: \(D_k^n\) is known by time \(r_{k+1}\), so the control should be adapted, and the telescoping identity is formally correct.

2. **Predictability and state-space regularity are not fully justified.**  
   To apply Hilbert-space Girsanov and then lower semicontinuity on \(E=C_0([0,1])\), the proof must show carefully that the controlled terminal variables \(X_N\) are \(E\)-valued and converge weakly in \(E\). The text says “as \(E\)-valued variables; equivalently one may view the supported laws on \(H\),” but this is not a valid substitute for proof. Entropy on \(H=L^2\) and entropy on \(E=C_0\) are not interchangeable without a precise measurable embedding argument.

3. **The entropy inequality is stated on the wrong level of rigor.**  
   The Girsanov/data-processing argument is standard for predictable \(H\)-valued controls with finite energy, but the proof should specify the path space, the cylindrical Brownian motion framework, and why the localized controls satisfy Novikov. The stopping argument is plausible but not complete as written.

4. **The passage from \(P_t^n\ll\nu_t^x\) to \(P_t\ll\nu_t^x\) depends on the topology issue.**  
   Lower semicontinuity of relative entropy is valid under weak convergence on a fixed Polish space. But the proof must establish that all measures and convergences are being considered on the same Polish space \(E\), not partly on \(H\) and partly on \(E\).

5. **The Feldman–Hájek step is correct in spirit but should be cited or stated precisely.**  
   The covariance eigenvalue comparison is right, and \(S_tx\in H_0^1\) for \(t>0\), so \(\nu_t^x\sim\gamma\) should follow. But for a complete proof on \(C_0([0,1])\), one should explicitly invoke Feldman–Hájek for Gaussian measures on the Banach space \(E\), not only in the Hilbert eigenbasis.

### Section “Conclusion: uniqueness”

Assuming all previous results, the uniqueness argument is essentially correct:

- Fixed-time absolute continuity gives \(\eta\ll\pi\) for every invariant \(\eta\).
- Symmetry gives \(P_t h=h\) in \(L^1(\pi)\).
- The truncation \(h_a=h\wedge a\), Jensen/concavity, and the spectral gap force \(h_a\) to be constant.
- Letting \(a\to\infty\) gives \(h\equiv1\).

This final argument is sound, modulo minor details about extending detailed balance from bounded continuous functions to bounded Borel functions and defining \(P_t h\) for \(h\in L^1(\pi)\). These are fixable.

## 4. Overall verdict

The solution contains a promising strategy and several correct formal ideas. But it is **not yet a complete rigorous proof**. The most serious unresolved issues are:

1. the unproved semigroup-valued stochastic sewing estimate used to prove the uniform \(3/4\)-Hilbert drift bound;

3. the insufficiently justified Girsanov/entropy argument across the two state spaces \(H=L^2\) and \(E=C_0([0,1])\);
4. the reliance on several essential “standard” analytic estimates without proof or precise citation.
