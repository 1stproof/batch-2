\section*{Research notes}

Problem interpreted as the diagonal coinvariant algebra
\[
R_n^{(m)}=\mathbb C[x_i^{(r)}:1\le i\le n,\ 1\le r\le m]/
\langle \mathbb C[x_i^{(r)}]^{\mathfrak S_n}_{+}\rangle ,
\]
where \(\mathfrak S_n\) simultaneously permutes the subscript \(i\).  The
universal Schur coefficients \(c_\lambda(n)\) are stable \(GL_m\)-multiplicities
in the multigraded Hilbert series.

Main reduction: use one bosonic and one fermionic alphabet.  Diagonal
supersymmetry gives the same universal multiplicity data for the superspace
coinvariant ring
\[
SR_n=(\mathbb C[x_1,\ldots,x_n]\otimes \bigwedge(\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_n))/
\langle(\mathbb C[x]\otimes\bigwedge\theta)^{\mathfrak S_n}_{+}\rangle .
\]
At Frobenius-character level the coefficients are \(\mathfrak S_n\)-modules (or
symmetric functions); applying the dimension map to the \(\mathfrak S_n\)-factor
recovers the Hilbert coefficients \(c_\lambda(n)\).  Hence
\[
H_n(q,z):=\operatorname{Hilb}(SR_n;q,z)=\sum_\lambda c_\lambda(n)s_\lambda(q/z).
\]
Here \(s_\lambda(q/z)\) is the super Schur polynomial in one even variable \(q\)
and one odd variable \(z\).  Only partitions in the \((1,1)\)-hook survive, and
\[
s_{(a,1^b)}(q/z)=q^az^b+q^{a-1}z^{b+1}=q^{a-1}z^b(q+z).
\]
Therefore
\[
H_n(q,z)=1+(q+z)\sum_{a\ge1,b\ge0}c_{(a,1^b)}(n)q^{a-1}z^b.
\]

Rhoades--Wilson Hilbert series:
\[
H_n(q,z)=\sum_{k=1}^n z^{n-k}[k]!_q\operatorname{Stir}_q(n,k),
\]
where
\[
\operatorname{Stir}_q(r,k)=\operatorname{Stir}_q(r-1,k-1)+[k]_q\operatorname{Stir}_q(r-1,k),
\]
with initial conditions \(\operatorname{Stir}_q(0,0)=1\),
\(\operatorname{Stir}_q(r,0)=0\) for \(r>0\), and vanishing outside
\(0\le k\le r\).  Set \(F_{r,k}=[k]!_q\operatorname{Stir}_q(r,k)\).  Then
\(F_{r,k}=0\) outside \(1\le k\le r\) (apart from \(F_{0,0}=1\)) and
\[
F_{r,k}=[k]_qF_{r-1,k-1}+[k]_qF_{r-1,k}.
\]
Using \([k+1]_q=1+q[k]_q\),
\[
H_n=H_{n-1}+(q+z)\sum_{k=1}^{n-1}[k]_qF_{n-1,k}z^{n-1-k},
\]
so, since \(H_1=1\),
\[
\frac{H_n-1}{q+z}=\sum_{r=1}^{n-1}\sum_{k=1}^r [k]_qF_{r,k}z^{r-k}.
\]

Combinatorial interpretation: \(F_{r,k}=\sum_{\sigma\in OP_{r,k}}q^{\operatorname{inv}(\sigma)}\), where an inversion of
\(\sigma=(B_1|\cdots|B_k)\) is a pair \(i<j\) with \(i=\min B_m\),
\(j\in B_\ell\), and \(\ell<m\).  Inserting the largest letter into final
position \(i\) creates \(k-i\) inversions.  The singleton insertion case and the
existing-block insertion case give the two recurrence terms.  Restricting to
insertion of \(r+1\) into an existing block gives
\[
[k]_qF_{r,k}=\sum_{\substack{\sigma\in OP_{r+1,k}\\r+1\text{ not singleton}}}
q^{\operatorname{inv}(\sigma)}.
\]
Consequently \(c_{(a,1^b)}(n)\) counts ordered set partitions of \([r]\), with
\(2\le r\le n\), \(r-k=b+1\), largest element \(r\) not singleton, and
\(\operatorname{inv}=a-1\).  Also \(c_\varnothing(n)=1\).

Sanity checks from Python.  Let
\[
G_n(q,z)=\sum_{a\ge1,b\ge0}c_{(a,1^b)}(n)q^{a-1}z^b=(H_n(q,z)-1)/(q+z).
\]
For \(n=4\),
\[
G_4=q^5+3q^4+5q^3+6q^2+5q+3
+(q^3+4q^2+5q+3)z+z^2.
\]
For \(n=5\),
\[
\begin{aligned}
G_5={}&q^9+4q^8+9q^7+15q^6+20q^5+22q^4+20q^3+15q^2+9q+4\\
&+(q^7+5q^6+14q^5+24q^4+29q^3+26q^2+16q+6)z\\
&+(q^4+5q^3+10q^2+9q+4)z^2+z^3.
\end{aligned}
\]
Direct enumeration of ordered set partitions agrees with these polynomials.
