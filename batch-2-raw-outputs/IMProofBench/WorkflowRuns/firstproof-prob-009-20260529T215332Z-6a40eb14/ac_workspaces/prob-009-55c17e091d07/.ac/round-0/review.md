### Literature validation

The interpretation of \(R_n^{(m)}\) as the diagonal coinvariant algebra is consistent with Bergeron’s definition of diagonal coinvariant spaces as quotients by the ideal generated by constant-term-free diagonal invariants. Bergeron also explains that the multigraded Hilbert series is the \(GL_r\)-character and expands in Schur polynomials with multiplicity coefficients independent of the number \(r\) of variable sets. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1105.4358))

The use of diagonal supersymmetry is supported by Lentfer’s theorem: bosonic–fermionic coinvariant rings have universal super-Schur coefficients independent of the number of bosonic and fermionic alphabets, and the symmetric-group corollary gives the corresponding Frobenius expansion. Forgetting the \(\mathfrak S_n\)-module structure gives the Hilbert-series coefficients used in the answer. Lentfer also defines the super Schur functions \(s_\lambda(q/u)\) in the needed positive form. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2505.14885))

The formula
\[
\operatorname{Hilb}(SR_n;q,z)=\sum_{k=1}^n z^{n-k}[k]!_q\operatorname{Stir}_q(n,k)
\]
and the recurrence for \(\operatorname{Stir}_q(n,k)\) are exactly the Rhoades–Wilson Hilbert series formula for the superspace coinvariant ring. ([cambridge.org](https://www.cambridge.org/core/services/aop-cambridge-core/content/view/D7F1C91BE28068FC384AFE85B6AED8B8/S2050508624000143a.pdf/div-class-title-the-hilbert-series-of-the-superspace-coinvariant-ring-div.pdf))

### Paragraph-by-paragraph mathematical audit

1. **Problem statement and interpretation.**  
   The interpretation is explicit and faithful to the standard diagonal coinvariant algebra. The statement that \(c_\lambda(n)\) are stable \(GL_m\)-multiplicities is correct: the multigraded Hilbert series is the diagonal-torus character of the \(GL_m\)-module.

2. **Definition of ordered set partitions and inversions.**  
   The inversion statistic is well-defined. For singleton blocks it agrees with the ordinary inversion number of the corresponding permutation. No issue.

3. **The theorem statement.**  
   The count over ordered set partitions of \([r]\) for \(2\le r\le n\), with \(r\) not singleton and \(r-k=b+1\), is finite and unambiguous. The edge case \(n=1\) is handled correctly: the sum is empty and only \(c_\varnothing(1)=1\) remains.

4. **Use of diagonal supersymmetry.**  
   This is the main external input. It is valid, but the answer would be clearer if it explicitly said that Lentfer’s universal coefficients for the Frobenius/character series become the Hilbert coefficients \(c_\lambda(n)\) after taking dimensions of the \(\mathfrak S_n\)-irreducible factors. This is not a mathematical gap.

5. **Specialization of super Schur functions.**  
   The claim
   \[
   s_{(a,1^b)}(q/z)=q^az^b+q^{a-1}z^{b+1}
   \]
   is correct for one bosonic and one fermionic variable. Non-hooks vanish because the condition for nonzero \(s_\lambda(q/z)\) is \(\lambda_2\le 1\). The notation \(q/z\) could be briefly defined as super-Schur notation, but the formula itself is enough for the proof.

6. **Rhoades–Wilson Hilbert series and algebraic recurrence.**  
   The recurrence manipulation from
   \[
   H_n(q,z)=\sum_{k=1}^n z^{n-k}F_{n,k}
   \]
   to
   \[
   \frac{H_n(q,z)-1}{q+z}
   =
   \sum_{r=1}^{n-1}\sum_{k=1}^r [k]_qF_{r,k}z^{r-k}
   \]
   is correct. Minor improvement: state the standard boundary conditions
   \(\operatorname{Stir}_q(0,0)=1\), \(\operatorname{Stir}_q(r,0)=0\) for \(r>0\), or equivalently \(F_{r,k}=0\) outside \(1\le k\le r\).

7. **Identification of \(F_{r,k}\) with the inversion enumerator.**  
   The insertion proof is correct. Inserting the largest letter into position \(i\) creates exactly \(k-i\) new inversions, giving the factor \([k]_q\). The two cases—largest letter inserted as a singleton or into an existing block—match the two recurrence terms.

8. **Restricted insertion and final coefficient extraction.**  
   Restricting to insertion of \(r+1\) into an existing block correctly gives ordered set partitions of \([r+1]\) with \(r+1\) not singleton. The reindexing \(r+1\mapsto r\) gives the exponent \(z^{r-k-1}\), so the conditions \(r-k=b+1\) and \(\operatorname{inv}=a-1\) are exactly right.

9. **Computational cross-check.**  
   I enumerated ordered set partitions and compared the claimed generating function with \((H_n(q,z)-1)/(q+z)\) computed from the Rhoades–Wilson formula for \(1\le n\le 6\). No discrepancies occurred. For example, for \(n=4\) the ordered-partition side gives
   \[
   3+5q+6q^2+5q^3+3q^4+q^5
   +(3+5q+4q^2+q^3)z+z^2,
   \]
   matching the Hilbert-series extraction.

10. **Working notes.**
