## Section-by-section audit

### Normal support bookkeeping

The new Lemma `normal support bookkeeping` addresses the previous main objection. It now separates the ordinary open support of \(X\) from that of \(K_X(N)\), proves that their compressed normal supports agree, and formulates the annihilator characterization explicitly.

This is supported by DKEP’s normal-bidual construction: DKEP describe \((A^\sharp)^*\) as the normal-support corner \(p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\), and for a subalgebra \(B\subset A\) whose multiplier action is faithful they identify \((B^\sharp)^*\) with the corresponding compressed support corner. The simultaneous \(N,JNJ\)-normal version is exactly the role of Proposition 2.10. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The equality of the supports of \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) is now justified via DKEP Theorem 5.6 and Proposition 3.6: DKEP identify \(K_X^{\infty,1}\) as the relevant \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)-closure and characterize it by annihilation against \(\sharp_J\)-normal functionals vanishing on \(X B(L^2N) X\); since \(X\) is hereditary, vanishing on \(X\) gives vanishing on that product. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

This resolves the previous support-calculus objection.

### Subalgebra coefficients

The proof that the coefficient boundary of

The argument that \(Z\subset U\Rightarrow X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_U}\) via \(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_U}\) is correct.

DKEP Theorem 5.10 gives the equivalence between mixing of correspondences and relative compactness of all bounded-vector coefficient operators; Proposition 5.9 converts rectangular relative compactness into \(T_\xi^*T_\xi\in K_X(N)\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The finite-fusion algebra argument is also now adequate. The coefficient product formula

is consistent with DKEP’s Connes-fusion identity \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) The construction of a contractive approximate unit from functions \(g_\varepsilon(s)=s(s+\varepsilon)^{-1}\), and the polynomial approximation argument, are standard and sufficient.

H_{U_1}(V_1,V_2)\otimes_{M_{V_2}}H_{U_2}(V_2,V_3)

## Referee report on `answer.tex`

### Overall verdict

`answer.tex` **compiles and satisfies the formal LaTeX contract**, but I do **not** regard it as a complete rigorous proof. The main theorem may well be true, and several cited external ingredients are directionally appropriate, but the submitted proof contains essential unproved support-calculus steps, a new relative Bass–Serre proposition proved only sketchily, and several places where DKEP results are invoked in stronger forms than the cited statements visibly provide.

I compiled `answer.tex` with `pdflatex` twice. Thus there is **no LaTeX-contract failure**.

### Literature cross-check

The broad external references are real and relevant. DKEP’s paper defines the normal-bidual framework and Lemma 8.5 indeed characterizes proper proximality relative to a boundary piece by absence of central states on a normal-bidual operator system \(\widetilde S_X(M)\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) DKEP Theorem 5.6 gives an annihilator/mixing characterization, but in the form involving functionals vanishing on \(X B(L^2N,L^2M)Y\), with an additional special condition for \(T\in M(X)\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) DKEP also records the fusion coefficient identity \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\), and Example 5.11 identifies coefficient boundary pieces for correspondences. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743)) CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 gives factoriality/fullness for join-irreducible graph products with at least three vertices under the state-zero-unitary hypothesis, and Proposition 6.3 gives the amenability criterion cited near the end. The problem is not that the cited papers are irrelevant; the problem is that `answer.tex` uses several consequences that are not stated/proved with sufficient precision.

## Paragraph-by-paragraph mathematical audit

### Problem statement and interpretation

The interpretation “irreducible = not a non-trivial join = complement connected” is reasonable and should be acceptable. The answer correctly records this interpretation. Minor issue: the original problem’s wording using subgraphs is slightly imprecise about nonempty/proper/disjoint join factors, but the adopted interpretation is standard.

### Normal-bidual setup

The definition of \(\mathcal A_N=(A_N^{\sharp_J})^*\), \(q_X\), and \(\widetilde S_X(N)\) follows DKEP’s framework. However, the answer immediately uses a very strong support-calculus model in \(A_N^{**}\), and later arguments depend critically on this model. This must be proved carefully, not just asserted.

### Lemma “normal support bookkeeping”

This is one of the main weak points.

1. The proof says DKEP Proposition 2.10 “identifies” \((Y^{\sharp_J})^*\) with  
   \[
   p_N\bar q_Y A_N^{**}\bar q_Yp_N.
   \]
   DKEP Proposition 2.10 visibly states an inclusion/equality of simultaneous normal biduals inside \(M(A)^{**}\), and an inclusion for subalgebras \(B\subset A\); it does not, as quoted, explicitly give the ordinary open-support formula in the form used here. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) This support-corner identification may be true, but `answer.tex` does not prove it.

2. The proof claims that DKEP Theorem 5.6 implies that the annihilators of \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) inside \(A_N^{\sharp_J}\) coincide. Theorem 5.6’s annihilator condition is stated for functionals vanishing on \(X B(L^2N,L^2M)Y\), and the simplified “vanishing on \(X\)” form appears only under additional hypotheses. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) The answer’s argument “DKEP use this in Lemma 8.5” is not a proof of equality of normal supports.

3. The final assertion about \(T\in K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\) being killed by every \(\phi\in A_N^{\sharp_J}\) with \(\phi|_X=0\) is essential later, but it is not rigorously derived. The answer needs to show precisely why Theorem 5.6 applies to such \(T\), and why vanishing on \(X\) is enough.

This lemma is foundational for the boundary-intersection proof. As written, it is not rigorous enough.

### Lemma “subalgebra coefficients”

The conclusion is plausible, but the proof is incomplete.

DKEP Example 5.11 identifies the boundary piece generated by coefficients of a correspondence, and notes that for \(L^2M\) as an \(M\)-\(B\) correspondence one gets the subalgebra boundary piece. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) But `answer.tex` then passes to the fused correspondence
\[
{}_N L^2N_B\otimes_B {}_B L^2N_N
\]
and asserts that its coefficients lie in the hereditary algebra generated by the \(e_B\)-coefficients. The displayed identity \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\) alone gives products with a middle coefficient \(T_\eta^*T_{\eta'}\) on \(L^2B\); it does not by itself prove membership in the hereditary algebra generated by \(e_B\). A linking-algebra or hereditary-corner argument is needed.

The conjugate identification \(\overline{\xi\otimes\eta}\mapsto J\eta\otimes J\xi\) is also stated without checking well-definedness on the Connes fusion quotient.

### Lemma “direct sums and finite fusions”

This lemma contains several nontrivial assertions that are not fully justified.

- The direct-sum argument applies DKEP Theorem 5.10. That theorem gives equivalences between mixing of a correspondence and membership of all bounded-vector operators in relative compact classes. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) But the proof needs to check carefully which side is the \(X\)-boundary and which side is the full boundary \(A_N\). The notation in `answer.tex` is too compressed to verify this unambiguously.

- The infinite direct-sum passage uses coordinate projections and says the closedness argument in DKEP Theorem 5.10 applies. DKEP’s proof uses a specific approximation/cutdown argument for bounded vectors. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) The answer does not prove that the direct-sum bounded vectors satisfy the required uniform boundedness and closure hypotheses in the exact topology used by DKEP.

- The approximate-unit statement for \(X_{M_U}\) depends on the previous “subalgebra coefficients” lemma. Since that lemma is incomplete, this one inherits the gap.

### Lemma “intersection of boundary supports”

The CDHJKN fusion input is correctly aimed: their Theorem 5.4 decomposes graph-product bimodules, and Proposition 5.5 gives fusions over intersections. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743)) The algebraic identity
\[
hkh=T_{\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta}^*
T_{\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta}
\]
is also consistent with DKEP’s coefficient identity.

However, the proof still depends on unresolved support and annihilator assertions:

1. The step from coefficients of direct sums of \(H_C\)’s to \(hkh\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\) depends on the direct-sum lemma, which is not fully proved.

2. The passage
   \[
   \phi(h_\alpha k_\beta h_\alpha)=0
   \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
   \phi(\bar q_W\bar q_U\bar q_W)=0
   \]
   uses strong-* convergence in \(A_M^{**}\) and normality of \(\phi\)’s extension. This can be made valid, but the proof does not spell out the required normal-extension and iterated-limit argument.

3. The compression by \(p_M\) and the conclusion that all normal functionals annihilating \(X_{M_I}\) vanish on \(q_Wq_Uq_W\) relies exactly on Lemma 1’s support identification, which is not established rigorously.

Thus the boundary-support intersection lemma remains an essential unproved component.

### Lemma “from finitely many relative boundaries to compact boundary”

This lemma is conceptually plausible, but again has gaps.

- The compression
  \[
  y\mapsto \omega(q^\perp yq^\perp)
  \]
  is asserted to define a state on \(\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\) after observing \(q^\perp\widetilde S_{X_i}q^\perp\subset \widetilde S(N)\). This inclusion should be proved explicitly at the operator-system level.

- The hypertrace step invokes a u.c.p. map
  \[
  \Theta_0:\mathbb B(L^2N)\to q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0
  \]
  with \(\Theta_0(1)=q_0\) and \(\Theta_0(x)=xq_0\). DKEP Lemma 8.5 does mention natural u.c.p. maps from \(B(L^2M)\) into the normal-bidual compact corner, but `answer.tex` does not construct this map or justify the exact formulas used. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) Since this is the step that converts \(\omega(q_0)=1\) into a Connes hypertrace, it is essential.

### Proposition “relative Bass–Serre paradox”

This is a new substantial proposition, not directly quoted from the literature. It needs a complete proof, but the current proof is closer to a sketch.

- The AFP first-letter projection formulas are plausible, but they are not proved in detail. In amalgamated free products, cancellations into \(B\) must be handled carefully.

- The assertion that the commutators lie in \(X_B\) because they are in the \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodule generated by \(e_B\) depends on the precise DKEP definition of the subalgebra boundary \(X_B\), and this is not expanded.

- The application of DKEP Lemma 6.1 is compressed. DKEP Lemma 6.1 is a closure criterion for the set of algebra elements whose commutators with a vector lie in the mixing part. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) The answer should explicitly connect the checked commutators with the hypotheses of that lemma.

- The orthogonality of \(h^n e_Bh^{-n}\) is asserted from \(E_B(h^n)=0\). This is standard, but the actual calculation
  \[
  (h^m e_Bh^{-m})(h^n e_Bh^{-n})=0\quad(m\ne n)
  \]
  should be included.

Because this proposition is the mechanism proving relative proper proximality for each link, its proof must be fully rigorous. As written, it is not.

### Application to graph products

The graph-product part is mostly plausible.

- The AFP decomposition
  \[
  M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}(M_{L_v}\bar\otimes M_v)
  \]
  is standard, but no citation or proof is supplied.

- The choices of \(w_1,w_2\) are combinatorially reasonable. The vanishing expectations are dismissed as “graph-product reduced words”; this should be expanded, but it is probably fixable.

- The final use of CDHJKN factoriality and nonamenability is correct in spirit. The cited theorem is better referred to as CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 or Main Theorem 0.5, not “Theorem E.” CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 indeed gives a diffuse full factor for join-irreducible graphs with \(|V|\ge3\) and state-zero unitaries. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743)) CDHJKN Proposition 6.3 indeed implies nonamenability under the connected-complement hypothesis with at least three vertices. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

## Final assessment

The submitted proof has a coherent strategy, and several major ingredients are plausible. But it is **not yet a complete rigorous solution**. The central support-calculus lemma, direct-sum coefficient lemma, boundary-intersection lemma, and relative Bass–Serre proposition all contain essential gaps or rely on stronger unstated consequences of DKEP than the text proves. These are not merely cosmetic omissions; they are load-bearing steps in the proof.
