\section*{Problem statement and interpretation}

Irreducible means not a non-trivial graph join; equivalently the complement
graph \(\Gamma^c\) is connected.  All traces are faithful normal traces.  For
\(U\subset V\), \(M_U\) denotes the graph product over the induced subgraph and
\(E_U:M\to M_U\) its canonical trace-preserving expectation.

We use the notation of Ding--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Peterson \cite{DKEP}.
For a finite algebra \(N\), put \(A_N=\mathbb B(L^2N)\) and
\[
        \cA_N=(A_N^{\sharp_J})^*,
\]
the simultaneous \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-normal bidual.  If \(X\) is an
\(N\)-boundary piece, \(K_X(N)\) is DKEP's compact hereditary algebra and
\(q_X\in\cA_N\) is the support of \((K_X(N)^{\sharp_J})^*\).  For
\(X=\mathbb K(L^2N)\) write \(q_0\).  Set
\[
 \widetilde S_X(N)=\{T\in\cA_N:[T,a]\in q_X\cA_Nq_X\hbox{ for all }
 a\in JNJ\}.
\]
For a separable finite factor, DKEP Lemma 8.5 says that proper proximality
relative to \(X\) is equivalent to the absence of an \(N\)-central state on
\(\widetilde S_X(N)\) whose restriction to \(N\) is normal.

For a bounded vector \(\xi\) in an \(M\)-\(N\) correspondence, \(T_\xi\) denotes
DKEP's coefficient operator \(L^2M\to L^2N\); if \(\eta\) is bounded in an
\(N\)-\(P\) correspondence, then
\[
        T_{\xi\otimes_N\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi,\qquad
        T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^* .                                    \tag{1}
\]

\section*{Boundary supports and coefficients}

Let \(A=A_N\) and let \(p\in A^{**}\) be the simultaneous normal support
projection, so that \(\cA_N=pA^{**}p\).  If \(Y\subset A\) is hereditary and
invariant under both multiplier actions of \(N\) and \(JNJ\), let
\(r_Y\in A^{**}\) be its ordinary open support.

\begin{lemma}[support bookkeeping]\label{lem:support}
For such \(Y\),
\[
        r_Yp=pr_Y,\qquad
        (Y^{\sharp_J})^*=(pr_Y)\cA_N(pr_Y).
\]
If \((e_\lambda)\) is a contractive approximate unit of \(Y\), then
\(e_\lambda\to r_Y\) strongly* in \(A^{**}\), and \(pe_\lambda p\to pr_Y\)
ultra-strongly* in \(\cA_N\).  If \(X\) is a boundary piece with
\(N,JNJ\subset M(X)\), then
\[
        pr_X=pr_{K_X(N)}=q_X .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For one normal multiplier action, DKEP Section 2.3 identifies the normal
bidual with \(p_1A^{**}p_1\).  If \(B\subset A\) is invariant and hereditary,
then the universal normal representation of \(B\) is the restriction of the
universal normal representation of \(A\) to the reducing subspace with support
\(r_B\); equivalently, inside \(M(A)^{**}\),
\[
        (B^\sharp)^*=r_Bp_1A^{**}p_1r_B
\]
and \(r_Bp_1=p_1r_B\).  Applying this to the two commuting actions and using
DKEP Proposition 2.10, which realizes the simultaneous normal bidual as the
intersection of the two one-normal biduals and gives the corresponding
non-unital inclusion for invariant subalgebras, gives the displayed corner
formula.  The convergence of approximate units is the usual strong* convergence
to the open support, compressed by \(p\).

DKEP Proposition 2.4 says that simultaneous-normal functionals vanish on a
subspace precisely when they vanish on its simultaneous strong bimodule
closure.  By DKEP Corollary 3.2 and Proposition 3.6,
\[
        \overline X^{\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}}
        =\overline{K_X(N)}^{\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}},
\]
and this common space is the simultaneous strong closure of both \(X\) and
\(K_X(N)\).  Thus \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) have the same annihilator in
\(A_N^{\sharp_J}\).  In the corner description above, the annihilator of
\((pr_Y)\cA_N(pr_Y)\) is exactly the functionals supported on
\(1-pr_Y\); hence equal annihilators give equal compressed supports.  This is
\(pr_X=pr_{K_X(N)}=q_X\).
\end{proof}

For an expected inclusion \(B\subset N\), put \(H={}_NL^2N_B\) and
\[
        \cH_B=H\otimes_B\overline H
             ={}_NL^2N_B\otimes_B{}_BL^2N_N .
\]
Let \(\mathcal E_B=\{T_{\eta\otimes_B\bar\xi}:\xi,\eta\in H_{\rm b}\}\).

\begin{lemma}[subalgebra fusion]\label{lem:subalg}
The boundary piece \(X_B\) is hereditarily generated by the positive
operators \(s^*s\), \(s\in\mathcal E_B\), equivalently by
\(T_\alpha^*T_\alpha\) with \(\alpha\) an elementary bounded tensor in
\(\cH_B\).  Moreover \(\cH_B\cong\overline{\cH_B}\), and every coefficient
operator \(T_\alpha\) of a bounded vector in any direct sum of copies of
\(\cH_B\) is in \(K_{X_B}(N)\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
DKEP Example 5.11 associates to \(H={}_NL^2N_B\) the hereditary algebra
generated by the operators \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\), \(\xi,\eta\in H_{\rm b}\), and
states that this algebra is exactly the subalgebra boundary \(X_B\).  By (1),
\[
        T_{\eta\otimes_B\bar\xi}=T_{\bar\xi}T_\eta=T_\xi^*T_\eta,
\]
so \(X_B\) is hereditarily generated by \(\mathcal E_B\).

Let \(Y\) be the hereditary algebra generated by
\(\{s^*s:s\in\mathcal E_B\}\).  Since \(\mathcal E_B\) is self-adjoint
(\(T_{\eta\otimes\bar\xi}^*=T_{\xi\otimes\bar\eta}\)), \(Y\) contains both
\(s^*s\) and \(ss^*\) for every \(s\in\mathcal E_B\).  If \(r\) is the open
support of \(Y\) in \(A_N^{**}\), then \(Y=rA_N^{**}r\cap A_N\).  From
\(ss^*,s^*s\in Y\) we get \((1-r)s=0=s(1-r)\), whence \(s=rsr\in Y\).
Thus \(\mathcal E_B\subset Y\), and the hereditary algebra generated by
\(\mathcal E_B\), namely \(X_B\), is contained in \(Y\).  The reverse inclusion
is immediate from \(\mathcal E_B\subset X_B\).  Hence \(X_B=Y\).
Notice that no linearity of \(\alpha\mapsto T_\alpha\) is used here; only
elementary tensors enter the generating set.

The map \(\overline{\xi\otimes_B\bar\eta}\mapsto\eta\otimes_B\bar\xi\) extends
to an \(N\)-\(N\) correspondence isomorphism
\(\overline{\cH_B}\cong\cH_B\).  Also, the displayed identity says that the
coefficient operators of a cyclic set of bounded elementary tensors for
\(\cH_B\) lie in \(X_B\).  The cyclic-vector criterion of DKEP Theorem 5.10
therefore makes \(\cH_B\) mixing relative to \(X_B\times X_B\).  Thus every
bounded vector \(\alpha\) in \(\cH_B\) has
\(T_\alpha\in K_{X_B,X_B}(N,N)\).  DKEP Proposition 5.9 gives
\(T_\alpha^*T_\alpha,T_\alpha T_\alpha^*\in K_{X_B}(N)\), and heredity then
gives \(T_\alpha\in K_{X_B}(N)\).  Direct sums are handled by taking the union
of the cyclic sets in the summands.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[finite fusion calculus]\label{lem:fusion}
Let \(X\) be a boundary piece for \(N\), with \(N,JNJ\subset M(X)\).  Finite
Connes fusions, conjugates, and arbitrary direct sums of \(N\)-\(N\)
correspondences which are mixing relative to \(X\times X\) are again mixing
relative to \(X\times X\).  Consequently, if \(K\) is any such finite-fusion
correspondence and \(\xi\in K\) is bounded, then \(T_\xi\in K_X(N)\).

For \(U\subset V\), put \(H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\).  Let \(\cT_U\) be the
linear span of all \(T_\xi\), where \(\xi\) is a bounded vector in a finite
direct sum of non-empty finite fusions of \(H_U\) and \(\overline{H_U}\).
Then \(\cT_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra, and \(X_{M_U}\) has a contractive
approximate unit in the norm closure of \(\{t^*t:t\in\cT_U\}\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For direct sums the cyclic set in DKEP Theorem 5.10(1) is the union of the
cyclic sets in the summands.  For a fusion, elementary tensors from the two
cyclic sets are cyclic and satisfy (1); since \(X\) is hereditary,
\(X\mathbb B(L^2N)X\subset X\).  Conjugates are handled by taking adjoints in
(1).  Theorem 5.10 then gives \(T_\xi\in K_{X,X}(N,N)\) for every bounded
\(\xi\).  By Proposition 5.9, \(T_\xi^*T_\xi\) and \(T_\xi T_\xi^*\) are in
the hereditary algebra \(K_X(N)\); since a hereditary subalgebra contains
\(x\) whenever it contains \(x^*x\) and \(xx^*\), \(T_\xi\in K_X(N)\).

The algebra property of \(\cT_U\) is now just (1):
\(T_\eta T_\xi=T_{\xi\otimes\eta}\), and
\(T_\xi^*=T_{\bar\xi}\); direct sums absorb finite sums.  By Lemma
\ref{lem:subalg}, \(X_{M_U}\) is hereditarily generated by the positive
operators \(T_\xi^*T_\xi\) coming from bounded elementary tensors in \(H_U\),
and the corresponding \(T_\xi\)'s are elements of \(\cT_U\).  For a
finite positive sum \(s\) of such generators,
\(g_\varepsilon(s)=s(s+\varepsilon)^{-1}\) belongs to the hereditary algebra
generated by \(s\), and the net over \(s\) and \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\) is a
contractive approximate unit for \(X_{M_U}\).  Polynomial approximation to
\(g_\varepsilon^{1/2}\), with zero constant term, puts \(g_\varepsilon(s)\) in
the norm closure of \(\{t^*t:t\in\cT_U\}\).
\end{proof}

If \(Z\subset U\), then \(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_U}\), hence \(X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_U}\)
and, by monotonicity of the DKEP compact construction, \(K_{X_{M_Z}}(M)\subset
K_{X_{M_U}}(M)\).

\begin{lemma}[intersection of boundary supports]\label{lem:intersection}
Let \(q_U=q_{X_{M_U}}\).  For \(U,W\subset V\), with \(I=U\cap W\),
\[
        0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_I .                                  \tag{2}
\]
Consequently, if \(I=\cap_{i=1}^m U_i\), then
\[
q_{U_m}q_{U_{m-1}}\cdots q_{U_1}q_{U_2}\cdots q_{U_{m-1}}q_{U_m}
   \in q_I\cA_Mq_I .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The graph-product bimodule decomposition and fusion rule of
\cite[Theorem 5.4 and Proposition 5.5]{CDHJKN} give
\[
        H_A\otimes_M H_B\cong
        \bigoplus_{C\subset A\cap B}H_C^{\oplus m(A,B,C)} .        \tag{3}
\]
Since \(H_A\cong\overline{H_A}\), induction shows that any finite fusion with
labels \(A_1,\ldots,A_n\) decomposes as a direct sum of \(H_C\)'s with
\(C\subset A_1\cap\cdots\cap A_n\).

Let \(h\) and \(k\) first be finite sums of \(t^*t\)'s with
\(t\in\cT_W\) and \(t\in\cT_U\), respectively.  Expanding \(hkh\) gives a
finite sum of monomials in \(T_\xi\)'s and adjoints whose labels include both
\(W\) and \(U\).  By (1) each monomial is \(T_\zeta\) for a bounded vector
\(\zeta\) in a finite fusion of the corresponding \(H_W\)'s and \(H_U\)'s.
By (3) this fusion is a direct sum of \(H_C\)'s with \(C\subset I\).  Lemma
\ref{lem:fusion} and monotonicity give
\[
        hkh\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M).                                   \tag{4}
\]
Norm closedness gives (4) for \(h,k\) in the closures of those positive cones.

Now take limits in the ordinary bidual \(A=\mathbb B(L^2M)^{**}\), where the
canonical copy of \(\mathbb B(L^2M)\) is multiplicative.  Choose contractive
approximate units \(h_\alpha\subset X_{M_W}\) and \(k_\beta\subset X_{M_U}\)
from the positive cones above.  If \(r_R\) denotes the ordinary support of
\(X_{M_R}\), and \(r_{K_I}\) that of \(K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\), then
\(h_\alpha\to r_W\) and \(k_\beta\to r_U\) strongly*.  By (4),
\(h_\alpha k_\beta h_\alpha\in r_{K_I}Ar_{K_I}\).  This corner is strongly*
closed, so first letting \(\beta\) and then \(\alpha\) tend to infinity gives
\(r_Wr_Ur_W\in r_{K_I}Ar_{K_I}\).  Since it is a positive contraction,
\(0\le r_Wr_Ur_W\le r_{K_I}\).  Compressing by the simultaneous normal support
\(p\) and using Lemma \ref{lem:support},
\[
        q_Wq_Uq_W=pr_W\,pr_U\,pr_W=p(r_Wr_Ur_W)p\le pr_{K_I}p=q_I .
\]
The palindromic assertion follows by induction from (2).
\end{proof}

\section*{From finitely many relative boundaries to the compact boundary}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:finite}
Let \(N\) be a separable non-amenable finite factor.  Let
\(X_1,\ldots,X_m\) be boundary pieces, \(q_i=q_{X_i}\), and suppose
\[
        R:=q_mq_{m-1}\cdots q_1q_2\cdots q_{m-1}q_m\in q_0\cA_Nq_0. \tag{5}
\]
If \(N\) is properly proximal relative to each \(X_i\), then \(N\) is properly
proximal.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Assume not.  DKEP Lemma 8.5 gives an \(N\)-central state \(\omega\) on
\(\widetilde S(N)\) with \(\omega|_N=\tau\).  Fix \(i\) and put \(q=q_i\).
Since \(q\) commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\),
\[
 [q^\perp yq^\perp,a]=q^\perp[y,a]q^\perp=0,\qquad a\in JNJ,
\]
for \(y\in\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\); hence \(q^\perp yq^\perp\in\widetilde S(N)\).
If \(\omega(q^\perp)>0\), then
\[
        y\mapsto \omega(q^\perp yq^\perp)/\omega(q^\perp)
\]
is an \(N\)-central state on \(\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\).  On \(N_+\) it is
dominated by \(\omega(q^\perp)^{-1}\tau\), hence is normal; since \(N\) is a
factor and the state is \(N\)-central, its restriction to \(N\) is \(\tau\).
This contradicts relative proper proximality.  Thus \(\omega(q_i^\perp)=0\)
for all \(i\).

Extend \(\omega\) to a state on \(\cA_N\).  In the GNS representation each
\(q_i\) fixes the cyclic vector, so \(\omega(R)=1\).  The element \(R\) is a
positive contraction in \(q_0\cA_Nq_0\); hence \(0\le R\le q_0\) and
\(\omega(q_0)=1\).  The extension is used only for this support argument.  The
corner \(q_0\cA_Nq_0\) is contained in \(\widetilde S(N)\), so on that corner
we still evaluate with the original \(N\)-central state.  DKEP Lemma 8.5
supplies the canonical \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodular u.c.p. map
\[
        \Theta_0:\mathbb B(L^2N)\to q_0\cA_Nq_0
\]
which sends \(a\in N\) to \(aq_0\).  Then
\(\psi(T)=\omega(\Theta_0(T))\) is \(N\)-central by bimodularity and centrality
of \(\omega\) on \(\widetilde S(N)\), and
\(\psi(a)=\omega(aq_0)=\omega(a)=\tau(a)\) because \(q_0\) fixes the GNS cyclic
vector.  Thus \(\psi\) is a Connes hypertrace on \(\mathbb B(L^2N)\),
contradicting non-amenability of the finite factor \(N\).
\end{proof}

\section*{A relative Bass--Serre paradox}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:BS}
Let \(N=P*_{B}Q\) with trace-preserving expectations and \(Q=B\bbarotimes A\).
Assume that \(A\) has a trace-zero unitary \(a\), and that \(P\) has unitaries
\(w_1,w_2\) satisfying
\[
        E_B(w_i)=0,\qquad E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
Then \(N\) is properly proximal relative to \(X_B\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H_P=L^2P\ominus L^2B\) and \(H_Q=L^2Q\ominus L^2B\).  The AFP normal form
is
\[
 L^2N=L^2B\oplus\!\!\bigoplus_{n\ge1,\ i_1\ne\cdots\ne i_n}
 H_{i_1}\otimes_B\cdots\otimes_B H_{i_n},\quad i_j\in\{P,Q\}.
\]
Let \(p\) and \(q\) project onto the summands whose first letter is \(P\),
respectively \(Q\); then \(1=e_B+p+q\).  If \(x\in P\ominus B\), right
multiplication by \(x^*\) preserves the first letter except that \(L^2B\) is
sent to a one-letter \(P\)-word and a one-letter \(P\)-word may acquire an
\(L^2B\)-component.  Checking the three classes \(L^2B\), first-letter \(P\),
and first-letter \(Q\),
\[
 [p,JxJ]=(1-e_B)JxJe_B-e_BJxJ(1-e_B),\qquad [q,JxJ]=0.             \tag{6}
\]
The two terms in (6) are \(N,JNJ\)-translates of \(e_B\), hence lie in \(X_B\).
The same computation with \(P\) and \(Q\) interchanged handles
\(x\in Q\ominus B\); adjoints are included because these centered subspaces are
self-adjoint, and elements of \(B\) commute with \(p,q\).  Since these
subspaces generate \(N\), DKEP Lemma 6.1 gives \(p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)\).

Let \(\varphi\) be an \(N\)-central state on \(S_{X_B}(N)\) whose restriction to
\(N\) is normal.  For \(\eta\in qL^2N\), left multiplication by a centered
element of \(P\) prepends a \(P\)-letter, so \(w_iqL^2N\subset pL^2N\).  Also
\(w_1^*w_2\in P\ominus B\), and such an element sends first-letter \(Q\) words
into first-letter \(P\) words; hence \(q\,w_1^*w_2\,q=0\).  Thus
\[
        w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p,\qquad        a p a^*\le q .
\]
Centrality gives \(2\varphi(q)\le\varphi(p)\le\varphi(q)\), so
\(\varphi(p)=\varphi(q)=0\).

The unitary \(h=w_1a\) is \(B\)-Haar: every non-zero power is an alternating
reduced word in centered \(P\)- and \(Q\)-letters, so \(E_B(h^n)=0\) for
\(n\ne0\).  Hence \(h^ne_Bh^{-n}\) are pairwise orthogonal and have the same
\(\varphi\)-value; thus \(\varphi(e_B)=0\).  This contradicts
\(1=\varphi(e_B+p+q)\).  By DKEP Theorem 6.2, \(N\) is properly proximal
relative to \(X_B\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Application to graph products}

Fix \(v\in V\) and put \(L_v=\Lk_\Gamma(v)\).  The graph-product reduced-word
model gives the standard amalgamated free product decomposition
\[
        M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}
        (M_{L_v}\bbarotimes M_v).                                \tag{7}
\]
Let \(C(v)=\{r\ne v:r\not\sim_\Gamma v\}\).  Since \(\Gamma^c\) is connected,
\(C(v)\ne\emptyset\).  If \(C(v)\) contains distinct \(r,t\), choose trace-zero
unitaries \(u_r,u_t\) and set \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_t\).  If \(C(v)=\{r\}\), then
connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge3\) give \(s\in L_v\) with
\(s\not\sim_\Gamma r\); otherwise \(\{v,r\}\) would be a component of
\(\Gamma^c\).  Set \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_su_r\).  In both cases the graph-product
reduced-word calculus gives
\[
        E_{M_{L_v}}(w_i)=0,\qquad E_{M_{L_v}}(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
A trace-zero unitary in \(M_v\) supplies the unitary \(a\) in (7), so
Proposition \ref{prop:BS} shows that \(M\) is properly proximal relative to
\(X_{M_{L_v}}\) for every \(v\).

Enumerate \(V=\{v_1,\ldots,v_m\}\).  Since no vertex belongs to its own link,
\(\cap_i L_{v_i}=\emptyset\).  Lemma \ref{lem:intersection} puts the associated
palindromic product in \(q_\emptyset\cA_Mq_\emptyset\).  Here
\(M_\emptyset=\C\), \(e_\C\) is the rank-one projection onto
\(\C\widehat1\), and DKEP Example 3.4 gives \(X_{M_\emptyset}=\mathbb
K(L^2M)\).

Finally, CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 implies that a join-irreducible graph product with
\(|V|\ge3\) and trace-zero vertex unitaries is a diffuse full factor.  A
diffuse amenable finite factor is the hyperfinite II\(_1\) factor and is not
full, so \(M\) is non-amenable.  It is separable because the graph is finite
and the vertices are separable.  Lemma \ref{lem:finite}, applied to the finite
family \(\{X_{M_{L_v}}:v\in V\}\), proves that \(M\) is properly proximal.
