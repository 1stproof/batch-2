\section*{Problem statement and interpretation}

I use the standard graph-product meaning of irreducible: the finite graph
$\Gamma=(V,E)$ is not a non-trivial join, equivalently the complement graph
$\Gamma^c$ is connected.  All traces are faithful normal tracial states.  For
$U\subset V$, $M_U$ denotes the graph product over the induced subgraph on $U$,
and $E_U:M\to M_U$ denotes the canonical trace-preserving expectation.

We use the boundary-piece formalism of Ding--Kunnawalkam
Elayavalli--Peterson \cite{DKEP}.  For an inclusion $B\subset N$ with
expectation, $X_B$ is the boundary piece generated by the Jones projection
$e_B:L^2(N)\to L^2(B)$.

\section*{Two boundary facts}

Let $\cA_N=(\mathbb B(L^2N)^\sharp_J)^*$ be the normal bidual used in
\cite[Lemma 8.5]{DKEP}.  If $X$ is a boundary piece, write $q_X\in\cA_N$ for
the support projection of the normal corner $(\mathbb K_X(N)^\sharp_J)^*$.
Then $q_X$ commutes with $N$ and $JNJ$, and
\[
 \widetilde S_X(N)=
 \{T\in\cA_N:[T,a]\in q_X\cA_Nq_X\hbox{ for all }a\in JNJ\}.
\]
For $X=\cK(L^2N)$ write $q_0$ and $\widetilde S(N)$.  When $N$ is a separable
finite factor, \cite[Lemma 8.5]{DKEP} says that proper proximality relative to
$X$ is equivalent to the non-existence of an $N$-central state on
$\widetilde S_X(N)$ whose restriction to $N$ is the trace.

For graph products we need the following intersection property of the Jones
boundary pieces.  Put $q_U=q_{X_{M_U}}$.

\begin{lemma}[intersection of graph-product boundary corners]\label{lem:intersection}
For all $U,W\subset V$,
\[
        q_W\,(q_U\cA_Mq_U)\,q_W\subset q_{U\cap W}\cA_Mq_{U\cap W}.        \tag{1}
\]
Consequently, if $U_1,\ldots,U_m\subset V$ and
\[
R_m=q_{U_m}q_{U_{m-1}}\cdots q_{U_1}q_{U_2}\cdots q_{U_{m-1}}q_{U_m},
\]
then $R_m\in q_I\cA_Mq_I$, where $I=\cap_i U_i$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $\cH_U=L^2(M)\otimes_{M_U}L^2(M)$ and let
$\zeta_U=\widehat 1\otimes_{M_U}\widehat 1$.  DKEP Example 5.11 identifies
$X_{M_U}$ with the coefficient boundary piece of $\cH_U$: the coefficient of
$\zeta_U$ is $e_{M_U}$, and the coefficients of its left and right $M$-translates
generate $X_{M_U}$.

We first record the concrete consequence of DKEP's coefficient formula
$T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi$.  If one forms
\[
        \cL=\cH_W\otimes_M\cH_U\otimes_M\cH_W,                      \tag{2}
\]
then the coefficients of bounded elementary vectors in $\cL$ contain the
products of a $W$-coefficient, a $U$-coefficient, and a $W$-coefficient.  For
example the coefficient of
$\zeta_W\otimes\zeta_U\otimes\zeta_W$ is $e_{M_W}e_{M_U}e_{M_W}$, and translating
the three tensor factors gives the general generators.  Passing to hereditary
closures and then to the normal bidual gives
\[
        q_W(q_U\cA_Mq_U)q_W
        \subset q_{\cL}\cA_Mq_{\cL},                                \tag{3}
\]
where $q_{\cL}$ is the support of the coefficient boundary of $\cL$.

By the fusion rule of Charlesworth--de Santiago--Hayes--Jekel--Kunnawalkam
Elayavalli--Nelson \cite[Theorem 5.4 and Proposition 5.5]{CDHJKN}, the
correspondence $\cL$ is a direct sum of correspondences
$L^2(M)\otimes_{M_Z}L^2(M)$ with $Z\subset U\cap W$.  Since
$Z\subset U\cap W$ implies $X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_{U\cap W}}$, the coefficient
boundary of $\cL$ is contained in $X_{M_{U\cap W}}$.  Thus
$q_{\cL}\le q_{U\cap W}$ and (1) follows from (3).  The final assertion follows
by induction, repeatedly applying (1) to
$R_{k+1}=q_{U_{k+1}}R_kq_{U_{k+1}}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[finite palindromic boundary criterion]\label{lem:finite}
Let $N$ be a separable non-amenable finite factor.  Let
$X_1,\ldots,X_m$ be boundary pieces such that, with $q_i=q_{X_i}$,
\[
        q_mq_{m-1}\cdots q_1q_2\cdots q_{m-1}q_m\in q_0\cA_Nq_0.      \tag{4}
\]
If $N$ is properly proximal relative to each $X_i$, then $N$ is properly
proximal.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Assume that $N$ is not properly proximal.  Then there is an $N$-central state
$\omega$ on $\widetilde S(N)$ with $\omega|_N=\tau$.  Since each $q_i$ commutes
with $JNJ$, all $q_i$ and the product $R$ below belong to $\widetilde S(N)$.
Fix $i$ and put $q=q_i$.  If $T\in\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)$ and $a\in JNJ$, then
\[
 [q^\perp Tq^\perp,a]=q^\perp[T,a]q^\perp=0,
\]
so $q^\perp\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)q^\perp\subset\widetilde S(N)$.  If
$\omega(q^\perp)>0$, then
\[
        T\mapsto \frac{\omega(q^\perp Tq^\perp)}{\omega(q^\perp)}
\]
is an $N$-central state on $\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)$ normal on $N$, contradicting
relative proper proximality.  Thus $\omega(q_i^\perp)=0$ for every $i$.

In the GNS representation of $\omega$, the cyclic vector is fixed by every
$q_i$.  Hence it is fixed by the positive contraction
$R=q_m\cdots q_1q_2\cdots q_m$ from (4), and $\omega(R)=1$.  Since
$0\le R\le q_0$, we get $\omega(q_0)=1$.  The compact corner
$q_0\cA_Nq_0$ is canonically $\cK(L^2N)^{**}=\mathbb B(L^2N)$; restricting
$\omega$ to this corner gives an $N$-central state on $\mathbb B(L^2N)$
extending $\tau$.  This is a Connes hypertrace, so $N$ is amenable, a
contradiction.
\end{proof}

\section*{A relative Bass--Serre paradox}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:BS}
Let
\[
        N=P*_{B}Q,\qquad Q=B\bbarotimes A,
\]
with trace-preserving expectations.  Suppose that $A$ contains a trace-zero
unitary $a$, and that $P$ contains unitaries $w_1,w_2$ such that
\[
        E_B(w_i)=0,\qquad E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
Then $N$ is properly proximal relative to $X_B$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Use the amalgamated free-product decomposition
\[
L^2(N)=L^2(B)\oplus
\bigoplus H_{i_1}^\circ\otimes_B\cdots\otimes_B H_{i_n}^\circ,
\]
where $i_j\in\{P,Q\}$ alternate and
$H_P^\circ=L^2(P)\ominus L^2(B)$, $H_Q^\circ=L^2(Q)\ominus L^2(B)$.  Let
$p$ and $q$ be the projections onto the sums of reduced words whose first
letter lies respectively in $H_P^\circ$ and $H_Q^\circ$.  Then
$1=e_B+p+q$.

We first verify $p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)$.  It is enough, by \cite[Lemma 6.1]{DKEP},
to check right multiplication by elements of $P$ and $Q$.  If
$x\in P\ominus B$ and $R_x=Jx^*J$, then inspection of reduced words gives
\[
        [p,R_x]=R_xe_B-e_BR_x,\qquad [q,R_x]=0.                 \tag{5}
\]
The first formula says that the only possible change of first letter occurs on
the $B$-summand or when a one-letter $P$-word collapses to $B$.  Similarly, for
$x\in Q\ominus B$,
\[
        [q,R_x]=R_xe_B-e_BR_x,\qquad [p,R_x]=0.                 \tag{6}
\]
Elements of $B$ commute with $p$ and $q$.  The operators in (5) and (6) are
right translates of $e_B$ and their adjoints, hence belong to
$\mathbb K^{\infty,1}_{X_B}(N)$.  Thus $p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)$.

Let $\varphi$ be an $N$-central state on $S_{X_B}(N)$ normal on $N$.  Since
$E_B(w_i)=0$, left multiplication by $w_i$ sends the $Q$-initial subspace into
the $P$-initial subspace.  The two images are orthogonal because
$E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0$.  Hence
\[
        w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p .
\]
Also $a\in Q\ominus B$, so $apa^*\le q$.  Centrality gives
$2\varphi(q)\le\varphi(p)\le\varphi(q)$, and therefore
$\varphi(p)=\varphi(q)=0$.

Finally $h=w_1a$ is a $B$-Haar unitary: $h^n$ is a non-trivial reduced
alternating word for $n\ne0$, so the subspaces $h^nL^2(B)$ are pairwise
orthogonal.  Thus the projections $h^ne_Bh^{-n}$ are pairwise orthogonal and
all have the same $\varphi$-value.  Hence $\varphi(e_B)=0$, contradicting
$1=e_B+p+q$.  Therefore no such $\varphi$ exists.
\end{proof}

\section*{Application to graph products}

Fix $v\in V$ and put $L_v=\Lk_\Gamma(v)$.  The graph product has the standard
decomposition
\[
        M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}
        \bigl(M_{L_v}\bbarotimes M_v\bigr).                    \tag{7}
\]
We check Proposition \ref{prop:BS}.  Let
$C(v)=\{r\ne v:r\not\sim_\Gamma v\}$.  Since $\Gamma^c$ is connected, $C(v)$ is
nonempty.  If $C(v)$ contains distinct $r,t$, choose trace-zero unitaries
$u_r\in M_r$, $u_t\in M_t$ and set $w_1=u_r$, $w_2=u_t$.  The expectation onto
$M_{L_v}$ kills $w_1,w_2,w_1^*w_2$ because each reduced word contains a
centered letter from a vertex outside $L_v$.

If $C(v)=\{r\}$, connectedness of $\Gamma^c$ and $|V|\ge3$ give
$s\ne v,r$ with $r\not\sim_\Gamma s$; then $s\in L_v$.  Choose trace-zero
unitaries $u_r\in M_r$, $u_s\in M_s$ and put $w_1=u_r$, $w_2=u_su_r$.  Again
\[
        E_{M_{L_v}}(w_i)=0,\qquad
        E_{M_{L_v}}(w_1^*w_2)=E_{M_{L_v}}(u_r^*u_su_r)=0 .
\]
The given trace-zero unitary in $M_v$ supplies the unitary $a$ in the second
free-product leg of (7).  Hence $M$ is properly proximal relative to
$X_{M_{L_v}}$ for every $v\in V$.

It remains to pass from these relative boundaries to the compact boundary.
Enumerate $V=\{v_1,\ldots,v_m\}$.  Since no vertex belongs to its own link,
$\cap_i L_{v_i}=\varnothing$.  Lemma \ref{lem:intersection} gives
\[
 q_{L_{v_m}}\cdots q_{L_{v_1}}q_{L_{v_2}}\cdots q_{L_{v_m}}
 \in q_\varnothing\cA_Mq_\varnothing .
\]
Here $M_\varnothing=\C$, and $X_{M_\varnothing}$ is the compact boundary piece,
because $e_{\C}$ is rank one and its left and right $M$-translates generate the
finite-rank operators.

The remaining hypotheses of Lemma \ref{lem:finite} hold.  Separability is
immediate from finiteness of $V$ and separability of the vertex algebras.  By
\cite[Main Theorem 0.5]{CDHJKN}, factoriality can fail only through a universal
vertex, or through an exceptional non-edge pair adjacent to every other vertex
with both vertex algebras two-dimensional.  These would give respectively an
isolated vertex or an isolated edge in $\Gamma^c$, impossible because
$\Gamma^c$ is connected and has at least three vertices.  Hence $M$ is a
factor.

Also $M$ is non-amenable.  If it were amenable, then all expected vertex
subalgebras would be amenable; applying \cite[Main Theorem 0.1]{CDHJKN} with
$V_1=V$ and $V_2=\varnothing$ would then force every non-edge of $\Gamma$ would have to be such an exceptional pair adjacent to all
remaining vertices.  In the complement graph this says that every edge is an
isolated component of size two, again impossible for a connected graph with at
least three vertices.  Lemma \ref{lem:finite} therefore applies and proves that
$M$ is properly proximal.
