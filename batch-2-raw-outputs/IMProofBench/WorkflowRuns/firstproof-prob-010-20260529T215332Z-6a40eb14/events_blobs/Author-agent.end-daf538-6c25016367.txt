\section*{Problem statement and interpretation}

Irreducible means not a non-trivial graph join; equivalently the complement
graph \(\Gamma^c\) is connected.  All traces are faithful normal traces.  For
\(U\subset V\), \(M_U\) denotes the graph product over the induced subgraph and
\(E_U:M\to M_U\) its canonical trace-preserving expectation.

We use the notation of Ding--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Peterson \cite{DKEP}.
For a finite algebra \(N\), put \(A_N=\mathbb B(L^2N)\) and
\[
        \cA_N=(A_N^{\sharp_J})^*,
\]
the simultaneous \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-normal bidual.  If \(X\) is an
\(N\)-boundary piece, \(K_X(N)\) is DKEP's associated compact hereditary
algebra and \(q_X\in\cA_N\) is the support of
\((K_X(N)^{\sharp_J})^*\).  For \(X=\mathbb K(L^2N)\) write \(q_0\).  Set
\[
 \widetilde S_X(N)=\{T\in\cA_N:[T,a]\in q_X\cA_Nq_X\hbox{ for all }
 a\in JNJ\}.
\]
For a separable finite factor, DKEP Lemma 8.5 says that proper proximality
relative to \(X\) is equivalent to the absence of an \(N\)-central state on
\(\widetilde S_X(N)\) restricting to the trace on \(N\).

\section*{Normal supports and coefficient boundaries}

Let \(A=A_N\), and let \(p\in A^{**}\) be DKEP's simultaneous normal support
projection for the two commuting multiplier actions of \(N\) and \(JNJ\).  Thus
\(\cA_N=pA^{**}p\).  If \(Y\subset A\) is a hereditary C\(^*\)-subalgebra on
which both actions remain faithful by multipliers, let \(r_Y\in A^{**}\) denote
the ordinary open support of \(Y\).

\begin{lemma}[support bookkeeping]\label{lem:support}
With the notation above,
\[
        r_Yp=pr_Y,\qquad
        (Y^{\sharp_J})^*=(pr_Y)\cA_N(pr_Y).
\]
If \((e_\lambda)\) is a contractive approximate unit of \(Y\), then
\(pe_\lambda p\to pr_Y\) strongly* in \(\cA_N\), and a functional
\(\phi\in A_N^{\sharp_J}\) vanishes on \(Y\) if and only if its normal extension
to \(\cA_N\) vanishes on the corner \((pr_Y)\cA_N(pr_Y)\).

If \(X\) is a boundary piece with \(N,JNJ\subset M(X)\), then \(X\) and
\(K_X(N)\) have the same simultaneous normal support; in particular
\(q_X=pr_X\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For a normal \(M\)-C\(^*\)-algebra \(A\), DKEP identify
\((A^\sharp)^*\) with \(p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\).  Moreover, for an
\(M\)-invariant subalgebra \(B\subset A\), if \(r_B\) is the support of
\(B^{**}\) in \(A^{**}\), then \(r_B\) commutes with \(p_{\rm nor}\) and
\((B^\sharp)^*\simeq r_Bp_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}r_B\)
\cite[Section 2.3]{DKEP}.  DKEP Proposition 2.10 applies the same construction
simultaneously to the two commuting normal actions.  This gives the displayed
corner formula.  The convergence of approximate units is just the usual
strong* convergence \(e_\lambda\to r_Y\) in \(A^{**}\), compressed by \(p\).
The annihilator statement follows from the weak* density of \(pYp\) in this
corner.

For the last assertion, DKEP Proposition 3.6 gives that
\(K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\) is the simultaneous strong \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodule
closure of \(X\), and Proposition 2.4 is exactly the corresponding annihilator
criterion.  Hence the functionals in \(A_N^{\sharp_J}\) vanishing on \(X\) are
precisely those vanishing on \(K_X(N)\) (the reverse inclusion is trivial since
\(X\subset K_X(N)\)).  By the first paragraph, equality of these normal
annihilators is equality of the support projections.
\end{proof}

For an expected inclusion \(B\subset N\), let
\[
        \cH_B={}_NL^2N_B\otimes_B{}_BL^2N_N .
\]
In the tracial case \({}_BL^2N_N\) is the conjugate of \({}_NL^2N_B\).

\begin{lemma}[subalgebra coefficients]\label{lem:coeff}
The coefficient boundary of the \(N\)-\(N\) correspondence \(\cH_B\) is the
subalgebra boundary \(X_B\) of DKEP Example 3.4.  Also
\(\overline{\cH_B}\cong\cH_B\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H={}_NL^2N_B\).  DKEP Example 5.11 says that the coefficient boundary of
the \(N\)-\(B\) correspondence \(H\) is \(X_B\).  For bounded elementary
vectors,
\[
 T_{\xi\otimes\bar\alpha}^{\,*}T_{\eta\otimes\bar\beta}
     =T_\xi^{\,*}(T_{\bar\alpha}^{\,*}T_{\bar\beta})T_\eta.        \tag{2.1}
\]
This is the standard identity \(T_{\xi\otimes\zeta}=T_\zeta T_\xi\) for Connes
fusion.  The middle factor in (2.1) belongs to the natural copy of
\(\mathbb B(L^2B)=e_B\mathbb B(L^2N)e_B\), and DKEP Example 3.4 defines
\(X_B\) as the hereditary algebra generated by all
\(T_\xi^{\,*} S T_\eta\) with \(S\in\mathbb B(L^2B)\).  Thus all coefficients
of \(\cH_B\) lie in \(X_B\).  Conversely, choosing
\(\alpha=\beta=\widehat 1_B\) in (2.1) gives \(T_{\bar\alpha}^{*}T_{\bar\beta}=1\)
on \(L^2B\), hence recovers the coefficient generators of \(H\), whose
hereditary algebra is \(X_B\).  The map
\(\overline{\xi\otimes_B\eta}\mapsto J\eta\otimes_BJ\xi\) is a well-defined
bimodular unitary, by traciality.
\end{proof}

If \(Z\subset U\), then \(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_U}\), so \(X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_U}\).

\begin{lemma}[direct sums and finite fusions]\label{lem:dsupport}
Let \(X\subset A_N\) be a boundary piece with \(N,JNJ\subset M(X)\).  If
\(\cK=\bigoplus_j\cK_j\) is a direct sum of \(N\)-\(N\) correspondences and the
left and right DKEP coefficient boundary pieces of every \(\cK_j\) are
contained in \(X\), then every bounded coefficient of \(\cK\) belongs to
\(K_X(N)\).

For \(U\subset V\), let \(\cF_U\) be the class of non-empty finite Connes fusions
of copies of
\[
        \cH_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M
\]
and its conjugate.  The linear span \(\mathcal C_U\) of coefficients of finite
direct sums of members of \(\cF_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra, and \(X_{M_U}\) has a
contractive approximate unit in the norm-closure of positive coefficients from
such finite direct sums.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
DKEP Theorem 5.10, applied with \(X\) on both sides, says that the boundary
containment hypothesis is equivalent to
\(T_\xi\in K_{X,X}(N,N)\) for every bounded vector \(\xi\in\cK_j\).
DKEP Proposition 5.9 then gives \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\in K_X(N)\).  For a direct sum,
finite-support bounded vectors form a cyclic dense subspace and their operators
are finite rows of the preceding mixing operators; applying Theorem 5.10 once
more to the direct sum gives the same conclusion for all bounded vectors.

The coefficient calculus follows from DKEP's formula
\(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\) and the same formula for conjugates:
adjoints of coefficients are coefficients of conjugate correspondences, and a
finite product of coefficients is a finite sum of coefficients after inserting
the corresponding conjugate factors.  Hence \(\mathcal C_U\) is a
\(*\)-algebra.  By Lemma \ref{lem:coeff}, \(X_{M_U}\) is generated
hereditarily by positive coefficients of \(\cH_U\).  If
\(s=\sum_iT_{\xi_i}^*T_{\xi_i}\) is a finite positive sum of such generators,
then \(g_\varepsilon(s)=s(s+\varepsilon)^{-1}\) is a contractive approximate
unit for the hereditary algebra generated by \(s\).  Polynomial approximation
to \(g_\varepsilon^{1/2}\), with zero constant term, writes
\(g_\varepsilon(s)\) as a norm limit of \(c^*c\)'s with
\(c\in\mathcal C_U\), i.e. of positive coefficients from finite direct sums of
finite fusions.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[intersection of boundary supports]\label{lem:intersection}
Let \(q_U=q_{X_{M_U}}\).  For \(U,W\subset V\), with \(I=U\cap W\),
\[
        0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_I .                                  \tag{3.1}
\]
Consequently, if \(I=\cap_{i=1}^m U_i\), then
\[
q_{U_m}q_{U_{m-1}}\cdots q_{U_1}q_{U_2}\cdots q_{U_{m-1}}q_{U_m}
   \in q_I\cA_Mq_I .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Put \(H_R=L^2M\otimes_{M_R}L^2M\).  Theorem 5.4 and Proposition 5.5 of
\cite{CDHJKN} identify the graph-product bimodules and give the fusion rule
\[
        H_A\otimes_M H_B\cong
        \bigoplus_{C\subset A\cap B}H_C^{\oplus m(A,B,C)} .        \tag{3.2}
\]
Since \(H_A\cong\overline{H_A}\), induction gives: any finite fusion with labels
\(A_1,\ldots,A_n\) decomposes as a direct sum of \(H_C\)'s with
\(C\subset A_1\cap\cdots\cap A_n\).

Let \(h\) be a positive coefficient from finite sums of \(\cF_W\), and \(k\)
one from finite sums of \(\cF_U\).  By Lemma \ref{lem:dsupport} and (3.2),
\(hkh\) is a norm limit of finite sums of coefficients of direct sums of
\(H_C\)'s with \(C\subset I\); since \(X_{M_C}\subset X_{M_I}\), another
application of Lemma \ref{lem:dsupport} gives
\[
        hkh\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M).                                  \tag{3.3}
\]
By norm closedness, (3.3) remains true when \(h\) and \(k\) are in the norm
closures of the corresponding positive coefficient cones.

Now work in the ordinary bidual \(A=\mathbb B(L^2M)^{**}\), where products are
ordinary products.  Let \(r_U\) be the ordinary open support of \(X_{M_U}\), and
let \(r_{K_I}\) be the support of \(K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\).  Lemmas
\ref{lem:support} and \ref{lem:dsupport} provide contractive approximate units
\(h_\alpha\subset X_{M_W}\), \(k_\beta\subset X_{M_U}\) from these norm-closed
positive coefficient cones, with \(h_\alpha\to r_W\) and \(k_\beta\to r_U\)
strongly*.  By (3.3),
\(h_\alpha k_\beta h_\alpha\in r_{K_I}Ar_{K_I}\).  Since this hereditary corner
is strongly* closed, first letting \(\alpha\) and then \(\beta\) tend to
infinity gives
\[
        r_Wr_Ur_W\in r_{K_I}Ar_{K_I}.
\]
The element is a positive contraction, hence \(0\le r_Wr_Ur_W\le r_{K_I}\).
Compressing by the simultaneous normal support \(p\), and using Lemma
\ref{lem:support},
\[
        q_Wq_Uq_W
        =pr_W\,pr_U\,pr_W
        =p(r_Wr_Ur_W)p
        \le pr_{K_I}p=q_I .
\]
The palindromic assertion follows by induction from (3.1).
\end{proof}

\section*{From finitely many relative boundaries to the compact boundary}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:finite}
Let \(N\) be a separable non-amenable finite factor.  Let
\(X_1,\ldots,X_m\) be boundary pieces, \(q_i=q_{X_i}\), and suppose
\[
        R:=q_mq_{m-1}\cdots q_1q_2\cdots q_{m-1}q_m\in q_0\cA_Nq_0. \tag{4.1}
\]
If \(N\) is properly proximal relative to each \(X_i\), then \(N\) is properly
proximal.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Assume not.  DKEP Lemma 8.5 gives an \(N\)-central state \(\omega\) on
\(\widetilde S(N)\) with \(\omega|_N=\tau\).  Fix \(i\) and put \(q=q_i\).
Since \(q\) commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\), for \(y\in\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\)
we have
\[
 [q^\perp yq^\perp,a]=q^\perp[y,a]q^\perp=0,\qquad a\in JNJ,
\]
so \(q^\perp yq^\perp\in\widetilde S(N)\).  If \(\omega(q^\perp)>0\), then
\(y\mapsto\omega(q^\perp yq^\perp)/\omega(q^\perp)\) is an \(N\)-central state
on \(\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\).  Its restriction to \(N\) is a non-zero central
positive normal functional, hence a scalar multiple of \(\tau\), contradicting
relative proper proximality.  Thus \(\omega(q_i^\perp)=0\) for all \(i\).

Extend \(\omega\) to a state on \(\cA_N\).  In its GNS representation each
\(q_i\) fixes the cyclic vector; hence \(\omega(R)=1\).  Since (4.1) gives
\(0\le R\le q_0\), we get \(\omega(q_0)=1\).  DKEP Lemma 8.5 constructs the
canonical \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodular u.c.p. map
\[
        \Theta_0:\mathbb B(L^2N)\to
        (\mathbb K(L^2N)^{\sharp_J})^*=q_0\cA_Nq_0
\]
with \(\Theta_0(1)=q_0\) and \(\Theta_0(x)=xq_0\) for \(x\in N\).  Therefore
\(\psi(T)=\omega(\Theta_0(T))\) is an \(N\)-central state on
\(\mathbb B(L^2N)\) and \(\psi|_N=\tau\), a Connes hypertrace.  This
contradicts non-amenability of the finite factor \(N\).
\end{proof}

\section*{A relative Bass--Serre paradox}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:BS}
Let \(N=P*_{B}Q\) with trace-preserving expectations and \(Q=B\bbarotimes A\).
Assume that \(A\) has a trace-zero unitary \(a\), and that \(P\) has unitaries
\(w_1,w_2\) satisfying
\[
        E_B(w_i)=0,\qquad E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
Then \(N\) is properly proximal relative to \(X_B\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H_P=L^2P\ominus L^2B\) and \(H_Q=L^2Q\ominus L^2B\).  The AFP normal
form is
\[
 L^2N=L^2B\oplus\!\!\bigoplus_{n\ge1,\ i_1\ne\cdots\ne i_n}
 H_{i_1}\otimes_B\cdots\otimes_B H_{i_n},\quad i_j\in\{P,Q\}.
\]
Let \(p\) and \(q\) be the projections onto the reduced-word summands whose
first letter is \(P\), respectively \(Q\); then \(1=e_B+p+q\).  If
\(x\in P\ominus B\), right multiplication by \(x\) preserves the first letter
except on the \(B\)-vacuum and on one-letter \(P\)-words.  Thus
\[
 [p,JxJ]=(1-e_B)JxJe_B-e_BJxJ(1-e_B),\qquad [q,JxJ]=0.             \tag{5.1}
\]
The two terms in (5.1) lie in the \(N,JNJ\)-bimodule generated by \(e_B\), hence
in \(X_B\).  The same argument with \(P,Q\) interchanged applies to
\(x\in Q\ominus B\), and elements of \(B\) commute with \(p,q\).  Since
\(B\), \(P\ominus B\), and \(Q\ominus B\), together with their adjoints,
generate \(N\), DKEP Lemma 6.1 gives \(p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)\).

Let \(\varphi\) be an \(N\)-central state on \(S_{X_B}(N)\) whose restriction to
\(N\) is normal.  Left multiplication by \(w_i\in P\ominus B\) maps \(qL^2N\)
into \(pL^2N\), and the two images are orthogonal because
\(q\,w_1^*w_2\,q=0\), which is exactly the reduced-word consequence of
\(E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0\).  Hence \(w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p\).  Similarly
\(a p a^*\le q\), since \(a\in Q\ominus B\).  Centrality gives
\[
        2\varphi(q)\le\varphi(p)\le\varphi(q),
\]
so \(\varphi(p)=\varphi(q)=0\).

The unitary \(h=w_1a\) is \(B\)-Haar: every non-zero power of \(h\) is an
alternating reduced word in centered \(P\)- and \(Q\)-letters, so
\(E_B(h^n)=0\) for \(n\ne0\).  Thus the projections \(h^ne_Bh^{-n}\) are
pairwise orthogonal and have the same \(\varphi\)-value; that value is \(0\).
Consequently
\[
        1=\varphi(1)=\varphi(e_B+p+q)=0,
\]
a contradiction.  By DKEP Theorem 6.2, \(N\) is properly proximal relative to
\(X_B\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Application to graph products}

Fix \(v\in V\) and put \(L_v=\Lk_\Gamma(v)\).  The graph-product reduced-word
model (equivalently, the universal property; see \cite{CF17,CDHJKN}) gives the
standard amalgamated free product decomposition
\[
        M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}
        (M_{L_v}\bbarotimes M_v).                                \tag{6.1}
\]
Let \(C(v)=\{r\ne v:r\not\sim_\Gamma v\}\).  Since \(\Gamma^c\) is connected,
\(C(v)\ne\emptyset\).  If \(C(v)\) contains distinct \(r,t\), choose trace-zero
unitaries \(u_r,u_t\) and set \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_t\).  If \(C(v)=\{r\}\), then
connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge3\) give \(s\in L_v\) with
\(s\not\sim_\Gamma r\); set \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_su_r\).  In both cases the
graph-product reduced-word calculus gives
\[
        E_{M_{L_v}}(w_i)=0,\qquad E_{M_{L_v}}(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
A trace-zero unitary in \(M_v\) supplies the unitary \(a\) in (6.1).  Proposition
\ref{prop:BS} shows that \(M\) is properly proximal relative to
\(X_{M_{L_v}}\) for every \(v\).

Enumerate \(V=\{v_1,\ldots,v_m\}\).  Since no vertex belongs to its own link,
\(\cap_i L_{v_i}=\emptyset\).  Lemma \ref{lem:intersection} gives the
palindromic product of the \(q_{L_{v_i}}\)'s in
\(q_\emptyset\cA_Mq_\emptyset\).  But \(M_\emptyset=\C\), so
\(X_{M_\emptyset}=\mathbb K(L^2M)\).

Finally, CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 implies that a join-irreducible graph product with
\(|V|\ge3\) and trace-zero vertex unitaries is a diffuse full factor.  A diffuse
amenable finite factor is the hyperfinite II\(_1\) factor and is not full, so
\(M\) is non-amenable.  Separability follows from the finite graph and the
separability of the vertices.  Lemma \ref{lem:finite}, applied to the finite
family \(\{X_{M_{L_v}}:v\in V\}\), proves that \(M\) is properly proximal.
