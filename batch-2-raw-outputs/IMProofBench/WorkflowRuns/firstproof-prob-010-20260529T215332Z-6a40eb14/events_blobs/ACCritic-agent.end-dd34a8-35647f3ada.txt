## Referee report on `answer.tex`

### LaTeX contract check

I compiled `answer.tex` with `pdflatex` twice. It compiles successfully and produces a 6-page PDF, within the 12-page limit. The document class is exactly `\documentclass[12pt]{article}`, `fullpage` is the only layout package, and I found no forbidden font-size or spacing commands. Thus I do **not** reject it on LaTeX-contract grounds.

### Source validation summary

The cited DKEP and CDHJKN papers exist and contain many of the broad tools invoked. For example, DKEP Example 3.4 defines the subalgebra boundary \(X_B\) using operators with arbitrary middle term \(T\in e_B\mathbb B(L^2M)e_B\), and identifies it as a hereditary algebra generated by \(xJyJe_B\)-type operators. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) DKEP Theorem 5.10 gives the equivalence between a cyclic bounded-vector condition and relative mixing/relative compactness of all bounded vectors. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) DKEP Lemma 8.5 gives the bidual characterization of relative proper proximality under an ergodic-center action hypothesis. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) CDHJKN Theorem 5.4 and Proposition 5.5 do give the graph-product bimodule decomposition and fusion rules used in simplified form in the proof. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150)) CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 also supports the final assertion that join-irreducible graph products with at least three vertices and trace-zero vertex unitaries are diffuse full factors. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150))

However, several invocations in `answer.tex` are substantially stronger than what is explicitly established in the cited sources, or require additional bridging lemmas that are not proved. These gaps affect core steps of the proof.

## Main mathematical issues

### 1. The support-bookkeeping lemma is not rigorously established

The lemma claims that for every hereditary \(N,JNJ\)-invariant subalgebra \(Y\subset \mathbb B(L^2N)\),
\[
r_Yp=pr_Y,\qquad (Y^{\sharp_J})^*=(pr_Y)\mathcal A_N(pr_Y),
\]
and then identifies \(pr_X=pr_{K_X(N)}=q_X\).

This is an essential technical bridge, because later the proof multiplies ordinary open supports in \(\mathbb B(L^2M)^{**}\) and then compresses by \(p\). But the proof only sketches this from DKEP Section 2.3 and Proposition 2.10. DKEP Proposition 2.10 states an inclusion/intersection result for simultaneous normal biduals and nonunital inclusion of \((B^{\sharp_J})^*\) into \((A^{\sharp_J})^*\); it does not, as stated, provide the ordinary-open-support commutation and corner formula asserted here. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

This is not a harmless omission. The later inequality
\[
q_Wq_Uq_W=p(r_Wr_Ur_W)p\le pr_{K_I}p=q_I
\]
depends exactly on this support formula. Without a fully proved lemma connecting ordinary bidual supports to DKEP’s simultaneous normal-bidual supports, the central intersection argument is not justified.

### 2. Equality of supports for \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) is underproved

The proof asserts that \(A_N^{\sharp_J}\)-functionals annihilating \(X\) are exactly those annihilating \(K_X(N)\), using DKEP Proposition 2.4 and Proposition 3.6. DKEP Proposition 3.6 does identify
\[
K_X^{\infty,1}(N)=\overline X^{\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}}
=\overline{K_X(N)}^{\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}}
\]
and shows this is a strong bimodule. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) But turning this into equality of the **compressed support projections** again relies on the unproved support-corner formula above. The argument is plausible, but it is not written at a level sufficient for a standalone proof.

### 3. The coefficient identification for \(X_B\) is incomplete

In Lemma “subalgebra fusion”, the proof says that since \(e_B\) is a coefficient of \(\zeta_B\), and DKEP Example 3.4 defines \(X_B\) from translates of \(e_B\mathbb B(L^2N)e_B\), it follows that \(X_B\) is hereditarily generated by positive coefficients of \(\mathcal H_B\).

This is too fast. DKEP Example 3.4 includes arbitrary operators \(T\in e_B\mathbb B(L^2N)e_B\), not just the projection \(e_B\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) To justify the claim, one must prove that the hereditary algebra generated by positive coefficients of
\[
\mathcal H_B={}_NL^2N_B\otimes_B{}_BL^2N_N
\]
indeed contains all operators
\[
x_1Jy_1J\,T\,Jy_2Jx_2,\qquad T\in e_B\mathbb B(L^2N)e_B.
\]
The present proof does not show how the whole middle corner \(e_B\mathbb B(L^2N)e_B\cong \mathbb B(L^2B)\) is generated by these coefficients. This gap propagates directly into Lemma `finite fusion calculus`, where approximate units for \(X_{M_U}\) are built from coefficient generators.

### 4. The use of DKEP Theorem 5.10 for \(\mathcal H_B\) is not fully checked

The proof says that elementary bounded tensors \(\xi\otimes_B\bar\eta\) are cyclic in \(\mathcal H_B\) and satisfy
\[
T_{\xi\otimes\bar\eta}=T_{\bar\eta}T_\xi\in X_B\,\mathbb B(L^2N)\,X_B,
\]
so Theorem 5.10 applies. DKEP Theorem 5.10 requires a precise cyclic set of bounded vectors satisfying the relative boundary condition. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) The answer does not verify the boundedness conventions, the exact endpoint boundary pieces, or the domain/codomain compatibility of the \(T\)-operators. These are technical but essential details in this framework.

### 5. The finite-fusion coefficient algebra \(\mathcal C_U\) is not rigorously proved to be a \(*\)-algebra

The identity
\[
c_1c_2
=
T_{\xi\otimes\bar\eta\otimes\alpha}^{\,*}T_\beta
\tag{2.1}
\]
is central to the claim that \(\mathcal C_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra. But the proof does not spell out the precise convention under which the two vectors in the displayed coefficient live in a common correspondence. The phrase “viewed in the finite direct sum \((K\otimes_N\bar K\otimes_N L)\oplus L\)” is not sufficient: off-diagonal coefficients in direct sums require careful treatment, and depending on the \(T_\xi\)-operator convention, the displayed expression may not be literally meaningful as a coefficient of a single correspondence.

Because the construction of approximate units for \(X_{M_U}\) and the intersection lemma both rely on \(\mathcal C_U\) being a \(*\)-algebra, this is an essential gap.

### 6. The boundary-support intersection lemma depends on the preceding unproved machinery

The proof of
\[
0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_{U\cap W}
\]
is the technical core of the whole solution. CDHJKN does provide the relevant graph-product fusion rule in a form that supports the intended strategy. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150)) But the proof of the intersection lemma also needs:

1. the support-bookkeeping lemma;
2. the coefficient identification \(X_{M_U}=X_{H_U}\) in the strong enough sense used;
3. the \(*\)-algebra property of \(\mathcal C_U\);
4. the assertion that coefficients of arbitrary direct sums of relevant fusions land in \(K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\);
5. the monotonicity \(X_{M_C}\subset X_{M_I}\Rightarrow K_{X_{M_C}}\subset K_{X_{M_I}}\), which is used but not explicitly justified.

Since several of these are not fully proved, the intersection lemma is not established rigorously.

### 7. The finite-boundary-to-compact-boundary lemma is plausible but still has missing details

The argument in Lemma `finite` is mostly coherent. Still, two details need proof:

- When compressing a central state by \(q_i^\perp\), the answer says the restriction to \(N\) is a nonzero central normal positive functional. This is true if one proves domination by a scalar multiple of \(\tau\), but the proof only states it.
- The use of the u.c.p. map
  \[
  \Theta_0:\mathbb B(L^2N)\to q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0
  \]
  is attributed to DKEP Lemma 8.5. DKEP does mention natural \(M\)-bimodular u.c.p. maps into the normal-bidual compact corner. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) But the exact formula \(\Theta_0(x)=xq_0\) and the verification that composing with \(\omega\) gives a Connes hypertrace should be written out more carefully, especially because the canonical map into the normal bidual is not being treated as multiplicative elsewhere.

These are repairable, but they are still gaps in a standalone proof.

### 8. The Bass–Serre proposition is the strongest part, but still somewhat terse

The AFP reduced-word argument for the projections \(p,q\) is plausible. DKEP Lemma 6.1 supports reducing membership in \(S_X(M)\) to checking commutators on a generating set. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) The paradoxical inequalities
\[
w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p,\qquad apa^*\le q
\]
also look correct under the stated centeredness assumptions.

However, the proof only sketches the right-multiplication commutator computation and does not explicitly check all adjoint generators required by DKEP Lemma 6.1. This is less serious than the support/fusion gaps above, but still not fully polished.

### 9. The graph-product application is mostly correct conditional on the earlier lemmas

The graph combinatorics used to choose \(w_1,w_2\) appear correct. The final use of CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 to obtain that \(M\) is a diffuse full factor is supported by the cited theorem. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150)) The conclusion that such a factor is nonamenable is standard.

One minor missing justification is the assertion
\[
M_\emptyset=\mathbb C,\qquad X_{M_\emptyset}=\mathbb K(L^2M).
\]
This is true, but the answer does not prove it. It is not the main obstruction.

---

## Overall verdict

The proof has a plausible global strategy, and many individual components are likely repairable. But in its present form, `answer.tex` does **not** give a complete rigorous solution. The decisive unresolved issues are the unproved normal-bidual support calculus and the insufficiently justified coefficient/fusion machinery used to prove the boundary-support intersection lemma. Since that intersection lemma is essential for upgrading finitely many relative boundary results to ordinary proper proximality, the current document is not answer-ready.
