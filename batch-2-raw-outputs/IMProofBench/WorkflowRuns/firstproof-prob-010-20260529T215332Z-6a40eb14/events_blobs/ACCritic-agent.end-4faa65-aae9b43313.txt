## 3. Previous concerns and whether they were resolved

### Previous fatal concern: support comparison \(q_X=p_Nr_X=p_N\rho_X\)

This is now adequately repaired. The earlier proof relied on a questionable assertion that \(K_X^L\) was exactly the simultaneous strong right module generated by \(A_NX\). The revised proof instead uses:

\[
K_X(N)\subset K_X^{\infty,1}(N)=\overline X^{\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}},
\]
from DKEP Proposition 3.6, and then DKEP Theorem 5.6 to show that every simultaneous-normal functional vanishing on \(X A_N X\) vanishes on \(K_X(N)\). Since \(X\) is hereditary, vanishing on \(X\) implies vanishing on \(X A_N X\). This proves equality of the simultaneous-normal annihilators of \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\), and therefore equality of the corresponding normal-bidual supports.

A tiny expository improvement would be to explicitly say that, for hereditary \(X\subset A_N\), \(X A_N X\) is norm dense in, indeed essentially recovers, \(X\). But the current argument is already sufficient because it only needs \(X A_N X\subset X\) for the annihilation implication, and DKEP Theorem 5.6 is applied to the relevant \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)-closure. This is not a remaining gap.

### Previous concern: nonmultiplicativity of the normal-bidual embedding

Resolved. The revised proof consistently performs multiplicative support computations inside the ordinary bidual \(A_N^{**}\) and only then compresses by \(p_N\). This avoids treating the canonical map \(A_N\to (A_N^{\sharp_J})^*\) as multiplicative, which DKEP explicitly warns is generally false. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

### Previous concern: direct sums and nonlinearity of \(\xi\mapsto T_\xi\)

Resolved. The revised proof now states the \(*\)-algebra property of \(\mathcal T_U\) term-by-term and no longer relies on false linearity of \(\xi\mapsto T_\xi\). The direct-sum step in the boundary-intersection lemma is also now handled through DKEP’s cyclic-vector/mixing criterion rather than by expanding arbitrary direct-sum vectors linearly.

### Graph-product application

The vertex AFP decomposition
\[
M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}(M_{L_v}\bar\otimes M_v)
\]
is standard for graph products.

The combinatorial choice of \(w_1,w_2\) is correct in both cases:

- If \(C(v)\) has two vertices, choose two trace-zero vertex unitaries outside \(L_v\).
- If \(C(v)=\{r\}\), connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge3\) produce \(s\in L_v\) with \(s\not\sim r\), and \(w_1=u_r,\ w_2=u_su_r\) satisfy the required expectation conditions.

The intersection of all vertex links is empty, so the intersection lemma gives the palindromic product in the compact corner \(q_\emptyset\mathcal A_Mq_\emptyset\). Since \(M_\emptyset=\mathbb C\), DKEP Example 3.4 gives \(X_{M_\emptyset}=\mathbb K(L^2M)\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

Finally, CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 gives that \(M\) is a diffuse full factor under the stated hypotheses, and hence \(M\) is nonamenable because the diffuse amenable finite factor is hyperfinite and not full. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))
