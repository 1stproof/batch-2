## Referee report

### Overall verdict

`answer.tex` **compiles and satisfies the formal LaTeX contract**, but I do **not** regard it as a complete rigorous proof. The main theorem may well be true by the intended strategy, but the submitted proof contains essential operator-algebraic support-calculus assertions that are not established by the cited results and are used critically in the boundary-intersection argument. Therefore the answer is **not answer-ready**.

## 2. Literature cross-check

The cited DKEP paper does contain the relevant framework: DKEP Lemma 8.5 gives the normal-bidual central-state criterion for relative proper proximality, with \(\widetilde S_X(M)\) defined using the support of \((K_X(M)^{\sharp_J})^*\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) DKEP Theorem 5.10 gives the cyclic-vector/mixing/relative-compactness equivalences for correspondences, and records the Connes-fusion identity \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) DKEP Example 5.11 identifies the coefficient boundary \(X_H\) and says that for \(H={}_M L^2M_B\) it recovers the subalgebra boundary \(X_B\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The CDHJKN paper is published in PRIMS and does contain the graph-product bimodule decomposition and fusion rules used by the author. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/journals/prims/articles/14299216)) Theorem 5.4 decomposes \(L^2(M)\) as a direct sum of \(M_{V_1}\)-\(M_{V_2}\) bimodules indexed by subsets \(U\subset V_1\cap V_2\), and Proposition 5.5 gives the fusion rule with summands indexed by \(W\subset U_1\cap U_2\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150)) CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 also supports the final assertion that a join-irreducible graph product with \(|V|\ge3\) and state/trace-zero vertex unitaries is a diffuse full factor. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150))

However, the most important support-projection claims in `answer.tex` go beyond what is explicitly stated in the cited propositions.

## 3. Detailed mathematical audit

### Problem statement and interpretation

The interpretation “irreducible = not a nontrivial graph join = connected complement graph” is standard and is explicitly recorded. This is good and addresses the ambiguity in the problem statement.

The assumption that all traces are faithful normal traces is also recorded. This is reasonable, since graph products of tracial von Neumann algebras are normally formulated with faithful normal traces.

No issue here.

---

### DKEP notation and \(\widetilde S_X(N)\)

The definition of \(\mathcal A_N=(A_N^{\sharp_J})^*\), the support \(q_X\), and \(\widetilde S_X(N)\) is compatible with DKEP Lemma 8.5. The statement that for a separable finite factor, “normal restriction to \(N\)” is equivalent to restriction equal to the trace is acceptable: an \(N\)-central normal state on a finite factor is the unique trace.

No major issue here.

---

### Lemma “support bookkeeping”

This is the first serious problem.

The lemma asserts that for a hereditary \(N,JNJ\)-invariant subalgebra \(Y\subset A=\mathbb B(L^2N)\), with ordinary open support \(r_Y\in A^{**}\), one has
\[
r_Yp=pr_Y,\qquad (Y^{\sharp_J})^*=(pr_Y)\mathcal A_N(pr_Y),
\]
where \(\mathcal A_N=pA^{**}p\).

This is an essential structural assertion. It is later used to identify the DKEP support \(q_X\) with the compression \(pr_X\), and then to turn an ordinary-bidual inequality
\[
r_Wr_Ur_W\le r_{K_I}
\]
into the normal-bidual inequality
\[
q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_I.
\]

The cited DKEP Proposition 2.10 does **not**, as stated, give this open-support formula. It records that the simultaneous normal bidual is the intersection of the two one-normal biduals and gives a non-unital inclusion for invariant subalgebras, but it does not identify the unit of \((Y^{\sharp_J})^*\) with the ordinary open support \(r_Y\) compressed by a projection \(p\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The proof sketch says that “the universal normal representation of \(B\) is the restriction of the universal normal representation of \(A\) to the reducing subspace with support \(r_B\).” This is a nontrivial statement for hereditary, non-ideal subalgebras in the simultaneous \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-normal category. It is not proved in the answer and is not directly quoted from DKEP.

This is not a cosmetic gap: the main boundary-intersection lemma depends on precisely this support identification. Without a rigorous proof of this support bookkeeping lemma, the proof of the theorem is incomplete.

---

### Equality of supports for \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\)

The second part of the support lemma claims
\[
pr_X=pr_{K_X(N)}=q_X.
\]

The annihilator argument using DKEP Proposition 2.4 and Proposition 3.6 is plausible: DKEP Proposition 3.6 indeed says that \(K_X^{\infty,1}\) is the \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)-closure of both \(X\) and \(K_X(M)\), and DKEP Proposition 2.4 relates simultaneous strong bimodule closure to annihilation by simultaneous-normal functionals. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

But the conclusion again depends on the previous, insufficiently justified identification of normal-bidual support projections with compressed ordinary open supports. Equal annihilators in \(A^{\sharp_J}\) imply equality of the weak-* closures inside \(\mathcal A_N\), but the answer still needs a rigorous bridge from those weak-* closures to the specific projections \(pr_X\) and \(pr_{K_X}\). That bridge is exactly the missing support formula above.

So this part is not independently fatal, but it inherits the fatal gap in the support bookkeeping.

---

### Lemma “subalgebra fusion”

The first half, identifying \(X_B\) with the hereditary algebra generated by the coefficient operators \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) for \(H={}_NL^2N_B\), is supported by DKEP Example 5.11. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) The positive-generation argument using hereditary supports is reasonable and fixes a common pitfall involving the nonlinearity of \(\xi\mapsto T_\xi\).

The later claim that every coefficient operator \(T_\alpha\) of a bounded vector in any direct sum of copies of \(\mathcal H_B\) lies in \(K_{X_B}(N)\) is plausible via DKEP Theorem 5.10 and Proposition 5.9, but the proof is compressed. In particular, it should explicitly justify that the elementary tensors used are cyclic in the sense of Theorem 5.10 and that their coefficient operators lie in the required \(X_B\mathbb B(L^2N)X_B\) class, not merely in \(X_B\). This is likely repairable, but as written it is a gap in the details.

Not independently fatal, but it requires expansion.

---

### Lemma “finite fusion calculus”

The stability of relative mixing under finite Connes fusion, conjugation, and direct sums is plausible from DKEP Theorem 5.10 and the identity \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) But this is a nontrivial closure statement, and the proof is very terse.

The approximate-unit construction for \(X_{M_U}\) from the norm closure of \(\{t^*t:t\in\mathcal T_U\}\) is reasonable, provided the previous subalgebra-fusion lemma is fully justified.

This section is not the main obstruction, but it rests on earlier insufficiently detailed coefficient-boundary arguments.

---

### Lemma “intersection of boundary supports”

This is the central technical lemma, and it depends critically on the flawed support bookkeeping.

The CDHJKN input is valid: their Theorem 5.4 and Proposition 5.5 do provide the required decomposition and fusion rule for the graph-product bimodules. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150))

The algebraic part of the argument—expanding \(hkh\) into monomials in \(T_\xi\)’s and using the fusion rule to force all labels into \(U\cap W\)—is plausible, but several details are suppressed:

1. One must carefully handle that \(\xi\mapsto T_\xi\) is not linear. The proof uses linear spans of \(T_\xi\)’s, so finite sums are acceptable only because \(K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\) is a linear space, but this should be stated explicitly.

2. One must justify that the CDHJKN Hilbert-bimodule decomposition preserves bounded vectors and the \(T_\xi\)-operator calculus sufficiently to conclude membership in \(K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\).

3. The passage from norm-closure of the positive cones to the ordinary-bidual support inequality depends on the unproved formula \(q_X=pr_X\).

The final compression step
\[
q_Wq_Uq_W=pr_W\,pr_U\,pr_W=p(r_Wr_Ur_W)p\le pr_{K_I}p=q_I
\]
is not justified unless the support bookkeeping lemma is fully proved. Since that lemma is not adequately established, this intersection lemma remains unproved.

This is a fatal gap because the finite-family-to-compact-boundary step needs precisely this intersection result.

---

### Lemma “from finitely many relative boundaries to the compact boundary”

This lemma is mostly sound in outline.

The compression argument using \(q_i^\perp\) is plausible: \(q_i\) commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\) by the DKEP support construction, and the compressed state would contradict relative proper proximality via DKEP Lemma 8.5. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The GNS argument with an extension of \(\omega\) to \(\mathcal A_N\) is also plausible. The author correctly notes that the extension is only used to show \(\omega(R)=1\) and \(\omega(q_0)=1\), while centrality is used only on \(q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0\subset \widetilde S(N)\).

The use of the canonical u.c.p. map
\[
\Theta_0:\mathbb B(L^2N)\to q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0
\]
is consistent with the map appearing in the proof of DKEP Lemma 8.5. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

This lemma is acceptable, modulo the prior unproved fact that the palindromic product really lies in \(q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0\).

---

### Proposition “relative Bass–Serre paradox”

The proof is a reasonable relative analogue of the free-product paradoxical-decomposition argument in DKEP. The projection computations with first-letter \(P\) and first-letter \(Q\) subspaces are plausible.

Some details are compressed but likely repairable:

- The proof should explicitly state that the commutator computations verify both \(x\) and \(x^*\) hypotheses needed to apply DKEP Lemma 6.1.
- It should justify that \(p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)\) and that \(w_iqw_i^*, apa^*\) remain in the operator system where the state is evaluated.
- The pairwise orthogonality of \(h^ne_Bh^{-n}\) follows from \(B\)-Haarity, but the proof should spell out the calculation \(E_B(h^{-n}h^m)=0\) for \(m\ne n\).

I do not see a fatal error here, but the proof is terse.

---

### Application to graph products

The graph-product AFP decomposition
\[
M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}(M_{L_v}\bar\otimes M_v)
\]
is standard, but the answer should cite CF17 or CDHJKN explicitly at this point.

The combinatorial construction of \(w_1,w_2\) is correct: if \(C(v)=\{r\}\), connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge3\) force another complement-neighbor \(s\) of \(r\), and since \(C(v)=\{r\}\), this \(s\) lies in \(L_v\).

The expectation computations onto \(M_{L_v}\) are plausible by reduced-word calculus, though again a few lines of detail would improve rigor.

The final use of CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 is valid: the cited theorem gives diffuse full factoriality for join-irreducible graph products with at least three vertices and trace/state-zero vertex unitaries. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150)) The argument that a diffuse amenable finite factor cannot be full is standard.

Thus the final application is sound assuming all previous technical lemmas, especially the boundary-support intersection lemma, are valid. But because the support-intersection lemma is not rigorously established, the final proof does not close.

## 4. Main blocking issues

The following issues prevent readiness:

1. **Unproved normal-bidual support formula.**  
   The identity
   \[
   (Y^{\sharp_J})^*=(pr_Y)\mathcal A_N(pr_Y)
   \]
   and the commutation \(r_Yp=pr_Y\) are essential and are not supplied by the cited DKEP Proposition 2.10 as stated. This is the main fatal gap.

2. **Boundary-support intersection depends on that unsupported formula.**  
   The proof of
   \[
   0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_{U\cap W}
   \]
   collapses unless the ordinary-bidual support calculation can be rigorously transported into DKEP’s simultaneous normal bidual.

3. **Several coefficient/mixing closure arguments are too compressed.**  
   These are probably repairable, but the current text does not fully verify the hypotheses of DKEP Theorem 5.10 in the subalgebra-fusion and finite-fusion lemmas.

Because these are essential to the proof, `answer.tex` is not a complete rigorous solution.
