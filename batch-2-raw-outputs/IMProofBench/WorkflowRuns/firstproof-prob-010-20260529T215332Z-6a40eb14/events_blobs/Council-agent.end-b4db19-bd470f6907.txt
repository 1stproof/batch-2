Three angles, in order of how I'd try them.

**(1) First, re-read CDHJKN's main theorems before doing further work.** Their Theorem A (which you cite) is the relative amenability classification, but the structural payoff theorems of "On the structure of graph product von Neumann algebras" almost certainly include proper proximality of irreducible graph products as a stated consequence — they typically list it alongside primeness/solidity. There is a real chance Prop BS (or directly your conclusion) is one of their numbered results with a slightly different label. *Uncertain* on the exact theorem number, but I'd bet odds it's in there.

**(2) If you do prove Prop BS yourself, the right framework is DKEP boundary pieces.** Set $\mathbb{K}_B \subset \mathbb{B}(L^2 M)$ = the compact ideal of $\langle M,e_B\rangle$ (norm closure of $\overline{Me_BM}$). With two $B$-orthogonal, $B$-centered unitaries $u,v \in P\ominus B$ (graph-specific!) and one trace-zero $a\in A$:

  - The half-tree projections $P_1, P_2$ (onto AFP words with first letter from $P\ominus B$ vs.\ $B\bar\otimes A\ominus B$) lie in $\widetilde{\mathbb{S}}_{\mathbb{K}_B}(M)$, as you note.
  - Paradoxical inequalities: $u P_2 u^* + v P_2 v^* \leq P_1$ (use $E_B(v^*u)=0$ to get orthogonality of the $u$- and $v$-images in $\mathrm{Range}(P_1)$ via the $B$-Hilbert-module structure on $L^2(P)\ominus L^2(B)$) and $a P_1 a^* \leq P_2$.
  - Haar argument for the vacuum: $h=ua$ has reduced AFP form of length $2n$ alternating, so $E_B(h^n)=0$ for $n\ne 0$, hence $\{h^n L^2(B)\}_n$ pairwise orthogonal, hence any $M$-central $\varphi$ on $\mathbb{S}_{\mathbb{K}_B}(M)$ has $\varphi(e_B)=0$. Combined with $P_1 + P_2 + e_B = 1$, no such $\varphi$ exists.

So $M$ is $\mathbb{K}_B$-properly proximal.

**(3) Upgrade $\mathbb{K}_B$-pp to pp using non-amenability of $P$ rel $B$.** The clean folklore statement:

> *If $M$ is $\mathbb{K}_B$-properly proximal and $M$ is not amenable relative to $B$ inside $M$, then $M$ is properly proximal.*

Sketch: Given an $M$-central $\varphi$ on $\mathbb{S}(M)$ with normal $M$-restriction, look at $\varphi$ on the image of $\mathbb{K}_B\cap\widetilde{\mathbb{S}}(M)$. Vanishing there lets $\varphi$ descend to an $M$-central state on $\mathbb{S}_{\mathbb{K}_B}(M)$, contradicting (2). Non-vanishing gives (via Hahn–Banach + a Day-style averaging) an $M$-central state on $\langle M, e_B\rangle$ with normal restriction to $M$, i.e., $M$ amenable rel $B$ inside $M$. Then $M$ amenable rel $B$ implies $P$ amenable rel $B$ inside $P$ (restrict to $\langle P, e_B\rangle \subset \langle M, e_B\rangle$), contradicting your hypothesis. A version of this upgrade — restated as "transferring central states between $\mathbb{S}_X$ and $\mathbb{S}$ uses relative non-amenability" — is essentially the technical content of the relative-to-full upgrade in **DKE, *Structure of relatively bi-exact group vN algebras*** (your DKE_rel_biexact). *Uncertain* whether the precise statement is there verbatim, but the proof template is.

**Other places to look:** any Caspers-and-collaborators paper on proper proximality for Hecke/Coxeter/graph-product algebras — they have been running exactly this Bass–Serre + boundary-piece induction. Also worth searching recent DKE preprints — AFP permanence is a question they have been circling.

**My recommendation:** Don't try to prove Prop BS in full generality. State your AFP permanence as a graph-flavored lemma — "$P$ properly proximal, $P$ not amenable rel $B$, $P$ contains two $B$-orthogonal $B$-centered unitaries, $A$ has trace-zero unitary ⇒ $M$ properly proximal" — and prove it by (2)+(3) cleanly in 2–3 pages. This avoids citation gambling on whether the abstract AFP statement is in the literature.
