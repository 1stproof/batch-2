\section*{Problem statement and interpretation}

Irreducible means not a non-trivial graph join; equivalently the complement
graph \(\Gamma^c\) is connected.  All traces are faithful normal traces.  For
\(U\subset V\), \(M_U\) denotes the graph product over the induced subgraph and
\(E_U:M\to M_U\) its canonical trace-preserving expectation.

We use the notation of Ding--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Peterson \cite{DKEP}.
For a finite algebra \(N\), put \(A_N=\mathbb B(L^2N)\) and
\[
        \cA_N=(A_N^{\sharp_J})^*,
\]
where \(\sharp_J\) means simultaneous normality for the \(N\)- and
\(JNJ\)-bimodule structures.  If \(X\) is an \(N\)-boundary piece, \(K_X(N)\)
is DKEP's compact hereditary algebra and \(q_X\in\cA_N\) is the support of
\((K_X(N)^{\sharp_J})^*\), viewed by DKEP Proposition 2.10 as a corner of
\(\cA_N\).  For \(X=\mathbb K(L^2N)\) write \(q_0\).  Set
\[
 \widetilde S_X(N)=\{T\in\cA_N:[T,a]\in q_X\cA_Nq_X\hbox{ for all }
 a\in JNJ\}.
\]
For a separable finite factor, DKEP Lemma 8.5 says that proper proximality
relative to \(X\) is equivalent to the absence of an \(N\)-central state on
\(\widetilde S_X(N)\) whose restriction to \(N\) is normal.

For a bounded vector \(\xi\) in an \(M\)-\(N\) correspondence, \(T_\xi\) denotes
DKEP's coefficient operator \(L^2M\to L^2N\); if \(\eta\) is bounded in an
\(N\)-\(P\) correspondence, then
\[
        T_{\xi\otimes_N\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi,\qquad
        T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^* .                                    \tag{1}
\]

\section*{Boundary supports and coefficients}

We will use DKEP's ordinary-bidual realization of the normal bidual.  Thus
\(\cA_N\) is viewed as a von Neumann subalgebra of \(A_N^{**}\); its unit will
be denoted by \(p_N\).  Products of support projections are therefore computed
inside the honest von Neumann algebra \(A_N^{**}\), not by multiplying the
non-multiplicative canonical images of concrete operators.

\begin{lemma}[ordinary support bookkeeping]\label{lem:support}
Let \(Y\subset A_N\) be a hereditary \(C^*\)-subalgebra invariant under the
multiplier actions of \(N\) and \(JNJ\), and assume these actions on \(Y\) are
faithful.  Let \(r_Y\in A_N^{**}\) be its ordinary open support projection.
Then \(r_Y\) commutes with \(p_N\), and the support in \(\cA_N\) of the corner
\((Y^{\sharp_J})^*\) is \(p_Nr_Y\).  Every contractive approximate unit of
\(Y\) converges weak* in \(A_N^{**}\) to \(r_Y\).

If \(X\) is a boundary piece and \(\rho_X\) denotes the ordinary support of
\(K_X(N)\), then, writing \(r_X\) for the ordinary support of \(X\),
\[
        q_X=p_Nr_X=p_N\rho_X .                                    \tag{2}
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(p_1\) and \(p_2\) be DKEP's normal projections in \(A_N^{**}\) for the
\(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodule structures separately.  They belong to the biduals
of the commuting multiplier algebras \(N\) and \(JNJ\), so they commute, and
the unit of the simultaneous normal bidual is \(p_N=p_1p_2\).  For one normal
structure, DKEP identify \((A^\sharp)^*\) with
\(p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\); moreover, for an invariant hereditary
subalgebra \(Y\), the paragraph preceding DKEP Remark 2.8 gives
\(r_Yp_{\rm nor}=p_{\rm nor}r_Y\) and identifies \((Y^\sharp)^*\) with
\(r_Yp_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}r_Y\).  Applying this to the two commuting
normal structures and then using DKEP Proposition 2.10, which realizes the
simultaneous bidual as the intersection of the two one-normal biduals inside
\(A_N^{**}\), gives
\[
   (Y^{\sharp_J})^*=r_Yp_N\,A_N^{**}\,p_Nr_Y\subset \cA_N .
\]
Thus the unit of the included corner \((Y^{\sharp_J})^*\subset\cA_N\) is
\(p_Nr_Y\).  The weak* convergence of an approximate unit to \(r_Y\) is the
standard support property in the ordinary bidual \(A_N^{**}\).

For a boundary piece \(X\), DKEP Proposition 3.6 says that the
\(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)-closure of \(X\) agrees with that of \(K_X(N)\), and
DKEP Proposition 2.4 translates this into equality of the annihilators of
\(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) in \(A_N^{\sharp_J}\).  Hence their supports in the
normal bidual coincide, which is exactly (2).
\end{proof}

For an expected inclusion \(B\subset N\), put \(H={}_NL^2N_B\) and
\[
        \cH_B=H\otimes_B\overline H
             ={}_NL^2N_B\otimes_B{}_BL^2N_N .
\]
Let \(\mathcal E_B=\{T_{\eta\otimes_B\bar\xi}:\xi,\eta\in H_{\rm b}\}\).

\begin{lemma}[subalgebra fusion]\label{lem:subalg}
The boundary piece \(X_B\) is hereditarily generated by the positive
operators \(s^*s\), \(s\in\mathcal E_B\), equivalently by
\(T_\alpha^*T_\alpha\) with \(\alpha\) an elementary bounded tensor in
\(\cH_B\).  Moreover \(\cH_B\cong\overline{\cH_B}\), and every coefficient
operator \(T_\alpha\) of a bounded vector in any direct sum of copies of
\(\cH_B\) is in \(K_{X_B}(N)\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
DKEP Example 5.11 applied to \(H={}_NL^2N_B\) says that \(X_B\) is the
hereditary algebra generated by \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\), \(\xi,\eta\in H_{\rm b}\).
By (1), \(T_{\eta\otimes_B\bar\xi}=T_{\bar\xi}T_\eta=T_\xi^*T_\eta\).

Let \(Y\) be the hereditary algebra generated by
\(\{s^*s:s\in\mathcal E_B\}\).  Since \(\mathcal E_B\) is self-adjoint, \(Y\)
contains both \(s^*s\) and \(ss^*\).  If \(r\) is the ordinary open support of
\(Y\) in \(A_N^{**}\), then \((1-r)s=s(1-r)=0\), so \(s=rsr\in
rA_N^{**}r\cap A_N=Y\).  Thus \(X_B\subset Y\), and the reverse inclusion is
immediate.  This avoids any linearity assumption on \(\alpha\mapsto T_\alpha\).

The map \(\overline{\xi\otimes_B\bar\eta}\mapsto\eta\otimes_B\bar\xi\) gives
\(\overline{\cH_B}\cong\cH_B\).  The elementary bounded tensors are
\(N\)-\(N\) cyclic in \(\cH_B\), and their coefficients lie in \(X_B\subset
\overline{X_BA_NX_B}\).  Hence DKEP Theorem 5.10 makes \(\cH_B\) mixing
relative to \(X_B\times X_B\).  The same cyclic-set argument applies to direct
sums.  DKEP Theorem 5.10 and Proposition 5.9 then give
\(T_\alpha^*T_\alpha,T_\alpha T_\alpha^*\in K_{X_B}(N)\); by heredity
\(T_\alpha\in K_{X_B}(N)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[finite fusion calculus]\label{lem:fusion}
Let \(X\) be a boundary piece for \(N\), with \(N,JNJ\subset M(X)\).  Finite
Connes fusions, conjugates, and arbitrary direct sums of \(N\)-\(N\)
correspondences which are mixing relative to \(X\times X\) are again mixing
relative to \(X\times X\).  Consequently, if \(K\) is any such finite-fusion
correspondence and \(\xi\in K\) is bounded, then \(T_\xi\in K_X(N)\).

For \(U\subset V\), put \(H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\).  Let \(\cT_U\) be the
linear span of all \(T_\xi\), where \(\xi\) is a bounded vector in a finite
direct sum of non-empty finite fusions of \(H_U\) and \(\overline{H_U}\).
Then \(\cT_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra, and \(X_{M_U}\) has a contractive
approximate unit in the norm closure of \(\{t^*t:t\in\cT_U\}\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For direct sums, the union of cyclic sets is cyclic.  For a fusion, elementary
tensors from the two cyclic sets are cyclic and satisfy (1); their
coefficients lie in \(X\mathbb B(L^2N)X\).  Conjugates correspond to adjoints
in (1).  Thus DKEP Theorem 5.10 proves stability and gives
\(T_\xi\in K_{X,X}(N,N)\) for every bounded \(\xi\).  Proposition 5.9 and
heredity give \(T_\xi\in K_X(N)\).

The algebra property of \(\cT_U\) follows from \(T_\eta T_\xi=T_{\xi\otimes
\eta}\) and \(T_\xi^*=T_{\bar\xi}\); finite sums are kept by the explicit
linear span.  By Lemma \ref{lem:subalg}, \(X_{M_U}\) is hereditarily generated
by \(T_\xi^*T_\xi\) for elementary tensors in \(H_U\), and these \(T_\xi\)'s
belong to \(\cT_U\).  For a finite positive sum \(s\) of such generators,
\(g_\varepsilon(s)=s(s+\varepsilon)^{-1}\) belongs to the hereditary algebra it
generates; the net over \(s\) and \(\varepsilon\downarrow0\) is a contractive
approximate unit.  Polynomial approximation of \(g_\varepsilon^{1/2}\), with
zero constant term, puts \(g_\varepsilon(s)\) in the norm closure of
\(\{t^*t:t\in\cT_U\}\).
\end{proof}

If \(Z\subset U\), then \(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_U}\), hence \(X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_U}\)
and \(K_{X_{M_Z}}(M)\subset K_{X_{M_U}}(M)\).

\begin{lemma}[intersection of boundary supports]\label{lem:intersection}
Let \(q_U=q_{X_{M_U}}\).  For \(U,W\subset V\), with \(I=U\cap W\),
\[
        0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_I .
\]
Consequently, if \(I=\cap_{i=1}^m U_i\), then
\[
q_{U_m}q_{U_{m-1}}\cdots q_{U_1}q_{U_2}\cdots q_{U_{m-1}}q_{U_m}
   \in q_I\cA_Mq_I .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The graph-product bimodule decomposition and fusion rule of
\cite[Theorem 5.4 and Proposition 5.5]{CDHJKN} give
\[
        H_A\otimes_M H_B\cong
        \bigoplus_{C\subset A\cap B}H_C^{\oplus m(A,B,C)} .        \tag{3}
\]
Since \(H_A\cong\overline{H_A}\), induction shows that every finite fusion with
labels \(A_1,\ldots,A_n\) decomposes as a direct sum of \(H_C\)'s with
\(C\subset A_1\cap\cdots\cap A_n\).

Let \(\mathcal P_R\) be the norm closure of \(\{t^*t:t\in\cT_R\}\).  We first
claim that
\[
        a\,b\,c\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M)             \tag{4}
\]
whenever \(a,c\in\mathcal P_W\) and \(b\in\mathcal P_U\).  For elementary
squares this follows by expanding, for instance,
\(t_1^*t_1s^*st_2^*t_2\) into a finite linear combination of monomials
\[
T_{\alpha_1}^*T_{\alpha_2}T_{\beta_1}^*T_{\beta_2}
T_{\alpha_3}^*T_{\alpha_4}.
\]
Here the \(\alpha_j\)'s come from finite fusions with label \(W\), and the
\(\beta_j\)'s from label \(U\).  Replacing adjoints by conjugates and using
(1), each monomial is \(T_\zeta\) for a bounded vector \(\zeta\) in a finite
fusion whose labels include both \(W\) and \(U\).  By (3), \(\zeta\) belongs to
a direct sum of \(H_C\)'s with \(C\subset I\).  Lemma \ref{lem:fusion} and
monotonicity give \(T_\zeta\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\).  Since \(K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\) is a
linear norm closed space, (4) follows for the norm closures.

Let \(A=\mathbb B(L^2M)\), let \(p=p_M\) be the unit of \(\cA_M\) in the
ordinary bidual model, let \(r_R\) be the ordinary support of \(X_{M_R}\), and
let \(\rho_R\) be the ordinary support of \(K_{X_{M_R}}(M)\).  Lemma
\ref{lem:support} gives
\[
        q_R=pr_R=p\rho_R,\qquad [p,r_R]=[p,\rho_R]=0 .              \tag{5}
\]
Choose contractive approximate units
\(h_\alpha\subset\mathcal P_W\) for \(X_{M_W}\) and
\(k_\beta\subset\mathcal P_U\) for \(X_{M_U}\), as in Lemma
\ref{lem:fusion}.  In the ordinary bidual \(A^{**}\) we have
\(h_\alpha\to r_W\) and \(k_\beta\to r_U\) weak*.  By (4),
\(h_\alpha k_\beta h_\gamma\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\) for all
\(\alpha,\beta,\gamma\); hence these products all lie in the weak* closed
corner \(\rho_I A^{**}\rho_I\).  Since the inclusion \(A\hookrightarrow A^{**}\)
is multiplicative and multiplication in \(A^{**}\) is separately weak*
continuous, successive limits give
\[
        r_W r_U r_W\in \rho_I A^{**}\rho_I .
\]
This is a positive contraction, so \(r_Wr_Ur_W\le \rho_I\).  Using (5), and
only now compressing to the normal bidual, we get
\[
 q_Wq_Uq_W
 =(pr_Wp)(pr_Up)(pr_Wp)
 =p\,r_Wr_Ur_W\,p
 \le p\rho_Ip=q_I .
\]
The left side is positive, proving the first assertion.

The palindromic assertion follows by induction.  If
\(R_{m-1}\le q_{\cap_{i<m}U_i}\), then
\[
q_{U_m}R_{m-1}q_{U_m}
 \le q_{U_m}q_{\cap_{i<m}U_i}q_{U_m}
 \le q_{\cap_iU_i},
\]
where the last inequality is the two-set case just proved.
\end{proof}

\section*{From finitely many relative boundaries to the compact boundary}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:finite}
Let \(N\) be a separable non-amenable finite factor.  Let
\(X_1,\ldots,X_m\) be boundary pieces, \(q_i=q_{X_i}\), and suppose
\[
        R:=q_mq_{m-1}\cdots q_1q_2\cdots q_{m-1}q_m\in q_0\cA_Nq_0. \tag{6}
\]
If \(N\) is properly proximal relative to each \(X_i\), then \(N\) is properly
proximal.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Assume not.  DKEP Lemma 8.5 gives an \(N\)-central state \(\omega\) on
\(\widetilde S(N)\) with \(\omega|_N=\tau\).  Fix \(i\) and put \(q=q_i\).
Since \(q\) commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\),
\[
 [q^\perp yq^\perp,a]=q^\perp[y,a]q^\perp=0,\qquad a\in JNJ,
\]
for \(y\in\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\); hence \(q^\perp yq^\perp\in\widetilde
S(N)\).  If \(\omega(q^\perp)>0\), then
\(y\mapsto \omega(q^\perp yq^\perp)/\omega(q^\perp)\) is an \(N\)-central state
on \(\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\).  On \(N_+\) it is dominated by a scalar multiple
of \(\tau\), hence is normal, and centrality on the factor forces its
restriction to be \(\tau\), contradicting relative proper proximality.  Thus
\(\omega(q_i^\perp)=0\) for all \(i\).

Extend \(\omega\) to a state on \(\cA_N\).  In its GNS representation each
\(q_i\) fixes the cyclic vector, so \(\omega(R)=1\).  Since \(R\) is a positive
contraction in \(q_0\cA_Nq_0\), \(R\le q_0\), hence \(\omega(q_0)=1\).  The
corner \(q_0\cA_Nq_0\) is contained in \(\widetilde S(N)\).  DKEP Lemma 8.5
provides the canonical \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodular u.c.p. map
\[
        \Theta_0:\mathbb B(L^2N)\to q_0\cA_Nq_0
\]
sending \(a\in N\) to \(aq_0\).  Then
\(\psi(T)=\omega(\Theta_0(T))\) is an \(N\)-central state on
\(\mathbb B(L^2N)\), and \(\psi(a)=\omega(aq_0)=\omega(a)=\tau(a)\).  This
Connes hypertrace contradicts non-amenability of \(N\).
\end{proof}

\section*{A relative Bass--Serre paradox}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:BS}
Let \(N=P*_{B}Q\) with trace-preserving expectations and \(Q=B\bbarotimes A\).
Assume that \(A\) has a trace-zero unitary \(a\), and that \(P\) has unitaries
\(w_1,w_2\) satisfying
\[
        E_B(w_i)=0,\qquad E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
Then \(N\) is properly proximal relative to \(X_B\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H_P=L^2P\ominus L^2B\) and \(H_Q=L^2Q\ominus L^2B\).  The AFP normal form
is
\[
 L^2N=L^2B\oplus\!\!\bigoplus_{n\ge1,\ i_1\ne\cdots\ne i_n}
 H_{i_1}\otimes_B\cdots\otimes_B H_{i_n},\quad i_j\in\{P,Q\}.
\]
Let \(p\) and \(q\) project onto the summands whose first letter is \(P\),
respectively \(Q\); then \(1=e_B+p+q\).  If \(x\in P\ominus B\), checking
\(L^2B\), first-letter \(P\), and first-letter \(Q\) words gives
\[
 [p,JxJ]=(1-e_B)JxJe_B-e_BJxJ(1-e_B),\qquad [q,JxJ]=0.             \tag{7}
\]
The two terms in (7) are \(N,JNJ\)-translates of \(e_B\), hence lie in \(X_B\).
The same computation with \(P,Q\) interchanged handles \(x\in Q\ominus B\);
adjoints are included because the centered spaces are self-adjoint, and
elements of \(B\) commute with \(p,q\).  DKEP Lemma 6.1 therefore gives
\(p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)\).

Let \(\varphi\) be an \(N\)-central state on \(S_{X_B}(N)\) whose restriction to
\(N\) is normal.  For \(\eta\in qL^2N\), \(w_i\eta\) has first letter \(P\),
and \(q\,w_1^*w_2\,q=0\) because \(E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0\).  Thus
\[
        w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p,\qquad        a p a^*\le q ,
\]
and these conjugates lie in \(S_{X_B}(N)\).  Centrality gives
\(2\varphi(q)\le\varphi(p)\le\varphi(q)\), so \(\varphi(p)=\varphi(q)=0\).

The unitary \(h=w_1a\) is \(B\)-Haar: every non-zero power is an alternating
reduced word in centered \(P\)- and \(Q\)-letters, hence \(E_B(h^n)=0\) for
\(n\ne0\).  For \(m\ne n\),
\[
h^ne_Bh^{-n}h^me_Bh^{-m}
 =h^n e_B h^{m-n}e_Bh^{-m}=h^nE_B(h^{m-n})e_Bh^{-m}=0 .
\]
The projections \(h^ne_Bh^{-n}\) are therefore pairwise orthogonal and have
equal \(\varphi\)-value, so \(\varphi(e_B)=0\).  This contradicts
\(1=\varphi(e_B+p+q)\).  By DKEP Theorem 6.2, \(N\) is properly proximal
relative to \(X_B\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Application to graph products}

Fix \(v\in V\) and put \(L_v=\Lk_\Gamma(v)\).  The graph-product reduced-word
model, or \cite{CF17}, gives the standard amalgamated free product
decomposition
\[
        M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}
        (M_{L_v}\bbarotimes M_v).                                \tag{8}
\]
Let \(C(v)=\{r\ne v:r\not\sim_\Gamma v\}\).  Since \(\Gamma^c\) is connected,
\(C(v)\ne\emptyset\).  If \(C(v)\) contains distinct \(r,t\), choose trace-zero
unitaries \(u_r,u_t\) and set \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_t\).  If \(C(v)=\{r\}\), then
connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge3\) give \(s\in L_v\) with
\(s\not\sim_\Gamma r\); otherwise \(\{v,r\}\) would be a component of
\(\Gamma^c\).  Set \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_su_r\).  In both cases the graph-product
reduced-word calculus gives
\[
        E_{M_{L_v}}(w_i)=0,\qquad E_{M_{L_v}}(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
A trace-zero unitary in \(M_v\) supplies the unitary \(a\) in (8), so
Proposition \ref{prop:BS} shows that \(M\) is properly proximal relative to
\(X_{M_{L_v}}\) for every \(v\).

Enumerate \(V=\{v_1,\ldots,v_m\}\).  Since no vertex belongs to its own link,
\(\cap_i L_{v_i}=\emptyset\).  Lemma \ref{lem:intersection} puts the associated
palindromic product in \(q_\emptyset\cA_Mq_\emptyset\).  Here
\(M_\emptyset=\C\), \(e_\C\) is the rank-one projection onto
\(\C\widehat1\), and DKEP Example 3.4 gives \(X_{M_\emptyset}=\mathbb
K(L^2M)\).

Finally, CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 implies that a join-irreducible graph product with
\(|V|\ge3\) and trace-zero vertex unitaries is a diffuse full factor.  A
diffuse amenable finite factor is the hyperfinite II\(_1\) factor and is not
full, so \(M\) is non-amenable.  It is separable because the graph is finite
and the vertices are separable.  Lemma \ref{lem:finite}, applied to the finite
family \(\{X_{M_{L_v}}:v\in V\}\), proves that \(M\) is properly proximal.
