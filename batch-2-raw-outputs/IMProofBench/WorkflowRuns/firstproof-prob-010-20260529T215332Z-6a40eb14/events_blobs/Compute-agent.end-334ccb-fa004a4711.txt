## 1. The concrete `K_X` product lemma is not justified as stated

The draft currently claims
\[
 \mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}(N)\,\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal K}}(N)\,
 \mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}(N)
 \subset
 \mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\overline{\mathcal H}\otimes_N\mathcal K}}(N).
\tag{*}
\]
I do **not** find this statement in DKEP, and the cited results do not prove it.

What DKEP does give:

- \(X_{\mathcal H}\) is the hereditary \(C^*\)-algebra generated by the coefficient operators \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) for bounded vectors; see DKEP source `DKEP.tex:1144-1148`.
- For bounded vectors, the contragredient and fusion formulas are
  \[
  T_{\overline\xi}=T_\xi^*, \qquad T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi;
  \]
  see `DKEP.tex:1068-1079`.
- Hence on raw coefficient generators one has the algebraic identity
  \[
  (T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2})(T_{\xi_3}^*T_{\xi_4})
  =
  T_{\xi_1\otimes\overline{\xi_2}\otimes\eta_1}^{\,*}
  T_{\xi_4\otimes\overline{\xi_3}\otimes\eta_2}.
  \]

The flaw is the passage from this raw coefficient identity to the closed hereditary algebras \(\mathbb K_X\). DKEP's \(\mathbb K_X\) is not simply the norm closure of the coefficient span. It is defined from the \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,2}\)-closed left ideal \(\mathbb K_X^L=\overline{B(L^2N)X}^{\|\cdot\|_{\infty,2}}\), with \(\mathbb K_X=(\mathbb K_X^L)^*\mathbb K_X^L\); see `DKEP.tex:638-671`. Thus elements of \(\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}\) may contain the extra left-ideal closure and arbitrary \(B(L^2N)\)-factors not controlled by the displayed coefficient identity.

The cited DKEP results do not fill that gap:

- Corollary 5.2 is separate continuity of multiplication in \(M\otimes_{\rm Con}N^{\rm op}\) for the relative topology, not continuity of operator multiplication in \(B(L^2N)\) for the \(\mathbb K_X\)-closures; see `DKEP.tex:942-949`.
- Proposition 5.9 identifies \(T\in\mathbb K_{X,Y}(N,M)\) by the conditions \(T^*T\in\mathbb K_Y\), \(TT^*\in\mathbb K_X\); see `DKEP.tex:1045-1055`.
- Theorem 5.10 characterizes mixing correspondences; it does not state functoriality of \(\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}\) under products of closed hereditary algebras; see `DKEP.tex:1085-1099`, especially the proof step at `1125-1127`.

So (*) should not be cited as a consequence of DKEP Corollary 5.2, Proposition 5.9, and Theorem 5.10. It may be true under an additional left-ideal support lemma, but that lemma must be stated and proved separately.

## 2. Correct replacement for the proof

The safe replacement is a **normal-support** statement, not a concrete \(\mathbb K\)-product statement. For the graph-product application it is cleaner to use the two-fold fusion:
\[
\mathcal L=\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U.
\]
For bounded \(\xi,\xi'\in\mathcal H_W\) and \(\eta,\eta'\in\mathcal H_U\),
\[
T_{\xi\otimes\eta}^*T_{\xi'\otimes\eta'}
=T_\xi^*(T_\eta^*T_{\eta'})T_{\xi'}.
\tag{1}
\]
Thus the coefficient calculation directly has the desired \(W\)-\(U\)-\(W\) sandwich shape. Passing from (1) to supports should be formulated as an added lemma:
\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_M q_U)q_W
\subset
q_{\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U}\mathcal A_M
q_{\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U}.
\tag{2}
\]
One possible proof is to define the weak-* closed left ideal in \(\mathcal A_M\) generated by the images of the \(T_\xi\)'s, show its right support is \(q_W\) because \(X_{\mathcal H_W}\) is generated by \(T_\xi^*T_{\xi'}\), and then use (1) plus weak-* separate continuity in the von Neumann algebra \(\mathcal A_M\). This is an additional support lemma extracted from DKEP's definitions; it is not a named DKEP theorem.

I recommend replacing the paragraph around current `answer.tex` lines 91-116 by something like:

```tex
For the graph-product correspondences we use the following support form of
DKEP's coefficient calculation.  Let
\(\mathcal K=\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U\).  For bounded
\(\xi,\xi'\in\mathcal H_W\) and \(\eta,\eta'\in\mathcal H_U\), DKEP's
formula \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\) gives
\[
T_{\xi\otimes\eta}^*T_{\xi'\otimes\eta'}
=T_\xi^*(T_\eta^*T_{\eta'})T_{\xi'} .
\]
Equivalently, the normal left ideal generated by the coefficient maps
\(T_\xi\), \(\xi\in\mathcal H_W\), sandwiches the \(U\)-coefficient corner
into the coefficient corner of \(\mathcal K\).  Since the right support of
that normal left ideal is \(q_W\), this gives
\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W
\subset q_{\mathcal K}\mathcal A_Mq_{\mathcal K}.
\]
This is a support-level consequence of DKEP Example 5.11 and the fusion
formula; no multiplication in the normal bidual of concrete operators is used.
```

Then apply CDHJKN to \(\mathcal K=\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U\), not to the triple fusion. This is stronger and avoids the unproved concrete inclusion (*).

If the author wants to keep the triple notation, the only safe statement is:
\[
\text{raw coefficient products of the displayed form are coefficients of }
\mathcal H\otimes_N\overline{\mathcal H}\otimes_N\mathcal K.
\]
Do not state the full \(\mathbb K\)-product inclusion unless a separate proof of the \(\mathbb K\)-closure functoriality is added.

## 3. The cut-down support lemma is sound, with a citation nuance

The cut-down lemma
\[
C B C\subset D
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
q_C(q_B\mathcal A_Nq_B)q_C\subset q_D\mathcal A_Nq_D
\]
is valid by ordinary open-projection calculus in \(A^{**}\), followed by cutting by the simultaneous normal projection \(p\).

Details:

- DKEP define \(p_{\rm nor}\) and identify \((A^\sharp)^*\cong p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\); see `DKEP.tex:467-470`.
- For an \(M\)-bimodule hereditary subalgebra \(B\subset A\), DKEP say its ordinary support \(q_B\) commutes with \(p_{\rm nor}\), and \((B^\sharp)^*\cong q_Bp_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}q_B\); see `DKEP.tex:473`.
- Proposition 2.10 is the simultaneous version for \(M\) and \(\widetilde M\), identifying
  \[
  (A^{M\sharp M}\cap A^{\widetilde M\sharp\widetilde M})^*
  =(A^{M\sharp M})^*\cap(A^{\widetilde M\sharp\widetilde M})^*
  \subset M(A)^{**};
  \]
  see `DKEP.tex:495-502`.
- DKEP later explicitly uses this by saying \((\mathbb K_X(M)_J^\sharp)^*\) is a corner in \((B(L^2M)_J^\sharp)^*\) whose support commutes with both \(M\) and \(JMJ\); see `DKEP.tex:1571`.

Thus in the simultaneous \(N,JNJ\) setting, the ordinary support \(e_C\) of a hereditary bimodule subalgebra \(C\) commutes with both one-sided normal projections and hence with the simultaneous normal projection \(p\). The proof in `answer.tex` is therefore legitimate after citing the one-sided support statement (`467-473`) plus the simultaneous intersection statement (`495-502`). I would add the mild hypothesis that the multiplier actions on the hereditary subalgebras are faithful, or say explicitly that this holds for the boundary-piece algebras used in the proof, since DKEP Proposition 2.10 includes that faithfulness assumption for subalgebras.

Also, DKEP's warning at `DKEP.tex:476-478` is exactly why this cut-down proof is the right approach: the canonical embedding \(A\to(A^\sharp)^*\) is completely order isomorphic onto its range but is not generally a \(*\)-homomorphism.

## 4. CDHJKN triple fusion check

The graph-product fusion claim itself is correct:
\[
\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\overline{\mathcal H_W}\otimes_M\mathcal H_U
\]
decomposes as a direct sum of \(\mathcal H_Z=L^2(M)\otimes_{M_Z}L^2(M)\) with \(Z\subset U\cap W\).

CDHJKN notation is
\[
\mathscr H_U(V_1,V_2)
=\,_{M_{V_1}}L^2(M_{V_1})\otimes_{M_U}L^2(M_{V_2})_{M_{V_2}},
\]
and \(\mathcal H_U=\mathscr H_U(V,V)\). Their Proposition 5.5 says
\[
\mathscr H_{U_1}(V_1,V_2)\otimes_{M_{V_2}}\mathscr H_{U_2}(V_2,V_3)
\cong
\bigoplus_{Z\subset U_1\cap U_2}\mathscr H_Z(V_1,V_3)^{\oplus k_{\Gamma_2}(U_1,U_2,Z)};
\]
see `cube.tex:1223-1233`. Theorem 5.4 supplies the underlying decomposition and multiplicities; see `cube.tex:1196-1205`.

Since \(\overline{\mathcal H_W}\cong\mathcal H_W\), apply Proposition 5.5 twice. First
\[
\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_W
\cong \bigoplus_{Y\subset W}\mathcal H_Y^{\oplus k(W,W,Y)}.
\]
Then
\[
\mathcal H_Y\otimes_M\mathcal H_U
\cong \bigoplus_{Z\subset Y\cap U}\mathcal H_Z^{\oplus k(Y,U,Z)}.
\]
Since \(Y\subset W\), every final \(Z\) satisfies \(Z\subset U\cap W\). Therefore the draft's triple-fusion containment by \(q_{U\cap W}\) is correct as a CDHJKN consequence. The point is only that the DKEP support step should use a separately proved support-level coefficient lemma, preferably the two-fold version above, rather than the unproved concrete \(\mathbb K\)-product inclusion.
