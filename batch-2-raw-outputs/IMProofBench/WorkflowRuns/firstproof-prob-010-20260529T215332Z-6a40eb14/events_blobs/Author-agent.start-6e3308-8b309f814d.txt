## TeX-ready replacement proof of the support lemma

Use this as a separate lemma; it makes explicit the rectangular support step that is only implicit in the current draft.

```tex
\begin{lemma}[rectangular support sandwich]\label{lem:rectangular-support-sandwich}
Let \(N\) be finite, \(A=\mathbb B(L^2N)\), and
\(\mathcal A_N=p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\).  For an \(N\)-\(N\)
correspondence \(\mathcal H\), let \(X_{\mathcal H}\) be the DKEP
coefficient boundary of Example 5.11 and let \(q_{\mathcal H}\) be the
support in \(\mathcal A_N\) of
\((\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}(N)^\sharp_J)^*\).  Then for any
\(N\)-\(N\) correspondences \(\mathcal H,\mathcal K\), with
\[
  \mathcal L=\mathcal H\otimes_N\overline{\mathcal H}\otimes_N\mathcal K ,
\]
one has
\[
q_{\mathcal H}(q_{\mathcal K}\mathcal A_Nq_{\mathcal K})q_{\mathcal H}
\subset
q_{\mathcal L}\mathcal A_Nq_{\mathcal L}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
We first work in the ordinary enveloping algebra \(A^{**}\).  Let
\(e_{\mathcal H},e_{\mathcal K},e_{\mathcal L}\in A^{**}\) be the ordinary
open supports of the hereditary algebras
\(\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}},\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal K}},\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal L}}\).
By the standard support calculus for the left ideal
\(\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}^L\), together with DKEP Proposition 5.9 applied
to the coefficient boundary of Example 5.11, \(e_{\mathcal H}\) is the right
support of the weak-* closed rectangular left module generated by the concrete
maps \(T_\xi\), \(\xi\in\mathcal H\) bounded; similarly for
\(\mathcal K\) and \(\mathcal L\).  Equivalently, \(e_{\mathcal H}\) is
obtained as the weak-* limit of the usual support approximate units built from
finite sums of \(T_\xi^*T_\xi\).

Fix finite diagonal coefficient sums
\[
  h=\sum_i T_{\xi_i}^*T_{\xi_i},\qquad
  k=\sum_j T_{\eta_j}^*T_{\eta_j},
\]
with \(\xi_i\in\mathcal H\), \(\eta_j\in\mathcal K\) bounded.  For each
\(i,j\), DKEP's concrete fusion identities give
\[
  T_{\eta_j}T_{\xi_i}^*T_{\xi_i}
  =
  T_{\xi_i\otimes\overline{\xi_i}\otimes\eta_j}.
\]
Thus \(T_{\eta_j}T_{\xi_i}^*T_{\xi_i}\) lies in the rectangular module
for \(\mathcal L\), and left multiplication by \(T_{\eta_j}^*\) does not
increase right support.  Hence
\[
  kh=\sum_{i,j}T_{\eta_j}^*
       \bigl(T_{\eta_j}T_{\xi_i}^*T_{\xi_i}\bigr)
  \in A^{**}e_{\mathcal L}.
\]
Passing to the weak-* limits over the support approximate units for
\(\mathcal K\) and \(\mathcal H\) gives
\[
  e_{\mathcal K}e_{\mathcal H}\in A^{**}e_{\mathcal L}.
\]
Therefore, for \(x\in e_{\mathcal K}A^{**}e_{\mathcal K}\),
\[
  e_{\mathcal H}xe_{\mathcal H}
  =(e_{\mathcal K}e_{\mathcal H})^*x(e_{\mathcal K}e_{\mathcal H})
  \in e_{\mathcal L}A^{**}e_{\mathcal L}.
\]

Now cut by \(p_{\rm nor}\).  By DKEP Proposition 2.10, the supports above
commute with \(p_{\rm nor}\), and \(q_{\mathcal H}=p_{\rm nor}e_{\mathcal H}\),
etc.  If \(y\in q_{\mathcal H}(q_{\mathcal K}\mathcal A_Nq_{\mathcal K})
q_{\mathcal H}\), then, for some \(a\in A^{**}\),
\[
 y=p_{\rm nor}\, e_{\mathcal H}(e_{\mathcal K}ae_{\mathcal K})
 e_{\mathcal H}\,p_{\rm nor}.
\]
The ordinary inclusion puts the middle term in
\(e_{\mathcal L}A^{**}e_{\mathcal L}\).  Since \(p_{\rm nor}\) commutes
with \(e_{\mathcal L}\), this lies in
\[
 p_{\rm nor}e_{\mathcal L}A^{**}e_{\mathcal L}p_{\rm nor}
 =
 q_{\mathcal L}\mathcal A_Nq_{\mathcal L}.
\]
No multiplication in the normal bidual is used before this final cut.
\end{proof}
```

The important audit point is the phrase “support approximate units built from finite sums of \(T_\xi^*T_\xi\).” This is the rectangular support assertion that must be made explicit. DKEP Proposition 5.9/polar decomposition and Example 5.11 are the inputs, but the manuscript should present the support assertion as part of this lemma rather than as a raw quote from DKEP.

## Graph-product corner proof

For \(\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\), the vector
\(\zeta_U=\hat1\otimes_{M_U}\hat1\) has \(T_{\zeta_U}=e_{M_U}\). The \(M\)-\(M\) translates of \(\zeta_U\) give the generators \(xJyJ e_{M_U}\), so DKEP Example 3.4 and Example 5.11 give \(X_{\mathcal H_U}=X_{M_U}\).

Apply the rectangular support lemma with \(\mathcal H=\mathcal H_W\) and
\(\mathcal K=\mathcal H_U\). Since \(\mathcal H_W\) is self-contragredient,
the target is
\[
\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U.
\]
CDHJKN Proposition 5.5 decomposes
\[
\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_W
\cong \bigoplus_{Z\subset W}\mathcal H_Z^{\oplus k_Z},
\]
and fusing each summand with \(\mathcal H_U\) gives only summands
\(\mathcal H_Y\) with \(Y\subset Z\cap U\subset W\cap U\). DKEP Theorem 5.10 handles direct sums: finite-support bounded vectors are cyclic, so the support of the direct sum is below the join of the summand supports. Since \(Y\subset U\cap W\) implies \(X_{M_Y}\subset X_{M_{U\cap W}}\), the target support is dominated by \(q_{U\cap W}\). Hence
\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W
\subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A_Mq_{U\cap W}.
\]

Edge check: if \(\mathcal H=L^2N\), then \(T_{\hat1}=1\), \(q_{\mathcal H}=1\), and \(\mathcal H\otimes_N\overline{\mathcal H}\otimes_N\mathcal K\cong\mathcal K\). The lemma reduces to the tautology
\[
q_{\mathcal K}\mathcal A_Nq_{\mathcal K}\subset q_{\mathcal K}\mathcal A_Nq_{\mathcal K}.
\]
This is why the proof must not claim
\(\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal K}}\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}
\subset\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal L}}\): for \(\mathcal H=L^2N\), that would read
\(A\,\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal K}}\,A\subset\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal K}}\), which is false unless the hereditary algebra is an ideal.
