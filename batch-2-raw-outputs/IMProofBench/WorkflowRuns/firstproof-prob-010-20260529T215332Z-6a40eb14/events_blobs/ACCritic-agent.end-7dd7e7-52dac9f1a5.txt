## Literature validation

DKEP does define boundary pieces \(X_B\) associated to a subalgebra \(B\subset M\) using the Jones projection \(e_B\), and defines \(S_X(M)\) as the corresponding small-at-infinity operator system. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

DKEP’s normal-bidual Lemma 8.5 is indeed a normal-bidual characterization, but in the paper it is stated for a trace-preserving group action \(\Gamma\curvearrowright M\) with ergodic action on the center and a \(\Gamma\)-boundary piece. The draft uses it in the trivial-action/factor specialization, which is plausible but should be explicitly stated and checked. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.4 gives a decomposition and fusion rules for graph-product bimodules \(H_U(V_1,V_2)\), with summands indexed by \(W\subset U_1\cap U_2\). ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743)) However, this theorem does **not itself state** anything about DKEP support projections \(q_{X_U}\) or their meets in the normal bidual. The draft’s use of it requires an additional theorem or proof connecting fusion of correspondences to meets/products of boundary-support projections.

CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.5 supports the factoriality claim in the final application: the obstruction to factoriality is exactly a universal vertex with nonfactor vertex algebra, or a nonedge pair adjacent to all other vertices with both dimensions \(2\). ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743)) CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.1 supports the relative amenability/nonamenability criterion under the trace-zero unitary hypothesis. ## Detailed mathematical audit

### 1. Problem statement and interpretation

The interpretation of irreducibility as connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\), equivalently join-irreducibility, is standard and faithful to the intended graph-product meaning. This part is acceptable.

### 2. Boundary-piece notation

The notation \(X_B\), \(S_{X_B}(N)\), and relative proper proximality is consistent with DKEP’s framework. However, the draft introduces \(q_X\), \(\widetilde S(N)\), and \(\widetilde S_X(N)\) with very little definition. Since the proof relies heavily on support projections and corners in the normal bidual, this is not merely notational; these objects must be specified with enough precision for the subsequent arguments to be checkable.

### 3. Lemma: finite boundary criterion

The lemma states:

\[
q_{X_1}\wedge\cdots\wedge q_{X_m}=q_{\mathcal K}
\quad\text{and relative proper proximality for all }X_i
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
N\text{ properly proximal}.
\]

This may be true, but the proof is not rigorous enough as written.

Specific gaps:

- The phrase “The boundary calculus gives”
  \[
  q_{X_i}^{\perp}\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)q_{X_i}^{\perp}
  \subset \widetilde S(N)
  \]
  is an essential assertion, but no theorem or proof is given.

- When defining
  \[
  T\mapsto
  \omega(q_{X_i}^{\perp}Tq_{X_i}^{\perp})/\omega(q_{X_i}^{\perp}),
  \]
  the proof must justify that this is normal on \(N\). This is not automatic from \(\omega|_N=\tau\), because \(q_{X_i}^{\perp}\) is not an element of \(N\). One can likely prove normality by domination by \(\tau\), but the draft does not do so.

- The passage from \(\omega(q_{X_i}^{\perp})=0\) for all \(i\) to \(\omega(q_{\mathcal K})=1\) requires a support argument for possibly noncommuting projections, or a statement that the relevant support projections commute. This is not supplied.

- The final claim that being supported on the compact boundary gives an \(N\)-central state on \(\mathbb B(L^2N)\) extending \(\tau\) is plausible but again not proved. The identification of the “compact corner” with the normal bidual of \(\mathbb B(L^2N)\) is stated, not established.

Because this lemma is essential to the final step, these are serious gaps.

### 4. Lemma: link boundary pieces fill the boundary

This is the largest new gap. The lemma claims:

\[
\bigwedge_{v\in V} q_{X_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}}=q_{\mathcal K}.
\]

The proof says that CDHJKN’s fusion rule implies that products of support projections correspond to intersections of vertex sets. But CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.4 is a statement about graph-product bimodule decompositions and Connes fusion; it does not directly identify DKEP boundary-piece support projections or their meets. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

To make this lemma rigorous, the author would need to prove at least the following missing bridge:

\[
q_{X_{U_1}}\wedge q_{X_{U_2}}=q_{X_{U_1\cap U_2}}
\]

for graph-product subalgebras \(M_{U_1},M_{U_2}\), or cite an exact theorem proving it. The current proof only says “no larger boundary piece survives,” which is handwaving. The CDHJKN fusion formula gives direct sums indexed by \(W\subset U_1\cap U_2\); the proof must explain why this corresponds exactly to the support projection for \(U_1\cap U_2\), rather than merely to some join/meet of smaller boundary pieces.

This lemma is indispensable for the final application of the finite-boundary criterion. As written, it is not established.

### 5. Proposition: relative Bass–Serre paradox

The strategy of Proposition 3 is plausible and resembles DKEP’s free-product paradoxical projection argument. However, the proof remains somewhat compressed.

Issues:

- The assertion that \([p,JxJ]\) and \([q,JxJ]\) lie in \(K^{\infty,1}_{X_B}(N)\) for algebraic-free-product \(x\) is not proved in detail. One must explicitly analyze cancellations in the AFP Fock space and show the resulting operators lie in the \(X_B\)-compact ideal.

- The invocation of DKEP Lemma 6.1 is abbreviated. DKEP Lemma 6.1 is a generated-subalgebra criterion for proper proximal/mixing points; to use it here, the draft should verify the required commutators for a weakly dense \(*\)-subalgebra and also for adjoints. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

- The inequalities
  \[
  w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p,\qquad apa^*\le q
  \]
  are believable, but the proof should spell out the AFP Hilbert-module orthogonality more explicitly.

These issues may be repairable, but in the current draft this proposition is still not fully rigorous.

### 6. Application to graph products

The graph-product application of Proposition 3 is mostly sound.

- The decomposition
  \[
  M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}}\bigl(M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}\bar\otimes M_v\bigr)
  \]
  is standard.

- The construction of \(w_1,w_2\) works in both cases \(|C(v)|\ge2\) and \(|C(v)|=1\). In the second case, connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) ensures a complement-neighbor \(s\) of \(r\), and since \(C(v)=\{r\}\), this \(s\) lies in \(\operatorname{Lk}(v)\). The expectation computations are correct, assuming standard graph-product reduced-word expectations.

- The proof that \(M\) is a factor is essentially correct, though the wording “factoriality can fail only if...” is slightly imprecise relative to CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.5. In the present irreducible, \(|V|\ge3\) situation, the problematic configurations are indeed impossible.

- The proof that \(M\) is nonamenable using CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.1 is also essentially correct, though it should explicitly say that amenability of \(M\) is amenability relative to \(M_\varnothing=\mathbb C\).

- The graph product \(M\) is separable because \(V\) is finite and the vertex algebras are separable; this should be stated before applying the finite-boundary criterion.

### 7. Final conclusion

The final line of the proof depends on:

1. relative proper proximality for all link boundary pieces;
2. the assertion that these link boundary pieces fill the boundary;
3. the finite-boundary criterion converting finitely many relative proper proximality statements into ordinary proper proximality.

Item 1 is plausible but underproved. Items 2 and 3 are essential and not rigorously justified in the draft. Therefore the proof is not complete.
