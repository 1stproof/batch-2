Problem interpretation: ``irreducible'' is taken to mean join-irreducible for a
finite simplicial graph, i.e. no nonempty proper partition $V=S\sqcup T$ with
all cross edges; equivalently $\Gamma^c$ is connected.  The traces are assumed
faithful normal tracial states.

Strategy recorded:
Use induction over an ordering of vertices coming from a spanning tree of the
connected complement graph.  Start with a length-two path in the complement.
For $V_k=\{v_1,\ldots,v_k\}$, maintain that the induced complement graph is
connected and $M_{V_k}$ is properly proximal.

Inputs used:
1. DKEP permanence for proper proximality:
   (a) free products of tracial von Neumann algebras with centered unitaries are
       properly proximal except the $\mathbb C^2*\mathbb C^2$ exceptional case;
   (b) if $P$ is properly proximal and not amenable relative to $B\subset P$,
       then $P*_B(B\bar\otimes A)$ is properly proximal when $A$ has a
       trace-zero unitary.
2. CHHJKN relative amenability criterion for graph-product subalgebras:
   $M_X$ amenable relative to $M_Y$ iff amenability of the outside vertex
   algebras and the adjacency/2-dimensional exceptional condition hold.

Key induction details:
For adding $v_k$, let $L=V_{k-1}\cap\operatorname{Lk}_\Gamma(v_k)$.  The graph
product decomposition is
\[
M_{V_k}=M_{V_{k-1}}*_{M_L}(M_L\bar\otimes M_{v_k}).
\]
The chosen ordering gives a complement edge $v_k-r_k$ with $r_k\in V_{k-1}$,
and a complement edge $r_k-s_k$ with $s_k\in V_{k-1}$.  Thus $r_k\notin L$ and
$r_k,s_k$ are nonadjacent in $\Gamma$.  If $M_{V_{k-1}}$ were amenable relative
to $M_L$, the relative amenability criterion would force $r_k,s_k$ to be the
exceptional pair, adjacent in $\Gamma$ to all other vertices of $V_{k-1}$.
Equivalently $\{r_k,s_k\}$ is a connected component of the induced complement,
contradicting connectedness and $|V_{k-1}|\ge3$.

Base:
For complement path $v_1-v_2-v_3$, $M_{V_3}=M_{\{v_1,v_3\}}*M_{v_2}$ since
$v_2$ is not adjacent in $\Gamma$ to $v_1,v_3$.  The first free-product factor
is not $\mathbb C^2$ because it is generated by two non-scalar vertex algebras.
DKEP free-product theorem gives proper proximality.

Potential scrutiny point:
The DKEP AFP permanence statement should be cited in the exact form if a later
round wants theorem numbers.  It is the Bass--Serre deformation plus
normal-bidual upgrading result.  If challenged, one can replace it by a more
detailed quoted proposition with theorem numbers from DKEP.



\section*{Round 1 post-critic refinements}

The previous argument used an unvalidated permanence theorem for proper
proximality under AFPs.  Literature check: DKEP, \emph{Properly proximal von
Neumann algebras}, has Proposition 6.7 proving $M_1*M_2$ properly proximal when
both $M_i$ are diffuse, but no amalgamated-free-product theorem in the form
needed.  DKE, \emph{Structure of relatively bi-exact group von Neumann
algebras}, has a relative-to-full upgrading theorem (Theorem 1.1) with extra
hypotheses: diffuse ambient and subalgebras, commuting Jones projections,
bounded Pimsner--Popa bases, mixing inclusions, and a central-projection
decomposition.  It does not immediately imply the desired AFP permanence for
$P*_B(B\bar\otimes A)$ with arbitrary $B,A$.

A useful finite-dimensional base replacement is available.  Proposition:
if $A$ has two trace-zero unitaries $a_1,a_2$ with $\tau(a_1^*a_2)=0$, and
$B$ has a trace-zero unitary $b$, then $A*B$ is properly proximal.  Proof is
DKEP Prop. 6.7's paradoxical projection argument:
let $P_A,P_B$ be projections onto reduced words whose first letter is in $A$
or $B$.  Their commutators with right multiplication by generators are finite
rank, hence $P_A,P_B\in S(A*B)$ by DKEP Lemma 6.1.  Left multiplication gives
$a_1P_Ba_1^*+a_2P_Ba_2^*\le P_A$ and $bP_Ab^*\le P_B$, so any central state
on $S(A*B)$ has $P_A=P_B=0$.  The unitary $a_1b$ is Haar, hence the central
state also vanishes on the vacuum projection; contradiction.

This handles the graph-product base case for a complement path
$v_1-v_2-v_3$: $M_{V_3}=M_{\{v_1,v_3\}}*M_{v_2}$, and
$M_{\{v_1,v_3\}}$ contains two orthogonal trace-zero unitaries, one from
$M_{v_1}$ and one from $M_{v_3}$.

The induction still reduces to the following Bass--Serre permanence statement:

\[
\text{If }B\subset P,\quad P\text{ properly proximal and not amenable relative
to }B,\quad A\text{ has a trace-zero unitary,}
\]
then $P*_B(B\bar\otimes A)$ is properly proximal.

Why this is nontrivial: in the AFP Fock space, the natural projections onto
words starting with the $P\ominus B$ side and with the $A$ side have commutators
with right multiplication compact only relative to $e_B$, not compact in the
DKEP $K_{\infty,1}$ sense unless $B$ is finite-dimensional/amenable in a strong
way.  Thus the scalar paradoxical-projection proof proves relative proper
proximality with respect to the boundary piece $X_B$, but upgrading to full
proper proximality should use the assumption that $P$ is not amenable relative
to $B$.  This is exactly the gap.

Graph-specific extra structure in the induction: when adding $v_k$ with
$L=V_{k-1}\cap\Lk(v_k)$, choose a complement edge $v_k-r$ with $r\in V_{k-1}$
and then a complement edge $r-s$ inside the connected previous complement
graph.  Besides proving non-relative amenability by the CDHJKN criterion, these
vertices also provide $M_L$-centered unitaries in $P=M_{V_{k-1}}$: if
$a\in M_r$ and $b\in M_s$ are trace-zero, then $a$ and $ba$ are unitaries in
$P\ominus M_L$ and $E_{M_L}(a^*ba)=0$ (the word $a^*,b,a$ is reduced relative
to $M_L$ because $r$ and $s$ are nonadjacent in $\Gamma$ and $r\notin L$).
This may be useful for a graph-specific AFP paradoxical argument, though the
main obstruction remains that the half-tree projections live in a relative
boundary piece rather than $S(M)$.

Potential next attacks:
1. Locate an exact theorem proving the Bass--Serre permanence statement above,
possibly in a later/preprint version by Ding--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Peterson
or in work on relatively bi-exact von Neumann algebras.
2. Prove a narrower graph-specific permanence theorem: assume in addition that
$P$ contains two $B$-orthogonal $B$-centered unitaries as above.  Try to build
operators in $S(P*_B(B\bar\otimes A))$ by correcting the relative half-tree
projections with a state/conditional expectation argument using proper
proximality of $P$.
3. Explore whether non-relative amenability of $P$ over $B$ plus a hypothetical
central state on $S(M)$ yields a $P$-central state on $S(P)$ or on the basic
construction $\langle P,e_B\rangle$, contradicting proper proximality or the
CDHJKN criterion.



\section*{Boundary-piece proof strategy}

A more promising route avoids an induction over amalgamated free products.  For
each vertex \(v\), use the graph-product splitting
\[
M=M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}}
   (M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}\bar\otimes M_v).
\]
Let \(X_v=X_{M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}}\) be the DKEP boundary piece generated
by the Jones projection onto \(L^2(M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)})\).  The AFP Fock
space gives half-tree projections \(p_v,q_v\) onto words beginning on the
\(M_{V\setminus\{v\}}\)-side and the \(M_v\)-side.  Their commutators with the
right action are compact relative to \(M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}\), hence
\(p_v,q_v\in S_{X_v}(M)\).  If \(M_{V\setminus\{v\}}\) contains two
\(M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}\)-orthogonal centered unitaries and \(M_v\) contains
a trace-zero unitary, the standard paradoxical inequalities prove that \(M\) is
properly proximal relative to \(X_v\).

Graph supply of the two unitaries for each \(v\): let
\(C(v)=\{r\ne v: r\not\sim_\Gamma v\}\).  If \(|C(v)|\ge2\), take trace-zero
unitaries in two such vertex algebras.  If \(C(v)=\{r\}\), connectedness of
\(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge3\) gives \(s\ne v,r\) with \(r\not\sim_\Gamma s\);
then \(s\in\operatorname{Lk}(v)\), and \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_su_r\) are
\(M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}\)-centered and orthogonal:
\(E(w_i)=E(w_1^*w_2)=0\).

Boundary filling mechanism: for graph products the boundary piece \(X_U\)
associated with \(M_U\) corresponds to the correspondence
\(L^2(M)\otimes_{M_U}L^2(M)\).  CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.4 (bimodule decomposition
and fusion rules) implies that the meet/product of the support projections for
\(X_{U_1},\ldots,X_{U_m}\) is the support projection for
\(X_{U_1\cap\cdots\cap U_m}\).  Since \(\cap_{v\in V}\operatorname{Lk}(v)=\emptyset\),
the meet of the link boundary pieces is the compact boundary.

Finite-boundary criterion in DKEP normal-bidual language: if \(N\) is a
nonamenable finite factor, \(q_{X_1}\wedge\cdots\wedge q_{X_m}=q_{\mathcal K}\),
and \(N\) is properly proximal relative to each \(X_i\), then \(N\) is properly
proximal.  A central state on \(\widetilde S(N)\) would, after cutting by
\(q_{X_i}^\perp\), give a central state on \(\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\) unless it is
supported by every \(q_{X_i}\); hence it is supported by \(q_{\mathcal K}\),
which gives an \(N\)-central hypertrace on \(\mathbb B(L^2N)\), forcing
amenability.

For the final application, CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.5 gives factoriality: in an
irreducible graph with \(|V|\ge3\), no vertex is adjacent to all others and no
non-edge pair is adjacent to all remaining vertices, because either would give
an isolated component in \(\Gamma^c\).  CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.1 gives
nonamenability by the same isolated-component obstruction.
