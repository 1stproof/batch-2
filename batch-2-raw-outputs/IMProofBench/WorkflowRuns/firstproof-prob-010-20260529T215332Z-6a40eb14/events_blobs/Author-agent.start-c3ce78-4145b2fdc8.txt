\section*{Problem statement and interpretation}

I use the standard graph-product meaning of irreducible: the finite graph
$\Gamma=(V,E)$ is not a non-trivial join, equivalently the complement graph
$\Gamma^c$ is connected.  Traces are faithful normal tracial states.  For
$U\subset V$, $M_U$ denotes the graph product over the induced subgraph and
$E_U:M\to M_U$ the canonical trace-preserving expectation.

We use Ding--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Peterson's boundary-piece formalism
\cite{DKEP}.  Put
\[
  \cA_N=(\mathbb B(L^2N)^\sharp_J)^*
       =p_{\rm nor}\mathbb B(L^2N)^{**}p_{\rm nor}.
\]
If $X$ is a boundary piece, let $q_X$ be the support projection in $\cA_N$ of
$(\K_X(N)^\sharp_J)^*$.  It commutes with $N$ and $JNJ$, and
\[
 \widetilde S_X(N)=
 \{T\in\cA_N:[T,a]\in q_X\cA_Nq_X\hbox{ for all }a\in JNJ\}.
\]
For the compact boundary $X=\mathcal K(L^2N)$ write $q_0$ and
$\widetilde S(N)$.  For a separable finite factor, \cite[Lemma 8.5]{DKEP}
says that proper proximality relative to $X$ is equivalent to the absence of an
$N$-central state on $\widetilde S_X(N)$ whose restriction to $N$ is the trace.

\section*{Boundary supports for graph products}

Let $A=\mathbb B(L^2N)$ and let $X\subset A$ be a boundary piece with ordinary
open support $e_X\in A^{**}$.  We shall use the following identification of
normal supports:
\[
        q_X=p_{\rm nor}e_X .                                      \tag{1}
\]
Here \(q_X\) is, by definition, the support of \((\K_X(N)^\sharp_J)^*\).  Since
\(X\subset\K_X(N)\), \(p_{\rm nor}e_X\le q_X\).  For the converse, let
\(\psi\in A^\sharp_J\) vanish on \(X\).  If \(S,R\in\K_X^L\), choose
\(S_i,R_i\in AX\) with \(S_i\to S\) and \(R_i\to R\) in
\(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,2}\).  Since \(X\) is hereditary,
\((S_i)^*R_i\in X\), and the standard estimate
\[
 \|(S_i)^*R_i-S^*R\|_{\infty,1}
 \le \|S_i-S\|_{\infty,2}\|R_i\|_{\infty,2}
     +\|S\|_{\infty,2}\|R_i-R\|_{\infty,2}
\]
shows that \(S^*R\) is in the \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)-closure of \(X\).
DKEP's strong-closure proposition for normal \(N,JNJ\)-bimodular functionals
then gives \(\psi(S^*R)=0\) \cite[Section 3]{DKEP}.  Thus every such \(\psi\)
vanishing on \(X\) also vanishes on \(\K_X=(\K_X^L)^*\K_X^L\), so \(X\) and
\(\K_X\) have the same support after cutting by \(p_{\rm nor}\).  This is the
only point where the normal support of \(\K_X\) is replaced by the more concrete
coefficient support.

For $U\subset V$ set
\[
        \cH_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M
\]
as an $M$-$M$ correspondence.  Let $P_U$ be the Jones projection of $L^2M$ onto
$L^2M_U$.  For $\zeta_U=\hat1\otimes_{M_U}\hat1$ one has
$T_{\zeta_U}=P_U$, and the $M$-$M$ translates of $\zeta_U$ give precisely the
generators of the boundary piece $X_{M_U}$ from \cite[Example 3.4]{DKEP}.
Let $\mathcal B_U$ be the norm-closed linear span of all coefficient
operators \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) coming from non-empty finite Connes fusions of correspondences
\(\cH_Z\) and \(\overline{\cH_Z}\) with \(Z\subset U\).  DKEP's identities
\(T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*\) and \(T_{\alpha\otimes\beta}=T_\beta T_\alpha\) show
that \(\mathcal B_U\) is a \(C^*\)-algebra.  CDHJKN's fusion rule decomposes all
these non-empty finite fusions into direct sums of \(\cH_L\)'s with \(L\subset U\).
For \(L\subset U\) one has \(X_{M_L}\subset X_{M_U}\): the Jones projection
\(P_L\) lies in \(P_U\mathbb B(L^2M)P_U\), which is part of DKEP's model for
\(X_{M_U}\).  Thus \(\mathcal B_U\) is supported by \(X_{M_U}\), while it also
contains the coefficients of \(\cH_U\) that hereditarily generate \(X_{M_U}\).
Hence \(\mathcal B_U\) has the same ordinary support as \(X_{M_U}\).  Denote
this support by \(s_U\).  The previous paragraph gives
\[
        q_U:=q_{X_{M_U}}=p_{\rm nor}s_U .                         \tag{2}
\]

We shall use the following elementary support fact.  Suppose \(B,C\subset A\)
are \(C^*\)-subalgebras with open supports \(r,s\in A^{**}\), and \(p\in A^{**}\)
is a projection such that
\[
        BC\subset pA^{**}s,\qquad CB\subset sA^{**}p .
\]
Then
\[
        r(sA^{**}s)r\subset pA^{**}p .                            \tag{3}
\]
Indeed, let \((b_\alpha)\subset B\) and \((c_\beta)\subset C\) be approximate
units, converging weak-* to \(r\) and \(s\).  If \(x\in sA^{**}s\), then
\[
 b_\alpha c_\beta x c_{\beta'}b_{\alpha'}\in pA^{**}p
\]
for all indices, and successive weak-* limits give (3).

\begin{lemma}[intersection of graph-product boundary corners]\label{lem:intersection}
For all $U,W\subset V$,
\[
        q_W(q_U\cA_Mq_U)q_W\subset q_{U\cap W}\cA_Mq_{U\cap W}.     \tag{4}
\]
Consequently, if $U_1,\ldots,U_m\subset V$ and
\[
R_m=q_{U_m}q_{U_{m-1}}\cdots q_{U_1}q_{U_2}\cdots q_{U_{m-1}}q_{U_m},
\]
then $R_m\in q_I\cA_Mq_I$, where $I=\cap_i U_i$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Work first in the ordinary enveloping algebra \(A^{**}\), and put
\(I=U\cap W\).  We claim that
\[
        \mathcal B_W\mathcal B_U\subset s_I A^{**}s_U,\qquad
        \mathcal B_U\mathcal B_W\subset s_U A^{**}s_I .           \tag{5}
\]
By the definition of the algebras \(\mathcal B_\bullet\) and norm closure, it
is enough to check products of two coefficient operators, the first coming from
a finite fusion using only subsets of \(W\) and the second from one using only
subsets of \(U\).  DKEP's concrete identities for bounded vectors,
\(T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*\) and \(T_{\alpha\otimes\beta}=T_\beta T_\alpha\), give
\[
 (T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2})
 =
 T_{\xi_1\otimes\overline{\xi_2}\otimes\eta_1}^{\,*}T_{\eta_2}.   \tag{6}
\]
The non-empty finite fusion containing \(\xi_1,\xi_2\) decomposes into summands
\(\cH_Z\) with \(Z\subset W\), while the one containing \(\eta_1,\eta_2\)
decomposes into summands \(\cH_Y\) with \(Y\subset U\).  Since
\(\overline{\cH_Z}\cong\cH_Z\), the graph-product fusion rule of
Charlesworth--de Santiago--Hayes--Jekel--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Nelson
\cite[Theorem 5.4 and Proposition 5.5]{CDHJKN} decomposes the correspondence
containing \(\xi_1\otimes\overline{\xi_2}\otimes\eta_1\) into copies of
\(\cH_L\) with \(L\subset I\).  Therefore the first factor on the right of
(6) is supported by \(s_I\), while \(T_{\eta_2}\) is supported by \(s_U\), and
the first inclusion in (5) follows.  The second is obtained by taking adjoints.

Applying the support fact (3) with \(B=\mathcal B_W\), \(C=\mathcal B_U\), and
\(p=s_I\) gives
\[
        s_W(s_UA^{**}s_U)s_W\subset
        s_IA^{**}s_I.                                            \tag{7}
\]
Now cut by $p_{\rm nor}$.  The supports $s_U$ commute with $p_{\rm nor}$ by
DKEP Proposition 2.10, and (2) gives $q_U=p_{\rm nor}s_U$.  Thus (7) becomes
(4) inside $\cA_M=p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}$.  The palindromic assertion is
obtained by induction from $R_{k+1}=q_{U_{k+1}}R_kq_{U_{k+1}}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[finite palindromic boundary criterion]\label{lem:finite}
Let $N$ be a separable non-amenable finite factor.  Let
$X_1,\ldots,X_m$ be boundary pieces such that, with $q_i=q_{X_i}$,
\[
        R=q_mq_{m-1}\cdots q_1q_2\cdots q_{m-1}q_m\in q_0\cA_Nq_0. \tag{8}
\]
If $N$ is properly proximal relative to each $X_i$, then $N$ is properly
proximal.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Assume that $N$ is not properly proximal.  By DKEP's normal-bidual criterion
there is an $N$-central state $\omega$ on $\widetilde S(N)$ with
$\omega|_N=\tau$.  Fix $i$ and put $q=q_i$.  Since $q$ commutes with $N$ and
$JNJ$,
\[
        q^\perp\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)q^\perp\subset \widetilde S(N).
\]
If $\omega(q^\perp)>0$, compression by $q^\perp$ gives an $N$-central state on
$\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)$.  On $N_+$ it is dominated by
$\omega(q^\perp)^{-1}\tau$, hence is normal; since $N$ is a factor its
restriction is $\tau$, contradicting relative proper proximality.  Thus
$\omega(q_i^\perp)=0$ for every $i$.

Extend $\omega$ to a state $\bar\omega$ on $\cA_N$.  In the GNS representation
of $\bar\omega$, every $q_i$ fixes the cyclic vector.  Hence
$R=(q_1q_2\cdots q_m)^*(q_1q_2\cdots q_m)$ satisfies $\bar\omega(R)=1$.  Since
$0\le R\le q_0$, $\bar\omega(q_0)=1$.  DKEP's proof of Lemma 8.5 supplies a
natural $N$- and $JNJ$-bimodular u.c.p. map
\[
        \Theta:\mathbb B(L^2N)\longrightarrow q_0\cA_Nq_0,\qquad
        \Theta(x)=q_0xq_0\quad(x\in N).
\]
The range of $\Theta$ lies in $q_0\cA_Nq_0\subset\widetilde S(N)$, where
$\bar\omega$ agrees with the original central state $\omega$.  Therefore
$\bar\omega\circ\Theta$ is an $N$-central state on $\mathbb B(L^2N)$ extending
$\tau$, a Connes hypertrace.  This contradicts non-amenability of the finite
factor $N$.
\end{proof}

\section*{A relative Bass--Serre paradox}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:BS}
Let $N=P*_{B}Q$ with trace-preserving expectations and
$Q=B\bbarotimes A$.  Suppose that $A$ contains a trace-zero unitary $a$, and
that $P$ contains unitaries $w_1,w_2$ satisfying
\[
        E_B(w_i)=0,\qquad E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
Then $N$ is properly proximal relative to $X_B$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Use the reduced-word decomposition of $L^2(N)$ over $B$ and let $p$ and $q$ be
the projections onto the sums of reduced words whose first letter lies in
$L^2(P)\ominus L^2(B)$ and $L^2(Q)\ominus L^2(B)$, respectively.  Then
$1=e_B+p+q$.  If $R_x=Jx^*J$, direct inspection of reduced words gives, for
$x\in P\ominus B$,
\[
 [p,R_x]=R_xe_B-e_BR_x,\qquad [q,R_x]=0,
\]
and the analogous formulas with $p,P$ and $q,Q$ interchanged for
$x\in Q\ominus B$.  Together with the trivial commutation for $x\in B$, DKEP
Lemma 6.1 gives $p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)$.

Let $\varphi$ be an $N$-central state on $S_{X_B}(N)$ normal on $N$.  Left
multiplication by $w_i$ sends the $Q$-initial subspace into the $P$-initial
subspace, and the two images are orthogonal because $E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0$; hence
$w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p$.  Also $a\in Q\ominus B$, so $apa^*\le q$.
Centrality gives $2\varphi(q)\le\varphi(p)\le\varphi(q)$, so
$\varphi(p)=\varphi(q)=0$.

Finally $h=w_1a$ is a $B$-Haar unitary: for $n\ne0$, $h^n$ is a non-trivial
reduced alternating word, so $E_B(h^n)=0$.  Thus the projections
$h^ne_Bh^{-n}$ are pairwise orthogonal and have the same $\varphi$-value,
forcing $\varphi(e_B)=0$.  This contradicts $1=e_B+p+q$.  By
\cite[Theorem 6.2]{DKEP}, $N$ is properly proximal relative to $X_B$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Application to graph products}

Fix $v\in V$ and put $L_v=\Lk_\Gamma(v)$.  The graph product decomposes as
\[
        M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}
        \bigl(M_{L_v}\bbarotimes M_v\bigr).                    \tag{9}
\]
Let $C(v)=\{r\ne v:r\not\sim_\Gamma v\}$.  Since $\Gamma^c$ is connected,
$C(v)\ne\emptyset$.  If $C(v)$ contains distinct $r,t$, choose trace-zero
unitaries $u_r\in M_r$, $u_t\in M_t$ and set $w_1=u_r$, $w_2=u_t$.  If
$C(v)=\{r\}$, connectedness of $\Gamma^c$ and $|V|\ge3$ give
$s\in L_v$ with $r\not\sim_\Gamma s$; choose trace-zero unitaries
$u_r\in M_r$, $u_s\in M_s$ and set $w_1=u_r$, $w_2=u_su_r$.  In both cases
reduced-word considerations give
\[
        E_{M_{L_v}}(w_i)=0,\qquad E_{M_{L_v}}(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
The given trace-zero unitary in $M_v$ supplies the unitary $a$ in the second
free-product leg of (9).  Proposition \ref{prop:BS} therefore shows that $M$
is properly proximal relative to $X_{M_{L_v}}$ for every $v\in V$.

Enumerate $V=\{v_1,\ldots,v_m\}$.  Since no vertex belongs to its own link,
$\cap_i L_{v_i}=\varnothing$.  Lemma \ref{lem:intersection} gives
\[
 q_{L_{v_m}}\cdots q_{L_{v_1}}q_{L_{v_2}}\cdots q_{L_{v_m}}
 \in q_\varnothing\cA_Mq_\varnothing .
\]
Here $M_\varnothing=\C$, and $X_{M_\varnothing}$ is the compact boundary piece,
because $P_\varnothing$ is rank one and its left and right $M$-translates
generate the finite-rank operators.

It remains to verify the hypotheses of Lemma \ref{lem:finite}.  The algebra
$M$ is separable.  By \cite[Main Theorem 0.5]{CDHJKN}, factoriality can fail
only through a universal vertex, or through an exceptional non-edge pair
adjacent to every other vertex with both vertex algebras two-dimensional.
These would give respectively an isolated vertex or an isolated edge in
$\Gamma^c$, impossible because $\Gamma^c$ is connected and has at least three
vertices.  Hence $M$ is a factor.  Also $M$ is non-amenable: CDHJKN's
amenability characterization \cite[Proposition 6.3]{CDHJKN} would otherwise
force every non-edge of $\Gamma$ to be such an isolated exceptional pair,
again impossible for connected $\Gamma^c$ with at least three vertices.  Lemma
\ref{lem:finite} applies, and $M$ is properly proximal.
