\section*{Problem statement and interpretation}

Irreducible means not a non-trivial join, equivalently the complement
\(\Gamma^c\) is connected.  All traces are faithful normal traces.  For
\(U\subset V\), \(M_U\) denotes the graph product over the induced subgraph and
\(E_U:M\to M_U\) the canonical expectation.

We use the normal-bidual notation of Ding--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Peterson
(DKEP) \cite{DKEP}.  Put \(A_N=\mathbb B(L^2N)\) and
\[
        \cA_N=(A_N^{\sharp_J})^* ,
\]
the simultaneous \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-normal bidual.  For a boundary piece \(X\)
let \(q_X\) be the support of \((K_X(N)^{\sharp_J})^*\) in \(\cA_N\), and for
\(X=\mathbb K(L^2N)\) write \(q_0\).  Let
\[
 \widetilde S_X(N)=\{T\in\cA_N:[T,a]\in q_X\cA_Nq_X\hbox{ for all }a\in JNJ\}.
\]
For a separable finite factor \(N\), DKEP Lemma 8.5 says that proper
proximality relative to \(X\) is equivalent to non-existence of an
\(N\)-central state on \(\widetilde S_X(N)\) whose restriction to \(N\) is the
trace.

\section*{Normal supports and coefficients}

The following bookkeeping is used repeatedly.  Let \(p_N\in A_N^{**}\) be the
simultaneous normal support, so \(\cA_N=p_NA_N^{**}p_N\).

\begin{lemma}[normal support bookkeeping]\label{lem:support}
Let \(X\subset A_N\) be a DKEP boundary piece with \(N,JNJ\subset M(X)\).  Let
\(\bar q_X\) and \(\bar q_K\) be the ordinary open supports in \(A_N^{**}\) of
\(X\) and \(K_X(N)\).  Then
\[
       p_N\bar q_X=\bar q_Xp_N=p_N\bar q_K=\bar q_Kp_N .
\]
This common projection is \(q_X\).  For \(Y=X\) and \(Y=K_X(N)\),
\[
       (Y^{\sharp_J})^*=q_X\cA_Nq_X
       =\{T\in\cA_N:\phi(T)=0\hbox{ for every }
          \phi\in A_N^{\sharp_J}\hbox{ with }\phi|_Y=0\}.       \tag{2.1}
\]
Consequently every contractive approximate unit of \(X\) converges to \(q_X\)
in the weak* topology of \(\cA_N\); and, if \(T\in K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\), then
\[
       \phi|_X=0,\ \phi\in A_N^{\sharp_J}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad \phi(T)=0
\]
whenever the expression is defined in \(A_N^{**}\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
DKEP Proposition 2.10, applied to the non-unital inclusions
\(X,K_X(N)\subset A_N\), identifies \((Y^{\sharp_J})^*\) with the normal
support corner \(p_N\bar q_YA_N^{**}\bar q_Yp_N\); in particular
\(p_N\bar q_Y=\bar q_Yp_N\), and the corner is the annihilator of the
normal functionals vanishing on \(Y\).  It remains only to see that the two
annihilators coincide.  DKEP Theorem 5.6 says that \(K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\) is
detected by exactly the functionals in \(A_N^{\sharp_J}\) vanishing on \(X\);
DKEP use this in Lemma 8.5 in the form \(S_X(N)=S_{K_X(N)}(N)\) and
\(\widetilde S_X(N)=\widetilde S_{K_X(N)}(N)\).  Equivalently, the
\(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)-closure associated with \(X\) is the common closure of
\(X\) and \(K_X(N)\).  Thus a normal functional vanishes on \(X\) iff it
vanishes on \(K_X(N)\).  The support corners are therefore equal.  The
approximate-unit assertion is the usual open-support convergence in \(A_N^{**}\),
compressed by \(p_N\).
\end{proof}

For an expected inclusion \(B\subset N\) let
\[
        \cH_B={}_NL^2N_B\otimes_B{}_BL^2N_N .
\]

\begin{lemma}[subalgebra coefficients]\label{lem:coeff}
The coefficient boundary of \(\cH_B\) is DKEP's subalgebra boundary \(X_B\).
Moreover \(\overline{\cH_B}\cong\cH_B\) in the tracial case.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
DKEP Example 5.11 identifies \(X_B\) with the boundary generated by the
coefficients of \({}_NL^2N_B\): the coefficient of \(\widehat1\) is the Jones
projection \(e_B\).  For bounded vectors,
\(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\); hence coefficients of \(\cH_B\) lie in the
hereditary algebra generated by these \(e_B\)-coefficients.  Conversely
\(T_{\xi\otimes\widehat1}^{\,*}T_{\eta\otimes\widehat1}=T_\xi^{\,*}T_\eta\), so
all generators of \(X_B\) occur.  The map
\(\overline{\xi\otimes\eta}\mapsto J\eta\otimes J\xi\) gives the conjugate
identification.
\end{proof}

If \(Z\subset U\), then \(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_U}\), hence \(X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_U}\).

\begin{lemma}[direct sums and finite fusions]\label{lem:dsupport}
Let \(X\subset A_N\) be a boundary piece with \(N,JNJ\subset M(X)\).  If
\(\cK=\bigoplus_{j\in J}\cK_j\) is a direct sum of \(N\)-\(N\) correspondences
and \(X_{\cK_j}\subset X\) for every \(j\), then every bounded coefficient
\(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) of \(\cK\) belongs to \(K_X(N)\).

For \(U\subset V\), let \(\cF_U\) be the non-empty finite Connes fusions of
copies of
\[
        \cH_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M
\]
and of its conjugate.  Let \(\mathcal C_U\) be the linear span of coefficients
\(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) with \(\xi,\eta\) bounded vectors in finite direct sums of
members of \(\cF_U\).  Then \(\mathcal C_U\) is a self-adjoint algebra, and
\(X_{M_U}\) has a contractive approximate unit in the norm-closure of positive
coefficients \(T_\zeta^*T_\zeta\) with \(\zeta\) in finite direct sums of
members of \(\cF_U\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Apply DKEP Theorem 5.10 to \(N\)-\(N\) correspondences with left boundary
\(A_N=\mathbb B(L^2N)\) and right boundary \(X\).  For a finite set
\(F\subset J\), the finite sum \(\bigoplus_{j\in F}\cK_j\) is \(X\)-mixing,
because finite sums of mixing operators are mixing.  If \(P_F\) is the
coordinate projection and \(\xi\) is left- and right-bounded, then
\(P_F\xi\) is left- and right-bounded with
\(\|L_{P_F\xi}\|\le\|L_\xi\|\) and \(\|R_{P_F\xi}\|\le\|R_\xi\|\).  The net
\(P_F\xi\) converges to \(\xi\) in Hilbert norm.  The closedness argument in
the proof of DKEP Theorem 5.10 (using DKEP Proposition 5.1: the mixing
operators form a strong right module and are strongly closed under uniformly
bounded approximants) therefore shows that every bounded \(\xi\in\cK\) is
\(X\)-mixing.  By Theorem 5.10(3) and Proposition 5.9,
\(T_\xi\in K_{A_N,X}(N,N)\), hence \(T_\xi^*T_\xi\in K_X(N)\).  Polarization
gives \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\in K_X(N)\).

For coefficients \(a=T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2}\) and \(b=T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2}\),
the identity \(T_{\alpha\otimes\beta}=T_\beta T_\alpha\) gives
\[
        ab=T_{\xi_1\otimes\overline{\xi_2}\otimes\eta_1}^{\,*}T_{\eta_2},
\]
and adjoints are immediate; hence \(\mathcal C_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra.  By
Lemma \ref{lem:coeff}, \(X_{M_U}\) is the hereditary algebra generated by the
positive coefficients of \(\cH_U\).  For a finite sum
\(s=\sum T_{\xi_i}^*T_{\xi_i}\) of such generators, the functions
\(g_\varepsilon(t)=t(t+\varepsilon)^{-1}\) form the usual directed
contractive approximate unit.  Since polynomials with zero constant term
approximate \(g_\varepsilon^{1/2}\) uniformly on \([0,\|s\|]\), each
\(g_\varepsilon(s)\) is a norm limit of elements \(c^*c\) with
\(c\in\mathcal C_U\).  The product formula above writes such \(c^*c\) as one
positive coefficient from a finite direct sum of finite fusions.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[intersection of boundary supports]\label{lem:intersection}
Let \(q_U=q_{X_{M_U}}\).  For \(U,W\subset V\), with \(I=U\cap W\),
\[
        0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_I .                                  \tag{3.1}
\]
Consequently, if \(I=\cap_{i=1}^mU_i\), then
\[
q_{U_m}q_{U_{m-1}}\cdots q_{U_1}q_{U_2}\cdots q_{U_{m-1}}q_{U_m}
   \in q_I\cA_Mq_I .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Write \(H_R=H_R(V,V)\) for the graph-product correspondence of CDHJKN.  Their
Theorem 5.4 identifies \(H_R\) with
\(L^2M\otimes_{M_R}L^2M\), and Proposition 5.5 gives
\[
        H_A\otimes_M H_B\cong
        \bigoplus_{C\subset A\cap B} H_C^{\oplus m(A,B,C)} .       \tag{3.2}
\]
Since \(\overline{H_A}\cong H_A\), induction on the number of factors in
(3.2) shows: a finite fusion whose labels are \(A_1,\ldots,A_n\) is a direct
sum of \(H_C\)'s with \(C\subset A_1\cap\cdots\cap A_n\).

Let \(h=T_\xi^*T_\xi\) be a positive coefficient from finite direct sums of
\(\cF_W\), and \(k=T_\eta^*T_\eta\) one from finite direct sums of \(\cF_U\).
Then
\[
        hkh=T_{\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta}^{\,*}
             T_{\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta}.                    \tag{3.3}
\]
By the preceding label induction, the correspondence containing
\(\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta\) is a direct sum of \(H_C\)'s with
\(C\subset W\cap U=I\).  Lemma \ref{lem:dsupport}, together with
\(X_{M_C}\subset X_{M_I}\), implies \(hkh\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\).  Norm limits of
such \(h\)'s and \(k\)'s still give elements of \(K_{X_{M_I}}^{\infty,1}(M)\).

Choose the contractive approximate units \(h_\alpha\subset X_{M_W}\) and
\(k_\beta\subset X_{M_U}\) supplied by Lemma \ref{lem:dsupport}.  If
\(\phi\in A_M^{\sharp_J}\) vanishes on \(X_{M_I}\), Lemma \ref{lem:support}
gives
\[
        \phi(h_\alpha k_\beta h_\alpha)=0 .
\]
In \(A_M^{**}\), \(h_\alpha\to\bar q_W\) and \(k_\beta\to\bar q_U\) strongly*
and boundedly; hence, after first taking \(\alpha\) and then \(\beta\),
\[
        \phi(\bar q_W\bar q_U\bar q_W)=0 .
\]
Compressing by \(p_M\) and using \(p_M\bar q_Y=\bar q_Yp_M\), this says that
all normal functionals annihilating \(X_{M_I}\) vanish on \(q_Wq_Uq_W\).
By (2.1), \(q_Wq_Uq_W\in q_I\cA_Mq_I\).  It is a positive contraction, so it
is dominated by \(q_I\), proving (3.1).

The palindromic statement follows by induction.  If the product for
\(U_1,\ldots,U_{m-1}\) is a positive contraction supported by
\(q_J\), \(J=\cap_{i<m}U_i\), then compression by \(q_{U_m}\) is dominated by
\(q_{U_m}q_Jq_{U_m}\), hence is supported by \(q_{J\cap U_m}\) by (3.1).
\end{proof}

\section*{From finitely many relative boundaries to the compact boundary}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:finite}
Let \(N\) be a separable non-amenable finite factor.  Let
\(X_1,\ldots,X_m\) be boundary pieces and put \(q_i=q_{X_i}\).  Assume
\[
        R:=q_mq_{m-1}\cdots q_1q_2\cdots q_{m-1}q_m\in q_0\cA_Nq_0. \tag{4.1}
\]
If \(N\) is properly proximal relative to every \(X_i\), then \(N\) is properly
proximal.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Suppose not.  DKEP Lemma 8.5 gives an \(N\)-central state \(\omega\) on
\(\widetilde S(N)\) with \(\omega|_N=\tau\).  Fix \(i\), write \(q=q_i\), and
note that \(q\) commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\).  Hence
\(q^\perp\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)q^\perp\subset\widetilde S(N)\).  If
\(\omega(q^\perp)>0\), then
\[
        y\mapsto \omega(q^\perp yq^\perp)/\omega(q^\perp)
\]
is \(N\)-central on \(\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\).  On \(N_+\) it is dominated by
\(\omega(q^\perp)^{-1}\tau\), so its restriction to \(N\) is normal; because
\(N\) is a factor and the functional is central, after normalization it equals
\(\tau\).  This contradicts relative proper proximality.  Thus
\(\omega(q_i^\perp)=0\) for all \(i\).

Extend \(\omega\) to a state on \(\cA_N\).  In its GNS representation each
\(q_i\) fixes the cyclic vector, and therefore \(\omega(R)=1\).  Since
\(0\le R\le q_0\), we have \(\omega(q_0)=1\).  Also
\(q_0\cA_Nq_0\subset\widetilde S(N)\), because commutators with \(JNJ\) remain
in the same compact corner.

For the compact boundary, the natural map used in the proof of DKEP Lemma 8.5
is an \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodular u.c.p. map
\[
        \Theta_0:\mathbb B(L^2N)\longrightarrow
        (\mathbb K(L^2N)^{\sharp_J})^*=q_0\cA_Nq_0
\]
with \(\Theta_0(1)=q_0\) and \(\Theta_0(x)=xq_0\) for \(x\in N\).  Thus
\(\psi(T)=\omega(\Theta_0(T))\) is an \(N\)-central state on
\(\mathbb B(L^2N)\), and
\(\psi(x)=\omega(xq_0)=\omega(x)=\tau(x)\) for \(x\in N\).  This is a Connes
hypertrace, contradicting non-amenability of \(N\).
\end{proof}

\section*{A relative Bass--Serre paradox}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:BS}
Let \(N=P*_{B}Q\) with trace-preserving expectations and \(Q=B\bbarotimes A\).
Assume that \(A\) has a trace-zero unitary \(a\), and that \(P\) has unitaries
\(w_1,w_2\) satisfying
\[
        E_B(w_i)=0,\qquad E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
Then \(N\) is properly proximal relative to \(X_B\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H_P=L^2P\ominus L^2B\), \(H_Q=L^2Q\ominus L^2B\).  The AFP normal form is
\[
 L^2N=L^2B\oplus\!\!\bigoplus_{n\ge1,\ i_1\ne\cdots\ne i_n}
 H_{i_1}\otimes_B\cdots\otimes_B H_{i_n},\quad i_j\in\{P,Q\}.
\]
Let \(p\) and \(q\) be the projections onto the summands whose first letter is,
respectively, \(P\) and \(Q\); then \(1=e_B+p+q\).  If \(x\in P\ominus B\),
right multiplication by \(x\) preserves the first letter except on the vacuum
and on one-letter \(P\)-words.  Hence
\[
 [p,JxJ]=(1-e_B)JxJ e_B-e_BJxJ(1-e_B),\qquad [q,JxJ]=0,
\]
and these operators lie in the \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodule generated by
\(e_B\), hence in \(X_B\).  The analogous formulas with \(p\) and \(q\)
interchanged hold for \(x\in Q\ominus B\), while \(B\) commutes with \(p,q\).
Since \(B+(P\ominus B)+(Q\ominus B)\) generates \(N\), DKEP Lemma 6.1 gives
\(p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)\).

Let \(\varphi\) be an \(N\)-central state on \(S_{X_B}(N)\) whose restriction
to \(N\) is normal.  Left multiplication by \(w_i\in P\ominus B\) sends
\(qL^2N\) into \(pL^2N\).  Moreover \(q\,w_1^*w_2\,q=0\), since
\(w_1^*w_2\in P\ominus B\) sends a \(Q\)-first reduced word to a \(P\)-first
word.  Thus \(w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p\).  Similarly
\(a p a^*\le q\), because \(a\in Q\ominus B\).  Centrality gives
\[
        2\varphi(q)\le\varphi(p)\le\varphi(q),
\]
so \(\varphi(p)=\varphi(q)=0\).

The unitary \(h=w_1a\) is \(B\)-Haar: every non-zero power of \(h\) is an
alternating reduced word in centered \(P\)- and \(Q\)-letters, so
\(E_B(h^n)=0\).  Therefore
\(h^ne_Bh^{-n}\) are pairwise orthogonal projections.  They all have the same
\(\varphi\)-value; hence that value is \(0\), and \(\varphi(e_B)=0\).  This
contradicts \(\varphi(1)=\varphi(e_B+p+q)=1\).  By DKEP Theorem 6.2, \(N\) is
properly proximal relative to \(X_B\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Application to graph products}

Fix \(v\in V\), put \(L_v=\Lk_\Gamma(v)\), and use the standard graph-product
amalgamated free product decomposition
\[
        M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}
        (M_{L_v}\bbarotimes M_v).                                \tag{6.1}
\]
Let \(C(v)=\{r\ne v:r\not\sim_\Gamma v\}\).  Since \(\Gamma^c\) is connected,
\(C(v)\ne\emptyset\).  If \(C(v)\) contains distinct \(r,t\), choose trace-zero
unitaries \(u_r,u_t\) and set \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_t\).  If \(C(v)=\{r\}\), then
connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge3\) give \(s\in L_v\) with
\(s\not\sim_\Gamma r\); set \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_su_r\).  Graph-product reduced
words give
\[
        E_{M_{L_v}}(w_i)=0,\qquad E_{M_{L_v}}(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
The trace-zero unitary in \(M_v\) is the unitary \(a\) in (6.1).  Proposition
\ref{prop:BS} shows that \(M\) is properly proximal relative to
\(X_{M_{L_v}}\) for every \(v\in V\).

Enumerate \(V=\{v_1,\ldots,v_m\}\).  Since no vertex lies in its own link,
\(\cap_iL_{v_i}=\emptyset\).  Lemma \ref{lem:intersection} gives the
palindromic product of the \(q_{L_{v_i}}\)'s in
\(q_{\emptyset}\cA_Mq_{\emptyset}\).  Here \(M_\emptyset=\C\), hence
\(X_{M_\emptyset}=\mathbb K(L^2M)\).

Finally, a trace-zero unitary implies \(\dim M_v\ge2\).  CDHJKN Theorem E
(equivalently their Theorem 2.4 for join-irreducible graphs) implies that this
join-irreducible graph product with at least three vertices is a factor.  By
CDHJKN Proposition 6.3 it is not amenable: otherwise any non-edge would be an
isolated two-point component of \(\Gamma^c\), contradicting connectedness of
\(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge3\).  Thus \(M\) is a separable non-amenable finite
factor.  Lemma \ref{lem:finite}, applied to the finite family
\(\{X_{M_{L_v}}:v\in V\}\), proves that \(M\) is properly proximal.
