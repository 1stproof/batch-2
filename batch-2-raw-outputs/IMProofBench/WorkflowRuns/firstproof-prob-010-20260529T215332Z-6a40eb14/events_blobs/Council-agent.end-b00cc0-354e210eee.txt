The strategy is right but two steps are compressed in a way I think a careful reader (or referee) will flag. Here is how I would re-organize, with the specific sub-lemmas to insert.

**(1) Algebraic intermediate before any bidual.** Prove first, at the $C^*$-level,
$$\mathbb{K}_{X_W}(M)\cdot \mathbb{K}_{X_U}(M)\cdot \mathbb{K}_{X_W}(M)\;\subset\; \mathbb{K}_{X_{\mathcal L}}(M).\qquad(*)$$
DKEP's $T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi$ gives this on triples of coefficient generators; the $M$-bimodule closure on the LHS absorbs into translates of elementary tensors on the RHS via DKEP's translation rules $aT_\xi b=T_{a\xi b}$. This is genuinely a separate statement from Example 5.11 (which is about a single $\mathcal H_U$) and is worth writing once, even briefly.

**(2) Bidual upgrade.** This is where "passing to hereditary closures and then to the normal bidual" hides an actual approximate-unit argument. Pick a positive contractive approximate unit $(e_\alpha)$ in $\mathbb K_{X_W}(M)$; then $e_\alpha\to q_W$ weak-* in $\mathcal A_M$. For $T\in q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U$, weak-* approximate $T=\lim_\beta S_\beta$ with $S_\beta\in\mathbb K_{X_U}(M)$. Multiplication is *separately* weak-* continuous, so by iterated (not joint) limits
$$q_WTq_W=\lim_\beta\lim_{\alpha,\gamma}\,e_\alpha S_\beta e_\gamma,$$
with each $e_\alpha S_\beta e_\gamma\in \mathbb K_{X_W}\mathbb K_{X_U}\mathbb K_{X_W}\subset \mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal L}}(M)$ by $(*)$. The triple limit therefore lies in $\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal L}}(M)^{**}=q_{\mathcal L}\mathcal A_Mq_{\mathcal L}$. Note that nothing in this step uses $[q_W,q_U]=0$ — which is precisely why the lemma is phrased as a corner inclusion rather than $q_W\wedge q_U=q_{U\cap W}$. I would move the caution from your research notes about non-commuting supports into the body of the proof, because it is the reason for this organization.

**(3) The $Z\subset U\cap W$ inclusion.** This is the step I would most encourage you to expand, because it is doing all the graph-product-specific work and the citation is bearing a lot of weight. One self-contained paragraph: in CDHJKN's reduced-word picture, a vector in the centered part of $L^2M\otimes_{M_W}L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\otimes_{M_W}L^2M$ is (after the standard simplifications) a reduced word whose outer two ends are centered modulo $M_W$ and whose middle tube is centered modulo $M_U$. The simultaneous $M$-stabilizer of such a word is the intersection of the link conditions imposed by every letter type appearing, and this intersection sits inside $U\cap W$ by the graph-product centralizer calculus. Then $X_{\mathcal L}\subset X_{M_{U\cap W}}$ and so $q_{\mathcal L}\le q_{U\cap W}$.

**Sanity checks** that will catch most slip-ups here: (i) for $U\subset W$, $(*)$ should give $X_{\mathcal L}\subset X_U$, matching $U\cap W=U$; (ii) for your actual application $\bigcap L_{v_i}=\varnothing$, the conclusion $q_{\mathcal L}\le q_\varnothing=q_0$ is exactly what Lemma 2 needs — worth verifying at the level of the empty-word sector.

With (1)–(3) written out, I think the lemma is rigorous as stated. The bilinear weak-* continuity in (2) is the most subtle ingredient and I would not skip it; the $Z\subset U\cap W$ paragraph in (3) is where I would invest the most additional words.
