I would not leave this lemma as written. The inclusion is very plausible, but the step

\[
\text{products of coefficients } \Longrightarrow
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W\subset q_{\mathcal L}\mathcal A_Mq_{\mathcal L}
\]

is exactly where noncommutativity of support projections matters. It needs an abstract “support-sandwich” lemma; otherwise it reads like an implicit meet/commutation argument.

I would replace the proof by first proving a C\(^*\)-level containment. Let  
\(\mathfrak K_U=(\mathbb K_{X_{M_U}}(M)^\sharp_J)\), so that
\(\mathfrak K_U^{**}=q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U\). Prove:

\[
\overline{\operatorname{span}}\,\mathfrak K_W\mathfrak K_U\mathfrak K_W
\subset \mathfrak K_{U\cap W}. \tag{*}
\]

This is the real graph-product/fusion statement. DKEP’s coefficient formula gives

\[
\mathfrak K_W\mathfrak K_U\mathfrak K_W
\subset \mathfrak K_{\mathcal L},
\qquad
\mathcal L=\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U\otimes_M\mathcal H_W,
\]

after taking the hereditary/norm-closed coefficient boundary. Then CDHJKN fusion gives \(\mathcal L\) as a direct sum of \(\mathcal H_Z=L^2M\otimes_{M_Z}L^2M\) with \(Z\subset U\cap W\). To conclude \(\mathfrak K_{\mathcal L}\subset \mathfrak K_{U\cap W}\), explicitly cite/prove the monotonicity lemma:

> If \(B\subset C\subset M\) are expected subalgebras, then \(X_B\subset X_C\).

For Jones pieces this follows from \(e_B\le e_C\) and hereditary closure of the boundary piece generated by \(e_C\). Thus \(Z\subset U\cap W\) implies \(X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_{U\cap W}}\). Direct sums of correspondences give closed sums of coefficient boundaries, so \(\mathfrak K_{\mathcal L}\subset\mathfrak K_{U\cap W}\). This proves \((*)\).

Then insert the missing general lemma:

> **Support-sandwich lemma.** Let \(A\) be the ambient C\(^*\)-algebra and let \(B,C,D\subset A\) be hereditary C\(^*\)-subalgebras with support projections \(p_B,p_C,p_D\in A^{**}\). If  
> \[
> \overline{\operatorname{span}}\, C B C\subset D,
> \]
> then
> \[
> p_C(p_BA^{**}p_B)p_C\subset p_DA^{**}p_D.
> \]

Proof: for \(x\in p_BA^{**}p_B=B^{**}\), approximate \(x\) ultraweakly by bounded \(b_i\in B\). For \(c,d\in C\), \(cb_id\in CBC\subset D\), hence \(cxd\in D^{**}\). Let \(c,d\) run through an approximate unit of \(C\); the ultraweak limit is \(p_Cxp_C\in D^{**}\).

Applying this with \(B=\mathfrak K_U\), \(C=\mathfrak K_W\), \(D=\mathfrak K_{U\cap W}\) gives precisely

\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W
\subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A_Mq_{U\cap W}.
\]

So: the inclusion can be made rigorous, but as written it skips the crucial support-sandwich passage. Add that lemma and the Jones-piece monotonicity statement; then the palindromic finite-boundary upgrade avoids any assumption that the \(q_U\)’s commute.
