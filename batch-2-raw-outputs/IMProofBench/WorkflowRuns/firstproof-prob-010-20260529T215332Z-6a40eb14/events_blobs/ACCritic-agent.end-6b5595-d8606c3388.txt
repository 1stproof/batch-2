## Remaining serious issues

### 1. The normal-bidual support paragraph is still not fully rigorous

The proof’s support paragraph remains an essential bottleneck. It states that for a boundary piece \(X\),
\[
p_N\bar q_X=\bar q_Xp_N,\qquad
(X^{\sharp_J})^*=q_X\mathcal A_Nq_X,\quad q_X=p_N\bar q_X,
\]
where earlier \(q_X\) was defined as the support of \((K_X(N)^{\sharp_J})^*\), not of \((X^{\sharp_J})^*\). This creates a notational and logical conflation: one must distinguish the support of \(X\) from the support of \(K_X(N)\), then prove they agree.

DKEP provides the ingredients: \(K_X^{\infty,1}\) is the \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)-closure of both \(X\) and \(K_X\), and Theorem 5.6 gives annihilator characterizations involving \(XB(L^2N)X\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) But `answer.tex` does not carefully prove the equality of the normal-bidual supports. It asserts it in the same paragraph where it is needed. This is not merely expository: Lemma 3 later uses approximate units of \(X_{M_U}\) to converge to the support projection \(q_{X_{M_U}}\) that was defined via \(K_{X_{M_U}}\).

A rigorous proof should introduce two projections, say \(q(X)\) and \(q(K_X)\), prove \(q(X)=q(K_X)\) via Theorem 5.6, and only then use approximate units of \(X\) to approximate the DKEP support projection.

### 2. Lemma `direct sums and finite fusions` remains underproved

The direct-sum part now cites the closure argument from DKEP Theorem 5.10. DKEP Theorem 5.10 does contain exactly the kind of closure argument involving bounded vectors, projection cutdowns, and strong-module closedness. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) However, the answer still does not spell out enough details to make the lemma standalone.

Specific missing points:

- The proof does not explicitly specify the right boundary piece being used, presumably \(Y=\mathbb B(L^2N)\), when applying DKEP Theorem 5.10.
- The proof treats finite-coordinate truncations as a sequence \(\xi_n\), but the direct sum is indexed by an arbitrary set \(J\), so one should use a net over finite subsets unless countability is justified.
- It says finite-coordinate truncations of a bounded vector are bounded and converge to the vector. This is true, but the proof should explicitly check both left- and right-boundedness and the uniform operator-norm bounds.
- The conversion from “all bounded vectors are \(X\)-mixing” to \(T_\xi^*T_\xi\in K_X(N)\) requires the exact orientation of DKEP Theorem 5.10 and Proposition 5.9. Proposition 5.9 distinguishes \(K_{X,Y}\), \(K_X\), and \(K_X^{\infty,1}\), so this should not be left implicit. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

This lemma is essential for the support-intersection argument, so these are not harmless omissions.

### 3. The boundary-support intersection lemma is still the central unresolved gap

The revised Lemma `intersection of boundary supports` is much better than the previous version, but it is still not a complete proof.

The coefficient identity
\[
hkh
=
T_{\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta}^{*}
T_{\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta}
\]
is correct. The use of CDHJKN fusion rules is also plausible: their Proposition 5.5 gives fusion decompositions indexed by subsets of intersections. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150)) However, the answer applies this to arbitrary finite fusions of \(\mathcal H_W\), \(\overline{\mathcal H_W}\), \(\mathcal H_U\), and \(\overline{\mathcal H_U}\) in a single sentence. A real proof must induct over the number of fusion factors and track how each label changes under repeated applications of CDHJKN Proposition 5.5.

More importantly, the passage
\[
\phi(h_\alpha k_\beta h_\alpha)=0
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
q_Wq_Uq_W\in q_I\mathcal A_Mq_I
\]
still depends on the unresolved normal-support identification discussed above. The proof says that approximate units converge to ordinary open supports in \(A_M^{**}\), then compresses by \(p_M\), and then invokes an annihilator description. This is exactly the delicate nonmultiplicative normal-bidual point that needed a fully explicit proof. DKEP does provide support-corner machinery, but the answer does not prove the required compatibility of ordinary supports, \(p_M\), \(X\), and \(K_X(M)\) in sufficient detail. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

Thus Lemma `intersection` remains insufficiently justified. Since this lemma is the bridge from relative proper proximality of the link-boundary pieces to ordinary proper proximality, this is a fatal gap.

### 4. The finite-boundary criterion is improved but still slightly compressed

The replacement of \(q_0Tq_0\) by DKEP’s canonical u.c.p. map is an improvement. DKEP’s proof of Lemma 8.5 indeed constructs natural \(M\)-bimodular u.c.p. maps from \(\mathbb B(L^2M)\) into the normal compact corner. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

However, the proof should still explicitly verify:

- that \(\Theta_0(x)=xq_0\) for \(x\in N\), or at least that \(\omega(\Theta_0(x))=\tau(x)\);
- that \(q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0\subset \widetilde S(N)\), so \(\omega\) is actually defined on the range of \(\Theta_0\);
- that \(\psi(T)=\omega(\Theta_0(T))\) is \(N\)-central using the \(N\)-bimodularity of \(\Theta_0\) and centrality of \(\omega\).

These are probably repairable, but the argument is still not fully written.

### 5. The Bass–Serre proposition is plausible but not fully proved

The revised Bass–Serre argument gives more detail, but it still relies on “reduced-word inspection” for several essential facts:

- \([p,JxJ]\) and \([q,JxJ]\) are finite sums of \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-translates of \(e_B\);
- these commutators satisfy the hypotheses of DKEP Lemma 6.1 for an ultraweakly dense generating set;
- \(q w_1^*w_2 q=0\);
- \(h=w_1a\) is \(B\)-Haar and the projections \(h^ne_Bh^{-n}\) are pairwise orthogonal.

All of these are believable, but they are not fully demonstrated. DKEP Theorem 6.2 characterizes proper proximality through the nonexistence of central states on \(S_X(M)\), so this proposition is a key input, not a side calculation. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) A complete proof should include the AFP normal-form decomposition and check these operator equalities on each reduced-word summand.

### 6. Final graph-product application is mostly correct, conditional on earlier lemmas

The combinatorial construction of \(w_1,w_2\) from complement connectedness is correct. The AFP decomposition
\[
M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}(M_{L_v}\bar\otimes M_v)
\]
is standard, though it should be cited or proved. The final factoriality and nonamenability argument is supported by CDHJKN Theorem E / Theorem 2.4 and Proposition 6.3. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150))

But this final section depends critically on Lemma `intersection` and Lemma `finite`, so the proof is not complete unless those are fully rigorous.
