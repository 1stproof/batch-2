\section*{Problem statement and interpretation}

Irreducible means not a non-trivial graph join; equivalently the complement
graph \(\Gamma^c\) is connected.  All traces are faithful normal traces.  For
\(U\subset V\), \(M_U\) denotes the graph product over the induced subgraph and
\(E_U:M\to M_U\) its canonical trace-preserving expectation.

We use the notation of Ding--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Peterson \cite{DKEP}.
For a finite algebra \(N\), put \(A_N=\mathbb B(L^2N)\) and
\[
        \cA_N=(A_N^{\sharp_J})^*,
\]
the simultaneous \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-normal bidual.  If \(X\) is an
\(N\)-boundary piece, \(K_X(N)\) is DKEP's associated compact hereditary
algebra and \(q_X\in\cA_N\) is the support of
\((K_X(N)^{\sharp_J})^*\).  For \(X=\mathbb K(L^2N)\) write \(q_0\).  Set
\[
 \widetilde S_X(N)=\{T\in\cA_N:[T,a]\in q_X\cA_Nq_X\hbox{ for all }
 a\in JNJ\}.
\]
For a separable finite factor, DKEP Lemma 8.5 says that proper proximality
relative to \(X\) is equivalent to the absence of an \(N\)-central state on
\(\widetilde S_X(N)\) restricting to the trace on \(N\).

\section*{Boundary supports and coefficients}

Let \(A=A_N=\mathbb B(L^2N)\), and let \(p\in A^{**}\) be DKEP's
simultaneous normal support projection for the two commuting multiplier actions
of \(N\) and \(JNJ\); thus \(\cA_N=pA^{**}p\).  If
\(Y\subset A\) is hereditary and invariant under both actions, let \(r_Y\in
A^{**}\) be its ordinary open support projection.

\begin{lemma}[support bookkeeping]\label{lem:support}
For such a hereditary subalgebra \(Y\),
\[
        r_Yp=pr_Y,\qquad
        (Y^{\sharp_J})^*=(pr_Y)\cA_N(pr_Y).
\]
If \((e_\lambda)\) is a contractive approximate unit of \(Y\), then
\(e_\lambda\to r_Y\) strongly* in \(A^{**}\), and \(pe_\lambda p\to pr_Y\)
ultra-strongly* in \(\cA_N\).  If \(X\) is a boundary piece with
\(N,JNJ\subset M(X)\), then
\[
        pr_X=pr_{K_X(N)}=q_X .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For one normal multiplier action, DKEP Section 2.3 identifies the normal bidual
with \(p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\).  If \(B\subset A\) is an invariant
C\(^*\)-subalgebra with ordinary support \(r_B\), the same section shows that
\(r_B\) commutes with \(p_{\rm nor}\) and that
\((B^\sharp)^*=r_Bp_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}r_B\).  Applying this to the
two commuting actions and using DKEP Proposition 2.10, which realizes the
simultaneous bidual as the intersection of the two one-normal biduals inside
\(A^{**}\), gives the displayed commutation and corner formula.  The convergence
of approximate units is the usual strong* convergence to the open support,
compressed by \(p\).

It remains to compare \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\).  DKEP Proposition 2.4 says that a
functional in \(A_N^{\sharp_J}\) which vanishes on a subspace also vanishes on
the smallest subspace containing it which is strong both as an \(N\)-bimodule
and as a \(JNJ\)-bimodule.  DKEP Proposition 3.6, together with the preceding
Corollary 3.2 there, identifies this strong closure of a boundary piece \(X\)
with
\[
        K_X^{\infty,1}(N)=\overline X^{\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}}
        =\overline{K_X(N)}^{\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}},
\]
and in particular every element of \(K_X(N)\) lies in that strong closure of
\(X\).  Thus the functionals in \(A_N^{\sharp_J}\) annihilating \(X\) are
exactly those annihilating \(K_X(N)\) (the converse is immediate from
\(X\subset K_X(N)\)).  By the corner formula, equal normal annihilators are
equivalent to equality of the compressed support projections, so
\(pr_X=pr_{K_X(N)}=q_X\).
\end{proof}

For an expected inclusion \(B\subset N\), let
\[
        \cH_B={}_NL^2N_B\otimes_B{}_BL^2N_N .
\]
The cyclic vector \(\zeta_B=\widehat 1\otimes_B\widehat 1\) has coefficient
\(T_{\zeta_B}^*T_{\zeta_B}=e_B\).

\begin{lemma}[subalgebra fusion]\label{lem:subalg}
The boundary piece \(X_B\) of DKEP Example 3.4 is hereditarily generated by the
positive coefficients of \(\cH_B\).  Moreover \(\cH_B\cong\overline{\cH_B}\),
and every bounded coefficient of every direct sum of copies of \(\cH_B\) is in
\(K_{X_B}(N)\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Since \(e_B\) is the coefficient of \(\zeta_B\), and DKEP Example 3.4 defines
\(X_B\) as the hereditary algebra obtained from the \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-translates
of \(e_B\mathbb B(L^2N)e_B\), \(X_B\) is hereditarily generated by positive
coefficients of \(\cH_B\).

Let \(H={}_NL^2N_B\).  DKEP Example 5.11 identifies the left coefficient
boundary of \(H\) with \(X_B\), while the right boundary is
\(\mathbb B(L^2B)\) (the case \(B\subset B\) in Example 3.4).  Thus, by DKEP
Theorem 5.10, \(H\) is mixing relative to
\(X_B\times \mathbb B(L^2B)\), and its conjugate is mixing relative to
\(\mathbb B(L^2B)\times X_B\).  The elementary bounded tensors
\(\xi\otimes_B\bar\eta\) are \(N\)-\(N\) cyclic in \(\cH_B\), and
\[
        T_{\xi\otimes\bar\eta}=T_{\bar\eta}T_\xi
        \in X_B\,\mathbb B(L^2N)\,X_B
\]
for the cyclic family supplied by Theorem 5.10.  Applying Theorem 5.10 again to
\(\cH_B\) gives \(T_\alpha\in K_{X_B,X_B}(N,N)\) for every bounded
\(\alpha\in\cH_B\); DKEP Proposition 5.9 then gives
\(T_\alpha^*T_\beta\in K_{X_B}(N)\).  For a direct sum, take the union of the
cyclic families in the summands, supported in one coordinate.  It is cyclic for
the direct sum and satisfies the same \(T_\xi\)-condition, so Theorem 5.10 and
Proposition 5.9 give the assertion for all bounded vectors in the direct sum.
Finally,
\(\overline{\xi\otimes_B\eta}\mapsto J\eta\otimes_BJ\xi\) gives the stated
self-conjugacy by traciality.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[finite fusion calculus]\label{lem:fusion}
Let \(X\) be a boundary piece for \(N\), with \(N,JNJ\subset M(X)\).  Finite
Connes fusions, conjugates, and arbitrary direct sums of \(N\)-\(N\)
correspondences which are mixing relative to \(X\times X\) are again mixing
relative to \(X\times X\); in particular all their bounded coefficients lie in
\(K_X(N)\).

For \(U\subset V\), put \(H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\), and let \(\cC_U\) be
the linear span of coefficients of finite direct sums of non-empty finite
fusions of \(H_U\) and \(\overline{H_U}\).  Then \(\cC_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra,
and \(X_{M_U}\) has a contractive approximate unit in the norm closure of
\(\{c^*c:c\in\cC_U\}\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The first assertion is a direct use of DKEP Theorem 5.10.  For a direct sum,
the cyclic set required in condition (1) of that theorem is the union of the
corresponding cyclic sets in the summands.  For a fusion, the elementary
tensors from the cyclic sets are cyclic and satisfy
\(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\); since \(X\) is hereditary,
\(X\mathbb B(L^2N)X\subset X\), so condition (1) again holds.  Theorem 5.10
then gives \(T_\zeta\in K_{X,X}\) for every bounded vector, and DKEP
Proposition 5.9 gives the coefficient conclusion.

For the algebra calculation, if \(c_1=T_\xi^*T_\eta\) and
\(c_2=T_\alpha^*T_\beta\) are coefficients of finite fusions \(K,L\), then,
using \(T_{\bar\eta}=T_\eta^*\),
\[
 c_1c_2=T_{\xi\otimes\bar\eta\otimes\alpha}^{\,*}T_\beta,          \tag{2.1}
\]
where the two vectors are viewed in the finite direct sum
\((K\otimes_N\bar K\otimes_N L)\oplus L\).  Adjoints are handled by conjugating
the correspondence.  Hence \(\cC_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra.

By Lemma \ref{lem:subalg}, \(X_{M_U}\) is hereditarily generated by positive
coefficients of \(H_U\).  If \(s\) is a finite positive sum of such generators,
then \(g_\varepsilon(s)=s(s+\varepsilon)^{-1}\) is in the hereditary algebra
generated by \(s\), and the net of these elements, over finite sums \(s\) and
\(\varepsilon\downarrow0\), is a contractive approximate unit for \(X_{M_U}\).
Polynomial approximation to \(g_\varepsilon^{1/2}\), with zero constant term,
puts \(g_\varepsilon(s)\) in the norm closure of \(c^*c\)'s with
\(c\in\cC_U\).
\end{proof}

If \(Z\subset U\), then \(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_U}\), hence \(X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_U}\).

\begin{lemma}[intersection of boundary supports]\label{lem:intersection}
Let \(q_U=q_{X_{M_U}}\).  For \(U,W\subset V\), with \(I=U\cap W\),
\[
        0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_I .                                  \tag{3.1}
\]
Consequently, if \(I=\cap_{i=1}^m U_i\), then
\[
q_{U_m}q_{U_{m-1}}\cdots q_{U_1}q_{U_2}\cdots q_{U_{m-1}}q_{U_m}
   \in q_I\cA_Mq_I .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The graph-product bimodule decomposition and fusion rule of
\cite[Theorem 5.4 and Proposition 5.5]{CDHJKN} give
\[
        H_A\otimes_M H_B\cong
        \bigoplus_{C\subset A\cap B}H_C^{\oplus m(A,B,C)} .        \tag{3.2}
\]
Since \(H_A\cong\overline{H_A}\), induction gives that any finite fusion with
labels \(A_1,\ldots,A_n\) decomposes as a direct sum of \(H_C\)'s with
\(C\subset A_1\cap\cdots\cap A_n\).

Let \(h\in\overline{\{c^*c:c\in\cC_W\}}\) and
\(k\in\overline{\{d^*d:d\in\cC_U\}}\).  For \(h,k\) before taking the norm
closures, formula (2.1) writes \(hkh\) as a finite sum of coefficients of
finite fusions whose labels are all \(W\)'s and \(U\)'s; by (3.2) these are
direct sums of \(H_C\)'s with \(C\subset I\).  Lemma \ref{lem:fusion} and
\(X_{M_C}\subset X_{M_I}\) then give
\[
        hkh\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M).                                   \tag{3.3}
\]
Norm closedness gives (3.3) for the displayed closures.

Now take the limits in the ordinary bidual \(A=\mathbb B(L^2M)^{**}\), where
the canonical copy of \(\mathbb B(L^2M)\) is multiplicative.  Choose
contractive approximate units \(h_\alpha\subset X_{M_W}\) and
\(k_\beta\subset X_{M_U}\) from the above positive cones.  If \(r_R\) denotes
the ordinary support of \(X_{M_R}\), and \(r_{K_I}\) that of
\(K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\), then \(h_\alpha\to r_W\) and \(k_\beta\to r_U\) strongly*.
By (3.3),
\(h_\alpha k_\beta h_\alpha\in r_{K_I}Ar_{K_I}\).  This hereditary corner is
strongly* closed, so first letting \(\beta\) and then \(\alpha\) tend to
infinity gives
\[
        r_Wr_Ur_W\in r_{K_I}Ar_{K_I}.
\]
The element is a positive contraction, hence \(0\le r_Wr_Ur_W\le r_{K_I}\).
Compressing by the simultaneous normal support \(p\) and using Lemma
\ref{lem:support},
\[
        q_Wq_Uq_W=pr_W\,pr_U\,pr_W=p(r_Wr_Ur_W)p\le pr_{K_I}p=q_I .
\]
The palindromic assertion follows by induction from (3.1).
\end{proof}

\section*{From finitely many relative boundaries to the compact boundary}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:finite}
Let \(N\) be a separable non-amenable finite factor.  Let
\(X_1,\ldots,X_m\) be boundary pieces, \(q_i=q_{X_i}\), and suppose
\[
        R:=q_mq_{m-1}\cdots q_1q_2\cdots q_{m-1}q_m\in q_0\cA_Nq_0. \tag{4.1}
\]
If \(N\) is properly proximal relative to each \(X_i\), then \(N\) is properly
proximal.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Assume not.  DKEP Lemma 8.5 gives an \(N\)-central state \(\omega\) on
\(\widetilde S(N)\) with \(\omega|_N=\tau\).  Fix \(i\) and put \(q=q_i\).
Since \(q\) commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\),
\[
 [q^\perp yq^\perp,a]=q^\perp[y,a]q^\perp=0,\qquad a\in JNJ,
\]
for \(y\in\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\); hence \(q^\perp yq^\perp\in\widetilde S(N)\).
If \(\omega(q^\perp)>0\), compression by \(q^\perp\) gives an \(N\)-central
state on \(\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\).  Its restriction to \(N\) is a non-zero
central normal positive functional, hence a scalar multiple of \(\tau\), which
contradicts relative proper proximality.  Thus \(\omega(q_i^\perp)=0\) for all
\(i\).

Extend \(\omega\) to a state on \(\cA_N\).  In the GNS representation each
\(q_i\) fixes the cyclic vector, so \(\omega(R)=1\).  Since (4.1) is a positive
contraction in the corner \(q_0\cA_Nq_0\), \(0\le R\le q_0\); hence
\(\omega(q_0)=1\).  DKEP Lemma 8.5 supplies the canonical \(N\)- and
\(JNJ\)-bimodular u.c.p. map
\[
        \Theta_0:\mathbb B(L^2N)\to q_0\cA_Nq_0,\qquad
        \Theta_0(x)=xq_0\quad (x\in N).
\]
Then \(\psi(T)=\omega(\Theta_0(T))\) is an \(N\)-central state on
\(\mathbb B(L^2N)\) with \(\psi|_N=\tau\), i.e. a Connes hypertrace.  This
contradicts non-amenability of the finite factor \(N\).
\end{proof}

\section*{A relative Bass--Serre paradox}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:BS}
Let \(N=P*_{B}Q\) with trace-preserving expectations and \(Q=B\bbarotimes A\).
Assume that \(A\) has a trace-zero unitary \(a\), and that \(P\) has unitaries
\(w_1,w_2\) satisfying
\[
        E_B(w_i)=0,\qquad E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
Then \(N\) is properly proximal relative to \(X_B\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H_P=L^2P\ominus L^2B\) and \(H_Q=L^2Q\ominus L^2B\).  The AFP normal form
is
\[
 L^2N=L^2B\oplus\!\!\bigoplus_{n\ge1,\ i_1\ne\cdots\ne i_n}
 H_{i_1}\otimes_B\cdots\otimes_B H_{i_n},\quad i_j\in\{P,Q\}.
\]
Let \(p\) and \(q\) be the projections onto the summands whose first letter is
\(P\), respectively \(Q\); then \(1=e_B+p+q\).  If \(x\in P\ominus B\), right
multiplication by \(x^*\) (that is, \(JxJ\)) preserves the first letter except that \(L^2B\) is sent
to a one-letter \(P\)-word and a one-letter \(P\)-word may acquire an
\(L^2B\)-component.  Hence, checking the three classes \(L^2B\), first-letter
\(P\), and first-letter \(Q\),
\[
 [p,JxJ]=(1-e_B)JxJe_B-e_BJxJ(1-e_B),\qquad [q,JxJ]=0.             \tag{5.1}
\]
The two terms in (5.1) are \(N,JNJ\)-translates of \(e_B\), hence lie in
\(X_B\).  Interchanging \(P\) and \(Q\) gives the analogous formula for
\(x\in Q\ominus B\), and elements of \(B\) commute with \(p,q\).  Since these
subspaces generate \(N\), DKEP Lemma 6.1 gives \(p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)\).

Let \(\varphi\) be an \(N\)-central state on \(S_{X_B}(N)\) whose restriction to
\(N\) is normal.  For \(\eta\in qL^2N\), left multiplication by a centered
element of \(P\) prepends a \(P\)-letter, so \(w_iqL^2N\subset pL^2N\).  Moreover
\(w_1^*w_2\in P\ominus B\), and left multiplication by such an element sends
first-letter \(Q\) words into first-letter \(P\) words; hence
\(q\,w_1^*w_2\,q=0\).  Therefore the ranges \(w_1qL^2N\) and \(w_2qL^2N\) are
orthogonal and
\[
        w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p .
\]
Likewise \(a\in Q\ominus B\), so \(a p a^*\le q\).  Centrality gives
\(2\varphi(q)\le\varphi(p)\le\varphi(q)\), whence \(\varphi(p)=\varphi(q)=0\).

The unitary \(h=w_1a\) is \(B\)-Haar: every non-zero power of \(h\) is an
alternating reduced word in centered \(P\)- and \(Q\)-letters, so
\(E_B(h^n)=0\) for \(n\ne0\).  Thus \(h^ne_Bh^{-n}\) are pairwise orthogonal
and all have the same \(\varphi\)-value; hence \(\varphi(e_B)=0\).  But then
\[
        1=\varphi(1)=\varphi(e_B+p+q)=0,
\]
a contradiction.  By DKEP Theorem 6.2, \(N\) is properly proximal relative to
\(X_B\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Application to graph products}

Fix \(v\in V\) and put \(L_v=\Lk_\Gamma(v)\).  The graph-product reduced-word
model gives the standard amalgamated free product decomposition
\[
        M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}
        (M_{L_v}\bbarotimes M_v).                                \tag{6.1}
\]
Let \(C(v)=\{r\ne v:r\not\sim_\Gamma v\}\).  Since \(\Gamma^c\) is connected,
\(C(v)\ne\emptyset\).  If \(C(v)\) contains distinct \(r,t\), choose trace-zero
unitaries \(u_r,u_t\) and set \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_t\).  If \(C(v)=\{r\}\), then
connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge3\) give \(s\in L_v\) with
\(s\not\sim_\Gamma r\); set \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_su_r\).  In both cases the
graph-product reduced-word calculus gives
\[
        E_{M_{L_v}}(w_i)=0,\qquad E_{M_{L_v}}(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
A trace-zero unitary in \(M_v\) supplies the unitary \(a\) in (6.1), so
Proposition \ref{prop:BS} shows that \(M\) is properly proximal relative to
\(X_{M_{L_v}}\) for every \(v\).

Enumerate \(V=\{v_1,\ldots,v_m\}\).  Since no vertex belongs to its own link,
\(\cap_i L_{v_i}=\emptyset\).  Lemma \ref{lem:intersection} puts the associated
palindromic product in \(q_\emptyset\cA_Mq_\emptyset\).  But
\(M_\emptyset=\C\), so \(X_{M_\emptyset}=\mathbb K(L^2M)\).

Finally, CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 implies that a join-irreducible graph product with
\(|V|\ge3\) and trace-zero vertex unitaries is a diffuse full factor.  A diffuse
amenable finite factor is the hyperfinite II\(_1\) factor and is not full, so
\(M\) is non-amenable.  It is separable because the graph is finite and the
vertices are separable.  Lemma \ref{lem:finite}, applied to the finite family
\(\{X_{M_{L_v}}:v\in V\}\), proves that \(M\) is properly proximal.
