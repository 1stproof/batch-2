### 2. Boundary support calculus

This is still the main problematic part.

#### 2.1 The support paragraph is mostly sourced, but does not solve the multiplication problem

The paragraph about \(p_{\rm nor}\), ordinary support projections, and nonunital inclusions is closer to DKEP than before. DKEP does describe \(p_{\rm nor}\), the identification \((A^\sharp)^*\cong p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\), and the inclusion for a hereditary subalgebra \(B\subset A\) using the support of \(B^{**}\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

But DKEP also explicitly warns that the canonical map
\[
A\to (A^\sharp)^*
\]
is **not generally a \(*\)-homomorphism**. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) This warning is directly relevant to the new proof, because the proof later multiplies images of coefficient maps in \(\mathcal A_N\) and assumes those products behave like concrete operator composition.

#### 2.2 The “normal left ideal” \(\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}\) is not adequately defined

The proof introduces a weak-* closed normal left ideal \(\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}\subset \mathcal A_N\) “generated by the coefficient maps \(T_\xi\).” But DKEP defines \(T_\xi\) first as concrete bounded operators \(T_\xi\in B(L^2M,L^2N)\), and the formula
\[
T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi
\]
is a formula for concrete operator composition. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The revised proof does not rigorously explain:

- how the concrete \(T_\xi\)’s are embedded into the normal bidual \(\mathcal A_N\);
- why their products in \(\mathcal A_N\) agree with concrete products;
- why polar decomposition in \(\mathcal A_N\) applies to these \(T_\xi\)’s;
- why the right support of this newly defined ideal is exactly the DKEP support projection \(q_{\mathcal H}\).

These are not minor details. They are exactly where DKEP’s nonmultiplicativity warning becomes relevant.

#### 2.3 The key inclusion \(\mathcal L_{\mathcal K}\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}\subset \mathcal L_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\mathcal K}\) is unproved

The proof asserts
\[
\mathcal L_{\mathcal K}\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}
\subset
\mathcal L_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\mathcal K}.
\tag{2}
\]

The generator-level identity \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\) only proves something for products of two concrete coefficient maps. It does **not** prove the inclusion for products of arbitrary elements of the weak-* closed left ideals. An element of \(\mathcal L_{\mathcal K}\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}\) has products of the form
\[
(aT_\eta)(bT_\xi),
\]
with arbitrary \(a,b\in\mathcal A_N\). The middle factor \(b\) is not handled by the concrete fusion formula.

The cited DKEP Corollary 5.2 and Proposition 5.9 do not state this normal-bidual left-ideal multiplication theorem. Corollary 5.2 concerns separate continuity in the \(X\)-\(Y\) topology for a different object, while Proposition 5.9 characterizes relative compactness of concrete rectangular operators. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

Thus equation (2) remains an essential unproved lemma.

#### 2.4 The proof of Lemma `coefficient support sandwich` is not valid as written

The proof says:

\[
q_{\mathcal K}q_{\mathcal H}\in \mathcal L_{\mathcal K}\mathcal L_{\mathcal H},
\]
hence by (2)
\[
q_{\mathcal K}q_{\mathcal H}\in \mathcal A_Nq_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\mathcal K}.
\]

But this depends entirely on the unproved inclusion (2). Moreover, if \(\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}\) is a weak-* closed left ideal, then \(\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}=\mathcal A_Nq_{\mathcal H}\), so \(\mathcal L_{\mathcal K}\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}\) is much larger than the span of products of concrete coefficient maps. The generator fusion formula does not control this whole product.

This is a fatal gap. The sandwich lemma is the bridge from relative boundary supports to graph-product fusion. Without it, the proof of the intersection lemma does not go through.

#### 2.5 The twofold fusion conclusion is especially suspect

The previous draft used a triple-fusion/coefficient-product heuristic. The revision now claims the support sandwich is controlled by the twofold fusion
\[
\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U.
\]

But products of coefficient corners naturally look like
\[
(T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2})(T_{\xi_3}^*T_{\xi_4}),
\]
which are controlled by a triple correspondence, not immediately by a twofold one. The new left-ideal argument is intended to bypass this, but because the left-ideal multiplication step is unproved, the twofold-fusion support bound is not justified.

#### 2.6 Direct sums are still only sketched

The proof says that if \(\mathcal H=\oplus_j\mathcal H_j\), then \(q_{\mathcal H}\) is dominated by the join of the \(q_{\mathcal H_j}\)’s, with off-diagonal coefficients controlled by \(2\times2\) positivity and infinite sums by truncation.

For finite sums this is plausible. For infinite direct sums, however, “truncating bounded vectors” is not automatically valid in the relevant operator norm/topology: Hilbert-norm convergence of vector truncations does not by itself imply norm convergence of the corresponding coefficient operators \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\). A proof is needed. Since CDHJKN’s decompositions may involve infinite multiplicities, this is another nontrivial missing support lemma.

DKEP does define coefficient boundary pieces for correspondences, but it does not provide in Example 5.11 the direct-sum support assertion in the form used here. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

### 3. Intersection of graph-product boundary corners

Conditional on the support-sandwich lemma, the use of CDHJKN fusion rules is plausible. CDHJKN Theorem 5.4 decomposes the relevant \(L^2(M)\)-bimodules over subgraph algebras, and Proposition 5.5 gives the fusion rule with summands indexed by \(W\subset U_1\cap U_2\). ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

But because the support-sandwich lemma and direct-sum support lemma are not rigorously proved, Lemma `intersection of graph-product boundary corners` remains unproved.

This is fatal: the later palindromic compact-corner inclusion depends entirely on this lemma.

### 4. Finite palindromic boundary criterion

This section is improved.

The operator-system issue from the previous draft is now addressed by extending \(\omega\) to a state \(\bar\omega\) on the ambient von Neumann algebra \(\mathcal A_N\). That is the right fix.

The compression argument is mostly sound: since \(q_i\) commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\), one gets
\[
q_i^\perp \widetilde S_{X_i}(N)q_i^\perp\subset \widetilde S(N).
\]
The domination argument on \(N_+\) also correctly gives normality of the compressed restriction.

One minor gap remains: to show \(\omega\circ\Theta\) extends \(\tau\), the proof should explicitly state that DKEP’s map satisfies
\[
\Theta(x)=q_0xq_0 \quad (x\in N),
\]
not merely \(\Theta(1)=q_0\). DKEP’s Lemma 8.5 proof does discuss the natural u.c.p. maps from \(B(L^2M)\) into the normal-bidual compact corner, so this is probably fixable. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

This section is not the main obstruction.

### 5. Relative Bass–Serre paradox

This section is plausible but still somewhat compressed.

The reduced-word projections \(p,q\) and the commutator formulas with right multiplication are standard and believable. DKEP Lemma 6.1 indeed allows checking the commutator condition on an ultraweakly generating subalgebra. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The inequalities
\[
w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p,
\qquad
apa^*\le q
\]
are also plausible under the stated \(B\)-orthogonality assumptions. The \(B\)-Haar unitary argument with \(h=w_1a\) is sound.

A fully polished proof would spell out the reduced-word verification in more detail, but I do not regard this as the fatal issue.

### 6. Application to graph products

This section is mostly correct, conditional on the unresolved boundary support machinery.

The amalgamated free-product decomposition
\[
M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}}
(M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}\bar\otimes M_v)
\]
is standard for graph products.

The construction of \(w_1,w_2\) in the two cases for \(C(v)\) is correct. In the singleton case \(C(v)=\{r\}\), connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge3\) indeed give \(s\in \operatorname{Lk}(v)\) with \(r\not\sim s\).

The final factoriality and nonamenability checks are also supported by CDHJKN. Main Theorem 0.5 gives the stated factoriality criterion, and Proposition 6.3 gives the amenability characterization. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743)) The connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) with at least three vertices excludes both a universal vertex obstruction and an isolated exceptional nonedge component.

Thus the graph-theoretic final step is fine if the preceding boundary intersection lemma is available. But that lemma is not rigorously established.

## Final assessment

The revision fixes some earlier formal issues, especially in the palindromic criterion, but the proof is still not complete. The central unresolved problem is the new normal-left-ideal support mechanism:

1. The ideal \(\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}\) is not rigorously constructed inside \(\mathcal A_N\).
2. The identification of its right support with \(q_{\mathcal H}\) is not proved.
3. The product inclusion
   \[
   \mathcal L_{\mathcal K}\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}
   \subset
   \mathcal L_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\mathcal K}
   \]
   is not justified by the cited DKEP results and is much stronger than the concrete generator fusion formula.
4. The resulting coefficient support sandwich, and hence the graph-product boundary-corner intersection lemma, remain unproved.

Since this support calculus is essential to the proof, `answer.tex` is not yet a complete rigorous solution.
