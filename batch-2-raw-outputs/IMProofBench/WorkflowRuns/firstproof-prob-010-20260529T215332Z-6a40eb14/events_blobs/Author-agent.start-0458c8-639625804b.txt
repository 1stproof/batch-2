### Resolution of previous concerns

**1. Support comparison \(X\) vs. \(K_X(N)\).**  
The previous draft had an insufficiently justified assertion that \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) have the same normal-bidual support. The revised proof now uses the column \(\binom SR\), DKEP Corollary 3.2, and the simultaneous projection criterion from DKEP Proposition 2.4 to obtain common cuts for \(S,R\in K_X^L\), then shows
\[
d_\alpha S^*R d_\beta=(Sd_\alpha)^*(Rd_\beta)\in X A_N X\subset X.
\]
This is the right mechanism. The argument now adequately proves equality of the simultaneous-normal annihilators, hence equality of the compressed supports \(p_Nr_X=p_N\rho_X=q_X\).

**2. Fusion/direct-sum stability.**  
The revised Lemma `finite fusion calculus` now explicitly checks DKEP Theorem 5.10(1): cyclic vectors with \(T_\xi\in XA_NX\) remain cyclic under direct sums, conjugation, and Connes fusion, using
\[
T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi,\qquad T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*.
\]
This addresses the earlier criticism. One minor wording point: the map \(\xi\mapsto T_\xi\) is not linear in general, so “finite sums are absorbed by finite direct sums” should not be read literally for arbitrary complex linear combinations. But \(\mathcal T_U\) is defined as a linear span, and the proof only needs closure under products term-by-term, so this is not a fatal issue.

**3. Boundary-support intersection.**  
The revised proof now computes products in the ordinary bidual \(A^{**}\), not in the nonmultiplicative normal-bidual image. The monomial bookkeeping is explicit:
\[
T_{\alpha_1}^*T_{\alpha_2}T_{\beta_1}^*T_{\beta_2}T_{\alpha_3}^*T_{\alpha_4}
=
T_{\alpha_4\otimes\bar\alpha_3\otimes\beta_2\otimes\bar\beta_1\otimes\alpha_2\otimes\bar\alpha_1}.
\]
Together with the CDHJKN fusion rule, this correctly yields coefficients controlled by \(X_{M_{U\cap W}}\). The weak-* limiting argument with ordinary open supports \(r_U,\rho_U\), followed only afterwards by compression by \(p_M\), is now mathematically sound.

**4. Finite relative boundaries to compact boundary.**  
The revised draft now correctly notes that the extension of the central state to \(\mathcal A_N\) is used only for the GNS projection argument and is not claimed to be central. The final hypertrace is obtained through the original state on the compact corner and the canonical DKEP u.c.p. map. This addresses the operator-system nuance from the previous review.

**5. Bass–Serre paradox.**  
The AFP normal-form index has been corrected to \(i_j\ne i_{j+1}\). The commutator formulas for the first-letter projections are now sufficiently explained on reduced words, and the translation of DKEP Lemma 6.1 into the right-action convention is explicit. The paradoxical inequalities and the \(B\)-Haar unitary argument are valid.

**6. Graph-product application.**

I also brute-force checked the corresponding finite-graph combinatorial claims for all graphs up to six vertices; no counterexample appeared. The expectation computations onto \(M_{\Lk(v)}\) are standard reduced-word computations. The final use of CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 gives a diffuse full factor, and therefore nonamenability follows because the diffuse amenable finite factor is hyperfinite and not full.

The revision resolves the previous essential objections. I do not find any remaining mathematical gap that affects the validity of the proof, nor any LaTeX-contract violation.

#### DKEP notation and the normal-bidual setup

The definition of

\mathcal A_N=(\mathbb B(L^2N)^{\sharp_J})^*
\]
and of

No fatal issue here.

### 4. Lemma “ordinary support bookkeeping”

This is the first major technical bottleneck.

The initial discussion of one-normal support projections \(p_\ell,p_r\), and the warning not to multiply concrete operators after applying the normal-bidual embedding, is appropriate. DKEP explicitly notes that the canonical embedding into the normal bidual is generally not multiplicative. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The claimed formula
\[
(Y^{\sharp_J})^*=(p_Nr_Y)\mathcal A_N(p_Nr_Y)
\]
is plausible for hereditary \(Y\) with faithful multiplier actions, and DKEP’s support discussion before Remark 2.8 plus Proposition 2.10 goes in this direction. DKEP Proposition 2.10 gives the simultaneous-normal inclusion statement. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

However, the second half of the lemma is **not adequately justified**:

> “DKEP Corollary 3.2, in its one-sided form, identifies \(K_X^L\) with the simultaneous strong right \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-module generated by \(A_NX\).”

This is stronger than what the cited result visibly states. DKEP Corollary 3.2 says that if \(T_i\) are **uniformly bounded** and converge in the \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,2}\)-sense, then the limit belongs to the corresponding strong module. It does not by itself prove that every element of
\[
K_X^L=\overline{A_NX}^{\|\cdot\|_{\infty,2}}
\]
has uniformly bounded approximants, nor that \(K_X^L\) equals the strong right module generated by \(A_NX\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

This matters because the proof then applies the projection criterion to arbitrary \(S,R\in K_X^L\) and obtains cuts
\[
Sd_{k,j},\ Rd_{k,j}\in A_NX.
\]
That implication is not established. Without it, the proof that every \(\sharp_J\)-normal functional vanishing on \(X\) also vanishes on \(K_X(N)\) is incomplete. Consequently, the claimed equality
\[
q_X=p_Nr_X=p_N\rho_X
\]
is not rigorously proved.

This is an **essential gap**, because the later intersection lemma depends directly on this support identity.

### 5. Lemma “subalgebra fusion”

The first part, identifying \(X_B\) with the hereditary algebra generated by coefficient operators coming from
\[
{}_NL^2N_B\otimes_B{}_BL^2N_N,
\]
is mostly sound. DKEP Example 5.11 does identify the coefficient boundary for \({}_NL^2N_B\) with the subalgebra boundary \(X_B\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The positive-generation argument using the hereditary support projection is acceptable.

The use of DKEP Theorem 5.10 to show that \(\mathcal H_B\) and its Hilbert direct sums are mixing relative to \(X_B\times X_B\) is plausible, though somewhat compressed. The direct-sum argument should explicitly note that Theorem 5.10 only requires a cyclic set of good bounded vectors, not that arbitrary finite linear combinations remain “good.” Since \(T_\xi=L_\xi^*R_\xi\) is quadratic in \(\xi\), careless linearity would be invalid; the text mostly avoids this, but the point should be stated more clearly. DKEP defines \(T_\xi\) as \(L_\xi^*R_\xi\), so this nonlinearity is real. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

Not fatal by itself, but the proof is terse.

### 6. Lemma “finite fusion calculus”

The stability of mixing under finite Connes fusion and conjugation follows naturally from
\[
T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi,\qquad T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*,
\]
which DKEP records. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The proof that \(\mathcal T_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra is essentially correct after expanding finite sums term-by-term. However, the sentence “finite sums are absorbed by finite direct sums” is potentially misleading because \(T_\xi\) is not linear in \(\xi\). Since \(\mathcal T_U\) is defined as a linear span, this imprecision does not by itself invalidate the argument, but it should be corrected.

The approximate-unit construction from \(\{t^*t:t\in\mathcal T_U\}\) is acceptable, assuming the preceding \(*\)-algebra claim.

No independent fatal issue here.

### 7. Lemma “intersection of boundary supports”

This is the central bridge from relative boundaries to the compact boundary.

The graph-product fusion input is correctly aligned with CDHJKN Theorem 5.4 and Proposition 5.5: finite fusions of
\[
H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M
\]
decompose into direct sums of \(H_C\)’s with \(C\) contained in the intersection of the labels. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

The algebraic product calculation
\[
T_{\alpha_1}^*T_{\alpha_2}T_{\beta_1}^*T_{\beta_2}
T_{\alpha_3}^*T_{\alpha_4}=T_\zeta
\]
for an appropriate fused vector \(\zeta\) is plausible.

There is a minor gap when passing from a direct sum over possibly several \(C\subset I\) to membership in \(K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\). The text says “Lemma \(\ref{lem:subalg}\) gives coefficient operators of such direct sums in \(K_{X_{M_C}}(M)\),” but if the decomposition contains several different \(C\)’s, one should explicitly split the vector into its finitely many \(C\)-components and use monotonicity \(X_{M_C}\subset X_{M_I}\). This is fixable.

The **fatal issue** is the final support-passage:
\[
q_R=pr_R=p\rho_R.
\]
This is imported from Lemma \(\ref{lem:support}\), whose proof is incomplete for the reason explained above. Therefore the conclusion
\[
0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_{U\cap W}
\]
is not rigorously established in the submitted proof.

Because this support-intersection lemma is essential for producing the compact-corner palindromic product, the main proof cannot be accepted as complete.

### 8. Lemma “from finitely many relative boundaries to the compact boundary”

Assuming its hypothesis
\[
R\in q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0,
\]
this lemma is largely sound.

The compression argument
\[
q^\perp \widetilde S_X(N)q^\perp\subset \widetilde S(N)
\]
is correct because \(q\) commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\). The normality of the compressed restriction to \(N\) follows from domination by a scalar multiple of the trace.

The GNS argument using the palindromic product is a good way to avoid non-normal meet issues.

The final hypertrace contradiction is also standard: a Connes hypertrace on \(\mathbb B(L^2N)\) extending the trace implies amenability of the finite factor. The use of the canonical u.c.p. map from DKEP Lemma 8.5 is consistent with the proof of that lemma. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

So this lemma is acceptable **conditional on** the compact-corner hypothesis, but that hypothesis is not rigorously obtained because of the earlier support gap.

The AFP first-letter decomposition and the projection argument are plausible.

The commutator computations for \(p\) and \(q\) are consistent with DKEP Lemma 6.1, which says that the set of elements whose commutators are \(X\)-mixing is a von Neumann subalgebra. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

This proposition is one of the stronger parts of the proof. I do not see a fatal error here, though some details about \(S_{X_B}(N)\) versus the relative proper proximality definition could be cited more explicitly.

### 10. Final graph-product application

The AFP decomposition at a vertex,
\[
M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}(M_{L_v}\bar\otimes M_v),
\]
is standard for graph products.

The combinatorial construction of \(w_1,w_2\) is correct:

- If \(C(v)\) has at least two vertices, choose two outside-link centered unitaries.
- If \(C(v)=\{r\}\), connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge3\) gives \(s\in L_v\) with \(s\not\sim r\), and \(u_r,u_su_r\) satisfy the required expectation conditions.

The intersection of all links is indeed empty, since each vertex is absent from its own link.

The identification \(X_{M_\emptyset}=\mathbb K(L^2M)\) follows from DKEP Example 3.4 when \(M_\emptyset=\mathbb C\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The use of CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 to prove that \(M\) is a diffuse full factor is valid in the tracial setting, because the centralizer condition is automatic. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

Thus the final application would be convincing if the boundary-support intersection lemma were rigorous. But because that lemma depends on the unproved support comparison, the final proof remains incomplete.
