# Round 4: boundary-corner intersection lemma

## Bottom line

The lemma
\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W\subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A_Mq_{U\cap W}
\]
is still salvageable, but the proof currently in `answer.tex` is not correct as written.  The flaw is the sentence claiming that DKEP's coefficient formula for the **triple** fusion
\[
\mathcal L=\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U\otimes_M\mathcal H_W
\]
directly gives the normal closure of the \(W\)-\(U\)-\(W\) sandwich corner.  A direct computation shows that the DKEP coefficient algebra of the triple fusion gives a five-factor expression, not the desired three-factor sandwich.

The clean repair is to use the **two-fold** fusion
\[
\mathcal K=\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U
\]
for the DKEP coefficient step.  CDHJKN then gives
\[
\mathcal K\cong \bigoplus_{Z\subset U\cap W}\mathcal H_Z^{\oplus k_\Gamma(W,U,Z)},
\]
so \(q_\mathcal K\le q_{U\cap W}\), and the desired inclusion follows.  If one wants to keep the triple fusion notation, CDHJKN implies \(q_{\mathcal H_W\otimes\mathcal H_U}=q_{\mathcal H_W\otimes\mathcal H_U\otimes\mathcal H_W}\), but this equality comes from the fusion rules, not from the raw DKEP coefficient formula.

## Explicit coefficient computation

Let \(B\subset M\), \(\mathcal H_B=L^2(M)\otimes_B L^2(M)\), and \(\zeta_B=\hat 1\otimes_B\hat 1\).  For \(a,b,x\in M\),
\[
T_{a\zeta_B b}(\hat x)
=\widehat{\,b^*E_B(a^*xa)b\,}.
\]
Equivalently,
\[
T_{a\zeta_B b}=L_{b^*}R_b\,e_B\,L_{a^*}R_a
\]
on \(L^2(M)\).  In particular \(T_{\zeta_B}=e_B\).  This matches DKEP Example 5.11: the coefficient boundary of \(\mathcal H_B\) is \(X_B\).

For the two-fold fusion \(\mathcal K=\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U\), if \(\xi,\xi'\in\mathcal H_W\) and \(\eta,\eta'\in\mathcal H_U\) are bounded, DKEP gives
\[
T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi,
\qquad
T_{\xi\otimes\eta}^*T_{\xi'\otimes\eta'}
=T_\xi^*(T_\eta^*T_{\eta'})T_{\xi'}.
\]
Thus the coefficient algebra of \(\mathcal K\) contains exactly the \(W\)-left-ideal / \(U\)-coefficient / \(W\)-left-ideal sandwich.  Passing through DKEP's hereditary closure and normal-bidual support construction gives
\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W
\subset q_\mathcal K\mathcal A_Mq_\mathcal K.
\]
More explicitly, if \(S_W=\operatorname{span}\{T_\xi:\xi\in\mathcal H_W\text{ bounded}\}\), then \(S_W^*S_W\) generates \(X_W\), so the normal left ideal generated by \(S_W\) has right support \(q_W\).  The displayed coefficient formula gives \(S_W^*X_US_W\subset X_\mathcal K\), and normalizing this inclusion gives the displayed corner inclusion.

For the triple fusion \(\mathcal L=\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U\otimes_M\mathcal H_W\), the cyclic vector \(\omega=\zeta_W\otimes\zeta_U\otimes\zeta_W\) has
\[
T_\omega=e_We_Ue_W,
\]
where \(e_U=e_{M_U}\) and \(e_W=e_{M_W}\).  The DKEP coefficient generator is \(T_\omega^*T_\omega=(e_We_Ue_W)^2\), not \(T_\omega\) by definition.  For translated bounded vectors
\[
\alpha=\xi\otimes\eta\otimes\theta,\qquad
\beta=\xi'\otimes\eta'\otimes\theta',
\]
with \(\xi,\xi',\theta,\theta'\in\mathcal H_W\) and \(\eta,\eta'\in\mathcal H_U\),
\[
T_\alpha^*T_\beta
=T_\xi^*T_\eta^*(T_\theta^*T_{\theta'})T_{\eta'}T_{\xi'}.
\]
This is \(W^*\,U^*\,(\text{\(W\)-coefficient})\,U\,W\), not a \(W\)-\(U\)-\(W\) sandwich.  Therefore the triple-fusion sentence in `answer.tex:74-88` does not by itself justify
\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W\subset q_\mathcal L\mathcal A_Mq_\mathcal L.
\]

## CDHJKN fusion-rule check

Apply CDHJKN Proposition 5.5 with \(V_1=V_2=V_3=V\), \(U_1=W\), and \(U_2=U\).  Since
\[
\mathcal H_S=L^2(M)\otimes_{M_S}L^2(M)=\mathscr H_S(V,V),
\]
one obtains
\[
\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U
\cong
\bigoplus_{Z\subset W\cap U}
\mathcal H_Z^{\oplus k_\Gamma(W,U,Z)}.
\]
For \(Z\subset I:=U\cap W\), \(X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_I}\): indeed \(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_I}\), and DKEP's description of \(X_B\) as the hereditary algebra generated by \(xJyJ e_B\) gives the inclusion.  Hence the coefficient boundary of \(\mathcal K\) is contained in \(X_{M_I}\), so \(q_\mathcal K\le q_I\).

Combining the two-fold DKEP coefficient inclusion with this CDHJKN containment gives the desired lemma:
\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W
\subset q_\mathcal K\mathcal A_Mq_\mathcal K
\subset q_I\mathcal A_Mq_I.
\]

Also, the triple boundary is contained in \(X_{M_I}\) as the current proof says: either apply Proposition 5.5 twice, or first decompose \(\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U\) as above and then fuse each \(\mathcal H_Z\) with \(\mathcal H_W\), yielding only summands \(\mathcal H_Y\) with \(Y\subset Z\subset I\).  Moreover, the empty relatively reduced word gives the \(Y=Z\) summand, so the triple and double fusions have the same coefficient support.  This is useful, but it is a CDHJKN consequence, not the direct DKEP coefficient calculation claimed in the current proof.
