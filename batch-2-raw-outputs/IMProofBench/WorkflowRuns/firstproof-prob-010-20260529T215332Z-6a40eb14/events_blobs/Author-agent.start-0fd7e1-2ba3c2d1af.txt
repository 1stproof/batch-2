## Referee report on the revised `answer.tex`

### Overall verdict

The revision is **substantially improved**, but I still **cannot certify it as a complete rigorous proof**. The main remaining obstruction is still the support calculation behind the boundary-corner intersection lemma. The author has addressed several earlier concerns, but the revised proof still relies on nontrivial identifications of DKEP boundary pieces, coefficient models, and normal-bidual supports that are asserted rather than fully proved.

### LaTeX contract

I ran `pdflatex` twice on the revised `answer.tex`. It compiles successfully to a 5-page PDF. The file uses exactly `\documentclass[12pt]{article}`, uses only the permitted `fullpage` package for layout, and I found no prohibited margin, spacing, or in-document font-size changes. So there is **no LaTeX-contract violation**.

## Paragraph-by-paragraph mathematical audit

### 1. Problem statement and interpretation

This section is acceptable. The author explicitly adopts the standard graph-product interpretation of irreducibility as nontrivial join-irreducibility, equivalently connected complement graph. That resolves the ambiguity in the problem statement.

### 2. DKEP normal-bidual setup

The general notation is mostly consistent with DKEP. However, the revised draft uses \(p_{\rm nor}\) later without defining it in the introduction. Since \(p_{\rm nor}\) is essential to the claimed formula \(q_X=p_{\rm nor}s_X\), it should be explicitly defined in the paper, not merely invoked.

The use of DKEP Lemma 8.5 for a separable finite factor is appropriate: the trivial action has ergodic center because the algebra is a factor. DKEP’s Lemma 8.5 indeed gives the normal-bidual criterion in terms of absence of a central state on \(\widetilde S_X(M)\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

### 3. Support formula \(q_X=q(X)\)

This is still a major weak point.

The proof states that for a boundary piece \(X\), Proposition 3.6 implies that \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) have the same \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)-closure, and since \(\sharp_J\)-functionals are continuous for this closure, they have the same annihilator in \(A_N^{\sharp_J}\). This is plausible but not fully justified as written.

DKEP Proposition 3.6 says that \(K_X^{\infty,1}\) is both the \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)-closure of \(X\) and of \(K_X\), and identifies it as a strong \(M\)-\(M\) and \(JMJ\)-\(JMJ\) bimodule. But `answer.tex` still needs to explain precisely why this implies equality of annihilators in \(A_N^{\sharp_J}\), and hence equality of normal-bidual support projections. This is an essential bridge, not a harmless notational point. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

Similarly, the formula
\[
(B^{\sharp_J})^*=q(B)\mathcal A_N q(B),\qquad q(B)=p_{\rm nor}s_B,
\]
is still not proved in sufficient detail. DKEP Proposition 2.10 gives inclusions/intersections of normal biduals for simultaneous normal categories, but the author’s precise ordinary-open-support cutdown formula requires a derivation. The revised text says it follows from the paragraph before DKEP Remark 2.8 plus Proposition 2.10, but it does not spell this out. This remains a gap because the entire boundary-corner intersection lemma depends on replacing \(q_U\) by \(p_{\rm nor}s_U\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

### 4. Coefficient model for \(X_{M_U}\)

The revision avoids the earlier assertion \(\mathcal B_U=K_{X_{M_U}}\), which is good. However, it replaces it with another nontrivial assertion:

> DKEP Examples 3.4 and 5.11 identify \(X_{M_U}\) with the hereditary algebra generated by coefficients \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) of bounded vectors in Hilbert amplifications of \(\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\).

This is plausible, but it is not literally stated by the cited examples in this form. DKEP Example 3.4 defines \(X_B\) using the corner \(e_BB(L^2M)e_B\), and Example 5.11 defines coefficient boundary pieces for correspondences; it also notes that a certain correspondence model recovers Example 3.4. But the present proof needs a precise proof that the basic-construction correspondence \(\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\), with Hilbert amplifications, gives exactly the DKEP \(X_{M_U}\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The sentence “the amplifications account for the whole corner \(e_{M_U}A_Me_{M_U}\)” is an important lemma, not a self-evident fact. It should be proved or cited precisely. Without it, the subsequent reduction to products of coefficient operators is not justified.

### 5. Infinite direct sums

The new direct-sum paragraph is an improvement over the prior draft. The use of finite-coordinate truncations and strong-* convergence of the coefficient operators is a plausible way to avoid the earlier incorrect norm-convergence claim.

However, this still needs more detail. In particular:

- One must verify that coordinate projections in the direct sum preserve bounded vectors and that the associated \(T_{\xi_F}\) satisfy \(T_{\xi_F}\to T_\xi\) strong-*.
- The off-diagonal coefficient support argument uses the rectangular support lemma but should explicitly identify the hereditary subalgebras to which the diagonal coefficients belong.
- The proof states the direct-sum fact for “coefficient algebras supported by \(s\),” but later uses it to conclude membership in \(sA^{**}s\), then applies a rectangular lemma originally stated for membership in concrete hereditary algebras. This mismatch should be fixed.

This is likely repairable, but as written it is still too compressed for a central lemma.

### 6. Boundary-corner intersection lemma

This remains the central unresolved issue.

The proof aims to establish
\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W\subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A_Mq_{U\cap W}.
\]

The CDHJKN fusion input is appropriate in spirit: \(\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\overline{\mathcal H_W}\otimes_M\mathcal H_U\) should decompose into summands \(\mathcal H_Z\) with \(Z\subset U\cap W\), by repeated use of the fusion rule. CDHJKN Proposition 5.5 indeed gives this type of rule. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

But the proof still has several gaps:

1. **Coefficient density in \(X_{M_U}\)** is not established. The proof says it is enough by norm closure to multiply two amplified coefficient operators, but that requires the amplified coefficients to be norm-linearly dense in \(X_{M_U}\). This is not proved.

2. **The product identity is used beyond its stated domain.** The identity
   \[
   (T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2})
   =
   T_{\xi_1\otimes\overline{\xi_2}\otimes\eta_1}^{\,*}T_{\eta_2}
   \]
   is standard for bounded vectors, but the proof must explicitly handle Hilbert amplifications and the relevant boundedness of the fused vector.

3. **The rectangular support lemma is applied in a stronger form than stated.** The lemma is stated for \(T^*T\in D\) and \(S^*S\in C\), where \(D,C\) are hereditary subalgebras. In the proof, the author only obtains
   \[
   T_\alpha^*T_\alpha\in s_IA^{**}s_I,\qquad
   T_{\eta_2}^*T_{\eta_2}\in s_UA^{**}s_U.
   \]
   To apply the stated lemma, one must either show these concrete operators lie in \(X_{M_I}\) and \(X_{M_U}\), respectively, or restate/prove the lemma in the stronger support-corner form. This is currently missing.

4. **The cut by \(p_{\rm nor}\)** depends on the unresolved support formula \(q_U=p_{\rm nor}s_U\). Thus even if the ordinary-envelope inclusion were accepted, the normal-bidual inclusion is not yet rigorously obtained.

Because Lemma \(\ref{lem:intersection}\) is essential for the finite palindromic criterion, this gap prevents certification of the whole proof.

### 7. Finite boundary criterion

This section is now mostly convincing.

The compression argument using \(q_i^\perp\) is sound in outline, and the author has fixed the normality point by domination by a scalar multiple of \(\tau\). The GNS argument with the palindromic product is also valid.

The final hypertrace step still cites “DKEP’s natural normal-bidual map” without giving the exact construction. DKEP’s proof of Lemma 8.5 does produce natural \(M\)-bimodular u.c.p. maps from \(B(L^2M)\) into the relevant normal-bidual corner, so this is probably acceptable if cited more precisely. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

This part is not the main obstruction.

### 8. Relative Bass–Serre proposition

This part is improved, but still somewhat compressed.

The reduced-word commutator formulas are plausible and match the free-product argument used in DKEP’s free-product section. DKEP Lemma 6.1 supports the general principle that checking commutators on an ultraweakly dense generating algebra suffices. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

However, the proof still does not fully spell out:

- why the displayed commutators are \(X_B\)-mixing in exactly the DKEP sense, rather than merely lying in \(X_B\);
- why the images \(w_1qL^2N\) and \(w_2qL^2N\) are orthogonal for all reduced \(Q\)-initial vectors;
- why \(apa^*\le q\) in the full AFP decomposition, including all word-length cases.

These are standard reduced-word verifications and probably repairable, but they are not written at full referee-level rigor.

### 9. Application to graph products

The graph-product application is mostly correct, assuming the previous machinery.

The AFP splitting over the link is standard. The construction of \(w_1,w_2\) from connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) is correct. The expectation computations onto \(M_{L_v}\) are plausible by graph-product reduced-word calculus.

The factoriality and nonamenability checks using CDHJKN are correct in spirit. Main Theorem 0.5 rules out factoriality failures except universal vertices or exceptional nonedge pairs, and Proposition 6.3 characterizes amenability by vertex amenability plus the isolated two-dimensional nonedge condition. Connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) with at least three vertices rules these out. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

## Previous concerns: addressed vs remaining

### Addressed or substantially improved

- The author no longer asserts \(\mathcal B_U=K_{X_{M_U}}\) as directly as before.
- The infinite-direct-sum issue is addressed more intelligently using strong-* convergence rather than operator-norm convergence.
- The finite palindromic criterion now includes the normality domination argument.
- The Bass–Serre argument has more reduced-word detail.
- The final CDHJKN factor/nonamenability argument is clear and essentially correct.

### Still unresolved

- The equality \(q_X=q(X)\), i.e. computing the DKEP normal-bidual support from the original boundary piece \(X\), remains underproved.
- The formula \(q(B)=p_{\rm nor}s_B\) is still not derived carefully enough from DKEP.
- The coefficient model \(X_{M_U}=\) “amplified coefficient boundary of \(\mathcal H_U\)” is asserted but not fully proved.
- The boundary-corner intersection lemma still depends on unproved coefficient-density and support-corner arguments.
- The rectangular support lemma is applied in a stronger form than stated.

### New or reintroduced issues

- \(p_{\rm nor}\) is used but not explicitly defined in the revised `answer.tex`.
- The proof now relies heavily on “Hilbert amplifications account for the whole corner \(e_{M_U}A_Me_{M_U}\),” but this is not proved.
- The support lemma (2) and its later use are not perfectly aligned: the lemma assumes membership in hereditary subalgebras, while the proof obtains only support-corner membership.

---

## Final assessment

The revised draft is closer to a viable proof, and much of the strategy is plausible. But as a strict referee, I cannot regard the central boundary-support intersection lemma as fully proved. Since that lemma is essential to pass from relative proper proximality over the vertex links to ordinary proper proximality, the solution is **not yet answer-ready**.
