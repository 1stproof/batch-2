## 3. Mathematical audit

### Problem interpretation

The interpretation of “irreducible” as “not a nontrivial graph join,” equivalently connected complement graph, is reasonable and is explicitly recorded. This satisfies the ambiguity-handling requirement.

### DKEP notation and normal-bidual setup

The definition of \(\widetilde S_X(N)\) is compatible with DKEP Lemma 8.5, but the text says that for a separable finite factor relative proper proximality is equivalent to absence of an \(N\)-central state whose restriction to \(N\) is **normal**. DKEP Lemma 8.5 states the restriction is exactly the trace; for a factor, an \(N\)-central normal state is a scalar multiple of the trace, so this is repairable, but the argument should explicitly include normalization.

### Lemma “ordinary support bookkeeping”

This is a central technical lemma, and it is not adequately justified.

The author claims that for a hereditary \(Y\subset A_N\),
\[
(Y^{\sharp_J})^*=r_Yp_NA_N^{**}p_Nr_Y,\qquad q_Y=p_Nr_Y.
\]
DKEP does give a one-normal support statement before Remark 2.8 and a simultaneous-normal Proposition 2.10, but `answer.tex` does not prove carefully that the two one-normal support projections commute in the needed ordinary-bidual representation or that the simultaneous corner is exactly obtained by multiplying the two supports. This is not a harmless detail: later products \(q_Wq_Uq_W\) are computed using this ordinary-bidual model.

The second claim,
\[
q_X=p_Nr_X=p_N\rho_X,
\]
also needs more proof. The author invokes DKEP Proposition 3.6 and Proposition 2.4, but the implication “same \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)-closure \(\Rightarrow\) same simultaneous-normal annihilator \(\Rightarrow\) same compressed support” is not written out with all hypotheses. This is an essential bridge, not a standard one-line consequence.

### Lemma “subalgebra fusion”

The first half, identifying \(X_B\) with the hereditary algebra generated by \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\), is supported by DKEP Example 5.11. The positive-generation argument using hereditary supports is plausible.

However, the later assertion that every coefficient \(T_\alpha\) of a bounded vector in **any direct sum of copies** of \(\mathcal H_B\) lies in \(K_{X_B}(N)\) is underproved. The proof says “the same cyclic-set argument applies to direct sums,” but for arbitrary direct sums one must check bounded-vector approximation and the applicability of DKEP Theorem 5.10 to infinite direct sums. This is not automatic from the union of cyclic sets unless one spells out the boundedness and closure argument.

### Lemma “finite fusion calculus”

This lemma is essential for the boundary-intersection proof, but its proof is too compressed.

The stability of mixing under finite Connes fusion and conjugation is plausible using \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\), but the proof should verify:

1. cyclicity of the chosen elementary tensors after Connes fusion;
2. boundedness of the fused vectors;
3. closure under arbitrary direct sums;
4. the precise passage from DKEP Theorem 5.10 to \(T_\xi\in K_X(N)\).

The statement that \(\mathcal T_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra also requires more care because \(\mathcal T_U\) is defined using coefficient operators of bounded vectors in finite direct sums of finite fusions. The product formula is correct for elementary bounded tensors, but the proof should explain how finite direct sums and linear combinations are handled.

### Boundary support intersection lemma

This is the most important and most fragile part of the proof. The desired conclusion
\[
0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_{U\cap W}
\]
is exactly what makes the finite-boundary argument work.

The use of the CDHJKN fusion rule is directionally correct. But the proof of the key claim
\[
abc\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M),\qquad a,c\in\mathcal P_W,\ b\in\mathcal P_U,
\]
has serious gaps.

In particular:

- The expansion of
  \[
  t_1^*t_1s^*st_2^*t_2
  \]
  into monomials of \(T\)-operators is plausible, but the proof does not rigorously track the corresponding Connes-fusion correspondence, boundedness of the resulting vector, or preservation of boundedness under the CDHJKN decomposition.

- The statement “each monomial is \(T_\zeta\)” is not enough: one must prove that \(\zeta\) is a bounded vector in a direct sum of \(H_C\)’s with \(C\subset I\), and then justify that DKEP’s relative compactness applies after passing through the decomposition.

- The proof relies on arbitrary direct sums of \(H_C\)’s being controlled by \(X_{M_I}\). This again depends on the direct-sum mixing closure that was not rigorously established earlier.

- The passage from concrete approximate units \(h_\alpha,k_\beta\) to ordinary-bidual support projections depends critically on the support bookkeeping lemma. Since that lemma is not fully proved, the final inequality \(q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_I\) remains unsupported.

Thus the intersection lemma is not yet a complete proof.

### Finite-boundary-to-compact-boundary lemma

This lemma is mostly sound, but some points need clarification.

The compression argument
\[
y\mapsto \omega(q^\perp yq^\perp)
\]
is plausible because \(q\) commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\). The normality of the compressed restriction to \(N\) is correctly argued by domination by the trace, and factor centrality forces the trace.

However:

- The proof extends \(\omega\) from the operator system \(\widetilde S(N)\) to \(\mathcal A_N\). This is legitimate by Hahn–Banach/Arveson extension, but then the argument must explicitly note which later values are independent of the chosen extension. In particular, \(R\) and \(q_0\) should be checked to lie in the original operator system, or the proof should say why the arbitrary extension suffices.

- The map \(\Theta_0:\mathbb B(L^2N)\to q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0\) is cited as coming from DKEP Lemma 8.5. DKEP does discuss natural u.c.p. maps into the compact corner, but the submitted proof should state the exact property needed: \(N\)-bimodularity and \(\Theta_0(a)=aq_0\) for \(a\in N\).

These are fixable, but still require more precision.

### Relative Bass–Serre proposition

The Bass–Serre paradox is plausible and resembles DKEP’s free-product argument, but several verification steps are too terse.

Specific issues:

- The commutator formula
  \[
  [p,JxJ]=(1-e_B)JxJe_B-e_BJxJ(1-e_B),\qquad [q,JxJ]=0
  \]
  is asserted after “checking” reduced words. This is an essential computation and should be written in detail, especially over a general amalgam \(B\).

- The deduction \(p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)\) uses DKEP Lemma 6.1. That lemma says the set of \(x\)’s for which the commutators are mixing is a von Neumann algebra. The proof must explicitly verify the hypotheses for a generating ultraweakly dense \(*\)-subalgebra of \(N=P*_BQ\). DKEP Lemma 6.1 is indeed the relevant tool, but the present invocation is too compressed. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

- The inequalities
  \[
  w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p,\qquad apa^*\le q
  \]
  are plausible, but the proof only states a first-letter argument. A rigorous AFP proof must check all reduced-word summands, including length-one summands and possible \(B\)-expectation cancellations.

- The final appeal to DKEP Theorem 6.2 should explicitly connect the nonexistence of an \(N\)-central state on \(S_{X_B}(N)\) to proper proximality relative to \(X_B\). DKEP Theorem 6.2 is an equivalence about central states, but the definition-level link should be stated. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

### Application to graph products

The graph-product AFP decomposition is correctly sourced from Caspers–Fima. The combinatorial choice of \(w_1,w_2\) is plausible and appears correct.

The expectation computations
\[
E_{M_{L_v}}(w_i)=0,\qquad E_{M_{L_v}}(w_1^*w_2)=0
\]
are only stated as “graph-product reduced-word calculus.” This is standard, but in a complete proof the two cases should be explicitly checked.

The use of CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 to get a diffuse full factor is valid, and the nonamenability conclusion from fullness is fine in the separable finite-factor setting. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

## 4. Overall assessment

The proof has a coherent high-level strategy:

1. prove relative proper proximality with respect to link subalgebras;
2. prove a boundary-support intersection result;
3. combine finitely many relative boundaries into the compact boundary;
4. use nonamenability/fullness to conclude proper proximality.

However, the most important technical lemma—the boundary-support intersection—is not proved at the required level of rigor. It depends on several nontrivial support-calculus and correspondence-mixing assertions that are either only sketched or asserted beyond the exact cited statements. Because these are essential to the proof, `answer.tex` cannot be certified as a complete rigorous solution.
