## Section-by-section audit

### 1. Problem statement and interpretation

The interpretation of irreducibility as join-irreducibility, equivalently connected complement graph, is explicitly recorded. This resolves the ambiguity in the problem statement and matches the standard graph-product terminology.

### 2. Ordinary support bookkeeping

This was the main previous obstruction. The revision now:

1. restores the needed faithfulness hypothesis for the multiplier representations;
2. explains why the one-normal supports \(p_\ell\) and \(p_r\) commute;
3. derives \(p_N=p_\ell p_r\) from the intersection realization of the simultaneous normal bidual;
4. applies the one-normal support statement separately to \(Y\);
5. proves the equality \(q_X=p_Nr_X=p_N\rho_X\) using the strong-bimodule criterion rather than the earlier imprecise topology formulation.

This now adequately bridges concrete hereditary subalgebra supports and DKEP normal-bidual support projections. The proof of equality of supports for \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) is no longer a handwave: it uses \(K_X=(K_X^L)^*K_X^L\), projection refinements, and Proposition 2.4 to show equality of simultaneous-normal annihilators.

### 3. Subalgebra fusion and finite fusion calculus

The revised treatment of direct sums via DKEP’s cyclic-vector criterion is acceptable. The proof no longer relies on an unjustified norm approximation of arbitrary bounded vectors. The use of
\[
T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi,\qquad T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*
\]
is consistent with DKEP’s correspondence calculus.

The positive-generation argument for \(X_B\) is also now sound: it avoids assuming linearity of \(\alpha\mapsto T_\alpha\) and correctly uses hereditary support.

### 4. Boundary-support intersection lemma

The revised proof of
\[
0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_{U\cap W}
\]
is now rigorous enough.

The monomial expansion
\[
T_{\alpha_1}^*T_{\alpha_2}T_{\beta_1}^*T_{\beta_2}
T_{\alpha_3}^*T_{\alpha_4}
\]
is correctly interpreted as a coefficient operator of a finite Connes fusion containing both \(W\)- and \(U\)-labels. CDHJKN’s fusion rule then forces the resulting summands to be indexed by subsets of \(W\cap U\). The final limiting step is performed in the ordinary bidual \(A^{**}\), before compressing by \(p_M\), so the previous nonmultiplicativity problem is resolved.

### 5. Finite relative boundaries to compact boundary

The finite-boundary lemma is now satisfactory. The revised proof correctly handles the fact that \(\widetilde S(N)\) is an operator system, not a C\(^*\)-algebra, by extending the state only for the GNS projection-fixing argument. The final hypertrace construction uses the DKEP u.c.p. map into the compact normal corner, so it no longer incorrectly identifies \(q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0\) with \(\mathbb B(L^2N)\).

### 6. Relative Bass–Serre paradox

The AFP reduced-word calculation is sufficiently detailed. The projections \(p,q\) onto first-letter \(P\)- and \(Q\)-subspaces are shown to lie in \(S_{X_B}(N)\), and the paradoxical inequalities
\[
w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p,\qquad apa^*\le q
\]
are justified by reduced-word orthogonality. The \(B\)-Haar unitary argument then correctly forces a central state to vanish on \(e_B,p,q\), contradiction.

### 7. Graph-product application

The graph-combinatorial construction of \(w_1,w_2\) is correct in both cases for \(C(v)\). The expectation computations onto \(M_{L_v}\) are now explicit enough.

The intersection of all links is empty because no vertex belongs to its own link, and the boundary-intersection lemma therefore gives the compact-boundary palindromic product. The final use of CDHJKN to get a diffuse full factor, hence nonamenability, is valid under the trace-zero-unitary hypotheses.
