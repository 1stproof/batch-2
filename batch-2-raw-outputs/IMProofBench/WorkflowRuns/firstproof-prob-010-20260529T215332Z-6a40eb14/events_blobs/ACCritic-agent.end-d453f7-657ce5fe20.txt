## 2. Previous fatal objection: resolved

The previous fatal issue was that the proof multiplied concrete operators after passing into DKEP’s simultaneous normal bidual, where the canonical map from \(B(L^2M)\) is not generally multiplicative. The revised proof now explicitly moves the boundary-support intersection calculation into the ordinary bidual \(A^{**}\), where \(A\hookrightarrow A^{**}\) is a genuine \(*\)-homomorphism, and only compresses by the DKEP normal-support projection afterward.

This is the correct repair. DKEP explicitly realize the one-normal bidual as \(p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\), identify invariant hereditary subalgebras by ordinary support projections commuting with \(p_{\rm nor}\), and also warn that the canonical embedding into the normal bidual is not generally a \(*\)-homomorphism. DKEP Proposition 2.10 supplies the simultaneous-normal intersection framework. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

### Intersection of boundary supports

This is the central repaired section, and it now works.

The proof first establishes the concrete operator statement:
\[
a b c\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M),\qquad I=U\cap W,
\]
for \(a,c\in\mathcal P_W\), \(b\in\mathcal P_U\). The expansion into monomials in \(T_\xi\)’s and adjoints is legitimate because \(\mathcal T_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra and adjoints are handled by conjugate correspondences. The CDHJKN fusion rule supports the conclusion that any resulting finite fusion with both \(U\)- and \(W\)-labels decomposes into summands \(H_C\) with \(C\subset U\cap W\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150))

The limiting argument is now done in the ordinary bidual \(A^{**}\):
\[
h_\alpha\to r_W,\qquad k_\beta\to r_U,
\]
and since each \(h_\alpha k_\beta h_\gamma\) lies in \(K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\), its weak-* limit lies in the ordinary support corner \(\rho_I A^{**}\rho_I\). This gives
\[
r_Wr_Ur_W\le \rho_I.
\]
Only after this does the proof compress by \(p\):
\[
q_Wq_Uq_W=p\,r_Wr_Ur_W\,p\le p\rho_Ip=q_I.
\]
This directly resolves the earlier nonmultiplicativity objection.

No fatal issue remains in this lemma.

### Application to graph products

The graph-product AFP decomposition is standard and appropriately cited to CF17. The graph-combinatorial construction of \(w_1,w_2\) is correct in both cases:

- if \(C(v)\) has two vertices \(r,t\), take \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_t\);
- if \(C(v)=\{r\}\), connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge3\) gives \(s\in L_v\) with \(s\not\sim r\), and \(w_2=u_su_r\) works.

The expectation computations onto \(M_{L_v}\) are standard reduced-word consequences.

Finally, CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 supports the claim that a join-irreducible graph product with at least three vertices and vertex state-zero unitaries is a diffuse full factor. In the tracial setting, “state-zero” is exactly trace-zero. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150)) The conclusion that \(M\) is nonamenable follows because a diffuse amenable finite factor is the hyperfinite II\(_1\) factor, which is not full.

No issue.
