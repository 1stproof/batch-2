### Paragraph-by-paragraph audit

#### Problem statement and interpretation

The proof explicitly interprets irreducibility as nontrivial join-irreducibility, equivalently connected complement graph. This is a faithful and standard interpretation of the problem statement. No issue remains here.

#### Support bookkeeping

The previous draft’s support bookkeeping was too abrupt. The current draft gives a clearer route:

1. Work with ordinary open supports \(r_Y\subset A^{**}\).
2. Use DKEP’s simultaneous-normal bidual construction to identify the normal corner.
3. Use Proposition 2.4, Corollary 3.2, and Proposition 3.6 to show that \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) have the same simultaneous-normal annihilator, hence the same compressed support.

This now sufficiently explains why approximate units from the original boundary \(X\) may be used to approximate the DKEP support \(q_X\). This was one of the central earlier gaps, and it is now addressed.

Minor note: the support formula is still written compactly, but the cited DKEP framework plus the argument supplied in the proof is enough for this level of solution.

#### Subalgebra fusion lemma

The previous draft did not adequately justify the coefficient-boundary identification for \(L^2N\otimes_B L^2N\). The revision fixes this by avoiding a naive elementary-tensor density argument and instead using DKEP Theorem 5.10.

The proof now correctly uses:

- \(H={}_NL^2N_B\),
- DKEP Example 5.11 for the left boundary \(X_B\),
- the full boundary \(B(L^2B)\) on the \(B\)-side,
- the fusion identity \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\),
- DKEP Theorem 5.10 to pass from cyclic elementary tensors to all bounded vectors,
- DKEP Proposition 5.9 to obtain coefficient membership in \(K_{X_B}(N)\).

This addresses the earlier objection that arbitrary bounded coefficients were not controlled.

The statement that \(X_B\) is hereditarily generated by positive coefficients of \(\mathcal H_B\) is also acceptable: \(e_B\) is the coefficient of the cyclic vector \(\widehat1\otimes_B\widehat1\), and DKEP Example 3.4 identifies \(X_B\) as the hereditary algebra generated by \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-translates of the Jones projection/corner. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

#### Boundary-support intersection lemma

This was the central technical weakness in the earlier drafts. The revision now gives a coherent and rigorous proof.

The key chain is:

1. Finite-fusion coefficient products \(hkh\) decompose into coefficients of finite fusions whose labels include both \(W\) and \(U\).
2. CDHJKN’s fusion rule decomposes those fusions into direct sums of \(H_C\) with \(C\subset U\cap W\). ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))
3. The direct-sum/fusion lemma puts those coefficients in \(K_{X_{M_{U\cap W}}}(M)\).
4. The limiting argument is performed in the ordinary bidual \(B(L^2M)^{**}\), not in the normal bidual, so the nonmultiplicativity issue is avoided.
5. Only after obtaining \(r_Wr_Ur_W\le r_{K_I}\) is the result compressed by the simultaneous normal support \(p\).

This proves

\[
0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_{U\cap W}.
\]

The induction to the palindromic product is then valid.

#### Graph-product application

The graph-product AFP decomposition is standard from the reduced-word model. The local graph combinatorics are correct. I also brute-force checked the two combinatorial claims for all graphs with \(3\le |V|\le 6\): connected complement implies \(C(v)\neq\varnothing\), and if \(C(v)=\{r\}\), then there is \(s\in\operatorname{Lk}(v)\) with \(s\not\sim r\). No counterexample was found.

The reduced-word expectation computations for \(w_i\) and \(w_1^*w_2\) are valid.

The final use of CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 is correct: for a join-irreducible graph with at least three vertices and trace-zero vertex unitaries, the graph product is a diffuse full factor. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743)) Since the graph product is separable, diffuse amenability would imply hyperfiniteness, and the hyperfinite II\(_1\) factor is not full; hence \(M\) is nonamenable.
