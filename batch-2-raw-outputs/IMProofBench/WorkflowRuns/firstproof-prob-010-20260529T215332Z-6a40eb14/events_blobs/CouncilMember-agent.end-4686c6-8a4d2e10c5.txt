My take: the strategy is plausible, but both lemmas need to be reformulated as explicit “support-calculus” corollaries. As written, the proofs cite more than CDHJKN/DKEP literally state.

**1. Finite boundary criterion.**  
This is essentially correct if the \(q_X\)’s are the DKEP central/support projections in a common normal-bidual ambient algebra. But you should insert the exact DKEP fact being used:

\[
q_X^\perp\,\widetilde S_X(N)\,q_X^\perp \subset \widetilde S_{\mathcal K}(N),
\]
and \(q_X\) commutes with the \(N\)-bimodule action. Then the compression argument works. Also spell out normality: for \(0\le a\in N\),
\[
0\le q_X^\perp a q_X^\perp \le a
\]
since \(q_X^\perp\) commutes with \(N\), so the compressed state restricts to a normal positive functional dominated by a scalar multiple of \(\tau\). Thus relative proper proximality forces \(\omega(q_X^\perp)=0\).

After \(\omega(q_{X_i})=1\) for all \(i\), the finite meet gives \(\omega(q_{\mathcal K})=1\); this is fine even if you phrase it via GNS support. Finally, say precisely that the compact corner is \(K(L^2N)^{**}\cong B(L^2N)\), so \(\omega\) yields an \(N\)-central hypertrace extending \(\tau\), hence injectivity/amenability by Connes. So: correct, but needs these support-calculus details cited or stated.

**2. Link boundary pieces fill the boundary.**  
This is not justified as written. CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.4 gives a fusion rule for correspondences; it does not by itself identify DKEP support projections. You need an intermediate lemma:

For \(U,W\subset V\), writing \(q_U=q_{X_{M_U}}\),
\[
q_U\wedge q_W=q_{U\cap W}.
\]

A clean proof should use three ingredients:

- DKEP support dictionary: \(X_{M_U}\) corresponds to
  \[
  H_U=L^2(M)\otimes_{M_U}L^2(M),
  \]
  and supports satisfy
  \[
  q_{H\otimes_M K}=q_H\wedge q_K,\qquad
  q_{H\oplus K}=q_H\vee q_K.
  \]
- CDHJKN fusion: \(H_U\otimes_M H_W\) decomposes as a direct sum/amplification of \(H_Z\)’s with \(Z\subset U\cap W\), and the identity double-coset summand gives \(Z=U\cap W\).
- Monotonicity: if \(Z\subset I\), then \(X_Z\subset X_I\), hence \(q_Z\le q_I\).

Then the support of the fused correspondence is exactly \(q_{U\cap W}\): all summands have support \(\le q_{U\cap W}\), and the \(H_{U\cap W}\) summand gives the reverse inequality. Iterating gives
\[
\bigwedge_{v\in V}q_{X_{\Lk(v)}}=q_{X_{\cap_v\Lk(v)}}=q_{X_\emptyset}=q_{\mathcal K}.
\]

So the equality is likely right, but the current proof must be replaced by this explicit support/fusion lemma. Also avoid saying “product” unless you have already noted these support projections are central/commuting; use \(\wedge\).
