\section*{Problem statement and interpretation}

Irreducible is interpreted in the standard graph-product sense: \(\Gamma\) is
not a non-trivial join, equivalently the complement graph \(\Gamma^c\) is
connected.  All traces are faithful normal traces.  For \(U\subset V\),
\(M_U\) denotes the graph product over the induced subgraph and
\(E_U:M\to M_U\) its canonical expectation.

We use Ding--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Peterson's normal-bidual notation
\cite{DKEP}.  If \(N\) is finite, set \(A_N=\mathbb B(L^2N)\) and
\[
        \cA_N=(A_N^{\sharp_J})^* ,
\]
the simultaneous \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-normal bidual.  If \(X\) is an
\(N\)-boundary piece, \(K_X(N)\) denotes DKEP's compact algebra associated
with \(X\), and \(q_X\in\cA_N\) is the support projection of
\((K_X(N)^{\sharp_J})^*\).  For the compact boundary
\(X=\mathbb K(L^2N)\) write \(q_0\).  Put
\[
 \widetilde S_X(N)=\{T\in\cA_N:[T,a]\in q_X\cA_Nq_X\hbox{ for every }
 a\in JNJ\}.
\]
For a separable finite factor, DKEP Lemma 8.5 says that proper proximality
relative to \(X\) is equivalent to the non-existence of an \(N\)-central state
on \(\widetilde S_X(N)\) whose restriction to \(N\) is the trace.

\section*{Normal supports and coefficients}

Let \(A=A_N\).  DKEP Proposition 2.10 realizes
\(\cA_N=pA^{**}p\), where \(p\) is the simultaneous normal support projection
for the two commuting actions of \(N\) and \(JNJ\).  If \(Y\subset A\) is a
hereditary C\(^*\)-subalgebra for which \(N\) and \(JNJ\) act faithfully by
multipliers, let \(r_Y\in A^{**}\) be its ordinary open support projection.

\begin{lemma}[normal support bookkeeping]\label{lem:support}
For such \(Y\), \(r_Y\) commutes with \(p\),
\[
       (Y^{\sharp_J})^*=(pr_Y)\,\cA_N\,(pr_Y),
\]
and the unit of this corner is \(s(Y)=pr_Y\).  If \((e_\lambda)\) is a
contractive approximate unit of \(Y\), then \(pe_\lambda p\to s(Y)\) strongly*
in \(\cA_N\).  Moreover, for \(\phi\in A_N^{\sharp_J}\), the normal extension
\(\widetilde\phi\in(\cA_N)_*\) vanishes on \(s(Y)\cA_Ns(Y)\) if and only if
\(\phi|_Y=0\).

If \(X\) is a boundary piece with \(N,JNJ\subset M(X)\) (in particular, a
subalgebra boundary \(X_B\)), then \(s(X)=s(K_X(N))\); hence \(q_X=s(X)\).
For any boundary piece \(X\), whenever \(T\in K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\),
\[
       \phi|_X=0,\ \phi\in A_N^{\sharp_J}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
       \widetilde\phi(T)=0                                             \tag{2.1}
\]
in the normal-bidual sense.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The first paragraph is precisely the concrete form of DKEP Proposition 2.10:
applied to \(Y\subset A\), it identifies \((Y^{\sharp_J})^*\) with the corner
\(r_YpA^{**}pr_Y\).  Since \(r_Yp=pr_Y\), this is
\((pr_Y)\cA_N(pr_Y)\).  A contractive approximate unit of \(Y\) converges
strongly* to \(r_Y\) in \(A^{**}\), hence its image \(pe_\lambda p\) converges
strongly* to \(pr_Y\).  The annihilator assertion follows because \(pYp\) is
weak* dense in that corner and the canonical pairing is
\(\phi(y)=\widetilde\phi(pyp)\).

For a boundary piece \(X\), DKEP Proposition 3.6 and Corollary 3.2 show that
\(K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\) is contained in the simultaneous strong
\(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodule generated by \(X\); Proposition 2.4 is the
corresponding annihilator criterion.  Hence every \(\phi\in A_N^{\sharp_J}\)
that vanishes on \(X\) vanishes on \(K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\), giving (2.1), and in
particular vanishes on \(K_X(N)\).  The converse is immediate from
\(X\subset K_X(N)\).  When \(N\) and \(JNJ\) act by multipliers on \(X\), the
normal corners associated with \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) therefore have the same
preannihilator, so their support projections agree.
\end{proof}

For an expected inclusion \(B\subset N\), let
\[
        \cH_B={}_NL^2N_B\otimes_B{}_BL^2N_N .
\]
In the tracial case we identify \({}_BL^2N_N\) with the conjugate
\(\overline{{}_NL^2N_B}\) through the Tomita conjugation.

\begin{lemma}[subalgebra coefficients]\label{lem:coeff}
The DKEP coefficient boundary of \(\cH_B\) is exactly the subalgebra boundary
\(X_B\) of Example 3.4 in \cite{DKEP}.  Also
\(\overline{\cH_B}\cong\cH_B\) as an \(N\)-\(N\) correspondence.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H={}_NL^2N_B\).  DKEP Example 5.11 identifies the left coefficient
boundary of \(H\) with \(X_B\).  For elementary bounded vectors in
\(H\otimes_B\overline H\),
\[
   T_{\xi\otimes\bar\alpha}^{\,*}T_{\eta\otimes\bar\beta}
        =T_\xi^{\,*}\,\theta_{\alpha,\beta}\,T_\eta,                 \tag{2.2}
\]
where \(\theta_{\alpha,\beta}\in \mathbb B(L^2B)\) is the rank-one coefficient
coming from the right \(B\)-module \(H\).  Formula (2.2) is the usual
linking-algebra form of \(T_{\xi\otimes\zeta}=T_\zeta T_\xi\).  DKEP
Example 3.4 realizes \(X_B\) as the hereditary algebra generated by the
operators \(T_\xi^*\,\mathbb B(L^2B)\,T_\eta\); hence all coefficients of
\(\cH_B\) lie in \(X_B\).  Conversely, taking
\(\alpha=\beta=\widehat1\) recovers the generators \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) of
\(X_B\).  Finally,
\(\overline{\xi\otimes_B\eta}\mapsto J\eta\otimes_BJ\xi\) is a well-defined
bimodular unitary, using traciality.
\end{proof}

If \(Z\subset U\), then \(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_U}\); hence \(X_{M_Z}\subset
X_{M_U}\).

\begin{lemma}[direct sums and finite fusions]\label{lem:dsupport}
Let \(X\subset A_N\) be a boundary piece.  If
\(\cK=\bigoplus_{j\in J}\cK_j\) is a direct sum of \(N\)-\(N\) correspondences
and the coefficient boundary \(X_{\cK_j}\) is contained in \(X\) for every
\(j\), then every bounded coefficient \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) of \(\cK\) belongs to
\(K_X(N)\).

For \(U\subset V\), let \(\cF_U\) be the class of non-empty finite Connes
fusions of copies of
\[
        \cH_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M
\]
and of its conjugate.  The linear span \(\mathcal C_U\) of coefficients of
finite direct sums of members of \(\cF_U\) is a self-adjoint algebra, and
\(X_{M_U}\) has a contractive approximate unit in the norm-closure of positive
coefficients \(T_\zeta^*T_\zeta\) from such finite direct sums.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
DKEP Theorem 5.10, applied with the full boundary on the left and \(X\) on the
right, says that every bounded vector in each \(\cK_j\) gives a mixing operator
\(T_\xi\in K_{A_N,X}(N,N)\).  Finite direct sums are handled by finite row
matrices of such operators.  For an infinite direct sum, if \(P_F\) is the
projection onto a finite sub-sum, then \(P_F\xi\to\xi\) and
\(T_{P_F\xi}\to T_\xi\) in the strong module topology; the mixing operators are
strongly closed in Theorem 5.10.  DKEP Proposition 5.9 gives
\(T_\xi^*T_\xi\in K_X(N)\), and polarization gives all off-diagonal
coefficients.

The standard coefficient calculus for Connes fusion says that the product of two
bounded coefficients is again a bounded coefficient after adjoining the
correspondences and their conjugates as extra fusion factors; adjoints are
coefficients of conjugate correspondences.  Thus \(\mathcal C_U\) is a
\(*\)-algebra.  By Lemma \ref{lem:coeff}, \(X_{M_U}\) is the hereditary algebra
generated by positive
coefficients of \(\cH_U\).  If \(s=\sum_iT_{\xi_i}^*T_{\xi_i}\) is a finite sum
of such generators, the functions \(g_\varepsilon(t)=t(t+\varepsilon)^{-1}\)
form a contractive approximate unit for the hereditary algebra generated by
\(s\).  Polynomial approximation to \(g_\varepsilon^{1/2}\), with zero constant
term, writes \(g_\varepsilon(s)\) as a norm limit of \(c^*c\)'s with
\(c\in\mathcal C_U\), hence as a norm limit of positive coefficients from
finite direct sums of finite fusions.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[intersection of boundary supports]\label{lem:intersection}
Let \(q_U=q_{X_{M_U}}\).  For \(U,W\subset V\), with \(I=U\cap W\),
\[
        0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_I .                                  \tag{3.1}
\]
Consequently, if \(I=\cap_{i=1}^mU_i\), then
\[
q_{U_m}q_{U_{m-1}}\cdots q_{U_1}q_{U_2}\cdots q_{U_{m-1}}q_{U_m}
   \in q_I\cA_Mq_I .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For \(R\subset V\), write \(H_R=L^2M\otimes_{M_R}L^2M\).  CDHJKN Theorem 5.4
identifies this with their graph-product correspondence, and Proposition 5.5
gives the fusion rule
\[
        H_A\otimes_M H_B\cong
        \bigoplus_{C\subset A\cap B}H_C^{\oplus m(A,B,C)} .        \tag{3.2}
\]
Since \(H_A\cong\overline{H_A}\), induction shows that any finite fusion with
labels \(A_1,\ldots,A_n\) decomposes as a direct sum of \(H_C\)'s with
\(C\subset A_1\cap\cdots\cap A_n\).

Take positive coefficients \(h\) from finite direct sums of \(\cF_W\) and \(k\)
from finite direct sums of \(\cF_U\).  By the coefficient calculus just used in
Lemma \ref{lem:dsupport}, \(hkh\) is a finite sum of coefficients of finite
fusions whose labels are all \(W\)'s and \(U\)'s.  By the preceding label
calculus, each such fusion decomposes into a direct sum of \(H_C\)'s with
\(C\subset I\).  Lemma \ref{lem:dsupport}, and \(X_{M_C}\subset X_{M_I}\),
imply \(hkh\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\).  The same holds for norm limits of such
positive coefficients.

Now the support computation is made in the ordinary bidual, where multiplication
is the usual one.  Let \(A=\mathbb B(L^2M)\), let \(p\in A^{**}\) be DKEP's
simultaneous normal support, and let \(r_U\in A^{**}\) be the ordinary support
of \(X_{M_U}\).  By Lemma \ref{lem:support}, \(q_U=pr_U\), and each \(r_U\)
commutes with \(p\).  Choose the contractive approximate units
\(h_\alpha\subset X_{M_W}\) and \(k_\beta\subset X_{M_U}\) supplied by Lemma
\ref{lem:dsupport}.  In \(A^{**}\),
\(h_\alpha\to r_W\) and \(k_\beta\to r_U\) strongly*.  Since
\(h_\alpha k_\beta h_\alpha\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\), passing first in \(\alpha\)
and then in \(\beta\) gives
\[
        r_Wr_Ur_W\in r_{K_I}A^{**}r_{K_I},
\]
where \(r_{K_I}\) is the ordinary support of \(K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\).  This element
is a positive contraction, hence \(0\le r_Wr_Ur_W\le r_{K_I}\).  Compressing by
\(p\) and using the commutation with \(p\),
\[
        q_Wq_Uq_W
        =pr_W\,pr_U\,pr_W
        =p(r_Wr_Ur_W)p
        \le pr_{K_I}p=q_I,
\]
the last equality again being Lemma \ref{lem:support}.  This proves (3.1).
The palindromic statement follows by induction: if the product for
\(U_1,\ldots,U_{m-1}\) is a positive contraction dominated by \(q_J\),
\(J=\cap_{i<m}U_i\), then after compression by \(q_{U_m}\) it is dominated by
\(q_{U_m}q_Jq_{U_m}\le q_{J\cap U_m}\).
\end{proof}

\section*{From finitely many relative boundaries to the compact boundary}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:finite}
Let \(N\) be a separable non-amenable finite factor.  Let
\(X_1,\ldots,X_m\) be boundary pieces, \(q_i=q_{X_i}\), and suppose
\[
        R:=q_mq_{m-1}\cdots q_1q_2\cdots q_{m-1}q_m\in q_0\cA_Nq_0. \tag{4.1}
\]
If \(N\) is properly proximal relative to every \(X_i\), then \(N\) is properly
proximal.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Assume not.  By DKEP Lemma 8.5, there is an \(N\)-central state \(\omega\) on
\(\widetilde S(N)\) with \(\omega|_N=\tau\).  Fix \(i\), set \(q=q_i\).  Since
\(q\) commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\), for \(y\in\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\),
\[
 [q^\perp yq^\perp,a]=q^\perp[y,a]q^\perp=0,\qquad a\in JNJ,
\]
so \(q^\perp yq^\perp\in\widetilde S(N)\).  If \(\omega(q^\perp)>0\), then
\(y\mapsto\omega(q^\perp yq^\perp)/\omega(q^\perp)\) is an \(N\)-central state
on \(\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\).  On \(N\) it is a positive central functional
dominated by a scalar multiple of \(\tau\), hence normal; since \(N\) is a
factor, its normalized restriction is \(\tau\).  This contradicts relative
proper proximality, so \(\omega(q_i^\perp)=0\) for every \(i\).

Extend \(\omega\) to a state on the ambient C\(^*\)-algebra \(\cA_N\).  In its
GNS representation, each \(q_i\) fixes the cyclic vector, hence \(\omega(R)=1\).
The element \(R\) is a positive contraction and, by (4.1), \(0\le R\le q_0\);
therefore \(\omega(q_0)=1\).  DKEP Lemma 8.5 uses the canonical \(N\)- and
\(JNJ\)-bimodular u.c.p. map
\[
        \Theta_0:\mathbb B(L^2N)\longrightarrow
        (\mathbb K(L^2N)^{\sharp_J})^*=q_0\cA_Nq_0
\]
with \(\Theta_0(1)=q_0\) and \(\Theta_0(x)=xq_0\) for \(x\in N\).  Thus
\(\psi(T)=\omega(\Theta_0(T))\) is an \(N\)-central state on
\(\mathbb B(L^2N)\), and \(\psi(x)=\tau(x)\) for \(x\in N\).  This is a
Connes hypertrace, contradicting non-amenability of \(N\).
\end{proof}

\section*{A relative Bass--Serre paradox}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:BS}
Let \(N=P*_{B}Q\) with trace-preserving expectations and \(Q=B\bbarotimes A\).
Assume that \(A\) has a trace-zero unitary \(a\), and that \(P\) has unitaries
\(w_1,w_2\) satisfying
\[
        E_B(w_i)=0,\qquad E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
Then \(N\) is properly proximal relative to \(X_B\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H_P=L^2P\ominus L^2B\) and \(H_Q=L^2Q\ominus L^2B\).  The AFP normal
form is
\[
 L^2N=L^2B\oplus\!\!\bigoplus_{n\ge1,\ i_1\ne\cdots\ne i_n}
 H_{i_1}\otimes_B\cdots\otimes_B H_{i_n},\quad i_j\in\{P,Q\}.
\]
Let \(p\) and \(q\) be the projections onto the summands whose first letter is
\(P\), respectively \(Q\); then \(1=e_B+p+q\).  If \(x\in P\ominus B\), right
multiplication by \(x\) preserves the first letter except for the vacuum and
one-letter \(P\)-words, and
\[
 [p,JxJ]=(1-e_B)JxJ e_B-e_BJxJ(1-e_B),\qquad [q,JxJ]=0.             \tag{5.1}
\]
The two terms in (5.1) are in the \(N,JNJ\)-bimodule generated by \(e_B\), so
they lie in \(X_B\).  For \(x\in Q\ominus B\) the same statement holds with
\(p\) and \(q\) interchanged, while \(B\) commutes with \(p,q\).  Since these
subspaces generate \(N\), DKEP Lemma 6.1 gives \(p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)\).

Let \(\varphi\) be an \(N\)-central state on \(S_{X_B}(N)\) with normal
restriction to \(N\).  Left multiplication by \(w_i\in P\ominus B\) maps
\(qL^2N\) into \(pL^2N\), and \(q\,w_1^*w_2\,q=0\) because
\(E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0\).  Thus \(w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p\).  Similarly
\(a p a^*\le q\), since \(a\in Q\ominus B\).  Centrality gives
\[
        2\varphi(q)\le\varphi(p)\le\varphi(q),
\]
so \(\varphi(p)=\varphi(q)=0\).

The unitary \(h=w_1a\) is \(B\)-Haar: every non-zero power is an alternating
reduced word in centered \(P\)- and \(Q\)-letters, hence \(E_B(h^n)=0\) for
\(n\ne0\).  Therefore, for \(m\ne n\),
\[
 (h^me_Bh^{-m})(h^ne_Bh^{-n})
   =h^m e_BE_B(h^{\,n-m})e_Bh^{-n}=0 .
\]
The projections \(h^ne_Bh^{-n}\) are pairwise orthogonal and have the same
\(\varphi\)-value, so that value is \(0\).  Hence
\(\varphi(1)=\varphi(e_B+p+q)=0\), a contradiction.  By DKEP Theorem 6.2,
\(N\) is properly proximal relative to \(X_B\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Application to graph products}

Fix \(v\in V\), put \(L_v=\Lk_\Gamma(v)\), and use the standard graph-product
amalgamated free product decomposition
\[
        M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}
        (M_{L_v}\bbarotimes M_v).                                \tag{6.1}
\]
Let \(C(v)=\{r\ne v:r\not\sim_\Gamma v\}\).  Since \(\Gamma^c\) is connected,
\(C(v)\ne\emptyset\).  If \(C(v)\) contains distinct \(r,t\), choose
trace-zero unitaries \(u_r,u_t\) and set \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_t\).  If
\(C(v)=\{r\}\), connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge3\) give
\(s\in L_v\) with \(s\not\sim_\Gamma r\); set \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_su_r\).  In both
cases graph-product reduced-word calculus gives
\[
        E_{M_{L_v}}(w_i)=0,\qquad E_{M_{L_v}}(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
A trace-zero unitary in \(M_v\) supplies the unitary \(a\) in (6.1).  Proposition
\ref{prop:BS} shows that \(M\) is properly proximal relative to
\(X_{M_{L_v}}\) for every \(v\).

Enumerate \(V=\{v_1,\ldots,v_m\}\).  Since no vertex lies in its own link,
\(\cap_iL_{v_i}=\emptyset\).  Lemma \ref{lem:intersection} gives the
palindromic product of the \(q_{L_{v_i}}\)'s in
\(q_{\emptyset}\cA_Mq_{\emptyset}\).  But \(M_\emptyset=\C\), so
\(X_{M_\emptyset}=\mathbb K(L^2M)\).

Finally, CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 implies that a join-irreducible graph product with
\(|V|\ge3\) and trace-zero vertex unitaries is a diffuse full factor, hence non-amenable; separability follows from the finite graph and the separability of the
vertices.  Lemma \ref{lem:finite}, applied to the finite family
\(\{X_{M_{L_v}}:v\in V\}\), proves that \(M\) is properly proximal.
