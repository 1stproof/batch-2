## Referee report on the revised draft

### Verdict

The revision fixes one major defect from the previous version: it no longer uses the false claim that a non-normal state taking value \(1\) on several noncommuting projections must be supported on their lattice meet. The new “palindromic product” idea is a reasonable way to avoid that specific error.

However, `answer.tex` is still **not answer-ready**. The proof now depends on a new graph-product boundary-corner intersection lemma whose key normal-bidual/support step is not proved and is not a stated consequence of the cited references. There is also a serious unresolved issue in the finite-boundary criterion: the proof identifies the compact support corner \(q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0\) with \(\mathcal K(L^2N)^{**}=B(L^2N)\), but this is not justified in DKEP’s normal-bidual setup and is likely not literally correct as stated.

### LaTeX contract

I recreated the revised `answer.tex` and ran `pdflatex` twice. It compiled successfully to a 4-page PDF. The document uses exactly `\documentclass[12pt]{article}`, uses `fullpage`, and I found no forbidden margin, line-spacing, or in-document font-size changes. The LaTeX contract appears satisfied.

### Literature cross-check

DKEP indeed define \(S_X(M)\) and prove the generator-checking lemma used for membership in \(S_X(M)\); their Lemma 6.1 says one may check the commutator condition on an ultraweakly dense generating subalgebra. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) DKEP Example 5.11 defines coefficient boundary pieces for correspondences. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) DKEP Lemma 8.5 gives the normal-bidual characterization of relative proper proximality under the relevant separability and center-ergodicity hypotheses. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

CDHJKN do provide the graph-product bimodule decomposition and fusion rules cited in the proof. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743)) They also give the factoriality criterion used in the last section. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743)) Their Main Theorem 0.1 gives the relative amenability criterion used for nonamenability. The problem is not that these references are irrelevant; rather, the draft uses them to infer additional support-projection and normal-bidual statements that are not stated in those references and are not proved in the draft.

## Paragraph-by-paragraph mathematical audit

### 1. Problem statement and interpretation

This section is acceptable. The author explicitly adopts the standard graph-product interpretation of irreducibility as nontrivial join-irreducibility, equivalently connectedness of the complement graph. That resolves the ambiguity in the problem statement.

### 2. “Two boundary facts”: setup

The normal-bidual notation is broadly consistent with DKEP Lemma 8.5. For a factor and the trivial action, the center-ergodicity hypothesis in Lemma 8.5 is automatic, so using the normal-bidual no-central-state criterion is reasonable. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

However, the draft silently assumes several structural facts about the support projections \(q_X\) and corners \(q_X\mathcal A_Nq_X\) that are not proved. This becomes serious in the two subsequent lemmas.

### 3. Lemma: intersection of graph-product boundary corners

This is the main unresolved gap.

The lemma asserts
\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W\subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A_Mq_{U\cap W}.
\tag{1}
\]
The proof tries to derive this from coefficient boundary pieces and CDHJKN fusion rules. But the crucial implication
\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W
\subset q_{\mathcal L}\mathcal A_Mq_{\mathcal L}
\tag{3}
\]
is not established.

DKEP Example 5.11 defines the coefficient boundary piece \(X_{\mathcal H}\) of a correspondence \(\mathcal H\), but it does **not** state the normal-support calculus needed here. In particular, the proof needs a precise theorem of the following kind:

> If \(X_{\mathcal H}\) and \(X_{\mathcal K}\) are coefficient boundary pieces, then sandwiching the normal support corner of \(X_{\mathcal H}\) by the support of \(X_{\mathcal K}\) is dominated by the coefficient support of an appropriate Connes fusion correspondence.

No such theorem is cited or proved.

There is also a coefficient-order problem. DKEP’s coefficient boundary is generated by operators of the form \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\). Under Connes fusion, DKEP’s formula is \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) From this formula, coefficients of a triple fusion are not automatically arbitrary products of a \(W\)-coefficient, a \(U\)-coefficient, and a \(W\)-coefficient in the order needed to identify \(q_W(q_U\mathcal A q_U)q_W\). The sentence

> “translating the three tensor factors gives the general generators”

is therefore insufficient and potentially misleading.

Even after applying CDHJKN’s fusion rules, one still needs to prove that coefficient boundary pieces behave correctly under possibly infinite direct sums and multiplicities. CDHJKN prove Hilbert-bimodule decompositions; they do not identify DKEP normal-bidual support projections or prove containment of coefficient boundary pieces after passing to the DKEP support corners. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

Thus Lemma 1 remains an essential unproved lemma. Since the final palindromic compact-corner inclusion depends entirely on it, this is fatal.

### 4. Lemma: finite palindromic boundary criterion

The revision successfully fixes the previous invalid meet-of-projections argument. The palindromic product
\[
R=q_mq_{m-1}\cdots q_1q_2\cdots q_m
\]
is a positive contraction, and if a state \(\omega\) satisfies \(\omega(q_i^\perp)=0\) for every \(i\), then its GNS cyclic vector is fixed by each \(q_i\), hence by \(R\). This part is sound.

The compression argument also looks essentially correct: if \(\omega(q_i^\perp)>0\), compression by \(q_i^\perp\) gives an \(N\)-central state on \(\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\), and normality on \(N\) follows by domination by a scalar multiple of \(\tau\).

However, the final step is not justified as written:

> “The compact corner \(q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0\) is canonically \(\mathcal K(L^2N)^{**}=B(L^2N)\).”

In DKEP’s setup, \(q_X\) is the support of the normal corner associated with \(\mathbb K_X(M)\), not simply the bidual of the original hereditary algebra \(X\). For \(X=\mathcal K(L^2M)\), DKEP’s associated relatively compact spaces are not just the literal compact operators; DKEP explicitly notes that \(K^{\infty,1}(M)\) can be much larger than \(\mathcal K(L^2M)\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) Therefore the identification
\[
q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0\cong \mathcal K(L^2N)^{**}=B(L^2N)
\]
needs a proof or a precise citation. It is not a formal consequence of the definitions as presented.

The intended conclusion may be salvageable by using DKEP’s natural u.c.p. map from \(B(L^2N)\) into the normal-bidual compact corner, but that map is not described in `answer.tex`. As written, the hypertrace step is unsupported.

### 5. Proposition: relative Bass–Serre paradox

This section is improved compared to the earlier draft. The commutator formulas
\[
[p,R_x]=R_xe_B-e_BR_x,\qquad [q,R_x]=0
\]
for \(x\in P\ominus B\), and the analogous formulas for \(x\in Q\ominus B\), are plausible and are the right kind of calculation. DKEP Lemma 6.1 is indeed the relevant tool for reducing membership in \(S_{X_B}(N)\) to generators. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

Still, the proof remains terse. The formulas are asserted by “inspection of reduced words” rather than proved. For a final rigorous proof, one should explicitly verify the action on the \(B\)-summand, one-letter words, and longer reduced words, including the collapse-to-\(B\) case. This is a lesser issue than the boundary-corner lemma, because the formulas are likely correct, but the current exposition is still somewhat handwavy.

The paradoxical inequalities
\[
w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p,\qquad apa^*\le q
\]
are plausible and the central-state contradiction is standard once \(p,q,e_B\in S_{X_B}(N)\). This section is probably repairable with a fuller reduced-word calculation.

### 6. Application to graph products

The graph-product decomposition
\[
M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}}
(M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}\bar\otimes M_v)
\]
is standard. The construction of \(w_1,w_2\) in the two complement-neighbor cases appears correct.

The factoriality argument using CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.5 is sound: connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) with at least three vertices rules out both universal vertices and isolated exceptional complement edges. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

The nonamenability argument using CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.1 is also essentially sound: if \(M\) were amenable, then applying the relative amenability criterion with \(V_1=V\), \(V_2=\varnothing\) would force every nonedge to be an exceptional pair adjacent to all remaining vertices, which would make each complement edge an isolated component, impossible in a connected complement graph with at least three vertices. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

But the final passage from relative proper proximality with respect to the link boundaries to ordinary proper proximality depends on Lemma 1 and Lemma 2. Since those remain insufficiently justified, the final theorem is not proved.

## Previous concerns: addressed or remaining

- **Previous false meet/state argument:** addressed by the palindromic-product criterion.
- **Previous unsupported fusion-meet identity:** not fully addressed. It has been replaced by a weaker intersection lemma, but that lemma still requires an unproved DKEP normal-support/fusion calculus.
- **Previous handwavy \(p,q\in S_{X_B}\):** partially addressed by explicit formulas, though the reduced-word verification is still abbreviated.
- **New issue introduced/emphasized:** the finite-boundary criterion now relies on the unproved identification \(q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0\cong B(L^2N)\), which is not justified in DKEP’s normal-bidual framework.

### Final assessment

The revised draft is closer than the previous one, but it still contains essential unproved lemmas in the boundary-support part of the argument. The proof is therefore not complete.
