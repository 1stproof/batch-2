I would not leave the current normal-left-ideal proof as written. The danger is not only the nonmultiplicativity of the canonical map \(B(L^2N)\to \mathcal A_N\), but also the line
\[
\mathcal L_{\mathcal K}\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}\subset \mathcal L_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\mathcal K}.
\]
If \(\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}\) is a weak-* closed left ideal in the von Neumann algebra \(\mathcal A_N\), then \(\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}=\mathcal A_N q_{\mathcal H}\). Products contain arbitrary middle \(\mathcal A_N\)-terms, and the concrete formula \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\) only controls products of generators, not \(T_\eta a T_\xi\) for \(a\in\mathcal A_N\). Thus the double-fusion sandwich is at least under-justified, and maybe too strong in that form.

The clean replacement is to do all multiplicative support calculus in the ordinary enveloping algebra \(A^{**}\), \(A=B(L^2N)\), and use a triple coefficient product.

Suggested replacement lemma:

Let \(K_X,K_Y,K_Z\subset A\) be the hereditary \(C^*\)-algebras of three boundary pieces, with ordinary open supports \(e_X,e_Y,e_Z\in A^{**}\). If
\[
\overline{\operatorname{span}}\, K_X K_Y K_X \subset K_Z,
\]
then, after cutting by DKEP’s simultaneous normal projection \(p=p_{\rm nor}\),
\[
q_X(q_Y\mathcal A_Nq_Y)q_X\subset q_Z\mathcal A_Nq_Z,
\qquad q_X=pe_X.
\]
Proof: \(e_X(e_YA^{**}e_Y)e_X\subset e_ZA^{**}e_Z\) by approximating elements of \(e_YA^{**}e_Y\) by bounded nets from \(K_Y\), sandwiching by approximate units of \(K_X\), and taking weak-* limits. Then cut by \(p\), using \(pe_\bullet=e_\bullet p\). This avoids any multiplicativity claim for the normal-bidual embedding.

For coefficient pieces, prove the hypothesis with triple fusion. On elementary coefficients,
\[
(T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2})(T_{\xi_3}^*T_{\xi_4})
=
T_{\xi_1\otimes\bar\xi_2\otimes\eta_1}^*
T_{\xi_4\otimes\bar\xi_3\otimes\eta_2},
\]
so
\[
K_{X_{\mathcal H}}K_{X_{\mathcal K}}K_{X_{\mathcal H}}
\subset
K_{X_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\overline{\mathcal H}\otimes_N\mathcal K}},
\]
with the passage from generators to hereditary closures justified by DKEP’s relative compact/operator correspondence results, e.g. Prop. 5.9/Theorem 5.10.

Then for graph products take \(\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\). Since \(\mathcal H_W\cong\overline{\mathcal H_W}\), the relevant triple fusion is
\[
\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U.
\]
Apply the CDHJKN fusion rule twice: first fusing the two \(W\)-pieces gives summands \(\mathcal H_Z\) with \(Z\subset W\), and fusing with \(U\) gives only \(\mathcal H_Y\) with \(Y\subset U\cap W\). Hence its coefficient boundary is supported by \(q_{U\cap W}\). Combining with the support-transfer lemma gives exactly
\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W
\subset
q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A_Mq_{U\cap W}.
\]

This is slightly less slick than the left-ideal argument, but it is robust against the normal-bidual nonmultiplicativity issue.

The left-ideal argument in your current draft has a fatal domain flaw: for a correspondence $\cH$, the coefficient maps $T_\xi$ are operators from $L^2M$ to $\cH \otimes_M L^2M$. Because they do not belong to $B(L^2M)$, they cannot be embedded into the normal bidual $\cA_M = p_{\rm nor} B(L^2M)^{**} p_{\rm nor}$, nor can they generate a normal left ideal there. While DKEP does construct rectangular normal biduals for mapping spaces, multiplying across different rectangular biduals is heavy machinery and prone to topological subtleties. 

Your Round 6 notes contained the ideal fix: compute the support sandwich in the ordinary enveloping von Neumann algebra $A^{**} = B(L^2M)^{**}$ (where the canonical embedding is a strictly multiplicative *-homomorphism), and only cut by $p_{\rm nor}$ at the very end. 

Furthermore, for graph products, you can completely bypass the abstract contragredient calculus ($T_{\xi \otimes \bar{\eta} \dots}$) in favor of a much more concrete argument. Here is the cleanest rigorous replacement for the boundary support calculus:

**1. The algebraic coefficient product**
Because the inclusion $M_U \subset M$ is expected, the boundary piece $X_U$ is precisely the norm closure of the span of $M e_U M$ (which forms a $C^*$-algebra because $e_U x e_U = E_U(x)e_U$).
Consequently, the algebraic product $X_W X_U X_W$ is spanned by elements of the form $x e_W y e_U z e_W w$ for $x,y,z,w \in M$.

**2. Containment via triple fusion**
These spanning elements are exactly the left/right $M$-translates of $e_W e_U e_W$. But $e_W e_U e_W$ is the coefficient operator $T_\zeta^* T_\zeta$ for the cyclic vector $\zeta = \hat{1} \otimes \hat{1} \otimes \hat{1}$ of the 3-fold fusion correspondence $\cH_W \otimes_M \cH_U \otimes_M \cH_W$. 
Thus, algebraically, $X_W X_U X_W$ is contained in the coefficient boundary $X_{\cH_W \otimes \cH_U \otimes \cH_W}$. 

By applying the CDHJKN fusion rule twice, $\cH_W \otimes_M \cH_U \otimes_M \cH_W$ decomposes into a direct sum of correspondences $\cH_Z$ for $Z \subset U \cap W$. Since the coefficient boundary of a direct sum is contained in the join of the summand boundaries, and $X_Z \subset X_{U \cap W}$, we immediately get:
\[
X_W X_U X_W \subset X_{U \cap W}
\]

**3. The double-dual support sandwich**
Now pass to $A^{**}$. If $B, C, D \subset A$ are any $C^*$-algebras satisfying $BCB \subset D$, their open support projections in $A^{**}$ satisfy
\[
e_B(e_C A^{**} e_C)e_B \subset e_D A^{**} e_D.
\]
*(Proof: Any $x \in e_C A^{**} e_C$ is the weak-* limit of a net $x_i \in C$. For any $y,z \in B$, $y x_i z \in BCB \subset D \subset e_D A^{**} e_D$, so taking limits gives $y x z \in e_D A^{**} e_D$. Sandwiching by an approximate identity for $B$ and taking limits again yields $e_B x e_B \in e_D A^{**} e_D$.)*

Applying this to our boundary pieces yields $e_W (e_U A^{**} e_U) e_W \subset e_{U\cap W} A^{**} e_{U\cap W}$. 

Finally, by DKEP Proposition 2.10, the simultaneous normal projection $p_{\rm nor}$ commutes with these support projections. Multiplying the $A^{**}$ inclusion on both sides by $p_{\rm nor}$ maps $e_\bullet$ to $q_\bullet$, yielding exactly the desired relation:
\[
q_W (q_U \cA_M q_U) q_W \subset q_{U\cap W} \cA_M q_{U\cap W}
\]

This approach handles the non-multiplicativity of $\iota_{\rm nor}$ perfectly, requires no rectangular normal biduals, and turns the graph-product boundary intersection into a clean algebraic exercise.
