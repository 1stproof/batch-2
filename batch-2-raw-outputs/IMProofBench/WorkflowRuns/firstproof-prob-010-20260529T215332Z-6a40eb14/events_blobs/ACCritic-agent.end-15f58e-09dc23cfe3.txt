\section*{Problem statement and interpretation}

I interpret ``irreducible'' in the standard graph-product sense: the finite
simplicial graph $\Gamma=(V,E)$ is not a non-trivial join, equivalently the
complement graph $\Gamma^c$ is connected.  Thus there is no partition
$V=S\sqcup T$ with $S,T\ne\varnothing$ such that every vertex of $S$ is adjacent
in $\Gamma$ to every vertex of $T$.  The traces are faithful normal tracial
states.  For $U\subset V$, $M_U$ denotes the graph product over the induced
subgraph $\Gamma|_U$.

\section*{A scalar free-product input}

I first record a free-product lemma which is the finite-dimensional replacement
for the diffuse free-product result of Ding--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Peterson
\cite[Proposition 6.7]{DKEP}.  I use their definition of proper proximality:
a tracial von Neumann algebra $N$ is properly proximal if there is no
$N$-central state on $S(N)$ whose restriction to $N$ is normal.

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:scalar-free}
Let $(A,\tau_A)$ and $(B,\tau_B)$ be tracial von Neumann algebras.  Assume that
$B$ contains a trace-zero unitary $b$, and that $A$ contains trace-zero
unitaries $a_1,a_2$ with $\tau_A(a_1^*a_2)=0$.  Then $A*B$ is properly
proximal.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let $N=A*B$ and write
$L^2(N)=\mathbb C\hat 1\oplus\bigoplus H_{i_1}^0\otimes\cdots\otimes H_{i_m}^0$
for the usual free-product decomposition, where $i_j\in\{A,B\}$ are alternating
and $H_A^0=L^2(A)\ominus\mathbb C\hat1$, $H_B^0=L^2(B)\ominus\mathbb C\hat1$.
Let $P_A$ and $P_B$ be the projections onto the sums of reduced words whose
first letter is, respectively, from $A$ and from $B$.

Exactly as in \cite[Proposition 6.7]{DKEP}, the commutators
$[P_A,JxJ]$ and $[P_B,JxJ]$ are finite-rank for $x$ in the trace-zero part of
one free factor and vanish for $x$ in the trace-zero part of the other.
DKEP's Lemma 6.1 therefore gives $P_A,P_B\in S(N)$.

Suppose that $\varphi$ is an $N$-central state on $S(N)$ with normal
restriction to $N$.  Left multiplication by $a_i$ sends the $B$-initial
summand isometrically into the $A$-initial summand; the two images are
orthogonal because $\tau_A(a_1^*a_2)=0$.  Hence
\[
        a_1P_Ba_1^*+a_2P_Ba_2^*\le P_A .
\]
Similarly, $bP_Ab^*\le P_B$.  Centrality of $\varphi$ gives
\[
        2\varphi(P_B)\le \varphi(P_A)\le \varphi(P_B),
\]
so $\varphi(P_A)=\varphi(P_B)=0$.  Finally $h=a_1b$ is a Haar unitary in
$N$, so the projections $h^n e_{\mathbb C}h^{-n}$ are pairwise orthogonal,
where $e_{\mathbb C}$ is the projection onto $\mathbb C\hat1$.  Centrality
implies $N\varphi(e_{\mathbb C})\le1$ for every $N$, and hence
$\varphi(e_{\mathbb C})=0$.  Since
$1=P_A+P_B+e_{\mathbb C}$, this contradicts $\varphi(1)=1$.
\end{proof}

\section*{The graph-product reduction}

We shall use the relative amenability theorem of Charlesworth--de Santiago--
Hayes--Jekel--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Nelson \cite[Theorem A]{CDHJKN}: if
$X$ is a finite induced subgraph and $Y\subset X$, then, under the present
trace-zero-unitary hypothesis, $M_X$ is amenable relative to $M_Y$ precisely
when every vertex algebra outside $Y$ is amenable and the only possible
non-adjacencies involving vertices outside $Y$ are the exceptional
two-dimensional isolated pairs described in that theorem.

We also use the standard graph-product decomposition
\begin{equation}\label{eq:afp}
        M_{W\cup\{v\}}
        \cong
        M_W*_{M_L}\bigl(M_L\bbarotimes M_v\bigr),
        \qquad L=W\cap \Lk_\Gamma(v),
\end{equation}
with respect to the canonical trace-preserving expectations.

The remaining permanence statement needed for the induction is the following
Bass--Serre upgrading assertion.

\begin{proposition}[Bass--Serre permanence still to be supplied]\label{prop:BS}
Let $B\subset P$ be an inclusion of separable tracial von Neumann algebras with
expectation, and let $A$ contain a trace-zero unitary.  If $P$ is properly
proximal and $P$ is not amenable relative to $B$ inside $P$, then
\[
        P*_B(B\bbarotimes A)
\]
is properly proximal.
\end{proposition}

Assuming Proposition \ref{prop:BS}, the proof of the theorem is as follows.
Since $\Gamma^c$ is connected and $|V|\ge3$, choose a length-two path
$v_1-v_2-v_3$ in $\Gamma^c$.  Extend it to an ordering
$V=\{v_1,\ldots,v_n\}$ so that the induced complement graph on
$V_k=\{v_1,\ldots,v_k\}$ is connected for every $k\ge3$; for instance, add
vertices one at a time along a spanning tree of $\Gamma^c$.

For the base case, $v_2$ is not adjacent in $\Gamma$ to $v_1$ or $v_3$, hence
\[
        M_{V_3}\cong M_{\{v_1,v_3\}}*M_{v_2}.
\]
If $u_i\in M_{v_i}$ are trace-zero unitaries, then $u_1,u_3$ are trace-zero and
orthogonal in $M_{\{v_1,v_3\}}$ (whether $v_1$ and $v_3$ commute or are free).
Thus Proposition \ref{prop:scalar-free} implies that $M_{V_3}$ is properly
proximal.

Assume that $P=M_{V_{k-1}}$ is properly proximal, where $k\ge4$, and put
$L=V_{k-1}\cap\Lk_\Gamma(v_k)$.  By \eqref{eq:afp},
\[
        M_{V_k}\cong P*_{M_L}(M_L\bbarotimes M_{v_k}).
\]
It remains to check that $P$ is not amenable relative to $M_L$.  Choose a
complement edge from $v_k$ to $r\in V_{k-1}$.  Since the induced complement
graph on $V_{k-1}$ is connected and has at least three vertices, $r$ has a
complement-neighbor $s\in V_{k-1}$.  We have $r\notin L$, and $r,s$ are not
adjacent in $\Gamma$.  If $P$ were amenable relative to $M_L$, the relative
amenability criterion applied to $X=V_{k-1}$ and $Y=L$ would force the
exceptional alternative: $r$ and $s$ would be adjacent in $\Gamma$ to every
vertex of $V_{k-1}\setminus\{r,s\}$.  Equivalently, $\{r,s\}$ would be a
connected component of $(\Gamma|_{V_{k-1}})^c$, contradicting connectedness and
$|V_{k-1}|\ge3$.  Proposition \ref{prop:BS} would therefore give proper
proximality of $M_{V_k}$, and induction would give proper proximality of
$M=M_V$.

\section*{Remaining open issues}

The proof above is complete except for Proposition \ref{prop:BS}.  The scalar
base case no longer uses the unvalidated diffuse free-product permanence
statement; it is proved above by the DKEP paradoxical-projection argument.
The unresolved point is the amalgamated step: the natural projections onto
Bass--Serre half-trees have commutators compact only relative to the
amalgamating algebra $B$, so a genuine proof must upgrade relative proper
proximality to ordinary proper proximality using the hypothesis that
the non-relative-amenability hypothesis on $P$ over $B$.  In this round I verified that
the graph-theoretic induction supplies precisely this non-relative-amenability
hypothesis via \cite[Theorem A]{CDHJKN}.  What is still needed is either an
exact literature theorem proving Proposition \ref{prop:BS} in this form, or a
self-contained normal-bidual/Bass--Serre proof of that proposition.

\[

The chosen ordering gives a complement edge $v_k-r_k$ with $r_k\in V_{k-1}$,

\section*{Round 1 post-critic refinements}

The previous argument used an unvalidated permanence theorem for proper
proximality under AFPs.  Literature check: DKEP, \emph{Properly proximal von
Neumann algebras}, has Proposition 6.7 proving $M_1*M_2$ properly proximal when
both $M_i$ are diffuse, but no amalgamated-free-product theorem in the form
needed.  DKE, \emph{Structure of relatively bi-exact group von Neumann
algebras}, has a relative-to-full upgrading theorem (Theorem 1.1) with extra
hypotheses: diffuse ambient and subalgebras, commuting Jones projections,
bounded Pimsner--Popa bases, mixing inclusions, and a central-projection
decomposition.  It does not immediately imply the desired AFP permanence for
$P*_B(B\bar\otimes A)$ with arbitrary $B,A$.

A useful finite-dimensional base replacement is available.  Proposition:
if $A$ has two trace-zero unitaries $a_1,a_2$ with $\tau(a_1^*a_2)=0$, and
$B$ has a trace-zero unitary $b$, then $A*B$ is properly proximal.  Proof is
DKEP Prop. 6.7's paradoxical projection argument:
let $P_A,P_B$ be projections onto reduced words whose first letter is in $A$
or $B$.  Their commutators with right multiplication by generators are finite
rank, hence $P_A,P_B\in S(A*B)$ by DKEP Lemma 6.1.  Left multiplication gives
$a_1P_Ba_1^*+a_2P_Ba_2^*\le P_A$ and $bP_Ab^*\le P_B$, so any central state
on $S(A*B)$ has $P_A=P_B=0$.  The unitary $a_1b$ is Haar, hence the central
state also vanishes on the vacuum projection; contradiction.

This handles the graph-product base case for a complement path
$v_1-v_2-v_3$: $M_{V_3}=M_{\{v_1,v_3\}}*M_{v_2}$, and
$M_{\{v_1,v_3\}}$ contains two orthogonal trace-zero unitaries, one from
$M_{v_1}$ and one from $M_{v_3}$.

The induction still reduces to the following Bass--Serre permanence statement:

\[
\text{If }B\subset P,\quad P\text{ properly proximal and not amenable relative
to }B,\quad A\text{ has a trace-zero unitary,}
\]
then $P*_B(B\bar\otimes A)$ is properly proximal.

Why this is nontrivial: in the AFP Fock space, the natural projections onto
words starting with the $P\ominus B$ side and with the $A$ side have commutators
with right multiplication compact only relative to $e_B$, not compact in the
DKEP $K_{\infty,1}$ sense unless $B$ is finite-dimensional/amenable in a strong
way.  Thus the scalar paradoxical-projection proof proves relative proper
proximality with respect to the boundary piece $X_B$, but upgrading to full
proper proximality should use the assumption that $P$ is not amenable relative
to $B$.  This is exactly the gap.

Graph-specific extra structure in the induction: when adding $v_k$ with
$L=V_{k-1}\cap\Lk(v_k)$, choose a complement edge $v_k-r$ with $r\in V_{k-1}$
and then a complement edge $r-s$ inside the connected previous complement
graph.  Besides proving non-relative amenability by the CDHJKN criterion, these
vertices also provide $M_L$-centered unitaries in $P=M_{V_{k-1}}$: if
$a\in M_r$ and $b\in M_s$ are trace-zero, then $a$ and $ba$ are unitaries in
$P\ominus M_L$ and $E_{M_L}(a^*ba)=0$ (the word $a^*,b,a$ is reduced relative
to $M_L$ because $r$ and $s$ are nonadjacent in $\Gamma$ and $r\notin L$).
This may be useful for a graph-specific AFP paradoxical argument, though the
main obstruction remains that the half-tree projections live in a relative
boundary piece rather than $S(M)$.

Potential next attacks:
1. Locate an exact theorem proving the Bass--Serre permanence statement above,
possibly in a later/preprint version by Ding--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Peterson
or in work on relatively bi-exact von Neumann algebras.
2. Prove a narrower graph-specific permanence theorem: assume in addition that
$P$ contains two $B$-orthogonal $B$-centered unitaries as above.  Try to build
operators in $S(P*_B(B\bar\otimes A))$ by correcting the relative half-tree
projections with a state/conditional expectation argument using proper
proximality of $P$.
3. Explore whether non-relative amenability of $P$ over $B$ plus a hypothetical
central state on $S(M)$ yields a $P$-central state on $S(P)$ or on the basic
construction $\langle P,e_B\rangle$, contradicting proper proximality or the
CDHJKN criterion.
