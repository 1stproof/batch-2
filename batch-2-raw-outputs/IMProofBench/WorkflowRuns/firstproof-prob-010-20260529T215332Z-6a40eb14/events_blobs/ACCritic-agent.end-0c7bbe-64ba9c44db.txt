## Detailed audit

### 1. Problem statement and interpretation

This section is acceptable. The original irreducibility wording is ambiguous, and the author explicitly adopts the standard graph-product interpretation: \(\Gamma\) is not a nontrivial join, equivalently \(\Gamma^c\) is connected. That is a reasonable faithful interpretation.

The DKEP normal-bidual criterion is invoked only after factoriality is later checked, which is the correct order: DKEP Lemma 8.5 requires the trivial action to be ergodic on the center, i.e. the algebra must be a factor in this application. DKEP’s Lemma 8.5 indeed gives the normal-bidual criterion in terms of absence of central states on \(\widetilde S_X(M)\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

### 2. Boundary support calculus

This remains the fatal part.

#### 2.1 Confusion between \(X_{\mathcal H}\) and \(\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}\)

The support projection \(q_X\) used in DKEP Lemma 8.5 is the support of the normal corner associated to \(\mathbb K_X(M)\), not merely the ordinary support of the boundary piece \(X\). DKEP defines \(\mathbb K_X(M)\) separately from \(X\), via the left ideal \(\mathbb K_X^L\), and only then uses \(\mathbb K_X(M)\) in the normal-bidual construction. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

In the revised proof, the key argument says “the ordinary support of \(X_{\mathcal H}\)” is the right support of the rectangular module generated by \(T_\xi\), and then uses coefficient identities for \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\). But the \(q_{\mathcal H}\) appearing later is \(q_{X_{\mathcal H}}\), i.e. the support of \(\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}(N)\). The draft never proves that the ordinary support of \(X_{\mathcal H}\) agrees with the ordinary support of \(\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}(N)\), nor that coefficient-generator computations for \(X_{\mathcal H}\) control the larger DKEP compact algebra \(\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}(N)\).

This object mismatch is essential.

#### 2.2 The support-sandwich inclusion is still unproved

The proof asserts the crucial inclusion

\[
q_{\mathcal H}(q_{\mathcal K}\mathcal A_Nq_{\mathcal K})q_{\mathcal H}
\subset q_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\overline{\mathcal H}\otimes_N\mathcal K}
\mathcal A_N
q_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\overline{\mathcal H}\otimes_N\mathcal K}.
\tag{1}
\]

The generator identity

\[
(T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2})(T_{\xi_3}^*T_{\xi_4})
=
T_{\xi_1\otimes\overline{\xi_2}\otimes\eta_1}^{*}
T_{\xi_4\otimes\overline{\xi_3}\otimes\eta_2}
\]

is plausible and follows from DKEP’s fusion formula \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\). DKEP records that formula in its correspondence section. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

But this identity only handles products of three elementary coefficient operators. It does **not** prove the support-corner inclusion (1). To get (1), one needs to control arbitrary elements of the hereditary algebras and of the weak-* support corners. The proof says this follows from “hereditary closure and weak-* limiting steps” in DKEP Proposition 5.9 and Theorem 5.10, but those cited results do not state such a support-sandwich theorem. Proposition 5.9 characterizes a single rectangular operator \(T\) by the conditions \(T^*T\in \mathbb K_Y\) and \(TT^*\in \mathbb K_X\); Theorem 5.10 gives equivalent definitions of mixing for correspondences relative to boundary pieces. Neither supplies the asserted weak-* support-corner multiplication result. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

This is not a minor citation gap: the entire graph-product intersection lemma depends on (1).

#### 2.3 Hereditary closure is not controlled by the displayed triple coefficient identity

DKEP Example 5.11 defines \(X_{\mathcal H}\) as the hereditary \(C^*\)-algebra generated by coefficients \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) A hereditary \(C^*\)-algebra generated by these coefficients contains norm limits of much more complicated expressions than single coefficients. Products of coefficients of \(\mathcal H\) already naturally lead to higher fusion powers, not just back to \(\mathcal H\).

Therefore the sentence

> “elements of the corner \(e_{\mathcal H}(e_{\mathcal K}A^{**}e_{\mathcal K})e_{\mathcal H}\) are weak-* limits of finite sums of products controlled by (2)”

is a substantial unproved assertion. A rigorous proof would need an explicit lemma showing that the support corner generated by \(\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}\), \(\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal K}}\), and \(\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}\) is dominated by the support for the triple-fusion correspondence. The draft does not prove this, and DKEP does not state it in the cited locations.

#### 2.4 The direct-sum support assertion is still underjustified

The draft now says that if \(\mathcal H=\oplus_j\mathcal H_j\), then \(q_{\mathcal H}\le \bigvee_j q_{\mathcal H_j}\), citing DKEP Theorem 5.10. But Theorem 5.10 is formulated for concrete boundary pieces \(X,Y\), not arbitrary joins of normal-bidual support projections. To use it as written, one must construct a boundary piece whose DKEP support is the join and prove the finite-support vectors are mixing relative to that concrete boundary piece. This is not done.

For finite direct sums, the assertion is plausible via \(2\times2\) positivity; for arbitrary multiplicities in CDHJKN’s decomposition, the infinite direct-sum passage still needs a real proof.

### 3. Graph-product boundary intersection lemma

Conditional on the missing support calculus, the use of CDHJKN fusion rules is plausible. CDHJKN Theorem 5.4 decomposes \(M_{V_1}\)-\(M_{V_2}\) bimodules as direct sums indexed by subgraphs, and Proposition 5.5 gives the corresponding fusion rule. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

But since the support-sandwich inclusion and direct-sum support statement remain unproved, Lemma `intersection of graph-product boundary corners` is not rigorously established. This is fatal: the later palindromic compact-corner inclusion relies exactly on this lemma.

### 4. Finite palindromic boundary criterion

This section is substantially improved.

The previous operator-system issue is now handled by extending \(\omega\) to a state on \(\mathcal A_N\), which is the correct formal move.

There remains one small technical omission: the extension \(\bar\omega\) need not be \(N\)-central on all of \(\mathcal A_N\). To prove that \(\bar\omega\circ\Theta\) is \(N\)-central, one must explicitly use that \(\Theta\) is \(N\)-bimodular and that its range lies in the compact corner \(q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0\subset \widetilde S(N)\), where \(\bar\omega\) agrees with the original central state \(\omega\). This is fixable, but as written the centrality conclusion is a little too quick.

This is not the main obstruction.

### 5. Relative Bass–Serre proposition

This part is plausible. The reduced-word projections \(p,q\), the commutator computations with right multiplication, and the paradoxical inequalities are standard free-product arguments. DKEP Lemma 6.1 supports the “check on generators” step for proper proximality/mixing conditions. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The proof is terse, but I do not see a fatal mathematical error here.

### 6. Final graph-product application

This section is mostly correct, conditional on the boundary intersection lemma.

The amalgamated free-product decomposition over \(M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}\), the construction of \(w_1,w_2\), and the vanishing of expectations are all plausible by reduced-word arguments.

The factoriality and nonamenability checks are supported by CDHJKN. Main Theorem 0.5 gives the stated factoriality obstruction, and Proposition 6.3 gives the amenability characterization for graph products. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743)) Connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) with at least three vertices excludes both a universal vertex and an isolated exceptional complement edge.

Thus the final graph-theoretic part is fine **if** Lemma `intersection` is available. But that lemma remains unproved.

## Final assessment

The revised proof has improved presentation and fixes several earlier formal issues, but it is still not a complete rigorous solution. The essential missing piece is the boundary support-sandwich calculus. The cited DKEP results define coefficient boundary pieces, characterize relative compactness, and characterize mixing, but they do not provide the asserted support-corner inclusion. The proof also still conflates or at least fails to bridge the gap between the coefficient boundary \(X_{\mathcal H}\) and the DKEP compact algebra \(\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}\) whose normal support is actually used.

Because the graph-product boundary intersection lemma depends on this unproved support calculus, `answer.tex` is not answer-ready.
