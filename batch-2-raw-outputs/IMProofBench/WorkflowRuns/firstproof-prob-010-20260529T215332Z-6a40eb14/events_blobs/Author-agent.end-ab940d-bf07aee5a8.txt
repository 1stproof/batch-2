\section*{Problem statement and interpretation}

Irreducible is interpreted in the standard graph-product sense: \(\Gamma\) is
not a non-trivial join, equivalently the complement graph \(\Gamma^c\) is
connected.  All traces are faithful normal traces.  For \(U\subset V\),
\(M_U\) denotes the graph product over the induced subgraph and
\(E_U:M\to M_U\) its canonical expectation.

We use Ding--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Peterson's normal-bidual notation
\cite{DKEP}.  If \(N\) is finite, set \(A_N=\mathbb B(L^2N)\) and
\[
        \cA_N=(A_N^{\sharp_J})^* ,
\]
the simultaneous \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-normal bidual.  If \(X\) is an
\(N\)-boundary piece, \(K_X(N)\) denotes DKEP's compact algebra associated
with \(X\), and \(q_X\in\cA_N\) is the support projection of
\((K_X(N)^{\sharp_J})^*\).  For the compact boundary
\(X=\mathbb K(L^2N)\) write \(q_0\).  Put
\[
 \widetilde S_X(N)=\{T\in\cA_N:[T,a]\in q_X\cA_Nq_X\hbox{ for every }
 a\in JNJ\}.
\]
For a separable finite factor, DKEP Lemma 8.5 says that proper proximality
relative to \(X\) is equivalent to the non-existence of an \(N\)-central state
on \(\widetilde S_X(N)\) whose restriction to \(N\) is the trace.

\section*{Normal supports and coefficients}

We first isolate the support bookkeeping.  If \(Y\subset A_N\) is hereditary
and \(N,JNJ\subset M(Y)\) faithfully, DKEP Proposition 2.10 embeds
\((Y^{\sharp_J})^*\) as a non-unital von Neumann subalgebra of \(\cA_N\).
Let \(s(Y)\) denote its unit.

\begin{lemma}[normal support bookkeeping]\label{lem:support}
With \(Y\) as above,
\[
       (Y^{\sharp_J})^*=s(Y)\cA_Ns(Y),
\]
the canonical image of \(Y\) is weak* dense in this corner, and every
contractive approximate unit of \(Y\) converges strongly* to \(s(Y)\) in
\(\cA_N\).  Moreover, for \(\phi\in A_N^{\sharp_J}\), the normal extension
\(\widetilde\phi\in(\cA_N)_*\) vanishes on \(s(Y)\cA_Ns(Y)\) if and only if
\(\phi|_Y=0\).

If \(X\) is a boundary piece, then \(s(X)=s(K_X(N))\).  Thus \(q_X=s(X)\), and
whenever \(T\in K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\),
\[
       \phi|_X=0,\ \phi\in A_N^{\sharp_J}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
       \widetilde\phi(T)=0                                             \tag{2.1}
\]
in the normal-bidual sense.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For a hereditary C\(^*\)-subalgebra, the weak closure in any representation is
the corner cut out by the strong limit of any contractive approximate unit.
Applying this to the universal simultaneous normal representation used in
DKEP Proposition 2.10 gives the first assertions and the annihilator
description.

It remains to compare \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\).  If \(\phi|_X=0\), then
\(\phi\) vanishes on \(XA_NX\), since \(X\) is hereditary.  DKEP
Theorem 5.6(4), with \(M=N\) and \(X=Y\), annihilates every element of
\(K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\) by such a \(\phi\).  DKEP Proposition 3.6 gives
\(K_X(N)\subset K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\), so \(\phi\) vanishes on \(K_X(N)\).
The converse is trivial because \(X\subset K_X(N)\).  The two hereditary
algebras therefore have the same annihilator in \(A_N^{\sharp_J}\), hence the
same support in \(\cA_N\), and (2.1) is precisely the same use of Theorem 5.6.
\end{proof}

For an expected inclusion \(B\subset N\), let
\[
        \cH_B={}_NL^2N_B\otimes_B{}_BL^2N_N .
\]

\begin{lemma}[subalgebra coefficients]\label{lem:coeff}
The DKEP coefficient boundary of \(\cH_B\) is exactly the subalgebra boundary
\(X_B\) of Example 3.4 in \cite{DKEP}.  In the tracial case
\(\overline{\cH_B}\cong\cH_B\) as an \(N\)-\(N\) correspondence.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H={}_NL^2N_B\).  DKEP Example 5.11 identifies the left coefficient
boundary of \(H\) with \(X_B\).  For elementary bounded vectors in
\(H\otimes_B\overline H\),
\[
   T_{\xi\otimes\bar\alpha}^{\,*}T_{\eta\otimes\bar\beta}
        =T_\xi^{\,*}\,\theta_{\alpha,\beta}\,T_\eta,                 \tag{2.2}
\]
where \(\theta_{\alpha,\beta}\in \mathbb B(L^2B)\) is the rank-one coefficient
coming from the right \(B\)-module \(H\).  Formula (2.2) is the usual
linking-algebra form of the Connes-fusion identity
\(T_{\xi\otimes\zeta}=T_\zeta T_\xi\).  DKEP Example 3.4 realizes \(X_B\) as
the hereditary algebra generated by these \(T_\xi^*\,\mathbb B(L^2B)\,T_\eta\);
hence all coefficients of \(\cH_B\) lie in \(X_B\).  Conversely, taking
\(\alpha=\beta=\widehat1\), for which \(\theta_{\alpha,\alpha}=1_{L^2B}\),
recovers the generators \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) of \(X_B\).  The conjugation
\(\overline{\xi\otimes_B\eta}\mapsto J\eta\otimes_BJ\xi\) respects the
balanced relation and is a bimodular unitary, using traciality.
\end{proof}

If \(Z\subset U\), then \(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_U}\); hence \(X_{M_Z}\subset
X_{M_U}\).

\begin{lemma}[direct sums and finite fusions]\label{lem:dsupport}
Let \(X\subset A_N\) be a boundary piece.  If
\(\cK=\bigoplus_{j\in J}\cK_j\) is a direct sum of \(N\)-\(N\) correspondences
and the coefficient boundary \(X_{\cK_j}\) is contained in \(X\) for every
\(j\), then every bounded coefficient \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) of \(\cK\) belongs to
\(K_X(N)\).

For \(U\subset V\), let \(\cF_U\) be the class of non-empty finite Connes
fusions of copies of
\[
        \cH_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M
\]
and of its conjugate.  The linear span \(\mathcal C_U\) of coefficients of
finite direct sums of members of \(\cF_U\) is a self-adjoint algebra, and
\(X_{M_U}\) has a contractive approximate unit in the norm-closure of positive
coefficients \(T_\zeta^*T_\zeta\) from such finite direct sums.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
DKEP Theorem 5.10 is applied with the full boundary on the left and \(X\) on
the right.  For each \(\cK_j\) and bounded \(\xi\in\cK_j\), the inclusion
\(X_{\cK_j}\subset X\) gives \(T_\xi^*T_\xi\in X\); by heredity this is
equivalent to \(T_\xi\in A_NX\), which is condition (1) of that theorem.  Hence
\(T_\xi\in K_{A_N,X}(N,N)\) for every bounded \(\xi\in\cK_j\).  Finite direct sums have the same property.  If
\(P_F\) is the projection onto a finite sub-sum and \(\xi\) is bounded, then
\(P_F\xi\) is bounded with the same left and right bounds and
\(P_F\xi\to\xi\) in Hilbert norm.  Hence \(T_{P_F\xi}\to T_\xi\) in DKEP's
strong module topology, uniformly on bounded sets.  The strong closedness of
the mixing operators (DKEP Proposition 5.1, used in the proof of Theorem 5.10)
gives \(T_\xi\in K_{A_N,X}(N,N)\).  Therefore
\(T_\xi^*T_\xi\in K_X(N)\) by DKEP Proposition 5.9, and polarization gives the
general coefficient.

For coefficients \(a=T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2}\) and
\(b=T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2}\), the identity
\(T_{\alpha\otimes\beta}=T_\beta T_\alpha\) gives
\[
        ab=T_{\xi_1\otimes\overline{\xi_2}\otimes\eta_1}^{\,*}T_{\eta_2},
\]
and adjoints are immediate.  Thus \(\mathcal C_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra.  By
Lemma \ref{lem:coeff}, \(X_{M_U}\) is the hereditary algebra generated by the
positive coefficients of \(\cH_U\).  If
\(s=\sum_iT_{\xi_i}^*T_{\xi_i}\) is a finite sum of such generators, the
functions \(g_\varepsilon(t)=t(t+\varepsilon)^{-1}\) form the standard
contractive approximate unit for the hereditary algebra they generate.
Polynomial approximation to \(g_\varepsilon^{1/2}\) by polynomials with zero
constant term writes \(g_\varepsilon(s)\) as a norm limit of \(c^*c\) with
\(c\in\mathcal C_U\), and the product formula above makes each \(c^*c\) a
positive coefficient from a finite direct sum of finite fusions.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[intersection of boundary supports]\label{lem:intersection}
Let \(q_U=q_{X_{M_U}}\).  For \(U,W\subset V\), with \(I=U\cap W\),
\[
        0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_I .                                  \tag{3.1}
\]
Consequently, if \(I=\cap_{i=1}^mU_i\), then
\[
q_{U_m}q_{U_{m-1}}\cdots q_{U_1}q_{U_2}\cdots q_{U_{m-1}}q_{U_m}
   \in q_I\cA_Mq_I .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H_R=H_R(V,V)\) be the graph-product correspondence of
Charlesworth--de Santiago--Hayes--Jekel--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Nelson
(CDHJKN).  Their Theorem 5.4 identifies
\(H_R\cong L^2M\otimes_{M_R}L^2M\), and Proposition 5.5 gives
\[
        H_A\otimes_M H_B\cong
        \bigoplus_{C\subset A\cap B}H_C^{\oplus m(A,B,C)} .        \tag{3.2}
\]
Since \(H_A\cong\overline{H_A}\), induction on the number of factors shows
that a finite fusion with labels \(A_1,\ldots,A_n\) decomposes as a direct sum
of \(H_C\)'s with \(C\subset A_1\cap\cdots\cap A_n\).

Take positive coefficients \(h=T_\xi^*T_\xi\) from finite direct sums of
\(\cF_W\) and \(k=T_\eta^*T_\eta\) from finite direct sums of \(\cF_U\).  Then
\[
        hkh=T_{\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta}^{\,*}
             T_{\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta}.                    \tag{3.3}
\]
By the label induction, the vector in (3.3) lies in a direct sum of
\(H_C\)'s with \(C\subset I\).  Lemma \ref{lem:dsupport}, and
\(X_{M_C}\subset X_{M_I}\), imply \(hkh\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\).

Choose the contractive approximate units \(h_\alpha\subset X_{M_W}\) and
\(k_\beta\subset X_{M_U}\) supplied by Lemma \ref{lem:dsupport}; by the
preceding paragraph and norm-closedness,
\(h_\alpha k_\beta h_\alpha\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\).  If
\(\phi\in A_M^{\sharp_J}\) vanishes on \(X_{M_I}\), Lemma \ref{lem:support}
gives \(\widetilde\phi(h_\alpha k_\beta h_\alpha)=0\).  In \(\cA_M\),
\(h_\alpha\to q_W\) and \(k_\beta\to q_U\) strongly*, and multiplication is
separately normal on bounded sets.  Hence first letting \(\alpha\) tend to
infinity and then \(\beta\) gives
\[
        \widetilde\phi(q_Wq_Uq_W)=0 .
\]
All normal functionals annihilating \(X_{M_I}\) therefore vanish on
\(q_Wq_Uq_W\), so Lemma \ref{lem:support} places this positive contraction in
\(q_I\cA_Mq_I\), proving (3.1).

The palindromic statement follows by induction: if the product for
\(U_1,\ldots,U_{m-1}\) is a positive contraction supported by
\(q_J\), \(J=\cap_{i<m}U_i\), then compression by \(q_{U_m}\) is dominated by
\(q_{U_m}q_Jq_{U_m}\), which is supported by \(q_{J\cap U_m}\) by (3.1).
\end{proof}

\section*{From finitely many relative boundaries to the compact boundary}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:finite}
Let \(N\) be a separable non-amenable finite factor.  Let
\(X_1,\ldots,X_m\) be boundary pieces, \(q_i=q_{X_i}\), and suppose
\[
        R:=q_mq_{m-1}\cdots q_1q_2\cdots q_{m-1}q_m\in q_0\cA_Nq_0. \tag{4.1}
\]
If \(N\) is properly proximal relative to every \(X_i\), then \(N\) is properly
proximal.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Assume not.  By DKEP Lemma 8.5, there is an \(N\)-central state \(\omega\) on
\(\widetilde S(N)\) with \(\omega|_N=\tau\).  Fix \(i\), set \(q=q_i\).  Since
\(q\) commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\), for \(y\in\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\),
\[
 [q^\perp yq^\perp,a]=q^\perp[y,a]q^\perp=0,\qquad a\in JNJ,
\]
so \(q^\perp yq^\perp\in\widetilde S(N)\).  If \(\omega(q^\perp)>0\), then
\(y\mapsto\omega(q^\perp yq^\perp)/\omega(q^\perp)\) is an \(N\)-central state
on \(\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\).  Its restriction to \(N\) is a normal central
state on the factor \(N\), hence is \(\tau\), contradicting relative proper
proximality.  Thus \(\omega(q_i^\perp)=0\) for every \(i\).

Extend \(\omega\) to a state on the C\(^*\)-algebra \(\cA_N\).  In the GNS
representation, each \(q_i\) fixes the cyclic vector, hence \(\omega(R)=1\).
Since \(0\le R\le q_0\), we get \(\omega(q_0)=1\).  DKEP Lemma 8.5 uses the
canonical \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodular u.c.p. map
\[
        \Theta_0:\mathbb B(L^2N)\longrightarrow
        (\mathbb K(L^2N)^{\sharp_J})^*=q_0\cA_Nq_0
\]
induced by the normal-bidual quotient; it satisfies \(\Theta_0(1)=q_0\) and
\(\Theta_0(x)=xq_0\) for \(x\in N\).  Since \(q_0\cA_Nq_0\subset
\widetilde S(N)\), the state \(\psi(T)=\omega(\Theta_0(T))\) is \(N\)-central
on \(\mathbb B(L^2N)\), and
\(\psi(x)=\omega(xq_0)=\omega(x)=\tau(x)\) for \(x\in N\).  This is a Connes
hypertrace, contradicting non-amenability of \(N\).
\end{proof}

\section*{A relative Bass--Serre paradox}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:BS}
Let \(N=P*_{B}Q\) with trace-preserving expectations and \(Q=B\bbarotimes A\).
Assume that \(A\) has a trace-zero unitary \(a\), and that \(P\) has unitaries
\(w_1,w_2\) satisfying
\[
        E_B(w_i)=0,\qquad E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
Then \(N\) is properly proximal relative to \(X_B\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H_P=L^2P\ominus L^2B\) and \(H_Q=L^2Q\ominus L^2B\).  The AFP normal
form is
\[
 L^2N=L^2B\oplus\!\!\bigoplus_{n\ge1,\ i_1\ne\cdots\ne i_n}
 H_{i_1}\otimes_B\cdots\otimes_B H_{i_n},\quad i_j\in\{P,Q\}.
\]
Let \(p\) and \(q\) be the projections onto the summands whose first letter is
\(P\), respectively \(Q\); then \(1=e_B+p+q\).  If \(x\in P\ominus B\), right
multiplication by \(x\) preserves the first letter except for the vacuum and
one-letter \(P\)-words, and
\[
 [p,JxJ]=(1-e_B)JxJ e_B-e_BJxJ(1-e_B),\qquad [q,JxJ]=0.             \tag{5.1}
\]
The two terms in (5.1) are in the \(N,JNJ\)-bimodule generated by \(e_B\), so
they lie in \(X_B\).  For \(x\in Q\ominus B\) the same statement holds with
\(p\) and \(q\) interchanged, while \(B\) commutes with \(p,q\).  Since these
subspaces generate \(N\), DKEP Lemma 6.1 gives \(p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)\).

Let \(\varphi\) be an \(N\)-central state on \(S_{X_B}(N)\) with normal
restriction to \(N\).  Left multiplication by \(w_i\in P\ominus B\) maps
\(qL^2N\) into \(pL^2N\), and
\(q\,w_1^*w_2\,q=0\) because \(w_1^*w_2\in P\ominus B\).  Thus
\(w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p\).  Similarly \(a p a^*\le q\), since
\(a\in Q\ominus B\).  Centrality gives
\[
        2\varphi(q)\le\varphi(p)\le\varphi(q),
\]
so \(\varphi(p)=\varphi(q)=0\).

The unitary \(h=w_1a\) is \(B\)-Haar: every non-zero power is an alternating
reduced word in centered \(P\)- and \(Q\)-letters, hence \(E_B(h^n)=0\) for
\(n\ne0\).  Therefore, for \(m\ne n\),
\[
 (h^me_Bh^{-m})(h^ne_Bh^{-n})
   =h^m e_BE_B(h^{\,n-m})e_Bh^{-n}=0 .
\]
The projections \(h^ne_Bh^{-n}\) are pairwise orthogonal and have the same
\(\varphi\)-value, so that value is \(0\).  Hence
\(\varphi(1)=\varphi(e_B+p+q)=0\), a contradiction.  By DKEP Theorem 6.2,
\(N\) is properly proximal relative to \(X_B\).
\end{proof}

\section*{Application to graph products}

Fix \(v\in V\), put \(L_v=\Lk_\Gamma(v)\), and use the standard graph-product
amalgamated free product decomposition
\[
        M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}
        (M_{L_v}\bbarotimes M_v).                                \tag{6.1}
\]
Let \(C(v)=\{r\ne v:r\not\sim_\Gamma v\}\).  Since \(\Gamma^c\) is connected,
\(C(v)\ne\emptyset\).  If \(C(v)\) contains distinct \(r,t\), choose
trace-zero unitaries \(u_r,u_t\) and set \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_t\).  If
\(C(v)=\{r\}\), connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge3\) give
\(s\in L_v\) with \(s\not\sim_\Gamma r\); set \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_su_r\).  In both
cases graph-product reduced-word calculus gives
\[
        E_{M_{L_v}}(w_i)=0,\qquad E_{M_{L_v}}(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
A trace-zero unitary in \(M_v\) supplies the unitary \(a\) in (6.1).  Proposition
\ref{prop:BS} shows that \(M\) is properly proximal relative to
\(X_{M_{L_v}}\) for every \(v\).

Enumerate \(V=\{v_1,\ldots,v_m\}\).  Since no vertex lies in its own link,
\(\cap_iL_{v_i}=\emptyset\).  Lemma \ref{lem:intersection} gives the
palindromic product of the \(q_{L_{v_i}}\)'s in
\(q_{\emptyset}\cA_Mq_{\emptyset}\).  But \(M_\emptyset=\C\), so
\(X_{M_\emptyset}=\mathbb K(L^2M)\).

Finally, CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 implies that a join-irreducible graph product with
\(|V|\ge3\) and trace-zero vertex unitaries is a factor.  CDHJKN Proposition
6.3 implies it is not amenable: the amenable exceptional case would force an
isolated two-point non-edge component of \(\Gamma^c\), impossible because
\(\Gamma^c\) is connected and has at least three vertices.  Thus \(M\) is a
separable non-amenable finite factor.  Lemma \ref{lem:finite}, applied to the
finite family \(\{X_{M_{L_v}}:v\in V\}\), proves that \(M\) is properly
proximal.
