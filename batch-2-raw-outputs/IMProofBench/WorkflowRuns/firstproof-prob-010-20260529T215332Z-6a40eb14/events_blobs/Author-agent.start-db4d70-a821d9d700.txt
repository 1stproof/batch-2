### 4. Boundary-support intersection lemma

## 3. Section-by-section mathematical audit

### Problem statement and interpretation

The interpretation of “irreducible” as “not a nontrivial graph join,” equivalently connected complement graph, is reasonable. The original problem statement is slightly ambiguous because it does not explicitly say the two subgraphs in the forbidden decomposition are nonempty/proper, but `answer.tex` records an adopted interpretation. That is acceptable.

The specialization of DKEP Lemma 8.5 to finite factors is acceptable, since the trivial group action is ergodic on the center exactly when the algebra is a factor.

### Boundary support bookkeeping: serious gap

The first major technical lemma claims
\[
q_X=p_Nr_X=p_N\rho_X,
\]
where \(r_X\) is the ordinary open support of \(X\) and \(\rho_X\) that of \(K_X(N)\). This equality is essential for the later support-intersection argument.

Parts of the setup are consistent with DKEP Proposition 2.10 and the support discussion before DKEP Remark 2.8, which do describe normal biduals using support projections and warn that the canonical map into the normal bidual is not multiplicative. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) But the proof as written still has an essential gap:

- The comparison of \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) is not rigorously established. The proof says that for \(S,R\in K_X^L\), “after refining partitions” one may arrange \(Se,Re\in A_NX\) on each product projection. This is a nontrivial use of DKEP Corollary 3.2 and Proposition 2.4, but the argument does not spell out the required projection families, the passage from \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,2}\)-closure to exact block membership, or the common refinement for two different operators. DKEP Proposition 2.4 has a precise block criterion for strong bimodule closure, but `answer.tex` does not verify its hypotheses in the needed rectangular-left-ideal setting.
- The conclusion that \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) have the same simultaneous-normal annihilator is central to the whole proof. As written, it is an unproved essential lemma, not a routine bookkeeping step.
- Since the later proof computes products of support projections using \(q_X=p_Nr_X\), failure to justify this equality invalidates Lemma `intersection` downstream.

This is a major reason the solution is not yet rigorous.

### Lemma “subalgebra fusion”

The first half, identifying \(X_B\) with the hereditary algebra generated by the operators \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) for \(H={}_NL^2N_B\), is supported by DKEP Example 5.11. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) The self-adjointness argument for generating by \(s^*s\) is plausible.

However, the final assertion that every coefficient operator of a bounded vector in an arbitrary Hilbert direct sum of copies of \(\mathcal H_B\) belongs to \(K_{X_B}(N)\) relies on applying DKEP Theorem 5.10 to an infinite direct sum. The proof says “cross coefficients are zero and same-summand coefficients are as above,” which is not enough by itself: one must verify that the chosen bounded cyclic set is cyclic for the Hilbert direct sum and satisfies the exact coefficient condition in Theorem 5.10. This is likely repairable, but the proof is compressed and should explicitly handle infinite direct sums and boundedness of vectors.

### Lemma “finite fusion calculus”: serious gap

This lemma is another essential technical bridge. It claims stability of DKEP mixing under finite Connes fusions, conjugates, and Hilbert direct sums, and then uses that to build the \(*\)-algebras \(\mathcal T_U\).

The proof is not sufficiently rigorous:

- DKEP Theorem 5.10 condition (1) requires a cyclic set of bounded vectors whose coefficient operators lie in the appropriate \(YB(\cdot)X\) space, not merely that they are “\(X\)-compact.” The proof says “since \(K_X(N)\) is hereditary and \(X\mathbb B(L^2N)X\subset K_X(N)\), the new coefficients are \(X\)-compact.” That does not directly verify the cyclic-vector hypothesis of Theorem 5.10.
- Closure of mixing under fusion is nontrivial because \(K_X(N)\) is not an ideal in \(\mathbb B(L^2N)\). One needs an explicit argument showing products of cyclic coefficients remain in the correct \(X\)-controlled class. The proof only sketches this.
- The assertion that \(\mathcal T_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra is plausible from the formulas \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\) and \(T_{\bar \xi}=T_\xi^*\), but the proof should carefully track order conventions and boundedness of fused vectors.

Because Lemma `finite fusion calculus` is used in the support-intersection lemma, this is an essential unresolved gap.

### Monotonicity \(X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_U}\)

The statement \(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_U}\) for \(Z\subset U\) is correct, and the resulting inclusion \(X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_U}\) is plausible from DKEP Example 3.4. This part is fine.

### Lemma “intersection of boundary supports”: dependent on unresolved machinery

The use of CDHJKN Theorem 5.4 and Proposition 5.5 is broadly correct: their fusion rule gives summands indexed by \(C\subset A\cap B\). ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

However, the proof of the key claim
\[
a\,b\,c\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M)
\]
for \(a,c\in\mathcal P_W\), \(b\in\mathcal P_U\), depends critically on the previous finite-fusion lemma. Since that lemma is not fully justified, this claim remains unproved.

Additional issues:

- The passage from monomials such as
  \[
  T_{\alpha_1}^*T_{\alpha_2}T_{\beta_1}^*T_{\beta_2}T_{\alpha_3}^*T_{\alpha_4}
  \]
  to a single \(T_\zeta\) is plausible but should be written with precise fusion-order conventions. The current proof only says “replacing adjoints by conjugates and repeatedly using (1).”
- The support-limit argument in \(A^{**}\) is only valid if Lemma `support` is valid. Since that support lemma has an unresolved annihilator/support comparison, the conclusion
  \[
  0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_{U\cap W}
  \]
  is not rigorously established.

This is the central technical bottleneck of the whole proof.

### Lemma “from finitely many relative boundaries to compact boundary”

This part is mostly sound. The compression argument with \(q_i^\perp\), the GNS argument for the palindromic product, and the final hypertrace contradiction are plausible. DKEP Lemma 8.5 supplies the normal-bidual operator system framework and the natural u.c.p. maps used in this step. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

Minor points needing clarification:

- The state extension from \(\widetilde S(N)\) to \(\mathcal A_N\) is only a GNS device and need not be \(N\)-central. The proof implicitly handles this correctly later by using the original state on \(q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0\), but this should be stated more carefully.
- The canonical map \(\Theta_0\) should be cited more precisely from the proof of DKEP Lemma 8.5.

I would not reject the solution on this lemma alone.

### Relative Bass–Serre proposition

The Bass–Serre argument is plausible but still underwritten.

Issues:

- The AFP normal-form index notation
  \[
  i_1\ne\cdots\ne i_n
  \]
  is inaccurate if read literally; it should mean \(i_j\ne i_{j+1}\).
- The commutator formula for \(p,q\) with \(JxJ\) is asserted after a verbal reduced-word discussion. This is believable, but for a rigorous proof it needs a more explicit verification on all reduced-word summands.
- The invocation of DKEP Lemma 6.1 requires translating the lemma into the right-action bimodule convention used in the definition of \(S_X(M)\). DKEP Lemma 6.1 is not literally phrased as “for a fixed operator, the set of \(x\) whose commutator with \(JxJ\) is \(X\)-compact is a von Neumann algebra”; this translation should be written out. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The paradoxical inequalities
\[
w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p,\qquad apa^*\le q
\]
and the \(B\)-Haar unitary argument are otherwise convincing.

### Graph-product application

This final section is mostly correct, assuming the previous technical propositions.

- The AFP decomposition
  \[
  M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}(M_{L_v}\bar\otimes M_v)
  \]
  is standard for graph products and is consistent with CF17. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/journals/jncg/articles/14680))
- The graph combinatorics for choosing \(w_1,w_2\) are correct.
- The expectation computations onto \(M_{L_v}\) are plausible from reduced-word theory.
- The intersection \(\bigcap_v L_v=\varnothing\) is correct.
- The use of CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 to get a diffuse full factor is correct under the trace-zero unitary hypothesis. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))
- The nonamenability conclusion from diffuseness plus fullness is correct: a diffuse amenable finite factor is hyperfinite and not full.

But this final application depends on the unresolved support-intersection and finite-fusion machinery.

## 4. Overall verdict

`answer.tex` is well organized and appears to have the right high-level strategy. The final graph-product reduction and the Bass–Serre paradox are plausible. However, the proof is **not answer-ready** because several essential technical lemmas are not rigorously proved as written, especially:

1. the equality of DKEP normal-bidual supports \(q_X=p_Nr_X=p_N\rho_X\);
2. stability of relative mixing under finite Connes fusion/direct sums in the precise DKEP sense;
3. the key boundary-support intersection inequality \(q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_{U\cap W}\).

These are not cosmetic omissions; they are the core operator-algebraic mechanisms needed to pass from relative proper proximality for the link boundaries to ordinary proper proximality. Until these are proved with precise DKEP hypotheses and exact normal-bidual/support arguments, the solution remains incomplete.
