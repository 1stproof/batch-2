## Referee report on revised `answer.tex`

### Verdict

The revision fixes several earlier problems, especially the incorrect “central \(p_{\rm nor}\)” shortcut and the misuse of DKEP Proposition 2.6. However, `answer.tex` is **still not answer-ready**. The main remaining fatal gap is the unproved identification
\[
X_{\mathcal H_U}=X_{M_U},\qquad \mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M,
\]
and the subsequent use of this identification to construct approximate units in \(X_{M_U}\) from finite Connes-fusion coefficients. This is essential for Lemma 1, the boundary-corner intersection lemma, and therefore for the entire finite-boundary reduction.

## 3. Previous concerns addressed

### 3.1 Incorrect centrality of \(p_{\rm nor}\)

This has been fixed. The revised proof no longer calls the normal-support projection central. This is important because DKEP explicitly note that the canonical embedding into the normal bidual is not generally multiplicative precisely because \(p_{\rm nor}\) need not be central. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

### 3.2 Annihilator equality \(X^\perp=K_X^\perp\)

The revision substantially improves this part by invoking DKEP Theorem 5.6. The argument is now plausible: if \(\phi\in A^{\sharp_J}\) vanishes on \(X\), then since \(X\) is hereditary, \(\phi\) vanishes on \(XAX\), so Theorem 5.6 gives \(\phi(T)=0\) for \(T\in K_X^{\infty,1}\), hence for \(T\in K_X\). This missing intermediate phrase, “because \(XAX\subset X\) and is norm dense in \(X\),” should be written explicitly, but the underlying citation is now appropriate.

### 3.3 Finite boundary criterion

The palindromic criterion is now basically sound. The compression by \(q_i^\perp\), the GNS argument for the palindromic product, and the final hypertrace contradiction are credible. This section is not the main obstruction.

## 4. Remaining fatal issue: \(X_{\mathcal H_U}=X_{M_U}\) is not proved

The proof says:

> Thus DKEP Example 5.11 identifies the coefficient boundary of \(\mathcal H_U\) with the subalgebra boundary \(X_{M_U}\) of Example 3.4.

This is too fast and, as written, unsupported.

DKEP Example 5.11 defines \(X_H\) for an \(M\)-\(N\) correspondence \(H\) as the hereditary \(C^*\)-algebra generated by coefficient operators \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\). It then notes that if \(B\subset M\) and one considers \(L^2M\) as an \(M\)-\(B\) correspondence, then the resulting boundary piece is the subalgebra boundary of Example 3.4. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) But `answer.tex` uses instead the induced **\(M\)-\(M\)** correspondence
\[
\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M.
\]
The equality between the coefficient boundary of this induced correspondence and the Example 3.4 boundary \(X_{M_U}\) is an additional lemma. It may be true, but it is not stated by DKEP Example 5.11 in the form cited, and the proof in `answer.tex` only checks \(T_{\Omega_U}=e_{M_U}\). That proves one inclusion at best, not equality of hereditary coefficient algebras.

The missing direction is essential: one must show that coefficients of **all bounded vectors** in \(L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\) lie in \(X_{M_U}\), or at least have the same normal support as \(X_{M_U}\). Without this, the later claim that \(X_{M_U}\) is hereditarily generated by positive coefficients of \(\mathcal H_U\) is unproved.

## 5. Consequence: approximate-unit construction is not justified

Lemma 1 relies on the assertion:

> The boundary \(X_{M_U}\) has a contractive approximate unit whose elements are norm limits of finite sums of coefficients of finite Connes fusions of \(\mathcal H_U\) and \(\overline{\mathcal H_U}\).

This follows only if \(X_{M_U}\) is indeed the coefficient boundary of \(\mathcal H_U\), or if a separate support-level replacement is proved. Since the equality \(X_{\mathcal H_U}=X_{M_U}\) is not established, this approximate-unit construction remains unsupported.

This is not cosmetic: those approximate units are used to prove
\[
s_W(s_UA_M^{**}s_U)s_W\subset s_{U\cap W}A_M^{**}s_{U\cap W},
\]
which is then used to prove the crucial normal-bidual corner inclusion
\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W\subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A_Mq_{U\cap W}.
\]
Thus the central boundary-intersection lemma still rests on an unproved essential lemma.

## 6. Additional technical gap in Lemma 1

Even granting the coefficient-boundary equality, the proof should explicitly record two further facts:

1. In the tracial setting,
   \[
   \overline{\mathcal H_U}\cong \mathcal H_U
   \]
   as an \(M\)-\(M\) correspondence. The proof uses conjugate correspondences inside finite fusions but does not state this identification.

2. The passage from CDHJKN’s fusion decomposition to support containment of all diagonal and off-diagonal coefficients in \(s_I A^{**}s_I\) is only sketched. CDHJKN provide the fusion decomposition, but the support conclusion requires a separate direct-sum coefficient-support lemma. The text mentions a “standard \(2\times2\) positivity argument,” but for a standalone rigorous proof this should be stated as a lemma and proved or cited.

These are secondary to the missing \(X_{\mathcal H_U}=X_{M_U}\) proof, but they are part of the same unresolved support-calculus block.

## 7. Relative Bass–Serre proposition

The relative Bass–Serre argument is plausible but still compressed. The commutator formulas, projection inequalities
\[
w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p,\qquad apa^*\le q,
\]
and the \(B\)-Haar claim for \(h=w_1a\) are standard reduced-word arguments, but they are not fully written out. I would not reject solely on this section, but it remains more of a sketch than a fully detailed proof.

## 8. Final graph-product application

Assuming the earlier lemmas, the final graph-product application is sound. The graph combinatorics for choosing \(w_1,w_2\) are correct. The use of CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.5 to get factoriality and Proposition 6.3 to get nonamenability is appropriate: a universal vertex in \(\Gamma\) would be an isolated vertex in \(\Gamma^c\), and an exceptional non-edge pair adjacent to all other vertices would be an isolated edge component in \(\Gamma^c\), both impossible when \(\Gamma^c\) is connected with at least three vertices. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

---

## 9. Summary

The revision addresses several earlier objections, especially the false centrality statement and the annihilator argument. However, the proof still has an essential gap in the identification and use of the graph-product boundary piece \(X_{M_U}\) as a coefficient boundary for \(\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\). Because Lemma 1 depends on this identification and Lemma 1 is central to the proof, the solution is not yet complete.
