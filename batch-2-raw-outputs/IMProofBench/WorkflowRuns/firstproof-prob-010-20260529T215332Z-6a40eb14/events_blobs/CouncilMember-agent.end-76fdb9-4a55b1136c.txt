Both lemmas point in a reasonable direction, but they lean on machinery the current write-up under-cites. Constructive notes:

**Lemma 1 (finite boundary criterion).** The cutting argument is essentially right but tacitly uses:

(i) *$q_{X_i}$ commutes with the left $N$-action on $\widetilde S(N)$,* i.e., $q_{X_i}\in N'\cap\widetilde S(N)$. This is what guarantees the cut state $\omega_i(\cdot)=\omega(q_{X_i}^\perp\cdot q_{X_i}^\perp)/\omega(q_{X_i}^\perp)$ stays $N$-central *and* tracial on $N$ (for $N$ a factor with unique $\tau$). DKEP's universal-representation construction (Sec. 8) delivers this centrality, but Lemma 8.5 itself doesn't — you should cite the construction explicitly.

(ii) The clean form of "compact corner = bidual of $\mathbb B$" is $\mathcal K(L^2N)^{**}\cong\mathbb B(L^2N)$ as vN algebras, with this $\mathbb B(L^2N)$ realized inside $\widetilde S(N)$ as $q_{\mathcal K}\widetilde S(N)q_{\mathcal K}$. The restricted $\omega$ is then literally a Connes hypertrace. Spell out the identification.

**Lemma 2 (links fill the boundary).** Two distinct steps are conflated and should be separated:

(a) *Commutativity of the $q_{X_U}$.* The Jones projections commute pairwise — $e_{M_U}e_{M_W}=e_{M_U\cap M_W}=e_{M_{U\cap W}}$ (commuting square for graph products). This is what makes "product of $q$'s" a projection and equal to the meet.

(b) *Direction supplied by CDHJKN.* The assignment $B\mapsto X_B$ is monotone — $B\subset B'\Rightarrow e_B\le e_{B'}\Rightarrow e_B\in X_{B'}$ by hereditariness, hence $X_B\subset X_{B'}$. So $q_{X_{U\cap W}}\le q_{X_U}\wedge q_{X_W}$ is *trivial* from $M_{U\cap W}\subset M_U,M_W$. The *non-trivial* direction $q_{X_U}\wedge q_{X_W}\le q_{X_{U\cap W}}$ is what CDHJKN Theorem 0.4 provides via the bimodule decomposition $L^2\otimes_{M_U}L^2\otimes_{M_W}L^2=\bigoplus_{U'\subset U\cap W}L^2\otimes_{M_{U'}}L^2$.

Currently the proof writes "no larger boundary piece survives" and elides this. Spell out the two directions.

(c) *Language bridge.* CDHJKN argues at the bimodule level; DKEP at the hereditary-$C^*$ level. The bridge "$X_U$ corresponds to $L^2(M)\otimes_{M_U}L^2(M)$ via Connes' correspondence" deserves a sentence — without it, "Main Theorem 0.4 fusion rules imply..." feels like a black box.

**Minor.** "$\bigcap_v\Lk(v)=\varnothing$ because there are no loops" — this holds unconditionally since each $\Lk(v)$ excludes $v$. Irreducibility and $|V|\ge 3$ enter only in supplying complement-neighbours for Prop. 1, not here.
