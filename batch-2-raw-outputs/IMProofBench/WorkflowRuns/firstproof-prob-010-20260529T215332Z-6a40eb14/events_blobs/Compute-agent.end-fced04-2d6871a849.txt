## Bottom line

I do **not** find in DKEP an explicit statement of the correspondence support formula
\[
q_{\mathcal H\otimes_M\mathcal K}=q_{\mathcal H}\wedge q_{\mathcal K}.
\]
The current wording in `answer.tex` claiming this from “Example 5.11 and the proof of Theorem 5.10” overstates what DKEP says. DKEP gives the coefficient boundary piece \(X_{\mathcal H}\), the formula \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\), and the normal-bidual support projection for a boundary piece, but it does not appear to prove the lattice identity for fusion supports.

The issue is not cosmetic: DKEP only says the support projection of \((\mathbb K_X(M)^\sharp_J)^*\) commutes with \(M\) and \(JMJ\), not that it is central in the ambient normal bidual and not that two such supports commute with each other. Without centrality/commutation, “support of a product/fusion = meet” is not a formal consequence of hereditary-subalgebra support calculus.

## Exact DKEP locations

1. Boundary pieces and \(X_B\):
   - DKEP Section 3, after Corollary 3.2: an \(M\)-boundary piece is a hereditary \(C^*\)-subalgebra \(X\subset B(L^2M)\) whose multiplier algebra contains ultraweakly dense parts of \(M\) and \(JMJ\). See `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt` around lines 653-659.
   - Example 3.4 defines \(X_B\) for \(B\subset M\) as the closure of the span of
     \[
     x_1Jy_1J\,T\,Jy_2Jx_2,\qquad T\in e_BB(L^2M)e_B,
     \]
     and says equivalently that it is the hereditary algebra generated by \(xJyJ e_B\). See `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt` around lines 661-670.

2. Normal-bidual support:
   - DKEP Proposition 2.10 is the ambient normal-bidual corner statement. More concretely, just before Lemma 8.5 DKEP writes
     \[
     \mathbb K_X(M)^\sharp_J=\mathbb K_X(M)^{M\sharp M}\cap \mathbb K_X(M)^{JMJ\sharp JMJ}
     \]
     and recalls that \((B(L^2M)^\sharp_J)^*\) is a von Neumann algebra containing \(M\) and \(JMJ\), while \((\mathbb K_X(M)^\sharp_J)^*\) is a corner whose support projection commutes with both \(M\) and \(JMJ\). See `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt` around lines 1920-1928.
   - This is the exact source for the support \(q_X\). It gives commutation with \(M,JMJ\), not centrality in \((B(L^2M)^\sharp_J)^*\).

3. Correspondence coefficient boundary:
   - DKEP correspondence preliminaries before Theorem 5.10 give Connes fusion and the operator formula
     \[
     T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi
     \]
     for bounded vectors. See `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt` around lines 1245-1250.
   - Theorem 5.10 characterizes mixing relative to boundary pieces. See `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt` around lines 1250-1265.
   - Example 5.11 defines
     \[
     X_{\mathcal H}\subset B(L^2M)
     \]
     as the hereditary algebra generated by \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) for bounded \(\xi,\eta\in\mathcal H\), and says it is the smallest boundary piece making \(\mathcal H\) mixing relative to \(X_{\mathcal H}\times Y_{\mathcal H}\). See `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt` around lines 1335-1343.

## What support calculus is justified

The direct-sum formula is safe:
\[
q_{\mathcal H\oplus\mathcal K}=q_{\mathcal H}\vee q_{\mathcal K}.
\]
Indeed, the coefficient algebra of a direct sum is the hereditary algebra generated by the two diagonal coefficient algebras, with cross terms controlled by the usual \(2\times2\) positivity/Douglas factorization argument. Hence its support is the join of the two supports. This is a short proof from Example 5.11, but I did not find it stated as a named lemma.

For fusion, DKEP gives only the raw coefficient formula. For bounded simple tensors,
\[
T_{\xi\otimes\eta}^*T_{\xi'\otimes\eta'}
 =T_\xi^*(T_\eta^*T_{\eta'})T_{\xi'}.
\]
This says the fusion coefficient algebra is built from the two coefficient algebras by a product/sandwich construction. If the two normal support projections \(q_{\mathcal H},q_{\mathcal K}\) were known to commute (for example in a central/commutative boundary situation), then the usual open-projection argument would give support \(q_{\mathcal H}q_{\mathcal K}=q_{\mathcal H}\wedge q_{\mathcal K}\). But DKEP does not give this commutation.

This distinction matters. For arbitrary noncommuting open projections \(p,q\), the support of a product such as \(pqp\) need not be \(p\wedge q\): in \(B(\mathbb C^2)\), take \(p\) the projection onto \(e_1\) and \(q\) the projection onto \((e_1+e_2)/\sqrt2\). Then \(p\wedge q=0\), but \(pqp\) has support \(p\). Thus the desired fusion/meet identity cannot be justified merely by saying “support of product = meet” unless one proves centrality/commutation or a special graph-product replacement.

## \(L^2(M)\otimes_B L^2(M)\) and \(X_B\)

This part is fine, with one nuance. DKEP Example 5.11 explicitly says that if \(B\subset M\) and \(L^2M\) is viewed as an \(M\)-\(B\) correspondence, then its coefficient boundary piece is the \(X_B\) of Example 3.4. For the \(M\)-\(M\) correspondence
\[
\mathcal H_B=L^2(M)\otimes_B L^2(M),
\]
the same boundary piece is obtained: for the cyclic vector \(\zeta=\hat1\otimes_B\hat1\), the coefficient operator is \(e_B\); translating \(\zeta\) by the left and right \(M\)-actions gives the generators \(xJyJ e_B\) appearing in Example 3.4, and the hereditary algebra generated by all coefficients is exactly \(X_B\). So \(q_{\mathcal H_B}=q_{X_B}\) is justified.

## CDHJKN check

Assuming the missing support-fusion lemma, the graph-product meet computation is correct.

CDHJKN Theorem 5.4 states the bimodule decomposition
\[
{}_{M_{V_1}}L^2(M)_{M_{V_2}}
\cong
\bigoplus_{U\subseteq V_1\cap V_2}
\left(L^2(M_{V_1})\otimes_{M_U}L^2(M_{V_2})\right)^{\oplus k_{\Gamma}(V_1,V_2,U)}.
\]
See `data/CDHJKN_2404.08150.txt` around lines 1035-1058.

CDHJKN Proposition 5.5 gives the fusion rule
\[
\mathscr H_{U_1}(V_1,V_2)\otimes_{M_{V_2}}\mathscr H_{U_2}(V_2,V_3)
\cong
\bigoplus_{W\subseteq U_1\cap U_2}
\mathscr H_W(V_1,V_3)^{\oplus k_{\Gamma_2}(U_1,U_2,W)}.
\]
See `data/CDHJKN_2404.08150.txt` around lines 1065-1077. Applying this with \(V_1=V_2=V_3=V\), \(U_1=U\), \(U_2=W\), gives
\[
\mathcal H_U\otimes_M\mathcal H_W
\cong
\bigoplus_{Z\subseteq U\cap W}
\mathcal H_Z^{\oplus k_{\Gamma}(U,W,Z)}.
\]

The “empty relative word gives \(U\cap W\)” point is also correct: the empty word is relatively reduced vacuously, and in the definition of \(k_\Gamma(U,W,Z)\) its associated set is
\[
\{v\in U\cap W: v\sim w_j\text{ for all letters }w_j\}=U\cap W
\]
because there are no letters. Its multiplicity contribution is the empty product, hence \(1\). All other summands have \(Z\subseteq U\cap W\), and monotonicity \(Z\subseteq U\cap W\Rightarrow q_{X_Z}\le q_{X_{U\cap W}}\) follows from \(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_{U\cap W}}\) and DKEP Example 3.4. Therefore the join of the supports in the CDHJKN decomposition is \(q_{X_{U\cap W}}\).

So the CDHJKN part really gives
\[
q_{\mathcal H_U\otimes_M\mathcal H_W}=q_{X_{U\cap W}}.
\]
What remains unproved is precisely the extra identification
\[
q_{\mathcal H_U\otimes_M\mathcal H_W}
=q_{\mathcal H_U}\wedge q_{\mathcal H_W}.
\]

## Finite-boundary compact-corner step

This step looks sound. For \(X=\mathbb K(L^2N)\), the normal bidual corner is
\[
(\mathbb K(L^2N)^\sharp_J)^*\cong \mathbb K(L^2N)^{**}\cong B(L^2N),
\]
since all functionals on \(\mathbb K(L^2N)\) are trace-class/normal for the relevant \(N\) and \(JNJ\) module structures. Thus
\[
q_{\mathcal K}\mathcal A_Nq_{\mathcal K}\cong B(L^2N).
\]
If a counterexample central state \(\omega\) on \(\widetilde S(N)\) is supported on \(q_{\mathcal K}\), then its restriction to this corner is an \(N\)-central state on \(B(L^2N)\) extending the trace on \(N\). That is exactly a Connes hypertrace, so for a finite factor it implies amenability. This part does not require the disputed fusion calculus.

## Suggested replacement wording

I would replace the current paragraph beginning “We shall also use the following correspondence support identities from DKEP...” with something more cautious:

> DKEP Example 5.11 associates to an \(M\)-\(M\) correspondence \(\mathcal H\) the coefficient boundary piece \(X_{\mathcal H}\), and the support \(q_{\mathcal H}\) means the support of \((\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}(M)^\sharp_J)^*\) inside \((B(L^2M)^\sharp_J)^*\). The direct-sum identity \(q_{\mathcal H\oplus\mathcal K}=q_{\mathcal H}\vee q_{\mathcal K}\) follows directly from the definition of \(X_{\mathcal H}\). We will also need the following support-fusion lemma for the specific Jones correspondences used below: \(q_{\mathcal H_U\otimes_M\mathcal H_W}=q_{\mathcal H_U}\wedge q_{\mathcal H_W}\). This lemma is not stated in DKEP; it must be proved separately (for instance by proving the relevant normal supports commute and then applying the coefficient formula \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\)).

If no separate proof of that last lemma is added, then the meet formula lemma in `answer.tex` currently has a real gap. The CDHJKN computation proves the support of the fused correspondence is \(q_{X_{U\cap W}}\), but DKEP as written does not identify that fused support with the meet of the two original supports.
