## Referee report

### Overall verdict

`answer.tex` **is not answer-ready**. It satisfies the LaTeX-format contract, but the mathematical proof has serious unsupported steps. The most important fatal gaps are:

1. The claimed **finite-boundary criterion** uses an invalid support argument for non-normal states on a von Neumann algebra projection lattice.
2. The claimed correspondence identities  
   \[
   q_{\mathcal H\oplus\mathcal K}=q_{\mathcal H}\vee q_{\mathcal K},\qquad
   q_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\mathcal K}=q_{\mathcal H}\wedge q_{\mathcal K}
   \]
   are not stated in the cited references and are not proved; the fusion/meet identity is highly nontrivial and essential.
3. The proof that the Bass–Serre projections \(p,q\) lie in \(S_{X_B}(N)\) is only sketched and does not rigorously establish the necessary commutator estimates in the amalgamated setting.
4. The final conclusion depends essentially on these unproved/invalid lemmas.

## Detailed audit

### 1. Problem statement and interpretation

The interpretation of “irreducible” as “not a non-trivial join”, equivalently connected complement graph, is reasonable and is explicitly recorded. This is important because the problem statement’s formulation of irreducibility is slightly imprecise about nonempty/proper subgraphs.

The definition of relative proper proximality via absence of an \(N\)-central state on \(S_{X_B}(N)\) normal on \(N\) is consistent with DKEP’s Theorem 6.2 for inclusions. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) This part is acceptable.

### 2. Boundary support bookkeeping

This section introduces a normal-bidual formalism and support projections \(q_X\). DKEP Lemma 8.5 does use the normal-bidual object \(\widetilde S_X(M)\) and says, under separability and center-ergodicity hypotheses, that proper proximality is equivalent to the absence of an \(M\)-central state on \(\widetilde S_X(M)\) restricting to the trace. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) For a factor and the trivial action, this is plausibly applicable.

However, the two support identities
\[
q_{\mathcal H\oplus\mathcal K_0}=q_{\mathcal H}\vee q_{\mathcal K_0},
\qquad
q_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\mathcal K_0}=q_{\mathcal H}\wedge q_{\mathcal K_0}
\tag{1}
\]
are not adequately justified. DKEP Example 5.11 defines the coefficient boundary piece \(X_{\mathcal H}\), but it does not state the projection identities above. The direct-sum identity is plausible, but the fusion identity is a substantial assertion about supports of coefficient boundary pieces under Connes fusion. It cannot be inserted with only the sentence “the support … is exactly the greatest lower bound” unless a precise theorem is cited or proved.

This is a fatal gap because Lemma 2, the graph-product meet formula, depends entirely on (1).

### 3. Lemma “finite boundary criterion”

The first compression argument is mostly plausible: if \(q=q_{X_i}\), then \(q^\perp Tq^\perp\) commutes with \(JNJ\), so it lies in \(\widetilde S(N)\), and an \(N\)-central state on \(\widetilde S(N)\) can be cut down if \(\omega(q^\perp)>0\).

The fatal problem is the next sentence:

> “A state which takes value \(1\) on each \(q_{X_i}\) is supported on their meet…”

This is false for arbitrary non-normal states on a von Neumann algebra unless extra hypotheses such as pairwise commutativity of the projections or normality of the state are available. DKEP only gives that \(q_X\) commutes with \(M\) and \(JMJ\), not that different \(q_X\)’s commute with each other. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) Also, the states in the normal-bidual criterion are not required to be normal on the ambient von Neumann algebra; they are only required to restrict normally to \(N\).

A concrete illustration: in \(B(\ell^2\oplus \ell^2)\), let \(p\) be projection onto the first summand and let \(q\) be projection onto the graph of the injective compact diagonal operator \(Ae_n=n^{-1}e_n\). Then \(p\wedge q=0\), but an ultralimit of vector states on normalized graph vectors \((e_n,Ae_n)\) gives a state \(\omega\) with \(\omega(p)=\omega(q)=1\) and \(\omega(p\wedge q)=0\). Thus the GNS argument in `answer.tex` is invalid without normality or commutativity.

Since this step is essential to conclude \(\omega(q_{\mathcal K})=1\), the finite-boundary criterion is not proved. Consequently, the final upgrade from relative proper proximality to ordinary proper proximality is unsupported.

### 4. Lemma “meet formula for graph-product boundary pieces”

The use of CDHJKN’s fusion rule for the Hilbert bimodules is broadly in the right direction: CDHJKN do provide a decomposition of graph-product bimodules and fusion rules. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743)) The claim that the empty relative word contributes the \(Z=U\cap W\) summand is also compatible with the multiplicity formula in Theorem 5.4. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

But the passage from a Hilbert-bimodule direct-sum/fusion decomposition to the projection equality
\[
q_{X_U}\wedge q_{X_W}=q_{X_{U\cap W}}
\]
again depends on the unproved support identities from the previous section. CDHJKN prove Hilbert-bimodule decompositions; they do not identify DKEP normal-bidual support projections for boundary pieces. Therefore this lemma is not established.

The monotonicity argument \(Z\subset I\Rightarrow q_{X_Z}\le q_{X_I}\) is plausible, since \(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_I}\) and \(X_I\) is hereditary, but it does not repair the missing support-fusion step.

### 5. Proposition “relative Bass–Serre paradox”

The paradoxical inequalities themselves are plausible:

- \(w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p\) follows from the centeredness and orthogonality assumptions.
- \(apa^*\le q\) follows from \(a\in Q\ominus B\).
- The \(B\)-Haar unitary argument with \(h=w_1a\) is also plausible.

However, the proof that \(p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)\) is not rigorous. The statement

> “Right multiplication by \(x\) changes the first letter type … only in finitely many cases … Each such cancellation term factors through \(L^2(B)\)…”

needs a full reduced-word computation. In an amalgamated free product, one must explicitly show for generators and then for algebraic reduced words that \([p,JxJ]\) and \([q,JxJ]\) lie in \(K^{\infty,1}_{X_B}(N)\). DKEP Lemma 6.1 can upgrade a verified generator condition to the generated von Neumann algebra, but it does not replace the missing computation. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

This gap is essential: without \(p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)\), the central state \(\varphi\) cannot be evaluated on \(p,q\), and the paradoxical argument does not prove relative proper proximality.

### 6. Application to graph products

The graph-product decomposition
\[
M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}}(M_{\operatorname{Lk}(v)}\bar\otimes M_v)
\]
is standard and plausible, though a citation would be helpful.

The construction of the unitaries \(w_1,w_2\) in the two complement-neighbor cases appears correct. In the singleton case \(C(v)=\{r\}\), connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) and \(|V|\ge 3\) indeed give a vertex \(s\ne v,r\) with \(r\not\sim s\), and then \(s\in \operatorname{Lk}(v)\).

The factoriality argument using CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.5 is essentially correct. The theorem says factoriality can fail via a universal vertex with nonfactor vertex algebra or via an exceptional nonedge pair with both vertex algebras two-dimensional; connectedness of the complement with at least three vertices rules out the relevant graph configurations. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

The nonamenability argument using CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.1 is also plausible. The proof omits condition (1) of that theorem, namely amenability of all vertex algebras outside \(V_2\), but condition (2) already gives a contradiction from any nonedge in a connected complement graph with at least three vertices. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

Nevertheless, the final conclusion depends on the unproved Bass–Serre proposition, the unproved meet formula, and the invalid finite-boundary criterion. Hence the application section does not complete the proof.

## Final assessment

The submitted `answer.tex` is well organized and the intended strategy may be promising, but it is not a complete rigorous proof. The finite-boundary criterion and the support-projection calculus require substantial additional work, and one of the stated arguments is mathematically false as written.
