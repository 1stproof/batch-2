## Referee report on revised `answer.tex`

### Verdict

The revision improves several presentation points and fixes the LaTeX-contract issues, but the proof is still **not answer-ready**. The main remaining problems are not cosmetic: several essential technical lemmas are still asserted with insufficient proof, especially the coefficient-boundary identification for \(L^2N\otimes_B L^2N\), the direct-sum/finite-fusion coefficient calculus, and the passage from coefficient products to normal-bidual support inequalities.

## Section-by-section audit

### 1. Problem statement and interpretation

This section is acceptable. The draft explicitly adopts the standard interpretation of irreducibility as join-irreducibility, equivalently connected complement graph. This addresses the ambiguity in the problem statement.

### 2. Support bookkeeping

This section is improved relative to the previous version. The one-normal support formula is now closer to DKEP Section 2.3, and the simultaneous-normal version is at least plausibly obtained through Proposition 2.10.

However, the proof still compresses a nontrivial construction into a citation. In particular, the statement

\[
(Y^{\sharp_J})^*=(pr_Y)\mathcal A_N(pr_Y)
\]

requires checking that the ordinary open support \(r_Y\) in \(A^{**}\) is the same support appearing under the simultaneous normal representation, and that the two normal structures interact exactly as claimed. DKEP gives the one-normal formula and then states a simultaneous inclusion result, but the equality in this exact corner form is not written out in the draft. This may be repairable, but in a self-contained proof it remains an essential lemma rather than a transparent citation.

The last assertion, \(q_X=pr_X\) for a boundary piece \(X\), is also too brief. The proof says DKEP Proposition 3.6 and Proposition 2.4 imply that functionals vanishing on \(X\) are precisely those vanishing on \(K_X(N)\). This is plausible, but the draft does not verify all hypotheses needed to conclude that \(K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\) is the **smallest** simultaneous strong bimodule generated by \(X\), nor does it spell out why this gives equality of the normal support projections of \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\). Since the later intersection lemma depends directly on \(q_U=pr_U\), this is still an essential point.

### 3. Subalgebra coefficient boundary

The revision fixes one previous problem: it no longer relies on Hilbert-space rank-one operators generating all of \(B(L^2B)\). It correctly notes that DKEP Example 3.4 allows arbitrary \(S\in B(L^2B)\).

But the proof still does not establish the full lemma as stated. It verifies the formula

\[
T_{\xi\otimes\bar\alpha}^{*}T_{\eta\otimes\bar\beta}
=
T_\xi^*(T_{\bar\alpha}^{*}T_{\bar\beta})T_\eta
\]

for bounded elementary vectors and concludes that “all coefficients” of \(\mathcal H_B\) lie in \(X_B\). This is a gap. The coefficient boundary of a correspondence is generated by coefficients of **all bounded vectors**, not only elementary bounded tensors. To pass from elementary tensors to arbitrary bounded vectors, one needs a density/closure argument with uniform boundedness of the associated \(T_\xi\)-operators, or an application of DKEP Theorem 5.10. The draft does not provide this.

This matters because the later proof uses this lemma to identify \(X_{M_U}\) with the coefficient boundary of \(L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\), and then constructs approximate units of \(X_{M_U}\) from finite-fusion coefficients. Without the full coefficient-boundary identification, the main support-intersection argument is not established.

### 4. Direct sums and finite fusions

The revision adds the missing multiplier hypothesis \(N,JNJ\subset M(X)\). That addresses one prior objection.

However, the proof remains too compressed at a crucial point. It says that for a direct sum, finite-support bounded vectors are cyclic dense and then “applying Theorem 5.10 once more” gives the conclusion for all bounded vectors. DKEP Theorem 5.10 requires a cyclic set of bounded vectors satisfying a specific \(T_\xi\in YB(L^2M,L^2N)X\) condition, not merely that the summands are individually relatively compact. The draft does not verify the required cyclic set for the direct sum, nor the closure/uniform boundedness details needed to pass from finite-support vectors to arbitrary bounded vectors.

The finite-fusion coefficient algebra \(\mathcal C_U\) is also still asserted rather than proved. The statement that finite products of coefficients are finite sums of coefficients after inserting conjugate factors is standard, but in this context it is an essential lemma because it feeds directly into \(hkh\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\). The draft should at least give a precise formula with conventions and explain how finite sums/direct sums are handled.

### 5. Boundary-support intersection lemma

This is the central technical lemma, and it still depends on unresolved inputs.

The CDHJKN fusion rule itself is valid and appropriately cited. The rule decomposes fusions over \(M_{V_2}\) as sums indexed by subsets of the intersection of the relevant labels. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

The problem is the operator-algebraic passage from coefficient products to support inequalities. The line

\[
hkh\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M)
\]

rests on the direct-sum/finite-fusion lemma and on the coefficient-boundary identification above. Since those are not rigorously proved, this step is not secure.

The limiting argument in the ordinary bidual is clearer than before, and the use of strong-* convergence of approximate units is a reasonable route. But it still assumes the unproved support formula \(q_U=pr_U\) for the original boundary \(X_{M_U}\), and it assumes the existence of approximate units of \(X_{M_U}\) lying in the specified norm-closed coefficient cones. Both assumptions trace back to earlier underproved lemmas.

Thus the claimed inequality

\[
0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_{U\cap W}
\]

is still not rigorously established.

### 6. Finite-boundary-to-compact-boundary lemma

This lemma is mostly plausible. The compression by \(q_i^\perp\) is well motivated, and DKEP Lemma 8.5 supports the normal-bidual central-state criterion. The use of the compact-corner u.c.p. map \(\Theta_0\) is also consistent with DKEP’s proof of Lemma 8.5. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The main caveat is that this lemma depends on the preceding palindromic product being genuinely in \(q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0\), which in this proof is supplied only by the still-unproved intersection lemma.

### 7. Relative Bass–Serre paradox

This section remains plausible but not fully rigorous.

The AFP reduced-word commutator formula

\[
[p,JxJ]=(1-e_B)JxJe_B-e_BJxJ(1-e_B)
\]

is asserted after a brief explanation. A strict proof should check it on the vacuum, one-letter words, and longer reduced words. Similarly, the orthogonality claim

\[
q\,w_1^*w_2\,q=0
\]

from \(E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0\) needs a complete reduced-word verification.

These are likely repairable gaps, not obviously false statements. But as written, the proof invokes several reduced-word facts without enough detail for a standalone rigorous solution.

### 8. Graph-product application

This part is much improved. The graph-product AFP decomposition is now cited, the graph combinatorics are correct, and the appeal to CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 is appropriate. I also ran a small brute-force check of the graph combinatorics for graphs up to seven vertices; no counterexample was found to the local claims used in choosing \(w_1,w_2\).

The final nonamenability argument is acceptable: CDHJKN gives a diffuse full factor under the stated hypotheses, and a separable diffuse amenable finite factor would be the hyperfinite II\(_1\) factor, which is not full. The CDHJKN full-factor statement is explicitly supported by Theorem 2.4. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

But the final application depends essentially on the relative Bass–Serre proposition, the coefficient-boundary lemmas, and the boundary-support intersection lemma. Since those are not fully proved, the final conclusion is not established.
