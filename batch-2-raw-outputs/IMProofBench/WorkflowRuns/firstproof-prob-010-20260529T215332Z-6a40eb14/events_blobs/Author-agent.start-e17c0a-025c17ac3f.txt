I would avoid trying to make the Bass–Serre half-tree projections lie in \(S(M)\). The right framework is “proper proximality relative to \(B\)” plus the DKEP relative-to-absolute upgrade.

Concretely, for the graph-specific situation, I think the following lemma is the clean target.

**Relative Bass–Serre lemma.** Let
\[
M=P*_B Q,\qquad Q=B\bar\otimes A,
\]
with \(u\in Q\ominus B\) a unitary and with unitaries \(v_1,v_2\in P\ominus B\) satisfying
\[
E_B(v_i)=0,\qquad E_B(v_1^*v_2)=0.
\]
Then \(M\) is properly proximal relative to \(B\).

Proof sketch: let \(p\) and \(q\) be the projections onto reduced words whose first letter is respectively in \(P\ominus B\) and \(Q\ominus B\). Then
\[
1=e_B+p+q.
\]
For algebraic \(x\in M\), the commutators \([p,JxJ]\), \([q,JxJ]\) lie in the relative compact ideal
\[
\mathcal K_B=\overline{\operatorname{span}}\, M e_B M
\]
on \(L^2(M)\). Thus \(p,q\) define genuine projections in the relative Calkin/boundary algebra modulo \(\mathcal K_B\). In that quotient \(e_B=0\), so \(p+q=1\). The left multiplication estimates are exactly the scalar free-product estimates:
\[
v_1 q v_1^*+v_2 q v_2^*\le p,\qquad u p u^*\le q.
\]
The first uses \(B\)-orthogonality of \(v_1,v_2\); the second uses \(E_B(u)=0\). Hence any \(M\)-central state on the relative boundary algebra would satisfy
\[
2\psi(q)\le \psi(p)\le \psi(q),
\]
so \(\psi(p)=\psi(q)=0\), contradicting \(p+q=1\). This is exactly the relative proper proximality assertion.

Then use the DKEP relative-to-absolute criterion: if \(M\) is properly proximal relative to \(B\) and has no nonzero direct summand amenable relative to \(B\), then \(M\) is properly proximal. I am not fully certain of the theorem number, but it is in DKEP’s §4 normal-bidual/boundary-piece formalism; this is the result I would cite, rather than an AFP permanence theorem.

Finally, check the non-relative-amenability input descends correctly. If a nonzero central summand of \(M\) were amenable relative to \(B\), restricting the corresponding central state on \(\langle M,e_B\rangle\) to the expected subalgebra \(P\subset M\) gives relative amenability of a nonzero \(P\)-summand relative to \(B\). So in the induction you should phrase the hypothesis as “no nonzero direct summand of \(P\) is amenable relative to \(B\)” or explicitly justify this strengthening from the graph criterion.

For the full abstract proposition without the two \(B\)-orthogonal \(P\)-unitaries, I am less confident. Proper proximality of \(P\) should be usable by inducing DKEP boundary pieces from \(P\) to the \(P\)-vertex of the Bass–Serre decomposition, but that is a substantially heavier argument. For your graph application, the explicit two-unitary relative paradoxical argument is the safer rigorous route.

Three angles, in order of how I'd try them.

**(1) First, re-read CDHJKN's main theorems before doing further work.** Their Theorem A (which you cite) is the relative amenability classification, but the structural payoff theorems of "On the structure of graph product von Neumann algebras" almost certainly include proper proximality of irreducible graph products as a stated consequence — they typically list it alongside primeness/solidity. There is a real chance Prop BS (or directly your conclusion) is one of their numbered results with a slightly different label. *Uncertain* on the exact theorem number, but I'd bet odds it's in there.

**(2) If you do prove Prop BS yourself, the right framework is DKEP boundary pieces.** Set $\mathbb{K}_B \subset \mathbb{B}(L^2 M)$ = the compact ideal of $\langle M,e_B\rangle$ (norm closure of $\overline{Me_BM}$). With two $B$-orthogonal, $B$-centered unitaries $u,v \in P\ominus B$ (graph-specific!) and one trace-zero $a\in A$:

  - The half-tree projections $P_1, P_2$ (onto AFP words with first letter from $P\ominus B$ vs.\ $B\bar\otimes A\ominus B$) lie in $\widetilde{\mathbb{S}}_{\mathbb{K}_B}(M)$, as you note.
  - Paradoxical inequalities: $u P_2 u^* + v P_2 v^* \leq P_1$ (use $E_B(v^*u)=0$ to get orthogonality of the $u$- and $v$-images in $\mathrm{Range}(P_1)$ via the $B$-Hilbert-module structure on $L^2(P)\ominus L^2(B)$) and $a P_1 a^* \leq P_2$.
  - Haar argument for the vacuum: $h=ua$ has reduced AFP form of length $2n$ alternating, so $E_B(h^n)=0$ for $n\ne 0$, hence $\{h^n L^2(B)\}_n$ pairwise orthogonal, hence any $M$-central $\varphi$ on $\mathbb{S}_{\mathbb{K}_B}(M)$ has $\varphi(e_B)=0$. Combined with $P_1 + P_2 + e_B = 1$, no such $\varphi$ exists.

So $M$ is $\mathbb{K}_B$-properly proximal.

**(3) Upgrade $\mathbb{K}_B$-pp to pp using non-amenability of $P$ rel $B$.** The clean folklore statement:

> *If $M$ is $\mathbb{K}_B$-properly proximal and $M$ is not amenable relative to $B$ inside $M$, then $M$ is properly proximal.*

Sketch: Given an $M$-central $\varphi$ on $\mathbb{S}(M)$ with normal $M$-restriction, look at $\varphi$ on the image of $\mathbb{K}_B\cap\widetilde{\mathbb{S}}(M)$. Vanishing there lets $\varphi$ descend to an $M$-central state on $\mathbb{S}_{\mathbb{K}_B}(M)$, contradicting (2). Non-vanishing gives (via Hahn–Banach + a Day-style averaging) an $M$-central state on $\langle M, e_B\rangle$ with normal restriction to $M$, i.e., $M$ amenable rel $B$ inside $M$. Then $M$ amenable rel $B$ implies $P$ amenable rel $B$ inside $P$ (restrict to $\langle P, e_B\rangle \subset \langle M, e_B\rangle$), contradicting your hypothesis. A version of this upgrade — restated as "transferring central states between $\mathbb{S}_X$ and $\mathbb{S}$ uses relative non-amenability" — is essentially the technical content of the relative-to-full upgrade in **DKE, *Structure of relatively bi-exact group vN algebras*** (your DKE_rel_biexact). *Uncertain* whether the precise statement is there verbatim, but the proof template is.

**Other places to look:** any Caspers-and-collaborators paper on proper proximality for Hecke/Coxeter/graph-product algebras — they have been running exactly this Bass–Serre + boundary-piece induction. Also worth searching recent DKE preprints — AFP permanence is a question they have been circling.

**My recommendation:** Don't try to prove Prop BS in full generality. State your AFP permanence as a graph-flavored lemma — "$P$ properly proximal, $P$ not amenable rel $B$, $P$ contains two $B$-orthogonal $B$-centered unitaries, $A$ has trace-zero unitary ⇒ $M$ properly proximal" — and prove it by (2)+(3) cleanly in 2–3 pages. This avoids citation gambling on whether the abstract AFP statement is in the literature.

Here is a rigorous, self-contained proof strategy that bridges your gap perfectly. It uses exactly the graph-supplied unitaries you identified and the boundary piece quotienting typical of DKEP, sidestepping the need for an exact literature match. 

The crux of the gap is that the Bass--Serre half-tree projections $P_P$ and $P_Q$ (where $Q = B \bar\otimes A$) do not commute with the right action $J_M M J_M$, so they do not naturally live in the proper proximality boundary $S(M)$. However, their commutators with $J_M M J_M$ fall into the relative compact ideal $J_B = \overline{M e_B M} \subset \mathbb{B}(L^2M)$. 

You can upgrade this to full proper proximality using your non-relative-amenability hypothesis in the following sequence:

**1. Set up the Adjoint Action:**
Proper proximality of $M$ is equivalent to the non-existence of a state $\varphi$ on $\mathbb{B}(L^2M)$ that vanishes on the compacts $\mathbb{K}(L^2M)$, has normal restriction to $M$, and is invariant under the adjoint action $\text{Ad}(x)(T) = x J_M x J_M T J_M x^* J_M x^*$ for all unitaries $x \in M$. Assume such a $\varphi$ exists.

**2. Quotient by the Relative Boundary $J_B$:**
Because $P$ is not amenable relative to $B$, $M$ is also not amenable relative to $B$. By standard DKEP theory (cf. DKEP '23, Section 4), any $\text{Ad}(M)$-invariant state on $\mathbb{B}(L^2M)$ that gives non-zero weight to $J_B$ restricts to an $M$-central state on the basic construction $\langle M, e_B \rangle$, which would force $M$ to be amenable relative to $B$. Since this is false, your hypothetical state MUST have $\varphi(J_B) = 0$.

Thus, $\varphi$ drops to a well-defined, $\text{Ad}(M)$-invariant state on the quotient $X_B = \mathbb{B}(L^2M) / J_B$. 

**3. The Paradoxical Argument in $X_B$:**
Let $\pi : \mathbb{B}(L^2M) \to X_B$ be the quotient map. Because the commutators $[P_P, J_M x J_M]$ and $[P_Q, J_M x J_M]$ are in $J_B$, the projections $\pi(P_P)$ and $\pi(P_Q)$ strictly commute with the right action of $M$ inside $X_B$. 

Because they commute with the right action in the quotient, the left-action translations become equivalent to the adjoint-action translations in $X_B$:
\[
\text{For } u \in P, \quad \pi(u P_Q u^*) = \pi(u J_M u J_M P_Q J_M u^* J_M u^*) = \text{Ad}(u)(\pi(P_Q)).
\]

Now, use your graph-specific $B$-orthogonal unitaries $u, v \in P \ominus B$ and $w \in Q \ominus B$. As you noted, the left-action geometry yields $u P_Q u^* \le P_P$ and $v P_Q v^* \le P_P$ with orthogonal ranges, plus $w P_P w^* \le P_Q$. 

Pushing this down to $X_B$, the $\text{Ad}(M)$-invariance of $\varphi$ immediately yields:
\[
2\varphi(\pi(P_Q)) \le \varphi(\pi(P_P)) \quad \text{and} \quad \varphi(\pi(P_P)) \le \varphi(\pi(P_Q)).
\]
This forces $\varphi(\pi(P_P)) = \varphi(\pi(P_Q)) = 0$.

**4. The Contradiction:**
Since $P_P + P_Q + e_B = 1$ in $\mathbb{B}(L^2M)$, applying $\pi$ gives $\pi(P_P) + \pi(P_Q) + \pi(e_B) = 1_{X_B}$. But $e_B \in J_B$, so $\pi(e_B) = 0$. 
This implies $0 + 0 + 0 = \varphi(1) = 1$, a contradiction.

This cleanly bridges the gap: the relative compact ideal $J_B$ traps the failure of the projections to live in $S(M)$, and the hypothesis that $P$ is not amenable relative to $B$ is exactly what allows you to bypass $J_B$ entirely.
