\section*{Problem interpretation}

The graph is finite, simple and has at least three vertices.  The intended
``irreducible'' hypothesis is join-irreducibility: there is no nonempty proper
partition $V=S\sqcup T$ such that every vertex of $S$ is adjacent to every
vertex of $T$.  Equivalently the complement graph $\Gamma^c$ is connected.
All vertex traces are faithful normal traces.  A trace-zero unitary in each
$M_v$ implies each vertex algebra is nontrivial and provides centered unitaries
for reduced-word arguments.

For $U\subset V$, $M_U$ denotes the graph product over the induced subgraph and
$E_U:M\to M_U$ the canonical expectation.  If $B\subset N$, $X_B$ is DKEP's
boundary piece generated by the Jones projection $e_B$.

\section*{DKEP boundary support facts used}

Let
\[
\mathcal A_N=(\mathbb B(L^2N)_J^\sharp)^*
\]
be DKEP's normal bidual.  For a boundary piece $X$, let $q_X$ be the support of
the normal corner $(\mathbb K_X(N)_J^\sharp)^*$ inside $\mathcal A_N$.  The
projection $q_X$ commutes with $N$ and with $JNJ$, and
\[
\widetilde S_X(N)=\{T\in\mathcal A_N:[T,a]\in q_X\mathcal A_Nq_X
\text{ for every } a\in JNJ\}.
\]
For $X=\mathcal K(L^2N)$ write $q_{\mathcal K}$ and $\widetilde S(N)$.

DKEP Lemma 8.5 applies to the trivial group action when $N$ is a separable
finite factor: $N$ is properly proximal relative to $X$ iff there is no
$N$-central state on $\widetilde S_X(N)$ whose restriction to $N$ is normal.
The factor assumption is used because DKEP's ergodicity-on-center hypothesis is
then automatic.

Compression observation: if $T\in\widetilde S_X(N)$, then
\[
[q_X^\perp Tq_X^\perp,a]=q_X^\perp[T,a]q_X^\perp=0
\quad(a\in JNJ),
\]
so $q_X^\perp\widetilde S_X(N)q_X^\perp\subset\widetilde S(N)$.  If $\omega$
is an $N$-central state on $\widetilde S(N)$ normal on $N$ and
$\omega(q_X^\perp)>0$, then
\[
T\mapsto \omega(q_X^\perp Tq_X^\perp)/\omega(q_X^\perp)
\]
is $N$-central on $\widetilde S_X(N)$ and normal on $N$; normality follows from
domination by $\tau/\omega(q_X^\perp)$ on $N_+$.  Thus relative proper
proximality forces $\omega(q_X^\perp)=0$.

Finite-boundary criterion: if $N$ is a separable nonamenable finite factor,
properly proximal relative to $X_1,\ldots,X_m$, and
$\bigwedge_i q_{X_i}=q_{\mathcal K}$, then $N$ is properly proximal.  A
counterexample state $\omega$ on $\widetilde S(N)$ is supported on each
$q_{X_i}$, hence on their meet $q_{\mathcal K}$.  The compact corner
$q_{\mathcal K}\mathcal A_Nq_{\mathcal K}$ is $\mathcal K(L^2N)^{**}\cong
\mathbb B(L^2N)$; restricting $\omega$ gives a Connes hypertrace, contradicting
nonamenability.

\section*{Correspondence support and graph products}

DKEP Example 5.11 associates to an $M$--$M$ correspondence $\mathcal H$ a
boundary piece $X_{\mathcal H}$ generated by coefficient operators.  Supports
satisfy:
\[
q_{\mathcal H\oplus\mathcal K}=q_{\mathcal H}\vee q_{\mathcal K},\qquad
q_{\mathcal H\otimes_M\mathcal K}=q_{\mathcal H}\wedge q_{\mathcal K}.
\]
The fusion identity is the normal-support formulation of the coefficient
formula $T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi$: in the universal normal
representation, the coefficient support of a fusion is the greatest lower bound
of the two coefficient supports.

For $B\subset M$, the correspondence
$L^2(M)\otimes_B L^2(M)$ has coefficient boundary $X_B$: the coefficient of
$1\otimes1$ is $e_B$, and its left/right translates generate the Jones
projection boundary piece.

For graph products set
\[
\mathcal H_U=L^2(M)\otimes_{M_U}L^2(M).
\]
Then $q_{X_U}=q_{\mathcal H_U}$.  CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.4, in its precise form
Theorem 5.4 plus Proposition 5.5, gives
\[
\mathcal H_U\otimes_M\mathcal H_W
\cong
\bigoplus_{Z\subset U\cap W}\mathcal H_Z^{\oplus k_\Gamma(U,W,Z)},
\]
and the empty relative word contributes one copy of $Z=U\cap W$.  Monotonicity
$Z\subset I\Rightarrow X_Z\subset X_I$ holds because $e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_I}$ and
$X_I$ is hereditary and invariant.  Hence
\[
q_{X_U}\wedge q_{X_W}=q_{X_{U\cap W}}.
\]
Iterating over all links yields
\[
\bigwedge_{v\in V}q_{X_{\Lk(v)}}=q_{X_{\cap_v\Lk(v)}}=q_{X_\varnothing}
=q_{\mathcal K},
\]
since no vertex is in its own link and $M_\varnothing=\mathbb C$ gives the
coarse correspondence / compact boundary.

\section*{Relative Bass--Serre paradox}

For $N=P*_B(B\bar\otimes A)$ with $a\in A$ trace-zero unitary and unitaries
$w_1,w_2\in P$ satisfying
\[
E_B(w_i)=0,\qquad E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0,
\]
$N$ is properly proximal relative to $X_B$.

Fock-space notation:
\[
L^2(N)=L^2(B)\oplus\bigoplus H_{i_1}^\circ\otimes_B\cdots\otimes_B
H_{i_n}^\circ
\]
with alternating $P,Q$ letters.  Let $p$ be projection onto words whose first
letter is from $P^\circ$ and $q$ onto words whose first letter is from
$Q^\circ$.  Then $1=e_B+p+q$.

Membership $p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)$: for algebraic AFP $x$, right multiplication
changes first-letter type only through finitely many cancellations that land in
$B$; the resulting operators factor through $L^2(B)$ and are finite sums of
left/right translates of $e_B$, hence lie in $X_B\subset
\mathbb K_{X_B}^{\infty,1}$.  DKEP Lemma 6.1 upgrades from the algebraic AFP
to all of $N$.

Paradoxical inequalities:
\[
w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p,\qquad apa^*\le q.
\]
The first uses $E_B(w_i)=0$ to put images in the $P$-initial subspace and
$E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0$ for orthogonality.  The second uses $a\in Q^\circ$.  Any
central state on $S_{X_B}(N)$ has $2\varphi(q)\le\varphi(p)\le\varphi(q)$, so
$\varphi(p)=\varphi(q)=0$.  The unitary $h=w_1a$ is $B$-Haar; the projections
$h^ne_Bh^{-n}$ are pairwise orthogonal, forcing $\varphi(e_B)=0$.  This
contradicts $1=e_B+p+q$.

\section*{Graph-product application details}

For each $v$,
\[
M=M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{\Lk(v)}}(M_{\Lk(v)}\bar\otimes M_v).
\]
Need the two centered orthogonal unitaries in $P_v=M_{V\setminus\{v\}}$.

Let $C(v)=\{r\ne v:r\not\sim_\Gamma v\}$.
Connectedness of $\Gamma^c$ gives $C(v)\ne\varnothing$.

If $|C(v)|\ge2$, choose distinct $r,t\in C(v)$ and trace-zero unitaries
$u_r,u_t$.  Then $w_1=u_r,w_2=u_t$ satisfy
$E_{\Lk(v)}(w_i)=E_{\Lk(v)}(w_1^*w_2)=0$ because each word contains vertices
outside $\Lk(v)$.

If $C(v)=\{r\}$, connectedness and $|V|\ge3$ give $s\ne v,r$ with
$r\not\sim_\Gamma s$; since $r$ is the only complement-neighbor of $v$, this
$s$ lies in $\Lk(v)$.  With centered unitaries $u_r,u_s$, take
$w_1=u_r,w_2=u_su_r$.  Then $w_1^*w_2=u_r^*u_su_r$ is a reduced word containing
$r\notin\Lk(v)$, so the three expectations vanish.

CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.5 gives factoriality.  In a connected complement graph
with at least three vertices there is no universal vertex of $\Gamma$ (isolated
vertex of $\Gamma^c$) and no nonedge pair adjacent to all other vertices
(isolated edge of $\Gamma^c$).  Hence $M$ is a factor regardless of whether
individual vertex algebras are factors.

CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.1 with $V_1=V,V_2=\varnothing$ rules out amenability:
amenability of $M$ would force every nonedge to be the two-dimensional
exceptional pair adjacent to all remaining vertices, which again gives an
isolated complement component.  Thus $M$ is nonamenable.  Separability of $M$
follows from finite $V$ and separability of all $M_v$.

\section*{Abandoned route}

An earlier induction approach tried to use a broad permanence theorem for
proper proximality under
\[
P*_B(B\bar\otimes A).
\]
The available DKEP/DKE permanence results do not state this general AFP
permanence in the needed form without extra hypotheses.  The current approach
avoids that by proving only the relative Bass--Serre paradox for the boundary
piece $X_B$, and then using the finite family of link boundary pieces to pass
from relative to ordinary proper proximality.
