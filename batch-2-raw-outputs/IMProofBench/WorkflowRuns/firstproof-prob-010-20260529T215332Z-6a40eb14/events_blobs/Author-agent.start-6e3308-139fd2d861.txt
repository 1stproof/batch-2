Here is how I would approach the support calculus. The current inclusion $X_W X_U X_W \subset X_{W \cup W}$ (and formula (2)) has two technical issues, but you can fix the proof by restricting the algebraic product to a smaller generating $C^*$-algebra.

In the composition, the vectors $\xi_2$ and $\eta_1$ do not pair with each other directly; they pair with the inputs/outputs in $L^2M$, whereas the tensor product coefficient evaluates the internal $M$-valued inner product $\langle \xi_2, \eta_1\rangle_M$.

**The strategy for a corrected proof:**
Instead of multiplying the full hereditary algebras, multiply the smaller canonical $C^*$-algebras that generate them. 

Let $S_W = \overline{\text{span}}(M e_W M)^{\|\cdot\|}$.
