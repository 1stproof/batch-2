\section*{Problem statement and interpretation}

I use the standard graph-product meaning of irreducible: the finite graph
\(\Gamma=(V,E)\) is not a non-trivial join, equivalently the complement
\(\Gamma^c\) is connected.  The traces are faithful normal traces.  For
\(U\subset V\), \(M_U\) denotes the graph product over the induced subgraph and
\(E_U:M\to M_U\) its canonical expectation.  Let \(A_N=\mathbb B(L^2N)\) and
\[
        \cA_N=(A_N^{\sharp_J})^*
\]
be the simultaneous \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-normal bidual of
Ding--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli--Peterson (DKEP) \cite{DKEP}.  If \(X\) is a
boundary piece, \(q_X\in\cA_N\) denotes the support of
\((K_X(N)^{\sharp_J})^*\); for \(X=\mathbb K(L^2N)\) write \(q_0\).  Put
\[
 \widetilde S_X(N)=\{T\in\cA_N:[T,a]\in q_X\cA_Nq_X\hbox{ for all }a\in JNJ\}.
\]
DKEP Lemma 8.5 says that, for a separable finite factor \(N\), proper
proximality relative to \(X\) is equivalent to the non-existence of an
\(N\)-central state on \(\widetilde S_X(N)\) restricting to the trace.

\section*{Boundary supports}

We shall use the following standard consequences of DKEP's normal-bidual
construction.  Let \(p_N\in A_N^{**}\) be the simultaneous normal support, so
\(\cA_N=p_NA_N^{**}p_N\).  If \(X\subset A_N\) is a boundary piece with
\(N,JNJ\subset M(X)\), and \(\bar q_X\in A_N^{**}\) is the ordinary open
support of \(X\), then the paragraph preceding DKEP Remark 2.8 and its
two-sided form, Proposition 2.10, give
\[
        p_N\bar q_X=\bar q_Xp_N,\qquad
        (X^{\sharp_J})^*=q_X\cA_Nq_X,\quad q_X=p_N\bar q_X .
\]
Thus any contractive approximate unit of \(X\) converges to \(\bar q_X\) in
\(A_N^{**}\) and to \(q_X\) in \(\cA_N\).  Moreover,
\(q_X\cA_Nq_X\) is exactly the annihilator in \(\cA_N\) of the functionals
\(\phi\in A_N^{\sharp_J}\) with \(\phi|_X=0\).  Since \(X A_N X\subset X\),
DKEP Theorem 5.6 also gives the useful implication
\[
        T\in K_X^{\infty,1}(N),\ \phi|_X=0
        \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \phi(T)=0.              \tag{2.1}
\]
Consequently \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) have the same support in \(\cA_N\).  DKEP
Examples 3.4 and 5.11 ensure these hypotheses for the subalgebra pieces used
below.

\begin{lemma}[subalgebra correspondence]\label{lem:coeff}
For every expected subalgebra \(B\subset N\), the coefficient boundary of
\[
        \cH_B={}_NL^2N_B\otimes_B{}_BL^2N_N
\]
equals DKEP's subalgebra boundary \(X_B\).  In the tracial case
\(\overline{\cH_B}\cong\cH_B\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H={}_NL^2N_B\) and \(K={}_BL^2N_N\).  DKEP Example 5.11 identifies
\(X_H\) with \(X_B\): for \(\widehat1\in H\),
\(T_{\widehat1}:L^2N\to L^2B\) is \(E_B\), so
\(T_{\widehat1}^{\,*}T_{\widehat1}=e_B\).  For \(\widehat1\in K\),
\(T_{\widehat1}:L^2B\to L^2N\) is the inclusion.  If \(\xi\in H,\eta\in K\)
are bounded then \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\).  Hence the elementary
bounded tensors with \(\xi\) in the cyclic \(X_B\)-mixing set for \(H\) form a
cyclic set for \(H\otimes_BK\) satisfying DKEP Theorem 5.10(1) relative to
\(X_B\); so \(X_{\cH_B}\subset X_B\).  Conversely
\[
T_{\xi\otimes\widehat1}^{\,*}T_{\eta\otimes\widehat1}=T_\xi^{\,*}T_\eta
\]
for bounded \(\xi,\eta\in H\), so all generators of \(X_B=X_H\) occur as
coefficients of \(\cH_B\).  The conjugation
\(\overline{\xi\otimes\eta}\mapsto J\eta\otimes J\xi\) intertwines the left and
right actions, giving self-conjugacy.
\end{proof}

If \(Z\subset U\), then \(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_U}\), hence \(X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_U}\).

\begin{lemma}[direct sums and finite fusions]\label{lem:dsupport}
Let \(X\subset A_N\) be a boundary piece with \(N,JNJ\subset M(X)\).  If
\(\cK=\bigoplus_{j\in J}\cK_j\) and \(X_{\cK_j}\subset X\) for all \(j\), then
every coefficient \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) of \(\cK\) lies in \(K_X(N)\).

For \(U\subset V\), let \(\cF_U\) be the non-empty finite Connes fusions of
copies of \(\cH_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\) and its conjugate, and set
\[
 \mathcal C_U=\operatorname{span}\{T_\xi^*T_\eta:
       \xi\in\cK,\eta\in\cL\hbox{ bounded},\ \cK,\cL\in\cF_U\}.
\]
Then \(\mathcal C_U\) is a self-adjoint algebra.  Moreover \(X_{M_U}\) has a
contractive approximate unit contained in the norm closure of the cone of
positive coefficients \(T_\zeta^*T_\zeta\), where \(\zeta\) ranges over finite
direct sums of correspondences in \(\cF_U\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H_0\) be the bounded vectors \(\xi\in\cK\) for which
\(L_\xi:L^2N\to\cK\) is \(X\)-mixing.  Finite-coordinate vectors are in \(H_0\):
coordinate projections are bimodular, and finite direct sums of \(X\)-mixing
correspondences are \(X\)-mixing.  If \(\xi_n\in H_0\), \(\xi_n\to\xi\) in
Hilbert norm, and \(\xi\) is bounded, the closure argument in the proof of
DKEP Theorem 5.10 applies verbatim: for
\(\rho_n(a)=\langle(\xi-\xi_n)a,(\xi-\xi_n)\rangle\), choose projections
\(p_k\uparrow1\) with \(\|L_{\xi-\xi_n}p_k\|\to0\) for fixed \(k\); DKEP
Proposition 5.1 says the mixing operators form a strong right \(N\)-module, so
\(\xi p_k\in H_0\), and strong closedness gives \(\xi\in H_0\).  Since
finite-coordinate truncations of any bounded \(\xi\) are bounded and converge
to \(\xi\), \(\cK\) is \(X\)-mixing.  DKEP Theorem 5.10 and Proposition 5.9
give \(T_\xi^*T_\xi\in K_X(N)\), and polarization gives all coefficients.

For elementary coefficients
\(a=T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2}\), \(b=T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2}\), concrete
multiplication and \(T_{\alpha\otimes\beta}=T_\beta T_\alpha\) give
\[
 ab=T_{\xi_1\otimes\overline{\xi_2}\otimes\eta_1}^{\,*}T_{\eta_2},
\]
and adjoints are immediate; hence \(\mathcal C_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra.  By
Lemma \ref{lem:coeff}, \(X_{M_U}\) is the hereditary algebra generated by the
positive coefficients \(T_\xi^*T_\xi\) of \(\cH_U\).  For finite families
\(\xi_1,\ldots,\xi_n\) in \(\cH_U\), put
\(s=\sum T_{\xi_j}^*T_{\xi_j}\in(\mathcal C_U)_+\).  The usual directed net
\(g_\varepsilon(s)=s(s+\varepsilon)^{-1}\) is a contractive approximate unit
for this hereditary algebra.  Since \(g_\varepsilon(0)=0\), approximate
\(g_\varepsilon^{1/2}\) uniformly on \([0,\|s\|]\) by polynomials with zero
constant term.  If \(c\in\mathcal C_U\), write
\(c=T_\Xi^*T_\Theta\) with \(\Xi,\Theta\) bounded vectors in finite direct sums of
members of \(\cF_U\); then
\(c^*c=T_{\Theta\otimes\overline\Xi}^{\,*}T_{\Theta\otimes\overline\Xi}\), again a
positive finite-fusion coefficient.  Thus \(r(s)^*r(s)\) has the required form
and converges in norm to \(g_\varepsilon(s)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[intersection of boundary supports]\label{lem:intersection}
Let \(q_U=q_{X_{M_U}}\).  For \(U,W\subset V\), with \(I=U\cap W\),
\[
        0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_I .                                  \tag{3.1}
\]
Consequently, if \(I=\cap_{i=1}^mU_i\), then the palindromic product
\[
q_{U_m}q_{U_{m-1}}\cdots q_{U_1}q_{U_2}\cdots q_{U_{m-1}}q_{U_m}
\]
belongs to \(q_I\cA_Mq_I\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
All products in this paragraph are concrete products in \(A_M\).  Let
\(h=T_\xi^*T_\xi\) be a positive coefficient from a finite direct sum of
correspondences in \(\cF_W\), and \(k=T_\eta^*T_\eta\) one from \(\cF_U\).
Then
\[
        hkh=T_{\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta}^{\,*}
             T_{\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta}.                    \tag{3.2}
\]
CDHJKN identify \(\cH_Y\) with their graph-product correspondence \(H_Y(V,V)\);
their fusion rule \cite[Theorem 5.4 and Proposition 5.5]{CDHJKN} says that
fusing a \(Y\)-block with a \(Z\)-block decomposes as a direct sum of blocks
labelled by subsets of \(Y\cap Z\).  Iterating this rule (and using
\(\overline{\cH_Y}\cong\cH_Y\)) shows that the correspondence containing
\(\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta\) is a direct sum of \(\cH_R\)'s with
\(R\subset I\): after the first \(W\)-\(U\) fusion the label is contained in
\(I\), and further fusions only replace a label by a smaller intersection.
Lemma \ref{lem:dsupport} and \(X_{M_R}\subset X_{M_I}\) imply that (3.2) lies
in \(K_{X_{M_I}}^{\infty,1}(M)\).  By norm closure this remains true when
\(h\) and \(k\) are norm limits of such positive finite-fusion coefficients.

Choose contractive approximate units \(h_\alpha,k_\beta\) for \(X_{M_W}\) and
\(X_{M_U}\) of exactly this norm-limit form, as supplied by
Lemma \ref{lem:dsupport}.  If
\(\phi\in A_M^{\sharp_J}\) vanishes on \(X_{M_I}\), then (2.1) gives
\(\phi(h_\alpha k_\beta h_\alpha)=0\).  In \(A_M^{**}\),
\(h_\alpha\to\bar q_W\) and \(k_\beta\to\bar q_U\) strongly*, and multiplication
is jointly strong* continuous on bounded sets; hence
\[
        \phi(\bar q_W\bar q_U\bar q_W)=0 .
\]
Functionals in \(A_M^{\sharp_J}\) are supported on \(p_M\).  Using
\(p_M\bar q_Y=\bar q_Yp_M\), the image of \(\bar q_W\bar q_U\bar q_W\) in
\(\cA_M=p_MA_M^{**}p_M\) is \(q_Wq_Uq_W\).  The annihilator description in the
support paragraph therefore gives \(q_Wq_Uq_W\in q_I\cA_Mq_I\).  This positive
contraction is then dominated by \(q_I\), proving (3.1).

The last assertion follows by induction.  If the palindromic product for
\(U_1,\ldots,U_{m-1}\) is a positive contraction supported by
\(q_J\), \(J=\cap_{i<m}U_i\), then compressing by \(q_{U_m}\) gives a positive
contraction dominated by \(q_{U_m}q_Jq_{U_m}\), which is supported by
\(q_{J\cap U_m}\) by (3.1).
\end{proof}

\section*{A finite boundary criterion}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:finite}
Let \(N\) be a separable non-amenable finite factor.  Let
\(X_1,\ldots,X_m\) be boundary pieces and put \(q_i=q_{X_i}\).  Assume
\[
        q_mq_{m-1}\cdots q_1q_2\cdots q_{m-1}q_m\in q_0\cA_Nq_0.  \tag{4.1}
\]
If \(N\) is properly proximal relative to each \(X_i\), then \(N\) is properly
proximal.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Assume not.  By DKEP Lemma 8.5 there is an \(N\)-central state \(\omega\) on
\(\widetilde S(N)\) with \(\omega|_N=\tau\).  Fix \(i\) and write \(q=q_i\).
Since \(q\) commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\),
\(q^\perp\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)q^\perp\subset\widetilde S(N)\).  If
\(\omega(q^\perp)>0\), then
\(y\mapsto\omega(q^\perp yq^\perp)/\omega(q^\perp)\) is \(N\)-central on
\(\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)\); its restriction to \(N\) is normal and, because
\(N\) is a factor and the state is central, equals \(\tau\).  This contradicts
relative proper proximality, so \(\omega(q_i^\perp)=0\) for all \(i\).

Extend \(\omega\) to a state on \(\cA_N\).  In the GNS representation each
\(q_i\) fixes the cyclic vector, so the positive contraction in (4.1) has
\(\omega\)-value \(1\).  Since it is supported by \(q_0\), \(\omega(q_0)=1\).
The proof of DKEP Lemma 8.5 supplies a canonical \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodular
u.c.p. map
\[
        \Theta_0:\mathbb B(L^2N)\longrightarrow
        (\mathbb K(L^2N)^{\sharp_J})^*=q_0\cA_Nq_0
\]
obtained from the natural map into the normal compact corner.  Hence
\(\psi(T)=\omega(\Theta_0(T))\) is an \(N\)-central state on
\(\mathbb B(L^2N)\), and \(\psi(x)=\omega(xq_0)=\tau(x)\) for \(x\in N\).
Thus \(N\) has a Connes hypertrace, contradicting non-amenability.
\end{proof}

\section*{A relative Bass--Serre paradox}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:BS}
Let \(N=P*_{B}Q\) with trace-preserving expectations and \(Q=B\bbarotimes A\).
Assume that \(A\) has a trace-zero unitary \(a\) and that \(P\) has unitaries
\(w_1,w_2\) with
\[
        E_B(w_i)=0,\qquad E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
Then \(N\) is properly proximal relative to \(X_B\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(H_P=L^2P\ominus L^2B\) and \(H_Q=L^2Q\ominus L^2B\).  In the AFP normal
form let \(p\) and \(q\) project onto reduced words whose first letter lies in
\(H_P\) and \(H_Q\), so \(1=e_B+p+q\).  Right multiplication by a centered
\(P\)-letter preserves the \(H_Q\)-first subspace and can move the
\(H_P\)-first subspace only through the vacuum; hence
\([p,JxJ]\) is a finite sum of \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-translates of \(e_B\) for
\(x\in P\ominus B\), and \([q,JxJ]=0\).  The symmetric statement holds for
\(x\in Q\ominus B\), while \(B\) commutes with \(p,q\).  DKEP Lemma 6.1 gives
\(p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)\).

Let \(\varphi\) be an \(N\)-central state on \(S_{X_B}(N)\) normal on \(N\).
Left multiplication by \(w_i\in P\ominus B\) sends \(qL^2N\) into \(pL^2N\).
Moreover \(q w_1^*w_2 q=0\), since \(w_1^*w_2\in P\ominus B\) maps a
\(Q\)-first reduced word to a \(P\)-first one.  Thus
\(w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p\).  Similarly \(apa^*\le q\).  Centrality gives
\[
        2\varphi(q)\le\varphi(p)\le\varphi(q),
\]
so \(\varphi(p)=\varphi(q)=0\).  The unitary \(h=w_1a\) is \(B\)-Haar: every
non-zero power of \(h\) is a reduced alternating word, hence has \(B\)-expectation
zero.  Therefore \(e_Bh^ne_B=0\) for \(n\ne0\), and the projections
\(h^ne_Bh^{-n}\) are pairwise orthogonal.  They all have the same
\(\varphi\)-value, so \(\varphi(e_B)=0\), contradicting
\(\varphi(1)=\varphi(e_B+p+q)=1\).  DKEP Theorem 6.2 proves the claim.
\end{proof}

\section*{Application to graph products}

Fix \(v\in V\), put \(L_v=\Lk_\Gamma(v)\), and use the standard graph-product
normal form
\[
        M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}
        (M_{L_v}\bbarotimes M_v).                                \tag{6.1}
\]
Let \(C(v)=\{r\ne v:r\not\sim_\Gamma v\}\).  Since \(\Gamma^c\) is connected,
\(C(v)\ne\emptyset\).  If \(C(v)\) has distinct \(r,t\), choose trace-zero
unitaries \(u_r,u_t\) and put \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_t\).  If \(C(v)=\{r\}\), then
connectedness and \(|V|\ge3\) give \(s\in L_v\) with \(s\not\sim_\Gamma r\);
put \(w_1=u_r,w_2=u_su_r\).  In both cases graph-product reduced words give
\[
        E_{M_{L_v}}(w_i)=0,\qquad E_{M_{L_v}}(w_1^*w_2)=0 .
\]
The trace-zero unitary in \(M_v\) is the unitary \(a\) in (6.1), so
Proposition \ref{prop:BS} shows that \(M\) is properly proximal relative to
\(X_{M_{L_v}}\) for every \(v\in V\).

Enumerate \(V=\{v_1,\ldots,v_m\}\).  Since no vertex lies in its own link,
\(\cap_iL_{v_i}=\emptyset\).  Lemma \ref{lem:intersection} gives the required
palindromic product of the \(q_{L_{v_i}}\)'s in
\(q_{\emptyset}\cA_Mq_{\emptyset}\).  Here \(M_\emptyset=\C\), so
\(X_{M_\emptyset}=\mathbb K(L^2M)\).

A trace-zero unitary forces each vertex algebra to have dimension at least
two.  By CDHJKN Theorem E (equivalently Theorem 2.4), \(M\) is a factor: the
only factoriality obstructions are universal vertices or isolated exceptional
non-edge components, both excluded by connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) with at
least three vertices.  CDHJKN Proposition 6.3 also excludes amenability under
the same hypothesis.  Thus \(M\) is a separable non-amenable finite factor, and
Lemma \ref{lem:finite} proves that \(M\) is properly proximal.
