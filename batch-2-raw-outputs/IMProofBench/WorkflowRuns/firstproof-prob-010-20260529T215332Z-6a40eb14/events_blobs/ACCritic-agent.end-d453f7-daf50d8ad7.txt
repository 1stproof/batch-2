## 2. Literature cross-check

The cited literature supports several broad ingredients:

- DKEP Proposition 2.10 gives the simultaneous-normal bidual framework and a **non-unital inclusion** for invariant subalgebras, but its stated content is more limited than what the revised proof uses in the support-bookkeeping lemma. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))
- DKEP Theorem 5.10 gives the equivalence between cyclic bounded vectors with \(T_\xi\in YB(L^2M,L^2N)X\), mixing, and relative compactness; it also records \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))
- DKEP Example 5.11 identifies \(X_H\) as the hereditary algebra generated by \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\), and for \(H={}_M L^2M_B\) recovers the subalgebra boundary \(X_B\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))
- DKEP Lemma 8.5 gives the central-state criterion and explicitly works with u.c.p. maps from \(B(L^2M)\) into the normal compact corner. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))
- CDHJKN Proposition 5.5 gives the graph-product fusion rule with summands indexed by subsets of the intersection. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150))
- CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 supports the final statement that a join-irreducible graph product with at least three vertices and state-zero vertex unitaries is a diffuse full factor. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150))

Thus the broad cited framework is relevant. The problem is not the choice of references, but that the revised proof still uses several consequences not actually proved in the text or directly supplied by the cited results.

## 4. Section-by-section audit

### Problem statement and interpretation

This section is acceptable. The adopted interpretation is faithful to the usual meaning of join-irreducibility, and the proof later uses precisely this interpretation.

### Normal support bookkeeping lemma

This is still the main technical obstruction.

The lemma claims that for a hereditary \(C^*\)-subalgebra \(Y\subset A_N=\mathbb B(L^2N)\),
\[
(Y^{\sharp_J})^*
 =\overline Y^{\,\sigma(\mathcal A_N,A_N^{\sharp_J})}
 =q_Y\mathcal A_Nq_Y,
\]
and that every contractive approximate unit of \(Y\) converges ultraweakly in \(\mathcal A_N\) to \(q_Y\).

The restriction-map/annihilator part is plausible: DKEP Proposition 2.2 gives a Hahn–Banach extension theorem for simultaneous normal functionals, and DKEP Proposition 2.10 gives a non-unital inclusion of \((Y^{\sharp_J})^*\) into \(\mathcal A_N\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

However, the proof then says:

> This weak* closure \(W\) is hereditary: \(YA_NY\subset Y\), \(A_N\) is weak* dense in \(\mathcal A_N\), and multiplication in \(\mathcal A_N\) is separately weak* continuous, so \(W\mathcal A_NW\subset W\).

This step is not justified. It implicitly treats the canonical map
\[
A_N\to \mathcal A_N=(A_N^{\sharp_J})^*
\]
as multiplicative, at least when sandwiched by elements of \(Y\). But DKEP’s normal-bidual construction does not provide this multiplicativity in the form needed here. In Lemma 8.5, DKEP explicitly obtain u.c.p. maps from \(B(L^2M)\) into normal-bidual corners; u.c.p. is not enough to transfer concrete products \(yaz\) into products of their images in the von Neumann algebra \(\mathcal A_N\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

This is not a cosmetic point. The proof needs
\[
\iota(y_1)\,\iota(a)\,\iota(y_2)=\iota(y_1ay_2)
\]
or at least
\[
\iota(Y)\,\iota(A_N)\,\iota(Y)\subset \overline{\iota(Y)}^{\,uw}
\]
inside \(\mathcal A_N\). Neither is proved. Proposition 2.10, as stated, gives a non-unital inclusion of biduals for invariant subalgebras; it does not by itself prove that the weak-* closure of \(Y\) inside \(\mathcal A_N\) is the hereditary corner \(q_Y\mathcal A_Nq_Y\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

So the revised support-bookkeeping lemma remains unproved in an essential place.

### Subalgebra fusion lemma

This section is improved and mostly plausible.

The identification of \(X_B\) with the hereditary algebra generated by \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) is supported by DKEP Example 5.11. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) The positive-generation argument using \(s^*s\) and \(ss^*\) is sound.

The use of DKEP Theorem 5.10 to conclude mixing of \(\mathcal H_B\) relative to \(X_B\times X_B\) is terse but plausible: elementary tensors are cyclic, and their \(T\)-operators lie in \(X_B\subset \overline{X_BA_NX_B}\). Proposition 5.9 then supports passing from relative compactness to membership of \(T_\alpha^*T_\alpha\) and \(T_\alpha T_\alpha^*\) in \(K_{X_B}(N)\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

I do not regard this lemma as the fatal problem.

### Finite fusion calculus lemma

The stability of mixing under finite Connes fusion, conjugates, and direct sums is plausible from DKEP Theorem 5.10 and the formula \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The \(*\)-algebra property of \(\mathcal T_U\) is also plausible because the definition explicitly includes linear spans, finite direct sums, fusions, and conjugates.

This lemma is still compressed, but it is probably repairable. It is not the main obstruction.

### Intersection of boundary supports

This is the central fatal gap.

The algebraic fusion input is valid in broad outline: CDHJKN Proposition 5.5 gives the required fusion rule, with summands indexed by subsets of intersections. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150)) Thus the concrete coefficient calculation plausibly shows that the **concrete operator product**
\[
h_\alpha k_\beta h_\gamma
\]
belongs to \(K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\), where \(I=U\cap W\).

But the proof then takes ultraweak limits **inside \(\mathcal A_M\)** and treats
\[
h_\alpha k_\beta h_\gamma
\]
as the product in \(\mathcal A_M\) of the images of \(h_\alpha,k_\beta,h_\gamma\). This is exactly where the proof needs multiplicativity of the canonical map
\[
\mathbb B(L^2M)\to \mathcal A_M.
\]
The concrete coefficient calculus proves membership of the concrete product in \(K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\). It does **not** prove that the product of the three images in \(\mathcal A_M\) lies in the corner \(q_I\mathcal A_Mq_I\).

This invalidates the passage:
\[
h_\alpha\to q_W,\qquad k_\beta\to q_U
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
h_\alpha k_\beta h_\gamma\to q_Wq_Uq_W
\]
with all intermediate products lying in \(q_I\mathcal A_Mq_I\).

In short: the revised proof avoids the explicit ordinary-bidual formula \(q_X=pr_X\), but it replaces it with a direct normal-bidual multiplication argument that is not justified. The earlier ordinary-bidual strategy was designed precisely to avoid this nonmultiplicativity problem. The revised proof has not supplied a valid replacement.

Therefore the key inequality
\[
0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_{U\cap W}
\]
is still not rigorously proved.

### Finite-boundary-to-compact-boundary lemma

This lemma is largely sound assuming the palindromic product hypothesis. The compression by \(q_i^\perp\) and the use of DKEP Lemma 8.5 are reasonable. DKEP Lemma 8.5 indeed gives the relevant central-state criterion and the u.c.p. map into the compact normal corner. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

However, this lemma depends on the intersection lemma to verify the hypothesis
\[
R\in q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0.
\]
Since the intersection lemma is not proved, this step cannot complete the theorem.

### Relative Bass–Serre paradox

This section is improved and appears plausible. The reduced-word projection computations are standard, and the added calculation showing pairwise orthogonality of \(h^ne_Bh^{-n}\) is helpful.

I would still prefer an explicit citation for the AFP normal form and for the version of DKEP Theorem 6.2 being used, but I do not see a fatal mathematical error here.

### Application to graph products

The graph-combinatorial construction of \(w_1,w_2\) is correct. The AFP decomposition is standard for graph products and is now at least cited to CF17.

The final use of CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 is supported: for a join-irreducible graph with \(|V|\ge3\) and state-zero vertex unitaries, the graph product is a diffuse full factor. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150)) The conclusion that such a factor is nonamenable is standard, since the diffuse amenable finite factor is hyperfinite and not full.

This final application would be fine if the earlier boundary-intersection lemma were valid.

## 5. Summary of decisive problems

The revised proof is not answer-ready for one central reason:

- The proof still lacks a rigorous valid mechanism for multiplying approximating concrete operators inside DKEP’s simultaneous normal bidual. The coefficient/fusion calculation occurs in \(\mathbb B(L^2M)\), while the limiting support product \(q_Wq_Uq_W\) occurs in \(\mathcal A_M=(\mathbb B(L^2M)^{\sharp_J})^*\). The proof does not justify that the canonical map from \(\mathbb B(L^2M)\) to \(\mathcal A_M\) preserves the products being used.

This gap affects the essential intersection lemma and therefore the entire reduction from finitely many relative boundaries to the compact boundary. The proof may be repairable by returning to a carefully proved ordinary-bidual support-compression argument or by proving a genuine normal-bidual sandwich theorem, but the current `answer.tex` does not contain such a proof.
