## 4. Remaining fatal issue: \(\mathcal C_U\) is not proved to be an algebra

In Lemma 2, the author defines \(\mathcal C_U\) as:

> the linear span of coefficients of all finite Connes fusions of copies of \(\mathcal H_U\) and \(\overline{\mathcal H_U}\).

Then the proof says:

> The identities \(T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*\) and \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\) make \(\mathcal C_U\) a self-adjoint algebra...

This is **not justified as stated**.

If
\[
a=T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2},\qquad b=T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2}
\]
are coefficients of two finite fusions, then the product is written as
\[
ab
=
T_{\xi_1\otimes\overline{\xi_2}\otimes\eta_1}^{\,*}T_{\eta_2}.
\]
But the two vectors here generally lie in **different correspondences**: the left vector lies in a larger fusion, while \(\eta_2\) lies in the original \(U\)-fusion. This is a rectangular coefficient between different fusions, not a coefficient of a single finite Connes fusion in the sense of DKEP Example 5.11.

Thus the displayed identity does not prove that the product remains in the linear span of coefficients of finite fusions as defined. At minimum, the author would need to redefine \(\mathcal C_U\) to include all rectangular coefficients \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) where \(\xi,\eta\) lie in possibly different finite \(U\)-labelled fusions, and then prove that this class has the claimed closure and support properties.

This matters because the approximate-unit construction depends on this algebra claim. The proof says that a contractive approximate unit for \(X_{M_U}\) can be chosen as norm limits of elements of \(\mathcal C_U\). That conclusion is not established unless the polynomial functional calculus of finite positive coefficient sums stays in the claimed finite-fusion coefficient class. As written, this step is not rigorous.

This is a central gap: the approximate units \(h_\alpha^U,h_\alpha^W\) are then used to prove
\[
s_W(s_UA_M^{**}s_U)s_W\subset s_{U\cap W}A_M^{**}s_{U\cap W},
\]
which is the heart of the boundary-corner intersection lemma.

## 5. Related support-bookkeeping issue

The proof also uses the statement:

> If a correspondence is a direct sum of correspondences \(\mathcal K_j\) with \(X_{\mathcal K_j}\subset X\), then all its coefficients lie in \(s_XA^{**}s_X\).

This is plausible, and the author gives a one-sentence explanation. But because CDHJKN’s fusion theorem gives decompositions as direct sums, and the proof relies on support containment for arbitrary bounded vectors in those direct sums, this deserves a fully stated lemma. In particular, one should prove carefully that finite coordinate cutdowns of bounded vectors remain uniformly bounded and that their coefficient operators converge weak-* to the desired coefficient. The current statement is probably correct but too compressed for such a central use.

## 6. Finite-boundary criterion: small missing normality argument

In Lemma 3, the compression argument says that the restriction of the compressed state to \(N\) is normal. This is true, but the proof no longer includes the domination argument from the earlier version. The missing line is:
\[
0\le \omega(q^\perp xq^\perp)\le \omega(x)=\tau(x),\qquad x\in N_+,
\]
because \(q\) commutes with \(N\). Hence the compressed functional is dominated by a scalar multiple of \(\tau\), so it is normal. This is a minor repairable omission, not the main fatal flaw.
