Here is how I would approach the support calculus. The current inclusion $X_W X_U X_W \subset X_{W \cup W}$ (and formula (2)) has two technical issues, but you can fix the proof by restricting the algebraic product to a smaller generating $C^*$-algebra.

**The issues with the current draft:**
1. **Formula (2) is ill-typed:** DKEP's maps $T_\xi$ go from $L^2M \to \mathcal H$, so $T_\xi^* T_\eta \in \mathbb B(L^2M)$. The product $(T_{\xi_1}^* T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^* T_{\eta_2})$ is a valid composition in $\mathbb B(L^2M)$, but it does not match the coefficient $T_{\alpha}^* T_{\beta}$ of an internal tensor product. In the composition, the vectors $\xi_2$ and $\eta_1$ do not pair with each other directly; they pair with the inputs/outputs in $L^2M$, whereas the tensor product coefficient evaluates the internal $M$-valued inner product $\langle \xi_2, \eta_1\rangle_M$.
2. **The full hereditary algebra is too big:** The boundary piece $X_W$ is the hereditary $C^*$-subalgebra of $\mathbb B(L^2M)$ generated by $e_W$. Because it is hereditary, it contains $e_W \mathbb B(L^2M) e_W = \mathbb B(L^2M_W)$. Thus, $X_W X_U X_W$ contains $\mathbb B(L^2M_W)\mathbb B(L^2M_U)\mathbb B(L^2M_W)$, which is far too large to fit inside $X_{U \cap W}$ unless $W \subset U$. 

**The strategy for a corrected proof:**
Instead of multiplying the full hereditary algebras, multiply the smaller canonical $C^*$-algebras that generate them. 

Let $S_W = \overline{\text{span}}(M e_W M)^{\|\cdot\|}$. 
Notice that $S_W$ is already a $C^*$-algebra, because $e_W M e_W = M_W e_W \subset M e_W$, which implies that $(a e_W b)(c e_W d) = a E_W(bc) e_W d \in S_W$. 

Here are the three pieces you need to complete the support-corner lemma:

**1. The algebraic product matches the correspondence:**
The set $S_W S_U S_W$ is exactly the closed span of $M e_W M e_U M e_W M$. This space is precisely the algebra of coefficients $T_\xi^* T_\eta$ for the triple fusion correspondence $\mathcal H_W \otimes_M \mathcal H_U \otimes_M \mathcal H_W$ (generated by the cyclic vector $\hat 1 \otimes \hat 1 \otimes \hat 1$, which has coefficient operator $e_W e_U e_W$). 

**2. CDHJKN fusion dominates the product:**
Because CDHJKN decomposes this triple fusion into a direct sum of correspondences $\mathcal H_Z$ for $Z \subset U \cap W$, the coefficient algebra of the fusion is contained in the join of the boundary pieces $X_Z$. Thus, we have a clean inclusion of $C^*$-algebras:
\[
S_W S_U S_W \subset X_{U \cap W}.
\]

**3. Open supports in the ordinary envelope:**
Because $X_W = \overline{S_W \mathbb B(L^2M) S_W}$ is the hereditary closure of $S_W$, any approximate unit for $S_W$ acts as an approximate unit for $X_W$. Consequently, $S_W$ and $X_W$ share the exact same open support projection $e_W^{**}$ in the enveloping algebra $A^{**}$. 

Now, apply the support calculus to the $C^*$-algebra inclusion $S_W S_U S_W \subset X_{U \cap W}$. If $u_\lambda \in S_W$ and $v_\mu \in S_U$ are approximate units, the elements $u_\lambda v_\mu u_\lambda$ are positive elements of $X_{U \cap W}$ with norm $\leq 1$. Therefore:
\[
u_\lambda v_\mu u_\lambda \leq e_{U \cap W}^{**}.
\]
Taking strong limits gives $e_W^{**} e_U^{**} e_W^{**} \leq e_{U \cap W}^{**}$. 

Finally, cutting by the DKEP projection $p_{\rm nor}$ commutes with these open supports (by DKEP Proposition 2.10). Multiplying by $p_{\rm nor}$ immediately yields:
\[
q_W q_U q_W \leq q_{U \cap W},
\]
which implies your desired corner inclusion $q_W(q_U \mathcal A_M q_U)q_W \subset q_{U \cap W}\mathcal A_M q_{U \cap W}$.
