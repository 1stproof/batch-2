### 3. Mathematical audit by section

#### Problem statement and interpretation

The interpretation of irreducibility as “not a nontrivial graph join,” equivalently connected complement graph, is appropriate. The original problem statement’s wording is slightly imprecise about nonempty/disjoint join factors, but `answer.tex` explicitly records the adopted interpretation, as requested.

The reduction to faithful normal traces is also reasonable: “tracial von Neumann algebra” is normally understood in this context as equipped with a faithful normal tracial state.

No issue.

#### Boundary supports and coefficients

The support-bookkeeping lemma is the most delicate part of the proof. The argument carefully avoids multiplying images of concrete operators inside the normal bidual and instead computes products inside the ordinary bidual \(A_N^{**}\). This directly addresses the standard nonmultiplicativity issue of the canonical map \(A_N\to (A_N^{\sharp_J})^*\), which DKEP explicitly warns about. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The proof that \(q_X=p_Nr_X=p_N\rho_X\) is acceptable. The key point is the annihilator equality between \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\). The use of DKEP Proposition 3.6 and Theorem 5.6 is legitimate: \(K_X(N)\subset K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\), and simultaneous-normal functionals vanishing on \(X A_N X\) vanish on \(K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\). Since \(X\) is hereditary, \(X A_N X\subset X\), so a functional vanishing on \(X\) vanishes on \(K_X(N)\). The reverse inclusion follows from \(X\subset K_X(N)\), which DKEP also records. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

No fatal gap.

#### Subalgebra fusion lemma

The identification of \(X_B\) with the hereditary algebra generated by \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\) for \(H={}_NL^2N_B\) is exactly DKEP Example 5.11. The conversion
\[
T_{\eta\otimes_B\bar\xi}=T_{\bar\xi}T_\eta=T_\xi^*T_\eta
\]
is consistent with DKEP’s formula \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The positive-generation argument using ordinary open supports is sound. The proof correctly avoids the false claim that arbitrary finite sums of elementary tensors have linear coefficient operators; it only uses the hereditary algebra generated by \(s^*s\) and the self-adjointness of the generator set.

The direct-sum mixing argument via Theorem 5.10 is also acceptable: one-summand cyclic vectors give a cyclic set in the Hilbert direct sum, and the coefficient condition is preserved.

No fatal gap.

#### Finite fusion calculus

The closure of mixing correspondences under direct sums, conjugates, and finite Connes fusions is justified using DKEP Theorem 5.10’s cyclic-vector criterion. The product calculation
\[
T_{\xi\otimes_N\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi
\]
is exactly DKEP’s convention. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The construction of approximate units for \(X_{M_U}\) from the norm closure of \(\{t^*t:t\in\mathcal T_U\}\) is valid. The functional calculus argument with \(g_\varepsilon(s)=s(s+\varepsilon)^{-1}\) is standard and properly uses that \(\mathcal T_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra.

No fatal gap.

#### Intersection of boundary supports

This is another delicate section. The proof uses CDHJKN’s graph-product fusion rule in the correct specialization \(V_1=V_2=V_3=V\), giving
\[
H_A\otimes_M H_B\cong \bigoplus_{C\subset A\cap B} H_C^{\oplus m(A,B,C)}.
\]
This matches CDHJKN Proposition 5.5. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150))

The key claim
\[
abc\in K_{X_{M_I}}(M)
\]
for \(a,c\in\mathcal P_W\), \(b\in\mathcal P_U\), \(I=U\cap W\), is justified: elementary products expand into coefficient operators of finite fusions whose labels include both \(U\) and \(W\), and the fusion rule sends them to direct sums of \(H_C\) with \(C\subset I\). The direct-sum/fusion compactness lemmas then place the coefficients in \(K_{X_{M_I}}(M)\).

The limiting argument is correctly performed in the ordinary bidual \(A^{**}\), not in the nonmultiplicative normal-bidual image. The conclusion
\[
0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_I
\]
is therefore supported.

No fatal gap.

#### Finite relative boundaries imply compact boundary

The compression argument with \(q_i^\perp\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)q_i^\perp\subset \widetilde S(N)\) is valid because \(q_i\) commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\), and \([y,JNJ]\subset q_i\mathcal A_Nq_i\). DKEP records that these support projections commute with \(M\) and \(JMJ\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The GNS argument using an arbitrary extension of \(\omega\) to \(\mathcal A_N\) is carefully phrased and avoids claiming the extension is central. The palindromic product is positive and equals \((q_1\cdots q_m)^*(q_1\cdots q_m)\), so the conclusion \(\omega(R)=1\) is correct.

The final hypertrace contradiction is valid: DKEP Lemma 8.5 supplies the needed \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodular u.c.p. map from \(\mathbb B(L^2N)\) into the compact normal corner, and composing with the central state gives a Connes hypertrace, contradicting nonamenability.

No fatal gap.

#### Relative Bass–Serre paradox

The reduced-word analysis of the projections \(p,q\) onto first-letter \(P\)- and \(Q\)-subspaces is correct. The commutator formulas with \(JxJ\) are consistent with the AFP normal form. The use of DKEP Lemma 6.1 to upgrade the generator check to \(p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)\) is legitimate. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

The paradoxical inequalities
\[
w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p,\qquad apa^*\le q
\]
are valid. Orthogonality of the two \(w_iq\)-subspaces follows from \(E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0\). The \(B\)-Haar argument for \(h=w_1a\) is also correct.

This proves proper proximality relative to \(X_B\) in the DKEP \(S_X\)-criterion. The later use of the normal-bidual criterion is justified because the final graph product is a separable nonamenable factor, where the DKEP normal-bidual formulation is equivalent.

No fatal gap.

#### Application to graph products

The AFP decomposition
\[
M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{L_v}}(M_{L_v}\bar\otimes M_v)
\]
is standard for graph products and is consistent with Caspers–Fima.

The graph combinatorics used to find \(w_1,w_2\) is correct. I also brute-force checked all finite graphs with \(3\le n\le6\): whenever the complement is connected, each \(C(v)\) is nonempty, and in the singleton case \(C(v)=\{r\}\), there is an \(s\in L_v\) with \(s\not\sim r\). No counterexample was found.

The intersection of all links is empty because each vertex is absent from its own link. Thus the palindromic support product lies in the compact corner \(q_\emptyset\mathcal A_Mq_\emptyset\). The identification \(X_{M_\emptyset}=\mathbb K(L^2M)\) follows from DKEP Example 3.4: for \(B=\mathbb C\), the translated rank-one Jones projection generates the compact operators. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

Finally, CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 gives that \(M\) is a diffuse full factor under the stated hypotheses; hence it is nonamenable, since an amenable diffuse finite factor would be the hyperfinite II\(_1\) factor and not full. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150))

No fatal gap.

### 4. Minor nonfatal comments

1. The first paragraph says “DKEP Lemma 8.5 says” the normal-bidual criterion for finite factors. Strictly, DKEP Lemma 8.5 is phrased for actions, and one also uses DKEP’s equivalence between trivial-action proper proximality and von Neumann algebra proper proximality. This is harmless in the present factor setting, but adding a parenthetical citation to DKEP Proposition 8.2 would improve precision. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

2. Some equalities such as
   \[
   K_{X,X}(N,N)=K_X(N)A_NK_X(N)
   \]
   are slightly informal relative to DKEP’s notation. However, the needed conclusion \(T_\xi\in K_X(N)\) follows from DKEP Proposition 5.9 and the hereditary property, so this does not create a mathematical gap.

3. The proof uses several advanced DKEP support-identification facts in compressed form. They are unpacked enough to be referee-checkable, and the ordinary-bidual handling avoids the main known pitfall.

### Verdict

I find no mathematical error, missing essential lemma, unresolved issue, or LaTeX-contract violation that prevents the proof from being complete.
