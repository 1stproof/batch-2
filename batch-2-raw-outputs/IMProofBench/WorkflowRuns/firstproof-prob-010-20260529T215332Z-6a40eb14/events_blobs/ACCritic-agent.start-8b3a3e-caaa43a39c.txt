\section*{Problem statement and interpretation}

I interpret irreducibility in the standard graph-product sense: $\Gamma$ is not
a non-trivial join.  Equivalently, the complement graph $\Gamma^c$ is connected.
All traces are faithful normal tracial states.  For $U\subset V$ let $M_U$ be
the graph product over the induced subgraph on $U$ and let $E_U:M\to M_U$ be
the canonical trace-preserving expectation.

We use the boundary-piece formalism of Ding--Kunnawalkam
Elayavalli--Peterson \cite{DKEP}.  If $B\subset N$ is an inclusion with
expectation, $X_B$ denotes the boundary piece generated by the Jones projection
$e_B:L^2(N)\to L^2(B)$, and $S_{X_B}(N)$ is the corresponding small-at-infinity
operator system.  Proper proximality relative to $X_B$ means that there is no
$N$-central state on $S_{X_B}(N)$ whose restriction to $N$ is normal.

\section*{Boundary support bookkeeping}

Let $\cA_N=(\mathbb B(L^2N)_J^\sharp)^*$ be the normal bidual used in
\cite[Lemma 8.5]{DKEP}.  For a boundary piece $X$ write $q_X\in\cA_N$ for the
support projection of the normal corner $(\mathbb K_X(N)_J^\sharp)^*$; it
commutes with both $N$ and $JNJ$.  Also write $q_{\cK}$ for the support of the
compact boundary piece $X=\cK(L^2N)$.  In this notation
\[
 \widetilde S_X(N)=\{T\in\cA_N:[T,a]\in q_X\cA_Nq_X\text{ for all }a\in JNJ\}.
\]
For $X=\cK(L^2N)$ we write $\widetilde S(N)$.  DKEP's normal-bidual
characterization says, for a separable finite factor, that proper proximality
relative to $X$ is equivalent to the non-existence of an $N$-central state on
$\widetilde S_X(N)$ normal on $N$.

We shall also use the following correspondence support identities from
\cite[Example 5.11 and the proof of Theorem 5.10]{DKEP}.  If $\cH$ is an
$N$-$N$ correspondence, let $X_{\cH}$ be the boundary piece generated by its
coefficient operators and let $q_{\cH}=q_{X_{\cH}}$.  Then
\[
        q_{\cH\oplus\cK_0}=q_{\cH}\vee q_{\cK_0},
        \qquad
        q_{\cH\otimes_N\cK_0}=q_{\cH}\wedge q_{\cK_0}.        \tag{1}
\]
The first identity is immediate from the coefficient description of a direct
sum.  The second is the normal-support form of DKEP's coefficient identity for
fusion, $T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi$: in the universal normal
representation, the support of the coefficient boundary of a fusion is exactly
the greatest lower bound of the two coefficient supports.  Finally, if
$B\subset N$, the correspondence $L^2(N)\otimes_B L^2(N)$ has coefficient
boundary piece $X_B$: the coefficient of $1\otimes 1$ is exactly $e_B$, and its
$N$- and $JNJ$-translates generate DKEP's boundary piece.

\begin{lemma}[finite boundary criterion]\label{lem:finite-boundary}
Let $N$ be a separable non-amenable finite factor.  Let
$X_1,\ldots,X_m$ be boundary pieces for $N$ such that
\[
        q_{X_1}\wedge\cdots\wedge q_{X_m}=q_{\cK}.
\]
If $N$ is properly proximal relative to each $X_i$, then $N$ is properly
proximal.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Assume that $N$ is not properly proximal.  By the preceding normal-bidual
criterion there is an $N$-central state $\omega$ on $\widetilde S(N)$ with
$\omega|_N=\tau$.

Fix $i$ and put $q=q_{X_i}$.  If $T\in\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)$ and $a\in JNJ$,
then, since $q$ commutes with $JNJ$ and $[T,a]\in q\cA_Nq$,
\[
 [q^\perp Tq^\perp,a]=q^\perp[T,a]q^\perp=0.
\]
Thus $q^\perp\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)q^\perp\subset \widetilde S(N)$.  If
$\omega(q^\perp)>0$, the formula
\[
 \omega_i(T)=\frac{\omega(q^\perp Tq^\perp)}{\omega(q^\perp)}
 \qquad (T\in\widetilde S_{X_i}(N))
\]
defines an $N$-central state.  Its restriction to $N$ is normal: for
$0\le x\in N$, $0\le q^\perp xq^\perp\le x$, because $q$ commutes with $N$, so
$\omega_i(x)\le \tau(x)/\omega(q^\perp)$.  This contradicts proper
proximality relative to $X_i$.  Therefore $\omega(q_{X_i}^{\perp})=0$ for every
$i$.

A state which takes value $1$ on each $q_{X_i}$ is supported on their meet
(in the GNS representation the cyclic vector is fixed by every $q_{X_i}$).
Hence $\omega(q_{\cK})=1$.  The compact corner
$q_{\cK}\cA_Nq_{\cK}$ is canonically $\cK(L^2N)^{**}=\mathbb B(L^2N)$ and is
contained in $\widetilde S(N)$.  The restriction of $\omega$ to this corner is
therefore an $N$-central state on $\mathbb B(L^2N)$ extending $\tau$.  By
Connes's hypertrace criterion, $N$ is amenable, contradicting the hypothesis.
\end{proof}

For a graph product $M=M_V$ and $U\subset V$, write $X_U$ for $X_{M_U}$.

\begin{lemma}[meet formula for graph-product boundary pieces]\label{lem:meet}
For all $U,W\subset V$,
\[
        q_{X_U}\wedge q_{X_W}=q_{X_{U\cap W}}.
\]
Consequently $\bigwedge_{v\in V}q_{X_{\Lk(v)}}=q_{\cK}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Put $\cH_U=L^2(M)\otimes_{M_U}L^2(M)$, regarded as an $M$-$M$
correspondence.  By the support dictionary above, $q_{X_U}=q_{\cH_U}$.
Using (1) and then the fusion rule of
Charlesworth--de Santiago--Hayes--Jekel--Kunnawalkam
Elayavalli--Nelson \cite[Main Theorem 0.4, equivalently Theorem 5.4 and
Proposition 5.5]{CDHJKN}, we get
\[
 q_{X_U}\wedge q_{X_W}
 =q_{\cH_U\otimes_M\cH_W}
 =\bigvee_{\substack{Z\subset U\cap W\\ k_\Gamma(U,W,Z)\ne0}} q_{X_Z}.
\]
Here the multiplicities in the direct sum do not change the support projection.
The empty relative word in the CDHJKN formula contributes one copy of the
summand $Z=U\cap W$.  Every other summand has
$Z\subset U\cap W$, and monotonicity of boundary pieces gives
$q_{X_Z}\le q_{X_{U\cap W}}$: indeed $e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_{U\cap W}}$ and
$X_{U\cap W}$ is hereditary and invariant under the left and right $M$-actions.
Thus the displayed join is exactly $q_{X_{U\cap W}}$.

Iterating the two-set formula gives
$\bigwedge_{v\in V}q_{X_{\Lk(v)}}=q_{X_{\cap_v\Lk(v)}}$.  Since the graph has
no loops, no vertex belongs to its own link, so $\cap_v\Lk(v)=\varnothing$.
Finally $M_\varnothing=\C$, and $X_\varnothing$ is the compact boundary piece:
$L^2(M)\otimes_{\C}L^2(M)$ is the coarse correspondence and its coefficients
are the finite-rank operators.
\end{proof}

\section*{A relative Bass--Serre paradox}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:relative-BS}
Let
\[
        N=P*_{B}Q,\qquad Q=B\bbarotimes A,
\]
with trace-preserving expectations.  Suppose that $A$ contains a trace-zero
unitary $a$, and that $P$ contains unitaries $w_1,w_2$ satisfying
\[
        E_B(w_i)=0,\qquad E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0.
\]
Then $N$ is properly proximal relative to $X_B$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Use the amalgamated free-product Fock decomposition
\[
L^2(N)=L^2(B)\oplus
\bigoplus H_{i_1}^\circ\otimes_B\cdots\otimes_B H_{i_n}^\circ,
\]
where $i_j\in\{P,Q\}$ alternate,
$H_P^\circ=L^2(P)\ominus L^2(B)$, and
$H_Q^\circ=L^2(Q)\ominus L^2(B)$.  Let $p$ and $q$ be the projections onto the
sums of reduced words whose first letter lies respectively in $H_P^\circ$ and
$H_Q^\circ$.  Then $1=e_B+p+q$.

We first check that $p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)$.  Let $x$ be in the algebraic
amalgamated free product.  Right multiplication by $x$ changes the first
letter type of a reduced word only in the finitely many cases where initial
letters cancel down to a $B$-coefficient and the word is then rebuilt.  Each
such cancellation term factors through $L^2(B)$; explicitly it is a finite sum
of left and right $N$-translates of $e_B$.  Hence
$[p,JxJ]$ and $[q,JxJ]$ lie in the algebraic span generating $X_B$, and
therefore in $\mathbb K^{\infty,1}_{X_B}(N)$.  The same holds for $x^*$.
Since the algebraic free product is ultraweakly dense in $N$, DKEP's
generated-subalgebra lemma \cite[Lemma 6.1]{DKEP} gives
$p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)$.  Also $e_B\in X_B\subset S_{X_B}(N)$.

Suppose that $\varphi$ is an $N$-central state on $S_{X_B}(N)$ whose
restriction to $N$ is normal.  Since $E_B(w_i)=0$, left multiplication by
$w_i$ sends the $Q$-initial subspace into the $P$-initial subspace.  The two
images are orthogonal: for $Q$-initial vectors $\xi,\eta$,
$w_1^*w_2\eta$ is $P$-initial because $E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0$, and is therefore
orthogonal to $\xi$.  Hence
\[
        w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p .
\]
Likewise, viewing the given unitary as $1\otimes a\in Q\ominus B$, we have
$apa^*\le q$.  Centrality yields
\[
        2\varphi(q)\le \varphi(p)\le \varphi(q),
\]
so $\varphi(p)=\varphi(q)=0$.

Finally $h=w_1a$ is a $B$-Haar unitary: $h^n$ is a reduced alternating word
for $n\ne0$, so the subspaces $h^nL^2(B)$ are pairwise orthogonal.  Thus the
projections $h^ne_Bh^{-n}$ are pairwise orthogonal and belong to
$S_{X_B}(N)$.  Centrality gives $m\varphi(e_B)\le1$ for every $m$, hence
$\varphi(e_B)=0$.  This contradicts $1=e_B+p+q$.
\end{proof}

\section*{Application to the graph product}

For each vertex $v$ put $L_v=\Lk_\Gamma(v)$ and
$P_v=M_{V\setminus\{v\}}$.  The standard graph-product decomposition is
\[
        M\cong P_v*_{M_{L_v}}\bigl(M_{L_v}\bbarotimes M_v\bigr).
\]
We verify the hypotheses of Proposition \ref{prop:relative-BS} for this
decomposition.

Fix $v$ and let
$C(v)=\{r\ne v:r\not\sim_\Gamma v\}$ be the set of complement-neighbours of
$v$.  Since $\Gamma^c$ is connected, $C(v)$ is nonempty.  If $C(v)$ contains
two distinct vertices $r,t$, choose trace-zero unitaries
$u_r\in M_r$ and $u_t\in M_t$ and put $w_1=u_r$, $w_2=u_t$.  The expectation
onto $M_{L_v}$ kills $w_1,w_2,w_1^*w_2$, because the corresponding reduced
words contain centered letters from vertices outside $L_v$.

If $C(v)=\{r\}$, connectedness of $\Gamma^c$ and $|V|\ge3$ give a vertex
$s\ne v,r$ with $r\not\sim_\Gamma s$; necessarily $s\in L_v$.  Choose
trace-zero unitaries $u_r\in M_r$, $u_s\in M_s$, and set
$w_1=u_r$, $w_2=u_su_r$.  Then
\[
        E_{M_{L_v}}(w_i)=0,\qquad
        E_{M_{L_v}}(w_1^*w_2)=E_{M_{L_v}}(u_r^*u_su_r)=0,
\]
since these reduced words contain the outside vertex $r$.  Thus
Proposition \ref{prop:relative-BS} shows that $M$ is properly proximal
relative to $X_{L_v}$ for every $v\in V$.

It remains only to check the hypotheses of Lemma \ref{lem:finite-boundary}.
The algebra $M$ is separable because $V$ is finite and the vertex algebras are
separable.  By \cite[Main Theorem 0.5]{CDHJKN}, factoriality can fail only
through a vertex adjacent to all other vertices, or through a non-adjacent pair
which is adjacent to every remaining vertex and has both vertex algebras
two-dimensional.  The first alternative would be an isolated vertex in
$\Gamma^c$, and the second an isolated edge in $\Gamma^c$; both are impossible
because $\Gamma^c$ is connected and $|V|\ge3$.  Hence $M$ is a factor.

Also $M$ is not amenable.  If it were amenable, then $M_V$ would be amenable
relative to $M_\varnothing=\C$.  Applying \cite[Main Theorem 0.1]{CDHJKN} with
$V_1=V$ and $V_2=\varnothing$, every non-edge of $\Gamma$ would have to be a
two-dimensional exceptional pair adjacent to all other vertices.  Such a pair
is an isolated component of $\Gamma^c$, again impossible for a connected
complement graph with at least three vertices.

By Lemma \ref{lem:meet},
$\bigwedge_{v\in V}q_{X_{L_v}}=q_{\cK}$.  Since $M$ is properly proximal
relative to every $X_{L_v}$, Lemma \ref{lem:finite-boundary} gives that $M$ is
properly proximal.
