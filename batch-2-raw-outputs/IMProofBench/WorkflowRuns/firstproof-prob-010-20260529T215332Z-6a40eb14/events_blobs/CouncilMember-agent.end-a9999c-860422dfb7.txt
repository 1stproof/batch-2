I would be cautious here. The displayed inclusion is plausibly true in the intended coefficient-support sense, but I do **not** think it is justified by DKEP 5.9/5.10 in the way currently written.

The gap is the sentence “elements of \(e_{\mathcal H}(e_{\mathcal K}A^{**}e_{\mathcal K})e_{\mathcal H}\) are weak-* limits of finite sums of products controlled by (2).” That is not automatic from the coefficient identity. The middle corner \(e_{\mathcal K}A^{**}e_{\mathcal K}\) contains arbitrary \(A^{**}\)-middle terms; it is not just the weak-* closure of coefficient products. Also, replacing supports by hereditary \(C^*\)-algebra products would be too strong in general, e.g. when one correspondence is the identity.

The safer formulation is rectangular/right-support, not hereditary-product.

Let \(A=B(L^2N)\), and for a correspondence \(\mathcal H\) let \(\mathcal R_{\mathcal H}\) be the weak-* closed rectangular module generated by the concrete maps \(T_\xi\) for bounded \(\xi\in\mathcal H\). Let \(e_{\mathcal H}=r(\mathcal R_{\mathcal H})\in A^{**}\) be its right support. The support statement you want is:

\[
r\bigl(\mathcal R_{\mathcal K} e_{\mathcal H}\bigr)
\le
e_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\overline{\mathcal H}\otimes_N\mathcal K}.
\tag{*}
\]

Indeed, on generators,
\[
T_\eta\, T_\xi^*T_{\xi'}
=
T_{\xi'\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta},
\]
so \(\mathcal R_{\mathcal K}\mathcal R_{\mathcal H}^*\mathcal R_{\mathcal H}\) lands in the rectangular module for
\(\mathcal H\otimes\bar{\mathcal H}\otimes\mathcal K\). DKEP 5.9/5.10 are the right references for passing from this generator calculation to the weak-* rectangular support/polar-decomposition statement \((*)\). But I would spell this out; I would not cite them as directly proving the sandwich inclusion.

From \((*)\), the corner inclusion follows because the right support of \(e_{\mathcal K}e_{\mathcal H}\) agrees with the right support of \(\mathcal R_{\mathcal K}e_{\mathcal H}\), hence

\[
e_{\mathcal H}(e_{\mathcal K}A^{**}e_{\mathcal K})e_{\mathcal H}
=
(e_{\mathcal K}e_{\mathcal H})^*A^{**}(e_{\mathcal K}e_{\mathcal H})
\subset
e_{\mathcal L}A^{**}e_{\mathcal L},
\]

with \(\mathcal L=\mathcal H\otimes\bar{\mathcal H}\otimes\mathcal K\). Then cut by \(p_{\rm nor}\), using commutation of \(p_{\rm nor}\) with these ordinary supports.

For the graph-product lemma, the exact sufficient statement is even more concrete. Let \(\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\), let \(\mathcal R_U=\mathcal R_{\mathcal H_U}\), and let \(e_U=r(\mathcal R_U)\). It suffices to prove

\[
r(\mathcal R_U e_W)\le e_{U\cap W}. \tag{**}
\]

Then

\[
e_W(e_UA^{**}e_U)e_W
=(e_Ue_W)^*A^{**}(e_Ue_W)
\subset e_{U\cap W}A^{**}e_{U\cap W},
\]

and cutting by \(p_{\rm nor}\) gives

\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A q_U)q_W
\subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A q_{U\cap W}.
\]

To prove \((**)\), use the generator identity above with \(\mathcal H=\mathcal H_W\), \(\mathcal K=\mathcal H_U\), giving support bounded by
\[
\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\overline{\mathcal H_W}\otimes_M\mathcal H_U.
\]
Since \(\mathcal H_W\cong\overline{\mathcal H_W}\), CDHJKN fusion decomposes this into summands \(\mathcal H_Z\) with \(Z\subset U\cap W\); hence the support is \(\le e_{U\cap W}\). This is the statement I would put in the paper.
