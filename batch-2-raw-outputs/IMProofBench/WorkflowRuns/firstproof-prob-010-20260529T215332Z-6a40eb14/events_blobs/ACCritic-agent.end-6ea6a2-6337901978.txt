### Resolution of previous concerns

**1. Support comparison \(X\) vs. \(K_X(N)\).**  
The previous draft had an insufficiently justified assertion that \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) have the same normal-bidual support. The revised proof now uses the column \(\binom SR\), DKEP Corollary 3.2, and the simultaneous projection criterion from DKEP Proposition 2.4 to obtain common cuts for \(S,R\in K_X^L\), then shows
\[
d_\alpha S^*R d_\beta=(Sd_\alpha)^*(Rd_\beta)\in X A_N X\subset X.
\]
This is the right mechanism. The argument now adequately proves equality of the simultaneous-normal annihilators, hence equality of the compressed supports \(p_Nr_X=p_N\rho_X=q_X\).

**2. Fusion/direct-sum stability.**  
The revised Lemma `finite fusion calculus` now explicitly checks DKEP Theorem 5.10(1): cyclic vectors with \(T_\xi\in XA_NX\) remain cyclic under direct sums, conjugation, and Connes fusion, using
\[
T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi,\qquad T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*.
\]
This addresses the earlier criticism. One minor wording point: the map \(\xi\mapsto T_\xi\) is not linear in general, so “finite sums are absorbed by finite direct sums” should not be read literally for arbitrary complex linear combinations. But \(\mathcal T_U\) is defined as a linear span, and the proof only needs closure under products term-by-term, so this is not a fatal issue.

**3. Boundary-support intersection.**  
The revised proof now computes products in the ordinary bidual \(A^{**}\), not in the nonmultiplicative normal-bidual image. The monomial bookkeeping is explicit:
\[
T_{\alpha_1}^*T_{\alpha_2}T_{\beta_1}^*T_{\beta_2}T_{\alpha_3}^*T_{\alpha_4}
=
T_{\alpha_4\otimes\bar\alpha_3\otimes\beta_2\otimes\bar\beta_1\otimes\alpha_2\otimes\bar\alpha_1}.
\]
Together with the CDHJKN fusion rule, this correctly yields coefficients controlled by \(X_{M_{U\cap W}}\). The weak-* limiting argument with ordinary open supports \(r_U,\rho_U\), followed only afterwards by compression by \(p_M\), is now mathematically sound.

**4. Finite relative boundaries to compact boundary.**  
The revised draft now correctly notes that the extension of the central state to \(\mathcal A_N\) is used only for the GNS projection argument and is not claimed to be central. The final hypertrace is obtained through the original state on the compact corner and the canonical DKEP u.c.p. map. This addresses the operator-system nuance from the previous review.

**5. Bass–Serre paradox.**  
The AFP normal-form index has been corrected to \(i_j\ne i_{j+1}\). The commutator formulas for the first-letter projections are now sufficiently explained on reduced words, and the translation of DKEP Lemma 6.1 into the right-action convention is explicit. The paradoxical inequalities and the \(B\)-Haar unitary argument are valid.

**6. Graph-product application.**  
The graph combinatorics are correct: complement connectedness gives \(C(v)\ne\varnothing\), and in the singleton case \(C(v)=\{r\}\), connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) with \(|V|\ge3\) gives \(s\in \Lk(v)\) with \(s\not\sim r\). I also brute-force checked the corresponding finite-graph combinatorial claims for all graphs up to six vertices; no counterexample appeared. The expectation computations onto \(M_{\Lk(v)}\) are standard reduced-word computations. The final use of CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 gives a diffuse full factor, and therefore nonamenability follows because the diffuse amenable finite factor is hyperfinite and not full.

### Minor expository issues not affecting validity

- The support bookkeeping is technically dense; a future polished version should perhaps cite DKEP Proposition 2.4, Corollary 3.2, and Proposition 3.6 at the exact comparison step rather than only by name.
- The proposition on the Bass–Serre paradox is stated for a general AFP \(N\), while the normal-bidual finite-boundary criterion is ultimately used for the graph-product factor. This is harmless in the application, but the paper could explicitly say that the concrete DKEP notion of relative proper proximality agrees with the \(\widetilde S_X\)-criterion once the final factor/nonamenability hypotheses are known.

The revision resolves the previous essential objections. I do not find any remaining mathematical gap that affects the validity of the proof, nor any LaTeX-contract violation.
