\section*{Problem interpretation}

The graph is finite, simple, and has at least three vertices.  The intended
``irreducible'' hypothesis is join-irreducibility: there is no nonempty proper
partition $V=S\sqcup T$ such that every vertex of $S$ is adjacent to every
vertex of $T$.  Equivalently the complement graph $\Gamma^c$ is connected.
All vertex traces are faithful normal traces.  A trace-zero unitary in each
$M_v$ implies each vertex algebra is nontrivial and provides centered unitaries
for reduced-word and amalgamated-free-product arguments.

For $U\subset V$, $M_U$ denotes the graph product over the induced subgraph and
$E_U:M\to M_U$ the canonical expectation.  If $B\subset M$, $X_B$ is DKEP's
boundary piece generated by the Jones projection $e_B$.

\section*{DKEP normal-bidual notation}

Let
\[
  \mathcal A_N=(\mathbb B(L^2N)^{\sharp_J})^*
\]
be DKEP's normal bidual.  For a boundary piece $X$, let $q_X$ be the support of
the normal corner $(\mathbb K_X(N)^{\sharp_J})^*$ inside $\mathcal A_N$.  The
projection $q_X$ commutes with $N$ and with $JNJ$, and
\[
\widetilde S_X(N)=
\{T\in\mathcal A_N:[T,a]\in q_X\mathcal A_Nq_X
\text{ for every }a\in JNJ\}.
\]
For $X=\mathcal K(L^2N)$ write $q_0$ and $\widetilde S(N)$.

DKEP Lemma 8.5 applies to the trivial group action when $N$ is a separable
finite factor: $N$ is properly proximal relative to $X$ iff there is no
$N$-central state on $\widetilde S_X(N)$ whose restriction to $N$ is the trace.

Important caution: a state on a von Neumann algebra which is $1$ on two
noncommuting projections need not be $1$ on their lattice meet, because the GNS
representation of a non-normal state need not preserve meets.  Thus the naive
criterion ``relative to finitely many $X_i$ plus
$\bigwedge_iq_{X_i}=q_0$'' is not justified unless commutation/normality is
available.  The replacement is to use an actual positive palindromic product of
the $q_i$'s which lies in the compact corner.

\section*{Palindromic finite-boundary criterion}

Let $N$ be a separable nonamenable finite factor and $X_1,\dots,X_m$ boundary
pieces.  Put $q_i=q_{X_i}$.  Suppose
\[
  R=q_mq_{m-1}\cdots q_1q_2\cdots q_{m-1}q_m\in q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0.
\]
Then relative proper proximality with respect to every $X_i$ implies ordinary
proper proximality.

Proof details:
if $\omega$ is an $N$-central state on $\widetilde S(N)$ with $\omega|_N=\tau$,
then for each $i$,
\[
  q_i^\perp\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)q_i^\perp\subset \widetilde S(N)
\]
because $q_i$ commutes with $JNJ$ and $[T,JNJ]\subset q_i\mathcal A_Nq_i$.
If $\omega(q_i^\perp)>0$, compression by $q_i^\perp$ gives an $N$-central state
on $\widetilde S_{X_i}(N)$ normal on $N$ (normality is by domination by a scalar
multiple of $\tau$ on $N_+$), contradiction.  Hence the GNS cyclic vector for
$\omega$ is fixed by each $q_i$ and therefore by $R$.  Since $R$ is a positive
contraction in $q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0$, $1=\omega(R)\le \omega(q_0)$, so
$\omega(q_0)=1$.  Restricting $\omega$ to $q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0\cong
\mathbb B(L^2N)$ gives a Connes hypertrace, contradicting nonamenability.

\section*{Graph-product boundary corner intersections}

For graph products write $q_U=q_{X_{M_U}}$.  The needed replacement for a meet
formula is
\[
  q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W\subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A_Mq_{U\cap W}.
\]
This implies by induction that the palindromic product associated with
$U_1,\dots,U_m$ lies in the corner for $\cap_iU_i$.

Reasoning.  Let $\mathcal H_U=L^2(M)\otimes_{M_U}L^2(M)$ with cyclic vector
$\zeta_U=1\otimes 1$.  The coefficient of $\zeta_U$ is $e_{M_U}$ and translates
of this vector generate DKEP's $X_{M_U}$.  DKEP's formula
$T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi$ shows that products of a $W$-coefficient, a
$U$-coefficient, and a $W$-coefficient are coefficients of
\[
  \mathcal H_W\otimes_M \mathcal H_U\otimes_M \mathcal H_W
\]
(up to hereditary closure; the cyclic coefficient is
$e_{M_W}e_{M_U}e_{M_W}$).  Thus the normal corner on the left is contained in
the coefficient support of this triple fusion.  CDHJKN Theorem 5.4 and
Proposition 5.5 decompose this triple fusion as a direct sum of
$L^2(M)\otimes_{M_Z}L^2(M)$ with $Z\subset U\cap W$.  Since $X_{M_Z}\subset
X_{M_{U\cap W}}$, the whole coefficient boundary is contained in
$X_{M_{U\cap W}}$.

This is intentionally weaker than the unsupported identity
$q_U\wedge q_W=q_{U\cap W}$ and is tailored to non-normal states.

\section*{Relative Bass--Serre paradox}

For $N=P*_BQ$, $Q=B\bar\otimes A$, with $A$ containing a trace-zero unitary $a$
and with unitaries $w_1,w_2\in P$ satisfying
\[
  E_B(w_i)=0,\qquad E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0,
\]
one proves $N$ properly proximal relative to $X_B$.

Let $p$ and $q$ be the projections onto the free-product Fock subspaces whose
first letter is in $L^2(P)\ominus L^2B$ and $L^2(Q)\ominus L^2B$, respectively.
Then $1=e_B+p+q$.

Commutator formulas with right multiplication $R_x=Jx^*J$:
for $x\in P\ominus B$,
\[
  [p,R_x]=R_xe_B-e_BR_x,\qquad [q,R_x]=0,
\]
and for $x\in Q\ominus B$,
\[
  [q,R_x]=R_xe_B-e_BR_x,\qquad [p,R_x]=0.
\]
Elements of $B$ commute with $p,q$.  These formulas are checked directly on
reduced words; only the $B$-summand and one-letter words from the same free
component can change first-letter type.  Since right translates of $e_B$ are in
the boundary ideal, DKEP Lemma 6.1 gives $p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)$.

If $\varphi$ is an $N$-central state on $S_{X_B}(N)$ normal on $N$, then
\[
  w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p,\qquad apa^*\le q.
\]
The first inequality uses centeredness of $w_i$ and orthogonality from
$E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0$; the second uses $a\in Q\ominus B$.  Centrality gives
$2\varphi(q)\le\varphi(p)\le\varphi(q)$, so $\varphi(p)=\varphi(q)=0$.
The unitary $h=w_1a$ is $B$-Haar: the subspaces $h^nL^2B$ are pairwise
orthogonal.  Hence centrality forces $\varphi(e_B)=0$, contradicting
$1=e_B+p+q$.

\section*{Graph-product application}

For each vertex $v$,
\[
  M\cong M_{V\setminus\{v\}}*_{M_{\Lk(v)}}(M_{\Lk(v)}\bar\otimes M_v).
\]
Let $C(v)=\{r\ne v:r\not\sim v\}$.  Complement connectedness gives
$C(v)\ne\emptyset$.  If $C(v)$ has two vertices $r,t$, use trace-zero unitaries
$u_r,u_t$ and set $w_1=u_r,w_2=u_t$.  If $C(v)=\{r\}$, complement
connectedness and $|V|\ge3$ give $s\in\Lk(v)$ with $r\not\sim s$; set
$w_1=u_r,w_2=u_su_r$.  In both cases expectations of $w_i$ and $w_1^*w_2$ onto
$M_{\Lk(v)}$ vanish.  The vertex unitary in $M_v$ gives $a$.  Therefore $M$ is
properly proximal relative to every $X_{M_{\Lk(v)}}$.

The intersection of all links is empty.  The palindromic product of the link
boundary supports is therefore in the compact corner because
$M_\emptyset=\mathbb C$ and $X_{M_\emptyset}$ is the compact boundary piece.

CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.5 gives factoriality.  In a connected complement graph
with at least three vertices, there is no universal vertex and no isolated
exceptional nonedge component.  CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.1 gives nonamenability:
if $M$ were amenable, applying the relative amenability criterion with
$V_1=V,V_2=\emptyset$ would force every complement edge to be an isolated
exceptional edge, impossible in a connected complement graph with at least
three vertices.



\section*{Normal support calculus for correspondence boundaries}

For a correspondence $\mathcal H$, write $X_{\mathcal H}$ for DKEP's
coefficient boundary generated by $T_\xi^*T_\eta$ for bounded vectors.
Let $q_{\mathcal H}$ be the support in the normal bidual of
$\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}(M)$.  A useful way to view this support is via the
normal left ideal $\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}$ generated by the coefficient
operators $T_\xi$: the right support of $\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}$ is
$q_{\mathcal H}$, and $q_{\mathcal H}\mathcal A q_{\mathcal H}$ is the weak-*
closed hereditary corner generated by the coefficients.

For correspondences $\mathcal H,\mathcal K$ over $M$, DKEP's formula
$T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi$ gives the normal support-sandwich inclusion
\[
 q_{\mathcal H}(q_{\mathcal K}\mathcal A q_{\mathcal K})q_{\mathcal H}
 \subset
 q_{\mathcal H\otimes_M\mathcal K}\mathcal A
 q_{\mathcal H\otimes_M\mathcal K}.
\]
Proof template: first verify on generators
\[
T_\xi^*(T_\eta^*T_{\eta'})T_{\xi'}
 =T_{\xi\otimes\eta}^*T_{\xi'\otimes\eta'}.
\]
Then use separate weak-* continuity and approximate the right support
$q_{\mathcal H}$ by an approximate unit for
$\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}^*\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}$.  This avoids assuming
commutation of supports or identifying their lattice meet.

Direct sums: the coefficient boundary of $\bigoplus_i\mathcal H_i$ is supported
by the join of the supports of the $X_{\mathcal H_i}$.  Diagonal coefficients
are immediate; off-diagonal coefficients are controlled by the usual
$2\times2$ positivity/Douglas factorization argument.  Hence if every summand's
boundary is contained in $X_Y$, then the whole direct-sum boundary is contained
in $X_Y$.

For graph products, with $\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M$, CDHJKN fusion
gives
\[
  \mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U
  \cong \bigoplus_{Z\subset U\cap W}
       (L^2M\otimes_{M_Z}L^2M)^{\oplus k_Z}.
\]
Since $Z\subset I$ implies $e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_I}$ and $X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_I}$,
the support of the fusion is dominated by $q_I$.  Combining with the
support-sandwich inclusion yields
\[
  q_W(q_U\mathcal A q_U)q_W\subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A q_{U\cap W}.
\]

\section*{Compact-corner hypertrace step}

In the finite-boundary criterion, do not identify
$q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0$ with $\mathbb B(L^2N)$.  DKEP's proof of Lemma 8.5 gives
the needed replacement: a natural $N$- and $JNJ$-bimodular u.c.p. map
\[
  \Theta:\mathbb B(L^2N)\to q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0
\]
sending $1$ to $q_0$ and $x\in N$ to $q_0xq_0$.  If a central state
$\omega$ on $\widetilde S(N)$ satisfies $\omega(q_0)=1$, then
$\omega\circ\Theta$ is an $N$-central state on $\mathbb B(L^2N)$ extending the
trace.  This is a Connes hypertrace, forcing amenability of the finite factor.



\section*{Round 6 support-calculus refinement}

The critic objected that the previous support-sandwich argument multiplied
concrete operators after embedding them in the DKEP normal bidual, where the
canonical embedding is only u.c.p. and not multiplicative.  The safer route is:

Let \(A=B(L^2N)\) and \(p=p_{\rm nor}\in A^{**}\) be the DKEP projection for
simultaneous normality over \(N\) and \(JNJ\).  For a DKEP boundary piece \(X\),
write \(e_X\in A^{**}\) for the ordinary support projection of
\(\mathbb K_X(N)\).  DKEP Proposition 2.10 implies \(e_Xp=pe_X\), and the
support projection in \(\mathcal A_N=pA^{**}p\) is \(q_X=pe_X\).  Thus one can
do support computations in the ordinary enveloping von Neumann algebra
\(A^{**}\), where the canonical embedding is multiplicative, and only then cut
by \(p\).

Concrete lemma: if \(B,C,D\subset A\) are hereditary \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodule
\(C^*\)-subalgebras and \(CBC\subset D\), then
\[
e_C(e_BA^{**}e_B)e_C\subset e_DA^{**}e_D.
\]
Proof: approximate an element of \(e_BA^{**}e_B\) weak-* by a bounded net from
\(B\), sandwich by an approximate unit of \(C\), and pass to weak-* limits.
Cutting by \(p\), using \(e_\bullet p=pe_\bullet\), gives
\[
q_C(q_B\mathcal A_Nq_B)q_C\subset q_D\mathcal A_Nq_D.
\]
This addresses the nonmultiplicativity warning: no claim is made that
\(\iota_{\rm nor}(xy)=\iota_{\rm nor}(x)\iota_{\rm nor}(y)\).

For coefficient boundary pieces, the relevant product is not the previously
claimed double fusion but a triple/coefficient product.  If \(\mathcal H\) and
\(\mathcal K\) are \(N\)-\(N\) correspondences, then for bounded vectors
\[
(T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2})(T_{\xi_3}^*T_{\xi_4})
 =
T_{\xi_1\otimes\bar\xi_2\otimes\eta_1}^*
T_{\xi_4\otimes\bar\xi_3\otimes\eta_2},
\]
using \(T_{\alpha\otimes\beta}=T_\beta T_\alpha\) and
\(T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*\).  Hence the algebraic coefficient product
\(X_{\mathcal H}X_{\mathcal K}X_{\mathcal H}\) is contained in the coefficient
boundary of
\(\mathcal H\otimes_N\overline{\mathcal H}\otimes_N\mathcal K\).
DKEP Proposition 5.9/Theorem 5.10 and separate continuity of the relative
compact topologies are the intended justification for passing from algebraic
coefficient spans to the \(\mathbb K_X\)'s.

For graph products,
\(\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\) is self-contragredient.  Therefore
\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W
\subset
q_{\mathcal H_W\otimes\mathcal H_W\otimes\mathcal H_U}\mathcal A_M
q_{\mathcal H_W\otimes\mathcal H_W\otimes\mathcal H_U}.
\]
Applying the CDHJKN fusion rule twice decomposes this triple fusion into
summands \(\mathcal H_Z=L^2M\otimes_{M_Z}L^2M\) with \(Z\subset U\cap W\), so
the support is dominated by \(q_{U\cap W}\).  This gives the desired
palindromic-product inclusion without using non-normal lattice meets.


\section*{Refined normal-left-ideal support mechanism}

For an \(N\)-\(N\) correspondence \(\mathcal H\), DKEP's coefficient boundary
\(X_{\mathcal H}\) is generated hereditarily by the operators
\(T_\xi^*T_\eta\), \(\xi,\eta\) bounded.  The most economical support object is
not the concrete \(C^*\)-algebra product
\(\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal K}}
\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}\), but the weak-* closed normal left ideal
\(\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}\) generated by the coefficient maps \(T_\xi\).  Its
right support in \(\mathcal A_N=(B(L^2N)^{\sharp_J})^*\) is
\(q_{\mathcal H}\).  This follows because \(X_{\mathcal H}\) is the hereditary
algebra generated by \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\), and DKEP Proposition 5.9 identifies the
corresponding relative compact operators with the operators whose positive
squares lie in the relevant \(\mathbb K_X\)-corners.

Composition of rectangular normal bimodules is separately weak-* continuous in
the DKEP normal-bidual setting and agrees on bounded vectors with the concrete
Connes-fusion formula
\[
  T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi.
\]
Hence
\[
  \mathcal L_{\mathcal K}\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}
  \subset \mathcal L_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\mathcal K}.
\]
Since \(q_{\mathcal K}q_{\mathcal H}\in
\mathcal L_{\mathcal K}\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}\), for any
\(x=q_{\mathcal K}a q_{\mathcal K}\) one obtains
\[
 q_{\mathcal H}xq_{\mathcal H}
 =(q_{\mathcal K}q_{\mathcal H})^*a(q_{\mathcal K}q_{\mathcal H})
 \in q_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\mathcal K}\mathcal A_N
    q_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\mathcal K}.
\]
This is the support-level sandwich needed for graph products and avoids the
unproved closed-\(\mathbb K_X\) product inclusion.

For an expected inclusion \(B\subset N\), the induced correspondence
\(\mathcal H_B=L^2N\otimes_B L^2N\) has coefficient boundary \(X_B\).  The
cyclic vector \(\zeta=\hat1\otimes_B\hat1\) has coefficient operator \(e_B\),
and \(N\)-\(N\) translates of \(\zeta\) produce exactly the left/right
translates of \(e_B\) appearing in DKEP's definition of \(X_B\).

Direct sums of correspondences: if each summand \(\mathcal H_j\) has support
below a projection \(q\), then \(\oplus_j\mathcal H_j\) also has support below
\(q\).  Off-diagonal coefficients are controlled by the standard hereditary
corner fact \(a^*a,b^*b\in D\Rightarrow a^*b\in D\), proved from \(2\times2\)
positivity/Douglas factorization.  Infinite direct sums follow by truncating
bounded vectors.

\section*{Operator-system point in the palindromic criterion}

DKEP's \(\widetilde S_X(N)\) is an operator system, not generally a
\(C^*\)-algebra.  When a state \(\omega\) on \(\widetilde S(N)\) satisfies
\(\omega(q_i^\perp)=0\) for several support projections \(q_i\), extend
\(\omega\) to a state \(\bar\omega\) on the ambient von Neumann algebra
\(\mathcal A_N\).  In the GNS representation of \(\bar\omega\), each
\(q_i\) fixes the cyclic vector; hence the palindromic product
\((q_1\cdots q_m)^*(q_1\cdots q_m)\) has \(\omega\)-value one.  This justifies
the GNS step without pretending that \(\widetilde S(N)\) itself is a
\(C^*\)-algebra.

Compression normality: if \(q=q_X\) and \(\omega(q^\perp)>0\), the compressed
functional \(T\mapsto \omega(q^\perp Tq^\perp)/\omega(q^\perp)\) is normal on
\(N\) because \(q\) commutes with \(N\) and, on \(N_+\), it is dominated by
\(\omega(q^\perp)^{-1}\tau\).



\section*{Ordinary-envelope support calculus after Round 8}

The normal-bidual map \(A=\mathbb B(L^2N)\to p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\)
should not be used multiplicatively.  The safe pattern is:
work in the ordinary enveloping algebra \(A^{**}\), prove a support inclusion
there, and only then multiply by \(p_{\rm nor}\), using DKEP Proposition 2.10
(commutation of \(p_{\rm nor}\) with supports of hereditary \(N,JNJ\)-bimodular
subalgebras).

For a hereditary subalgebra \(C\subset A\), write \(e_C\in A^{**}\) for its open
support.  If a concrete hereditary-product inclusion \(B C B\subset D\) is
available, then
\[
  e_B(e_C A^{**}e_C)e_B\subset e_D A^{**}e_D.
\]
Proof: approximate elements of \(e_C A^{**}e_C\) by bounded nets from \(C\),
sandwich by elements and then an approximate unit of \(B\), and pass to weak-*
limits.  Cutting by \(p_{\rm nor}\) gives
\(q_B(q_C\mathcal A q_C)q_B\subset q_D\mathcal A q_D\).

For coefficient boundaries \(X_{\mathcal H}\), the desired support inclusion is
best viewed as a rectangular-support statement rather than as a product of
weak-* closed left ideals in the normal bidual.  In ordinary \(A^{**}\), the
support of \(X_{\mathcal H}\) is the right support of the rectangular module
generated by the concrete maps \(T_\xi\).  The concrete identities
\[
  T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*,\qquad T_{\alpha\otimes\beta}=T_\beta T_\alpha
\]
give
\[
 (T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2})
 (T_{\xi_3}^*T_{\xi_4})
 =
 T_{\xi_1\otimes\bar\xi_2\otimes\eta_1}^*
 T_{\xi_4\otimes\bar\xi_3\otimes\eta_2}.
\]
DKEP Proposition 5.9 (polar decomposition for rectangular relative compact
operators) and Theorem 5.10 (mixing/relative compactness equivalences) are the
references for passing from coefficient generators to hereditary closures and
supports.  The resulting support estimate is
\[
  q_{\mathcal H}(q_{\mathcal K}\mathcal A_N q_{\mathcal K})q_{\mathcal H}
  \subset
  q_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\bar{\mathcal H}\otimes_N\mathcal K}
  \mathcal A_N
  q_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\bar{\mathcal H}\otimes_N\mathcal K}.
\]
This is the replacement for the invalid binary-fusion/normal-left-ideal
argument.

In the graph-product case
\(\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\) is self-contragredient and
\(X_{\mathcal H_U}=X_{M_U}\).  CDHJKN Proposition 5.5 gives
\[
  \mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_W
  \cong \bigoplus_{Z\subset W}\mathcal H_Z^{\oplus k_Z},
\]
and fusing each summand with \(\mathcal H_U\) gives only summands
\(\mathcal H_Y\) with \(Y\subset Z\cap U\subset W\cap U\).  Direct sums are
handled through DKEP Theorem 5.10: finite-support bounded vectors are cyclic for
the direct sum, so the support of a direct-sum correspondence is below the join
of the supports of its summands.  Hence
\[
  q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W
  \subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A_Mq_{U\cap W}.
\]

A point for further audit is the exact formulation of the DKEP rectangular
support statement: one should avoid claiming that \(\K_{X_{\mathcal H}}
\K_{X_{\mathcal K}}\K_{X_{\mathcal H}}\subset
\K_{X_{\mathcal H\otimes\bar{\mathcal H}\otimes\mathcal K}}\) for arbitrary
correspondences, since it is too strong when \(\mathcal H\) is the identity
correspondence.  The correct assertion is the support-corner inclusion above.



\section*{Refined graph-product support route}

A safer way to prove the graph-product boundary intersection is to avoid an
abstract support-sandwich theorem for all correspondences and work with the
concrete coefficient algebras for the graph-product basic correspondences.

First, for a DKEP boundary piece \(X\subset A=\mathbb B(L^2N)\), let
\(e_X\in A^{**}\) be the ordinary open support.  The normal support used in
DKEP for \((\mathbb K_X(N)^{\sharp_J})^*\) agrees, after cutting by
\(p_{\rm nor}\), with the ordinary support of \(X\):
\[
        q_X=p_{\rm nor}e_X .
\]
Reason: \(X\subset \mathbb K_X(N)\) gives \(p_{\rm nor}e_X\le q_X\).  Conversely,
if a functional in \(A^{\sharp_J}\) vanishes on \(X\), DKEP's strong-closure
description of \(\mathbb K_X\) (the \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)/strong
\(N,JNJ\)-bimodule closure in Section 3) forces it to vanish on
\(\mathbb K_X\).  Thus \(X\) and \(\mathbb K_X\) have the same annihilator among
normal-bimodular functionals, hence the same normal support.  This is the bridge
between the concrete coefficient boundary and the \(q_X\) appearing in
\(\widetilde S_X\).

For \(U\subset V\), set
\[
        \mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M .
\]
The vector \(\hat1\otimes_{M_U}\hat1\) has \(T_\zeta=P_U\), the Jones projection.
Its \(M\)-\(M\) translates generate \(X_{M_U}\) (DKEP Example 3.4/5.11).  Let
\(B_U\) be the norm-closed span of coefficient operators coming from non-empty finite
Connes fusions of the correspondences \(\mathcal H_Z\) and
\(\overline{\mathcal H_Z}\) with \(Z\subset U\).  The DKEP identities for
\(T\)-operators make this a \(C^*\)-algebra, and CDHJKN fusion decomposes all
such non-empty finite fusions into \(\mathcal H_L\)'s with \(L\subset U\).  Since
\(L\subset U\) implies \(X_{M_L}\subset X_{M_U}\) (because \(P_L\in P_U
\mathbb B(L^2M)P_U\)), this algebra has the same support as \(X_{M_U}\).  Let \(s_U\)
be this support in \(A^{**}\).  Then
\[
        q_U=q_{X_{M_U}}=p_{\rm nor}s_U .
\]

The elementary \(C^*\)-support lemma needed is:

If \(B,C\subset A\) are \(C^*\)-subalgebras with supports \(r,s\), and if a
projection \(p\in A^{**}\) satisfies
\[
        BC\subset pA^{**}s,\qquad CB\subset sA^{**}p,
\]
then
\[
        r(sA^{**}s)r\subset pA^{**}p.
\]
Proof: let approximate units of \(B\) and \(C\) converge weak-* to \(r\) and
\(s\).  For \(x\in sA^{**}s\), the elements \(b_\alpha c_\beta x
c_{\beta'}b_{\alpha'}\) lie in \(pA^{**}p\), and successive weak-* limits give
the claim.

For graph products, the needed product-support hypotheses are
\[
        B_WB_U\subset s_{U\cap W}A^{**}s_U,\qquad
        B_UB_W\subset s_UA^{**}s_{U\cap W}.
\]
On coefficient generators, the first inclusion follows from
\[
 (T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2})
 =
 T_{\xi_1\otimes\bar\xi_2\otimes\eta_1}^{*}T_{\eta_2}.
\]
The \(\xi\)-vectors are from finite fusions of \(\mathcal H_Z\)'s with
\(Z\subset W\), and the \(\eta\)-vectors from finite fusions with \(Y\subset U\).
Since \(\mathcal H_Z\cong\overline{\mathcal H_Z}\), CDHJKN Proposition 5.5
decomposes the correspondence containing
\(\xi_1\otimes\bar\xi_2\otimes\eta_1\) into copies of \(\mathcal H_L\) with
\(L\subset U\cap W\), while \(T_{\eta_2}\) has right support \(s_U\).  The
second inclusion is the adjoint.  Norm closure extends this from generators to
\(B_WB_U\) and \(B_UB_W\).

Combining these product-support inclusions with the ordinary support lemma gives
\[
        s_W(s_UA^{**}s_U)s_W\subset s_{U\cap W}A^{**}s_{U\cap W}.
\]
Finally \(p_{\rm nor}\) commutes with the supports by DKEP Proposition 2.10, so
cutting gives
\[
        q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W
        \subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A_Mq_{U\cap W}.
\]
This route avoids the false/too-strong assertion
\(\mathbb K_{X_H}\mathbb K_{X_K}\mathbb K_{X_H}\subset \mathbb K_{X_L}\) and
also avoids needing a general correspondence support theorem beyond the
concrete graph-product coefficient/fusion calculation.



\section*{Round 10 consolidated proof details}

For a boundary piece \(X\subset B(L^2N)\), the normal support used in DKEP
Lemma 8.5 should be treated as the support of DKEP's hereditary algebra
\(K_X(N)\), not naively as the support of the originally displayed generators.
DKEP Prop. 2.10 says: if \(B\subset A=B(L^2N)\) is a \(C^*\)-algebra which is
simultaneously an \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodule with faithful multiplier actions,
and \(s_B\in A^{**}\) is its ordinary support, then
\[
  (B^{\sharp_J})^*=p_{\rm nor}s_BA^{**}s_Bp_{\rm nor}
\]
inside \((A^{\sharp_J})^*=p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\), and \(s_B\) commutes
with \(p_{\rm nor}\).  For \(B=K_X(N)\) this gives \(q_X=p_{\rm nor}s_B\).

For the graph-product subalgebra \(M_U\), \(K_{X_{M_U}}(M)\) can be described
as the norm-closed coefficient algebra generated by nonempty finite Connes
fusions of \(\mathcal H_Z=L^2M\otimes_{M_Z}L^2M\) and conjugates with
\(Z\subset U\).  The coefficient formulas
\[
  (T_\xi^*T_\eta)(T_\alpha^*T_\beta)
    =T_{\xi\otimes\bar\eta\otimes\alpha}^{\,*}T_\beta,\qquad
  (T_\xi^*T_\eta)^*=T_\eta^*T_\xi
\]
make this a \(C^*\)-algebra.  CDHJKN Theorem 5.4 and Proposition 5.5 imply that
finite fusions over subsets of \(U\) decompose into direct sums of
\(\mathcal H_L\)'s with \(L\subset U\), and fusing an \(U\)-piece with a
\(W\)-piece only produces \(L\subset U\cap W\).

Rectangular support principle used in the intersection lemma: if \(D\subset A\)
has open support \(d\), \(T:L^2N\to H\) is bounded, and \(T^*T\in D\), then for
an approximate unit \(u_i\) of \(D\), \(Tu_i\to T\) strongly; hence \(T=Td\) in
the bidual sense.  Therefore if \(S^*S\in C\) and \(C\) has support \(c\), then
\(T^*S\in dA^{**}c\).  This closes the previous rectangular gap in the product
\[
 (T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2})
 =
 T_{\xi_1\otimes\bar\xi_2\otimes\eta_1}^{\,*}T_{\eta_2}.
\]

Relative Bass--Serre paradox for \(N=P*_BQ\), \(Q=B\bar\otimes A\): use the
AFP decomposition
\[
L^2N=L^2B\oplus\bigoplus H_{i_1}^\circ\otimes_B\cdots\otimes_B H_{i_n}^\circ,
\qquad i_j\in\{P,Q\},\ i_j\ne i_{j+1}.
\]
Let \(p,q\) be the projections onto words with first letter \(P,Q\).  For
\(R_x=Jx^*J\),
\[
 [p,R_x]=R_xe_B-e_BR_x,\ [q,R_x]=0\quad (x\in P\ominus B),
\]
and with \(p,q\) interchanged for \(x\in Q\ominus B\).  These commutators are in
\(X_B\), so DKEP Lemma 6.1 yields \(p,q\in S_{X_B}(N)\).  If \(w_1,w_2\in P\)
satisfy \(E_B(w_i)=E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0\) and \(a\in A\subset Q\) is a trace-zero
unitary, then \(w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p\), \(apa^*\le q\), and
\(h=w_1a\) is \(B\)-Haar: \(E_B(h^n)=0\) for \(n\ne0\).  This forces any
\(N\)-central state on \(S_{X_B}(N)\) normal on \(N\) to vanish on
\(p,q,e_B\), contradiction.

Graph combinatorics preserved: connected \(\Gamma^c\), \(|V|\ge3\), implies
each vertex has a complement-neighbor.  If \(C(v)=\{r\}\), then \(r\) has a
complement-neighbor \(s\ne v\); necessarily \(s\in\Lk(v)\).  Thus the
Bass--Serre unitaries can be chosen as \(u_r,u_t\) or \(u_r,u_su_r\).
CDHJKN factoriality and amenability criteria exclude factor failure and
amenability because a universal vertex or an exceptional nonedge pair would be
an isolated vertex/edge of \(\Gamma^c\).



\section*{Round 11 refinement: normal support and coefficient model}

To avoid the earlier unsupported identity
\[
  K_{X_{M_U}}(M)=\text{``finite fusion coefficient algebra''},
\]
it is better to compute the normal-bidual support \(q_{X_{M_U}}\) from the
original DKEP boundary piece \(X_{M_U}\).  The relevant facts are:

\begin{itemize}
\item If \(B\subset A=\mathbb B(L^2N)\) is a hereditary \(C^*\)-subalgebra
which is an \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodule with faithful multiplier actions, and
\(s_B\in A^{**}\) is its ordinary open support, then the unit of
\((B^{\sharp_J})^*\subset (A^{\sharp_J})^*\) is \(p_{\rm nor}s_B\).  This is
not quoted as a literal statement of DKEP Proposition 2.10; rather it follows
from the paragraph before DKEP Remark 2.8 (one normal category) plus Proposition
2.10 for the simultaneous \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-normal category.  Equivalently, an
approximate unit of \(B\) converges to \(p_{\rm nor}s_B\) in the normal bidual.
\item DKEP Proposition 3.6 gives
\(K_X^{\infty,1}=\overline{X}^{\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}}
=\overline{K_X}^{\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}}\).  Since the \(\sharp_J\)-functionals
are continuous for this closure, \(X\) and \(K_X\) have the same annihilator in
\(A^{\sharp_J}\).  Thus \(q_X\) may be computed as the normal support of the
original boundary piece \(X\).
\item For \(U\subset V\), the boundary piece \(X_{M_U}\) is the amplified
coefficient algebra of the \(M\)-\(M\) correspondence
\(\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\).  The cyclic vector \(1\otimes1\) has
coefficient \(e_{M_U}\), and Hilbert amplifications account for the arbitrary
corner \(e_{M_U}\mathbb B(L^2M)e_{M_U}\) appearing in DKEP Example 3.4.
\item Infinite direct sums should be handled by support, not by norm
approximation of bounded vectors.  If \(\mathcal H=\oplus_i\mathcal H_i\) and
the coefficients of all \(\mathcal H_i\)'s are supported by \(s\), then every
coefficient of a bounded vector in \(\mathcal H\) is in \(sA^{**}s\): finite
coordinate truncations converge strong-* at the \(T_\xi\)-operator level, so
their coefficients converge weak-*.
\item With \(s_U=\operatorname{supp}(X_{M_U})\), the key product is
\(X_{M_W}X_{M_U}\subset s_{U\cap W}A^{**}s_U\).  For coefficients
\[
 (T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2})
 =
 T_{\xi_1\otimes\bar\xi_2\otimes\eta_1}^{\,*}T_{\eta_2}.
\]
The left vector belongs to an amplification of
\(\mathcal H_W\otimes\overline{\mathcal H_W}\otimes\mathcal H_U\).  Since
\(\overline{\mathcal H_W}\cong\mathcal H_W\), CDHJKN Theorem 5.4 and
Proposition 5.5 decompose this as a direct sum of \(\mathcal H_Z\)'s with
\(Z\subset U\cap W\).  The rectangular support lemma then gives the required
product inclusion.  Applying approximate units yields
\(s_W(s_UA^{**}s_U)s_W\subset s_{U\cap W}A^{**}s_{U\cap W}\), and cutting by
\(p_{\rm nor}\) gives the normal-bidual corner intersection.
\end{itemize}

This support-based approach directly addresses the earlier criticism about
finite-support vectors in infinite direct sums: the proof only uses rectangular support for finite off-diagonal component coefficients, strong-*
convergence of coordinate truncations, and weak-* closedness of support corners,
not operator-norm convergence of \(T_{\xi_F}\) to \(T_\xi\).


\section*{Round 12 refined support proof}

The current answer uses a more robust version of the graph-product boundary
intersection, avoiding the earlier unsupported density assertion
``\(X_{M_U}\) is norm-spanned by amplified coefficients''.

Normal-bidual support.  Let \(A=\mathbb B(L^2N)\) and let \(p_{\rm nor}\) be
the projection implementing the simultaneous \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-normal bidual
\((A^{\sharp_J})^*\).  For a hereditary \(N,JNJ\)-bimodular subalgebra
\(B\subset A\), the normal-bidual support of \(B\) is obtained from the ordinary
open support \(s_B\in A^{**}\):
\[
        q(B)=p_{\rm nor}s_B=s_Bp_{\rm nor}.
\]
The justification is the one-normal cutdown in the paragraph before DKEP
Remark 2.8 plus DKEP Proposition 2.10 for the simultaneous normal category.  A
contractive approximate unit of \(B\) converges ultraweakly to \(q(B)\) in the
normal bidual.  For a DKEP boundary piece \(X\), DKEP Proposition 3.6 and
Proposition 2.6 imply that \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) have identical annihilators in
\(A^{\sharp_J}\), so \(q_X\) may be computed from the original boundary piece.

Coefficient model for \(X_{M_U}\).  For
\(\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\), the vector
\(\Omega_U=1\otimes 1\) has \(T_{\Omega_U}=e_{M_U}\).  DKEP Example 5.11
therefore gives a coefficient boundary \(X_{\mathcal H_U}\) containing
\(e_{M_U}\); because it is hereditary and \(M,JMJ\)-invariant, it contains the
hereditary algebra generated by the translated \(e_{M_U}\)'s, i.e. DKEP
Example 3.4's \(X_{M_U}\).  Conversely, coefficients of \(M\)-\(M\) translates
of \(\Omega_U\) are translated \(e_{M_U}\)-coefficients, so
\(X_{\mathcal H_U}\subset X_{M_U}\).  Hence \(X_{\mathcal H_U}=X_{M_U}\), and
heredity explains why the full corner \(e_{M_U}A e_{M_U}\) is present.  If
\(Z\subset U\), then \(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_U}\), hence \(X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_U}\)
and \(s_Z\le s_U\).

Special approximate units.  Although the whole hereditary algebra \(X_{M_U}\)
need not be norm-spanned by simple coefficients, it has a contractive
approximate unit contained in the \(C^*\)-algebra generated by the positive
coefficient generators.  Taking functions \(f_\varepsilon(\sum T_{\xi_j}^*
T_{\xi_j})\), with \(f_\varepsilon(0)=0\), produces such an approximate unit.
Polynomials in the finite sums expand into monomials in coefficient generators,
and DKEP's formulas
\[
        T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*,\qquad
        T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi
\]
rewrite those monomials as finite sums of coefficients of finite Connes fusions
of \(\mathcal H_U\) and \(\overline{\mathcal H_U}\).  Thus we can choose
approximate units \(h_\alpha^U\) which are norm limits of finite-fusion
coefficients.

Fusion support.  CDHJKN Proposition 5.5, applied repeatedly, says that any
finite fusion whose labels all lie in \(U\) decomposes as a direct sum of
\(\mathcal H_Z=L^2M\otimes_{M_Z}L^2M\)'s with \(Z\subset U\).  If the labels of
one block lie in \(W\) and the next block lies in \(U\), then after reducing the
\(W\)-block and fusing with the \(U\)-block only labels \(Z\subset W\cap U\)
remain.  Direct sums are handled at the support level: coordinate truncations of
bounded vectors preserve boundedness and the associated \(T\)-operators
converge strong-*, so coefficients are weak-* limits of finite-coordinate
coefficients.  Off-diagonal finite-coordinate coefficients are controlled by
the rectangular support fact \(T^*T\in dA^{**}d,\ S^*S\in cA^{**}c
\Rightarrow T^*S\in dA^{**}c\).

Boundary intersection mechanism.  Let \(I=U\cap W\).  For the special
approximate units just described,
\[
        h_\alpha^W h_\beta^U\in s_I A^{**}s_U,\qquad
        h_\beta^U h_\alpha^W\in s_U A^{**}s_I.
\]
On finite-fusion coefficient terms this follows from
\[
 (T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2})
 =T_{\xi_1\otimes\bar\xi_2\otimes\eta_1}^{\,*}T_{\eta_2}.
\]
The left vector is supported by \(s_I\) by the preceding fusion calculation,
and the right vector by \(s_U\); the rectangular support lemma gives the
rectangular containment.  Then for \(x\in s_UA^{**}s_U\),
\[
s_Wxs_W=\text{weak-*}\lim_{\alpha,\beta,\gamma,\delta}
 h_\alpha^W h_\beta^U x h_\gamma^U h_\delta^W\in s_IA^{**}s_I.
\]
Cutting by \(p_{\rm nor}\) gives
\[
        q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W
        \subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A_Mq_{U\cap W}.
\]
This is the central repair: one only needs special approximate units in
\(X_{M_U}\), not norm-density of all of \(X_{M_U}\) by simple coefficients.



\section*{Refined normal-support calculus using DKEP Theorem 5.6}

For \(A=\mathbb B(L^2N)\) and
\(\mathcal A_N=(A^{\sharp_J})^*\), avoid representing \(\mathcal A_N\) as
\(p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\) with a central projection.  The safe statement
is the following.

If \(B\subset A\) is a hereditary \(C^*\)-subalgebra invariant under the
multiplier actions of \(N\) and \(JNJ\), and \(s_B\in A^{**}\) is the ordinary
open support, then DKEP's one-normal support paragraph, applied separately to
the two normal structures, together with Proposition 2.10 gives
\[
        (B^{\sharp_J})^*=\mathcal A_N\cap s_BA^{**}s_B
\]
inside \(A^{**}\).  This is a corner of \(\mathcal A_N\); its unit is denoted
\(q(B)\).  Thus a support inclusion in the ordinary enveloping algebra passes
to the normal bidual: if
\[
        s_C(s_BA^{**}s_B)s_C\subset s_DA^{**}s_D,
\]
then
\[
        q(C)(q(B)\mathcal A_Nq(B))q(C)\subset q(D)\mathcal A_Nq(D).
\]
This avoids any multiplicativity assertion for the canonical map
\(A\to\mathcal A_N\).

For a DKEP boundary piece \(X\), the support \(q_X\) defined from
\(K_X(N)\) agrees with \(q(X)\), the normal support of the original hereditary
boundary piece.  The proof uses DKEP Theorem 5.6 rather than Proposition 2.6:
with \(X=Y\), condition (4) says that if
\(T\in K_{X,X}^{\infty,1}(N,N)=K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\), then every
\(\sharp_J\)-functional vanishing on \(XB(L^2N)X\) vanishes on \(T\).  Since
\(X\) is hereditary, \(XB(L^2N)X\) is norm dense in \(X\).  Hence any
\(\sharp_J\)-functional vanishing on \(X\) vanishes on \(K_X(N)\), because
\(K_X(N)\subset K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\).  The converse is \(X\subset K_X(N)\).
Thus \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) have the same annihilator in \(A^{\sharp_J}\), hence
the same weak-* closed corner in \(\mathcal A_N\).

\section*{Graph-product boundary intersection in ordinary supports}

For \(U\subset V\), let \(s_U\) be the ordinary open support of the concrete
boundary piece \(X_{M_U}\).  The coefficient correspondence
\(\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\) has cyclic vector
\(\Omega_U=1\otimes1\) with \(T_{\Omega_U}=e_{M_U}\); hence
\(X_{\mathcal H_U}=X_{M_U}\).  If \(Z\subset U\), then
\(e_{M_Z}\le e_{M_U}\), and \(X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_U}\).

A useful approximate unit for \(X_{M_U}\) is obtained from finite sums of
positive coefficient generators:
\[
        f_\varepsilon\left(\sum_{j=1}^n T_{\xi_j}^*T_{\xi_j}\right),
        \qquad f_\varepsilon(0)=0 .
\]
Polynomial approximation and the identities \(T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*\) and
\(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\) show that these approximate-unit elements
are norm limits of the rectangular finite-fusion coefficient algebra generated by
\(\mathcal H_U\) and \(\overline{\mathcal H_U}\).

For rectangular finite-fusion coefficients
\(a=T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2}\) from \(W\)-labels and
\(b=T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2}\) from \(U\)-labels,
\[
        ab=T_{\xi_1\otimes\bar\xi_2\otimes\eta_1}^{\,*}T_{\eta_2}.
\]
CDHJKN's fusion rule for \(H_U(V,V)=\mathcal H_U\) implies that the left vector
is supported by a direct sum of \(\mathcal H_Z\)'s with
\(Z\subset U\cap W\), while the right vector is supported by \(s_U\).  The
rectangular fact \(T^*T\in sA^{**}s,\ S^*S\in tA^{**}t\Rightarrow
T^*S\in sA^{**}t\) gives
\[
        X_{M_W}X_{M_U}\subset s_{U\cap W}A^{**}s_U
\]
at the level of the chosen approximate-unit nets, and similarly for the
adjoint product.  Passing to weak-* limits of approximate units gives
\[
        s_W(s_UA^{**}s_U)s_W\subset s_{U\cap W}A^{**}s_{U\cap W}.
\]
The normal-support passage above then gives
\[
        q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W
        \subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A_Mq_{U\cap W}.
\]



\section*{Coefficient boundary of the induced subalgebra correspondence}

Let \(B\subset N\) be an expected inclusion of finite tracial von Neumann
algebras.  Write
\[
H={}_N L^2N_B,\qquad K={}_B L^2N_N,\qquad
\mathcal H_B=H\otimes_B K.
\]
DKEP Example 5.11 says \(X_H=X_B\), where \(X_B\) is the boundary piece of
Example 3.4.  On the \(B\)-side of \(H\), the vector \(\widehat 1\) gives
\(T_{\widehat 1}T_{\widehat 1}^{*}=1_{L^2B}\), so the right coefficient
boundary \(Y_H\) is all of \(\mathbb B(L^2B)\).  Likewise, for \(K\), the left
coefficient boundary is all of \(\mathbb B(L^2B)\).  Hence \(H\) is mixing
relative to \(X_B\times\mathbb B(L^2B)\), and \(K\) is mixing relative to
\(\mathbb B(L^2B)\times\mathbb B(L^2N)\).  Using DKEP Theorem 5.10 in condition
(1), the cyclic set of bounded elementary tensors in \(H\otimes_BK\), and
\[
        T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi,
\]
one gets that \(\mathcal H_B\) is mixing relative to
\(X_B\times\mathbb B(L^2N)\).  By the minimality property of Example 5.11,
\(X_{\mathcal H_B}\subset X_B\).

The opposite inclusion is even more direct.  If \(\xi,\eta\) are bounded
vectors in \(H={}_NL^2N_B\), then \(\xi\otimes\widehat1\) and
\(\eta\otimes\widehat1\) are bounded in \(\mathcal H_B\), and
\[
        T_{\xi\otimes\widehat1}^*T_{\eta\otimes\widehat1}
        =T_\xi^*(T_{\widehat1}^*T_{\widehat1})T_\eta=T_\xi^*T_\eta,
\]
because \(T_{\widehat1}^*T_{\widehat1}=1_{L^2B}\) for
\(K={}_BL^2N_N\).  Thus all coefficient generators of \(X_H=X_B\) are
coefficient generators for \(X_{\mathcal H_B}\), and
\(X_B\subset X_{\mathcal H_B}\).  Hence
\[
        X_{\mathcal H_B}=X_B.
\]
In the tracial setting the conjugate of \(\mathcal H_B\) is isomorphic to
\(\mathcal H_B\) through
\[
        \overline{\xi\otimes\eta}\mapsto J\eta\otimes J\xi .
\]

For \(Z\subset U\), \(e_{M_Z}\leq e_{M_U}\); since \(e_{M_U}\in X_{M_U}\) and
\(X_{M_U}\) is hereditary, \(e_{M_Z}\in X_{M_U}\), and multiplier invariance then
gives \(X_{M_Z}\subset X_{M_U}\).

\section*{Direct sums, normal bidual support, and finite-fusion coefficients}

For a boundary piece \(X\subset A=\mathbb B(L^2N)\) with
\(N,JNJ\subset M(X)\), the support projection \(q_X\) used in DKEP Lemma 8.5 is
best handled through the \(A^{\sharp_J}\)-annihilator, not through ordinary
strong-operator limits in the universal bidual.  DKEP Theorem 5.6 gives:
if \(\phi\in A^{\sharp_J}\) vanishes on \(X\), then \(\phi\) vanishes on
\(K_X(N)\).  The reason is that \(X\) is hereditary, so \(XAX\subset X\), and
every \(T\in K_X(N)\) lies in \(K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\).  Conversely \(X\subset
K_X(N)\).  Hence \(X\) and \(K_X(N)\) have the same \(A^{\sharp_J}\)-annihilator,
and the weak-* closure of \(X\) inside
\[
        \mathcal A_N=(\mathbb B(L^2N)^{\sharp_J})^*
\]
is the corner \(q_X\mathcal A_Nq_X\).

Important dead end: finite-coordinate cutdowns in an infinite direct sum often
converge strongly as operators on \(L^2N\), but this is not weak-* convergence
in the universal enveloping bidual of \(\mathbb B(L^2N)\).  For instance, tail
projections in \(B(\ell^2)\) converge strongly to \(0\) but not against singular
states.  Therefore support arguments should be made either in the
\(\sharp_J\)-normal bidual via annihilators, or via DKEP's mixing/strong-module
closure machinery.

A useful replacement is the following direct-sum principle.  Suppose
\(\mathcal K=\bigoplus_j\mathcal K_j\) and \(X_{\mathcal K_j}\subset X\) for all
\(j\).  DKEP Example 5.11 and monotonicity of boundary pieces make each
\(\mathcal K_j\) mixing relative to \(X\times\mathbb B(L^2N)\).  Let \(H_0\) be the set of bounded vectors
\(\xi\in\mathcal K\) for which \(L_\xi\in B_N(L^2N,\mathcal K)\) is
\(X\)-mixing.  Finite-coordinate vectors are in \(H_0\).  If
\(\xi_n\in H_0\), \(\xi_n\to\xi\) in Hilbert norm, and \(\xi\) is bounded, the
closure step in DKEP Theorem 5.10 applies: for
\(\rho_n(a)=\langle(\xi-\xi_n)a,(\xi-\xi_n)\rangle\) choose projections
\(p_k\uparrow1\) with \(\|L_{\xi-\xi_n}p_k\|\to0\) for each fixed \(k\); since
the mixing operators form a strong right \(N\)-module (DKEP Proposition 5.1),
\(\xi p_k\in H_0\), and strong closedness gives \(\xi\in H_0\).  Therefore the
direct sum is mixing relative to \(X\times\mathbb B(L^2N)\), and DKEP Theorem
5.10 plus Proposition 5.9 yields \(T_\xi^*T_\xi\in K_X(N)\) for every bounded
\(\xi\).  Polarization puts all coefficients in the corner
\(q_X\mathcal A_Nq_X\).
This is the support statement needed for corner computations; it should not be
silently replaced by the stronger norm inclusion \(X_{\mathcal K}\subset X\)
unless one has separately verified that the relevant coefficients actually lie
in \(X\) rather than merely in \(K_X(N)\) or its normal-bidual corner.

Inside \(\mathcal A_N\), the rectangular support calculation is purely
von Neumann algebraic:
\[
        R^*R\in r\mathcal A_Nr,\quad S^*S\in s\mathcal A_Ns
        \quad\Longrightarrow\quad R^*S\in r\mathcal A_Ns,
\]
because \(R(1-r)=0\) and \(S(1-s)=0\).

For graph-product applications the finite-fusion coefficient algebra remains:
\[
 \mathcal C_U=\operatorname{span}\{T_\xi^*T_\eta:
    \xi\in\mathcal K,\ \eta\in\mathcal L \text{ bounded},\
    \mathcal K,\mathcal L\in\mathcal F_U\},
\]
where \(\mathcal F_U\) consists of non-empty finite fusions of
\(\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\) and its conjugate.  Multiplication is
given by
\[
        (T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2})
        =T_{\xi_1\otimes\overline{\xi_2}\otimes\eta_1}^{\,*}T_{\eta_2}.
\]
Since \(X_{M_U}=X_{\mathcal H_U}\), the positive coefficient generators of
\(X_{M_U}\) lie in \(\mathcal C_U\); functional calculus of finite positive
sums gives a contractive approximate unit for \(X_{M_U}\) in
\(\overline{\mathcal C_U}\), and this net converges weak-* to \(q_{M_U}\) in
\(\mathcal A_M\).

The boundary-corner intersection is then proved by multiplying approximate
units.  If \(a\in\mathcal C_W\) and \(b\in\mathcal C_U\), CDHJKN fusion rules
decompose the vector in the first slot of
\[
        ab=T_{\zeta}^{\,*}T_\eta,\qquad
        \zeta\in(\text{finite fusion containing both }W\text{ and }U),
\]
as a direct sum of \(\mathcal H_R\)'s with \(R\subset W\cap U\), while
\(\eta\) is supported over subsets of \(U\).  The direct-sum principle and
rectangular support therefore give
\[
        ab\in q_{W\cap U}\mathcal A_Mq_U,\qquad
        ba\in q_U\mathcal A_Mq_{W\cap U}.
\]
Weak-* limiting the approximate units gives
\[
        q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W
        \subset q_{W\cap U}\mathcal A_Mq_{W\cap U}.
\]



\section*{Normal support method for graph-product boundary intersections}

For a boundary piece \(X\subset A=\mathbb B(L^2N)\), write
\(\bar q_X\in A^{**}\) for the open support of the hereditary algebra \(X\)
and \(p_N\in A^{**}\) for DKEP's simultaneous \(N,JNJ\)-normal support
projection.  DKEP Proposition 2.10 gives \(\bar q_Xp_N=p_N\bar q_X\); the
normal-bidual support is \(q_X=p_N\bar q_X\).  Multiplication of concrete
operators must not be moved into \((A^{\sharp_J})^*\): the safe procedure is to
multiply in \(A^{**}\), then compress by \(p_N\) only at the level of support
projections.

For graph-product subalgebra pieces \(X_U=X_{M_U}\), the key support relation is
\[
        q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_{U\cap W}.
\]
A robust proof is by normal annihilators.  Choose c.a.u.'s
\(h_\alpha^W\subset\overline{\mathcal C_W}_+\) and
\(h_\beta^U\subset\overline{\mathcal C_U}_+\).  Positive elements in these
closures are norm limits of positive finite-fusion coefficients
\(T_\xi^*T_\xi\).  If \(h=T_\xi^*T_\xi\) comes from a finite \(W\)-fusion and
\(k=T_\eta^*T_\eta\) from a finite \(U\)-fusion, then
\[
        hkh=T_{\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta}^*
             T_{\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta}.
\]
By the CDHJKN fusion rule, the correspondence containing
\(\xi\otimes\bar\xi\otimes\eta\) decomposes over subsets of \(U\cap W\).  The
direct-sum mixing closure then puts \(hkh\) in
\(K_{X_{U\cap W}}^{\infty,1}\).  Hence every \(\phi\in A^{\sharp_J}\) vanishing
on \(X_{U\cap W}\) satisfies
\(\phi(h_\alpha^Wh_\beta^Uh_\alpha^W)=0\), and weak-* limits in \(A^{**}\) (using strong-* convergence of approximate units to open supports and bounded joint strong-* continuity of multiplication)
give \(\phi(\bar q_W\bar q_U\bar q_W)=0\).  Compressing by \(p_N\) yields the
normal-bidual support relation without using the nonmultiplicative map
\(A\to(A^{\sharp_J})^*\).

This pairwise inequality is enough for the finite-boundary criterion.  The
palindromic product \(q_m\cdots q_1q_2\cdots q_m\) is positive.  If the shorter
palindrome is supported by \(q_J\), then its compression by \(q_{U_m}\) is
dominated by \(q_{U_m}q_Jq_{U_m}\le q_{J\cap U_m}\).
