## Fatal issue: the subalgebra-fusion lemma contains a false coefficient computation

In Lemma `subalgebra fusion`, the proof asserts:

\[
\text{“the positive coefficients of the vectors }x\zeta_By\text{ are }
xJyJ\,e_B\,Jy^*Jx^*.” 
\]

With DKEP’s convention \(T_\xi=L_\xi^*R_\xi\), this formula is not correct in general. DKEP explicitly defines \(T_\xi\) by \(T_\xi=L_\xi^*R_\xi\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

A simple check already disproves the displayed formula. Take \(B=\mathbb C\subset N\), with \(N\) a noncommutative finite factor, and take \(x,y\in\mathcal U(N)\). Then

\[
\mathcal H_B=L^2N\otimes L^2N,\qquad \zeta_B=1\otimes 1.
\]

For \(\xi=x\zeta_By=x\otimes y\), a direct computation gives

\[
T_\xi(\widehat b)
 = L_\xi^*R_\xi(\widehat b)
 = \tau(x^*bx)\widehat{y^*y}
 = \tau(b)\widehat 1,
\]

so \(T_\xi=e_{\mathbb C}\), hence \(T_\xi^*T_\xi=e_{\mathbb C}\). But the formula in the proof gives

\[
xJyJ\,e_{\mathbb C}\,Jy^*Jx^*,
\]

which is the rank-one projection onto the vector \(xy^*\) up to the usual identification. For \(xy^*\notin\mathbb C1\), this is not \(e_{\mathbb C}\). Thus the asserted coefficient identity is false.

This is not cosmetic. The proof uses that false identity to conclude that the hereditary algebra generated by positive coefficients of \(\mathcal H_B\) contains all generators \(xJyJe_B\) of \(X_B\). Without that step, the first assertion of Lemma `subalgebra fusion` is unproved.

### Consequences of this gap

The false coefficient computation propagates through the proof:

- Lemma `finite fusion calculus` uses Lemma `subalgebra fusion` to assert that \(X_{M_U}\) has an approximate unit built from positive coefficients of \(H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\).
- Lemma `intersection of boundary supports` depends on those special approximate units.
- The final passage from relative proper proximality for the link boundaries \(X_{M_{L_v}}\) to proper proximality depends on Lemma `intersection`.

So the main technical bridge of the proof is still not rigorously established.

A correct route may exist using DKEP Example 5.11 and a linking/coefficient argument with \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\), but the proof currently written does not provide it. DKEP Example 5.11 identifies \(X_H\) for \(H={}_NL^2N_B\) with the subalgebra boundary, but that is not the same as the false formula for positive coefficients of \(x\zeta_By\) in \(H\otimes_B\bar H\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

## Section-by-section audit

### Problem interpretation

The interpretation of irreducible as nontrivial join-irreducibility is appropriate, and the complement-connected equivalence is standard for finite simple graphs. The draft explicitly records this interpretation, satisfying the ambiguity requirement.

### Support bookkeeping lemma

This is much improved. The one-normal support statement is supported by DKEP Section 2.3, and the simultaneous-normal statement is plausibly obtained using DKEP Proposition 2.10. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) I no longer regard this as the primary fatal defect.

The equality \(pr_X=pr_{K_X(N)}=q_X\) is still compressed into a short annihilator argument, but it is plausible given DKEP Proposition 2.4 and Proposition 3.6. I would prefer more detail, but this is not the decisive obstruction.

### Lemma `subalgebra fusion`

This is the main failure. The proof’s central coefficient formula for \(x\zeta_By\) is false, as explained above. Since this lemma is used to build approximate units for \(X_{M_U}\), the rest of the proof cannot be certified.

The later use of DKEP Theorem 5.10 in the same lemma is plausible, but it does not repair the first assertion.

### Lemma `finite fusion calculus`

The shift from the previous coefficient \(*\)-algebra to a \(T_\xi\)-operator algebra is an improvement. DKEP indeed gives \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\) and \(T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) The claim that \(\mathcal T_U\) is a \(*\)-algebra is now much more credible.

However, the approximate-unit conclusion depends directly on the faulty Lemma `subalgebra fusion`. Therefore this lemma is not established as written.

### Lemma `intersection of boundary supports`

The use of CDHJKN’s fusion rule is appropriate in spirit. CDHJKN Theorem 5.4 decomposes the relevant graph-product bimodules, and Proposition 5.5 gives the fusion rule over intersections. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150))

But the proof relies on approximate units from the positive cones supplied by Lemma `finite fusion calculus`, which in turn relies on the false coefficient-generation argument. Thus the crucial inequality

\[
0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_{U\cap W}
\]

remains unproved in the submitted draft.

### Finite-boundary-to-compact-boundary lemma

This part is substantially improved and is likely correct conditional on the support framework. The compression argument, the GNS projection argument, and the use of the compact-corner u.c.p. map are now clearly stated. DKEP Lemma 8.5 supports the bidual central-state criterion used here. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

### Bass–Serre proposition

This section is also improved. The reduced-word commutator formulas and paradoxical inequalities are plausible. The invocation of DKEP Lemma 6.1 is now better justified; DKEP Lemma 6.1 does allow checking the commutator condition on an ultraweakly dense generating \(*\)-subalgebra. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) I do not see a fatal issue here.

### Graph-product application

Conditional on the previous lemmas, the graph-product application is mostly correct. The AFP decomposition is standard in graph products, the choice of \(w_1,w_2\) is combinatorially sound, and CDHJKN Theorem 2.4 supports the diffuse full factor conclusion for join-irreducible graphs with \(|V|\ge3\). ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

The final step still fails because the boundary-support intersection lemma is not proved.

---

## Overall verdict

The revision fixes several formal and expository issues, and the global strategy remains plausible. But `answer.tex` still does **not** constitute a complete rigorous proof. The revised Lemma `subalgebra fusion` contains a false coefficient identity, and that lemma is essential for constructing the approximate units used in the boundary-support intersection argument. Consequently the proof of the central inequality \(q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_{U\cap W}\) is not valid as written.
