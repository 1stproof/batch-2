Problem interpretation: ``irreducible'' is taken to mean join-irreducible for a
finite simplicial graph, i.e. no nonempty proper partition $V=S\sqcup T$ with
all cross edges; equivalently $\Gamma^c$ is connected.  The traces are assumed
faithful normal tracial states.

Strategy recorded:
Use induction over an ordering of vertices coming from a spanning tree of the
connected complement graph.  Start with a length-two path in the complement.
For $V_k=\{v_1,\ldots,v_k\}$, maintain that the induced complement graph is
connected and $M_{V_k}$ is properly proximal.

Inputs used:
1. DKEP permanence for proper proximality:
   (a) free products of tracial von Neumann algebras with centered unitaries are
       properly proximal except the $\mathbb C^2*\mathbb C^2$ exceptional case;
   (b) if $P$ is properly proximal and not amenable relative to $B\subset P$,
       then $P*_B(B\bar\otimes A)$ is properly proximal when $A$ has a
       trace-zero unitary.
2. CHHJKN relative amenability criterion for graph-product subalgebras:
   $M_X$ amenable relative to $M_Y$ iff amenability of the outside vertex
   algebras and the adjacency/2-dimensional exceptional condition hold.

Key induction details:
For adding $v_k$, let $L=V_{k-1}\cap\operatorname{Lk}_\Gamma(v_k)$.  The graph
product decomposition is
\[
M_{V_k}=M_{V_{k-1}}*_{M_L}(M_L\bar\otimes M_{v_k}).
\]
The chosen ordering gives a complement edge $v_k-r_k$ with $r_k\in V_{k-1}$,
and a complement edge $r_k-s_k$ with $s_k\in V_{k-1}$.  Thus $r_k\notin L$ and
$r_k,s_k$ are nonadjacent in $\Gamma$.  If $M_{V_{k-1}}$ were amenable relative
to $M_L$, the relative amenability criterion would force $r_k,s_k$ to be the
exceptional pair, adjacent in $\Gamma$ to all other vertices of $V_{k-1}$.
Equivalently $\{r_k,s_k\}$ is a connected component of the induced complement,
contradicting connectedness and $|V_{k-1}|\ge3$.

Base:
For complement path $v_1-v_2-v_3$, $M_{V_3}=M_{\{v_1,v_3\}}*M_{v_2}$ since
$v_2$ is not adjacent in $\Gamma$ to $v_1,v_3$.  The first free-product factor
is not $\mathbb C^2$ because it is generated by two non-scalar vertex algebras.
DKEP free-product theorem gives proper proximality.
