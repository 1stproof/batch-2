## 1. Normal-bidual support cutdown

The formula in `answer.tex` lines 51--60 is directionally right, but the citation should be softened. DKEP explicitly proves the one-normal formula before Remark 2.8, and then Proposition 2.10 gives the simultaneous \(M\)- and \(\widetilde M\)-normal category as an intersection/inclusion, not literally the displayed formula for every hereditary \(B\).

Evidence:

- DKEP p. 11 / `DKEP.tex` lines 467--473: for a normal \(M\)-\(C^*\)-algebra \(A\), \((A^\sharp)^*\cong p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\). If \(B\subset A\) is an \(M\)-bimodular \(C^*\)-subalgebra with faithful multiplier action, and \(q_B\) is the support of \(B^{**}\) in \(A^{**}\), then \(q_B\) commutes with \(p_{\rm nor}\) and
  \[
  (B^\sharp)^*\cong q_Bp_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}q_B.
  \]
  For hereditary \(B\), \(q_B=s_B\).
- DKEP Proposition 2.10 / `DKEP.tex` lines 495--502: in the simultaneous category,
  \[
  (A^{M\sharp M}\cap A^{\widetilde M\sharp\widetilde M})^*
  =(A^{M\sharp M})^*\cap(A^{\widetilde M\sharp\widetilde M})^*
  \]
  inside \(M(A)^{**}\), and similarly gives a nonunital inclusion for \(B\).
- Before Lemma 8.5, DKEP explicitly recalls the exact kind of corner needed for boundary pieces: `DKEP.tex` line 1571 says \((K_X(M)_J^\sharp)^*\) is “a corner” of \((B(L^2M)_J^\sharp)^*\), with support commuting with both \(M\) and \(JMJ\). This is also in the PDF on p. 40, lines 11--14 of `pdftotext` output.

Safe replacement wording:

```tex
Let \(q(B)\) denote the support projection of the embedded corner
\((B_J^\sharp)^*\subset \cA_N=(A_N{}_J^\sharp)^*\), equivalently the
ultraweak limit in \(\cA_N\) of a contractive approximate unit of \(B\).
In the one-sided normal category, DKEP's paragraph before Remark 2.8 gives
\((B^\sharp)^*\cong s_Bp_{\rm nor}A_N^{**}p_{\rm nor}s_B\) when \(B\) is
hereditary.  Proposition 2.10 gives the simultaneous \(N\)- and
\(JNJ\)-normal corner; for DKEP boundary pieces this is recalled explicitly
before Lemma 8.5.  We write this corner as \(q(B)\cA_Nq(B)\), and its support
commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\).
```

This avoids attributing the simultaneous \(p_{\rm nor}s_BA^{**}s_Bp_{\rm nor}\) formula directly to Prop. 2.10. If `answer.tex` keeps that formula, define \(p_{\rm nor}\) as the identity/support projection for the simultaneous normal category and say the formula is derived from the one-sided paragraph plus Prop. 2.10.

## 2. DKEP Proposition 3.6 and annihilators

The claim is valid, but the explanation in `answer.tex` lines 62--65 should not say simply that \(\sharp_J\)-functionals are continuous for the \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)-closure. The mechanism is strong-bimodule closure.

Evidence:

- DKEP defines \(K_X^{\infty,1}\) as the \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)-closure of \(X\): `DKEP.tex` lines 660--666.
- Proposition 3.6 / `DKEP.tex` lines 670--686: \(K_X^{\infty,1}=\overline{K_X}^{\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}}\), and this is a self-adjoint closed strong \(M\)-\(M\) and \(JMJ\)-\(JMJ\) bimodule.
- Proposition 2.6 / `DKEP.tex` lines 407--418: a \(\sharp_J\)-functional vanishing on \(X\) vanishes on the smallest simultaneous strong bimodule generated by \(X\).
- DKEP uses exactly this implication in the proof of Theorem 6.2: `DKEP.tex` line 1317 says that if \(\varphi\in B(L^2M)_J^\sharp\) and \(\varphi|_X=0\), then \(\varphi|_{K_X^{\infty,1}(M)}=0\).

Thus \(X\) and \(K_X\) have the same annihilator in \(A_N^{\sharp_J}\): one direction follows from the strong-closure argument above, and the other from \(X\subset K_X\). Consequently they have the same weak-* closure/support in the normal bidual.

Suggested replacement for lines 62--65:

```tex
For a DKEP boundary piece \(X\), Proposition 3.6 together with the
strong-bimodule characterization of Proposition 2.6 implies that every
\(\sharp_J\)-functional on \(A_N\) which vanishes on \(X\) vanishes on
\(K_X^{\infty,1}(N)\), hence on \(K_X(N)\).  Since \(X\subset K_X(N)\), \(X\)
and \(K_X(N)\) have the same annihilator in \(A_N^{\sharp_J}\), and hence the
same normal-bidual support.
```

## 3. \(X_{M_U}\) as a coefficient boundary

DKEP Examples 3.4 and 5.11 justify the intended identification, with one caveat: do not say that an arbitrary \(T\in e_{M_U}B(L^2M)e_{M_U}\) is literally a single amplified coefficient unless you add a proof. What DKEP directly gives is stronger and safer: \(X_{M_U}\) is the hereditary algebra containing that whole corner.

Evidence:

- DKEP Example 3.4 / `DKEP.tex` lines 630--631: \(X_B\) is the norm-closed span of \(x_1Jy_1J\,T\,Jy_2Jx_2\), with \(T\in e_BB(L^2M)e_B\), and it is the hereditary algebra generated by \(xJyJe_B\).
- DKEP Example 5.11 / `DKEP.tex` lines 1144--1147: for a correspondence \(\mathcal H\), \(X_\mathcal H\) is the hereditary \(C^*\)-algebra generated by \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\); if \(B\subset M\) and \(L^2M\) is viewed as an \(M\)-\(B\) correspondence, this is exactly Example 3.4.

For \(\mathcal H_U=L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M\), the vector \(\Omega=1\otimes 1\) has \(T_\Omega^*T_\Omega=e_{M_U}\). Since the coefficient algebra is hereditary, containing \(e_{M_U}\) implies it contains \(e_{M_U}B(L^2M)e_{M_U}\). So the answer’s use of the full corner is fine, but I would replace “amplifications account for the whole corner” by:

```tex
DKEP Example 3.4 defines \(X_{M_U}\) as the hereditary algebra generated by
the \(M\)- and \(JMJ\)-translates of \(e_{M_U}\), equivalently the norm-closed
span with arbitrary \(T\in e_{M_U}A_Me_{M_U}\).  Example 5.11 identifies this
with the coefficient boundary associated to the corresponding basic
construction correspondence; in \(\mathcal H_U\), \(1\otimes1\) has coefficient
\(e_{M_U}\), and hereditary closure gives the full corner.
```

## 4. CDHJKN fusion reference

The citation to CDHJKN Theorem 5.4 and Proposition 5.5 is accurate, but the proof should say “apply Proposition 5.5 twice.”

Evidence:

- CDHJKN Theorem 5.4 / `cube.tex` lines 1196--1206 gives the direct-sum decomposition of \({}_{M_{V_1}}L^2(M)_{M_{V_2}}\) into
  \[
  \mathscr H_U(V_1,V_2)
  ={}_{M_{V_1}}L^2(M_{V_1})\otimes_{M_U}L^2(M_{V_2})_{M_{V_2}}.
  \]
- CDHJKN Proposition 5.5 / `cube.tex` lines 1223--1233 gives
  \[
  \mathscr H_{U_1}(V_1,V_2)\otimes_{M_{V_2}}\mathscr H_{U_2}(V_2,V_3)
  \cong
  \bigoplus_{Z\subset U_1\cap U_2}\mathscr H_Z(V_1,V_3)^{\oplus k_{\mathcal G_2}(U_1,U_2,Z)}.
  \]

Taking \(V_1=V_2=V_3=V\), \(\mathcal H_U=\mathscr H_U(V,V)\). In the tracial case \(\overline{\mathcal H_W}\cong\mathcal H_W\). First
\[
\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\overline{\mathcal H_W}\cong
\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_W
\]
decomposes into \(\mathcal H_Y\)'s with \(Y\subset W\). Fusing each \(\mathcal H_Y\) with \(\mathcal H_U\) decomposes into \(\mathcal H_Z\)'s with \(Z\subset Y\cap U\subset W\cap U\). This proves the support inclusion needed in `answer.tex` lines 139--150.

Suggested wording:

```tex
Since \(\overline{\cH_W}\cong\cH_W\) in the tracial standard form, two
applications of CDHJKN Proposition 5.5, using the notation introduced in
Theorem 5.4, decompose
\(\cH_W\otimes_M\overline{\cH_W}\otimes_M\cH_U\) as a direct sum of
\(\cH_Z\)'s with \(Z\subset W\cap U\), with multiplicities irrelevant here.
```

Overall: I found no fatal gap in the boundary-corner intersection after these wording changes. The main citation inaccuracies are (i) over-attributing the simultaneous cutdown formula to DKEP Prop. 2.10, and (ii) describing the Prop. 3.6 annihilator step as plain \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)-continuity rather than strong-bimodule closure.
