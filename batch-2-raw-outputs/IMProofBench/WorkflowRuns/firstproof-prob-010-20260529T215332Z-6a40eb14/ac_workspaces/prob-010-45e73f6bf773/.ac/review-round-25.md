### What the revision fixed

The previous fatal translated-Jones-vector coefficient formula has been removed. The replacement correctly uses DKEP Example 5.11 for the elementary identity

The finite-boundary-to-compact-boundary lemma and Bass–Serre argument remain substantially improved and are plausible conditional on the technical boundary-intersection lemma.

### Remaining fatal issue: \(T_\xi\) is still treated as if it were linear in \(\xi\)

The revised Lemma `subalgebra fusion` still has a serious gap. It defines \(\mathcal E_B\) as the linear span of the elementary operators \(T_{\eta\otimes_B\bar\xi}\), then says:

> “The reverse inclusion follows from the displayed identity, because \(T_\alpha\in\mathcal E_B\) for every bounded finite elementary sum \(\alpha\).”

This does **not** follow. DKEP’s operator is
\[
T_\xi=L_\xi^*R_\xi,
\]
so it is quadratic in \(\xi\), not linear. In general,
\[
T_{\xi+\eta}
=
T_\xi+T_\eta+L_\xi^*R_\eta+L_\eta^*R_\xi,
\]
and \(T_{\lambda\xi}=|\lambda|^2T_\xi\), not \(\lambda T_\xi\). The fusion identity proves the formula for **elementary tensors**, but not for finite sums inside the same correspondence. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

Thus the proof does not establish that, for a bounded finite elementary sum
\[
\alpha=\sum_j \eta_j\otimes_B\bar\xi_j\in \mathcal H_B,
\]
one has \(T_\alpha\in\mathcal E_B\), nor that \(T_\alpha^*T_\alpha\in X_B\). This is exactly what is needed for the claimed reverse inclusion \(Y\subset X_B\) in Lemma `subalgebra fusion`.

This is not merely a wording problem: the later approximate-unit construction in Lemma `finite fusion calculus` depends on the assertion that \(X_{M_U}\) is hereditarily generated by the stated positive \(T_\xi^*T_\xi\)’s. Since that assertion is still unproved, the boundary-support intersection lemma is not rigorously established.

### Downstream effect

Because Lemma `subalgebra fusion` remains incomplete, the following chain is still broken:

1. Lemma `subalgebra fusion` does not rigorously prove the required positive-generator description of \(X_B\).
2. Lemma `finite fusion calculus` uses that generator description to build approximate units in the norm closure of \(\{t^*t:t\in\mathcal T_U\}\).
3. Lemma `intersection of boundary supports` uses those approximate units to prove
   \[
   0\le q_Wq_Uq_W\le q_{U\cap W}.
   \]
4. The final proof of proper proximality depends on that intersection lemma.

The global strategy remains plausible, and this may be repairable by explicitly proving that \(T_\alpha\) for finite elementary sums belongs to \(X_B\), perhaps via the full \(e_B\mathbb B(L^2N)e_B\) corner in Example 3.4 or a linking-operator computation. But that proof is not present in `answer.tex`.

### Other comments

The support-bookkeeping lemma is much better motivated than in earlier versions, though still somewhat compressed. Since the proof already fails at the subalgebra-fusion/approximate-unit step, I do not need to decide whether the support bookkeeping is fully acceptable.

The graph-product application is correct only conditional on the unresolved intersection lemma. The cited CDHJKN results do support the intended bimodule and full-factor inputs. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.08150))

### Verdict

The revision fixes the previous explicit false formula, but it introduces or leaves in place a related essential gap: it treats the DKEP coefficient operator \(T_\xi\) as if it were linear in finite sums of vectors. This invalidates the proof of the positive-generator description of \(X_B\), and hence the central boundary-support intersection argument remains incomplete.
