## Referee report on the revised draft

### Verdict

The revised `answer.tex` **satisfies the LaTeX contract** and compiles to a 6-page PDF. However, it is **not mathematically ready**. The previous fatal strong-operator-to-universal-bidual weak-* error has been addressed in spirit, but the revision introduces/reintroduces a different fatal issue: it performs concrete \(B(L^2N)\)-multiplication inside DKEP’s normal bidual \(\mathcal A_N=(B(L^2N)^{\sharp_J})^*\), even though the canonical map \(B(L^2N)\to\mathcal A_N\) is **not a \(*\)-homomorphism in general**. DKEP explicitly warns of this in Remark 2.8. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

This invalidates the “rectangular fact” (2), the product computations in the boundary-corner intersection lemma, and hence the palindromic compact-corner inclusion needed for the final finite-boundary criterion.

### Remaining / new fatal issue

The central new problem is that the proof now works “inside \(\mathcal A_M\)” while using concrete multiplication formulas from \(B(L^2M)\). This is not justified.

DKEP Remark 2.8 states that although the canonical embedding
\[
A\to (A^\sharp)^*
\]
is a complete order isomorphism onto its range, it is **not a \(*\)-homomorphism in general**. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) Thus, for concrete operators \(a,b\in B(L^2M)\), the product of their images in \(\mathcal A_M\) need not equal the image of the concrete product \(ab\).

This directly breaks several essential steps.

## Detailed audit by section

### 1. Problem statement and interpretation

The adopted interpretation of irreducibility as “not a nontrivial join,” equivalently \(\Gamma^c\) connected, is reasonable and explicitly recorded. No issue here.

---

### 2. Normal supports and graph-product boundary corners

The attempt to identify the weak-* closure of \(X\) in \(\mathcal A_N\) with the DKEP support corner is plausible for the relevant \(X_{M_U}\), using DKEP Theorem 5.6 and the equality of annihilators. But the section immediately introduces the fatal “rectangular fact”:

\[
R^*R\in r\mathcal A_Nr,\quad S^*S\in s\mathcal A_Ns
\Longrightarrow R^*S\in r\mathcal A_Ns.
\tag{2}
\]

This statement is true **inside a von Neumann algebra** if \(R,S\) are elements of that von Neumann algebra and \(R^*R,S^*S\) are computed using that von Neumann algebra’s multiplication. But in the proof, \(R,S\) are concrete operators in \(B(L^2N)\), and \(R^*R\), \(R^*S\) are concrete products. Their images in \(\mathcal A_N\) need not satisfy the same multiplicative identities.

So the sentence

> “Indeed \(R(1-r)=0\) and \(S(1-s)=0\).”

is unjustified. From the fact that the **image of the concrete operator** \(R^*R\) lies in \(r\mathcal A_Nr\), one cannot conclude that the image of \(R\) satisfies \(R(1-r)=0\) in \(\mathcal A_N\).

This is a fatal support-calculus error.

### 4. Lemma `\ref{lem:dsupport}`: direct sums and finite coefficient algebras

The replacement of the previous invalid strong-to-universal-bidual argument by a DKEP mixing-closure argument is a real improvement. DKEP’s proof of Theorem 5.10 does use exactly the kind of projection-cutdown closure argument the draft invokes. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

However, the lemma still culminates in:

> “every coefficient of \(\mathcal K\) belongs, after the canonical embedding \(B(L^2N)\subset\mathcal A_N\), to \(q_X\mathcal A_Nq_X\).”

This support conclusion is acceptable only as a statement about the **image of the concrete coefficient operator**. It does not justify later multiplying such images as if the concrete coefficient-product identities remained valid in \(\mathcal A_N\).

Thus, the direct-sum repair itself may be salvageable, but it does not support the later boundary-intersection proof as written.

---

### 5. Lemma `\ref{lem:intersection}`: boundary-corner intersection

This is the central failure.

The proof wants to show

\[
h_\alpha^W h_\beta^U\in q_I\mathcal A_M q_U.
\tag{4}
\]

It reduces by norm closure to concrete coefficients
\[
a=T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2}\in\mathcal C_W,\qquad
b=T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2}\in\mathcal C_U
\]
and then uses the concrete identity
\[
ab
=
T_{\xi_1\otimes\overline{\xi_2}\otimes\eta_1}^{\,*}T_{\eta_2}.
\tag{5}
\]

This identity is valid in the concrete algebra \(B(L^2M)\), by DKEP’s Connes-fusion formula. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) But the proof needs an identity for the product of the **images of \(a\) and \(b\) in \(\mathcal A_M\)**. Since the canonical map into \(\mathcal A_M\) is not multiplicative in general, the image of \(ab\) need not equal the product of the images of \(a\) and \(b\). DKEP explicitly warns about this nonmultiplicativity. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

Therefore:

- The reduction from \(h_\alpha^W h_\beta^U\) to concrete coefficient products is invalid.
- The use of the rectangular support fact (2) is invalid.
- The inclusion \(q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W\subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A_Mq_{U\cap W}\) is not proved.
- The palindromic compact-corner inclusion is not proved.

This is fatal because the final theorem depends on this lemma.

### 6. Finite-boundary criterion

This section is mostly plausible. The compression argument by \(q_i^\perp\) is sound in spirit because DKEP states that the relevant support projection commutes with \(M\) and \(JMJ\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) The GNS argument for the palindromic positive contraction is also reasonable.

The notation
\[
\Theta(T)=q_0Tq_0
\]
must be interpreted as DKEP’s u.c.p. compression, not as a multiplicative embedding of \(B(L^2N)\) into \(\mathcal A_N\). With that interpretation, this section is likely repairable. It is not the main obstruction.

### 7. Relative Bass–Serre paradox

The argument remains terse but plausible. The reduced-word commutator formulas and projection inequalities should be expanded in a complete proof, but I did not find a definite mathematical contradiction here.

This section is not the fatal problem.

### 8. Application to graph products

The graph-combinatorial part is correct. I also brute-force checked the finite-graph implications for small vertex numbers: connectedness of \(\Gamma^c\) with \(|V|\ge3\) rules out universal vertices and isolated complement edges, and gives the required Bass–Serre unitaries.

The use of CDHJKN for factoriality and nonamenability is appropriate: Main Theorem 0.5 gives the factor criterion, and Proposition 6.3 gives the amenability criterion. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

However, this final application depends on Lemma `\ref{lem:intersection}`, which is not proved.

## Summary of mathematical status

The revision fixes the previous direct-sum topology problem in a plausible way, but the proof still fails at the most important point: the boundary-corner intersection argument uses concrete coefficient multiplication inside a normal bidual where the canonical embedding is not multiplicative.

To make this proof rigorous, the author would need to return to a support calculation in an ordinary enveloping von Neumann algebra \(A^{**}\), or develop a genuine rectangular normal-bidual calculus showing that the relevant products in \(\mathcal A_M\) are controlled by DKEP’s \(K_{X,Y}\)-corners. The current text does neither.

Accordingly, the proof is not complete.
