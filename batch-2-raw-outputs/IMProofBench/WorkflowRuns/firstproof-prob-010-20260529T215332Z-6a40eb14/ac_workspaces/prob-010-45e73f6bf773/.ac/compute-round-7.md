# Round 7 source audit: support-sandwich lemma

## Files inspected

- Current canonical proof: `problem_documents_readonly/answer.tex`, especially lines 50-113 and Lemma `coefficient support sandwich` at lines 85-102.
- DKEP arXiv source: `papers/dkep_src/DKEP.tex`.
- Extracted DKEP PDF text: `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt`.

## Bottom line

I do **not** think Lemma `coefficient support sandwich` is fully justified as written. The problematic part is not the concrete Connes-fusion identity \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\); DKEP states that explicitly. The gap is the upgrade from that concrete identity to multiplication/composition inside
\[
\mathcal A_N=(B(L^2N)^\sharp_J)^*,
\]
where the canonical map \(B(L^2N)\to \mathcal A_N\) is explicitly **not** multiplicative in general.

The specific unsupported assertions in `answer.tex` are:

- lines 63-69: \(q_{\mathcal H}\) is asserted to be the right support of the weak-* closed normal left ideal generated by the concrete coefficient maps \(T_\xi\);
- lines 71-83: composition of “rectangular normal bimodules” is asserted to be separately normal and compatible with concrete operator composition, yielding
  \[
  \mathcal L_{\mathcal K}\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}
  \subset \mathcal L_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\mathcal K};
  \]
- lines 95-96: the proof uses this to put \(q_{\mathcal K}q_{\mathcal H}\) in the support of \(\mathcal H\otimes_N\mathcal K\).

I found DKEP statements supporting nearby ingredients, but not these exact upgrades.

## Exact DKEP source locations

### Normal bidual and the nonmultiplicativity warning

DKEP constructs the normal enveloping algebra via \(p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\):

- `DKEP.tex:467-473`: defines \(p_{\rm nor}\), identifies \((A^\sharp)^*\cong p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\), and says that for \(B\subset A\), the support \(q_B\) commutes with \(p_{\rm nor}\) and
  \[
  (B^\sharp)^*\cong q_Bp_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}q_B.
  \]
- `DKEP.tex:495-502` / PDF text `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt:533-540`: Proposition 2.10 gives the simultaneous \(M\) and \(\widetilde M\) version.

But DKEP immediately warns:

- `DKEP.tex:476-478` / PDF text `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt:504-510`: the canonical embedding
  \[
  A\to(A^\sharp)^*
  \]
  is a complete order embedding but is **not a \(*\)-homomorphism in general**, because \(p_{\rm nor}\) need not be central.

This directly blocks the naive inference
\[
\iota(T_\eta T_\xi)=\iota(T_\eta)\iota(T_\xi)
\]
inside \((B(L^2N)^\sharp_J)^*\).

### Concrete coefficient and fusion formulas

DKEP does state the concrete identities:

- `DKEP.tex:1062-1066` / PDF text `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt:1220-1230`: definition of bounded vectors and \(T_\xi=L_\xi^*R_\xi\).
- `DKEP.tex:1068-1072` / PDF text `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt:1231-1235`: for the contragredient correspondence, \(T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*\).
- `DKEP.tex:1074-1079` / PDF text `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt:1238-1246`: for bounded \(\xi,\eta\),
  \[
  T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi.
  \]

These are concrete operator equalities in \(B(L^2N)\), not multiplicative identities after applying \(B(L^2N)\to(B(L^2N)^\sharp_J)^*\).

### Proposition 5.9 and coefficient boundary

Relevant but insufficient:

- `DKEP.tex:1045-1055` / PDF text `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt:1203-1213`: Proposition 5.9 says
  \[
  T\in \mathbb K_{\mathcal X,\mathcal Y}(N,M)
  \iff
  T^*T\in \mathbb K_{\mathcal Y}(N),\quad TT^*\in \mathbb K_{\mathcal X}(M),
  \]
  with the proof using polar decomposition.
- `DKEP.tex:1144-1147` / PDF text `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt:1335-1342`: Example 5.11 defines the coefficient boundary \(X_{\mathcal H}\) as the hereditary \(C^*\)-algebra generated by \(T_\xi^*T_\eta\), and says it is the smallest boundary piece making \(\mathcal H\) mixing.

I did **not** find a DKEP statement saying that the support \(q_{\mathcal H}\) of \((\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}^\sharp{}_J)^*\) is the right support of the weak-* closed normal left ideal in \(\mathcal A_N\) generated by the images of \(T_\xi\). Proposition 5.9 is close in spirit, but it is a concrete relative-compactness criterion; because of the nonmultiplicativity warning, it does not by itself identify
\[
\iota(T_\xi)^*\iota(T_\xi)
\quad\text{with}\quad
\iota(T_\xi^*T_\xi).
\]

### Separate continuity/composition

DKEP has two separate-continuity statements, neither of which is the one used in `answer.tex`:

- `DKEP.tex:314` / PDF text `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt:259-263`: multiplication in \(M\otimes_{eh}N\) is weak-* continuous in the second variable but not generally in the first.
- `DKEP.tex:742-755` / PDF text `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt:828-835`: the smaller convolution algebra \(M\otimes_{\rm Con}N^{\rm op}\) is a dual Banach \(*\)-algebra with separately weak-* continuous multiplication.
- `DKEP.tex:942-949` / PDF text `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt:1053-1058`: Corollary 5.2 says multiplication in \(M\otimes_{\rm Con}N^{\rm op}\) is separately continuous in the \(X\)-\(Y\) topology.

These concern convolution/extended-Haagerup maps acting on \(B(L^2N,L^2M)\). I did not find a statement that the normal biduals of rectangular operator spaces carry a separately normal composition product extending concrete operator composition.

There is also a more basic algebraic issue: even for ordinary concrete left ideals, the product of left ideals generated by \(T_\eta\) and \(T_\xi\) contains terms \(aT_\eta bT_\xi\) with an arbitrary middle \(b\). The formula \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\) only handles the case \(b=1\). Thus the inclusion
\[
\mathcal L_{\mathcal K}\mathcal L_{\mathcal H}
\subset \mathcal L_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\mathcal K}
\]
does not follow from the concrete fusion formula.

## A safe replacement lemma

The following support lemma is justified by DKEP Proposition 2.10 and avoids the nonmultiplicative normal-bidual embedding.

**Lemma (ordinary-envelope sandwich).** Let \(A=B(L^2N)\), let \(p=p_{\rm nor}\), and let
\(\mathcal A_N=pA^{**}p\). Let \(B,C,D\subset A\) be hereditary \(C^*\)-subalgebras invariant under the left/right actions of \(N\) and \(JNJ\). Let \(e_B,e_C,e_D\in A^{**}\) be their ordinary open supports and put \(q_B=pe_B\), etc. If
\[
C\,B\,C\subset D
\]
as concrete products in \(A\), then
\[
q_C(q_B\mathcal A_Nq_B)q_C\subset q_D\mathcal A_Nq_D.
\]

**Proof.** Since \(e_BA^{**}e_B=B^{**}\), every element of \(e_BA^{**}e_B\) is a weak-* limit of a bounded net from \(B\). If \(z\in e_BA^{**}e_B\) and \(c_1,c_2\in C\), then \(c_1zc_2\) is a weak-* limit of elements of \(CBC\), hence lies in the weak-* closed corner \(e_DA^{**}e_D\). Taking a contractive approximate unit of \(C\) and passing to weak-* limits gives
\[
e_C(e_BA^{**}e_B)e_C\subset e_DA^{**}e_D.
\]
DKEP Proposition 2.10 gives \(pe_\bullet=e_\bullet p\), so cutting the last inclusion by \(p\) gives the claimed inclusion in \(pA^{**}p=\mathcal A_N\).

For coefficient boundaries there is a concrete generator calculation:
\[
(T_{\xi_1}^*T_{\xi_2})(T_{\eta_1}^*T_{\eta_2})(T_{\xi_3}^*T_{\xi_4})
=
T_{\xi_1\otimes\bar\xi_2\otimes\eta_1}^*
T_{\xi_4\otimes\bar\xi_3\otimes\eta_2}.
\]
This uses exactly DKEP’s concrete identities \(T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*\) and \(T_{\alpha\otimes\beta}=T_\beta T_\alpha\). Thus, at the level of the coefficient hereditary algebras,
\[
X_{\mathcal H}X_{\mathcal K}X_{\mathcal H}
\subset
X_{\mathcal H\otimes_N\bar{\mathcal H}\otimes_N\mathcal K}.
\]

What I did **not** find in DKEP is the additional upgrade needed to apply the ordinary-envelope lemma to the actual DKEP support projections \(q_X\), which are supports of \((\mathbb K_X(N)^\sharp_J)^*\), not just of the raw coefficient algebra \(X\). A sufficient missing statement would be
\[
\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}(N)\,
\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal K}}(N)\,
\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H}}(N)
\subset
\mathbb K_{X_{\mathcal H\otimes\bar{\mathcal H}\otimes\mathcal K}}(N),
\]
or an equivalent ordinary-support inclusion. I did not locate this statement in DKEP.

For the graph-product intersection, this would still be enough: take
\(\mathcal H=\mathcal H_W\), \(\mathcal K=\mathcal H_U\). The correspondence
\(\mathcal H_W=L^2M\otimes_{M_W}L^2M\) is self-contragredient, so the corrected target is
\[
\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_W\otimes_M\mathcal H_U.
\]
Applying the CDHJKN fusion rule twice should decompose this into summands \(\mathcal H_Z\) with \(Z\subset U\cap W\), so the desired graph-product corner inclusion follows once the above \(\mathbb K_X\)-level product/support upgrade is supplied.

## Auxiliary checks

1. **\(X_{L^2M\otimes_{M_U}L^2M}=X_{M_U}\).** This is fine, with a small direct computation. For \(\zeta=\hat1\otimes_{M_U}\hat1\), \(T_\zeta=e_{M_U}\). The \(M\)-\(M\) translates of \(\zeta\) give the generators \(xJyJ e_{M_U}\). DKEP Example 3.4 (`DKEP.tex:630-632`; PDF text `data/DKEP_2204.00517.txt:662-670`) defines \(X_B\) as the hereditary algebra generated by exactly these \(xJyJe_B\). DKEP Example 5.11 (`DKEP.tex:1144-1147`; PDF text `:1335-1342`) is the coefficient-boundary reference.

2. **Direct sums.** Finite direct sums are fine by the hereditary-corner/Douglas argument: if \(a^*a,b^*b\) are in a hereditary corner, then \(a^*b\) is in that corner. For infinite direct sums, I would not rely on the terse phrase “truncating bounded vectors” unless the relevant bounded-vector topology is stated. A cleaner justification is via DKEP Theorem 5.10 (`DKEP.tex:1085-1091`; PDF text `:1250-1258`): finite-support bounded vectors are cyclic for \(\oplus_j\mathcal H_j\), and if all summand supports are below a boundary support \(q\), those finite-support vectors are mixing relative to that boundary. Hence the coefficient boundary of the direct sum is supported below the join. This is the right way to handle possible infinite multiplicities in the CDHJKN fusion decomposition.

## Recommendation for `answer.tex`

Do not keep Lemma `coefficient support sandwich` in its current \(q_{\mathcal H\otimes\mathcal K}\) form. DKEP does not appear to justify the normal-left-ideal support characterization or the separately normal rectangular composition needed for it, and DKEP’s nonmultiplicativity warning points in the opposite direction.

Replace it either by the conditional ordinary-envelope lemma above plus an explicit proof of the missing \(\mathbb K_X\)-level triple-product/support inclusion, or state the remaining \(\mathbb K_X\)-level inclusion as a separate lemma to be proved. The target correspondence should be the triple fusion
\[
\mathcal H\otimes_N\bar{\mathcal H}\otimes_N\mathcal K,
\]
not merely \(\mathcal H\otimes_N\mathcal K\), unless a genuinely new normal-left-ideal composition theorem is established.
