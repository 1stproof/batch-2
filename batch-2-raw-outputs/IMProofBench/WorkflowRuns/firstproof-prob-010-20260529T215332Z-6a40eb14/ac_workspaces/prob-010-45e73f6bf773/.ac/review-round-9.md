## Referee report

### Overall verdict

I **do not certify** `answer.tex` as a complete rigorous solution. The LaTeX contract appears satisfied, and the high-level strategy may be plausible, but several essential operator-algebraic steps are either unproved or stated in a form stronger than what is justified by the cited literature. The most serious gap is the **boundary-support intersection lemma**, which is central to the proof and depends on multiple unsupported support-calculus assertions.

I verified the cited literature at a high level. DKEP’s normal-bidual and boundary-piece framework does contain the relevant ingredients: \(p_{\rm nor}A^{**}p_{\rm nor}\)-type normal biduals and corner inclusions for sub-\(C^*\)-algebras, plus Lemma 8.5’s normal-bidual criterion. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) CDHJKN indeed contains the graph-product bimodule decomposition/fusion rules, factoriality criterion, and amenability criterion cited by the author. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743)) However, `answer.tex` does not prove enough to legitimately apply these results in the way it does.

### LaTeX contract check

I compiled `answer.tex` twice with `pdflatex`. It compiles successfully to a **5-page** PDF. The file uses exactly `\documentclass[12pt]{article}`, uses the permitted `fullpage` package, and I found no prohibited margin/layout, spacing, or in-document font-size changes. Thus there is **no LaTeX-contract failure**.

## Detailed mathematical audit

### 1. Problem statement and interpretation

The interpretation of irreducibility as “not a nontrivial join,” equivalently \(\Gamma^c\) connected, is reasonable and should be explicitly stated because the problem statement’s wording omits some standard qualifiers such as nonempty/proper subgraphs. This part is acceptable.

The statement that DKEP Lemma 8.5 specializes to finite factors with the trivial group action is essentially correct: DKEP Lemma 8.5 assumes a separable finite von Neumann algebra with an action whose restriction to the center is ergodic; for a factor and the trivial action, the center condition is automatic. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517))

### 2. Normal support identification \(q_X=p_{\rm nor}e_X\)

This is a **major gap**.

The proof uses the identification
\[
q_X=p_{\rm nor}e_X,
\]
where \(e_X\) is the ordinary open support of the boundary piece \(X\subset B(L^2N)\), while \(q_X\) is the support of \((\mathbb K_X(N)^\sharp_J)^*\) in the DKEP normal bidual. DKEP does discuss \(p_{\rm nor}\), ordinary support projections of \(C^*\)-subalgebras, and the resulting normal-bidual corner inclusions. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) But `answer.tex` needs to prove, not merely assert, that the support of \(X\) and the support of \(\mathbb K_X(N)\) agree after cutting by \(p_{\rm nor}\).

The given argument is incomplete for several reasons:

- It does not precisely define \(A^\sharp_J\) or show why annihilation by all functionals in \(A^\sharp_J\) characterizes the normal support after cutting by \(p_{\rm nor}\).
- It invokes “DKEP’s strong-closure proposition for normal \(N,JNJ\)-bimodular functionals” without a precise theorem number or a statement sufficient to imply the desired annihilator equality.
- It uses convergence in \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\), but does not prove that every relevant \(\psi\in A^\sharp_J\) is continuous for the topology in which the closure is taken.
- It does not carefully handle passage from products \(S^*R\) to all of \(\mathbb K_X=(\mathbb K_X^L)^*\mathbb K_X^L\) and then to the normal-bidual support.

This support identification is foundational for all later \(q_U=p_{\rm nor}s_U\) computations. As written, it is not a complete proof.

### 3. The coefficient algebra \(\mathcal B_U\)

The paragraph defining \(\mathcal B_U\) contains several unproved assertions.

The author defines \(\mathcal B_U\) as the norm-closed linear span of coefficients coming from nonempty finite Connes fusions of \(\mathcal H_Z\) and \(\overline{\mathcal H_Z}\), \(Z\subset U\), and then says DKEP’s identities imply this is a \(C^*\)-algebra. DKEP does have the identities \(T_{\bar\xi}=T_\xi^*\) and \(T_{\xi\otimes\eta}=T_\eta T_\xi\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) But to conclude that the closed linear span is a \(C^*\)-algebra, one must check products of coefficients from possibly different fusions and show the resulting operators are again in the prescribed span. This is not fully written.

The statement that \(\mathcal B_U\) has the same ordinary support as \(X_{M_U}\) is also under-justified. The author uses the fact that \(P_L\in P_U B(L^2M)P_U\) when \(L\subset U\), but does not carefully prove that this implies \(X_{M_L}\subset X_{M_U}\) in DKEP’s boundary-piece sense. DKEP’s \(X_B\) is the hereditary \(C^*\)-subalgebra generated by operators of the form \(xJyJe_B\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) The inclusion is plausible, but the proof in `answer.tex` is only a sketch.

### 4. Lemma: intersection of graph-product boundary corners

This is the **central fatal gap**.

The lemma claims
\[
q_W(q_U\mathcal A_Mq_U)q_W\subset q_{U\cap W}\mathcal A_Mq_{U\cap W}.
\]
The proof reduces this to the product-support inclusion
\[
\mathcal B_W\mathcal B_U\subset s_{U\cap W}A^{**}s_U.
\]

The key unsupported step is the sentence:

> Therefore the first factor on the right of (6) is supported by \(s_I\), while \(T_{\eta_2}\) is supported by \(s_U\), and the first inclusion in (5) follows.

This is not automatic. From knowing that a vector belongs to a correspondence whose coefficient boundary has support \(s_I\), one only immediately controls coefficient operators such as \(T_\alpha^*T_\beta\). To conclude that a **rectangular operator** \(T_\alpha^*\) lies in \(s_I A^{**}\), or that \(T_{\eta_2}\) lies in \(A^{**}s_U\), one needs a rectangular support lemma: for example, a proof that both \(T_\alpha^*T_\alpha\) and \(T_\alpha T_\alpha^*\) are controlled by the relevant support, and that this implies the desired one-sided support relation. No such lemma is stated or proved.

This is not a minor omission: the entire support-sandwich argument depends on this rectangular support step.

The use of CDHJKN fusion rules is also too compressed. CDHJKN Theorem 5.4 and Proposition 5.5 give precise decompositions of \(M_{V_1}\)-\(M_{V_2}\) bimodules and their fusions. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743)) The author’s repeated-iteration use for arbitrary finite fusions of \(\mathcal H_Z\) and contragredients is plausible, but it is not written carefully enough to cover arbitrary bounded vectors in direct sums, off-diagonal coefficients, or infinite multiplicities.

Finally, the cut by \(p_{\rm nor}\) uses the assertion that all supports \(s_U\) commute with \(p_{\rm nor}\). DKEP gives such commutation for ordinary supports of appropriate \(C^*\)-subalgebras satisfying the normal multiplier hypotheses. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) But `answer.tex` does not verify those hypotheses for the newly defined \(\mathcal B_U\), especially simultaneous \(M\)- and \(JMJ\)-bimodularity and faithful multiplier actions.

### 5. Finite palindromic boundary criterion

This lemma is one of the more convincing parts, but it still has some missing details.

The compression argument using \(q_i^\perp\widetilde S_{X_i}q_i^\perp\subset\widetilde S(N)\) is plausible because \(q_i\) commutes with \(N\) and \(JNJ\). The GNS argument with the palindromic product is also plausible.

However, the final hypertrace step relies on a u.c.p. map
\[
\Theta:B(L^2N)\to q_0\mathcal A_Nq_0.
\]
DKEP’s proof of Lemma 8.5 does produce natural u.c.p. maps from \(B(L^2M)\) into the normal-bidual compact corner. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) But `answer.tex` should explicitly state the exact DKEP construction being used and why it is \(N\)- and \(JNJ\)-bimodular in the required sense. This is likely fixable, but as written it is still a cited black box with insufficient precision.

### 6. Relative Bass–Serre paradox proposition

The proposition is plausible but not rigorous enough as written.

The reduced-word commutator formulas
\[
[p,R_x]=R_xe_B-e_BR_x,\qquad [q,R_x]=0
\]
are asserted by “direct inspection.” For a self-contained proof at this level, one must check them on all relevant reduced-word summands in the amalgamated-free-product \(L^2\)-decomposition, including the one-letter and \(B\)-summand cases. The proof also needs to explicitly show that these commutators lie in the relative compact boundary piece \(X_B\) or its \(\|\cdot\|_{\infty,1}\)-closure, so that DKEP Lemma 6.1 applies. DKEP Lemma 6.1 is about when commutator conditions on a dense generating set imply proper proximality of a point. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.00517)) `answer.tex` does not spell out the membership in the correct \(X_B\)-mixing/compact space.

The inequalities
\[
w_1qw_1^*+w_2qw_2^*\le p,\qquad apa^*\le q
\]
also need a careful \(B\)-valued inner-product proof. Orthogonality of the two images is asserted from \(E_B(w_1^*w_2)=0\), but the proof should verify this on arbitrary \(Q\)-initial reduced-word vectors.

The “\(B\)-Haar” step for \(h=w_1a\) is plausible, but the proof should explicitly cover negative powers and show that the projections \(h^ne_Bh^{-n}\) are pairwise orthogonal by computing \(E_B(h^{m-n})=0\).

These are not merely stylistic omissions: Proposition \(\ref{prop:BS}\) is the only source of relative proper proximality for the graph-product links, so it must be completely rigorous.

### 7. Application to graph products

The graph-theoretic part is mostly sound.

I cross-checked the elementary combinatorial implications by brute force for graphs with \(3\le |V|\le 6\): complement connectedness implies every vertex has a non-neighbor; in the singleton \(C(v)\) case there is an \(s\in\operatorname{Lk}(v)\) nonadjacent to the unique \(r\in C(v)\); the intersection of all links is empty; and connected complement with at least three vertices has no isolated complement vertex or isolated complement edge. This supports the author’s graph-combinatorial reductions.

The use of CDHJKN’s factoriality criterion is valid in spirit. CDHJKN Main Theorem 0.5 says factoriality fails only through universal vertices with nonfactor vertex algebra or through an exceptional nonedge pair with both dimensions \(2\). ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743)) In a connected complement graph with at least three vertices, those obstructions are indeed impossible.

The use of CDHJKN Proposition 6.3 for nonamenability is also valid in spirit: amenability would force every nonedge pair to be a two-dimensional exceptional pair adjacent to all other vertices, which is incompatible with connected complement on at least three vertices. ([ems.press](https://ems.press/content/serial-article-files/51743))

However, because the earlier relative-proximality and boundary-intersection steps are not fully proved, the final application cannot be certified.

## Summary of blocking issues

The solution is **not answer-ready** because of the following essential unresolved gaps:

1. The identification \(q_X=p_{\rm nor}e_X\) for arbitrary boundary pieces is not rigorously proved.
2. The construction and support equality for \(\mathcal B_U\) are under-justified.
3. The boundary-corner intersection lemma relies on an unproved rectangular-support principle.
4. The use of CDHJKN fusion rules in the support argument is not detailed enough to handle arbitrary bounded vectors/direct sums/off-diagonal coefficients.
5. The relative Bass–Serre proposition is an essential new lemma but is proved only by sketches of reduced-word and orthogonality arguments.
6. Therefore the palindromic criterion cannot be applied to the graph-product boundary pieces as claimed.

The theorem may well be true, and parts of the strategy look promising, but `answer.tex` does not yet constitute a complete rigorous proof.
