# Round 1 compute report: Bass--Serre permanence gap

## Verdict

I did **not** find an exact published/preprint theorem proving the needed permanence statement
\[
  P\text{ properly proximal and not amenable relative to }B
  \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
  P*_B(B\bar\otimes A)\text{ properly proximal}
\]
for arbitrary separable tracial \(B\subset P\) with expectation and arbitrary tracial \(A\) containing a trace-zero unitary.

The closest available results are genuine near misses: they upgrade relative proper proximality to full proper proximality only under additional **mixing**, **bounded Pimsner--Popa basis**, **biexactness/weak exactness**, or **injective amalgam** assumptions. These hypotheses do not hold in the graph-product induction in general, especially because \(B\subset B\bar\otimes A\) is not mixing when \(B\) is diffuse and \(A\ne \mathbb C\).

## Literature checked

### 1. DKEP, *Properly proximal von Neumann algebras*, Duke Math. J. 172 (2023), 2821--2894; arXiv:2204.00517v3

Relevant items:

- Theorem 6.2: equivalent formulations of proper proximality relative to a boundary piece \(X\), via central states on \(S_X(M)\), central projections, and u.c.p. maps into basic constructions.
- Proposition 6.7: if \(M_1,M_2\) are **diffuse** tracial von Neumann algebras, then \(M_1*M_2\) is properly proximal.
- Theorem 6.11: if \(Q\subset M\) is regular and \(M\) is properly proximal relative to \(X_Q\), then weakly compact regular \(P\subset M\) must intertwine into \(Q\).
- Proposition 9.1 and Section 9: malleable-deformation criteria give proper proximality **relative to a boundary piece** when the deformation is compact relative to that boundary.

This paper does not contain the desired AFP permanence theorem. Proposition 6.7 is non-amalgamated and requires both factors diffuse, so it does not cover finite-dimensional \(A\), e.g. \(A=\mathbb C^2\), nor arbitrary \(B\).

### 2. Ding--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli, *Structure of relatively bi-exact group von Neumann algebras*, Comm. Math. Phys. 405 (2024), Paper 104; arXiv:2211.05298v3

Main near miss: Theorem 1.1.

Hypotheses, in the form relevant here:

- \(M\) diffuse finite.
- \(M_i\subset M\), \(i=1,\dots,n\), are diffuse subalgebras.
- The Jones projections \(e_{M_i}\) pairwise commute.
- Each \(M_i\subset M\) admits a bounded Pimsner--Popa basis.
- \(A\subset pMp\) is properly proximal relative to the boundary piece \(X\) generated by the \(M_i\)'s.
- Each \(M_i\subset M\) is mixing.

Conclusion: central projections split \(A\) into a properly proximal summand and summands amenable relative to the \(M_i\)'s.

Why it does not apply: the target boundary is the Bass--Serre boundary \(X_B\), and the relevant subalgebra \(B\) is not generally diffuse/mixing with bounded Pimsner--Popa basis. If one tries \(M_i=P\) and \(M_i=B\bar\otimes A\), the mixing assumptions also fail in general for AFPs over diffuse \(B\). This theorem is an upgrade theorem, but not under the hypotheses of Proposition BS.

### 3. Ding--Peterson, *Biexact von Neumann algebras*, arXiv:2309.10161

Relevant items:

- Definition 6.1: biexactness relative to a boundary piece.
- Corollary 6.11: if \(M\) is biexact, every subalgebra is either properly proximal or has a nonzero amenable summand.
- Proposition 6.13: if \(M\) is biexact relative to a family \(\{B_j\}\), then for finite \(N\subset M\), either \(N'\cap M\) is amenable or \(N\prec_M B_j\) for some \(j\).
- Proposition 6.16: non-amalgamated free products of weakly exact von Neumann algebras are biexact relative to the two factors; if the factors are biexact, the free product is biexact.

This does not prove the needed permanence: it gives no arbitrary-amalgam AFP theorem, and relative biexactness alone gives solidity-type conclusions, not full proper proximality without an additional upgrade theorem.

### 4. Toyosawa--Yang, *On Relative Biexactness of Amalgamated Free Product von Neumann Algebras*, arXiv:2505.19508v2

This is the most relevant later preprint I found.

Relevant results:

- Theorem 1.1: for AFPs \(M_1*_B M_2\) with **injective** common \(B\), the embedding into \(\langle M_1,e_B\rangle *_B \langle M_2,e_B\rangle\) is relatively nuclear.
- Corollary 1.2 / Proposition 4.15: if \(M_i\) are weakly exact tracial and \(B\) is injective, then \(M_1*_B M_2\) is biexact relative to \(\{M_1,M_2\}\). Proposition 4.15 includes finite-dimensional \(B\) as a case.
- Theorem 1.3 / 4.17: if the \(M_i\) are injective, then \(M_1*_B M_2\) is biexact relative to \(B\).
- Theorem 1.4 / 5.16: if \(M\) is biexact relative to \(X_F\) for a finite family \(F=\{N_i\}\) of **mixing** subalgebras, then any \(A\subset pMp\) splits into properly proximal, amenable, and relatively amenable pieces.
- Lemma 5.5 and Corollary 5.7: in an AFP, \(M_1\subset M_1*_B M_2\) is mixing if \(B\subset M_2\) is mixing; all factors and \(B\) are mixing if \(B\) is mixing in every \(M_i\).

Why it does not apply: in our target \(M_2=B\bar\otimes A\), the inclusion \(B\subset B\bar\otimes A\) is not mixing when \(B\) is diffuse and \(A\ne \mathbb C\). Indeed, for \(0\ne a\in A\ominus\mathbb C\) and weakly-null unitaries \(u_n\in B\),
\[
  E_B((1\otimes a)u_n(1\otimes a^*))=\tau_A(aa^*)u_n,
\]
which does not converge to \(0\) in \(L^2(B)\). Thus the Toyosawa--Yang upgrade theorem does not cover arbitrary \(B\), and its relative-biexactness results require injective \(B\) and weak exactness/injectivity assumptions absent from the graph-product induction.

### 5. Ding--Kunnawalkam Elayavalli, *Proper proximality among various families of groups*, Groups Geom. Dyn. 18 (2024), 921--938; arXiv:2107.02917

The group Bass--Serre results are also not an exact von Neumann algebra theorem. Theorem 1.2 proves proper proximality for groups acting non-elementarily on trees when intersections of pairs of edge stabilizers are finite. Corollaries include amalgamated free products/HNN extensions under weak malnormality/index hypotheses. These are stronger than mere non-coamenability of \(H<G\), the group analogue of \(L(G)\) not amenable relative to \(L(H)\).

## What can be proved cleanly: relative Bass--Serre proper proximality

The graph-specific extra unitaries do prove the expected **relative** statement.

**Lemma.** Let \(M=P*_B Q\), \(Q=B\bar\otimes A\), with trace-preserving expectations. Suppose \(A\) has a trace-zero unitary \(u\), and \(P\) contains two unitaries \(w_1,w_2\in P\ominus B\) such that \(E_B(w_i^*w_j)=0\) for \(i\ne j\). Let \(X_B\) be the DKEP boundary piece generated by \(e_B\). Then \(M\) is properly proximal relative to \(X_B\).

Proof outline. Decompose \(L^2(M)\) as the AFP Fock space over \(B\). Let \(p_P,p_Q\) be the projections onto reduced words whose first letter is respectively from \(P\ominus B\) and \(Q\ominus B\). The usual Bass--Serre commutator computation gives
\[
  p_P,p_Q\in S_{X_B}(M),
\]
because right multiplication can change the first letter only through cancellations detected by \(e_B\). If \(\varphi\) is an \(M\)-central state on \(S_{X_B}(M)\), then left multiplication gives
\[
  w_1p_Qw_1^*+w_2p_Qw_2^*\le p_P,\qquad
  up_Pu^*\le p_Q.
\]
The first inequality uses the \(B\)-orthogonality of \(w_1,w_2\); the second uses \(u\in Q\ominus B\). Centrality gives
\[
  2\varphi(p_Q)\le \varphi(p_P)\le \varphi(p_Q),
\]
so \(\varphi(p_P)=\varphi(p_Q)=0\). The remaining projection is \(e_B=1-p_P-p_Q\). The unitary \(h=w_1u\) is \(B\)-Haar in the sense that \(h^nL^2(B)\), \(n\in\mathbb Z\), are pairwise orthogonal \(B\)-submodules; hence the projections \(h^n e_B h^{-n}\) are pairwise orthogonal. Centrality gives \(\varphi(e_B)=0\), contradiction.

This is exactly the Bass--Serre paradoxical argument, but only in \(S_{X_B}(M)\), not in \(S(M)\).

## Where the full proof still breaks

For arbitrary \(B\), the half-tree projections \(p_P,p_Q\) need not lie in \(S(M)\). The obstruction is concrete: for \(x\in P\ominus B\), right multiplication by \(x\) on a reduced word ending in \(P\ominus B\) can replace the last letter by a \(B\)-coefficient \(E_B(\cdot x)\), shortening the word and changing the first letter. This gives an operator compact relative to \(e_B\), but not compact in the scalar \(K_{\infty,1}(M)\)-sense when \(B\) is infinite-dimensional.

Thus a central state on \(S(M)\) cannot currently be evaluated on \(p_P,p_Q\). The missing upgrade would have to show that non-relative amenability of \(P\) over \(B\), plus proper proximality of \(P\), converts this relative contradiction into an ordinary one. The existing DKE/DKEP/Toyosawa--Yang upgrade mechanisms require mixing or bounded-basis hypotheses that are unavailable here.

## A valid replacement statement: finite-dimensional amalgam

The above proof does give an ordinary proper proximality result when \(B\) is finite-dimensional.

**Proposition.** If \(B\) is finite-dimensional and the graph-specific hypotheses above hold, then
\[
  P*_B(B\bar\otimes A)
\]
is properly proximal.

Reason: when \(B\) is finite-dimensional, \(e_B\) is finite-rank and \(X_B\subset K(L^2M)\). Hence \(p_P,p_Q\in S(M)\), and the relative Bass--Serre paradoxical argument above is an ordinary DKEP central-state contradiction.

This covers finite-dimensional amalgams and finite-dimensional \(A\), but it does not close the induction in `answer.tex`, where \(B=M_L\) can be infinite-dimensional.

## Counterexample search

I did not find a concrete counterexample to Proposition BS itself. The obvious tensor-product attempt is excluded by the hypothesis that \(P\) is properly proximal: if \(P=N\bar\otimes R\) with \(R\) diffuse amenable and \(B=R\), then
\[
  P*_B(B\bar\otimes A)\cong R\bar\otimes(N*A),
\]
which is not properly proximal when \(R\) is diffuse amenable, but \(P\) is already not properly proximal by DKEP's tensor/product and property-Gamma obstructions.

What is false is several stronger routes one might hope to use:

- automatic mixing of the amalgam \(B\) in \(B\bar\otimes A\) is false for diffuse \(B\);
- automatic relative-biexactness of AFPs over arbitrary \(B\) is not known and Toyosawa--Yang require injective \(B\);
- relative biexactness does not automatically upgrade to actual biexactness/proper proximality without mixing-type hypotheses.
