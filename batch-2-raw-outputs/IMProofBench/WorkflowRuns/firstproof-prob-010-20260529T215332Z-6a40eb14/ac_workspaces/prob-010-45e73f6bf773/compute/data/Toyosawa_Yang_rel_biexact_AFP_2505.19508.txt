                                                 ON RELATIVE BIEXACTNESS OF AMALGAMATED FREE PRODUCT VON
                                                                     NEUMANN ALGEBRAS

                                                                                 KAI TOYOSAWA1 AND ZHIYUAN YANG2




                                                  Abstract. Given weakly exact tracial von Neumann algebras M1 , M2 with a common injective amalgam B,
                                                  we prove that the amalgamated free product M1 ∗B M2 is biexact relative to {M1 , M2 }. In the case where M1
                                                  and M2 are injective, we further show that M1 ∗B M2 is biexact relative to the amalgam B, and if B is mixing
                                                  in each of M1 and M2 , M1 ∗B M2 itself is biexact. As applications, we derive structural decomposition results
                                                  and subalgebra absorption theorems for amalgamated free product von Neumann algebras, extending those
                                                  previously known in the group case.

                                                                                             1. Introduction
                                              In [Oza06], Ozawa introduced a class of exact groups, which nowadays is referred to as groups of class
                                           S or biexact groups in [BO08], and proved that the group von Neumann algebra LΓ of a biexact group
                                           Γ is solid, that is, for any diffuse von Neumann subalgebra A in LΓ, the relative commutant A′ ∩ LΓ is
                                           injective [Oza04]. As a consequence, one recovers the result from [Ge98] proved via different techniques from
                                           Voiculescu’s free probability theory that every free group factor LFn is prime, i.e., if one decomposes LFn
                                           into tensor product of two von Neumann algebras, then one of which is finite dimensional.
                                              In [BO08], a relative version of biexact groups was introduced: given a countable group Γ and a collection
                                           G of subgroups of Γ, let c0 (Γ; G) denote the set of bounded functions f on Γ such that for every ε > 0, the set
                                           {x ∈ Γ : |f (x)| > ε} is contained in a finite union of sΛt’s, where s, t ∈ Γ and Λ ∈ G. The small-at-infinity
                                           boundary of Γ relative to G is defined to be
                                                                           S(Γ; G) = {f ∈ ℓ∞ Γ : f − Rt f ∈ c0 (Γ; G), ∀t ∈ Γ},
                                           where Rt denotes the right translation action of t ∈ Γ on ℓ∞ Γ. The countable group Γ is called biexact
                                           relative to G if Γ is exact and the left translation action of Γ on S(Γ; G) is amenable. In particular, the group
                                           Γ is biexact if and only if it is biexact relative to G = {{1}}.
                                              Relative biexactness is in general a weaker condition on groups than the genuine biexactness, but this
                                           property is often enough to conclude rigidity results in group von Neumann algebras. For example, it is
                                           shown in [BO08, Proposition 15.3.12] that if Γ1 , Γ2 are countable exact groups with a common amenable
                                           subgroup Λ, then the amalgamated free product Γ = Γ1 ∗Λ Γ2 is biexact relative to G = {Γ1 , Γ2 }, al-
                                           though the amalgamated free product group Γ is not biexact in general. Combining with Popa’s powerful
                                           intertwining-by-bimodule theorems [Pop06a], Ozawa proved a Kurosh-type theorem for free products of
                                           group von Neumann algebras associated with nonamenable exact groups [Oza06].
                                              Ding and Peterson extended the notion of relative biexactness to the setting of von Neumann algebras
                                           in [DP23]. Given a von Neumann algebra M and a collection B of von Neumann subalgebras of M with
                                           normal conditional expectations, Ding, Kunnawalkam Elayavalli, and Peterson introduced in [DEP23] the
                                           small-at-infinity boundary of M relative to B, which is an operator subsystem of B(L2 M ) that contains M .
                                           They defined M to be biexact relative to B if the inclusion M into the small-at-infinity boundary is M -
                                           nuclear, meaning the inclusion map can be pointwise approximated in a locally convex topology depending
                                           M by contractive completely positive (c.c.p.) maps that factor through finite-dimensional matrix algebras.
                                              This generalizes relative biexactness in the group case, as Ding and Peterson showed in [DP23, Theorem
                                           6.2] that the countable discrete group Γ is biexact relative to a collection G of subgroups of Γ if and only if
                                           the group von Neumann algebra LΓ is biexact relative to {LΛ : Λ ∈ G}.
                                              In particular, a von Neumann algebra M is called biexact M is biexact relative to the scalars B = {C1}.
                                           As noted above, this notion recovers the classical definition: LΓ is biexact in the sense of [DP23] if and only
                                           if Γ is biexact in the sense of [BO08]. However, Ding and Peterson also observed that many examples of
                                           biexact von Neumann algebras do not arise from groups, including the von Neumann algebras of the dual
                                           of free orthogonal and unitary quantum groups [Iso15], free Araki-Woods von Neumann algebras [Hou07],
                                           and q-Gaussian von Neumann algebras associated to finite-dimensional real Hilbert space [Shl04, Kuz23].

   In [DP23, Theorem 5.10], Ding and Peterson proved that when M1 ⊂ B(H1 ), M2 ⊂ B(H2 ) are weakly ex-
act σ-finite von Neumann algebras with fixed vector states ωξi , the canonical embedding of the free product
von Neumann algebra M = M1 ∗M2 into the free product (B(H1 ), ωξ1 )∗(B(H2 ), ωξ2 ) is M -nuclear. Further-
more, [DP23, Proposition 6.16] shows that (B(H1 ), ωξ1 )∗(B(H2 ), ωξ2 ) lies in small-at-infinity boundary of M
relative to M1 , M2 . These results together imply that M is biexact relative to {M1 , M2 }, thereby allowing
a recovery of Kurosh type theorems for free products of weakly exact von Neumann algebras that was first
proved in [HU16].
   Our first main result adapts [DP23, Theorem 5.10] to the the setting of amalgamated free product von
Neumann algebras with injective amalgam. In the following, we abbreviate “amalgamated free product” as
AFP. Note also that the theorems and corollaries below are proved for AFPs of arbitrarily finitely many
von Neumann algebras, but for simplicity, we state them only in the case of two.

basic construction associated with Ei , and the (not necessarily faithful) normal conditional expectation from
⟨Mi , eB ⟩ onto B is taken to be Ei (x) := i∗B xiB for x ∈ ⟨Mi , eB ⟩, where iB : L2 B → L2 Mi is the canonical
inclusion.
  Let Mr = M1 ∗B M2 be the reduced AFP C ∗ -algebra and M = M1 ∗B M2 be the AFP von Neumann algebra.
Then the inclusion

   Here (Mr ⊂ M )-nuclearity is a relative version of M -nuclearity; see Section 3.2 for precise definition.
   Our approach to Theorem 1.1 is different from that of [DP23, Theorem 5.10], in which Ding and Peterson
showed the nuclearity of the embedding map of the free product von Neumann algebra M1 ∗M2 by con-
structing free products of the unital completely positive (u.c.p.) maps that factor through finite-dimensional
matrix algebras arising from the weak exactness of M1 , M2 . However, this strategy does not extend to the
setting of AFP von Neumann algebras, where taking free products of u.c.p. maps is no longer feasible due to
the lack of B-linearity, so instead we adopt an approach inspired by [Toy25]. We show the embedding map
into ⟨M1 , eB ⟩∗B ⟨M2 , eB ⟩ factors through a Toeplitz-Pimsner algebra, and we apply its universal property to
deduce the nuclearity of the embedding map. An advantage of our method is that it does not require any
assumption on the states of Mi , and in particular, it remains valid when M1 , M2 are not σ-finite.
   When M1 , M2 are tracial, we show that ⟨M1 , eB ⟩∗B ⟨M2 , eB ⟩ lies in the small-at-infinity boundary relative
to {M1 , M2 }. Combining with Theorem 1.1, we can generalize the results in [BO08, Proposition 15.3.12] and
[DP23, Proposition 6.16] and prove the relative biexactness for AFPs of weakly exact tracial von Neumann
algebras over injective amalgams.
Corollary 1.2. If M1 , M2 are weakly exact tracial von Neumann algebras admitting a common injective
unital von Neumann subalgebra B with trace preserving conditional -expectations, then the amalgamated free
product M1 ∗B M2 is biexact relative to {M1 , M2 }.
   As in the group case, we immediately obtain from the above corollary and [DP23, Proposition 6.13] a
relative solidity result for von Neumann subalgebras N in M1 ∗B M2 , which states that either the relative
commutant N ′ ∩ M1 ∗B M2 is amenable, or N intertwines into one of the Mi ’s (in the sense of Popa, see
e.g., [Pop06a][AP17]). However, we do not pursue this direction further, as a more general result is already
proved in [HU16, Theorem 4.4] without the tracial or weakly exact assumptions.
   We note that the injectivity assumption on the amalgam B is essential, as counterexamples exist even for
the group case when the amalgam is not an amenable group (see e.g., the remark after [BO08, Proposition
15.3.12]). Also, in general we cannot improve the relative biexactness to actual biexactness. For example,
if we let R be the separable hyperfinite II1 factor and consider L(Z) ⊗ R with the canonical conditional
expectation onto C1 ⊗ R, then (L(Z) ⊗ R)∗C1⊗R (L(Z) ⊗ R) = L(F2 ) ⊗ R, which is not prime and therefore
not solid. In particular, it is not biexact.
   When M1 , M2 are injective, we can remove the tracial assumption in Corollary 1.2 and improve the
relative biexactness.
Theorem 1.3. If M1 , M2 are injective von Neumann algebras admitting a common unital von Neumann
subalgebra B with faithful normal conditional expectations, then the AFP von Neumann algebra M1 ∗B M2 is
biexact relative to B.

B with expectation. If B is mixing in both of M1 , M2 , then the AFP von Neumann algebra M = M1 ∗B M2
is biexact.

   In this paper, however, we focus on constructing AFP von Neumann algebras through the framework of
amalgamated free products of W ∗ -correspondences (i.e., Hilbert spaces with normal left and right actions),
which is equivalent to using self-dual right Hilbert B-modules (see, for example, [AD95]). The reason why
we choose to stick to W ∗ -correspondence is that, given a von Neumann subalgebra B ⊂ M equipped with
a faithful normal conditional expectation, the category of right Hilbert B-modules over B ⊂ M does not
admit the natural right action of M as morphisms, since such actions are not right B-linear. However, in
the context of analyzing biexactness, it is essential to study both left and right actions of a von Neumann
algebra. Therefore, we begin by reviewing the construction of the standard representation of the AFP von
Neumann algebra, which involves the use of relative tensor products of W ∗ -correspondence. We note that
our construction of AFP von Neumann algebras agrees with that of [Pop93] in the case when the involved
von Neumann algebras are tracial, and with [Ued99] when the von Neumann algebras are σ-finite.

             3. A C∗ -bimodule characterization of weak exactness and biexactness
   In this prelimnary section we collect some basic facts of a locally convex topology relative to von Neumann
algebras on C∗ -bimodules introduced by Magajna in [Mag98] and [Mag00], and later adapted and generalized

[DP23, Theorem 5.1] and biexact von Neumann algebras [DP23, Definition 6.1]. We refer the reader to
[Mag00], [DEP23], and [DP23] for the details on this topology.
3.1. C∗ -bimodule topologies associated with von Neumann algebras. Throughout this section, we
assume that M is a von Neumann algebra and A is a unital, ultraweakly dense C∗ -subalgebra of M . A

concrete realization can be made so that π extends to a normal representation of M . If X is moreover a
unital C∗ -algebra, we will say that it is an operator A-C∗ -algebra or an (A ⊂ M )-normal A-C∗ -algebra if X
is so as an operator system. We will often drop π in the notation.

3.2. Weakly exact von Neumann algebra. We now recall a definition of weak exactness of ultraweakly
dense C∗ -algebras, [Iso13, Definition 3.1.1], which generalizes the original definition of weakly exact von
Neumann algebra in [Kir95]. If M is a von Neumann algebra and A ⊂ M is a (unital) ultraweakly dense
C∗ -algebra, then we say A is weakly exact in M if for any unital C∗ -algebra B with a closed two-sided ideal
J and any representation π : A ⊗ B → B(K) with A ⊗ J ⊂ ker π such that π|A⊗C extends to an ultraweakly
continuous representation of M , the induced representation π  e : A ⊙ B/J → B(K) is min-continuous.
   In particular, when A = M , M is weakly exact in M if and only if M is weakly exact in the sense of
Kirchberg [Kir95]. Also, if M = A∗∗ , then A is weakly exact in A∗∗ if and only if A is an exact C∗ -algebra.
   Recall that if E is an operator system or a C∗ -algebra and if F is an operator A-system or A-C∗ -algebra
for a (unital) ultraweakly dense C∗ -subalgebra A of a von Neumann algebra M , then as defined in [DP23,
Section 4.1], we say a c.c.p. map ϕ : E → F is (A ⊂ M )-nuclear (or just M -nuclear when A = M ) if there
exist nets of c.c.p. maps ϕi : E → Mn(i) (C) and ψi : Mn(i) (C) → F such that ψi ◦ ϕi (x) converges to ϕ(x) in
the (A ⊂ M )-topology for every x ∈ E.

Neumann algebra and A ⊂ M is a (unital) ultraweakly dense C∗ -algebra, then A is weakly exact in M if
and only if the inclusion map A ⊂ B(H) is (A ⊂ M )-nuclear.

Theorem 3.9 ([Pop06b, Theorem 2.1], [HI17, Theorem 4.3]). Let M be a von Neumann algebra, B ⊂ M a
von Neumann subalgebra with expectation, and N ⊂ M a finite von Neumann subalgebra with expectation.
The following are equivalent:
    (1) There exist projections e ∈ P(N ), f ∈ P(B), a nonzero partial isometry v ∈ eM f , and a unital
        normal ∗-homomorphism ϕ : eN e → f Bf such that the inclusion ϕ(eN e) ⊂ f Bf is with expectation
        and xv = vϕ(x) for every x ∈ N .
    (2) There does not exist a net of unitaries (uj ) ⊂ U(N ) such that EB (aui b) → 0 in the ultrastrong-∗
        topology for all a, b ∈ M .
If one of the above equivalent statements holds, we say N embeds with expectation into B inside M and
write N ⪯M B.
