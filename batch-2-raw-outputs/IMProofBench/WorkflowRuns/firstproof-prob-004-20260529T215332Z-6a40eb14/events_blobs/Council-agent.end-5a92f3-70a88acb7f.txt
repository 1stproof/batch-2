The null-sheet obstruction is correctly identified — a literal pointwise source homotopy of $Z_y$ pays ordinary mass for null sheets that contribute nothing to $E_y$. Three directions, with honest assessment:

**(1) Essential filling reformulation.** Define
$$\Fill^{\text{ess}}_R(Z_y) := \inf\{\Mass B : B \in I_3(R),\ f_\#\partial B = \pm P_y \in I_2(S, \partial S)\}.$$
Lower bound $\ge cS_1S_2S_3$ holds: $\Mass B \ge \int_B f^*\omega_S = \int_{f_\#\partial B}\alpha_S = \int_{P_y}\alpha_S$, using $\|f^*\omega_S\|_{\text{co}} \le 1$ from $\Dil_3 f \le 1$ (which follows from $\Dil_2\le 1$). This norm algebraically discounts null sheets in $Z_y$ — only $f_\#\partial B$ matters — and an (AT)-analog implies the theorem the same way. The upper-bound construction has more freedom (any $B$ with correct $f_\#$-boundary works); the naive $B = [R] \llcorner f^*\gamma$ gives $\Mass B \sim \Vol(R)$, so this alone doesn't close (AT), but a cellular construction in target then transported back might.

**(2) Re-derive Guth's Estimate 1 tightening with explicit chain bookkeeping.** The proof of Estimate 1 in arXiv:0710.0403 (case $n=4, k=2, j=1, l=4$) does a 1-parameter cellular tightening that implicitly proves a 1-parameter (AT).
