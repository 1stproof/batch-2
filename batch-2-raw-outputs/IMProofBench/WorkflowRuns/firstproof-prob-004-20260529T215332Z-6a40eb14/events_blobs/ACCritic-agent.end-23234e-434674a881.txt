### Literature validation

The use of Guth’s *Area-expanding embeddings of rectangles* is appropriate. Guth’s Estimate 1 is stated for maps of pairs \((U,\partial U)\to(S,\partial S)\) of positive degree and gives the lower bound on \(k\)-dilation in terms of the quotients \(Q_i=S_i/R_i\); Guth also explicitly notes this estimate is more general than the embedding case because the map need not be a diffeomorphism. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/0710.0403))

The small-volume relative isoperimetric input is also supported by Guth’s Theorem 3, which estimates the relative isoperimetric profile of a rectangle for \(V\le c(n)R_1\cdots R_k\) and gives a general linear upper bound. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/0710.0403)) The draft’s \(k=2\) consequence
\[
\Fill_R(Z)\lesssim \max\{A^{3/2},A^2/R_1\}
\quad\text{for }A\lesssim R_1R_2
\]
is a reasonable specialization.

### Paragraph-by-paragraph audit

#### Problem statement and interpretation

The interpretation is faithful: rectangles are oriented Euclidean products, and degree means relative degree in \(H_4(\cdot,\partial\cdot;\mathbb Z)\).

The smoothing/approximation paragraph is still schematic. A complete proof would need a precise approximation theorem preserving the pair condition, relative degree, and \(\Dil_2\le1+\varepsilon\). However, because the draft openly remains incomplete for a much larger reason, this is secondary.

#### Standard inputs

The singular-value argument proving
\[
\Dil_2(f)\le1\implies \Dil_3(f)\le1,\quad \Dil_4(f)\le1
\]
is correct.

The specialization of Guth’s Estimate 1 to \(n=4,k=2,j=1,l=4\) gives
\[
R_1^2\Vol(R)\gtrsim S_1^3S_2S_3S_4,
\]
which matches the draft.

The inverse isoperimetric consequence is also correct: if \(\Fill_R(Z)\ge L\), then either \(\Mass Z\gtrsim R_1R_2\) or the small-volume profile forces
\[
\Mass Z\gtrsim L^{2/3}
\quad\text{or}\quad
\Mass Z\gtrsim (R_1L)^{1/2}.
\]

#### Lemma: filling a central target plane

The calibration argument is sound. The form
\[
d(\psi\,dx_1\wedge dx_2)
\]
has bounded comass, and the boundary terms vanish because \(dx_1\wedge dx_2\) restricts to zero on \(x_1,x_2\)-faces and \(\psi=0\) on the \(x_3,x_4\)-faces. This proves the claimed lower bound
\[
\Fill_S(P_y)\gtrsim S_1S_2S_3.
\]

#### Proposition: first alternative

The proof is now essentially correct. The constancy-theorem argument and slicing naturality justify
\[
f_\#\langle [[R]],F,y\rangle=\pm P_y
\]
for a.e. \(y\in Q\). Then any filling of \(Z_y\) pushes forward to a filling of \(P_y\), and \(\Dil_3(f)\le1\) controls its mass. The small-volume isoperimetric estimate then forces \(\Mass Z_y\gtrsim R_1R_2\), and coarea gives
\[
R_3R_4\gtrsim S_3S_4.
\]

This is a genuine proved partial result.

#### Best bound from single slices

The algebra is correct. Under failure of the first alternative and \(R_1=\alpha S_1\le S_1\), the draft obtains
\[
\Vol(R)\gtrsim \alpha^{1/2}S_1S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4
\]
and Guth’s estimate gives
\[
\Vol(R)\gtrsim \alpha^{-2}S_1S_2S_3S_4.
\]
Optimizing over \(\alpha\) gives
\[
\Vol(R)\gtrsim S_1S_2^{3/5}S_3^{7/5}S_4.
\]
This is valid but strictly weaker than the required second alternative by the stated factor \((S_2/S_3)^{1/10}\).

Minor wording issue: “unconditional partial bound” should be read as unconditional only within the complementary regime being discussed, not as a proof of the original theorem.

#### Sufficient averaged tightening estimate

The weighted coarea estimate
\[
I\le C\Vol(R)
\]
is plausible and essentially correct. At rank-two points of \(dF\), the argument
\[
\|dG|_K\|_{HS}^2J_2F\le C
\]
follows from \(\Dil_2(f)\le1\). The rank-deficient set should be handled explicitly in a final proof, but this is a minor technical omission.

The conditional implication
\[
(\ref{sufficient})\implies \text{desired second alternative}
\]
is algebraically correct.

However, \((\ref{sufficient})\) is explicitly unproved. This is the central fatal gap.

#### Low-energy plaque estimate

The plaque Fubini estimate is plausible: each coordinate \(2\)-plaque has \(F\)-image area at most its source area, and every coordinate \(2\)-plaque area is \(\le R_3R_4\). Therefore the average bad-mass term should be bounded by
\[
C\frac{R_3R_4}{S_3S_4}\Mass(B_y).
\]

But the actual low-energy plaque inequality \((\ref{lehaf})\) is unproved. Moreover, the draft correctly notes that sharp cutting by \({\mathcal B}_z\) creates seam terms, so even this route would need a serious chain-map deformation or new algebraic invariant. This is not a minor technicality; it is exactly the missing proof.

### New or remaining issues

- The proof still does not solve the stated problem.
- The approximation paragraph remains non-rigorous, though secondary.
- The measurable choice of near-minimizing fillings \(B_y\) is not supplied.
- The low-energy plaque route is only a proposal. It is not an established lemma and cannot be used as a black box.
- No located published result is cited that supplies the missing averaged tightening estimate.

### Final assessment

The revision improves the rigor of the proved partial results and fixes several earlier technical objections. The first-alternative proof and weaker volume estimate are credible. But the desired theorem is not proved, because the essential averaged tightening estimate remains open in the draft.
