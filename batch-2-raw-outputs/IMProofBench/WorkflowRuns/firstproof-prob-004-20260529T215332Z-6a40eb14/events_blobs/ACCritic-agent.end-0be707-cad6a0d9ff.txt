## Section-by-section mathematical audit

### 1. Problem statement and interpretation

The adopted interpretation is appropriate: rectangles are oriented Euclidean products, and degree one is relative degree one on
\[
H_4(R,\partial R;\mathbb Z)\to H_4(S,\partial S;\mathbb Z).
\]

The smoothing paragraph is improved compared with the previous draft: it explains scaling the target by \((1+\varepsilon)^{-1/2}\) after approximating a piecewise smooth map by a smooth map with \(\Dil_2\le1+\varepsilon\). This is a plausible standard reduction, but it is still only sketched. In a complete proof one would need to justify the approximation relative to boundary strata, preservation of degree, and the constant bookkeeping. This is a minor issue compared with the unproved `(QEAT)`.

Most importantly, this section explicitly says the document is “a conditional reduction, not a complete proof.” That alone prevents answer-readiness.

---

### 2. Standard inputs

The singular-value argument
\[
\Dil_2(f)\le1\implies \Dil_3(f),\Dil_4(f)\le1
\]
is correct.

The specialization of Guth’s Estimate 1 to
\[
R_1^2\Vol(R)\ge c_0S_1^3S_2S_3S_4
\]
is correct. Starting from
\[
\Dil_2(f)\ge c\,Q_1(Q_2Q_3Q_4)^{1/3}
\]
with \(\Dil_2(f)\le1\) gives
\[
R_1^2R_2R_3R_4\gtrsim S_1^3S_2S_3S_4.
\]

The small-volume relative isoperimetric estimate is now stated with the needed smallness hypothesis
\[
A=\Mass Z\le c_*R_1R_2.
\]
This fixes an earlier vulnerability. The contrapositive form
\[
\Fill_R(Z)\ge L
\Longrightarrow
\Mass Z\ge c\min\{R_1R_2,L^{2/3},(R_1L)^{1/2}\}
\]
is valid after adjusting constants.

### 3. Central target plane lemma

The calibration argument is sound. A \(1\)-Lipschitz function \(\psi\) on the \((x_3,x_4)\)-rectangle, vanishing on the boundary and bounded below by \(cS_3\) on the central \(Q\), gives
\[
\beta=\psi\,dx_1\wedge dx_2,\qquad \|d\beta\|_{\mathrm{comass}}\le1.
\]
Stokes’ theorem then gives
\[
\Mass Y\ge\left|\int_Yd\beta\right|
=\left|\int_{P_y}\beta\right|
\gtrsim S_1S_2S_3.
\]
The explanation of vanishing relative boundary terms is correct.

Minor detail: the proof should explicitly say whether it is working with integral currents, normal currents, or Lipschitz chains, but the argument is standard and valid in any of these usual categories.

---

### 4. Proposition: first alternative

The proof of the first alternative is essentially correct.

The current-theoretic slicing step
\[
f_\#\langle [[R]],F,y\rangle
=
\langle [[S]],(x_3,x_4),y\rangle
=\pm P_y
\]
is justified for a.e. regular \(y\), assuming the usual slicing and naturality hypotheses. The invocation of the constancy theorem is appropriate: relative degree one forces \(f_\#[[R]]\) to be \([[S]]\) as a relative \(4\)-current.

The filling-volume argument is correct:

- Any relative filling of \(Z_y\) in \(R\) pushes forward to a relative filling of \(P_y\) in \(S\).
- Since \(\Dil_3(f)\le1\), the pushed-forward filling has no larger mass.
- The central-plane calibration gives
  \[
  \Fill_R(Z_y)\gtrsim S_1S_2S_3.
  \]
- If \(R_1R_2^2\ll S_1S_2S_3\), the small-volume isoperimetric inequality forces
  \[
  \Mass Z_y\gtrsim R_1R_2.
  \]
- Coarea with \(J_2F\le1\) gives
  \[
  \Vol(R)\ge\int_Q\Mass Z_y\,dy\gtrsim R_1R_2S_3S_4,
  \]
  hence
  \[
  R_3R_4\gtrsim S_3S_4.
  \]

This part is one of the genuinely proved portions of the draft.

### 5. Unconditional scalar hard-case bound

The scalar hard-case derivation is correct.

Under failure of the first alternative and \(R_1=\alpha S_1\le S_1\), the proposition gives
\[
R_1R_2^2\gtrsim S_1S_2S_3.
\]
Combining this with the inverse isoperimetric profile yields
\[
\Mass Z_y\gtrsim
\alpha^{1/2}S_1(S_2S_3)^{1/2}.
\]
After coarea,
\[
\Vol(R)\gtrsim
\alpha^{1/2}S_1S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4.
\]
Guth’s monomial estimate gives
\[
\Vol(R)\gtrsim
\alpha^{-2}S_1S_2S_3S_4.
\]
Optimizing these two lower bounds gives
\[
\Vol(R)\gtrsim S_1S_2^{3/5}S_3^{7/5}S_4.
\]
The calculation is correct and explains why the scalar method misses the desired hard-case scale by \((S_2/S_3)^{1/10}\).

This is a valid partial result, but not the theorem.

---

### 6. Weighted coarea inequality

The pointwise linear algebra proving
\[
\|dG|_{\ker dF}\|_{HS}^2J_2F\le2
\]
is correct. At rank-two points of \(F\), if \(a\ge b\) are the singular values of \(dF|_{K^\perp}\), then for \(w\in K\) and \(v\in K^\perp\) with \(|dF(v)|=a\), the \(G\)-\(F\) mixed component of
\[
df(w)\wedge df(v)
\]
has norm \(|dG(w)|a\). Since \(\Dil_2(f)\le1\), this gives
\[
|dG(w)|a\le1,
\]
and summing over an orthonormal basis of \(K\) gives the displayed bound. Coarea then yields
\[
I\le C\Vol(R).
\]

This part is valid.

---

### 7. Essential filling lower bound

The relaxed essential filling norm
\[
\EFill_f(y)=\inf\{\Mass C:\ f_\#(\partial C)=\pm P_y\}
\]
is reasonable. If \(C\) is admissible, then \(f_\#C\) is a filling of \(P_y\) in \(S\). Since \(\Dil_3(f)\le1\), the calibration lower bound in \(S\) implies
\[
\Mass C\ge cS_1S_2S_3.
\]
Thus
\[
A_\Omega=\int_\Omega\EFill_f(y)\,dy
\gtrsim S_1S_2S_3q
\]
because \(|\Omega|\ge3q/4\). This is correct.

Minor technical issue: nonemptiness of the admissible class is not explicitly proved in this section. It follows by filling the actual slice \(Z_y\) in \(R\), but a complete proof should say this.

---

### 8. Exact-plaque Fubini estimate

The Fubini estimate
\[
\frac1q\int_Q\Mass(B\llcorner{\mathcal B}_z)\,dz
\le C\frac{R_3R_4}{S_3S_4}\Mass B
\]
is plausible and essentially correct.

For fixed \(x\), the set of \(z\in Q\) such that \(x\in{\mathcal B}_z\) is contained in the union of six \(F\)-images of coordinate \(2\)-plaques through \(x\). Each coordinate \(2\)-plaque has area at most \(R_3R_4\), and \(J_2F\le1\), so its image has area at most \(R_3R_4\). Integrating this pointwise probability bound against \(d\|B\|\) gives the estimate.

Technical details omitted but needed in a full proof:

- precise definition of coordinate \(2\)-plaques through boundary points;
- measurability of \({\mathcal B}_z\);
- whether \(\Mass(B\llcorner{\mathcal B}_z)\) is Borel/measurable in \(z\).

These are secondary compared with `(QEAT)`.

---

### 9. The quotient bad-seminorm formulation

The revision’s main new contribution is the definition
\[
\beta_z([C])=\inf_{N\in\ker f_\#}\Mass((C+N)\llcorner{\mathcal B}_z).
\]
This is an improvement over the previous raw bad-mass term because it is invariant under changing representatives by \(f\)-null chains. It directly addresses the earlier two-cell obstruction: a source-local bad-set restriction need not preserve the equivalence relation modulo \(\ker f_\#\).

However, this is only a reformulation. No argument proves the required estimate
\[
\tag{QEAT}
 A_\Omega
 \le C\left(R_1I+\frac{I^2}{S_1q}\right)
 +\frac{C}{q}\int_Q\int_\Omega
       \beta_z([C_y^0])\,dy\,dz .
\]
The proof still stops exactly where the previous proof stopped, only with a more refined missing lemma.

New technical issues introduced by the quotient formulation:

1. **Measurability of \(\beta_z([C_y^0])\)** is not addressed.
2. **Existence of measurable near-minimizers \(C_y^0\)** is asserted but not proved.
3. The quotient seminorm \(\beta_z\) can be much smaller than the bad mass of a fixed representative; this is useful for absorption, but it makes `(QEAT)` a stronger and more delicate statement. No construction or duality argument is supplied.
4. The energy \(E_y\) is still tied to the actual slice \(Z_y\), while \(C_y^0\) is an essential filling representative in a quotient class. The draft no longer ignores this mismatch, but it also does not resolve it.

Thus `(QEAT)` remains an unproved essential lemma.

---

### 10. Absorption algebra after `(QEAT)`

Assuming `(QEAT)`, the final algebra is correct.

Using
\[
\beta_z([C_y^0])\le \Mass(C_y^0\llcorner{\mathcal B}_z)
\]
and the exact-plaque Fubini estimate gives
\[
A_\Omega
\le C\left(R_1I+\frac{I^2}{S_1q}\right)
+C\frac{R_3R_4}{S_3S_4}A_\Omega.
\]
If \(R_3R_4\le\kappa S_3S_4\), the last term is absorbable for small \(\kappa\). If also \(R_1\le\kappa S_1\) and
\[
I\le\delta S_1S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4,
\]
then both remaining terms are \(O((\kappa\delta+\delta^2)S_1S_2S_3^2S_4)\), contradicting
\[
A_\Omega\gtrsim S_1S_2S_3^2S_4.
\]
Hence
\[
I\gtrsim S_1S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4,
\]
and \(I\lesssim\Vol(R)\) gives the desired second alternative.

This part is valid **conditional on `(QEAT)`**.

## Which previous concerns were addressed?

Addressed or improved:

1. **Small-volume hypothesis in the isoperimetric profile** is now explicitly included.
2. **Piecewise smooth approximation** is sketched more concretely.
3. **The source-local/null-chain obstruction** is acknowledged and partially addressed by passing to a quotient bad-seminorm formulation.
4. **The proof no longer presents the missing tightening estimate as proved.** It explicitly marks `(QEAT)` as missing.
5. **The first alternative and scalar partial bound are cleaner and remain credible.**

Still unresolved:

1. `(QEAT)` is unproved and uncited.
2. No proof of the full second alternative is supplied.
3. Measurable selection and measurability of the quotient bad seminorm are not justified.
4. The quotient-current complex is not developed enough to support the claimed “filling norm” interpretation rigorously.
5. The smoothing/approximation reduction remains only a sketch.

New issues introduced:

1. The quotient seminorm \(\beta_z\) may be analytically difficult or nonmeasurable; the draft does not address this.
2. The passage from actual slices \(Z_y\) and their energy \(E_y\) to quotient essential fillings \(C_y^0\) remains conceptually nontrivial.
3. The revised missing estimate `(QEAT)` is at least as strong and nonstandard as the previous missing estimate; no located literature proves it.
