I would not bet on (13c) in its present form. The cubical replacement fixes the seam problem, but I think the flap problem survives even for whole-cell bad sets.

Here is the countermodel I would use to test it. Work inside any hard-regime box, say with \(R_1\ll S_1\) and \(R_3R_4\ll S_3S_4\), and put a tiny tube \(U\) in the interior. Let \(D\subset Q\) be a very small parameter disk. On \(U\), arrange \(F=(f_3,f_4)\) to be a \(1\)-Lipschitz submersion onto \(D\), and make \(G=(f_1,f_2)\) constant. For each \(y\in D\), the slice then contains an extra closed component \(W_y\) with \(G_\#W_y=0\) and
\[
E_y(W_y)=\int_{W_y}\|dG|_{\ker dF}\|^2=0.
\]
Take \(W_y\) to be the boundary of a calibrated thin 3-chain \(V_y\), with \(\Fill(W_y)\sim M\). By taking many disjoint copies or multiplicity, \(M\) can be made large while the tube occupies only volume \(\sim M|D|\). This is a null bubble: it does not affect degree, and it can be grafted into a map without changing the target slice current except by a component whose pushforward is zero.

Now choose \(z\in Q\setminus D\). For a cubulation fine relative to the tube, every prescribed Manhattan coordinate-plaque track in \(U\) has \(F\)-image contained in \(D\). Hence no cell of \(V_y\) is bad for such \(z\):
\[
\Mass(B_{y,\theta}\llcorner \mathcal B^{\rm cell}_{z,\theta})=0
\]
for the natural filling \(B_{y,\theta}\supset V_y\). But any competitor filling the full slice \(Z_y\) must also fill \(W_y\), so it has mass at least \(\Fill(W_y)\sim M\). The low-energy term sees none of this bubble. Thus, after adding the ordinary degree-carrying slice, one can choose \(M\) much larger than
\[
R_1E_y+E_y^2/S_1
\]
without increasing the bad term for most punctures \(z\). Averaged in \(z\), the bad probability is only \(|D|/|Q|\), so the same obstruction persists at the family level if \(D\) is tiny.

This is not a counterexample to the rectangle theorem; it is a counterexample to the proposed cellular comparison as a statement about \(\Fill_R(Z_y)\). The issue is that \(\Fill_R(Z_y)\) includes null-homologous garbage invisible to \(G\)-energy and invisible to puncture badness. A Manhattan homotopy cannot turn a positive filling-volume bubble into cost \(0\) merely because \(dG=0\).

So I think the estimate must be modified. Two possible directions:

1. Replace \(Z_y\) by an “essential” representative carrying \(G_\#Z_y=[0,S_1]\times[0,S_2]\), after quotienting/filling \(G\)-null bubbles separately.

2. Keep \(B_y\), but add a term charging good-cell null fillings, not only bad cells. That term would need a new averaging mechanism; it will not self-absorb by the plaque probability alone.

In short: cellularization removes seams, but not flaps. The desired \(R_1E_y+E_y^2/S_1\) bound can only plausibly hold for the degree-carrying part of the slice, not for the raw current \(Z_y\) or its full filling volume.
