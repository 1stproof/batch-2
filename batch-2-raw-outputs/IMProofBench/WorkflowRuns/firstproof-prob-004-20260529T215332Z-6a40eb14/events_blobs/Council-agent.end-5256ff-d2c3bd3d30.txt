I don’t think the listed inequalities can close the estimate algebraically. Here is a clean scaling obstruction.

Fix small \(\kappa\), put \(p=\eta\kappa\) with \(\eta<1\) fixed, and take \(L\gg_\kappa 1\). Let
\[
S=(1,1,L^{10},L^{10}),\qquad
R=\bigl(L^{-2},\,L^6/p,\,p^{1/2}L^{10},\,p^{1/2}L^{10}\bigr).
\]
Then \(R\) is ordered for \(L^4p^{3/2}\gg1\), and
\[
R_1=L^{-2}\le \kappa S_1,\qquad
R_3R_4=pL^{20}\le \kappa S_3S_4.
\]
The volume is
\[
V=L^{24}.
\]
The desired mixed lower bound would be
\[
\kappa S_1S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4=\kappa L^{25},
\]
so the ratio is \(V/(\kappa L^{25})=(\kappa L)^{-1}\to0\).

But the trace inequalities hold. Here \(q=S_3S_4=L^{20}\) and the RHS of \((T_i)\) is \(L^{30}\). Indeed
\[
\frac{V^2}{R_1q}=\frac{L^{48}}{L^{-2}L^{20}}=L^{30},
\]
so \(T_1\) is saturated;
\[
R_2V=(L^6/p)L^{24}=L^{30}/p,
\]
so \(T_2\) holds; and
\[
R_3V=R_4V=p^{1/2}L^{34}\ge L^{30}
\]
for \(L\gg p^{-1/8}\), so \(T_3,T_4\) hold.

The standard Guth monomial estimates also hold. The critical one is exactly saturated:
\[
R_1^2V=L^{-4}L^{24}=L^{20}=S_1^3S_2S_3S_4.
\]
For example,
\[
R_1R_2=L^4/p\ge1,\quad
R_1R_2R_3=L^{14}/p^{1/2}\ge L^{10},
\]
\[
R_1^2R_2R_3=L^{12}/p^{1/2}\ge L^{10},\quad
(R_1R_2)^2R_3R_4=L^{28}/p\ge L^{20},
\]
and \(V=L^{24}\ge L^{20}=V_S\). So this satisfies the reductions up to harmless constants.

Thus the missing input cannot be another manipulation of the separate averaged trace bounds. Geometrically, this model lets the \(x_1\)-trace budget pay for the bad \(A^2/R_1\) filling branch while \(R_2\) is inflated enough to satisfy every monomial estimate. One needs a correlation/simultaneity estimate for the trace family, e.g. a bound involving the boundary sweepout on the \(x_1\)-faces, or mixed-face control such as an estimate on \(\int A_yL_{1,y}\) or a cubical tightening argument using \(df_1\wedge df_3,df_1\wedge df_4\), not merely \(\int L_{1,y}\).

I do not see a direct snake-map counterexample to the original theorem. This scaling would require quotients roughly
\[
Q=(L^2,\;pL^{-6},\;p^{-1/2},\;p^{-1/2}),
\]
saturating the \(j=1,l=4\) Guth monomial. A product snake would blow up mixed \(1\)-\(3\) or \(1\)-\(4\) two-dilation by \(L^2p^{-1/2}\), so any actual counterexample would need a genuine four-dimensional pinching construction, not the usual product snake.
