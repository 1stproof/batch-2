### 1. The Scaling Test is Extremely Dangerous

### 2. Portfolio of Proof Strategies (Assuming Face-Preservation)
If face-preservation is required (or if the theorem is true), here are three serious angles to rigorously bridge the gap:

**A. Anisotropic Chain-Level Tightening (The "Mixed Face Trace")**
You noted that naive $y_2$-intervals escape through the $x_1$-faces, paying only $O(S_1S_2)$ area. Formalize your idea of anisotropic tightening to penalize this escape. Build a grid on $R$ that is very fine in $x_1$ and coarser elsewhere. Tighten the map so it is cellular on the 2-skeleton. If a 1-cycle escapes through $x_1$, it must sweep out a relative 2-chain whose boundary intersects the $x_1$-mixed 2-faces (e.g., $R_1 \times R_3$). Because $\Dil_2 \le 1$, the total target area of the $x_1$-mixed 2-skeleton is strictly bounded by combinations like $R_1 R_3$. You can bound the "escape flux" by this available mixed area, mathematically forcing the majority of the 1-cycles to remain interior and fill with area $\gtrsim S_2 S_3$.

**B. Diagonal Projections**
Axis-parallel 1-cycles are too easy to short-circuit. Instead, pull back a family of diagonal 1-cycles. Consider the projection $P: S \to \mathbb{R}^3$ given by $(y_1, y_4, \frac{y_3}{S_3} - \frac{y_2}{S_2})$. The fibers are diagonal intervals that span across the $y_2$ and $y_3$ dimensions simultaneously. Escaping through the $x_1$ face now requires the 2-filling to span the long diagonal, entangling the $S_3$ length and thwarting the $O(S_1 S_2)$ cheap escape. Coarea on this map combined with local $R$-isoperimetry should naturally spin out the missing $S_2^{1/2} S_3^{1/2}$ geometric mean.

**C. Direct Boundary Slicing**
Instead of fighting the interior escape, leverage the boundary. If face preservation holds, the boundary inequality $R_2 R_3^2 R_4 \gtrsim S_2 S_3^2 S_4$ is true. Apply the coarea formula to the *restriction* of $f$ to the 3D boundary faces, pushing the rational homotopy lower bound directly into a volumetric boundary estimate. You can often splice this boundary volume bound with the interior coarea bound using the standard relative sequence to force the mixed estimate $S_1 S_2^{1/2} S_3^{3/2} S_4$.
