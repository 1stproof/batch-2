I would not regard the displayed LE-HAF estimate as presently having a standard chain-level proof. I also do not know a clean numerical counterexample after the “all coordinate plaques” correction; the obvious linear/permutation examples are exactly what that correction fixes. But a direct proof by cutting \(B_y\) along \({\mathcal B}_z\) is not rigorous: the missing seam term is real.

The only route I would trust is a cellular chain-map version, roughly as follows. For a shifted cubulation \(\mathcal K_\theta\), first replace \(B_y\) by a cubical near-minimizer. For fixed \(z\), declare a cell bad if one of the prescribed Manhattan tracks used by the deformation has \(F\)-image containing \(z\). Then construct a cellular operator \(P_{z,\theta}\) and homotopy \(H_{z,\theta}\) such that
\[
\partial H(Z)=Z-P(Z),\qquad \partial P(B)=P(\partial B),
\]
and define
\[
C_{y,z,\theta}=H_{z,\theta}(Z_y)+P_{z,\theta}(B_y).
\]
Then \(\partial C_{y,z,\theta}=Z_y\) relatively, with no interface seam, because \(P\) is a chain map on the whole cubical chain rather than a sharp restriction to \({\mathcal B}_z\). One would need estimates
\[
\Mass H_{z,\theta}(Z_y)\lesssim R_1E_y+E_y^2/S_1
\]
and
\[
\mathbb E_{z,\theta}\Mass P_{z,\theta}(B_y)
 \lesssim {1\over q}\int_Q \Mass(B_y\llcorner {\mathcal B}_z)\,dz
\]
or the corresponding bad-cell version. The Fubini plaque estimate is plausible only if every elementary track varies in at most two source coordinates; ordinary Federer–Fleming radial projections are too uncontrolled here.

This also explains the low-\(G\)-energy flap issue. A flap on which \(dG\approx0\) cannot be physically pushed at cost \(E_y\); any argument that deforms all of \(Z_y\) in the source is doomed. Such pieces must be killed through \(P(B_y)\), using the chosen filling and chain-map cancellation. So the proof must be genuinely “fill first, then deform as a chain map,” not “deform the slice and cap the seams.”

My concern is that the displayed estimate with the literal term \(\Mass(B_y\llcorner{\mathcal B}_z)\) may be slightly too sharp for this cellular proof: cubical bad stars usually enlarge \({\mathcal B}_z\), while enlarged/near-miss bad sets are not controlled by \(J_2F\) because rank-one plaque images can be long and dense with tiny area. Thus the exact Manhattan-track construction is essential.

So my recommendation: do not state LE-HAF as proved from existing tools. State it as a weighted Manhattan deformation lemma. If you can prove that lemma with the chain-map \(P,H\) above, the seam and flap objections disappear and the advertised inequality follows. Without such a lemma, the current estimate remains the genuine hard gap rather than a routine current-theoretic construction.
