## Relevant theorem/proof material found

### 1. Guth thesis, Chapter 8: first alternative in a diffeomorphism regime

The strongest rectangle-specific proof fragment is in Guth's MIT thesis,
Chapter 8, especially pages 143--144 of the extracted slice
`data/round17/thesis_pages_139_148.txt`.

Theorem 8.1, on thesis p.139, states a 4D unit-cube result: if
`F` is a 2-contracting diffeomorphism from the unit 4-cube to a
rectangle `S`, then `S_3 S_4 < C`.  Guth explicitly says this chapter
does **not** give a complete result for diffeomorphisms between general
4D rectangles.

Proposition 8.1.2, thesis p.143, gives the filling input.  In paraphrase:
if `z` is an oriented relative 2-cycle in a 4D rectangle `R` and
`area(z) < (1/2) R_1 R_2`, then `z` bounds a relative 3-chain of volume
`< C R_1 R_2^2`.  The exponent is OCR-sensitive; I rendered
`data/round17/thesis_page_images/thesis_page-143.png`, which confirms
the statement uses `R_1 R_2^2`.

Proposition 8.1.3, thesis p.144, is directly relevant.  Paraphrase:
if there is a 2-contracting diffeomorphism from `R` to `S`, and if
`C R_1 R_2^2 < S_1 S_2 S_3`, then
`R_3 R_4 > c S_3 S_4`.  Guth's proof takes central 2-planes in `S`,
observes they have filling volume at least `c S_1 S_2 S_3`, pulls back
under the diffeomorphism, applies Proposition 8.1.2 to force their
preimages to have area at least `c R_1 R_2`, and then uses a coarea /
transverse-area argument to obtain the `R_3 R_4` lower bound.  The
rendered page `data/round17/thesis_page_images/thesis_page-144.png`
confirms the statement and proof context.

This is essentially the **first alternative** in the present problem,
but only under the small-filling hypothesis `C R_1 R_2^2 < S_1 S_2 S_3`
and for diffeomorphisms.  It does not supply the missing hard-regime
volume alternative for arbitrary degree-one maps.

### 2. arXiv:0710.0403, area-expanding embeddings of rectangles

Source file: `data/round17/arxiv_0710_0403_src/EXEMB2.TEX`.

Relevant pieces:

- Lines 478--488: Theorem 3, isoperimetric profile of a rectangle.
  If `V <= c(n) R_1 ... R_k`, write
  `V = c(n) R_1 ... R_j rho^{k-j}` with
  `R_j <= rho <= R_{j+1}`; then
  `I_R^k(V) <= C(n) R_1 ... R_j rho^{k-j+1}`.
  Also always `I_R^k(V) <= C(n) R_{k+1} V`.
- Lines 656--663: Estimate 1 for a map of pairs with degree `D>0`.
  This is the monomial obstruction
  `dil_k >= c(n) D Q_1 ... Q_j (Q_{j+1} ... Q_l)^{(k-j)/(l-j)}`
  for suitable `j,l`.
- Lines 677--682: Estimate 2, another degree-sensitive obstruction.
- Lines 867--883: equivalent formulation using nontrivial
  `k`-contracting forms.

These estimates support the already-known monomial inequalities in
`answer.tex`, but I do not see a way in this paper alone to get the
hard-regime inequality.  In particular, the stress-test regime already
noted in the draft can satisfy these monomial inequalities while failing
the desired dichotomy.

### 3. arXiv:0802.3549 and thesis Chapter 7: rational homotopy / DGA method

Source file: `data/round17/arxiv_0802_3549_src/LINKDIL2.TEX`.

Important references:

- Lines 212--228: definition of the second-order linking invariant for
  maps to a wedge of spheres.
- Lines 318--326: Theorem 3, bounding a second-order linking invariant
  in terms of ellipse dimensions, volume, and `k_i`-dilations.
- Lines 371--386 and 1556--1565: for ellipses, in the simplest 4D case
  one gets a lower bound of the form
  `Dil_2(F) >= c |D|^{1/3} Q_1^{1/3} Q_2^{2/3} Q_3^{2/3} Q_4^{1/3}`.
- Lines 878--892: Proposition 2.2, a relative directional isoperimetric
  inequality in rectangles.  This is the closest explicit
  rectangle-directional primitive/filling estimate in the arXiv source.
- Lines 1414--1503: the construction compactifies/doubles a rectangular
  piece and maps it to a wedge of spheres.
- Lines 1522--1549: second-order linking lower-bound construction.

The thesis Chapter 7 slice `data/round17/thesis_pages_110_135.txt`
contains the underlying DGA machinery:

- Lines 5--9: Theorem 7.1, directional primitive estimate.
- Lines 24--27: Lemma 7.1, directional filling for rectilinear cycles.
- Lines 237--247: Chapter 7.6 says the goal is estimating
  rational-homotopy invariants using these directional primitive
  estimates.
- Lines 696--755: application to `k`-dilation of diffeomorphisms between
  rectangles using boundary and double constructions.
- Lines 760--768: 4D, `k=2` inequalities for diffeomorphisms, including
  OCR-paraphrased forms
  `R_2 R_3^2 R_4 \gtrsim S_2 S_3^2 S_4`,
  `R_1 R_2 R_3^2 R_4 \gtrsim S_1 S_2 S_3^2 S_4`, and
  `R_1 R_2^2 R_3^2 R_4 \gtrsim S_1 S_2^2 S_3^2 S_4`.

This DGA material does **not** appear to be the missing degree-one
rectangle proof.  It works through ellipses, doubles, boundary maps, or
diffeomorphisms, not through a quotient `R/\partial R` plus an averaged
family argument for arbitrary pair maps.  I found no statement in
Chapter 7 or arXiv:0802.3549 that gives equation (13) / LE-HAF.

## Implication for the current proof

What is solidly available:

1. If one can put the problem into Guth thesis Proposition 8.1.3's
   small-filling regime, then the first alternative
   `R_3 R_4 \gtrsim S_3 S_4` follows by Guth's central-plane/filling
   argument.
2. arXiv:0710.0403 gives the standard degree/monomial lower bounds.
3. arXiv:0802.3549 and thesis Chapter 7 give powerful directional
   primitive and second-order linking machinery, but I found no
   rectangle-specific statement that upgrades those monomial bounds into
   the hard-regime volume inequality.

Therefore, based on located sources, the present draft still needs a
new argument at equation (13) / LE-HAF, or an actual copy of Guth's
missing `[G4]` preprint.  The missing preprint likely bypassed LE-HAF by
a full case classification of 4D rectangle pairs, but I could not
recover it or a secondary theorem statement precise enough to cite as a
black box.
