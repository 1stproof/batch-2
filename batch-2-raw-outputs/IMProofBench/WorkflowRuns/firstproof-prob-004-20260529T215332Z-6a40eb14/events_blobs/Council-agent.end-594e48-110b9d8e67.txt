**Honest verdict: uncertain, but I am skeptical of (AF) as stated.** The structural shape is right, but the specific factor $S_1$ (not $R_1$) in the second term is essential ‚Äî replacing $S_1$ by $R_1$ loses a $\sqrt{\kappa}$ factor in the conditional argument and kills it ‚Äî yet $S_1$ is not what a naive source-side Federer‚ÄìFleming gives.

**Where the strangeness sits.**

*The $S_1$ in the FF term.* Standard FF for relative 2-cycles in $R$ has source branch $A^2/R_1$ (smallest source side). To get $A^2/S_1$, the argument cannot be purely a source FF; it must pull back a target FF. Fillings of $P_y$ in $S$ honestly enjoy $A^2/S_1$. The obstacle for the pullback: $J_3 f$ has no uniform bound from $\Dil_2(f)\le 1$ ‚Äî only $\lambda^2 J_2 F\le 1$ ‚Äî so pulling a 3-chain in $S$ back to $R$ along $f$ is not Jacobian-controlled in any obvious way.

*The $R_1 I$ slab term.* Per-fiber slab gives $\Fill(Z_y)\le R_1 M_y$, so averaging yields $R_1\int M_y\le R_1\,\Vol(R)$. Replacing $\int M_y$ by $I=\int\int\lambda^2$ is a genuine improvement when $\lambda\ll 1$ on average. A "weighted slab" ‚Äî sliding $x\in Z_y$ by $\sim R_1\lambda^2(x)/\sup\lambda^2$ ‚Äî has the right scaling, but the residual lid is no longer in $\partial R$ and must be patched by a correction layer.

**Sanity check.** Take $R=S$ with central planes $Z_y$. Then $\lambda\equiv 1$, $I=qS_1S_2$, LHS $\sim qS_1S_2S_3$, while the source-side RHS is $\sim S_1^2S_2q+S_1S_2^2q$, which is $\ll$ LHS when $S_3\gg S_2$. (AF) survives only via the defect term, which is $F_0q$ here. So (AF) is essentially vacuous in the $R=S$ regime and only carries content when $R_3R_4\ll S_3S_4$ ‚Äî exactly where no Guth-style explicit comparison map is available, which is why you cannot manufacture a counterexample without also disproving the theorem.

**Routes to try, in decreasing confidence.**

1. **Target-pullback for the $S_1$-term.** Fill $P_y$ in $S$ by a slab $V_y$ and FF-deform $V_y$ inside $S$ (which legitimately has $A^2/S_1$). Then pull back via $f$. The technical core is controlling $\Vol(f^{\#}V_y)$ by combining $\lambda^2 J_2 F\le 1$ with the boundary trace of $V_y$ on the $S_1$-face. This is the only natural source of the $S_1$.

2. **Weighted slab for the $R_1 I$ term.** Variable-distance slab plus a correction chain on $\{x_1=0\}$; the correction should cost $\sim$ FF on the residual lid, which is small precisely because $\lambda^2$ is the deficit.

3. **Anisotropic Guth deformation.** Decompose $R$ by cells of size $R_1$ in $x_1$ and adaptive sizes elsewhere chosen by local $J_2 F$ and $\lambda$. Apply Guth's deformation on this skeleton and bound family contributions per face by coarea. This is most likely to give a mildly weaker but still sufficient inequality.

**Adjacent literature.** Liokumovich‚ÄìNabutovsky‚ÄìRotman on parametric width and sweepout filling profiles; Guth's "Minimax problems related to cup powers and Steenrod squares" for parametric FF intuition; Wenger's filling inequalities; Papasoglu on filling functions. The two-parameter sweepout aspect is closest to LNR.

**Concrete intermediate.** Before tackling (AF), try
$$\int_Q\Fill_R(Z_y)\,dy\le C\!\left(R_1\Vol(R)+\frac{\int_Q M_y^2\,dy}{S_1}+\text{defect}\right),$$
and use Cauchy‚ÄìSchwarz with the Guth profile $\sup M_y\lesssim(S_1S_2S_3)^{2/3}$ to control $\int M_y^2$. If this already fails on your $L^{1/10}$ stress test, the theorem is in real trouble ‚Äî and that diagnostic is cheap.

**Counterexample.** I cannot give one without simultaneously refuting the theorem, so refuting (AF) by exhibiting a bad map is not a fruitful angle ‚Äî the two stand or fall together in your setup.
