I would try to prove **AT only in dual form**, not by producing a literal low-mass homotopy of \(Z_y\). The latter is exactly where dead low-\(G\)-energy flaps break things.

Here is the package I would aim for.

**1. Choose fillings by a penalized functional.**  
Instead of arbitrary near-minimizers, choose \(B_y\) almost minimizing
\[
\Phi_y(B)=\Mass B+\mu |Q|^{-1}\int_Q \Mass(B\llcorner\mathcal B_z)\,dz,
\qquad \partial B=Z_y .
\]
For \(\kappa\) small this is still a \(2\)-near-minimizer for mass, by the plaque Fubini bound. This avoids dependence on a malicious choice of minimizer.

**2. Dualize \(\Fill\).**  
For each \(y\in\Omega\), take a relative \(2\)-form/cochain \(\alpha_y\) with
\[
\|d\alpha_y\|_\infty\le 1,\qquad
\langle Z_y,\alpha_y\rangle\ge \Fill_R(Z_y)-\varepsilon .
\]
It suffices to prove, after averaging in \(y,z\),
\[
\int_\Omega \langle Z_y,\alpha_y\rangle dy
\lesssim R_1I+I^2/(S_1|Q|)
+|Q|^{-1}\!\int_Q\!\int_\Omega \Mass(B_y\cap\mathcal B_z)\,dy\,dz .
\]
This formulation automatically ignores null sheets if they are killed through \(B_y\).

**3. Exact-plaque acyclic carrier lemma.**  
Fix a shifted cubulation \(K_\theta\). For each cell \(\sigma\) of dimension \(\le3\), prescribe a finite list of coordinate \(2\)-plaques swept by a Manhattan contraction of \(\sigma\). Declare \(\sigma\) bad for \(z\) if one of those plaque images contains \(z\). Then construct chain maps and homotopies
\[
P_{z,\theta},H_{z,\theta},\qquad
\partial H+H\partial=P-\mathrm{Id},
\]
such that \(P\) sends good \(3\)-cells into \(\partial R\) relatively and sends all remaining mass into bad cells. There is no seam term because this is a chain map, not a sharp restriction. The exact-plaque definition gives
\[
\mathbb E_{z,\theta}\Mass(P_{z,\theta}B_y)
\lesssim (R_3R_4/|Q|)\Mass B_y .
\]
The acyclicity input is local: the prescribed good \(2\)-plaques give fillers in \(Q\setminus\{z\}\), avoiding the \(H_1(Q\setminus\{z\})\) obstruction.

**4. Prove only a dual energy estimate for \(H(Z_y)\).**  
Do not try to bound \(\Mass H(Z_y)\). Prove instead
\[
\mathbb E_{z,\theta}\int_\Omega
\big|\langle H_{z,\theta}Z_y,d\alpha_y\rangle\big|\,dy
\lesssim
\int_\Omega \left(R_1E_y+E_y^2/S_1\right)dy .
\]
This is the analytic core. I would attack it with a random grid in the \(G=(f_1,f_2)\)-rectangle: coarea controls the expected \(G\)-essential grid traces by \(E_y\); short-direction tracks give \(R_1E_y\); the remaining target-cell filling is a 2D rectangular/modulus estimate giving \(E_y^2/S_1\). Null flaps contribute zero here because the estimate is on pairings, not mass.

Then
\[
C_{y,z,\theta}=P_{z,\theta}B_y-H_{z,\theta}Z_y
\]
fills \(Z_y\), so testing by \(d\alpha_y\) yields AT. On \(\Omega\), \(\int E_y^2\le 4I^2/|Q|\). This is the cleanest route I see; the key lemma to isolate is the dual energy estimate in step 4.
