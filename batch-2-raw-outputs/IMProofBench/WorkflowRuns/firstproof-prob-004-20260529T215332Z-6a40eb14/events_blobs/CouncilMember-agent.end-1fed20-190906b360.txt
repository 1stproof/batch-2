The boundary-compatibility gap is indeed a **fatal mathematical obstruction** to the chain-level formulation of (EAT) as currently written. You cannot define $C_y^0$ purely as a global minimizer of $\EFill_f(y)$ and then bound a transition by $E_y$. Because $E_y$ is a localized property of the geometric slice $Z_y$, and an unconstrained minimizer $C_y^0$ could be geometrically disjoint, there is no analytic linkage to charge the tightening cost to $E_y$. 

Furthermore, as you astutely diagnosed with the "flap" obstruction, you cannot physically tighten $Z_y$ in the source first, because physical source homotopies cost ordinary mass, charging you for flaps where $dG \approx 0$ but volume is large.

To close this gap, you must pursue your third option: **abandon the decoupled global minimizer and construct an essential 2-cycle $W_y$ directly from $Z_y$ via an averaged selection of its degree-carrying sheets, paying for the switching seams analytically.** 

Here is the concrete proof route to construct the essential part of the slice and the homotopy connecting them, directly leveraging the target $G$-coordinates to make the flaps algebraically invisible:

**1. Target-Slicing to Isolate Flaps (The Coarea Step):**
Do not use a source-cubulation. Instead, slice the 2-current $Z_y$ by the target coordinate $f_1$. For regular $u \in [0, S_1]$, the slice $\gamma_u = \langle Z_y, f_1, u \rangle$ is a relative 1-cycle in $R$ mapping via $f_2$ onto $[0, S_2]$ with degree 1. The $G$-energy intrinsically controls these slices via the coarea formula. By Cauchy-Schwarz:
$$ \left( \int_0^{S_1} \Mass(\gamma_u) \, du \right)^2 \le S_1 \int_{Z_y} |\nabla f_1|^2 \, d\|Z_y\| \le S_1 E_y $$

**2. 1D Sheet Selection (Killing the Flaps):**
For each $u$, the 1-cycle $\gamma_u$ may contain large, algebraically null components (your flaps, where $df_2 \approx 0$). Define a new 1-current $\tilde{\gamma}_u$ by selecting a single degree-carrying arc of $\gamma_u$ and dropping the null flaps. Because this selection happens on the 1-dimensional slices, dropping the flaps costs exactly zero ambient 3-volume. 

**3. Bounding the Switching Seam Cost (The source of $E_y^2 / S_1$):**
To assemble $W_y = \int_0^{S_1} \tilde{\gamma}_u \, du$ into a valid 2-cycle, you must connect the selected arcs across adjacent $u$-levels. The "switching seams" required to make $\tilde{\gamma}_u$ a closed current consist of segments tracking along the source domain.
The 2-dimensional area spanned by the homotopy $H_y$ between $Z_y$ and $W_y$ consists of two parts:
*   Pushing the dropped flaps onto the selected sheet in the short source direction costs mass bounded by $R_1 \Mass(Z_y)$, yielding the $R_1 E_y$ term.
*   The cost of the switching seams between different $u$-levels is governed by the 1-dimensional traces. Using the coarea bound from Step 1, the total seam area is bounded by the length squared over the interval: $\frac{1}{S_1} \left(\int \Mass(\gamma_u) du\right)^2 \lesssim E_y^2 / S_1$.

**4. The Exact-Plaque Substitution:**
You have now constructed a specific essential 2-cycle $W_y$ and a 3-chain $H_y$ such that $\partial H_y = W_y - Z_y$, with $\Mass(H_y) \lesssim R_1 E_y + E_y^2 / S_1$. 
Because $W_y$ carries the degree and has been stripped of null-homologous flaps, any near-minimizing filling $B_y$ of $W_y$ will faithfully reflect the exact-plaque intersections. You now define your filling of the original slice as $C_y = B_y - H_y$, satisfying $\partial C_y = Z_y$. 

**Summary:**
By slicing with the target map $f_1$ before tightening, the flaps naturally have zero thickness in the slicing parameter, rendering them invisible to the mass penalty. Furthermore, this cleanly identifies the geometric origin of the nonlinear $I^2 / (S_1 |Q|)$ term: it is the analytic cost of optimizing and connecting 1-dimensional slices. This explicitly couples the essential chain to $Z_y$, completely resolving the boundary-compatibility gap.
