# Round 22 report: coordinate-specific second-order linking probe

I inspected Guth arXiv:0802.3549 from the existing source
`data/round21/arxiv_0802_3549_src/LINKDIL2.TEX` and wrote the check in
`code/round22_coordinate_l2_lp.py`.  The script output is saved at
`data/round22/coordinate_l2_lp_report.txt`.

## Bottom line

I did **not** find a coordinate-specific second-order linking inequality that
closes the hard-regime gap.  After specializing Guth's \(L_2\) proof to
\(n=4,\ k_1=k_2=k_3=2\) and keeping the relative rectangular directional terms,
the valid upper bound still has an unavoidable \(R_2R_3\Vol(R)\) worst term.
The certified target construction gives exactly the known rational-homotopy
monomial
\[
  R_1R_2^2R_3^2R_4 \gtrsim S_1S_2^2S_3^2S_4,
\]
which is too weak.  Even an optimistic formal strengthening using all
Hamilton-path coordinate choices with target factor \(\Vol(S)S_iS_j\) and the
same universal upper \(R_2R_3\Vol(R)\) still does not imply
\[
  \Vol(R)\gtrsim S_1S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4.
\]

## Specialized directional upper bound

Use the relative rectangle version of Guth's directional isoperimetric
inequality.  If \(A_{ij}=\Vol_{ij}(z_1)\) are the six directional volumes of the
first fiber, then the first filling \(y_1\) may be chosen with
\[
\begin{aligned}
\Vol_{123}(y_1)&\lesssim R_3A_{12}+R_2A_{13}+R_1A_{23},\\
\Vol_{124}(y_1)&\lesssim R_2A_{14}+R_1A_{24},\\
\Vol_{134}(y_1)&\lesssim R_1A_{34},\\
\Vol_{234}(y_1)&=0 .
\end{aligned}
\]
After slicing by the second \(S^2\)-factor and filling the resulting 1-cycle,
\[
  \Mass(y_2)
  \lesssim (R_2+2R_1)
  \{R_3A_{12}+R_2(A_{13}+A_{14})
      +R_1(A_{23}+A_{24}+A_{34})\}.
\]
Thus the dominant coefficients of the initial source directions are
\[
\begin{array}{c|c}
\text{initial direction} & \text{dominant coefficient}\\ \hline
12 & R_2R_3\\
13,14 & R_2^2\\
23,24,34 & R_1R_2 .
\end{array}
\]
For a composition \(h\circ f\), where the first \(S^2\)-factor of \(h\) is built
from target coordinate pair \(P\), this becomes
\[
 |L_2(h\circ f)|
 \lesssim L^6\{R_2R_3M_{P,12}
 +R_2^2(M_{P,13}+M_{P,14})
 +R_1R_2(M_{P,23}+M_{P,24}+M_{P,34})\},
\]
where
\[
  M_{P,ij}=\int_R\left|\frac{\partial f_P}{\partial(x_i,x_j)}\right|.
\]
Since \(\Dil_2 f\le1\), each \(M_{P,ij}\le \Vol(R)\).  The term
\(M_{P,12}\) cannot be discarded by a coordinate choice: after an arbitrary
degree-one \(2\)-contracting \(f\), the first fiber need not stay in the
coordinate-complement direction.  Therefore the valid scalar upper is still
\[
  |L_2(h\circ f)|\lesssim L^6 R_2R_3\Vol(R).
\]

## Target lower scaling

Guth's lower construction, specialized to \(n=4,k_i=2\), uses the ordered
coordinate blocks
\[
  (x_3,x_4),\qquad (x_2,x_3),\qquad (x_1,x_2).
\]
The three degrees to unit \(S^2\)'s at Lipschitz scale \(L\) are
\[
  D_1\sim S_3S_4L^2,\qquad
  D_2\sim S_2S_3L^2,\qquad
  D_3\sim S_1S_2L^2,
\]
so
\[
  L_2(h)\sim D_1D_2D_3
       \sim S_1S_2^2S_3^2S_4L^6
       = \Vol(S)S_2S_3L^6 .
\]
Naturality under the degree-one pair map gives \(L_2(h\circ f)=L_2(h)\).
Comparing with the domain upper gives only
\[
  R_2R_3\Vol(R)\gtrsim S_2S_3\Vol(S).
\]

I also enumerated the 24 Hamilton-path coordinate patterns
\[
  P_1=(\pi_3,\pi_4),\quad P_2=(\pi_2,\pi_3),\quad P_3=(\pi_1,\pi_2),
\]
whose formal target factor is \(\Vol(S)S_{\pi_2}S_{\pi_3}\).  These are listed in
`data/round22/coordinate_l2_lp_report.txt`.  Only the sorted Guth pattern above is
certified by the uniform Lipschitz lower construction for arbitrary sorted
rectangles; nevertheless I also tested the optimistic algebra where all six
possible middle factors \(\Vol(S)S_iS_j\) are granted.

## LP insufficiency check

The LP used logarithmic exponents and included:

- sorted \(R_i,S_i\), normalized by \(s_1=0,s_4=1\);
- hard regime \(r_1\le s_1\), \(r_3+r_4\le s_3+s_4\);
- failure of the first alternative, \(r_1+2r_2\ge s_1+s_2+s_3\);
- the monomial constraints listed in `research_notes.tex` and `answer.tex`,
  including Guth \(3r_1+r_2+r_3+r_4\ge3s_1+s_2+s_3+s_4\);
- the current partial bound
  \[
    \Vol(R)\ge (R_1/S_1)^{1/2}S_1S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4;
  \]
- the valid \(L_2\) inequality
  \[
    \Vol(R)R_2R_3\ge \Vol(S)S_2S_3.
  \]

With the valid \(L_2\) inequality, the LP minimum of
\(\log_L(\Vol(R)/(S_1S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4))\) is \(-0.1\).  One optimum is
\[
  s=(0,0,1,1),\qquad r=(-0.2,0.6,0.8,1.2).
\]
The hard condition \(r_3+r_4=s_3+s_4\) is only non-strict in that optimizer, so I
also checked the round-19 strict hard model
\[
  S=(1,1,L,L),\qquad
  R=(L^{-1/5},L^{13/15},L^{13/15},L^{13/15}).
\]
For this model,
\[
\Vol(R)=L^{12/5},\qquad
S_1S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4=L^{5/2},
\]
so it misses the desired estimate by \(L^{-1/10}\).  It also has
\[
  R_1/S_1=L^{-1/5},\qquad
  R_3R_4/(S_3S_4)=L^{-4/15}.
\]
The formal strongest Hamilton-path target inequality, even if granted,
\[
  \Vol(R)R_2R_3\ge \Vol(S)S_3S_4,
\]
has exponent slack
\[
  (12/5+13/15+13/15)-(2+2)=2/15>0.
\]
Thus even that optimistic scalar strengthening would not close the gap.

## Conclusion

The coordinate-specific \(L_2\) route does not bypass the missing \((13')\).
The directional calculation is useful because it identifies exactly where the
loss occurs: the first-fiber \(12\)-direction carries coefficient \(R_2R_3\), and
coordinate-pair choices do not give a valid way to rule out that component for
an arbitrary degree-one \(2\)-contracting \(f\).  Closing the theorem still seems
to require the nonlocal averaged tightening / compatibility estimate, not a
single coordinate second-order linking invariant.
