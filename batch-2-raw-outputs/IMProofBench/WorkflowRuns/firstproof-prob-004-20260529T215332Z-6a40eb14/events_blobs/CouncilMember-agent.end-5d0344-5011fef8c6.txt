### The Obstruction: Multi-Sheeted Flaps
To break LE-HAF, we construct a family of slices where the LHS ($\int \Fill_R$) scales with a large folding integer $N$, the RHS main term ($R_1 E_y + E_y^2/S_1$) remains $O(1)$, and the bad mass term is strictly too small to absorb the difference.

1. **The Folded Projection ($F$):** 
Because $F=(f_3,f_4)$ is not subject to pair-map boundary conditions on its own, it can cover $Q$ by folding the domain many times. Imagine an "accordion" fold that snakes back and forth $N$ times. This creates fibers $Z_y = F^{-1}(y)$ consisting of $N$ distinct, parallel 2-sheets crossing the domain. 
As long as the 1-dilation is large in the folding direction but correspondingly small in the complementary directions, we maintain $\Dil_2(F) \le 1$. Because $R_3R_4 \ll S_3S_4$, every coordinate 2-plaque $P_{ij}$ still has image area $\le R_iR_j \le R_3R_4 \ll |Q|$. Thus, the bad set $\mathcal{B}_z$ is genuinely tiny, and the Fubini estimate strictly bounds the bad mass term by $O(R_3R_4/S_3S_4) \int \Fill_R(Z_y)$. The bad mass term **cannot** save the inequality.

2. **The Localized Energy ($G$):**
We now define $G=(f_1,f_2)$ to carry the required degree 1 onto $S_1 \times S_2$. Crucially, we make $G$ active on exactly **one** of the $N$ sheets of $Z_y$, and set $G = \text{const}$ on the other $N-1$ sheets. 
Because $dG \wedge dF$ is only non-zero on the first sheet (and bounded by 1), $\Dil_2(F,G) \le 1$ is preserved. 

3. **Evaluating LE-HAF:**
- **The RHS:** The energy $E_y = \int_{Z_y} \|dG\|^2$ is integrated *only* over the single active sheet. Thus, $E_y \sim S_1S_2 = O(1)$. The main RHS term $R_1 E_y + E_y^2/S_1$ is entirely independent of $N$.
- **The LHS:** The geometric slice $Z_y$ contains all $N$ sheets. Because each sheet spans the domain, it is a relative cycle that must be pushed to $\partial R$ to be filled. The filling volume $\Fill_R(Z_y)$ is the sum of the filling costs of all $N$ sheets. Thus, $\int \Fill_R(Z_y) \sim N$.
- **Conclusion:** For large $N$ (permitted up to the global volume limit $\Vol(R) \ge N \cdot S_1S_2S_3S_4$), we get $N \lesssim 1 + \text{tiny} \cdot N$, which is a contradiction. LE-HAF fails.

### The Path Forward: Cohomological Tightening
The failure of LE-HAF teaches us something vital: **you cannot bound the geometric filling of the full slice $Z_y$ using an energy $E_y$ that only sees the degree-carrying part.** Any proof using the current definitions will be derailed by "dead" mass (null-homologous flaps or extra sheets where $dG=0$).

To bypass this, you must stop measuring the physical mass of the geometric fibers and shift to evaluating the homological/cohomological degree-carrying core. I suggest two concrete avenues:

**1. Slicing with Pushforward Currents:**
Instead of taking the physical slice $Z_y = \langle [R], F, y \rangle$ and minimizing its filling, redefine the target of your tightening to be $G_\# Z_y$ or the product current $(G \times F)_\# [R]$. 
If you push forward first, the "dead" sheets where $dG=0$ are algebraically crushed to dimension $<2$ and disappear from the mass of the current. You need an averaged parametric estimate not on the filling of $Z_y$ in $R$, but on the linking/filling of the active current $f_\#[R]$ in the target (or a tailored intermediate space).

**2. Rational Homotopy / DGA Probe:**
This obstruction is exactly why Guth's thesis utilizes DGA linking invariants: differential forms automatically integrate to zero over dead flaps. 
Instead of looking for a coordinate-specific Hamiltonian path, look for a second-order invariant of the form $\int_R \alpha \wedge d^{-1}(\beta \wedge \gamma)$, where $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$ are pullbacks of carefully chosen 2-forms on the target. If you weight the forms asymmetrically (e.g., placing the $x_1$ short-direction penalty directly on the primitive of the $G$-form), you can bypass the generic $R_2R_3$ ellipsoid bound and extract the missing $S_1(S_2 S_3)^{1/2}$ factor. 

In short: drop the attempt to physically deform the full slice $Z_y$. The gap must be closed by an algebraic mechanism that makes the flaps invisible by definition.
