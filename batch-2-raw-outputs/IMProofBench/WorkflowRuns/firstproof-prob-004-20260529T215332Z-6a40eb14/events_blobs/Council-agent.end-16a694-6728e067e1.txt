**Verdict on the two routes.** Neither closes the gap; the obstructions are structural.

*Route 1 (slicing $f_1=t$).* A pointwise bound $\int_{M_t}|\nabla f_1|^{-1}\gtrsim S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4$ from the data $\|dg\|\le w$, $\|\Lambda^2 dg\|\le1$, $\deg g=1$ alone is false: the identity on $[0,S_2]\times[0,S_3]\times[0,S_4]$ saturates with weighted volume $S_2S_3S_4$, off by exactly $(S_3/S_2)^{1/2}$. Any rescue requires inter-level compatibility, which is HAF in another guise.

*Route 2 (Guth map composition).* For composition with $g:S\to T$ to help, $g$ must shrink some target factor while keeping $\Dil_2(g)$ bounded. Double pinching needs $A^2<R_2R_3$; short-side-stretch needs $A<(R_3R_4)^{1/2}$. Neither parameter range reaches the stress regime — your Round-25 calculation stands.

**The angle I'd push next: parametric tightening over $Q$.** Your notes keep gesturing here; I believe it's the right structure.

Concrete setup: a randomly-shifted cubical complex $K_\delta$ on $Q$ at scale $\delta\sim S_1$. Vertices $y$ carry $Z_y$; edges $e=[y_1,y_2]$ carry slice 3-chains $W_e=\langle[R],F,e\rangle$ with $\partial W_e=Z_{y_2}-Z_{y_1}$ (rel $\partial R$); squares $\tau$ carry $V_\tau$. The square sum $\sum\Mass V_\tau\le\Vol(R)$ comes from $J_2F\le1$. The technical pinch is the edges: $\Dil_2 f\le1$ does *not* bound $|\nabla f_3|, |\nabla f_4|$ individually, so seam edges can be locally large. Two ways past this:

(a) *Random-shift good-edge selection.* For a positive-density set of shifts, a large fraction of edges have $\Mass W_e/\delta$ controlled by an averaged quantity; bad edges feed into the absorbing $R_3R_4/(S_3S_4)\cdot A$ term via your all-coordinate plaque Fubini.

(b) *Target-grid cobordisms.* Replace source edges by deformations of $G_\# Z_y$ in the target 2-plane, lifted via an $x_1$-push. This is the structure that naturally produces $R_1 E_y+E_y^2/S_1$: $E_y^2/S_1$ is Guth's Estimate-3 ($A^2/R_1$ branch) applied to the $G$-image at target scale $S_1$, and $R_1 E_y$ comes from lifting back through source $x_1$.

**A first milestone.** Try HAF under the extra hypothesis $\lambda\ge c\lambda_0$ uniformly on each $Z_y$ (no flaps), so $E_y\sim\lambda_0^2\Mass Z_y$ and the $G$-image is essentially a graph. The cubical tightening should reduce to weighted Federer–Fleming plus an explicit lift. If even this resists, that's strong evidence either to suspect HAF or that the missing Guth preprint is essential. Flap removal is a separate (probably soft) reduction since zero-energy flaps are null-homologous in their slice.
