My opinion: a **pure target-pullback Green-operator proof is not viable with the residual measured by the unsaturated exact-plaque seminorm \(\beta_z\)**. The obstruction is structural, not just technical.

The key conflict is this. A quotient cochain is a functional depending only on \(f_\# C\). A target Green operator produces residuals of the form “pull back a target cochain/current singular at \(z\),” so its pairing with \(C\) also depends only on \(f_\# C\). But
\[
\beta_z([C])=\inf_{N\in\ker f_\#}\Mass((C+N)\llcorner {\mathcal B}_z)
\]
is deliberately **not** a target-side norm; it allows changing the source representative to avoid the unsaturated bad set. In the two-lift model \(A,A'\) with \(f_\#A=f_\#A'=P\), if \({\mathcal B}_z\) contains \(A\) but not \(A'\), then \(\beta_z([A])=0\), while any target-pullback residual detecting \(P\) pairs equally with \(A\) and \(A'\). Hence an estimate
\[
|\langle f^*\theta_z,C\rangle|\lesssim \beta_z([C])
\]
cannot hold unless that residual vanishes on the whole target class. Replacing \(\beta_z\) by a saturated \(f^{-1}f({\mathcal B}_z)\)-seminorm would restore compatibility, but then the plaque Fubini absorption is likely destroyed.

There is also a geometric mismatch. A Green kernel on \(Q\setminus\{z\}\), e.g. \(d\eta_z=\delta_z-q^{-1}dy_3dy_4\), has nonlocal tails or singular level/ray sets. Pulling it back by \(F\) localizes to \(F^{-1}\) of those tails, not to the exact coordinate-plaque bad set. The exact plaque set is strong precisely because it charges only zero-thickness Manhattan tracks; any thickened or analytic near-miss set can be huge under rank-one comb behavior.

Finally, the good term
\[
R_1E_y+E_y^2/S_1
\]
does not naturally come from an \(F\)-Green operator. It is a \(G=(f_1,f_2)\)-side planar/Manhattan cost: \(R_1E_y\) is the short-direction sweep weighted by \(G\)-energy, and \(E_y^2/S_1\) is the one-dimensional coarea/planar filling cost in the \(G\)-rectangle. An \(F\)-Green estimate would more naturally give kernel integrals like \(\int_{Z_y}E_y|\nabla\eta_z(F)|^2\) or logarithmic terms, not the stated scale.

A viable formulation, if it exists, should be **cellular/quotient-dual rather than smooth target-Green**. One would need: for every quotient \(2\)-cochain \(\phi\) with \(\|\delta\phi\|\le1\),
\[
\phi([Z_y])=K_y(\delta\phi)+\Psi_{z,\phi}([C_y^0]),
\]
with
\[
|K_y(\delta\phi)|\lesssim R_1E_y+E_y^2/S_1,\qquad
|\Psi_{z,\phi}([C])|\lesssim \beta_z([C]).
\]
Here \(K_y\) should be the dual of a \(G\)-Manhattan tightening, while \(\Psi_{z,\phi}\) must lie in the polar of the quotient bad seminorm. That residual cannot be an ordinary target-pullback cochain.

So I would not pursue a smooth Green-operator proof except perhaps as a heuristic for choosing the puncture. The actual proof of QEAT seems to require a quotient cellular acyclic-carrier construction whose residual is built directly in the \(\beta_z\)-dual norm.
