I would be cautious about the quotient-dual route as currently stated. There is a structural obstruction to getting the **source-local** bad term from **quotient** cochains.

The dual picture is: a quotient \(2\)-cochain \(\phi\) on
\[
N_\ast(R,\partial R)/\ker f_\#
\]
is the same as a functional \(\bar\phi\) on \(\operatorname{im} f_\#\). In the smooth model \(\phi=f^\ast\beta\), and the unit dual condition for essential filling is
\[
\|f^\ast d\beta\|_{\infty}\le 1.
\]
A quotient homotopy proof would need operators \(\Pi_z,K_z\) with
\[
\phi=\Pi_z\phi+\delta K_z\phi+K_z\delta\phi
\]
in the quotient, so that pairing with \(Z_y\) and using \(f_\#\partial C_y^0=f_\#Z_y\) gives
\[
\langle\phi,Z_y\rangle
=\langle K_z\delta\phi,Z_y\rangle
+\langle\delta\Pi_z\phi,C_y^0\rangle .
\]
Then the needed estimates are exactly
\[
|\langle K_z\delta\phi,Z_y\rangle|
\lesssim R_1E_y+E_y^2/S_1,
\]
and
\[
|\langle\delta\Pi_z\phi,C_y^0\rangle|
\lesssim \Mass(C_y^0\llcorner{\mathcal B}_z).
\]

The obstruction is the second estimate. If \(\Pi_z\phi\) is still a quotient cochain, then \(\delta\Pi_z\phi\) also annihilates \(\ker f_\#\). Hence it cannot be genuinely supported in the non-\(f\)-saturated source set \({\mathcal B}_z\). Smoothly, \(\delta\Pi_z\phi=f^\ast\eta\), so its pointwise support is \(f^{-1}(\operatorname{spt}\eta)\), not an arbitrary source plaque set. More invariantly: if two small oriented \(3\)-parallelepipeds \(T,T'\) have the same \(f_\#\) image, then \(T-T'\in\ker f_\#\), so any quotient cochain must take the same value on them. If \(T'\subset{\mathcal B}_z\) but \(T\cap{\mathcal B}_z=\varnothing\), a bound by \(\Mass(C\llcorner{\mathcal B}_z)\) is impossible. The exact-plaque set is generically not \(f\)-saturated; the hard-regime assumptions do not seem to repair this.

So I do not think a quotient cochain homotopy can produce the advertised bad term unless the bad norm is changed to a quotient/saturated one. The natural quotient-compatible construction would be target-side: take \(r_z:Q\setminus\{z\}\to\partial Q\), define
\[
R_z(s_1,s_2,u)=(s_1,s_2,r_z(u)),
\]
and use the de Rham homotopy
\[
K_z\omega=\int_0^1 \iota_{V_t}(\Phi_t^\ast\omega)\,dt
\]
for a homotopy \(\Phi_t\) from identity to \(R_z\). This automatically descends through \(f_\#\). The \(K_z\delta\phi\) term is plausibly where
\[
R_1E_y+\frac{E_y^2}{S_1}
\]
comes from: the \(R_1E_y\) part from primitives in the short \(x_1\)-direction after killing \(dF\)-components, and the \(E_y^2/S_1\) part from choosing/coarea-averaging an \(f_1\)-slice and filling the resulting \(G\)-seams in the \((f_1,f_2)\)-plane.

But this target-side homotopy yields a **target-saturated** singular/bad set, not the source exact-plaque \({\mathcal B}_z\). The beautiful Fubini estimate
\[
q^{-1}\int_Q \Mass(C\llcorner{\mathcal B}_z)\,dz
\lesssim (R_3R_4/S_3S_4)\Mass C
\]
is precisely source-local and appears incompatible with quotient locality.

My recommendation: either reformulate the bad term as a quotient-compatible/saturated bad norm and see whether any replacement averaging survives, or abandon quotient cochains and build primal representatives plus explicit \(f\)-null corrections. As written, QEAT asks for both quotient invariance and source-local exact-plaque support; those two requirements seem fundamentally at odds.

The quotient/dual cochain formulation contains a subtle but fatal algebraic trap. It successfully hides the $f$-null boundary mismatch only as long as your cochain homotopy preserves the quotient space. But the geometry required to extract the $R_1$ factor breaks this equivariance. 

Here is the precise obstruction to the dual quotient formulation, followed by a constructive primal alternative that bypasses the "flap obstruction."

### The Algebraic Obstruction to the Quotient Dual
To use the quotient complex $\mathcal Q_* = N_* / \ker f_\#$, you must work with dual cochains $\phi \in (\ker f_\#)^\perp$, which are weakly pullbacks of target forms $f^*\alpha$. 

You want a cochain homotopy identity $\phi = K_z d\phi + d K_z \phi + \Pi_z \phi$ such that the integration track $K_z d\phi(Z_y)$ extracts the source bound $R_1 E_y$. To get $R_1$, the operator $K_z$ **must** integrate or translate along the source $x_1$-coordinate. 

However, any operator defined via source translation (like Manhattan tracks) does *not* commute with $f_\#$. Therefore, $K_z$ maps $(\ker f_\#)^\perp$ out of the quotient dual. The residual cochain $\Pi_z \phi$ will no longer annihilate $f$-null chains! 

When you pair this identity with your essential filling $C_y^0$, you get:
\[ \Pi_z \phi(\partial C_y^0) = \Pi_z \phi(Z_y) + \Pi_z \phi(\partial C_y^0 - Z_y) \]
Because $\Pi_z \phi \notin (\ker f_\#)^\perp$, the second term is non-zero. This is exactly the uncontrolled boundary-mismatch seam that the quotient space was supposed to eliminate. The quotient formulation merely translates the geometric seam into a failure of the cochain space to be closed under the required geometric homotopy.

### Constructive Alternative: Target-Pullback Skeleton
To get around both the boundary mismatch and the "flap obstruction" (where $Z_y$ has huge ordinary mass but small $G$-energy), you must abandon source-first deformations and build $C_y^0$ using **target-grid pullbacks**.

Instead of trying to deform the whole geometric fiber $Z_y$, use $G = (f_1, f_2)$ to pull back a grid from the target $S_{12}$-plane. Because you pull back from the target, dead flaps ($dG \approx 0$) naturally have zero image area and are algebraically discarded without paying their source mass.

1. **Grid Pullback & Coarea:** 
Place a regular grid $\mathcal K_\theta$ of mesh size $\sim S_1$ on the target $[0, S_1] \times [0, S_2]$. Pull back the 1-skeleton $\mathcal K_\theta^{(1)}$ to $Z_y$. By coarea over grid shifts $\theta$, the average source mass of the pullback 1-skeleton is:
\[ \mathbb E_\theta \Mass(G^{-1}(\mathcal K_\theta^{(1)}) \cap Z_y) \lesssim \frac{1}{S_1} \int_{Z_y} \|dG\| \, d\mathcal H^2 \le \frac{E_y}{S_1} \]
(The $E_y^2/S_1$ term in your bounds usually reflects a slightly different interpolation, but the scale originates here).

2. **Extracting the Essential Core:**
The pulled-back 1-skeleton partitions $Z_y$ into 2-cells. Discard any 2-cells that do not carry the homological degree of $G$. What remains is an "essential core" $\tilde{Z}_{y,\theta} \subset Z_y$ whose boundary is carried entirely on the 1-skeleton. The boundary $\partial \tilde{Z}_{y,\theta}$ forms the "seams."

3. **Building the 3-Chain $C_y^0$:**
   * **Push the core ($R_1 E_y$ cost):** Translate the essential 2-cells $\tilde{Z}_{y,\theta}$ along the source $x_1$-direction. The volume of this track is bounded by integrating $\|dG\|^2$ (since the area Jacobian $J_2 G$ selects exactly the non-flap regions), yielding the $R_1 E_y$ term.
   * **Cap the seams ($E_y^2 / S_1$ cost):** The pulled-back 1-skeleton must be filled. Since its mass is controlled by $E_y/S_1$, and it maps to the grid lines in the target, you can cap these seams efficiently without hitting the flap mass.

4. **The Plaque Bad Set $\mathcal B_z$:**
Because the construction is now dictated entirely by target coordinate grid lines pulling back to source level sets, the tracks naturally align with coordinate $2$-plaques. You can safely penalize the exact-plaque bad mass $\Mass(C_y^0 \llcorner \mathcal B_z)$ because your chain $C_y^0$ is built from exact coordinate cylinders (the $x_1$-push of $G$-pullbacks), meaning the support strictly respects the Fubini plaque bound.

**Summary:** Drop the quotient complex. It is a dead end because $x_1$-translations break $f$-equivariance. Instead, construct $C_y^0$ natively by pulling back a shifted target grid via $G$. This immediately bypasses the flap mass (because $dG$ weights the pullback) and produces the desired $R_1 E_y + E_y^2 / S_1$ cost via standard coarea sheet-selection, all while maintaining perfect boundary compatibility.
