I would not try to prove a pointwise upgrade
\[
E(y):=\int_{Z_y}\lambda^2 \gtrsim S_1(S_2S_3)^{1/2}
\]
from the single-fiber data. The sharp bad model is still the flat product sheet: low \(E(y)\), high filling, and no contradiction unless one uses that the sheets come from an ambient map and that \(R_3R_4\ll S_3S_4\).

A better target is an **averaged weighted filling inequality**. Let \(q=|Q|\sim S_3S_4\), \(F_0=S_1S_2S_3\), and
\[
I:=\int_Q E(y)\,dy.
\]
Try to prove
\[
\boxed{
\int_Q \Fill_R(Z_y)\,dy
\;\lesssim\;
R_1 I+\frac{I^2}{S_1 q}
+\frac{R_3R_4}{S_3S_4}\,F_0q .
}
\tag{A}
\]
This has exactly the right algebra. Since \(\Fill_R(Z_y)\gtrsim F_0\) on \(Q\),
\[
F_0q\lesssim R_1I+\frac{I^2}{S_1q}
+\frac{R_3R_4}{S_3S_4}F_0q.
\]
If \(R_3R_4\le \kappa S_3S_4\), absorb the last term. If also \(R_1\le\kappa S_1\) and \(I\le \eta qS_1(S_2S_3)^{1/2}\), then
\[
R_1I\le \eta\kappa F_0q
\]
because \(S_1\le (S_2S_3)^{1/2}\), while
\[
\frac{I^2}{S_1q}\le \eta^2 F_0q.
\]
Choosing \(\eta,\kappa\) small gives a contradiction. Hence
\[
I\gtrsim qS_1(S_2S_3)^{1/2},
\]
and weighted coarea gives the desired volume bound.

How to prove (A): I would formulate it as a parametric version of Guth’s deformation/filling theorem, not as independent fillings of the fibers. Use a random grid in the \((f_1,f_2)\)-square and deform the whole current \(F^{-1}(Q)\) relative to this grid. The weighted energy \(E(y)\) should control the averaged cost of pushing fiber pieces across the \((f_1,f_2)\)-grid. Optimizing the grid scale gives the quadratic term \(I^2/(S_1q)\). The short-side motion gives the \(R_1I\) term. The only uncaptured pieces are the flat-product pieces parallel to the \((x_3,x_4)\)-directions; their total contribution should be bounded by the defect term
\[
(R_3R_4/S_3S_4)F_0q.
\]
That last term is essential: without it, the flat central-sheet sweepout in \(S_1\times S_2\times S_3\times S_4\) is a counterexample.

So my recommendation is: do not seek a fiberwise inequality. Prove (A). If (A) fails, a blow-up/minimizing sequence should converge to a product foliation by low-energy sheets; then the linearized \(2\)-dilation bound should force the normal parameter area to be \(\gtrsim S_3S_4\), identifying precisely the obstruction.
