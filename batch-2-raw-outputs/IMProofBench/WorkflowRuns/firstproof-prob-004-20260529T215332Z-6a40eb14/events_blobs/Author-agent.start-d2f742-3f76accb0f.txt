The boundary-compatibility gap is real and, in my view, not closeable by purely formal homological devices. Of your three suggested fixes: a **dual cochain homotopy** only sharpens lower bounds on $\Mass C_y^0$ (which is the calibration argument you already use); a **cellular acyclic carrier** is organizational; **modifying the definition of essential filling** is the right direction, but it doesn't close the gap by itself — you still owe an explicit construction. Here is the cleanest constructive route I see.

**Two-step decomposition: "essentialize $Z_y$, then sweep."**

Replace (EAT) by:

(I) **Essentialization lemma.** For a.e. central $y$, there exists $\sigma_y \in I_2(R,\partial R)$ with $f_\#\sigma_y = P_y$ and $\Mass\sigma_y \le C E_y$.

(II) **Sweep in $x_1$.** Set $C_y^0 = $ the 3-chain swept between $\sigma_y$ and its $x_1$-projection onto $\{x_1=0\}\subset\partial R$. Then $\Mass C_y^0 \le R_1\Mass\sigma_y$ and $\partial C_y^0 = \sigma_y$ in $I_2(R,\partial R)$, so $f_\#\partial C_y^0 = P_y$.

(II) is trivial. (I) is the genuine missing parametric-Plateau-type lemma.

**Why (I) is plausible but technically hard.**

Naïvely, take $\sigma_y = Z_y\llcorner\{\|dG|_{TZ_y}\|^2 \ge \epsilon\}$ for a generic $\epsilon$. By Markov, $\Mass\sigma_y \le E_y/\epsilon$. On the complement both singular values of $dG|_{TZ_y}$ are $<\sqrt\epsilon$, so $G_\#(\text{complement})$ has 2-mass $\le\epsilon\Mass(\text{complement})\to0$; passing to a flat limit, $G_\#\sigma_y = G_\#Z_y = [0,S_1]\times[0,S_2]$, hence $f_\#\sigma_y = P_y$.

The catch: $\sigma_y$ has a **seam** $\{\|dG\|^2=\epsilon\}\cap Z_y$, generically a 1-cycle interior to $Z_y$, so $\sigma_y$ is not relative-closed. You must cap the seam — either inside the low-energy region of $Z_y$ or by an $f$-null 2-chain in $R\setminus Z_y$ — without re-introducing mass. The seam *length* is controlled by BV co-area on $Z_y$, but the *cap area* needs an isoperimetric inequality on $Z_y$, which a single fiber doesn't possess. This is where the family $\{Z_y\}$ has to enter: the substantive content of (EAT) is parametric, not just a redefinition.

**$E_y^2/(S_1 q)$ and bad-plaque terms.**

The sweep yields the $R_1 E_y$ term cleanly. The $E_y^2/S_1$ term should come from a target-rectangle isoperimetric optimization in the $G$-image (where $S_1$ is the relevant short direction) used when source-sweep is wasteful. The bad-plaque term tracks $\sigma_y\llcorner\mathcal{B}_z$ — the construction may accidentally place $\sigma_y$ mass in a coordinate plaque hitting $z$ — and self-absorbs after the all-coordinate plaque Fubini.

**Tools.**

(i) **Almgren/White Plateau with prescribed pushforward**: existence of a mass-minimizer of $\Mass C$ over $C$ with $f_\#\partial C = P_y$ is standard; the issue is the *constant* in the upper bound $\le C E_y$.

(ii) **Guth's width-volume inequality** (`GuthWidth`) applied to $G|_{Z_y}:Z_y\to\mathbb{R}^2$ parametrically in $y$. A 2-width lower bound on $Z_y$ controlled by $E_y$ would give exactly the essentialization mass bound.

(iii) **`Guth2DilationRectanglesPreprint`** is very likely where exactly this parametric essentialization sits, at the heart of the "complicated case distinction." Even an outline would tell you whether the seam-cap or a family-isoperimetric statement is canonical.

**Uncertain:** whether the constant in (I) is absolute or itself requires $R_3R_4\ll S_3S_4$. If yes, the right statement of (EAT) is *"the $f$-essential mass of $Z_y$ is controlled by $E_y$ after a parametric Plateau correction valid in the hard regime"* — which is a cleaner missing input than (EAT) as currently written, and makes it clearer that this is not a fiberwise statement.

The boundary-compatibility gap is indeed a **fatal mathematical obstruction** to the chain-level formulation of (EAT) as currently written. You cannot define $C_y^0$ purely as a global minimizer of $\EFill_f(y)$ and then bound a transition by $E_y$. Because $E_y$ is a localized property of the geometric slice $Z_y$, and an unconstrained minimizer $C_y^0$ could be geometrically disjoint, there is no analytic linkage to charge the tightening cost to $E_y$. 

Furthermore, as you astutely diagnosed with the "flap" obstruction, you cannot physically tighten $Z_y$ in the source first, because physical source homotopies cost ordinary mass, charging you for flaps where $dG \approx 0$ but volume is large.

To close this gap, you must pursue your third option: **abandon the decoupled global minimizer and construct an essential 2-cycle $W_y$ directly from $Z_y$ via an averaged selection of its degree-carrying sheets, paying for the switching seams analytically.** 

Here is the concrete proof route to construct the essential part of the slice and the homotopy connecting them, directly leveraging the target $G$-coordinates to make the flaps algebraically invisible:

**1. Target-Slicing to Isolate Flaps (The Coarea Step):**
Do not use a source-cubulation. Instead, slice the 2-current $Z_y$ by the target coordinate $f_1$. For regular $u \in [0, S_1]$, the slice $\gamma_u = \langle Z_y, f_1, u \rangle$ is a relative 1-cycle in $R$ mapping via $f_2$ onto $[0, S_2]$ with degree 1. The $G$-energy intrinsically controls these slices via the coarea formula. By Cauchy-Schwarz:
$$ \left( \int_0^{S_1} \Mass(\gamma_u) \, du \right)^2 \le S_1 \int_{Z_y} |\nabla f_1|^2 \, d\|Z_y\| \le S_1 E_y $$

**2. 1D Sheet Selection (Killing the Flaps):**
For each $u$, the 1-cycle $\gamma_u$ may contain large, algebraically null components (your flaps, where $df_2 \approx 0$). Define a new 1-current $\tilde{\gamma}_u$ by selecting a single degree-carrying arc of $\gamma_u$ and dropping the null flaps. Because this selection happens on the 1-dimensional slices, dropping the flaps costs exactly zero ambient 3-volume. 

**3. Bounding the Switching Seam Cost (The source of $E_y^2 / S_1$):**
To assemble $W_y = \int_0^{S_1} \tilde{\gamma}_u \, du$ into a valid 2-cycle, you must connect the selected arcs across adjacent $u$-levels. The "switching seams" required to make $\tilde{\gamma}_u$ a closed current consist of segments tracking along the source domain.
The 2-dimensional area spanned by the homotopy $H_y$ between $Z_y$ and $W_y$ consists of two parts:
*   Pushing the dropped flaps onto the selected sheet in the short source direction costs mass bounded by $R_1 \Mass(Z_y)$, yielding the $R_1 E_y$ term.
*   The cost of the switching seams between different $u$-levels is governed by the 1-dimensional traces. Using the coarea bound from Step 1, the total seam area is bounded by the length squared over the interval: $\frac{1}{S_1} \left(\int \Mass(\gamma_u) du\right)^2 \lesssim E_y^2 / S_1$.

**4. The Exact-Plaque Substitution:**
You have now constructed a specific essential 2-cycle $W_y$ and a 3-chain $H_y$ such that $\partial H_y = W_y - Z_y$, with $\Mass(H_y) \lesssim R_1 E_y + E_y^2 / S_1$. 
Because $W_y$ carries the degree and has been stripped of null-homologous flaps, any near-minimizing filling $B_y$ of $W_y$ will faithfully reflect the exact-plaque intersections. You now define your filling of the original slice as $C_y = B_y - H_y$, satisfying $\partial C_y = Z_y$.

By slicing with the target map $f_1$ before tightening, the flaps naturally have zero thickness in the slicing parameter, rendering them invisible to the mass penalty.
