### Literature/source validation

I checked the cited Guth source. Guth’s *Area-expanding embeddings of rectangles* is indeed arXiv:0710.0403 and states that it estimates area-expanding embeddings of rectangles up to dimensional constants. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/abs/0710.0403))

The use of Guth’s “Estimate 1” is consistent with the paper: Estimate 1 applies to a map of pairs \((U,\partial U)\to(S,\partial S)\) of nonzero degree and gives a lower bound for \(k\)-dilation in terms of products of side-length quotients. ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/0710.0403)) The algebra leading to
\[
R_1^2\operatorname{Vol}(R)\gtrsim S_1^3S_2S_3S_4
\]
for \(n=4,k=2,j=1,l=4\) is correct.

The cited relative isoperimetric profile is also supported by Guth’s Theorem 3, which gives a small-volume relative isoperimetric profile for rectangles and, separately, a general linear estimate \(I_R^k(V)\le C(n)R_{k+1}V\). ([arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/pdf/0710.0403)) The specialized \(2\)-cycle bound used in the draft is a reasonable consequence.

`GuthWidth` and `FedererGMT` are listed in the bibliography but are not materially used in the proof. This is not a mathematical error, but they do not fill the missing gap.

## Section-by-section audit

### 1. Problem statement and interpretation

The interpretation of degree \(1\) as relative degree on
\[
H_4(R,\partial R;\mathbb Z)\to H_4(S,\partial S;\mathbb Z)
\]
is appropriate.

The smoothing paragraph is still only sketched. It is plausible that a piecewise smooth pair map can be smoothed relative to boundary strata with small \(C^1\)-loss on strata, but this is not proved. The target-rescaling step also requires some explicit constant bookkeeping. This is likely fixable, but it remains a minor gap in the written reductions.

The major issue is that the section now openly says the document isolates the remaining hard input rather than proving it. That is appropriate exposition for a partial proof, but not for a complete solution.

### 2. Standard inputs

The singular-value argument that \(\Dil_2(f)\le1\) implies \(\Dil_3(f),\Dil_4(f)\le1\) is correct.

The Guth estimate \((\ref{guth14})\) is correctly derived from Estimate 1.

The stated relative isoperimetric profile is correctly restricted to the small-volume range \(A\le c_*R_1R_2\). This addresses a previous concern: the smallness hypothesis is essential and is now present.

The inverse estimate
\[
\Fill_R(Z)\ge L
\Rightarrow
\Mass Z\ge c\min\{R_1R_2,L^{2/3},(R_1L)^{1/2}\}
\]
is valid after adjusting constants. The consequence
\[
\Mass Z<c_*R_1R_2\Rightarrow \Fill_R(Z)\le C R_1R_2^2
\]
is also valid, using \(R_1\le R_2\).

### 3. Central target plane lemma

The calibration argument is sound. The choice of a \(1\)-Lipschitz function \(\psi\) vanishing on the \((x_3,x_4)\)-boundary and satisfying \(\psi\gtrsim S_3\) on the central rectangle \(Q\) is valid because \(S_3\le S_4\). The form
\[
\beta=\psi\,dx_1\wedge dx_2
\]
has \(d\beta\) of comass \(\le1\), and the relative boundary terms vanish for the reasons stated.

This proves the lower bound
\[
\Mass Y\gtrsim S_1S_2S_3.
\]

### 4. Proposition: first alternative mechanism

The proof is mostly correct.

The current-theoretic argument that \(f_\#[[R]]=[[S]]\) as a relative \(4\)-current is compressed but standard: the relative degree gives multiplicity \(1\) in the interior, and no nonzero \(4\)-current can be supported on \(\partial S\), a \(3\)-rectifiable set.

Slicing naturality gives
\[
f_\#\langle [[R]],F,y\rangle=\pm P_y
\]
for a.e. \(y\in Q\). Then any filling of \(Z_y\) pushes forward to a filling of \(P_y\), and \(\Dil_3(f)\le1\) controls the mass. The calibration lower bound and small-volume isoperimetric profile force
\[
\Mass Z_y\gtrsim R_1R_2.
\]
Coarea then gives
\[
\operatorname{Vol}(R)\gtrsim R_1R_2S_3S_4,
\]
hence \(R_3R_4\gtrsim S_3S_4\).

This proves the restricted first-alternative statement:
\[
R_1R_2^2\lesssim S_1S_2S_3
\Rightarrow
R_3R_4\gtrsim S_3S_4.
\]

Minor issue: the final theorem uses strict inequalities with a universal \(\kappa\). This can be handled by lowering constants, but the draft does not spell out the global constant selection.

### 5. Single-slice argument

This section is mathematically sound as a partial result.

Assuming \(R_1=\alpha S_1\le S_1\) and failure of the first alternative with a sufficiently small constant, Proposition \(\ref{firstalt}\) gives
\[
R_1R_2^2\gtrsim S_1S_2S_3.
\]
The lower bound
\[
\Mass Z_y
\gtrsim
\min\{R_1R_2,(S_1S_2S_3)^{2/3},(R_1S_1S_2S_3)^{1/2}\}
\]
then implies
\[
\operatorname{Vol}(R)\gtrsim
\alpha^{1/2}S_1S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4.
\]
Together with Guth’s monomial estimate
\[
\operatorname{Vol}(R)\gtrsim
\alpha^{-2}S_1S_2S_3S_4,
\]
optimization gives
\[
\operatorname{Vol}(R)
\gtrsim
S_1S_2^{3/5}S_3^{7/5}S_4.
\]
This is correctly identified as short of the desired bound in the high-aspect regime \(S_3\gg S_2\).

### 6. Corrected hard-case target

This section introduces the main remaining gap.

The weighted coarea estimate
\[
I\le C\operatorname{Vol}(R)
\]
is plausible and essentially correct. The pointwise inequality
\[
\|dG|_{\ker dF}\|_{HS}^2J_2F\le C
\]
does follow from \(\Dil_2(f)\le1\), although the draft should include the two-line singular-value proof. This was merely asserted.

The relaxed essential filling norm
\[
\EFill_f(y)=\inf\{\Mass C:\ C\in N_3(R,\partial R;\mathbb R),\
f_\#(\partial C)=\pm P_y\}
\]
is a reasonable idea, and the calibration lower bound
\[
\EFill_f(y)\gtrsim S_1S_2S_3
\]
is correct if such \(C\) exists and if push-forward mass is controlled by \(\Dil_3(f)\). Existence follows by filling the actual slice \(Z_y\), but the draft should explicitly say this.

However, several technical points remain underdeveloped:

1. **Measurable near-minimizers.** The draft assumes measurable \(C_y^0\) with \(\Mass C_y^0\le2\EFill_f(y)\). No measurable selection argument is given.

2. **Real normal currents and relative notation.** The notation \(N_3(R,\partial R;\mathbb R)\) and the condition \(f_\#(\partial C)=\pm P_y\) need precise interpretation in the relative current complex.

3. **Bad-set measurability.** The sets \({\mathcal B}_z\) should be shown to be measurable, and restrictions \(B\llcorner{\mathcal B}_z\) should be justified for normal currents.

4. **Bad-set Fubini estimate.** The estimate is plausible and probably true, but the proof is sketched only informally. It depends on the claim that for fixed \(x\), the set of \(z\) for which \(x\in{\mathcal B}_z\) has measure \(\lesssim R_3R_4\). This follows from the area bound for the six coordinate \(2\)-plaques, but a complete proof should spell out the area formula/measurability details.

The decisive problem is \((\mathrm{QEAT})\):
\[
 A_\Omega
 \le C\left(R_1I+\frac{I^2}{S_1q}\right)
 +\frac{C}{q}\int_Q\int_\Omega
       \Mass(C_y^0\llcorner{\mathcal B}_z)\,dy\,dz .
\]
This is not proved or cited. It is not a routine consequence of the preceding material. It is exactly the hard missing theorem.

### 7. Quotient formulation

The revision improves the previous draft by recognizing a genuine boundary-compatibility problem: if \(W_y=\partial C_y^0\), then \(f_\#W_y=f_\#Z_y\), but \(W_y-Z_y\) is only \(f\)-null, and ordinary source deformations need not preserve \(f\)-nullity.

This addresses one of the previous concerns at the conceptual level. But it introduces new technical vagueness:

- The quotient complex
  \[
  N_\ast(R,\partial R;\mathbb R)/\ker f_\#
  \]
  is not defined carefully.
- No quotient mass/filling norm is constructed.
- The bad term
  \[
  \Mass(C_y^0\llcorner{\mathcal B}_z)
  \]
  depends on a representative, not merely on a quotient class. Thus it is not clear in what precise sense \((\mathrm{QEAT})\) is “equivalent” to a quotient formulation.
- The proposed quotient or dual cochain tightening is not proved.

So the revision correctly diagnoses the earlier problem, but it does not solve it.

### 8. Conditional algebra assuming \((\mathrm{QEAT})\)

The absorption algebra is correct.

Assuming \((\mathrm{QEAT})\), the bad-set Fubini estimate gives
\[
A_\Omega
\le
C\left(R_1I+\frac{I^2}{S_1q}\right)
+
C\frac{R_3R_4}{S_3S_4}A_\Omega.
\]
If \(R_3R_4\le\kappa S_3S_4\), the last term can be absorbed for small \(\kappa\). If also
\[
I\le \delta S_1S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4,
\]
then the two remaining terms are smaller than the lower bound for \(A_\Omega\), provided \(\delta,\kappa\) are sufficiently small. Hence
\[
I\gtrsim S_1S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4,
\]
and weighted coarea yields the desired volume lower bound.

Thus, if \((\mathrm{QEAT})\) were proved, the hard case would be resolved. But the proof remains conditional.
