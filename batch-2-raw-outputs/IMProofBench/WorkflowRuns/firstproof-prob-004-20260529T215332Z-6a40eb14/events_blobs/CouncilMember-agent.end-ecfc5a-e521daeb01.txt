My take: neither replacement route closes the gap in its present form.

**(1) \(f_1=t\) slicing.** The level-set theorem you would need is false unless it contains a 4D “no escape through \(x_1\)” term. For \(M_t=f_1^{-1}(t)\), \(g=(f_2,f_3,f_4)\), \(w=|\nabla f_1|^{-1}\), the universal consequences are only
\[
\|\Lambda^2 dg\|\le 1,\qquad \|dg\|\le w,\qquad 
\int_{M_t} w\gtrsim S_2S_3S_4 .
\]
A stronger bound \(\int_{M_t}w\gtrsim S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4\) is already false for the identity map on \((1,1,L,L)\): \(w=1\), so the left side is \(L^2\), while the desired scale is \(L^{5/2}\). The missing ingredient is exactly that intervals in the target level \(\{x_1=t\}\) should not fill through the short \(x_1\)-direction. But preventing that is a parametric 4D statement, not a 3D level theorem.

**(2) Postcomposition with Guth maps.** There is a clean obstruction to this strategy if the final step is a published monomial estimate. Suppose \(h:S\to T\) has degree one and \(\Dil_2(h)\le D\). Guth’s \(j=1,k=2,l=4\) estimate applied to \(h\) gives
\[
D^{-3}T_1^3T_2T_3T_4 \lesssim S_1^2\Vol(S).
\]
Thus applying the same estimate to \(h\circ f:R\to T\) cannot produce more than the original \(S_1^2\Vol(S)\) monomial. In the stress case \(S=(1,1,L,L)\), this is \(L^2\), whereas the mixed target scale corresponds to \(L^{5/2}\). So postcomposition cannot manufacture the missing \((S_3/S_2)^{1/2}\) factor unless the auxiliary map already encodes a genuinely mixed estimate. The tempting Chapter 8 “stretch/split” interpretations are also constrained by Estimate 1, as you found.

A precise weaker substitute for HAF that I would isolate is the **low-energy plaque version**. Let \(q=|Q|\), \(E_y=\int_{Z_y}\|dG|_{TZ_y}\|^2\), \(I=\int_QE_y\), and
\[
\Omega=\{y:E_y\le 4I/q\}.
\]
Then \(|\Omega|\ge 3q/4\). Choose near-minimizing fillings \(B_y\) of \(Z_y\). For \(z\in Q\), define \(\mathcal B_z\) using all coordinate 2-plaques:
\[
\mathcal B_z=\{x:\ z\in F(P_{ij}(x)) \text{ for some } i<j\}.
\]
It is enough to prove
\[
\int_\Omega\Fill_R(Z_y)\,dy
\le C\int_\Omega\left(R_1E_y+\frac{E_y^2}{S_1}\right)dy
+\frac Cq\int_Q\int_\Omega \Mass(B_y\llcorner\mathcal B_z)\,dy\,dz. \tag{LE}
\]
Indeed, plaque averaging gives
\[
\frac1q\int_Q\Mass(B_y\llcorner\mathcal B_z)\,dz
\lesssim \frac{R_3R_4}{S_3S_4}\Mass B_y,
\]
because each coordinate plaque has \(F\)-image area \(\le R_3R_4\). In the hard regime this term absorbs. Since \(\Fill_R(Z_y)\gtrsim S_1S_2S_3\),
\[
S_1S_2S_3q
\lesssim R_1I+\frac{I^2}{S_1q}.
\]
Hence either \(I\gtrsim S_1S_2S_3q/R_1\), which is stronger under \(R_1\ll S_1\), or
\[
I\gtrsim S_1q(S_2S_3)^{1/2}
\sim S_1S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4.
\]
Then weighted coarea gives the desired volume bound.

So I would not try to prove full HAF first. Prove (LE), probably by a cubical “Manhattan” deformation using exact coordinate-plaque tracks. Avoid sharp cutting of \(B_y\cap\mathcal B_z\); the boundary/interface term is the main trap.
