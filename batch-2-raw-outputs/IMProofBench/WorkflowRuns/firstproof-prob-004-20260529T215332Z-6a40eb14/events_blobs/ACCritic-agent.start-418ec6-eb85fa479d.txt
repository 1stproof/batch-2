## Paragraph-by-paragraph mathematical audit

### 1. “Problem statement and interpretation”

The interpretation of degree as relative homology degree is faithful and reasonable. Renaming the small constant from \(k\) to \(\kappa\) is also sensible.

However, the second paragraph is fatal: the author states that the document proves only a partial replacement
\[
\operatorname{Vol}(R)\gtrsim S_1S_2^{3/5}S_3^{7/5}S_4
\]
and that the estimate needed to reach the problem’s required bound is unproved. This means the document does not prove the theorem.

### 2. “Standard tools”

The PL/transversality reduction is plausible but not fully written at proof level. In particular, the approximation relative to a boundary collar, preservation of relative degree, and passage of slices under flat convergence are asserted rather than proved. These are likely standard, but a complete solution should either cite precise theorems or avoid needing this reduction.

The singular-value argument
\[
\Dil_2(f)\le 1 \implies \Dil_j(f)\le 1,\quad j=3,4
\]
is correct: if \(\sigma_1\sigma_2\le1\), then \(\sigma_i\le1\) for \(i\ge2\), hence \(\sigma_1\cdots\sigma_j\le1\) for \(j\ge2\).

The use of Guth’s rectangle isoperimetric profile to obtain
\[
\Fill_R(Z)\le C_I\max\{A^{3/2},A^2/R_1\}
\]
for \(A\le c_IR_1R_2\) appears consistent with the known profile. But in a final proof, the theorem should be quoted precisely, including conventions for the small-volume branch and for relative currents.

The specialization of Guth’s Estimate 1 to
\[
R_1^3R_2R_3R_4\ge c_GS_1^3S_2S_3S_4
\]
is algebraically correct if the stated Estimate 1 is accepted.

### 3. “The first alternative”

The central target filling lemma is sound in outline. The calibration
\[
d(\psi\,dx_1\wedge dx_2)=d\psi\wedge dx_1\wedge dx_2
\]
has controlled comass, and the boundary terms vanish on \(\partial S\) for the reasons stated. Since \(y_3,y_4\) are central and \(S_3\le S_4\), a Lipschitz function \(\psi\) vanishing on the \((x_3,x_4)\)-boundary with \(\psi(y)\gtrsim S_3\) exists.

The proof of Proposition `first alternative` is also essentially correct:

- slicing naturality gives \(f_\#Z_y=P_y\) as a relative current;
- fillings of \(Z_y\) push forward to fillings of \(P_y\);
- \(\Dil_3(f)\le1\) controls the pushed-forward filling volume;
- Guth’s profile then forces \(\Mass Z_y\gtrsim R_1R_2\);
- coarea yields
  \[
  \Vol(R)\ge \int_Q\Mass Z_y\,dy\gtrsim R_1R_2S_3S_4,
  \]
  hence \(R_3R_4\gtrsim S_3S_4\).

This section is one of the strongest parts of the draft. Some technical details about relative-current representatives and regular values should be expanded in a polished proof, but no fatal error appears here.

### 4. “Consequences of the same slicing”

The derivation of the lower bound
\[
\Mass Z_y\gtrsim \min\{R_1R_2,(S_1S_2S_3)^{2/3},(R_1S_1S_2S_3)^{1/2}\}
\]
is correct given the previous filling lower bound and the profile estimate.

The interpolation algebra is correct. In particular, after failure of the first alternative and with \(\alpha=R_1/S_1\),
\[
\Vol(R)\gtrsim \alpha^{1/2}T,\qquad
\Vol(R)\gtrsim \alpha^{-2}S_1S_2S_3S_4,
\]
where \(T=S_1S_2^{1/2}S_3^{3/2}S_4\). Combining these with weights \(4/5\) and \(1/5\) indeed gives
\[
\Vol(R)\gtrsim S_1S_2^{3/5}S_3^{7/5}S_4.
\]

The draft correctly notes that this misses the desired bound by the unbounded factor \((S_3/S_2)^{1/10}\). Thus this section proves only a partial estimate.

### 5. “Weighted coarea”

The pointwise inequality
\[
\lambda^2J_2F\le1
\]
is correct. If \(T=\ker dF\), \(N=T^\perp\), and \(v\in T\) realizes \(\lambda=\|dG|_T\|\), then
\[
|df(v)\wedge df(n)|\ge \lambda |dF(n)|
\]
for \(n\in N\). Since \(\Dil_2(f)\le1\), this bounds the singular values of \(dF|_N\) by \(\lambda^{-1}\), giving \(\lambda^2J_2F\le1\). Coarea then gives
\[
I=\int_QE_y\,dy\le \Vol(R).
\]

The lower bound
\[
E_y\ge S_1S_2
\]
also follows from \(G_\#Z_y=[0,S_1]\times[0,S_2]\) as a relative current and the area formula.

This section is mathematically plausible and useful, but by itself gives only the basic volume lower bound, not the theorem.

### 6. “The self-absorbing plaque-puncture reduction”

The measurable selection paragraph is plausible but not fully rigorous as written. The use of Jankov–von Neumann selection is reasonable, but the transition from additive \(\varepsilon\)-minimizers to a measurable family satisfying
\[
\Mass B_y\le2\Fill_R(Z_y)
\]
requires more care. Since \(\Fill_R(Z_y)\) is positive on the central set by (3), this is likely fixable by selecting directly from the analytic set \(\Mass B<2\Fill_R(Z_y)\), but the present diagonal-sequence wording does not by itself prove the claimed pointwise bound without compactness or an attainment argument.

The plaque bad-mass lemma is basically correct. For fixed \(x\), the bad set of \(z\)’s is contained in the union of images of six coordinate plaques, each having area at most \(R_iR_j\le R_3R_4\), because \(J_2F\le1\). Integrating against \(d\|B_y\|\,dy\,dz\) gives the stated self-absorbing estimate.

The fatal gap is estimate (13):
\[
 \int_\Omega\Fill_R(Z_y)\,dy
 \le C\int_\Omega\left(R_1E_y+\frac{E_y^2}{S_1}\right)dy
      +\frac{C}{q}\int_Q\int_\Omega
        \Mass(B_y\llcorner\mathcal B_z)\,dy\,dz .
\]
This is not proved and is not cited as a known theorem. The author explicitly says it is unresolved. Since the subsequent derivation of the desired second alternative depends entirely on (13), the proof cannot be accepted.

The algebra after assuming (13) is correct: the low-energy condition gives (14), the plaque term self-absorbs when \(R_3R_4\le\kappa S_3S_4\), and the lower bound \(A_\Omega\gtrsim S_1S_2S_3q\) forces \(I\gtrsim T\), hence \(\Vol(R)\gtrsim T\). But this is conditional on the missing estimate.

### 7. “Directional filling check”

The stated directional filling inequality
\[
 \Mass Y\le C\{R_1(\Vol_{23}Z+\Vol_{24}Z+\Vol_{34}Z)
      +R_2(\Vol_{13}Z+\Vol_{14}Z)+R_3\Vol_{12}Z\}
\]
is plausible as a relative rectangular specialization of Guth’s directional isoperimetric inequality. However, because this exact relative version is not proved in the document and the accessible web rendering did not expose Proposition 2.2’s full formula, a final solution should either quote the exact proposition with hypotheses or prove the specialization.

The bookkeeping leading to (16) is correct: slicing by \(F=(f_3,f_4)\) makes the projected directional masses complementary to the minors of \(dF\). This section does not close the proof and correctly acknowledges that it is weaker than (13).

### 8. “\(f_1\)-level slicing is not a substitute”

This section is diagnostically useful but not part of a proof. The claim that slicing by \(f_1\) gives only
\[
\int_{M_t}w\,d\Mass\ge S_2S_3S_4
\]
is reasonable. The note that the stronger desired pointwise bound is false for the identity map is correct when \(S_3\gg S_2\).

### 9. “Remaining open issues”

This section explicitly states that the proof is incomplete at an essential point. It identifies (13) as the missing low-energy averaged fixed-puncture parametric tightening estimate. This confirms that the document is not a full solution.
