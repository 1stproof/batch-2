Relevant local excerpts:

- Guth-Liokumovich v3, text lines 114-149: recalls Federer-Fleming isoperimetric inequality, then explains the desired parametric version for families of relative cycles and continuous families of fillings.
- Guth-Liokumovich v3, text lines 148-160: says a continuous family of fillings needs a contractible family and depends on coefficient choices.
- Guth-Liokumovich v3, text lines 203-213: states a parametric isoperimetric conjecture for families of cycles in `R^n`; the difficulty is a mass bound independent of parameter dimension.
- Guth-Liokumovich v3, text lines 221-285: formulates parametric coarea and gives the spiral obstruction showing that a naive common coarea cut is false.
- Guth-Liokumovich v3, text lines 1351-1375: gives a counterexample to parametric isoperimetric inequality for contractible integer-coefficient 0-cycle families.
- Staffa v2, text lines 7-8 and 191-204: proves Guth-Liokumovich's parametric coarea conjecture for 1-cycles, including rectangular/cubical domains, but this is a 1-cycle coarea result, not the needed 2-cycle filling deformation.
- Guth `Area-expanding embeddings of rectangles`, existing text around lines 430-470: Theorem 3 gives the rectangular relative isoperimetric profile.
- Guth `Area-expanding embeddings of rectangles`, existing text around lines 560-640: introduces complexes of cycles and Lemma 4.1, the small-complex null-homotopy tool.
- Guth thesis, page text 143-144: Propositions 8.1.2 and 8.1.3 give the first-alternative filling argument.
- Guth thesis, page text 149: says the thesis techniques are not close to estimating best 2-dilation between 4D rectangles.
