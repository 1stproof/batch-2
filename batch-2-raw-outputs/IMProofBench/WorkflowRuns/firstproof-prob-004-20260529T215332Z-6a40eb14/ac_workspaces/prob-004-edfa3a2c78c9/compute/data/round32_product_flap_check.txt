# Round 32 coordinate-product flap check

Target normalized to S=(1,1,L,L), so q=|Q|~L^2.
A coordinate-product flap needs one coordinate 2-product
available as the F-normal parameter area with exponent >=2.

## stress_notes_R=(L^-1/5,L^13/15,L^13/15,L^13/15)
r = (-0.2, 0.866667, 0.866667, 0.866667)
hard exponent r3+r4 = 1.73333 < 2 ? True

Conclusion: any product construction where the null sheet factor and
the F-parameter factor are honest coordinate factors is impossible
in the hard regime.  A counterexample would need a genuinely
non-product/snake F-submersion using family compatibility, not just
a low-energy flap inserted in a coordinate slab.
