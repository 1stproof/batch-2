## Referee report

### Overall verdict

`answer.tex` is **not answer-ready**. The LaTeX contract appears satisfied, but the mathematical proof has a fatal gap: the central Proposition “Opposition for modular valuated quotients” is essentially asserted rather than proved. In particular, the claimed invariance of all tropical Plücker and incidence-Plücker relations under the flat-lattice anti-involution is not established, and one explanatory statement about the resulting “flat Plücker relations” is actually false as written.

### Literature check

The cited Brandt–Eur–Zhang paper is real and does establish the relevant background correspondence between valuated flag matroids / flag Dressians / flags of projective tropical linear spaces. In particular, their Theorem 4.3.1 states that valuated matroid quotients are equivalent to inclusions of the corresponding projective tropical linear spaces. ([ar5iv.org](https://ar5iv.org/pdf/2005.13727)) The Dress–Wenzel bibliographic citation is also real and is the standard source introducing valuated matroids. ([sciencedirect.com](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/000187089290028J))

However, I did **not** find that the specific “Opposition for modular valuated quotients” proposition in `answer.tex` is a cited theorem from these references. The proof must therefore supply the details, and it does not.

## Paragraph-by-paragraph / section-by-section audit

### Conventions

The valuated-matroid model and quotient-order convention are standard and are broadly supported by Brandt–Eur–Zhang’s quotient/inclusion theorem. ([ar5iv.org](https://ar5iv.org/pdf/2005.13727)) But `answer.tex` defines the map on valuated matroid representatives while the problem defines `Dr(M)` as a poset of tropical linear subspaces. To make this well-defined, one needs either to define `Dr(M)` as valuated matroids modulo scalar or to cite/prove that the tropical linear space determines the valuated matroid up to scalar. This uniqueness fact exists in the literature, but it is not mentioned in the solution. ([pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12031988/))

The treatment of loops and projective boundary strata is also not fully specified. Brandt–Eur–Zhang explicitly introduce projective tropical linear spaces to handle loops consistently; the final remark of `answer.tex` only says loops “may be deleted,” without proving that this preserves the relative Dressian and the required involution. ([ar5iv.org](https://ar5iv.org/pdf/2005.13727))

### Section 1: “A consequence of the hypothesis”

The lemma that a finite geometric lattice with an order-reversing involution is modular is essentially correct. Flat lattices of matroids are geometric/semimodular, and the rank inequality used is standard. ([macaulay2.com](https://macaulay2.com/doc/OLD/Macaulay2-1.20/share/doc/Macaulay2/Matroids/html/_lattice__Of__Flats.html))

But the proof omits a needed justification: before “applying the order-reversing involution” to the rank inequality, one must prove that the anti-automorphism sends rank \(i\) to rank \(r-i\). This is true in a finite ranked lattice because maximal chains are reversed, but it should be stated. This is a repairable gap, not the fatal one.

### Section 2: Proposition “Opposition for modular valuated quotients”

This is the critical part of the paper, and it is not rigorously proved.

The first paragraph, proving that a quotient coordinate \(\nu(B)\) depends only on the flat \(\operatorname{cl}_M(B)\), is plausible and probably correct, assuming the precise incidence-Plücker relation being used. The argument should nevertheless explicitly state the relation and handle the edge cases \(k=0\), \(k=r\), and \(\infty\)-valued coordinates.

The fatal problem begins with:

> “Now rewrite all tropical Plucker and incidence-Plucker relations for \(\nu\) in these flat coordinates…”

No explicit list of these “flat Plücker relations” is given. The proof then says they are “intrinsic to the lattice” and supported on “residues,” but these terms are not defined sufficiently to verify the claim. The argument never shows, relation by relation, that applying \(X\mapsto X^\perp\) transforms the usual valuated matroid and quotient relations into the corresponding relations of the complementary rank.

There is also a concrete false statement in this paragraph. The text says:

> “For instance, when \(k=1\) they say exactly that on each rank-two flat of \(M\) the minimum of the point-coordinates is attained at least twice.”

This is false as written. Take \(M=U_{2,4}\), whose lattice is modular and self-dual. There is one rank-two flat, namely \(E\). The vector
\[
x=(0,0,1,1)
\]
has its minimum on \(E\) attained twice. But \(x\) is **not** a rank-one valuated quotient of \(U_{2,4}\), because the actual incidence relation for the triple \(\{1,3,4\}\) requires the minimum of \((0,1,1)\) to be attained at least twice, and it is attained uniquely. I verified this directly by a small code check. Thus the advertised “flat-coordinate” description is at least misstated, and the proof cannot rely on it as written.

This example does not disprove the theorem, but it does show that the proof’s description of the relevant relations is inaccurate. The real relations retain information about subsets/circuits inside a flat, not merely the minimum over the whole flat.

The sentence

> “Consequently the transformed list of tropical equations is precisely the flat Plucker and incidence-Plucker list…”

is the main unproved assertion of the paper. It is exactly the essential lemma needed for the construction, and it is neither proved nor cited.

The subsequent sentence

> “The same residue-by-residue check applied to a three-step flag…”

is also only a placeholder. Order reversal of quotients depends entirely on this claim, but no actual three-rank incidence relation is written down or checked.

### Section 3: Construction of \(\Phi\)

The definition
\[
\Phi(\operatorname{Trop}(\nu))=\operatorname{Trop}(\nu^\perp_M)
\]
depends on the unproved Proposition. It also needs a separate well-definedness argument on tropical linear spaces rather than representatives.

The flat-lattice extension check is mostly plausible under a loopless/simple or carefully projective interpretation. The modular equivalence
\[
Y^\perp\vee F=\hat 1
\iff
Y^\perp\wedge F=\hat 0
\iff
Y\vee F^\perp=\hat 1
\]
is correct given complementary ranks. But the proof should be more explicit about contraction bases, loops in \(F\), and the ground set of \(M/F\oplus U_{0,F}\).

### The final theorem statement

The final displayed formula is incomplete:
\[
\nu^\perp_M(A)=\nu(B),\qquad
\operatorname{cl}(B)=(\operatorname{cl}(A))^\perp,\quad |A|=r-k .
\]
It omits the necessary case distinction for dependent \(A\). If \(A\) is dependent, \((\operatorname{cl} A)^\perp\) has rank larger than \(k\), so the displayed condition on \(B\) is generally impossible or ambiguous. The earlier proposition includes an \(\infty\) case, but the theorem statement does not.

### Final remark on parallel elements and loops

The remark is not a proof. Parallel elements may be manageable because the incidence relations can force equality inside parallel classes, but this should be integrated into the proof. The statement that loops “may be deleted” is especially underjustified: deleting loops changes the ambient tropical projective space/boundary strata, and the problem did not assume loopless matroids.

## Required repairs before answer-readiness

To become rigorous, `answer.tex` would need at least:

1. A precise definition of the “flat Plücker” and “flat incidence-Plücker” relations.
2. A full proof that the order-reversing involution of the modular flat lattice sends those relations of rank \(k\) quotients to the corresponding relations of rank \(r-k\) quotients.
3. A full proof of quotient-order reversal for three-step flags.
4. A proof or citation that the construction is well-defined on tropical linear spaces, not just valuated matroid representatives.
5. A correct handling of dependent subsets in the theorem formula.
6. A rigorous treatment or explicit exclusion of loops and parallel elements.

Because the main proposition is not proved and contains a demonstrably false explanatory characterization, the solution is not complete.
