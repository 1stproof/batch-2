CAS/tool probe:
  sage, gap, singular/Singular, gp, python3, rg, curl/wget, TeX Live, pdftotext/pdfinfo were available.

Heuristic differential-evolution search:
  For p in {0.05,0.1,0.2,0.3,1/3} and n<=8, a direct discontinuous
  optimization of P(sum w_i Bernoulli(p)_i >= p) found minima numerically
  equal to p, typically at nearly concentrated weight vectors.  This is
  only weak support: the objective is discontinuous across subset-sum
  hyperplanes and the optimizer missed the known three-equal-weights
  counterexample when p=0.34.

Known counterexample check:
  For w=(1/3,1/3,1/3) and p>1/3, the event requires at least two successes,
  so the probability is 3p^2(1-p)+p^3 = 3p^2-2p^3, which is < p for
  1/3<p<1/2.

Cyclic interval reduction check:
  In the complement formulation q=1-p=a/b, Bernoulli(q) can be represented
  by random cyclic intervals of length a in Z/bZ.  A tempting deterministic
  strengthening says that for every load vector, at most a length-a intervals
  have load > a/b when a/b>=2/3.  This is false.

  Counterexample from LP feasibility:
    b=7, a=5, threshold=5/7.
    There are load vectors with six length-5 cyclic intervals above threshold.
    One numerical solution has starts (0,1,2,3,4,5); see
    code/check_interval_lemma.py for exact feasibility setup.

  Therefore the Fokkink-style circle route, if it works, must use averaging
  over independent random allocations of the original weights, not a purely
  deterministic interval-count lemma.
