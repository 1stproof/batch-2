## What changed since the previous review

The main addition is the centered two-tail comparison formulation:

\[
Y=\sum_i b_i(\zeta_i-p),\qquad 0\le b_i\le1,
\]
and the claimed equivalent inequality

\[
q\,\Pr[Y\ge p]\ge p\,\Pr[Y<-q]. \tag{CTC}
\]

This reformulation is correct: after scaling \(h=1\), HUG says

\[
\Pr\!\left[\sum_i b_i\zeta_i\ge p(B+1)\right]
\ge \frac pq
\Pr\!\left[\sum_i b_i\zeta_i<p(B+1)-1\right],
\]

and subtracting \(pB\) from the sum gives precisely \(Y\ge p\) and \(Y<-q\). Multiplying by \(q\) gives (CTC). However, this is only another equivalent form of the unproved conjecture. It does not close the gap.

The previous central objection therefore remains fully unresolved.

## Section-by-section mathematical audit

### 1. Problem statement and interpretation

The interpretation is clear and faithful: determine those \(p\in[0,1]\) for which the inequality holds for every finite nonnegative probability vector \(w\).

However, the introductory paragraph already states that the proof is incomplete: it says the full classification would follow from Conjecture~\(\ref{conj:missing}\), and that this remains “the only essential gap.” This is a fatal issue for answer-readiness.

### 2. Coloring criterion and positive examples

The coloring criterion is correct. The random-coloring argument transfers a deterministic lower bound on heavy \(a\)-subsets of color classes to the Bernoulli\((a/b)\) selection model.

The reciprocal cases \(p=1/k\) and the \(p=2/b\), \(b\ge6\), cases are correctly derived.

Lemma~\(\ref{lem:pairs}\) appears valid. The deletion/counting argument is somewhat compressed but correct: the invariant is preserved, the counted pairs are distinct, and the terminal/early-stop counts give at least \(b-1\) good pairs for \(b\ge6\).

The \(p=1/2\) proof is correct: \(X\stackrel d=1-X\), so \(\Pr[X<1/2]=\Pr[X>1/2]\), hence \(\Pr[X\ge1/2]\ge1/2\).

The deterministic coloring counterexample at \(a/b=3/10\) is correct: seven loads \(1/7\) and three zero loads give only \(\binom73=35\) heavy triples, below the required \(\binom92=36\). I cross-checked this numerically.

### 3. Multiplicative closure and topological closure

The multiplicative closure proposition is valid. The representation \(v_i=x_i y_i\), with \(x_i\sim\Ber(p)\), \(y_i\sim\Ber(q)\), gives independent Bernoulli\((pq)\) variables, and the conditional normalization argument is sound.

The closedness proof for \(\mathcal G\) is also valid. The finite set of subset sums is handled correctly in the two one-sided subsequence cases.

The base-interval corollary is correct: proving the inequality on \([1/6,1/3]\), together with \(1/2\in\mathcal G\) and multiplicative closure, would imply the full interval \([0,1/3]\).

But this is conditional, not a proof of the interval.

### 4. Negative direction

The negative examples are correct and complete for

\[
(1/3,1/2)\cup(1/2,1).
\]

For \(1/3<p<1/2\), three equal weights require at least two successes and give probability \(3p^2-2p^3<p\). For \(1/2<p<1\), two equal weights require two successes and give probability \(p^2<p\).

Thus the negative direction is fully proved.

### 5. Small-\(p\) assertion and sharpness examples

This is the fatal section. The desired positive result for \(0\le p\le1/3\) is introduced as a conjecture:

\[
\Pr\!\left[\sum_i w_i v_i\ge p\right]\ge p.
\]

The sharpness examples are correct, including the pivot-plus-dust construction. But examples showing sharpness do not prove the conjecture.

### 6. Unit-gap, homogeneous unit-gap, and centered two-tail forms

The equivalence between Conjecture~\(\ref{conj:missing}\) and UG is correct.

The homogeneous form HUG is correctly obtained by scaling.

The new centered two-tail comparison CTC is also correct. But it is only an equivalent restatement. No proof of CTC is given.

The subcritical shifted HUG proposition is valid as far as it goes. The induction is sound. However, as the draft itself says, it only proves HUG in the regime \(B\le qh/p\), which does not include the hard pivot normalization needed for the full conjecture.

Thus this section still leaves the central theorem unproved.

### 7. Cyclic, sequential, complement, pivot, and convex-hull reformulations

The rational cyclic formulation is correct. For a fixed cyclic interval, each original coefficient lands in that interval with probability \(r/b\), independently across coefficients, so the load has the desired Bernoulli distribution. The equivalence with the averaged inequality is valid.

The sequential identity is correct:

\[
\Pr[S_m<p]
=1-p\sum_i\Pr[p-w_i\le S_{i-1}<p].
\]

The complement formulation is correct: replacing \(v_i\) by \(1-v_i\) turns the desired lower-tail inequality into the strict upper-tail bound

\[
\Pr\!\left[\sum_i w_i\eta_i>q\right]\le q.
\]

The pivot inequality is correctly shown equivalent to the small-\(p\) assertion.

The convex-hull equivalence is essentially correct. The separation argument is a little terse, but the logic is sound: coordinatewise down-closedness of \(P(\mathcal D)\) allows separation from the upper orthant, producing a nonnegative separating vector.

The partition-function form follows correctly from \(\mu_p(\mathcal D)=(1+\lambda)^{-m}Z_\lambda(\mathcal D)\).

The capped slice lemma remains explicitly unproved. It also still needs conventions for edge cases such as \(\mathcal F=\varnothing\) and \(\lambda=0\), but since it is only proposed as an induction target, this is not the main defect.

### 8. Coalescence identity and failed route

The coalescence identity is correct, including the endpoint conventions for the strict event. The \(p=3/10\), \(w_i=1/4\) example is also correct:

\[
\Phi_q(w)=6517/10000,\qquad
\Phi_q(w^{ij})=637/1000.
\]

I cross-checked these values. The general equal-weight obstruction is also correct.

This section is useful as a negative result about a possible proof strategy, but it does not prove the main theorem.

### 9. Remaining open issues

The “Remaining open issues” section accurately states the problem: the positive direction for all \(p\in[0,1/3]\) remains unproved. This section alone makes the document non-answer-ready.
