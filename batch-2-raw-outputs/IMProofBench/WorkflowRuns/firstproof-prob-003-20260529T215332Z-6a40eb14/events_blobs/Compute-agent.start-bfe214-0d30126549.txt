Please work on the remaining small-\(p\) lemma for the weighted Bernoulli problem, focusing on the newly added unit-gap formulation in the current `answer.tex`.

Goal: either find a rigorous proof of the unit-gap lemma, find a precise existing theorem that directly implies it, or identify a concrete obstruction/counterexample.

Definitions to use:
For \(0<p\le 1/3\), \(q=1-p\), \(0\le b_i\le 1\), \(B=\sum_i b_i\), and iid \(\zeta_i\sim\mathrm{Bernoulli}(p)\), prove
\[
 \Pr\!\left[\sum_i b_i\zeta_i\ge p(B+1)\right]
 \ge {p\over q}
 \Pr\!\left[\sum_i b_i\zeta_i<p(B+1)-1\right]. \tag{UG}
\]
This is equivalent to the original small-\(p\) lemma by adding/conditioning on a unit pivot.

Use symbolic chamber analysis/MILP for small \(n\) and rational \(p\) to identify candidate extremal patterns. Record any conjectured extremal theorem and test it computationally.
