My strongest recommendation is to pivot away from a pure \(q\)-flow or polynomial proof and make the largest-weight induction into a **weighted shadow/transport theorem**.

Take a maximal weight \(a<p\), residual weights \(u_i\le a\), \(\sum u_i=1-a\), and \(\lambda=p/q\le1/2\). The induction target is exactly
\[
Z_\lambda(\{A:u(A)\ge p\})\ \ge\ \lambda\, Z_\lambda(\{A:u(A)<p-a\}), \tag{*}
\]
where \(Z_\lambda(\mathcal A)=\sum_{A\in\mathcal A}\lambda^{|A|}\). This is just your pivot inequality with the cap \(u_i\le a\). I would try to prove (*) directly as a fractional Hall/max-flow statement: supply \(\lambda^{|A|+1}\) at each low set \(A\) with \(u(A)<p-a\), capacity \(\lambda^{|B|}\) at each high set \(B\) with \(u(B)\ge p\), and allow monotone edges \(A\subseteq B\). The required inequality follows if this flow exists. The dual Hall cuts should compress to down/up ideals; after shifting, they look very close to your capped support-function certificate. This seems the cleanest finite-dimensional formulation of the missing lemma.

For the down-set route, I would sharpen the capped slice lemma into a transport statement. In the mixed shifted slice
\[
\mathcal D=\mathcal E\cup(\mathcal F+\{n+1\}),\qquad \mathcal F\subseteq\mathcal E,\quad \partial^+\mathcal F\subseteq\mathcal E,
\]
try to prove
\[
\mu_p(\mathcal E)+\lambda\mu_p(\mathcal F)>1
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\exists x\in P(\mathcal F):\ \lambda({\bf1}-x)\in P(\mathcal E).
\]
Equivalently, rule out the dual certificate
\[
\rho^\lambda_{\mathcal E}(c)+\lambda r_{\mathcal F}(c)<\lambda c([n]).
\]
Because the pair is shifted, I expect \(c\) can be assumed nondecreasing. Then prove the inequality first for step functions \(c=1_T\); that case should reduce to a finite shadow/routing statement inside \(T\), and general \(c\) follows by layer-cake. This is the direction I would bet on.

For the threshold-flow idea: bare monotonicity cannot work. Between subset-sum jumps,
\[
\frac{d}{dq}(q-\Phi_q)=1-I_q(\{w(A)>q\}),
\]
and \(I_q\) can be \(>1\) by a lot, e.g. four equal weights near \(q=.7\). But a **banked** flow might work: include reserve mass sitting at upcoming boundary levels, e.g. sets with \(q<w(A)\le q+\max_i w_i\), so derivative losses are paid by future strict-threshold jumps. Without such a bank, the \(q=2/3\) argument will not propagate.

I would not spend much more effort on fixed moment majorants; the continuity obstruction at \(0^+\) is structural. The relevant dual object is not a low-degree polynomial but a transport/cut certificate over subset sums.

Literature keywords worth checking: fractional matching/chromatic formulations of independence complexes, hard-core partition-function bounds, Frankl–Füredi fractional matching/shadow results, and Kellerer “\(p\)-small” type theorems. I am uncertain whether the exact lemma (*) already exists, but it has the right shape.
